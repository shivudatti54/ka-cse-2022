# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental problem of drawing lines on a raster display and why approximation algorithms are necessary.

2. Describe the Digital Differential Analyzer (DDA) algorithm, including its step-by-step procedure and the use of floating-point arithmetic.

3. Implement Bresenham's line algorithm and explain why its integer arithmetic approach makes it more efficient than DDA.

4. Apply the Midpoint line algorithm and understand its relationship to Bresenham's algorithm through the implicit line equation.

5. Handle various line orientations including lines with negative slopes, steep lines, and lines drawn in different directions.

6. Compare and contrast the three algorithms in terms of computational complexity, accuracy, and practical applications.

7. Analyze the error characteristics of different line drawing algorithms and understand how each minimizes the deviation from the ideal line.

8. Apply these algorithms to solve numerical problems commonly asked in examinations.
