# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the mathematical equation of a circle and its properties relevant to computer graphics rendering.

2. Describe the eight-way symmetry property of circles and explain how it reduces computational complexity in circle-generating algorithms.

3. Implement the Midpoint Circle Algorithm (Bresenham's Circle Algorithm) with correct initialization and decision parameter updates.

4. Calculate and interpret the decision parameter P at each iteration to determine whether to choose the East or Southeast pixel.

5. Compare the Midpoint Circle Algorithm with direct circle generation methods and explain why incremental algorithms are more efficient.

6. Apply the algorithm to generate pixel coordinates for circles of various radii and trace through the algorithm step-by-step.

7. Identify the stopping condition (x ≥ y) and explain its significance in completing the circle drawing process.

8. Analyze the time and space complexity of circle-generating algorithms in the context of raster graphics.
