# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Identify and explain the major components of a computer graphics system including display devices, input devices, and output devices.

2. Compare and contrast different display technologies (CRT, LCD, LED, Plasma) with respect to their working principles, advantages, and limitations.

3. Describe the working principle of Cathode Ray Tube (CRT) displays, including raster scan and random scan operations.

4. Classify and explain various input devices used in graphics systems, including keyboard, mouse, digitizer, and touch screens.

5. Understand the concept of resolution, refresh rate, and color depth and their impact on display quality.

6. Explain hard copy devices including different types of printers (impact, inkjet, laser) and plotters.

7. Differentiate between bitmap and vector graphics and understand their respective storage requirements.

8. Identify and describe graphics software components including system software (GDI, OpenGL, DirectX) and application software categories.
