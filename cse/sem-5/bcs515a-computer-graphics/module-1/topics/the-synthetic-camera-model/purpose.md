# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the concept of the synthetic camera model and its role in computer graphics rendering pipeline
2. Identify and describe the key camera parameters including position, look-at point, up vector, and field of view
3. Construct the viewing transformation matrix that converts world coordinates to camera coordinates
4. Distinguish between perspective and orthographic projection and explain their mathematical differences
5. Define the viewing frustum and explain the significance of near and far clipping planes
6. Apply the perspective projection matrix formula to transform 3D points to 2D screen coordinates
7. Analyze whether a given 3D point is visible within the viewing frustum
8. Understand how the synthetic camera model enables multiple viewpoints of the same 3D scene
