# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the evolution and working principles of various display technologies including CRT, LCD, LED, and OLED displays.

2. Describe the architecture and operation of raster scan display systems, including the concepts of resolution, refresh rate, and blanking intervals.

3. Analyze the role and organization of frame buffers and video RAM in graphics systems, including calculations for memory requirements.

4. Understand the functions of graphics display processors (GPUs) and their relationship with the central processing unit in the graphics pipeline.

5. Compare and contrast raster scan and random scan (vector) display systems with their respective advantages and limitations.

6. Explain the purpose and operation of video controllers in managing display timing and data flow between memory and display devices.

7. Identify and describe common input devices used in graphics systems, including mice, graphics tablets, and touch screens.

8. Apply the concept of double buffering to solve screen flicker problems in graphics rendering systems.
