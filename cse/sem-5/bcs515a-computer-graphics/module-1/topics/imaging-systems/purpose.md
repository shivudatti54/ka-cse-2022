# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Describe the fundamental components of a digital imaging system and their roles in the image acquisition process.

2. Explain the concepts of sampling and quantization as applied to digital image formation, including their effects on image quality.

3. Represent digital images in appropriate mathematical forms including matrix representation and understand the relationship between resolution and storage requirements.

4. Differentiate between various types of digital images including binary, grayscale, and color images in different color models.

5. Analyze spatial relationships between pixels including neighborhood definitions, adjacency, and connectivity concepts.

6. Compare and contrast common image file formats based on compression methods, quality characteristics, and suitable applications.

7. Calculate storage requirements for images of given resolutions and bit depths, and evaluate trade-offs between image quality and file size.
