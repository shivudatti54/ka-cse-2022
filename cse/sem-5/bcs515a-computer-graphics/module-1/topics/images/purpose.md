# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define a digital image as a two-dimensional function f(x, y) and explain the meaning of its components.

2. Explain the concepts of sampling and quantization in digital image formation and their effects on image quality.

3. Identify and describe different types of pixel neighborhoods (4-neighborhood and 8-neighborhood) and their importance in image processing.

4. Distinguish between various image types including grayscale, binary, and color images.

5. Calculate storage requirements for different image formats given dimensions and bit depth.

6. Explain common image file formats and their characteristics (lossy vs lossless compression).

7. Understand the fundamental relationship between image resolution parameters and image quality.

8. Apply knowledge of pixel relationships to solve basic image processing problems.
