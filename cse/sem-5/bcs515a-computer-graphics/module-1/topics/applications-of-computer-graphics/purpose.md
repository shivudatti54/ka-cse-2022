# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Identify and describe the major application domains of computer graphics across various industries

2. Explain how computer graphics is used in the entertainment industry, including CGI in films and real-time rendering in video games

3. Discuss the role of computer-aided design (CAD) in engineering and manufacturing, including parametric modeling and digital prototyping

4. Analyze how scientific visualization helps researchers interpret complex data in fields like meteorology, medicine, and physics

5. Compare real-time rendering requirements (like in video games) with offline rendering requirements (like in animated films)

6. Understand the mathematical foundations behind 2D and 3D transformations, including translation, scaling, rotation, and composite transformations

7. Apply transformation matrices to rotate, scale, and translate graphical objects in 2D space

8. Evaluate the impact of computer graphics on education and training through simulations and interactive learning environments
