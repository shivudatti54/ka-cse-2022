# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define affine transformations and explain their fundamental properties including parallelism preservation and collinearity.

2. Represent 2D and 3D points using homogeneous coordinates and understand why they are essential for matrix-based transformations.

3. Construct transformation matrices for translation, rotation, scaling, and shearing operations in two dimensions.

4. Apply transformation matrices to convert given coordinates to their transformed positions through matrix multiplication.

5. Compose multiple affine transformations into a single composite transformation matrix through matrix multiplication.

6. Perform rotations and scalings about arbitrary points (not origin) using the translate-transform-translate approach.

7. Analyze and solve problems involving sequential transformations to determine final object positions.

8. Apply the concepts of affine transformations to solve graphical problems and understand their applications in computer graphics pipelines.
