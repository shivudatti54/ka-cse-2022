# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the concept of homogeneous coordinates and their role in representing geometric transformations

2. Derive transformation matrices for basic operations: translation, rotation, scaling, and shearing in 2D and 3D

3. Apply matrix multiplication to concatenate two or more transformations into a single composite transformation matrix

4. Analyze how transformation order affects the final result and understand the non-commutative nature of matrix multiplication

5. Construct composite transformations for rotating or scaling objects about fixed points other than the origin

6. Evaluate the computational advantages of concatenation in graphics pipelines and real-world applications

7. Solve numerical problems involving sequential transformations on points and objects in both 2D and 3D spaces
