# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the physical basis and motivation behind the Phong lighting model in computer graphics.

2. Identify and describe the three components of the Phong lighting model: ambient, diffuse, and specular reflection.

3. Calculate the ambient, diffuse, and specular light intensities using the Phong lighting equations.

4. Compute the reflection vector given the surface normal and light direction vectors.

5. Analyze how the shininess exponent affects the appearance of specular highlights.

6. Apply the complete Phong lighting equation to solve numerical problems involving surface illumination.

7. Compare the Phong lighting model with other lighting models and explain its advantages and limitations in real-world applications.
