# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the concept of viewing coordinate system and its components (VRP, VPN, VUP, RIGHT vectors).

2. Describe the window and viewport concepts and their roles in mapping 3D scenes to 2D display areas.

3. Differentiate between parallel projection and perspective projection techniques with their respective properties and applications.

4. Apply parallel projection equations to transform 3D points to 2D projection coordinates.

5. Perform window-to-viewport coordinate transformations for displaying graphics on output devices.

6. Understand and explain the concept of clipping in viewing transformations and its importance in the graphics pipeline.

7. Construct perspective projection transformation matrices and calculate projected coordinates for given 3D points.

8. Analyze the viewing pipeline and explain how each stage contributes to the final image generation on display devices.
