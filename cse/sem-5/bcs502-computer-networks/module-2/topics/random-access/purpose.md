# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the need for Multiple Access Protocols in shared communication media and classify them into controlled, random, and channelization-based approaches.

2. Describe the ALOHA protocol (both Pure and Slotted) and analyze their throughput performance using mathematical formulas.

3. Calculate the vulnerable time for ALOHA protocols and determine maximum throughput under various traffic conditions.

4. Compare the different variants of CSMA (1-persistent, non-persistent, and p-persistent) with respect to efficiency, delay, and collision probability.

5. Explain the operation of CSMA/CD in Ethernet networks, including collision detection, jam signal, and binary exponential backoff algorithm.

6. Derive the minimum frame size requirement for CSMA/CD to ensure proper collision detection.

7. Describe CSMA/CA operation in wireless networks (802.11 WiFi), including IFS, random backoff, RTS/CTS exchange, and NAV mechanisms.

8. Analyze and compare the throughput performance of ALOHA, CSMA, CSMA/CD, and CSMA/CA protocols.

9. Identify real-world applications of these protocols in Ethernet (802.3), WiFi (802.11), and other networking technologies.

10. Solve numerical problems related to throughput calculation, vulnerable time, and minimum frame size in random access protocols.
