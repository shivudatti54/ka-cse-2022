# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define controlled access protocols and explain their role in managing shared communication channels in computer networks.

2. Compare and contrast controlled access protocols with random access protocols, highlighting the fundamental differences in their approach to channel access.

3. Describe the three main types of controlled access protocols—reservation, polling, and token passing—with sufficient detail to explain their operation.

4. Analyze the token passing mechanism, including the token frame structure, the process of capturing and releasing the token, and the handling of data frames in a ring topology.

5. Explain the polling operation in master-slave networks, distinguishing between SELECT and POLL commands and understanding station responses.

6. Evaluate the advantages and disadvantages of each controlled access protocol in terms of efficiency, complexity, reliability, and suitability for different network environments.

7. Apply theoretical concepts to solve numerical problems involving polling overhead, reservation system efficiency, and token passing cycles.

8. Connect the theoretical knowledge of controlled access protocols to practical implementations such as Token Ring (IEEE 802.5) and Token Bus (IEEE 802.4) networks.
