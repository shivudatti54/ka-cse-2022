# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define cyclic codes and explain the cyclic shift property that characterizes them as a special class of linear block codes.

2. Represent codewords as polynomials and understand how polynomial multiplication with the generator polynomial produces valid codewords.

3. Construct the generator polynomial for a cyclic code of given length and dimension by factoring xⁿ - 1 over the appropriate finite field.

4. Build generator and parity check matrices for cyclic codes and perform both non-systematic and systematic encoding.

5. Compute the syndrome of a received word by polynomial division and use it to detect the presence of errors.

6. Explain the error detection and correction capabilities of cyclic codes in terms of minimum distance and burst error detection.

7. Identify practical applications of cyclic codes, particularly CRC codes used in data communication and storage systems.

8. Understand the relationship between cyclic codes and other algebraic codes like BCH and Reed-Solomon codes.
