# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the concept of network congestion and identify its primary causes, including insufficient bandwidth, router buffer overflow, and synchronized packet drops.

2. Differentiate between congestion control and flow control, understanding their distinct scopes and mechanisms in computer networks.

3. Analyze the Leaky Bucket and Token Bucket algorithms, explaining how each controls traffic flow and prevents network congestion.

4. Describe the various TCP congestion control mechanisms, including slow start, congestion avoidance, fast retransmit, and fast recovery.

5. Calculate and trace the congestion window (cwnd) evolution during TCP's slow start and congestion avoidance phases.

6. Evaluate the advantages of Explicit Congestion Notification (ECN) over traditional congestion control approaches and its suitability for real-time applications.

7. Apply traffic shaping concepts to determine whether packet loss occurs under given burst conditions using Leaky Bucket analysis.
