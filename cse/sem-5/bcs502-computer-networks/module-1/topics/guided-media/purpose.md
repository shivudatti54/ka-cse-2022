# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Identify and describe the three main types of guided media used in computer networking: Twisted Pair, Coaxial, and Fiber Optic cables.

2. Distinguish between Unshielded Twisted Pair (UTP) and Shielded Twisted Pair (STP) cables and explain their applications in networking environments.

3. Explain the categories of twisted pair cables (Cat3 through Cat8) and their supported frequencies and data rates.

4. Compare Single-Mode and Multi-Mode fiber optic cables in terms of core diameter, bandwidth, distance, and cost considerations.

5. Analyze different networking scenarios and recommend appropriate guided media based on requirements such as bandwidth, distance, EMI, and budget.

6. Calculate maximum segment lengths and determine the number of repeaters required for coaxial cable installations.

7. Evaluate the advantages and disadvantages of each type of guided media for specific network implementations.

8. Understand the fundamentals of fiber optic link design including attenuation calculations and power budget analysis.
