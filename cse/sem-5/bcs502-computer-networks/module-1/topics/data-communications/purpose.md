# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Identify and explain the five fundamental components of a data communication system: message, sender, receiver, transmission medium, and protocol.

2. Differentiate between simplex, half-duplex, and full-duplex transmission modes with real-world examples.

3. Distinguish between serial and parallel transmission, and explain their respective applications and limitations.

4. Compare analog and digital data transmission, including their characteristics, advantages, and applications in modern communication systems.

5. Classify and describe various transmission media (guided and unguided) with their respective advantages, disadvantages, and typical applications.

6. Apply Nyquist Theorem and Shannon's Capacity Theorem to calculate maximum data rates and channel capacities in given scenarios.

7. Analyze the factors affecting data transmission efficiency, including bandwidth, signal-to-noise ratio, attenuation, and noise.

8. Evaluate the suitability of different communication methods for specific scenarios based on distance, speed requirements, and environmental considerations.
