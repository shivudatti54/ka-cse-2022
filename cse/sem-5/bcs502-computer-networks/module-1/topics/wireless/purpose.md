# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental principles of wireless communication, including electromagnetic wave propagation and the electromagnetic spectrum organization.

2. Identify and describe the different modes of signal propagation (ground wave, sky wave, and line-of-sight) and their appropriate frequency ranges and applications.

3. Classify wireless networks into categories (WPAN, WLAN, WMAN, WWAN) and explain the technologies associated with each type.

4. Analyze various modulation techniques used in wireless communication, distinguishing between analog and digital modulation methods.

5. Evaluate the characteristics and parameters of antennas, including gain, polarization, radiation pattern, and bandwidth.

6. Calculate signal propagation characteristics using formulas for path loss, wavelength, and channel capacity based on Shannon's theorem.

7. Compare multiple access techniques (FDMA, TDMA, CDMA, OFDMA) and explain how they enable efficient spectrum sharing.

8. Assess the factors affecting wireless signal quality, including attenuation, fading mechanisms, and interference sources.
