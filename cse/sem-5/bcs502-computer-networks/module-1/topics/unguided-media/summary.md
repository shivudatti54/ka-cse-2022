# Unguided Media (Wireless Transmission Media) - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Unguided Media**: Transmission media that transmits data without physical cables, using electromagnetic waves propagated through air, vacuum, or water
- **Electromagnetic Spectrum**: The range of all electromagnetic radiation types, organized by frequency and wavelength
- **Radio Waves**: Electromagnetic waves at frequencies 3 kHz to 300 GHz, capable of omnidirectional or directional transmission
- **Microwaves**: High-frequency waves (1-300 GHz) requiring line-of-sight propagation, used for point-to-point communication
- **Infrared**: Waves (300 GHz-400 THz) that cannot penetrate solid objects, used for short-range line-of-sight applications
- **Line-of-Sight (LOS) Propagation**: Direct path requirement between transmitter and receiver for high-frequency wireless communication

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Free Space Path Loss**: Loss increases with distance and frequency (approximately 20 log₁₀d + 20 log₁₀f)
- **Frequency Ranges**: Radio (3 kHz-300 GHz), Microwave (1-300 GHz), Infrared (300 GHz-400 THz)
- **Satellite Orbits**: GEO (~36,000 km), MEO (5,000-20,000 km), LEO (500-2,000 km)

## Key Points

- Unguided media enables wireless connectivity for mobile devices, remote locations, and broadcast applications
- Radio waves offer the best obstacle penetration and are used in most wireless LAN and cellular networks
- Microwave communication requires clear line-of-sight and is ideal for long-distance point-to-point links
- Infrared provides secure short-range communication due to its inability to penetrate walls
- Satellite communications offer global coverage but introduce latency proportional to orbital height
- Wireless signals are susceptible to interference, attenuation, and security breaches
- Frequency allocation is regulated by international bodies to prevent interference

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Confusing radio waves with microwaves; remember microwaves are a subset of radio waves with higher frequencies
- Assuming all wireless signals can penetrate obstacles; infrared and high-frequency microwaves require line-of-sight
- Neglecting security aspects; wireless networks are inherently less secure than wired networks
- Confusing satellite orbit characteristics; GEO provides fixed coverage but higher latency than LEO
