# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental concepts of packet switching and how it differs from circuit switching.

2. Describe the structure of data packets including header fields and their purposes.

3. Analyze the store-and-forward mechanism and its role in packet-switched networks.

4. Compare datagram packet switching with virtual circuit packet switching, citing their respective advantages and applications.

5. Calculate end-to-end delay in packet-switched networks considering transmission, propagation, processing, and queueing delays.

6. Evaluate the advantages of packet switching over circuit switching for data communications.

7. Identify and explain Quality of Service (QoS) mechanisms used in packet-switched networks.

8. Apply packet-switching concepts to analyze real-world network scenarios and web traffic transmission.
