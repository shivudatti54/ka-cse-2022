# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Describe the structure and components of the IPv6 datagram base header, including all eight fields and their bit lengths.

2. Explain the purpose and functionality of the Traffic Class and Flow Label fields in providing Quality of Service (QoS) in IPv6 networks.

3. Compare and contrast IPv6 header design with IPv4 header design, highlighting the improvements and simplifications introduced.

4. Identify and describe the various IPv6 extension headers, their purposes, and the order in which they must be processed.

5. Apply IPv6 address compression rules to represent IPv6 addresses in their shortest valid form.

6. Analyze IPv6 packets to determine their characteristics, including payload size, hop limit, and protocol type.

7. Evaluate the advantages of IPv6 over IPv4, particularly in terms of address space, security, and routing efficiency.

8. Explain the concept of Next Header in IPv6 and how it facilitates the chaining of extension headers and upper-layer protocols.
