# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the concept of Label Switched Router (LSR) and its role in MPLS networks.

2. Describe the three functional planes of an LSR architecture: control plane, forwarding plane, and management plane.

3. Differentiate between the three label operations: push, swap, and pop performed by LSRs.

4. Explain the structure and function of the Label Information Base (LIB) in LSRs.

5. Understand the Label Distribution Protocol (LDP) and its role in exchanging label information between LSRs.

6. Identify and describe the three types of LSRs: Ingress LSR, Intermediate LSR, and Egress LSR.

7. Explain the concept of Penultimate Hop Popping (PHP) and its benefits in MPLS networks.

8. Analyze how LSRs enable advanced network features such as traffic engineering, QoS, and VPNs.
