# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the Telnet protocol architecture, including the Network Virtual Terminal (NVT) concept and its role in enabling communication between different terminal types.

2. Describe the Telnet connection establishment process, including TCP connection setup, option negotiation, authentication, and data transfer phases.

3. Identify and explain the various Telnet commands (WILL, DO, WON'T, DON'T, SB, SE, IAC) and their purposes in option negotiation.

4. Analyze the security vulnerabilities of Telnet, including clear text transmission, lack of encryption, and susceptibility to man-in-the-middle attacks.

5. Compare Telnet with SSH (Secure Shell) and justify why SSH is preferred for secure remote access in modern networks.

6. Demonstrate practical applications of Telnet, including network troubleshooting, testing TCP port connectivity, and manually interacting with SMTP and other services.

7. Understand the significance of Telnet in the historical development of network management and its continued relevance in specific troubleshooting scenarios.

8. Apply Telnet commands to test network services and diagnose connectivity issues in computer networks.
