# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the architecture and components of an electronic mail system, including MUA, MTA, MDA, and MRA.

2. Describe the functioning of SMTP (Simple Mail Transfer Protocol) and its role in email transmission between servers.

3. Compare and contrast POP3 and IMAP protocols, including their advantages, limitations, and appropriate use cases.

4. Analyze the format of email messages according to RFC 822 standards and understand MIME extensions for rich content.

5. Understand the concept of email addressing and the role of DNS MX records in mail routing.

6. Evaluate the differences between synchronous and asynchronous email retrieval methods and their implications for multi-device access.

7. Demonstrate knowledge of SMTP commands and the client-server communication sequence involved in sending emails.

8. Apply understanding of email protocols to troubleshoot basic email delivery problems and design appropriate email solutions.
