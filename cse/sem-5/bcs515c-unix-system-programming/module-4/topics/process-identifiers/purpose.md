# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define process identifiers and explain their role in operating system process management
2. Describe the structure and contents of the Process Control Block (PCB)
3. Explain the relationship between PID, PPID, UID, and GID in process management
4. Differentiate between various process states including zombie, orphan, and daemon processes
5. Analyze the fork() system call and understand how child processes inherit and modify identifiers
6. Demonstrate practical usage of system calls to retrieve process identifiers in C programs
7. Understand the process hierarchy and how parent-child relationships are maintained through PPID
8. Apply knowledge of process identifiers to troubleshoot common process-related issues in Unix/Linux systems
