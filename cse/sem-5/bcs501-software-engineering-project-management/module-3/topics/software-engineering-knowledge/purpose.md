# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:
1. Define software engineering knowledge and identify its three primary categories: technical, domain, and process knowledge.
2. Distinguish between explicit knowledge and tacit knowledge in the context of software development.
3. Explain how agile methodologies differ from traditional approaches in managing and transferring software engineering knowledge.
4. Analyze how specific agile practices (pair programming, daily stand-ups, retrospectives) facilitate knowledge sharing.
5. Evaluate the role of knowledge management in determining team agility and adaptability.
6. Apply the concept of bus factor to assess knowledge concentration risks in software teams.
7. Compare knowledge acquisition strategies in agile versus waterfall development models.
8. Describe how customer collaboration in agile processes contributes to domain knowledge building.
