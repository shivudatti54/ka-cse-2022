The Unified Process (UP) is an iterative, incremental software engineering methodology developed by Jacobson, Booch, and Rumbaugh. It operates on four fundamental principles: iterative development, use-case driven requirements, architecture-centric design, and risk-driven management. The development lifecycle comprises four phases—inception (10% effort), elaboration (20%), construction (60%), and transition (10%)—each producing specific deliverables and achieving defined milestones. Nine core workflows organize development activities across all phases. The Rational Unified Process (RUP) represents a commercial implementation with additional tooling and prescriptive guidance. UP provides superior risk management compared to waterfall models while offering flexibility for evolving requirements.
