Evolutionary process models address the fundamental challenge of requirement volatility in software development through iterative and incremental approaches. The Incremental Model decomposes requirements into ordered subsets, delivering functional increments that satisfy the prefix property. The Spiral Model, with its four-quadrant architecture (Planning, Risk Analysis, Engineering, Evaluation), provides comprehensive risk management through systematic risk identification and mitigation at each iteration. The WINWIN model extends Spiral with formal negotiation theory for stakeholder alignment. Evolutionary Prototyping builds actual system foundations rather than throwaway models, enabling requirements discovery through user interaction. The Concurrent Development Model represents activities as states and events, capturing inherent parallelism in modern software projects. Students must analyze project characteristics—requirement stability, scale, team expertise, and customer involvement—to select appropriate evolutionary models, as demonstrated through the self-assessment questions requiring risk calculation, decomposition analysis, and utility optimization.
