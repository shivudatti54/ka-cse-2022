A generic process model is an abstract framework defining the ordered sequence of activities (Communication, Planning, Modeling, Construction, Deployment) required to transform stakeholder requirements into software products. The Linear Sequential (Waterfall) Model follows rigid phase progression suitable for stable requirements. The Incremental Model delivers functionality in small increments, reducing risk through early value delivery. The RAD Model accelerates development through prototyping and reusable components. The Spiral Model integrates iterative development with systematic risk assessment, optimizing risk mitigation efficiency. Agile represents a paradigm shift emphasizing empirical process control through iterative delivery, customer collaboration, and adaptability to change. Model selection depends on project size, requirement stability, risk profile, team skills, schedule constraints, and desired customer involvement levels.
