Specialized process models have evolved to address limitations of linear development approaches, incorporating iterative development, incremental delivery, risk management, and adaptive planning. This module covered seven major specialized models: Iterative (progressive refinement through cycles), Incremental (delivery in functional chunks), Spiral (iterative with systematic risk assessment), Agile/Scrum/XP (customer-centric, flexible approaches), RAD (rapid prototyping with user involvement), Prototype (clarifying uncertain requirements), and Formal Methods (mathematically rigorous specification and verification). Key learning outcomes include understanding each model's structural characteristics, theoretical foundations, advantages, limitations, and optimal application scenarios, enabling informed process model selection in professional practice.
