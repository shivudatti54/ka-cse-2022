Personal Software Process (PSP) and Team Software Process (TSP) are measurement-based software process improvement methodologies developed by Watts Humphrey at Carnegie Mellon University. PSP focuses on individual performance improvement through systematic tracking of time, defects, and size metrics, progressing through maturity levels from PSP0 to PSP3. Key PSP concepts include regression-based effort estimation, defect removal efficiency calculation, and formal code reviews. TSP extends PSP principles to teams by defining specific roles (Team Leader, Development Manager, Quality Manager, Planning Manager, Support Manager) and implementing a structured development cycle consisting of Launch, Strategy, Plan, Implementation, and Postmortem phases. The relationship between PSP and TSP is hierarchical—individual PSP competency is a prerequisite for effective TSP participation. Key metrics include PRED for estimation accuracy, defect removal efficiency, team velocity, and schedule performance index. These methodologies provide the empirical foundation for process capability improvement in software engineering.
