This unit provides comprehensive coverage of software engineering fundamentals for Standard-level (B.Tech/MSc/MCA) students. It establishes the historical context (software crisis), defines the unique nature of software, and systematically examines process models including Waterfall, Incremental, Spiral, Prototype, and Agile approaches. The unit covers the complete software development lifecycle from requirements engineering through design, testing, and maintenance. Technical depth includes formal definitions, references to IEEE standards and canonical literature (Sommerville, Pressman, Brooks), and comparative analysis of methodologies. The content prepares students to select appropriate process models based on project characteristics, understand quality assurance mechanisms, and apply engineering principles to software development.
