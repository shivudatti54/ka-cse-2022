Software is formally defined as a collection of executable instructions, data structures, and documentation enabling computational functionality. Unlike hardware, software exhibits unique characteristics: it is developed rather than manufactured, does not wear out through physical degradation, is typically custom-built for specific requirements, scales with non-linear complexity, and possesses high malleability enabling modification. Software classification includes system software, application software, embedded software, and Software as a Service (SaaS) models. The software crisis of the 1960s prompted the emergence of software engineering as a discipline. Development paradigms evolved through individual programming, structured approaches, object-oriented methods, agile frameworks, and modern DevOps practices. Quality attributes encompass correctness, reliability, efficiency, usability, maintainability, portability, and reusability. Software intellectual property protection involves copyright, patents, trade secrets, and licensing mechanisms, each requiring specific engineering practices.
