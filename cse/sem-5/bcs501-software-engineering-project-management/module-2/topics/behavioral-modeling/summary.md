Behavioral modeling captures dynamic system aspects through formal techniques including finite state machines (mathematical quintuple definition), extended UML state machines, sequence diagrams for temporal interactions, and activity diagrams for workflows. Key concepts covered: states, events (signal/call/change/time), transitions with guard conditions, entry/exit/do actions, combined fragments, and control nodes. The content integrates formal definitions with practical examples from ATM and e-commerce systems, supporting requirements engineering activities including elicitation, specification, validation, and traceability.
