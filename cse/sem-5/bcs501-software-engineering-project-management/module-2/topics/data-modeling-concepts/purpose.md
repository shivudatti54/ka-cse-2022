# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:
1. Explain the role of data modeling within the broader context of requirements engineering in software project management
2. Distinguish between conceptual, logical, and physical data models and articulate their purposes in the requirements lifecycle
3. Construct Entity-Relationship diagrams capturing entities, attributes, relationships, cardinalities, and participation constraints
4. Analyze business requirements to identify strong entities, weak entities, and associative entities appropriate for the data model
5. Apply transformation rules to resolve many-to-many relationships into associative entities when relationship attributes exist
6. Map ER model constructs to UML class diagram elements, facilitating the transition from requirements to design
7. Evaluate data models against stated requirements to ensure completeness and accuracy of captured data semantics
8. Integrate data modeling activities with other requirements modeling techniques including use case development and class-based modeling
