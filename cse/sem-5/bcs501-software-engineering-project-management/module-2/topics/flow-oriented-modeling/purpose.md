This chapter provides comprehensive coverage of Flow-Oriented Modeling, a fundamental systems analysis technique essential for B.Tech, MSc, and MCA students. The content enables students to construct and interpret Data Flow Diagrams (DFDs) at various levels of abstraction, understand balancing rules for hierarchical decomposition, and apply these skills to practical systems analysis scenarios. Students will learn to differentiate between logical and physical DFDs, compare flow-oriented approaches with other modeling paradigms, and identify common modeling errors. The knowledge acquired supports requirements analysis activities in software engineering projects and prepares students for roles as system analysts or software engineers working with structured analysis methodologies.
