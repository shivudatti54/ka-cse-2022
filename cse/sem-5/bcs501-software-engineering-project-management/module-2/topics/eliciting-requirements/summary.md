Requirements elicitation is the critical first phase of software development, involving systematic gathering of stakeholder needs through techniques including interviews, questionnaires, observation, document analysis, JAD workshops, use case modeling, prototyping, and brainstorming. The process addresses functional, non-functional, and domain requirements while managing diverse stakeholder interests through formal analysis methods. Success requires understanding communication theory, overcoming tacit knowledge extraction challenges, and applying conflict resolution strategies. Effective elicitation directly correlates with project success—poor requirements account for significant percentages of software failures. Students must master both theoretical foundations and practical application of these techniques.
