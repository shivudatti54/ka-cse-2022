Requirements Engineering (RE) is a systematic discipline for identifying, documenting, validating, and managing software requirements. The five-phase RE process—Elicitation, Analysis, Specification, Validation, and Management—transforms stakeholder needs into precise, testable requirements. Functional requirements specify what the system shall do (behaviors, features), while non-functional requirements specify how well the system shall perform (performance, security, reliability, usability). Key elicitation techniques include interviews, questionnaires, observation, prototyping, and JAD sessions. Requirements Analysis involves decomposition, prioritization, and conflict resolution. The IEEE 830 standard guides SRS (Software Requirements Specification) creation. Validation techniques include reviews, prototyping, and test case derivation. Requirements Management handles changes through version control, traceability, and change control processes. Effective RE reduces project failures, with studies showing ~40% of failures trace to inadequate requirements practices.
