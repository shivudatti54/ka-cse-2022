Scenario-based modeling represents system behavior through concrete narratives that bridge stakeholder communication and technical specification. A scenario is formally defined as S = (A, G, P, M, E, Q) tuple encompassing actors, goals, preconditions, main flows, exception flows, and postconditions. Scenarios are classified into positive (happy path), negative (failure cases), and alternative (different paths to same goal) categories. The methodology employs systematic elicitation through actor identification, goal decomposition, scenario construction, validation, and refinement. Use cases aggregate related scenarios with formal UML representation including include and extend relationships. This content provides comprehensive coverage for B.Tech/MCA level students, including formal definitions, specification templates, comparison tables, and practical examples.
