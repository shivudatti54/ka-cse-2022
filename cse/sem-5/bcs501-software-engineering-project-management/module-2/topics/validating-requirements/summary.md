Requirements validation ensures documented requirements accurately represent stakeholder needs. Key objectives include completeness, consistency, feasibility, testability, unambiguity, and traceability. Primary techniques include requirements reviews (informal walkthroughs and formal IEEE 1028 inspections), prototyping (throwaway and evolutionary), test case generation (boundary value analysis, equivalence partitioning), formal specification (Z, VDM, Petri nets), and traceability analysis. Cost-benefit analysis demonstrates 10-100x cost savings from early defect detection. The critical distinction between validation ("building the right product") and verification ("building the product right") is emphasized.
