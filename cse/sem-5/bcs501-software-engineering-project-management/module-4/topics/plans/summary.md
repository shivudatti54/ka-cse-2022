A plan in software engineering project management is a formal, approved document guiding project execution through defined objectives, scope, deliverables, schedules, resources, costs, and risk strategies. The planning process follows a hierarchical model encompassing strategic alignment, requirements aggregation, WBS decomposition, effort estimation using models like COCOMO II, schedule development via Critical Path Method, risk analysis, and plan integration. Software projects require multiple interconnected plans including Project Management Plan, Scope Management Plan (with WBS), Schedule Management Plan (with CPM/PERT), Cost Management Plan (with EVM metrics), Quality Assurance Plan (with ISO 25010 standards), Risk Management Plan (using probability-impact analysis), Configuration Management Plan, Test Plan, Communication Management Plan, Procurement Plan, and Resource/Staffing Management Plan. Planning operates at strategic (1-5 years), tactical (6 months-2 years), and operational (days-months) horizons. Performance measurement uses earned value metrics including CV, SV, CPI, and SPI, while risk exposure is quantified as probability multiplied by impact. Critical path analysis determines project duration through forward and backward pass calculations identifying zero-float activities.
