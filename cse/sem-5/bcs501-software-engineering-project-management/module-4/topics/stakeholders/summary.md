# Stakeholders - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Stakeholder**: Any individual, group, or organization that can affect, be affected by, or perceive itself to be affected by a project decision, activity, or outcome.
- **Internal Stakeholders**: Those within the organization (project sponsor, project manager, development team, management).
- **External Stakeholders**: Those outside the organization (customers, users, suppliers, regulators, general public).
- **Power/Interest Grid**: A matrix classifying stakeholders into four categories based on their power to influence and interest in the project.

## Important Formulas and Techniques

**Power/Interest Grid Classification:**

- Manage Closely: High Power + High Interest
- Keep Satisfied: High Power + Low Interest
- Keep Informed: Low Power + High Interest
- Monitor: Low Power + Low Interest

**Stakeholder Analysis Process:**

1. Identification → 2. Analysis → 3. Classification → 4. Engagement Planning → 5. Engagement

## Key Points

- Stakeholder management is critical for project success; projects with effective stakeholder engagement are more likely to meet objectives.
- Customers (who buy) and users (who use) are different stakeholders with distinct needs and expectations.
- The project sponsor provides financial resources and is accountable for project success.
- The Power/Interest Grid helps prioritize communication and engagement efforts.
- Stakeholder register documents identification information, assessment information, and classification.
- Engagement levels range from Unaware → Resistant → Neutral → Leading.
- External stakeholders like regulatory bodies (e.g., RBI for banking, Ministry of Health for healthcare) must be managed appropriately.
- Stakeholder analysis should be conducted throughout the project lifecycle, not just at initiation.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Assuming all stakeholders have the same interests and needs.
- Neglecting external stakeholders like regulators and end-users.
- Treating customers and users as the same entity.
- Failing to update stakeholder analysis when project conditions change.
- Over-engaging low-power stakeholders at the expense of high-power ones.

## Revision Tips

1. Memorize the four quadrants of the Power/Interest Grid with their management strategies.
2. Practice identifying stakeholders for different project scenarios (banking, healthcare, e-commerce).
3. Remember the five-step stakeholder analysis process in order.
4. Review the stakeholder register components for exam questions.
5. Understand the difference between internal and external stakeholders with concrete examples.
