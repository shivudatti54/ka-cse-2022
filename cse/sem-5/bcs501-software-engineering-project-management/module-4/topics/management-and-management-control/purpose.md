# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define management and management control in the context of software engineering projects, distinguishing their scope from general business management.

2. Explain the five classical management functions (planning, organizing, staffing, directing, controlling) and their application in software project environments.

3. Describe the four-stage management control process and apply it to software project scenarios, including setting standards, measurement, comparison, and corrective action.

4. Differentiate between feedforward, concurrent, and feedback control mechanisms in software projects, providing specific examples of each type.

5. Perform Earned Value Management (EVM) calculations including Schedule Variance (SV), Cost Variance (CV), Schedule Performance Index (SPI), Cost Performance Index (CPI), and Estimate at Completion (EAC).

6. Interpret EVM metrics to assess project health and determine appropriate corrective actions based on variance analysis.

7. Analyze agile control mechanisms including burndown charts, velocity tracking, and cumulative flow diagrams.

8. Apply statistical process control concepts to software quality management, including interpreting control charts and identifying special cause variation.

9. Explain the role of Management Information Systems (MIS) in software project control and monitoring.

10. Apply Anthony's hierarchical framework of control levels to software project management contexts.
