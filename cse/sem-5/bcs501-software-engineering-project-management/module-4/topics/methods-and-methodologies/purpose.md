This study material provides comprehensive coverage of software development methodologies for B.Tech, MSc, and MCA students at the Standard tier. The content establishes theoretical foundations through formal definitions (e.g., Waterfall's phase transition criteria, V-Model's verification-validation correspondence, Spiral's risk threshold model), enabling rigorous understanding beyond superficial descriptions. Comparative analysis tables and decision-making frameworks support practical methodology selection, a critical competency for software project management. The material connects methodologies to project management knowledge areas (scope, risk, schedule, quality) while maintaining software engineering focus. The comprehensive treatment of both traditional (Waterfall, V-Model, Spiral) and contemporary (Agile, Scrum, Kanban, DevOps) approaches ensures students develop a holistic perspective essential for professional practice. Content depth aligns with the Standard tier expectation of "thorough theory with proofs where needed" through formal modeling of phase transitions and risk-based iteration criteria.
