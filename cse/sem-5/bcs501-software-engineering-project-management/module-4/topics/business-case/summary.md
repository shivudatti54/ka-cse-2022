A business case is a formal document justifying software project investments by presenting rationale, benefits, costs, risks, and alternatives. Key components include Executive Summary, Background and Business Need, SMART Objectives, Options Analysis, Benefits Management Approach, Financial Analysis (TCO, NPV, ROI, IRR, Payback Period), Risk Assessment, Implementation Approach, and Recommendation. Financial evaluation employs time value of money principles with discounted cash flow techniques. The business case serves as a decision-making instrument and reference point throughout the project lifecycle, bridging business needs with technical solutions.
