A project is a temporary endeavor to create a unique product, service, or result, characterized by temporary nature, unique deliverables, progressive elaboration, and limited resources. Project management applies knowledge, skills, and techniques to meet project requirements within constraints. The Iron Triangle (scope, time, cost, quality) represents fundamental project constraints requiring continuous trade-off decisions. Software projects face unique challenges including requirements volatility, technology uncertainty, and technical complexity. Key stakeholders include sponsors, project managers, project teams, customers, and external parties. The five process groups are initiating, planning, executing, monitoring and controlling, and closing. Theoretical frameworks PMBOK and PRINCE2 provide structured approaches. Effective project management is critical given that approximately 31% of software projects fail before completion.

**Question 1**: In a software project, the client requests additional features during the implementation phase that were not in the original requirements document. According to the Iron Triangle principles, what is the MOST appropriate response?

A) Accept the additional features without any adjustments to constraints
B) Reject the request entirely to protect original scope
C) Evaluate trade-offs and negotiate adjustments to schedule, budget, or scope reduction elsewhere
D) Immediately extend the timeline without consulting stakeholders

**Correct Answer**: C

**Explanation**: The Iron Triangle demonstrates that modifying one constraint affects others. When scope increases (additional features), the project manager must evaluate trade-offs—either extend the schedule, increase the budget, reduce other scope elements, or some combination. This requires formal change control procedures and stakeholder negotiation. Options A and D ignore constraint interdependencies, while B is inflexible and may not serve stakeholder interests.

**Question 2**: A software company is developing an e-commerce platform with planned completion in 6 months. Midway through development, the sponsor demands the delivery be accelerated by 2 months without additional budget. As the project manager, what should be your PRIMARY consideration?

A) Accept the challenge to demonstrate team capability
B) Evaluate feasibility through scope reduction or quality trade-offs
C) Immediately hire additional developers regardless of cost
D) Reject any timeline changes citing the original contract

**Correct Answer**: B

**Explanation**: The project manager must objectively evaluate whether the accelerated timeline is achievable through legitimate trade-offs—scope reduction (fewer features), overtime (which may impact quality or cost), or process optimization. Pure acceptance (A) or rejection (D) without analysis is inappropriate. Option C ignores cost implications. The correct approach involves formal analysis and stakeholder negotiation.

**Question 3**: According to the CHAOS Report by the Standish Group, which of the following is NOT cited as a primary cause of software project failure?

A) Changing user requirements
B) Lack of user involvement
C) Unrealistic expectations
D) Excessive documentation

**Correct Answer**: D

**Explanation**: The CHAOS Report consistently identifies "changing requirements," "lack of user involvement," and "unrealistic expectations" among top causes of project failure. While documentation practices matter, excessive documentation is not typically cited as a primary failure cause compared to the other factors. Modern Agile methodologies actually advocate minimal viable documentation.

**Question 4**: In PRINCE2 methodology, which principle ensures that project objectives remain aligned with business justification throughout the project lifecycle?

A) Manage by stages
B) Manage by exception
C) Continued business justification
D) Learn from experience

**Correct Answer**: C

**Explanation**: PRINCE2 is built on seven principles, of which "Continued business justification" states that a project must remain aligned with its original business case throughout. If the justification becomes invalid, the project should be closed. This principle distinguishes PRINCE2 from purely technical project management approaches.

**Question 5**: A software project has 60% of its budget remaining at the halfway point, but only 40% of work is complete. What does this indicate and what is the MOST likely outcome?

A) The project is under budget and on track for early completion
B) Schedule variance indicates potential cost overrun at completion
C) The project has negative variance but will definitely complete within budget
D) Resource productivity is above average

**Correct Answer**: B

**Explanation**: This scenario reveals schedule variance (SV = EV - PV = 0.4 - 0.5 = -0.1, or -10%). The negative schedule variance indicates the project is behind schedule. Given the inverse relationship between time and cost in software projects, catching up typically requires additional resources, leading to cost overruns. This is a classic indicator of project performance issues requiring corrective action.

**Question 6**: Which of the following best describes "progressive elaboration" in the context of Agile software development?

A) Detailed upfront planning of all project requirements
B) Iteratively improving the plan as the project progresses and understanding improves
C) Gradually extending the project timeline
D) Progressive disclosure of project costs to stakeholders

**Correct Answer**: B

**Explanation**: Progressive elaboration refers to the iterative process of improving and refining project plans as the project advances and stakeholders gain clearer understanding of requirements. In Agile methodologies, this manifests through sprint planning, backlog refinement, and continuous requirement evolution. This contrasts with traditional waterfall approaches that emphasize comprehensive upfront planning.

**Card 1**: Project
**Definition**: A temporary endeavor undertaken to create a unique product, service, or result, with defined beginning and end dates.

**Card 2**: Project Management
**Definition**: The application of knowledge, skills, tools, and techniques to project activities to meet project requirements.

**Card 3**: Iron Triangle
**Definition**: The project constraint model comprising scope, time, and cost as inter dependent factors, with quality as the fourth dimension.

**Card 4**: Progressive Elaboration
**Definition**: The iterative process of improving the project plan as the project progresses and additional information becomes available.

**Card 5**: Stakeholder
**Definition**: An individual, group, or organization that may affect or be affected by project activities.

**Card 6**: PMBOK
**Definition**: Project Management Body of Knowledge—a comprehensive guide by PMI encompassing ten knowledge areas for project management.

**Card 7**: PRINCE2
**Definition**: Projects IN Controlled Environments—a structured project management methodology emphasizing business justification and controlled stages.

**Card 8**: Process Groups
**Definition**: Five interconnected groups: Initiating, Planning, Executing, Monitoring and Controlling, and Closing.
