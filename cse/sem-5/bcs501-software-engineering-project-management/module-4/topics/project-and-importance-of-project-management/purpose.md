This module establishes foundational concepts of project management specifically within software engineering contexts. Students will understand the definition and characteristics of projects, distinguish between operational work and project work, and comprehend the iron triangle of project constraints (scope, time, cost, and quality). The module introduces key stakeholders and the five project management process groups. Students will learn about software-specific challenges including requirements volatility and technology uncertainty, and understand how theoretical frameworks like PMBOK and PRINCE2 guide software project management. This knowledge is essential for subsequent modules covering project planning, risk management, and cost-benefit evaluation.
