# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental principles and characteristics of traditional project management (Waterfall model)

2. Describe the key frameworks and practices of modern project management (Agile, Scrum, Kanban)

3. Compare and contrast traditional versus modern project management approaches in terms of flexibility, documentation, and customer involvement

4. Analyze when to apply traditional or modern practices based on project requirements and environment

5. Understand the significance of iterative development and continuous feedback in modern project management

6. Evaluate the advantages and limitations of both approaches in software engineering contexts

7. Apply knowledge to select appropriate project management methodology for different scenarios
