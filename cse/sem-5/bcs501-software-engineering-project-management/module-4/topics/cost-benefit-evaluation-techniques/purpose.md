This study material covers Cost-Benefit Evaluation Techniques within Software Engineering Project Management. It provides B.Tech, MSc, and MCA students with comprehensive theoretical knowledge and mathematical frameworks necessary for analyzing the economic feasibility of software projects. The content includes detailed derivations of formulas, worked numerical examples, comparative analysis of techniques, and advanced considerations for risk-adjusted analysis. Students will learn to apply techniques including ROI, Payback Period, NPV, IRR, BCR, and Break-Even Analysis to make informed investment decisions in software engineering contexts.
