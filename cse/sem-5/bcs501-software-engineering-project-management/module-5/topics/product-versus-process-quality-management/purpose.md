# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Distinguish between product quality and process quality management approaches in software engineering.

2. Explain the key characteristics and attributes of software product quality using established quality models such as ISO 9126 and McCall's model.

3. Describe process quality improvement frameworks including CMMI, ISO 9001, and Six Sigma, and their relevance to software development.

4. Analyze the relationship between process quality and product quality, and explain why both approaches are necessary for effective quality management.

5. Apply quality management concepts to analyze and improve software development scenarios.

6. Evaluate the strengths and limitations of product-focused versus process-focused quality strategies.

7. Connect traditional quality management principles to contemporary software engineering practices such as DevOps and continuous integration.
