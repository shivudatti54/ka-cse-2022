# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the concept of software quality and its importance in software engineering
2. Describe McCall's Quality Model and identify its 11 quality factors with appropriate metrics
3. Compare and contrast ISO 9126 and ISO/IEC 25010 quality models
4. Apply ISO 9126 characteristics to evaluate software quality in given scenarios
5. Understand the CMMI maturity levels and explain how they relate to process improvement
6. Select appropriate quality metrics for measuring different quality characteristics
7. Analyze real-world software systems using quality models to identify critical quality requirements
8. Compare product-focused (McCall, ISO) and process-focused (CMMI) quality approaches
