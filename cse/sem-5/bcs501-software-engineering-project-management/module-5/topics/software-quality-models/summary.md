# Software Quality Models - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Software Quality**: The degree to which software meets specified requirements and user expectations
- **Quality Model**: A framework defining quality characteristics and their relationships
- **Quality Metric**: A quantitative measure used to assess quality characteristics
- **Maturity Level**: A well-defined evolutionary plateau in process improvement

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Defect Density** = (Number of Defects / Lines of Code) × 1000 (expressed as defects per KLOC)
- **CMMI Maturity Levels**: Initial (1) → Managed (2) → Defined (3) → Quantitatively Managed (4) → Optimizing (5)

## Key Points

- McCall's model (1977) defines 11 quality factors organized into three categories: Product Operation, Product Revision, and Product Transition
- ISO 9126 defines six quality characteristics: Functionality, Reliability, Usability, Efficiency, Maintainability, and Portability
- ISO/IEC 25010:2011 updated ISO 9126 with eight characteristics, adding Security and dividing compatibility from efficiency
- CMMI focuses on process quality rather than product quality
- Quality factors are measured using specific metrics like defect density, cyclomatic complexity, and test coverage
- Product quality models (McCall, ISO) and process quality models (CMMI) serve different but complementary purposes
- Sub-characteristics in ISO 9126/25010 provide more granular quality measurement

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Confusing ISO 9126 with ISO/IEC 25010 - remember 25010 replaced 9126 and has 8 characteristics vs 6
2. Treating CMMI as a product quality model - it measures process maturity, not product attributes
3. memorizing factors without understanding their practical application in evaluation scenarios
4. Ignoring sub-characteristics - questions often ask for specific sub-characteristics

## Revision Tips

1. Create a comparison table of all quality models showing number of characteristics/factors
2. Practice mapping McCall factors to ISO 9126 characteristics
3. Remember that CMMI Level 1 is "Initial" and Level 5 is "Optimizing"
4. For exam questions, first identify the type of software (critical systems, web applications) then determine which quality factors are most important
5. Focus on understanding metric formulas - application-based questions are common in exams
