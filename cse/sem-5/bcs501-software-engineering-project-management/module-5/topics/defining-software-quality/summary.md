# Defining Software Quality - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Software Quality**: The degree to which a software product satisfies specified requirements and meets user expectations
- **Quality Assurance (QA)**: Proactive, process-oriented activities that prevent defects through process improvement
- **Quality Control (QC)**: Reactive, product-oriented activities that identify defects through testing and inspection
- **Quality Attribute**: A specific property or characteristic of software that can be measured to determine quality
- **Defect Density**: Number of defects per thousand lines of code (KLOC)

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Defect Density = Total Defects / Size of Software (in KLOC)**
- Quality interpretation: Below 1.0 = Excellent, 1.0-2.0 = Good, 2.0-5.0 = Average, Above 5.0 = Poor

## Key Points

- Software quality encompasses multiple dimensions including functionality, reliability, usability, and maintainability
- McCall's Quality Model identifies 11 factors organized into Product Operation, Revision, and Transition categories
- ISO/IEC 25010:2011 defines 8 quality characteristics: Functional suitability, Performance efficiency, Compatibility, Usability, Reliability, Security, Maintainability, and Portability
- Quality is context-dependent—different applications prioritize different attributes
- Quality Assurance prevents defects through process improvement; Quality Control identifies defects in the final product
- Different stakeholders (developers, users, administrators, business owners) have different quality priorities
- Quantitative metrics like defect density provide objective quality measurements

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Confusing Quality Assurance with Quality Control—QA is proactive/process-focused, QC is reactive/product-focused
- Treating quality as absolute rather than relative to context and domain
- Focusing only on one quality attribute while ignoring others
- Assuming all stakeholders have the same quality priorities

## Revision Tips

- Memorize the 11 factors of McCall's model and their three categories
- Remember all 8 characteristics of ISO/IEC 25010 standard
- Practice applying quality models to example scenarios
- Review defect density calculations and interpretations
- Understand the stakeholder perspective for different quality attributes
