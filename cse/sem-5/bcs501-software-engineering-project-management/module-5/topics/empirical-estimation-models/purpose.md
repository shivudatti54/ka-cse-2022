# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Understand the fundamental concepts and importance of empirical estimation models in software project management.

2. Apply Basic COCOMO model to estimate effort and development time for different project types (organic, semi-detached, embedded).

3. Apply Intermediate COCOMO model with cost drivers to produce more accurate effort estimates.

4. Explain the evolution from COCOMO to COCOMO II and understand its three sub-models.

5. Calculate Function Points for a software system and understand their advantages over Lines of Code.

6. Understand the Putnam Model and its relationship between effort, size, and development time.

7. Compare different empirical estimation approaches including Wideband Delphi and analogy-based estimation.

8. Select appropriate estimation models based on project characteristics and available information.

9. Analyze how cost drivers and scale factors influence the final effort estimates.

10. Apply estimation techniques to solve numerical problems commonly appearing in examinations.
