# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental challenges that make software estimation inherently difficult compared to other engineering disciplines.

2. Distinguish between accuracy and precision in estimation and describe why precision alone does not guarantee useful estimates.

3. Analyze the psychological and organizational factors that influence estimation outcomes and contribute to estimation errors.

4. Evaluate the strengths and limitations of various estimation techniques including decomposition and empirical models.

5. Apply the concept of the Cone of Uncertainty to explain how estimation accuracy improves as projects progress.

6. Describe the relationship between estimation practices and software quality outcomes, including how unrealistic estimates compromise quality.

7. Develop strategies for improving estimation accuracy through continuous reestimation and historical data collection.
