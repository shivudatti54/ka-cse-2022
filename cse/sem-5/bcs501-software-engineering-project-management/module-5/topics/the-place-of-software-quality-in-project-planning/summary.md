# The Place of Software Quality in Project Planning - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Software Quality**: The degree to which a software product meets specified requirements and user expectations
- **Quality Assurance (QA)**: Planned systematic activities ensuring processes meet defined standards (prevention-focused)
- **Quality Control (QC)**: Testing and inspection activities that detect defects in the product (detection-focused)
- **Quality Planning**: Process of identifying quality standards relevant to the project and determining satisfaction methods

## Important Formulas and Concepts

- **Cost of Quality Categories**: Prevention Costs + Appraisal Costs + Internal Failure Costs + External Failure Costs
- **Defect Cost Multiplier**: Cost increases exponentially—defects cost 10x more to fix in each subsequent development phase
- **Quality Metrics**: Defect Density (defects/KLOC), Test Coverage (%), MTBF (Mean Time Between Failures)

## Key Points

- Quality must be integrated into project planning from the beginning, not added later
- The triple constraint (scope, time, cost) directly impacts achievable quality levels
- Prevention is always cheaper than detection—invest in QA activities early
- ISO 25010 defines 8 quality characteristics: functional suitability, performance efficiency, compatibility, usability, reliability, security, maintainability, and portability
- CMMI has 5 maturity levels: Initial, Managed, Defined, Quantitatively Managed, and Optimizing
- Quality metrics enable objective, data-driven quality assessment
- Quality risks must be identified, analyzed, and mitigated like any other project risk

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Treating testing as the primary quality activity rather than process improvement
- Setting unrealistic quality targets without resource and schedule implications
- Ignoring stakeholder quality expectations during requirements gathering
- Reducing quality activities to meet schedule pressures without analyzing consequences

## Revision Tips

1. Remember: QA = Prevention, QC = Detection
2. Review the exponential cost curve—early defect detection saves money
3. Know the ISO 25010 eight quality characteristics by heart
4. Practice identifying quality trade-offs in project scenarios
5. Understand that quality planning is iterative and must be maintained throughout the project lifecycle
