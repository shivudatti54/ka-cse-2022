# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define a Turing Machine formally using the 7-tuple notation M = (Q, Σ, Γ, δ, q₀, q_accept, q_reject) and explain the purpose of each component.

2. Describe the structure and working of a Turing Machine, including the tape, read-write head, control unit, and state register.

3. Explain how a Turing Machine functions as both a language acceptor and a general-purpose computing device that transforms inputs to outputs.

4. Distinguish between Turing-recognizable (recursively enumerable) and decidable (recursive) languages, understanding the implications of halting behavior.

5. State and explain the Church-Turing Thesis and its significance in establishing the theoretical foundations of computation.

6. Analyze various computational models and explain why they are computationally equivalent to the standard Turing Machine.

7. Design simple Turing Machines for basic language recognition problems and trace their execution through given input strings.

8. Apply knowledge of Turing Machines to understand the theoretical limits of computation and the concept of undecidability.
