# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the architecture and working of Multi-tape Turing Machines and demonstrate how they can be simulated by basic single-tape Turing Machines.

2. Describe Non-deterministic Turing Machines, their transition functions using power sets, and explain the equivalence between NDTM and DTM through simulation.

3. Understand the concept of a Universal Turing Machine and its significance in the theory of computation as the model for general-purpose computers.

4. Define Linear Bounded Automata and explain their relationship to context-sensitive languages and limited resource computation.

5. Explain the Church-Turing Thesis and its implications for understanding computability and the limits of algorithmic computation.

6. Distinguish between decidable and undecidable problems, with specific reference to the Halting Problem and its significance.

7. Apply formal definitions and notations to represent extended Turing machine variants and analyze their computational capabilities.
