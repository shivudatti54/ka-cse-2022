# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define decidability and undecidability in the context of computational theory, explaining their fundamental differences.

2. Explain Turing's proof of the Halting Problem's undecidability, including the diagonalization argument and self-reference technique.

3. Apply the reduction technique to prove that a given problem is undecidable by reducing from known undecidable problems.

4. Apply Rice's Theorem to determine whether specific language properties are decidable or undecidable.

5. Understand the Post Correspondence Problem (PCP) and its role as a fundamental undecidable problem used for proving other problems undecidable.

6. Identify and explain at least four undecidable problems in automata theory, including the Membership, Emptiness, Equivalence, and Regularity problems.

7. Explain the significance of the Church-Turing Thesis in establishing the theoretical limits of computation.
