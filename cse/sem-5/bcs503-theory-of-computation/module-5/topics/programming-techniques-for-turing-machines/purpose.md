# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental techniques for designing Turing machines to perform computational tasks.

2. Design Turing machines for language recognition problems, including regular, context-free, and recursively enumerable languages.

3. Construct Turing machines that compute specific functions, including arithmetic operations.

4. Apply the technique of building complex Turing machines through composition of simpler sub-machines.

5. Utilize marker symbols and systematic tape management techniques in TM design.

6. Analyze and trace the execution of Turing machines on given inputs to verify correctness.

7. Understand the relationship between Turing machine design and conventional programming concepts.
