# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define Chomsky Normal Form (CNF) and state its properties for context-free grammars

2. Define Greibach Normal Form (GNF) and distinguish it from CNF

3. Apply the step-by-step procedure to convert a CFG into Chomsky Normal Form

4. Convert a CFG into Greibach Normal Form using appropriate methods

5. Identify and eliminate nullable symbols, unit productions, and useless symbols from CFGs

6. Explain the importance and applications of normal forms in parsing algorithms

7. Prove that any CFG can be converted to equivalent CNF and GNF forms

8. Apply normal form conversions in compiler design contexts such as parsing
