# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define what is meant by a closure property of a language class and explain its significance in formal language theory.

2. Identify and explain the operations under which context-free languages are closed, including union, concatenation, Kleene star, intersection with regular languages, substitution, homomorphism, inverse homomorphism, and reversal.

3. Prove that context-free languages are closed under union, concatenation, and Kleene star using grammar construction techniques.

4. Prove that context-free languages are closed under intersection with regular languages using pushdown automaton construction.

5. Identify and explain the operations under which context-free languages are NOT closed, specifically intersection, complementation, and set difference.

6. Provide counterexamples to demonstrate that CFLs are not closed under intersection and complementation operations.

7. Apply closure properties to determine whether new languages constructed from known CFLs are context-free or not.

8. Relate closure properties to practical applications in compiler design and language processing.
