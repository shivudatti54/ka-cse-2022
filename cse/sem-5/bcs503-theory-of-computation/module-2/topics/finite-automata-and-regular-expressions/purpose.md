# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and explain the five components of a Finite Automaton (Q, Σ, δ, q₀, F) and their roles in computation.

2. Distinguish between Deterministic Finite Automaton (DFA) and Non-Deterministic Finite Automaton (NFA) based on their transition functions and computational behavior.

3. Design DFA to accept languages with specific patterns such as strings ending with certain substrings, having no consecutive symbols, or containing specific characteristics.

4. Convert an NFA to an equivalent DFA using the Subset Construction algorithm, including handling ε-closures.

5. Construct Regular Expressions from given language specifications and understand the algebraic laws governing regular expressions.

6. Convert Regular Expressions to NFA using Thompson's Construction algorithm.

7. Convert Finite Automata to Regular Expressions using the State Elimination method.

8. Apply the pumping lemma to prove whether a given language is regular or not.
