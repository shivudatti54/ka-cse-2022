# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define what is meant by closure properties of regular languages and explain their significance in automata theory.

2. Prove that regular languages are closed under union, intersection, and complement operations using formal constructions.

3. Apply the product automaton construction to determine whether the union or intersection of two regular languages is regular.

4. Demonstrate closure under concatenation and Kleene star using NFA construction with ε-transitions.

5. Explain how homomorphism and inverse homomorphism operations preserve regularity in regular languages.

6. Use closure properties strategically to prove that certain languages are not regular.

7. Construct finite automata for languages derived from existing regular languages through various closure operations.

8. Analyze and distinguish between operations that preserve regularity and those that do not in the context of formal language theory.
