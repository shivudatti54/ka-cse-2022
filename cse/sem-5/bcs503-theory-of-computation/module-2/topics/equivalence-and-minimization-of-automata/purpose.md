# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and explain the concepts of equivalent (indistinguishable) and distinguishable states in a DFA.

2. Apply the partition refinement algorithm to minimize a DFA to its canonical form.

3. Identify distinguishing strings between two states in a finite automaton.

4. Use the Myhill-Nerode theorem to determine the minimum number of states required to recognize a regular language.

5. Check whether two DFAs are equivalent by constructing product automata or by minimization and comparison.

6. Convert NFAs to DFAs and verify their equivalence through minimization techniques.

7. Remove unreachable (useless) states from a DFA before performing minimization.

8. Understand the uniqueness property of minimized DFAs and its significance in automata theory.
