# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and explain the components of Deterministic and Non-Deterministic Finite Automata, and construct DFA/NFA for given language specifications.

2. Convert regular expressions to NFAs using Thompson's construction and minimize DFAs using the equivalence method.

3. Apply the pumping lemma for regular languages to prove that specific languages are not regular.

4. Design Context-Free Grammars and Pushdown Automata for context-free languages, and explain their equivalence.

5. Construct Turing machines for simple computational problems and distinguish between recursive and recursively enumerable languages.

6. Classify computational problems into appropriate complexity classes (P, NP, NP-Complete, NP-Hard) based on their inherent difficulty.

7. Perform polynomial-time reductions between computational problems to establish NP-completeness.

8. Analyze the practical implications of complexity theory for algorithm design and the P vs NP problem.
