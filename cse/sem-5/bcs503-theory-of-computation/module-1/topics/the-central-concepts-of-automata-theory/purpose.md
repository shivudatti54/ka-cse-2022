# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and distinguish between alphabets, strings, and languages with appropriate examples

2. Explain the components and operation of Deterministic Finite Automata (DFA) and Non-Deterministic Finite Automata (NFA)

3. Design DFA and NFA for given language specifications

4. Convert an NFA to an equivalent DFA using subset construction

5. Write regular expressions for describing languages and convert between regular expressions and finite automata

6. Apply closure properties to determine if languages generated by operations on regular languages are regular

7. Use the Myhill-Nerode theorem to characterize regular languages and determine state minimization

8. Analyze and solve problems related to finite automata, regular expressions, and language properties in examination contexts
