# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental challenges of synchronizing physical clocks in distributed systems, including clock drift and network delays.

2. Describe Cristian's algorithm and its approach to clock synchronization, including the calculation of clock offset using round-trip time measurements.

3. Analyze the Berkeley algorithm and explain how it achieves clock synchronization through consensus averaging among multiple machines.

4. Understand the architecture and operation of the Network Time Protocol (NTP), including the concept of stratum levels and its hierarchical organization.

5. Differentiate between internal and external clock synchronization and explain why both are important in distributed systems.

6. Compare and contrast the various clock synchronization algorithms including their advantages, disadvantages, and suitable application scenarios.

7. Explain the importance of monotonic clock progression and describe the difference between clock stepping and clock slewing adjustment methods.
