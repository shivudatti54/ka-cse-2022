# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define what constitutes a distributed system and distinguish it from other computing paradigms.

2. Explain the four fundamental characteristics of distributed systems: concurrency, lack of shared memory, independent failure, and absence of global clock.

3. Identify and describe the five primary goals of distributed systems design.

4. Classify distributed systems into categories based on their purpose and architecture.

5. Analyze the key challenges involved in building and maintaining distributed systems.

6. Provide examples of real-world distributed systems and explain how they embody distributed systems principles.

7. Discuss the role of transparency in user experience and system design.

8. Evaluate the trade-offs involved in distributed systems design decisions.
