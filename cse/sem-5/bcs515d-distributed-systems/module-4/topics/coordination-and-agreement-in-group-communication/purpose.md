# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental challenges of coordination and agreement in distributed group communication systems.

2. Differentiate between various types of multicast communication including basic multicast, reliable multicast, and ordered multicast with their respective guarantees.

3. Describe the consensus problem and analyze the FLP impossibility result in the context of asynchronous distributed systems.

4. Compare and contrast major consensus algorithms including Paxos and Raft, understanding their approaches to achieving agreement in the presence of failures.

5. Solve the Byzantine Generals Problem and calculate the minimum number of processes required to tolerate Byzantine failures.

6. Analyze group membership protocols and explain the concept of view synchrony in dynamic group communication.

7. Apply total ordering protocols to ensure consistent message delivery across distributed processes in practical scenarios.
