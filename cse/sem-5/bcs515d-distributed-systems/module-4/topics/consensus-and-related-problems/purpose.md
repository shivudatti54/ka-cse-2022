# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define the consensus problem and explain its three fundamental properties: agreement, validity, and termination.

2. Analyze different failure models in distributed systems including crash failures, omission failures, and Byzantine failures.

3. Explain the Byzantine Generals Problem and the mathematical relationship between the number of faulty processes and total processes required for Byzantine consensus.

4. Describe the Paxos consensus algorithm, including its three phases (Prepare, Accept, Learn) and understand its safety guarantees.

5. Compare Paxos and Raft consensus algorithms, highlighting Raft's design goals for understandability and practical implementability.

6. Understand how Raft handles leader election, log replication, and ensures safety in distributed systems.

7. Apply the concept of majority voting and quorum systems in solving distributed consensus problems.

8. Recognize the practical applications of consensus algorithms in modern distributed systems like databases, blockchain, and cloud computing platforms.
