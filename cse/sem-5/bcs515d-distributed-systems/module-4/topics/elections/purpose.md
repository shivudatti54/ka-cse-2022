# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the concept of election algorithms and their necessity in distributed systems
2. Describe the Bully Algorithm with its message passing sequence and analyze its time and message complexity
3. Describe the Ring Algorithm and explain how it differs from the Bully Algorithm
4. Compare the Bully and Ring algorithms in terms of message complexity, advantages, and disadvantages
5. Identify the assumptions and requirements for correct election algorithm design
6. Explain safety and liveness properties required in any election algorithm
7. Apply election algorithms to solve coordinator selection problems in distributed systems
8. Relate election algorithms to real-world distributed systems applications
