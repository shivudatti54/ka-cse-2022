# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define a distributed system and explain how it differs from centralized and parallel computing systems.

2. Identify and describe the eight types of transparency in distributed systems with relevant examples.

3. Compare and contrast different distributed system architectures including client-server, three-tier, and peer-to-peer models.

4. Analyze the trade-offs implied by the CAP theorem and explain why partition tolerance is mandatory in practical systems.

5. Classify different types of failures (crash, omission, timing, Byzantine) and explain the challenges each presents.

6. Explain why designing distributed systems is inherently more complex than centralized systems, considering issues of heterogeneity, openness, scalability, and security.

7. Connect the fundamental challenges of distributed systems to the advanced topics of consensus, mutual exclusion, and leader election.

8. Evaluate real-world distributed systems (such as DNS, distributed databases, or blockchain) against the characteristics and challenges discussed.
