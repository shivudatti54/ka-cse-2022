# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define what constitutes a distributed system and distinguish it from centralized and parallel computing systems.

2. Explain the eight forms of transparency in distributed systems and provide examples of each.

3. Identify and describe the primary goals and advantages of distributed systems, including resource sharing, openness, scalability, and fault tolerance.

4. Analyze the fundamental challenges in distributed systems, including heterogeneity, network issues, security concerns, and scalability limitations.

5. Compare and contrast different architectural models for distributed systems, including client-server, peer-to-peer, and service-oriented architectures.

6. Evaluate the tradeoffs involved in designing distributed systems, particularly with respect to the CAP theorem and consistency-availability-partition tolerance.

7. Apply your understanding to analyze real-world distributed systems and explain how they address the challenges and achieve their design goals.

8. Prepare for advanced topics in distributed systems including directory services, name services, and file service architectures by establishing the foundational concepts upon which these topics build.
