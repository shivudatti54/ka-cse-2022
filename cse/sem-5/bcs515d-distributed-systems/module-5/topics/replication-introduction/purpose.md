# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define database replication and explain its role in distributed database systems

2. Distinguish between synchronous and asynchronous replication, including their advantages, disadvantages, and suitable application scenarios

3. Compare primary-replica (master-slave) and primary-primary (multi-master) replication architectures

4. Analyze the different consistency models (strong, eventual, and causal) used in replicated database systems

5. Identify and explain various conflict resolution strategies in multi-master replication environments

6. Evaluate trade-offs between consistency, availability, and partition tolerance based on the CAP theorem

7. Design basic replication strategies for specific application requirements considering performance, consistency, and fault tolerance needs
