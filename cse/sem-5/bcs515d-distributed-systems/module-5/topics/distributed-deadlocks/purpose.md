# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define distributed deadlocks and explain the fundamental challenges in detecting them compared to centralized deadlocks

2. Explain the four necessary conditions for deadlock (Coffman conditions) and their relevance in distributed systems

3. Describe the Chandy-Misra-Haas algorithm for distributed deadlock detection, including the probe message format and detection mechanism

4. Compare and contrast different approaches to distributed deadlock detection including path-pushing and edge-chasing algorithms

5. Apply resource ordering as a deadlock prevention technique and explain how it eliminates circular wait

6. Differentiate between deadlock detection, prevention, and avoidance strategies with their respective advantages and limitations

7. Analyze simple distributed deadlock scenarios and identify deadlocks using wait-for graph concepts

8. Evaluate the message complexity and scalability considerations of distributed deadlock detection algorithms
