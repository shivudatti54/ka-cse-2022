# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the concept of distributed transactions and understand why they are essential in distributed database systems

2. Distinguish between flat distributed transactions and nested distributed transactions, including their structural differences and trade-offs

3. Describe the two-phase commit (2PC) protocol in detail, including the prepare and commit phases, and explain its limitations

4. Analyze the three-phase commit (3PC) protocol and explain how it addresses the blocking problem of 2PC

5. Compare closed nested transactions and open nested transactions, understanding their commitment semantics and fault tolerance characteristics

6. Apply concurrency control techniques (lock-based, timestamp-based, and optimistic) in distributed transaction scenarios

7. Identify and explain deadlock detection strategies in distributed systems, including centralized, hierarchical, and distributed approaches

8. Design appropriate transaction models for practical distributed system scenarios, considering factors like atomicity requirements, performance needs, and failure handling
