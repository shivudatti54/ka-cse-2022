# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the challenges of implementing concurrency control in distributed database systems compared to centralized systems.

2. Describe and compare different lock-based concurrency control techniques in distributed environments, including centralized, primary copy, and distributed lock management.

3. Analyze the Two-Phase Commit (2PC) protocol for ensuring atomicity in distributed transactions, including its phases and limitations.

4. Apply timestamp-based concurrency control protocols to ensure serializability in distributed transactions.

5. Understand and implement distributed deadlock detection techniques, particularly the Chandy-Misra-Haas algorithm.

6. Evaluate the trade-offs between different concurrency control approaches in terms of performance, fault tolerance, and consistency.

7. Explain how ACID properties are maintained in distributed transactions and identify scenarios where they may be relaxed.

8. Design appropriate concurrency control strategies for specific distributed database scenarios based on system requirements.
