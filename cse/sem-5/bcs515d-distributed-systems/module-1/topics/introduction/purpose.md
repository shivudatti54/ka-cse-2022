# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define a distributed system and explain how it differs from centralized and parallel computing systems

2. Identify and describe the five primary goals of distributed systems: resource sharing, openness, concurrency, scalability, and transparency

3. Classify distributed systems by type and explain the characteristics of each category (computing, information, and pervasive systems)

4. Analyze the role of middleware in distributed systems and explain how it addresses heterogeneity

5. Compare different architectural styles used in distributed systems, including client-server, three-tier, peer-to-peer, and microservices

6. Evaluate real-world distributed systems (such as DNS and the Web) in terms of their architectural choices and design principles

7. Explain the fundamental challenges inherent in building distributed systems, including heterogeneity, openness, security, scalability, and failure handling
