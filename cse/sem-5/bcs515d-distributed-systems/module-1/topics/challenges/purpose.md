# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Identify and explain the eight fundamental challenges in designing distributed systems: heterogeneity, openness, security, scalability, concurrency, transparency, reliability, and performance.

2. Analyze how heterogeneity impacts interoperability in distributed systems and describe the role of middleware in addressing this challenge.

3. Evaluate the trade-offs involved in the CAP theorem (Consistency, Availability, Partition tolerance) and apply this understanding to system design decisions.

4. Compare different types of system failures (crash, omission, timing, Byzantine) and explain detection and recovery mechanisms for each.

5. Classify the various forms of transparency (access, location, migration, replication, concurrency, failure, performance) and assess their importance in user experience.

6. Discuss the security challenges unique to distributed systems and propose appropriate countermeasures including cryptographic techniques and protocols.

7. Design strategies for achieving scalability in distributed systems through horizontal scaling, vertical scaling, partitioning, and replication techniques.

8. Relate the eight fallacies of distributed computing to real-world system failures and explain why these misconceptions lead to poor architectural decisions.
