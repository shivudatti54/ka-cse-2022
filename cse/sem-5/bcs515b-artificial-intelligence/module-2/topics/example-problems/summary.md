This topic established the foundational skill of problem formalization in AI through examination of canonical example problems. We demonstrated how real-world challenges transform into state space representations via the formal quintuple (S, s₀, A, G, c), with states encoded through various representation schemes. The 8-puzzle illustrated path-finding with variable branching factors (~3 average) and parity constraints that partition reachable states into solvable and unsolvable classes. The 8-Queens problem exemplified constraint satisfaction with 92 solutions among 4.4 billion permutations, requiring backtracking approaches. Water jug and missionary-cannibal problems demonstrated multi-attribute state representation with explicit constraint checking during search. Understanding these canonical problems equips practitioners to analyze novel problem characteristics and select appropriate search strategies based on complexity, memory constraints, and optimality requirements.
