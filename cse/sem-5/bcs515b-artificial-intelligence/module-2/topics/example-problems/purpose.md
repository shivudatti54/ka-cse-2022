After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. **Formalize real-world problems** into appropriate state space representations suitable for AI search algorithms, including defining the quintuple (S, s₀, A, G, c).

2. **Identify the components of a problem** including initial state, goal state, operators, and state transition function for various canonical problems.

3. **Differentiate between path-finding problems** (8-puzzle, water jug) **and constraint satisfaction problems** (8-Queens) based on problem structure and solution criteria.

4. **Analyze the complexity of search problems** by calculating branching factors, state space sizes, and understanding the implications of solvability constraints (e.g., parity in 8-puzzle).

5. **Apply appropriate search strategies** to solve canonical AI problems, understanding the trade-offs between completeness, optimality, time complexity, and space complexity.
