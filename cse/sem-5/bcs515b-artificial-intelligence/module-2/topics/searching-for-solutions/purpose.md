# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Formulate AI problems by identifying the initial state, goal state, successor function, and path cost.

2. Explain the difference between informed (heuristic) and uninformed (blind) search strategies and their appropriate use cases.

3. Implement and analyze Breadth-First Search (BFS), Depth-First Search (DFS), and Uniform Cost Search (UCS) algorithms.

4. Compare and contrast Greedy Best-First Search and A\* Search, including their optimality conditions.

5. Design appropriate heuristic functions for specific problems and evaluate their admissibility.

6. Analyze the time and space complexity of different search algorithms in terms of branching factor and solution depth.

7. Select the most appropriate search strategy for a given problem based on completeness, optimality, and computational constraints.

8. Apply search algorithms to solve classic AI problems such as the 8-puzzle, route finding, and game playing scenarios.
