# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the structure and components of a planning graph, including proposition levels and action levels.

2. Define mutex (mutual exclusion) links and identify conditions under which propositions or actions are mutually exclusive.

3. Describe the GraphPlan algorithm and its two-phase operation (expansion and extraction).

4. Construct a planning graph for a given planning problem, identifying relevant propositions, actions, and mutex relationships.

5. Extract a valid plan from a planning graph using backward search.

6. Analyze the advantages and limitations of the planning graph approach compared to other planning methods.

7. Apply planning graph concepts to solve simple classical planning problems.
