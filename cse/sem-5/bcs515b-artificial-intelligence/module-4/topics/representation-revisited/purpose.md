# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain what makes a knowledge representation good or bad, including properties like expressivity, tractability, and modularity.

2. Compare and contrast different representation schemes across the expressivity spectrum, from ground atoms to description logics.

3. Distinguish between procedural and declarative knowledge representation and explain why declarative representations are often preferred in AI systems.

4. Analyze the relationship between representation choices and reasoning complexity.

5. Apply the situation calculus to represent dynamic worlds and solve the frame problem using successor state axioms.

6. Evaluate representation choices for specific AI problems, justifying trade-offs between expressivity and computational efficiency.

7. Connect representation concepts to practical AI applications including expert systems, semantic web, and knowledge graphs.
