# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define unification and explain its role in logic programming and automated reasoning
2. Explain the concept of substitution and apply substitutions to terms
3. Identify when two terms can be unified and when unification will fail
4. Compute the Most General Unifier (MGU) for given terms using the unification algorithm
5. Apply the occurs check to prevent infinite term construction during unification
6. Understand the recursive nature of the unification algorithm for compound terms
7. Analyze unification failures and identify the reasons for failure
8. Appreciate the importance of unification in Prolog and theorem proving systems
