# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and distinguish between valid arguments, sound arguments, and fallacious reasoning in propositional logic.

2. Apply the eight fundamental rules of inference (Modus Ponens, Modus Tollens, Hypothetical Syllogism, Disjunctive Syllogism, Addition, Simplification, Conjunction, and Resolution) to construct logical derivations.

3. Construct formal proofs by applying rules of inference step-by-step to derive conclusions from given premises.

4. Distinguish between direct proof, proof by contraposition, and proof by contradiction, and select appropriate strategies for given problems.

5. Analyze complex arguments to determine their validity using truth tables or proof techniques.

6. Apply reasoning patterns to solve real-world problems involving logical deduction and inference.

7. Identify common logical fallacies and explain why they represent invalid reasoning patterns.

8. Evaluate the correctness of given proofs and identify errors in faulty logical arguments.
