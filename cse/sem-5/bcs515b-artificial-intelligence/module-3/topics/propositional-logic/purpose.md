# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and identify propositions, distinguishing between atomic and compound propositions with appropriate examples.

2. Apply all five logical connectives (negation, conjunction, disjunction, implication, biconditional) to form compound propositions and construct their truth tables.

3. Classify propositions as tautologies, contradictions, or contingencies by analyzing their truth tables.

4. Prove logical equivalence between propositions using both truth tables and algebraic manipulation of logical equivalences.

5. Convert propositions to Conjunctive Normal Form (CNF) and Disjunctive Normal Form (DNF) using appropriate transformations.

6. Determine the validity of logical arguments using truth table method and identify common logical fallacies like affirming the consequent and denying the antecedent.

7. Apply De Morgan's laws and other logical equivalences to simplify complex logical expressions.

8. Translate real-world statements into propositional logic formulas and solve word-based problems systematically.
