# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define rationality in the context of Artificial Intelligence and explain its significance in agent design.

2. Distinguish between perfect rationality, bounded rationality, and computational rationality with examples of each.

3. Explain the components of a rational agent and how they contribute to rational decision-making.

4. Analyze different types of environments (fully observable, partially observable, deterministic, stochastic, static, dynamic) and their impact on rational agent design.

5. Apply the PEAS framework (Performance measure, Environment, Actuators, Sensors) to specify requirements for any intelligent agent.

6. Design simple rational agents for well-defined environments using the concepts of percept sequences and performance measures.

7. Evaluate the relationship between performance measure design and actual agent behavior, recognizing potential pitfalls.

8. Compare and contrast rationality with intelligence, understanding their distinct roles in AI systems.
