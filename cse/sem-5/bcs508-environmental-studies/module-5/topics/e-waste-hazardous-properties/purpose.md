# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Identify and classify different categories of e-waste generated from electronic devices and IT equipment.

2. Explain the hazardous properties of major toxic substances present in e-waste, including lead, mercury, cadmium, and brominated flame retardants.

3. Analyze the environmental and health impacts of improper e-waste disposal, including soil contamination, water pollution, and human health risks.

4. Describe the various e-waste management methods following the hierarchy of prevention, reuse, recycling, and safe disposal.

5. Apply the E-Waste (Management) Rules, 2022 to analyze the regulatory framework governing e-waste in India.

6. Evaluate the role of Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) in promoting sustainable e-waste management practices.

7. Critically assess the importance of green computing and sustainable design in reducing e-waste generation from an engineering perspective.

8. Propose appropriate e-waste handling procedures for IT equipment commonly used in educational and industrial settings.
