# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define e-waste and explain its scope under the E-Waste Management and Handling Rules 2011

2. Identify the major sources of e-waste generation and explain why IT industry is a significant contributor

3. Describe the hazardous substances present in e-waste and their environmental and health impacts

4. Explain the roles and responsibilities of producers, consumers, dealers, and recyclers under the Rules 2011

5. Understand the Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) principle and its application in e-waste management

6. Identify prohibited practices under the rules and explain proper disposal procedures

7. Analyze the significance of e-waste management for computer science engineers in developing sustainable technology solutions

8. Compare the E-Waste Rules 2011 with subsequent amendments to understand the evolution of e-waste regulation in India
