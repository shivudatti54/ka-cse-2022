# E-Waste Management and Handling Rules 2011 - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **E-waste**: Electrical and electronic equipment that has been discarded by their original users or reached end-of-life, including computers, mobile phones, televisions, printers, and IT equipment
- **Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR)**: Policy principle making producers responsible for collecting and recycling their products at end-of-life
- **Authorized Recycler**: Entity registered with State Pollution Control Board to process e-waste in an environmentally sound manner
- **Collection Center**: Facility established by producers to collect e-waste from consumers

## Important Provisions of the Rules 2011

- Enacted under Environment Protection Act, 1986, effective from May 1, 2012
- Established collection target of 30% of weight of EEE sold
- Mandated Extended Producer Responsibility for producers
- Required producers to set up take-back systems or collection centers
- Prohibited open burning of e-waste and disposal in municipal waste
- Required authorization for recyclers from State Pollution Control Board

## Key Points

1. E-waste is growing at 30% annually in India, making proper management critical

2. Hazardous substances in e-waste include lead, mercury, cadmium, hexavalent chromium, and brominated flame retardants

3. Four main stakeholders: Producers, Consumers, Dealers, and Authorized Recyclers

4. Producers must maintain records of e-waste collected and channelized

5. Consumers must dispose e-waste only through authorized collection centers

6. The rules introduced a manifest system for tracking e-waste movement

7. Subsequent E-Waste Management Rules 2016 replaced the 2011 rules with stricter provisions

8. CSE engineers play crucial role in developing sustainable and recyclable technology solutions

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Confusing E-Waste Rules 2011 with 2016 - remember 2011 was the original rules
- Thinking e-waste can be disposed with regular municipal waste - it is strictly prohibited
- Assuming all recyclers are authorized - only SPCB-authorized recyclers should be used
- Ignoring the hazardous nature of e-waste components - improper handling causes serious environmental damage

## Revision Tips

1. Memorize the effective date (May 1, 2012) and parent Act (Environment Protection Act, 1986)

2. Remember the four key stakeholders and their responsibilities in order

3. List the five hazardous substances found in e-waste for quick recall during exams

4. Understand the EPR principle thoroughly as it is frequently asked in examinations

5. Focus on the connection between e-waste management and sustainable computing practices
