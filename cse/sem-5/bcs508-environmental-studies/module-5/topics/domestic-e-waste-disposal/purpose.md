# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Identify and categorize domestic e-waste sources including computing devices, communication equipment, entertainment electronics, and small electronic gadgets.

2. Explain the environmental and health hazards associated with improper e-waste disposal, focusing on toxic substances like lead, mercury, cadmium, and chromium.

3. Describe the formal e-waste recycling process in India, including collection, dismantling, material separation, and safe disposal methods.

4. Analyze the Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) framework under India's E-Waste (Management) Rules, 2022, and its implications for consumers and manufacturers.

5. Apply the e-waste management hierarchy to make informed decisions about disposing of household electronic devices.

6. Evaluate the importance of secure data destruction before e-waste disposal and recommend appropriate methods for data sanitization.

7. Compare formal and informal e-waste recycling practices, highlighting the environmental and health impacts of each approach.

8. Develop practical strategies for household-level e-waste management, including segregation, storage, and proper disposal through authorized channels.
