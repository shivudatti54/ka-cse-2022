# Domestic E-Waste Disposal - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **E-Waste**: Discarded electrical or electronic devices that have reached end-of-life, including computers, mobile phones, televisions, and appliances
- **Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR)**: Policy framework holding manufacturers accountable for collecting and recycling their products after use
- **Formal Recycling**: Authorized e-waste processing using environment-friendly techniques meeting regulatory standards
- **Informal Recycling**: Unauthorized processing using primitive methods causing severe pollution

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **E-Waste Management Hierarchy**: Prevention → Reuse → Recycling → Energy Recovery → Disposal
- **E-Waste Growth Rate**: Approximately 30% annual increase in India
- **CRT (Cathode Ray Tube)**: Contains approximately 2-4 kg of lead, requires special handling

## Key Points

- E-waste contains over 1000 substances, many being toxic and carcinogenic
- Lead affects neurological system, mercury damages kidneys, cadmium causes bone accumulation
- India generates over 1.5 million tonnes of e-waste annually, growing rapidly
- E-Waste (Management) Rules, 2022 mandate producer registration and collection targets
- Consumers must not mix e-waste with municipal solid waste
- Data security is critical - hard drives must be wiped or physically destroyed
- Formal recycling recovers precious metals while informal processing causes pollution
- Take-back programs and collection centers are primary disposal channels for households

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Disposing e-waste in regular household trash bins - this is illegal and harmful
- Ignoring data security - personal data can be recovered from improperly disposed devices
- Believing that throwing away small devices is harmless - even small items contain toxic materials
- Assuming all recyclers are legitimate - only authorized E-Waste Recycler registration is valid

## Revision Tips

1. Focus on hazardous substances and their health effects - these are frequently tested
2. Remember the 5-tier e-waste hierarchy in order
3. Know key provisions of India's E-Waste Rules, 2022
4. Understand EPR concept and consumer responsibilities
5. Practice explaining the complete e-waste disposal process from household to recycling facility
