# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define e-waste and identify its major categories as per Indian regulations.

2. Explain the various sources of e-waste generation and factors contributing to its growth.

3. Describe the complete e-waste management cycle including collection, storage, transportation, and processing.

4. Differentiate between reuse, recycling, and disposal methods for e-waste.

5. Analyze the legal framework governing e-waste management in India, particularly the E-Waste (Management) Rules, 2022.

6. Understand the concept of Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) and its implementation.

7. Identify hazardous substances present in e-waste and their environmental and health impacts.

8. Evaluate the role of various stakeholders including producers, recyclers, consumers, and regulatory bodies in effective e-waste management.
