# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define e-waste and explain its significance in the context of environmental sustainability and public health.

2. Identify the major sources and categories of e-waste, particularly in the Information and Communication Technology sector.

3. Describe the composition of e-waste, distinguishing between valuable materials and hazardous substances.

4. Analyze the environmental and health impacts of improper e-waste disposal, including soil, water, and air pollution.

5. Explain the hierarchical approach to e-waste management and the principles of reduce, reuse, and recycle.

6. Understand the concept of Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) and its implementation in e-waste management.

7. Apply knowledge of e-waste management rules and regulations, specifically the E-Waste (Management) Rules, 2022 of India.

8. Evaluate the differences between formal and informal e-waste recycling sectors and their respective impacts.
