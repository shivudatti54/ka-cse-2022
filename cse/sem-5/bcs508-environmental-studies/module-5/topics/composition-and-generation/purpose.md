# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Identify and describe the major components of e-waste, including hazardous materials, precious metals, and recyclable materials.

2. Explain the composition of different categories of electronic equipment and quantify the proportions of various materials present in e-waste.

3. Analyze the factors that influence e-waste generation patterns, including technological obsolescence, consumer behavior, and economic factors.

4. Evaluate the environmental and health impacts of hazardous components present in e-waste.

5. Calculate e-waste generation volumes based on population, device penetration, and replacement cycles.

6. Compare the composition differences between legacy equipment (like CRT monitors) and modern electronic devices.

7. Discuss the significance of e-waste as a secondary source of precious and rare earth metals.
