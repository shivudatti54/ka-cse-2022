# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define e-waste and identify major sources of electronic waste in the context of computer science and engineering applications.

2. Classify and explain the different types of pollutants found in e-waste, including heavy metals, organic pollutants, and other hazardous substances.

3. Describe the health impacts of major e-waste pollutants such as lead, mercury, cadmium, and hexavalent chromium on human beings.

4. Explain the environmental pathways of e-waste pollution including soil contamination, groundwater pollution, and atmospheric release.

5. Discuss the various e-waste management strategies including source reduction, reuse, recycling, and regulatory frameworks.

6. Analyze the provisions of the E-Waste (Management) Rules, 2022, and understand Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) mechanisms.

7. Apply knowledge of e-waste pollutants to suggest sustainable practices for handling and disposing of electronic equipment in engineering contexts.

8. Evaluate the importance of green computing and sustainable IT practices in reducing e-waste generation and environmental impact.
