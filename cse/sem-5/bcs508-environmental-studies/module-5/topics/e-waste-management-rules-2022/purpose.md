# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define e-waste and explain its environmental and health impacts in the Indian context.

2. Describe the scope and applicability of the E-Waste Management Rules, 2022.

3. Explain the concept of Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) and its significance in e-waste management.

4. Identify and describe the roles and responsibilities of producers, consumers, refurbishers, and recyclers under the rules.

5. Calculate collection targets for producers based on the quantity of electrical and electronic equipment placed in the market.

6. Outline the various e-waste collection mechanisms established under the rules.

7. Understand the regulatory framework including the role of CPCB and State Pollution Control Boards.

8. Analyze the provisions related to import restrictions and hazardous substance reduction.

9. Apply the knowledge to identify violations and suggest compliance measures for various stakeholders.

10. Compare the key features of E-Waste Management Rules, 2022 with the earlier 2016 rules.
