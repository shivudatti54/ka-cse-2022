# E-Waste Management Rules 2022 - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **E-Waste:** Electrical and electronic equipment including all components, consumables, parts, and spares that become obsolete during use.
- **Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR):** A policy approach making producers accountable for end-of-life management of their products.
- **Producer Responsibility Plan (PRP):** A document outlining a producer's e-waste collection and recycling strategy.
- **E-waste Mark:** Mandatory labeling indicating proper disposal instructions for consumers.

## Important Formulas and Targets

- **Collection Target Year 1:** 60% of EEE placed in market
- **Collection Target Year 2:** 70% of EEE placed in market
- **Collection Target Year 3 onwards:** 80% of EEE placed in market

## Key Points

- The rules were notified on 2nd November 2022 and came into effect from 1st April 2023.
- Rules apply to 21 categories of electrical and electronic equipment.
- Producers must register on the CPCB portal and achieve specified collection targets.
- Consumers are prohibited from disposing of e-waste in municipal bins.
- Import of e-waste for disposal is strictly prohibited.
- Recyclers and refurbishers must obtain authorization from State Pollution Control Boards.
- Annual returns must be filed by April 30th each year.
- Rules align with RoHS restrictions on hazardous substances (lead, mercury, cadmium, hexavalent chromium).
- CPCB is the nodal agency for implementation at national level.
- Non-compliance can lead to penalties and imprisonment under Environmental Protection Act.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Confusing collection targets with recycling targets - they are different metrics.
2. Thinking e-waste rules apply only to manufacturers - they cover the entire supply chain.
3. Believing consumers have no obligation - they must use authorized disposal channels.
4. Assuming the rules are voluntary - they are mandatory with legal enforceability.
5. Ignoring the progressive nature of targets - they increase year over year.

## Revision Tips

1. Focus on understanding EPR as it's the core concept and frequently tested.
2. Memorize the collection targets (60%, 70%, 80%) as this is a common exam question.
3. Make a table listing responsibilities of each stakeholder (producers, consumers, recyclers).
4. Practice numerical problems on collection target calculations.
5. Remember key dates and regulatory bodies (CPCB, SPCB).
