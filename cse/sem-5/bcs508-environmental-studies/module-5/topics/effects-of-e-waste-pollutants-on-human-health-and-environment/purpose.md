# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Identify and classify the major pollutants present in e-waste, including heavy metals, persistent organic pollutants, and other toxic substances found in electronic devices.

2. Explain the different pathways through which e-waste pollutants enter the human body and cause adverse health effects, including neurological, carcinogenic, and reproductive impacts.

3. Analyze the environmental contamination mechanisms by which e-waste pollutants affect soil, water, and air quality, leading to ecosystem degradation.

4. Compare formal and informal e-waste recycling methods with respect to environmental release of pollutants and occupational health risks.

5. Evaluate the bioaccumulation and biomagnification processes through which e-waste toxins enter the food chain and affect human health at various exposure levels.

6. Apply knowledge of e-waste pollutant effects to suggest appropriate handling, storage, and disposal practices that minimize environmental and health impacts.

7. Interpret the regulatory framework governing e-waste management in India, including the E-Waste (Management) Rules, 2022 and Extended Producer Responsibility provisions.

8. Design sustainable e-waste management strategies using the 3R (Reduce, Reuse, Recycle) approach appropriate for institutional and industrial settings.
