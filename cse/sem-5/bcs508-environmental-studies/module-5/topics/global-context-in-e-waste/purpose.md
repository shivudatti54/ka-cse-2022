# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define e-waste and explain its global scale and growth trends, including current statistics and projections for the coming decades.

2. Identify and describe the key international frameworks and conventions governing e-waste management, particularly the Basel Convention and its amendments.

3. Explain the concept of Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) and its application in e-waste management, with reference to the European Union's WEEE Directive and India's E-Waste Rules.

4. Analyze the transboundary movement of e-waste and the challenges associated with preventing illegal shipments from developed to developing countries.

5. Evaluate the environmental and health impacts of informal e-waste recycling operations, particularly in developing countries including India.

6. Compare and contrast e-waste management approaches in different regions, including developed countries, developing countries, and emerging economies.

7. Discuss the resource recovery potential from e-waste and the concept of a circular economy in the context of electronic waste management.

8. Propose strategies for improving e-waste management in India by learning from global best practices while addressing local challenges.
