# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and distinguish between water-borne diseases and water-induced diseases with appropriate examples

2. Classify water-related diseases into four categories: water-borne, water-washed, water-based, and water-vector diseases

3. Explain the fecal-oral transmission route and the vector-borne transmission mechanism for water-related diseases

4. Identify the causative organisms, symptoms, and transmission pathways for major water-borne diseases (cholera, typhoid, dysentery, hepatitis A)

5. Describe the vector breeding habits and control measures for water-induced diseases (malaria, dengue, filariasis)

6. Interpret water quality parameters and their significance in preventing water-borne diseases

7. Apply knowledge of disease prevention methods to propose community-level interventions

8. Analyze the role of sanitation, water treatment, and vector control in reducing water-related disease burden
