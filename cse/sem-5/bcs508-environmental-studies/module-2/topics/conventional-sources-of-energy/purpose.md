# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Identify and classify conventional sources of energy, including fossil fuels (coal, petroleum, natural gas) and nuclear energy.

2. Explain the formation process, composition, and properties of coal, petroleum, and natural gas.

3. Describe the working principles of thermal power plants, including the Rankine cycle and major components.

4. Analyze the petroleum refining process and understand fractional distillation along with key petroleum products.

5. Calculate the power generation potential of hydroelectric plants using the fundamental power equation.

6. Evaluate the advantages, limitations, and environmental impacts of each conventional energy source.

7. Apply formulas for calorific value, thermal efficiency, and power calculations to solve engineering problems.

8. Compare conventional energy sources in terms of efficiency, cost, availability, and environmental sustainability for India's energy needs.
