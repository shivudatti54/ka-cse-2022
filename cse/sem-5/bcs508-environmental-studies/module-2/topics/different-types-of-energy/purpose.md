# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:
1. Classify energy sources based on renewability, form, and development status.
2. Distinguish between renewable and non-renewable energy sources with their environmental implications.
3. Explain various forms of energy (kinetic, potential, thermal, chemical, electrical, nuclear) and their inter conversions.
4. Analyze energy conversion efficiencies in different power generation systems.
5. Evaluate the advantages and limitations of conventional vs. non-conventional energy sources.
6. Apply formulas for calculating potential energy, kinetic energy, and power generation.
7. Assess the role of different energy types in sustainable development and environmental conservation.
8. Connect energy type selection to environmental impact and climate change mitigation strategies.
