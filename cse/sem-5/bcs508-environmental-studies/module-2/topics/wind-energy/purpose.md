# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental principles of wind energy conversion and the physics behind capturing kinetic energy from wind.

2. Derive and apply the wind power equation P = (1/2)ρAV³ to calculate theoretical power available in wind streams.

3. Classify wind turbines into horizontal axis (HAWT) and vertical axis (VAWT) types, and compare their characteristics, advantages, and applications.

4. Identify and describe the major components of a wind energy conversion system, including rotor blades, nacelle, gearbox, generator, and tower.

5. Understand the concept of Betz Limit (59.3%) and explain why actual wind turbine efficiencies are lower than this theoretical maximum.

6. Analyze wind resource assessment methods and interpret Weibull distribution parameters for wind energy project evaluation.

7. Evaluate the current status of wind energy in India, including major wind farms, installed capacity, and relevant government policies and incentives.

8. Assess the environmental benefits and limitations of wind energy as a renewable power source and its role in sustainable development.
