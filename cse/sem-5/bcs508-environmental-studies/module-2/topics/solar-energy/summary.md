# Solar Energy - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Solar Radiation**: Electromagnetic energy from the sun, with the solar constant of approximately 1367 W/m² at the outer atmosphere
- **Photovoltaic Effect**: Generation of electric current when photons with energy greater than bandgap strike a semiconductor material
- **Solar Collector**: Device that captures solar radiation and converts it to thermal energy
- **Concentration Ratio**: Ratio of aperture area to absorber area in concentrating collectors
- **Air Mass (AM)**: Measure of path length of sunlight through the atmosphere; AM1.5 is standard test condition
- **Fill Factor (FF)**: Ratio of maximum power output to the product of Isc and Voc in PV cells
- **Depth of Discharge (DOD)**: Percentage of battery capacity that has been discharged

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Collector Efficiency**: η = Qu / (G × A), where Qu is useful heat gain, G is solar radiation, A is collector area
- **Heat Transfer in Collectors**: Qu = FR × [G × τ × α - Ul × (Ti - Ta)]
- **PV Cell Efficiency**: η = (Pmax / G × A) × 100%
- **Battery Capacity**: Capacity (Ah) = (Energy requirement × Days of autonomy) / (System voltage × DOD)
- **PV Array Sizing**: PV size (W) = Daily energy requirement / (Peak sun hours × System efficiency)

## Key Points

- Solar energy is the most abundant renewable energy source, with the sun providing approximately 1.7 × 10^14 kW continuously
- Solar thermal systems convert radiation to heat (flat-plate: <80°C, concentrating: >400°C)
- Photovoltaic systems directly convert sunlight to electricity using semiconductor cells
- Grid-connected PV systems feed excess power to the utility grid, while off-grid systems require battery storage
- The fill factor indicates the quality of a solar cell; higher values indicate better performance
- Solar passive design uses building orientation, thermal mass, and shading for natural heating and cooling
- India has set a target of 100 GW solar capacity, with solar energy playing a crucial role in renewable energy expansion

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Confusing solar constant (1367 W/m²) with actual radiation reaching Earth's surface (100-300 W/m²)
2. Forgetting that concentrating collectors require direct sunlight while flat-plate collectors can use diffuse radiation
3. Not accounting for system losses when sizing PV systems; always include inverter and wiring efficiency
4. Overlooking temperature effects on PV cell efficiency—efficiency decreases with increasing temperature
5. Improper battery maintenance, especially for lead-acid batteries, significantly reduces battery life

## Revision Tips

1. Practice numerical problems on collector efficiency calculation, PV system sizing, and battery capacity determination
2. Memorize key terms: solar constant, fill factor, concentration ratio, air mass, depth of discharge
3. Understand the difference between active and passive solar systems
4. Review the components and operation of both solar thermal and photovoltaic systems
5. Focus on the advantages and applications of different types of solar collectors and PV systems
