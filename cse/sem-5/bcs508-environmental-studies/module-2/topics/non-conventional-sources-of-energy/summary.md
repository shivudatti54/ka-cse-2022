# Non-Conventional Sources of Energy - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Non-Conventional Energy Sources:** Renewable energy sources that are inexhaustible and environmentally friendly, including solar, wind, biomass, geothermal, ocean, and hydrogen energy.

- **Solar Photovoltaic (PV):** Technology that converts sunlight directly into electricity using semiconductor materials.

- **Wind Turbine:** Device that converts kinetic energy of wind into mechanical power, then electricity.

- **Biomass:** Organic materials from plants and animals that can be converted to energy through various processes.

- **Biogas:** Mixture of methane and carbon dioxide produced through anaerobic digestion of organic waste.

- **Fuel Cell:** Electrochemical device that converts chemical energy directly into electrical energy.

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Wind Power:** P = ½ × ρ × A × V³ × Cp
  - ρ = air density (1.225 kg/m³)
  - A = swept area of rotor (πr²)
  - V = wind velocity (m/s)
  - Cp = power coefficient (theoretical max 0.59)

- **Solar Panel Output:** Power Output = Panel Wattage × Peak Sun Hours × System Efficiency Factor

## Key Points

1. Non-conventional energy sources are renewable, abundant, and have minimal environmental impact compared to fossil fuels.

2. Solar energy can be utilized through PV systems (electricity) and solar thermal systems (heat).

3. Wind power depends on wind velocity cubed - small increases in wind speed dramatically increase power output.

4. Biomass can be converted through combustion, gasification, pyrolysis, anaerobic digestion, and fermentation.

5. Geothermal energy utilizes heat from Earth's interior for direct heating and electricity generation.

6. Ocean energy includes tidal, wave, OTEC, and marine current energy - vast untapped potential.

7. Hydrogen is a clean energy carrier produced through electrolysis, steam reforming, or biomass gasification.

8. Fuel cells offer high efficiency (40-60%) with only water as emission.

9. Key challenges include high initial costs, intermittency, energy storage, and geographic limitations.

10. For CSE students, renewable energy is crucial for green computing and sustainable data centers.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Confusing solar thermal with solar photovoltaic systems - they serve different purposes
- Forgetting to account for system losses (typically 20%) in solar calculations
- Using wind velocity directly without cubing in power calculations
- Not considering that wind power coefficient (Cp) is always less than Betz limit (0.59)
- Mixing up biomass conversion methods and their products

## Revision Tips

1. Focus on the classification of non-conventional sources and their working principles.

2. Practice numerical problems related to wind power and solar PV calculations.

3. Remember the advantages and limitations of each energy source for exam answers.

4. Understand the connection between renewable energy and sustainable computing.

5. Review the formula derivations and ensure all units are consistent in calculations.
