# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the natural and anthropogenic sources of fluoride contamination in groundwater, including geological formations and industrial activities.

2. Differentiate between dental fluorosis and skeletal fluorosis, describing their causes, symptoms, and affected populations.

3. Interpret the permissible limits for fluoride in drinking water according to BIS (India) and WHO standards.

4. Evaluate various defluoridation techniques including activated alumina adsorption, Nalgonda technique, reverse osmosis, and bone char methods based on their efficiency, cost, and applicability.

5. Calculate fluoride removal efficiency, determine mixing ratios for water sources, and estimate chemical/adsorbent requirements for treatment.

6. Analyze the fluoride problem scenario in India, identifying the most affected states and regions.

7. Apply appropriate detection and monitoring methods for fluoride concentration in water samples.
