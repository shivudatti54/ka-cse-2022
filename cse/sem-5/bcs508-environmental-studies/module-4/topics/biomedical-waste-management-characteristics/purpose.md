# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define biomedical waste and explain its scope according to the Biomedical Waste Management Rules, 2016 (India).

2. Classify biomedical waste into the 9 categories specified under Indian regulations and explain the characteristics of each category.

3. Describe the physical, chemical, and biological characteristics of biomedical waste that influence treatment and disposal decisions.

4. Identify hazardous properties of biomedical waste including infectious, toxic, corrosive, flammable, and reactive characteristics.

5. Explain appropriate treatment and disposal methods for different categories of biomedical waste.

6. Analyze the importance of segregation, collection, storage, and transportation in the overall biomedical waste management chain.

7. Evaluate the environmental and health impacts of improper biomedical waste management.

8. Apply knowledge of waste characteristics to solve practical problems in healthcare waste management scenarios.
