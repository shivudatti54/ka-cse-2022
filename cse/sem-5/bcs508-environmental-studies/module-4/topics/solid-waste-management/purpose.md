# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Classify solid wastes based on their origin, nature, and characteristics, distinguishing between municipal, industrial, hazardous, and biomedical wastes.

2. Analyze the composition and characteristics of municipal solid waste, including moisture content, bulk density, and calorific value relevant to Indian conditions.

3. Explain various waste collection systems and evaluate their suitability for different urban and rural scenarios.

4. Describe waste processing methods including composting, vermicomposting, incineration, and material recovery, with their advantages and limitations.

5. Design appropriate disposal strategies, particularly engineered landfills, considering environmental protection measures.

6. Apply the 3R (Reduce, Reuse, Recycle) principle to develop sustainable waste management solutions.

7. Interpret the regulatory framework governing solid waste management in India, including relevant rules and legislation.

8. Solve numerical problems related to composting requirements, landfill capacity calculations, and waste diversion rates.
