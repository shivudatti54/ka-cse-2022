# Environmental Legislation - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Environmental Legislation**: Collection of laws, regulations, and policies designed to protect environment and natural resources
- **E-Waste**: Discarded electrical or electronic devices and their components
- **Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR)**: Policy approach where producers take responsibility for end-of-life management of their products
- **Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA)**: Process of evaluating environmental effects of a project before decision-making

## Important Laws and Their Key Features

- **Environment (Protection) Act, 1986**: Framework law for environmental protection; empowers Central Government to set standards
- **Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974**: Established CPCB and SPCBs; prohibits discharge of pollutants into water bodies
- **Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981**: Regulates industrial and vehicular air emissions
- **E-Waste Management Rules, 2022**: Introduced EPR with collection targets starting at 10% increasing to 70%

## Key Points

- Article 48A (Directive Principles) and Article 51A (Fundamental Duties) form constitutional basis for environmental protection in India
- Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) coordinates pollution control; State Pollution Control Boards (SPCB) implement at state level
- National Green Tribunal (NGT) provides specialized justice for environmental cases
- India committed to 33-35% emission intensity reduction under Paris Agreement
- E-waste collection targets under 2022 rules increase progressively from 10% to 70% over seven years
- Industries must obtain consent from pollution control boards before operation

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Confusing roles of CPCB and SPCB - CPCB coordinates nationally, SPCBs implement at state level
- Believing E-Waste Rules apply only to manufacturers - they also cover consumers and recyclers
- Assuming environmental laws are optional - non-compliance leads to imprisonment and heavy fines

## Revision Tips

1. Focus on the three main Acts: Water (1974), Air (1981), and Environment Protection (1986) - these form the backbone
2. Remember key provisions of E-Waste 2022 Rules, especially EPR and collection targets
3. Understand the institutional framework - know which body does what
4. Practice case studies on compliance scenarios to reinforce learning
