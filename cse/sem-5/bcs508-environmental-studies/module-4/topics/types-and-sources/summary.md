Biomedical waste constitutes 10-25% of total healthcare waste and poses significant environmental and health hazards. The Biomedical Waste Management Rules, 2016, classify biomedical waste into ten categories: human anatomical waste, animal waste, microbiology and biotechnology waste, sharps, discarded medicines and cytotoxic drugs, chemical waste, radioactive waste, pressurized containers, solid waste, and liquid waste. Primary sources include hospitals (200-250 kg/day from 500-bed facility), diagnostic laboratories, medical colleges, and research institutions. Secondary sources encompass veterinary clinics, blood banks, dental clinics, and alternate medicine facilities. Generation rates range from 1.0-2.5 kg per bed per day in Indian healthcare settings, with approximately 20-25% being infectious waste. Understanding this classification and source identification forms the basis for implementing appropriate segregation, treatment, and disposal protocols.
