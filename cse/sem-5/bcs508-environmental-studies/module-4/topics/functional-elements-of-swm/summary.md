# Functional Elements of Solid Waste Management - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Solid Waste Management (SWM)**: Systematic control of waste generation, storage, collection, transport, processing, and disposal
- **Functional Elements**: The sequential stages in the waste management chain from generation to final disposal
- **Municipal Solid Waste (MSW)**: Waste from residential, commercial, and institutional sources
- **Source Separation**: Segregation of waste types at the point of generation
- **Transfer Station**: Facility where waste is consolidated from collection vehicles into larger transport vehicles
- **Material Recovery Facility (MRF)**: Plant for recovering recyclable materials from mixed waste
- **Sanitary Landfill**: Engineered facility for waste disposal with environmental controls

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Daily Waste Generation**: Total waste (kg/day) = Population × Per capita generation rate
- **Collection Vehicle Trips**: Trips = Total waste / Vehicle capacity
- **Composting Cycle Time**: Total volume = Daily input × Composting period
- **Compaction Ratio**: Ratio between loose and compacted waste density (typically 2:1 to 3:1)

## Key Points

1. The six functional elements are: Generation → Handling and Separation → Collection → Transfer and Transport → Processing and Recovery → Disposal

2. Collection is the most expensive element, consuming 50-70% of total SWM budget

3. Source separation significantly improves recycling efficiency and reduces processing costs

4. Transfer stations become essential when disposal sites are located far from collection areas

5. Composting, recycling, and energy recovery are the main processing and recovery methods

6. Engineered landfills include composite liners, leachate collection, and gas management systems

7. In India, per capita waste generation ranges from 200-600 grams per day

8. GIS and GPS technologies are used for optimal collection route planning

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Confusing "transfer" with "transport" - transfer involves consolidation at stations
- Assuming all landfills are the same - modern engineered landfills differ significantly from old dump sites
- Overlooking the importance of source separation in waste management systems
- Underestimating collection costs when planning waste management systems

## Revision Tips

1. Memorize the six functional elements in sequence for quick recall during exams

2. Focus on understanding the "why" behind each element rather than just definitions

3. Practice numerical problems related to collection system design and facility sizing

4. Know the key differences between sanitary and engineered landfills

5. Relate each functional element to its role in sustainable waste management
