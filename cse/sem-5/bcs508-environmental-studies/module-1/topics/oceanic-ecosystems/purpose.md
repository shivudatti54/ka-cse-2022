# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Classify oceanic ecosystems into distinct zones based on depth and environmental characteristics (epipelagic through hadopelagic)

2. Explain the structure and functioning of marine food webs, including the 10% energy transfer rule between trophic levels

3. Describe the ecological significance of coral reef ecosystems and their role in marine biodiversity

4. Analyze how oceanic ecosystems contribute to climate regulation through carbon sequestration and heat absorption

5. Identify and explain major threats to oceanic ecosystems including pollution, overfishing, ocean acidification, and coral bleaching

6. Apply knowledge of ocean zones to solve classification problems in environmental science contexts

7. Evaluate the importance of oceanic ecosystems in maintaining Earth's ecological balance
