# Oceanic Ecosystems - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Oceanic Ecosystems**: Marine environments covering 71% of Earth's surface, divided into five depth zones based on light penetration and environmental conditions
- **Phytoplankton**: Microscopic primary producers forming the base of marine food webs, responsible for ~50% of Earth's oxygen production
- **Trophic Levels**: Hierarchical levels in food chains (producers → primary consumers → secondary consumers → apex predators)
- **Coral Reefs**: Highly biodiverse ecosystems built by coral polyps with symbiotic zooxanthellae, supporting 25% of marine species
- **Thermohaline Circulation**: Global ocean circulation driven by temperature and salinity differences affecting climate

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Energy Transfer**: Only ~10% of energy transfers between trophic levels (important for population dynamics)
- **CO2 to Carbon**: Carbon in CO2 = CO2 × (12/44) = CO2 × 0.273

## Key Points

1. Ocean zones: Epipelagic (0-200m), Mesopelagic (200-1000m), Bathypelagic (1000-4000m), Abyssopelagic (4000-6000m), Hadopelagic (6000m+)

2. Ocean absorbs 25-30% of anthropogenic CO2 and over 90% of excess heat

3. Coral bleaching occurs when corals expel zooxanthellae due to temperature stress

4. Ocean acidification lowers pH, threatening shell-forming organisms

5. Marine ecosystems provide essential services: oxygen production, food, climate regulation, coastal protection

6. Phytoplankton are the primary producers driving ocean productivity

7. Decomposers recycle nutrients in deep ocean environments

## Common Mistakes to Confuse

- **Coral are animals, not plants** — they are cnidarians with algal symbionts
- **Mesopelagic vs Bathypelagic** — 200-1000m is twilight (meso), 1000-4000m is midnight (bathypelagic)
- **Food chain vs Food web** — chains are linear, webs show multiple interconnected relationships

## Revision Tips

1. Draw and label the five ocean zones with organisms found in each

2. Practice energy calculation problems using the 10% rule

3. Create a table comparing threats: ocean acidification, pollution, overfishing, coral bleaching

4. Remember key statistics: 71% surface coverage, 50% oxygen production, 25% biodiversity
