# Structure of Ecosystem - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Ecosystem:** A functional unit of the biosphere where living organisms interact with each other and their physical environment
- **Biotic Components:** Living elements including producers, consumers, and decomposers
- **Abiotic Components:** Non-living factors like sunlight, temperature, water, soil, and nutrients
- **Trophic Level:** Each stage in a food chain representing organisms at similar feeding levels
- **Ecological Pyramid:** A graphical representation showing the relationship between trophic levels

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **10% Law of Energy Transfer:** Only 10% of energy is transferred from one trophic level to the next; 90% is lost as heat
- **Energy Flow Formula:** Energy at level n = Energy at level (n-1) × 0.10
- **Gross Primary Productivity (GPP):** Total energy captured by producers
- **Net Primary Productivity (NPP):** Energy available to primary consumers (GPP - Respiration)

## Key Points

- Ecosystem has two main components: biotic (living) and abiotic (non-living)
- Producers (autotrophs) form the base of all food chains through photosynthesis
- Consumers are classified as primary, secondary, tertiary, and omnivores based on feeding habits
- Decomposers recycle nutrients back into the ecosystem
- Energy flow is unidirectional and non-cyclic (sun → producers → consumers → decomposers)
- Food webs are more realistic than food chains as they show multiple feeding relationships
- Ecological pyramids of energy are always upright; pyramids of numbers and biomass can be inverted
- Biogeochemical cycles ensure continuous nutrient circulation (carbon, nitrogen, water, phosphorus)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Confusing food chains with food webs—they are not interchangeable concepts
- Assuming all ecological pyramids are pyramid-shaped (numbers and biomass can be inverted)
- Forgetting that energy decreases at each trophic level (not the same as nutrients which cycle)
- Overlooking decomposers—they are essential for nutrient recycling
