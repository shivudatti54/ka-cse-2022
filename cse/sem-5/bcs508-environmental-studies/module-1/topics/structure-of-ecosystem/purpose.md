# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define ecosystem and explain its structural components (biotic and abiotic)

2. Classify organisms based on their trophic levels (producers, consumers, decomposers)

3. Describe the flow of energy through an ecosystem and apply the 10% law in calculations

4. Differentiate between food chains and food webs with suitable examples

5. Construct and interpret ecological pyramids (numbers, biomass, and energy)

6. Explain the major biogeochemical cycles (carbon, nitrogen, water, phosphorus)

7. Identify various types of ecosystems (terrestrial and aquatic) with characteristics

8. Analyze the interdependence between organisms and their physical environment in ecosystem structure
