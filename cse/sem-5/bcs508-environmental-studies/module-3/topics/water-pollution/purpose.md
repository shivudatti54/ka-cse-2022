# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define water pollution and explain its significance in the context of environmental engineering and public health.

2. Identify and differentiate between point sources and non-point sources of water pollution with relevant examples.

3. Classify water pollutants into major categories (physical, chemical, biological, nutrient) and describe their specific characteristics and impacts.

4. Explain key water quality parameters including BOD, COD, DO, pH, TDS, and turbidity, and interpret their values for water quality assessment.

5. Analyze the effects of water pollution on human health, aquatic ecosystems, and economic activities.

6. Describe various water pollution control measures including industrial wastewater treatment, sewage treatment, and agricultural best management practices.

7. Apply water quality parameter calculations (BOD, treatment efficiency) to solve numerical problems.

8. Discuss the legal framework for water pollution control in India, particularly the Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974.
