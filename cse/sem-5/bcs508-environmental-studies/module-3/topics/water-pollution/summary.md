# Water Pollution - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Water Pollution**: Contamination of water bodies making them harmful to human health and aquatic life
- **Point Sources**: Identifiable pollution sources with specific discharge points (industrial outlets, sewage pipes)
- **Non-Point Sources**: Diffuse pollution sources without specific discharge points (agricultural runoff, urban runoff)
- **Eutrophication**: Nutrient enrichment causing excessive algal growth and subsequent oxygen depletion

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **BOD = Initial DO - Final DO** (measures biodegradable organic matter)
- **Treatment Efficiency = [(Inlet Concentration - Outlet Concentration) / Inlet Concentration] × 100%**
- **COD > BOD** for the same sample (COD includes all organic matter)
- Standard DO for healthy aquatic life: > 5 mg/L
- Standard drinking water pH: 6.5-8.5

## Key Points

- Water pollution sources are classified as point (industrial discharge, sewage) and non-point (agricultural runoff, atmospheric deposition)
- Pollutants include physical (suspended solids, thermal waste), chemical (heavy metals, pesticides), biological (pathogens), and nutrients (N, P)
- BOD indicates organic pollution level; higher BOD means more pollution
- DO is essential for aquatic life; levels below 2 mg/L cause fish mortality
- Eutrophication results from excess nutrients, creating dead zones in water bodies
- Wastewater treatment involves three stages: primary (physical), secondary (biological), and tertiary (chemical)
- The Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974 governs water quality standards in India

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Confusing BOD with COD - BOD measures biodegradable only, COD measures all organic matter
- Assuming all pollutants are visible - many harmful pollutants (heavy metals, pathogens, chemicals) are invisible
- Overlooking non-point sources - they contribute significantly to water pollution but are harder to control
- Ignoring the cumulative effect of small pollution sources

## Revision Tips

- Focus on understanding the cause-effect relationship in pollution scenarios
- Memorize standard water quality parameters (BOD, DO, pH, TDS) and their normal ranges
- Practice numerical problems on BOD calculation and treatment efficiency
- Remember key differences between point and non-point sources with examples
- Review the three stages of wastewater treatment and their purposes
- Know the major provisions of India's Water Pollution Control Act
