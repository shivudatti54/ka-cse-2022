Environmental pollution encompasses the introduction of harmful substances into natural environments at levels causing adverse effects. Primary pollutants are directly emitted (SO₂, CO, PM), while secondary pollutants form through atmospheric reactions (ozone, acid rain). Sources include stationary (industrial plants), mobile (vehicles), and non-point (agricultural runoff) origins. Environmental impacts include climate change through greenhouse gas accumulation, acid precipitation damaging ecosystems, ozone depletion permitting harmful UV radiation, eutrophication depleting aquatic oxygen, and bioaccumulation of toxins in food chains. Health consequences encompass respiratory diseases (from PM and ozone), cardiovascular problems, waterborne illnesses, carcinogenesis, and neurodevelopmental toxicity. Prevention requires engineering controls (scrubbers, catalytic converters, treatment plants), regulatory frameworks (emission standards, environmental impact assessments), and individual actions (public transport, recycling, conservation). For engineering students, this knowledge supports green computing initiatives, environmental monitoring systems, and sustainable design practices.
