# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define air pollution and distinguish between primary and secondary pollutants with examples

2. Identify major air pollutants (CO, NOx, SO₂, PM, VOCs, lead) and their sources from various sectors

3. Explain the adverse effects of air pollution on human health, environment, and materials

4. Interpret Air Quality Index (AQI) and understand its significance for public health advisory

5. Describe various air pollution control technologies including electrostatic precipitators, scrubbers, and catalytic converters

6. Analyze the legislative framework for air pollution control in India, including the Air Act and emission standards

7. Evaluate the relationship between air pollution and global environmental issues like acid rain, global warming, and ozone depletion

8. Apply knowledge of pollution monitoring and control to develop sustainable engineering solutions
