# Air Pollution - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Air Pollution**: Presence of harmful substances in the atmosphere that cause damage to living organisms and the environment
- **Primary Pollutants**: Emitted directly from sources (CO, NOx, SO₂, PM, lead)
- **Secondary Pollutants**: Formed from chemical reactions (ozone, smog)
- **AQI (Air Quality Index)**: Scale 0-500 indicating air cleanliness and health risk
- **Particulate Matter (PM)**: Solid/liquid particles; PM10 (≤10μm) and PM2.5 (≤2.5μm)

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **AQI Calculation**: Breakpoint method comparing pollutant concentration to standard ranges
- **Emission Efficiency**: (Initial concentration - Final concentration) / Initial concentration × 100
- **Control Efficiency**: (Pollutant removed / Pollutant entering) × 100

## Key Points

1. Major air pollutants: CO (incomplete combustion), NOx (high-temp combustion), SO₂ (sulfur in fuel), PM (various sources), VOCs (solvents)
2. Health effects: Respiratory diseases, cardiovascular problems, eye irritation, premature death
3. Environmental effects: Acid rain, global warming, ozone depletion, eutrophication
4. Control technologies: Electrostatic precipitators (particles), scrubbers (gases), catalytic converters (vehicles)
5. India's regulatory framework: Air Act 1981, Environment Protection Act 1986, Bharat Stage VI standards
6. AQI categories: Good (0-50), Moderate (51-100), Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups (101-150), Unhealthy (151-200)
7. Indoor air pollution sources: Cooking fuels, paints, biological contaminants, tobacco smoke
8. Greenhouse gases: CO₂ (largest contributor), CH₄, N₂O from fossil fuels, agriculture, and industries

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Confusing primary and secondary pollutants - remember secondary form from reactions
2. Mixing up PM10 and PM2.5 - PM2.5 is more dangerous as it penetrates deeper into lungs
3. Assuming all control technologies work for all pollutants - each is designed for specific pollutants
4. Ignoring indoor air pollution - it can be more concentrated than outdoor pollution in poorly ventilated spaces
5. Forgetting units - pollutants are measured in ppm, ppb, or μg/m³

## Revision Tips

1. Create a table linking each pollutant to its source, effects, and control method
2. Practice AQI calculations using sample concentration values
3. Memorize key emission standards (Bharat Stage VI) and regulatory bodies (CPCB)
4. Focus on the cause-effect relationship between pollutants and environmental problems
5. Review control technologies with their applications in industries and vehicles
6. Use flowcharts to remember the formation of secondary pollutants
7. Practice previous years' exam questions on this topic
