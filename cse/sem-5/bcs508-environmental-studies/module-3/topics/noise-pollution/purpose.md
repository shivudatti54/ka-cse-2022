# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define noise pollution and distinguish between sound and noise based on human perception and regulatory standards.

2. Explain the fundamental properties of sound including frequency, wavelength, amplitude, and their relationship to noise perception.

3. Identify and categorize the major sources of noise pollution including transportation, industrial, commercial, and construction activities.

4. Describe the health effects of noise pollution on humans, including hearing loss, cardiovascular issues, sleep disturbance, and mental health impacts.

5. Apply the decibel scale and understand key reference points for assessing noise levels in various environments.

6. Solve numerical problems related to sound propagation, including calculating sound pressure levels at different distances using the inverse square law.

7. Interpret noise measurement parameters including Equivalent Sound Level (Leq), Day-Night Sound Level (Ldn), and Time-Weighted Average (TWA).

8. Explain the three-tier approach to noise control: source control, path control, and receptor control measures.

9. Apply knowledge of Indian noise pollution regulations (Noise Pollution Rules, 2000) to determine compliance in different zone categories.
