# Environmental Pollution - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Environmental Pollution**: The introduction of harmful substances or energies into the environment that cause adverse effects on living organisms and materials.
- **Point Source Pollution**: Pollution from identifiable, discrete sources like factory discharge pipes.
- **Non-Point Source Pollution**: Diffuse pollution from multiple scattered sources, such as agricultural runoff.
- **BOD (Biochemical Oxygen Demand)**: The amount of oxygen required by microorganisms to decompose organic matter in water; measured over 5 days at 20°C.
- **AQI (Air Quality Index)**: A numerical scale indicating air quality levels and associated health concerns.
- **Smog**: Atmospheric pollution combining smoke and fog, including photochemical smog (formed by sunlight reacting with vehicle emissions) and industrial smog (from coal combustion).

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **AQI Calculation**: AQI = [(I_high - I_low) / (C_high - C_low)] × (C - C_low) + I_low
- **BOD Calculation**: BOD = (DO_initial - DO_final) × Dilution Factor
- **Combined Noise Level**: L_total = L_single + 10 log(n) for identical sources

## Key Points

- Primary air pollutants: PM2.5, PM10, SO₂, NOₓ, CO, O₃, and lead; major sources include vehicles, industries, and biomass burning.
- Water pollutants include organic matter, pathogens, heavy metals, and nutrients causing eutrophication.
- Bioaccumulation refers to accumulation in organisms, while biomagnification refers to increasing concentration up the food chain.
- Electrostatic precipitators remove particulate matter, scrubbers remove SO₂, and catalytic converters reduce vehicle emissions.
- BOD < 30 mg/L is typically required for wastewater discharge into inland waters in India.
- Permissible noise levels: Industrial area - 75 dB, Commercial area - 65 dB, Residential area - 55 dB.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Confusing BOD with COD - BOD measures biodegradable organic matter using microorganisms, while COD measures total organic matter using chemical oxidants.
- Failing to distinguish between point and non-point pollution sources in answers.
- Forgetting that AQI is determined by the maximum sub-index, not the average.
- Mixing up dilution factors in BOD calculations - always multiply the DO consumed by the dilution factor.

## Revision Tips

- Practice AQI and BOD numerical problems as these frequently appear in exams.
- Memorize Indian air quality standards (NAAQS) and water quality standards for quick recall.
- Create a table comparing pollution types, sources, effects, and control measures for systematic revision.
- Focus on understanding the pollution prevention hierarchy: Prevention > Minimization > Recycling > Treatment > Disposal.
