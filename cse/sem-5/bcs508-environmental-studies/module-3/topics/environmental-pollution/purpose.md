# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define environmental pollution and distinguish between the various types (air, water, soil, noise, and radioactive pollution).

2. Identify the major sources of different pollutants and classify them as point sources or non-point sources.

3. Explain the adverse effects of pollution on human health, ecosystems, and climate change.

4. Describe the working principles of common pollution control equipment and techniques.

5. Calculate Air Quality Index (AQI) and Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD) for pollution assessment.

6. Apply the concept of dilution factor in wastewater analysis and interpret pollution levels against regulatory standards.

7. Evaluate pollution control measures and understand the pollution prevention hierarchy.

8. Discuss the relevant Indian environmental regulations and their significance in pollution management.
