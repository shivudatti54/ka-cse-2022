# X.509 Certificates

## Summary

X.509 certificates are digital documents that bind a public key to an entity, ensuring secure communication over the internet. They contain essential fields, such as the subject, issuer, public key, and validity period. There are several types of X.509 certificates, including self-signed, CA-signed, end-entity, and intermediate certificates.

### Important Formulas, Definitions, and Theorems

- X.509 certificate: A digital document that binds a public key to an entity.
- Certificate chain: A sequence of certificates that verify the authenticity of a public key.
- Root CA certificate: A self-signed certificate that serves as the starting point for a certificate chain.
- Intermediate CA certificate: A certificate used to sign other certificates, typically used by CAs.

### Key Points

- X.509 certificates are used to establish secure connections between web browsers and servers.
- Certificates are used to encrypt and decrypt emails.
- Certificates are used to authenticate VPN connections.
- A certificate chain is used to verify the authenticity of a public key.
- X.509 certificates contain essential fields, such as the subject, issuer, public key, and validity period.
- There are several types of X.509 certificates, including self-signed, CA-signed, end-entity, and intermediate certificates.
- Certificates can be encoded in ASN.1, Base64, or hexadecimal.
