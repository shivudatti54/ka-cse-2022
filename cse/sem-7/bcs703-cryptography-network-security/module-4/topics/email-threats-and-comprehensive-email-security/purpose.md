# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Identify and explain the major email-based threats including phishing, spear phishing, whaling, Business Email Compromise (BEC), email spoofing, and malware distribution.

2. Describe the mechanisms of email encryption using PGP and S/MIME protocols, and explain how digital signatures provide authentication, integrity, and non-repudiation.

3. Understand and implement email authentication protocols (SPF, DKIM, and DMARC) to prevent email spoofing and domain impersonation.

4. Analyze email headers and identify signs of malicious emails or spoofing attempts.

5. Explain the architecture and functionality of enterprise email security solutions including Secure Email Gateways and Multi-Factor Authentication.

6. Evaluate email security requirements for different organizational scenarios and recommend appropriate security controls.

7. Understand the legal and compliance aspects of email security including data protection regulations and email retention requirements.
