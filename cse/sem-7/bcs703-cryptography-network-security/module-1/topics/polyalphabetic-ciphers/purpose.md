# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental difference between monoalphabetic and polyalphabetic substitution ciphers and why polyalphabetic ciphers provide better security against frequency analysis.

2. Describe the Vigenère cipher mechanism, including the encryption and decryption processes, and apply it to encrypt and decrypt messages using a given keyword.

3. Understand the concept of the autokey cipher and explain its advantages and vulnerabilities compared to the standard Vigenère cipher.

4. Explain the one-time pad cipher and prove why it achieves perfect secrecy when used according to its three fundamental requirements.

5. Apply the Kasiski examination technique to identify possible key lengths from repeated sequences in ciphertext.

6. Calculate the index of coincidence and use the Friedman test to estimate the key length of a Vigenère cipher.

7. Analyze the security limitations of polyalphabetic ciphers and explain why they are considered historical rather than modern cryptographic methods.
