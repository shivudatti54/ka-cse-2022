# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define the Data Encryption Standard (DES) and its role in cryptography.
2. List the components of the DES structure, including initial permutation, expansion permutation, substitution boxes, permutation, and final permutation.
3. Explain the concept of substitution boxes and permutation in the context of DES.
4. Describe the DES encryption process, including the use of a 56-bit key and 16 rounds of substitution and permutation.
5. Apply the DES algorithm to a specific plaintext and key, walking through the encryption process step by step.
6. Analyze the importance of key length and block size in the DES algorithm.
7. Compare the DES algorithm with other block ciphers, identifying its strengths and weaknesses.
8. Justify the use of DES as an educational tool in cryptography courses, despite its limitations in modern applications.
