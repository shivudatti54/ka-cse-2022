# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define steganography and explain its fundamental objectives in the context of information security.

2. Distinguish between steganography and cryptography, highlighting how these techniques complement each other.

3. Describe the basic model of steganography, including the roles of cover object, message, stego-key, and stego-object.

4. Explain the Least Significant Bit (LSB) substitution technique for image steganography with appropriate examples.

5. Analyze different types of steganographic techniques including image, audio, video, and text steganography.

6. Understand the concept of steganalysis and explain basic detection methods used to identify hidden information.

7. Evaluate the applications of steganography in digital watermarking, copyright protection, and covert communication.

8. Apply combined cryptographic and steganographic approaches to enhance information security.
