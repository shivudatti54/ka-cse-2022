# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the mathematical foundations of public key cryptanalysis, including the Integer Factorization Problem, Discrete Logarithm Problem, and their complexities.

2. Describe the major attack vectors on RSA, including mathematical attacks (Fermat factorization, Pollard's rho), chosen ciphertext attacks, and timing attacks.

3. Analyze why certain RSA key generation practices (small difference between p and q, small primes, predictable randomness) create vulnerabilities.

4. Evaluate the security of padding schemes and explain why OAEP is preferred over PKCS#1 v1.5.

5. Understand the principles of side-channel attacks (timing, power analysis) and their mitigations.

6. Assess protocol-level attacks on key exchange mechanisms, including man-in-the-middle and small subgroup attacks.

7. Apply cryptanalytic principles to evaluate the practical security of public key implementations.
