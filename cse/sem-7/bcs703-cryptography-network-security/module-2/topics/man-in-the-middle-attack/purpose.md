# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental mechanism of a Man In The Middle attack and describe how an attacker positions themselves between two communicating parties

2. Analyze the Diffie-Hellman key exchange protocol and demonstrate step-by-step how a MITM attack succeeds against it

3. Identify the specific vulnerabilities in key exchange protocols that make them susceptible to MITM attacks

4. Evaluate the role of pseudorandom number generators in preventing MITM attacks and explain how weak PRNGs can facilitate such attacks

5. Compare and contrast passive versus active MITM attacks and their respective detection difficulties

6. Design appropriate countermeasures against MITM attacks, including digital certificates, authentication protocols, and key verification mechanisms

7. Apply knowledge of MITM attack principles to analyze real-world scenarios including SSL/TLS and public key infrastructure
