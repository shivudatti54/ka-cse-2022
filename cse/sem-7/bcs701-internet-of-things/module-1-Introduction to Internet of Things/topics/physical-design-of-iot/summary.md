Physical design of IoT encompasses the tangible hardware components that enable the interface between physical environments and digital systems. Key elements include sensors (converting physical quantities to electrical signals through resistive, capacitive, inductive, piezoelectric, and optical transduction mechanisms), actuators (performing inverse conversion for physical control), and processing units ranging from 8-bit microcontrollers to application processors. Communication technologies span short-range (BLE, Wi-Fi, Zigbee) to long-range (LoRaWAN, NB-IoT), with application protocols (MQTT, CoAP) enabling data exchange. Power management through duty cycling, dynamic voltage scaling, and energy harvesting extends device operational lifespans. Signal conditioning circuits provide amplification, filtering, and impedance matching between sensors and ADCs. Students should be able to calculate power budgets, select appropriate communication technologies based on range/bandwidth/power trade-offs, and design sensor interfacing circuits.
