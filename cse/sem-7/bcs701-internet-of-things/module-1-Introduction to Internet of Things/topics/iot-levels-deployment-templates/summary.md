The six-level IoT classification framework progresses from single-device systems (Level 1) to federated edge-cloud ecosystems (Level 6), each characterized by distinct computational distribution, storage topology, and communication patterns. Deployment templates—cloud-centric, edge-centric, and fog computing—provide standardized architectural blueprints, with template selection determined by latency requirements, bandwidth constraints, scalability needs, and cost considerations. Level selection involves trade-offs between latency, scalability, cost models, analytics depth, and network dependency, requiring systematic analysis of application-specific requirements and operational constraints.
