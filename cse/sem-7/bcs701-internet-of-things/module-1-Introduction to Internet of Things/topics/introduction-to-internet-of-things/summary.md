The Internet of Things (IoT) represents a transformative paradigm connecting physical devices to digital infrastructure through sensors, actuators, and communication modules. This module covers three-tier IoT architecture (perception, network, application layers), distinguishing between microcontrollers for constrained devices and microprocessors for complex processing. Key communication protocols—MQTT (publish-subscribe, TCP-based, QoS support) and CoAP (request-response, UDP-based, RESTful)—are analyzed for different application requirements. The physical layer encompasses sensors for data acquisition and actuators for physical control, while logical design addresses functional blocks and communication models. IoT deployment faces significant challenges in security, interoperability, scalability, and power consumption. Students develop competencies in IoT system design, protocol selection based on constraints (bandwidth, latency, reliability), and understanding of enabling technologies including WSN, cloud computing, edge computing, and AI/ML integration.
