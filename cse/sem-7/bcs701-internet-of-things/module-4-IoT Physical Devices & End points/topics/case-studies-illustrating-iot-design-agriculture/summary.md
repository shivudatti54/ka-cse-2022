This module examined four detailed case studies illustrating IoT design in agriculture: (1) John Deere Operations Center for large-scale precision farming achieving 12% fertilizer reduction and 8% yield improvement; (2) Dutch greenhouse automation demonstrating 25% energy savings and 40% pesticide reduction; (3) Australian livestock monitoring showing 30% labor cost reduction and 45% theft reduction; (4) Israeli irrigation systems delivering 42% water savings. Key topics covered include three-tier IoT architecture, communication protocol selection (LoRaWAN, Zigbee, NB-IoT), PID controller design with mathematical derivations, edge vs cloud processing trade-offs, and security implementations. The module included five analytical MCQs testing application-level understanding of network design calculations, controller parameter determination, data volume estimation, irrigation scheduling mathematics, and duty cycle constraints.
