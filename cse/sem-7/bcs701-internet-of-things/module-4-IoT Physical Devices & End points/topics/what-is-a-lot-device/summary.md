IoT devices constitute the fundamental building blocks of IoT ecosystems, integrating sensing, processing, communication, and power management subsystems. This content examined the multi-layer architecture of IoT devices, detailed component-level analysis including sensors operating on physical transduction principles, microcontrollers versus microprocessors with quantitative selection criteria, and connectivity protocols with mathematical optimization frameworks. Design constraints including power consumption modeling, security considerations, and device taxonomy based on RFC 7228 classifications were addressed. The assessment evaluates application-based analysis requiring synthesis of multiple technical concepts for optimal component selection in constrained deployment scenarios.
