This study material establishes the foundational distinction between Machine-to-Machine (M2M) and Internet of Things (IoT) paradigms. It enables students to understand the evolutionary trajectory from legacy M2M systems to modern cloud-native IoT architectures. The comparative analysis equips learners to make informed architectural decisions based on requirements for scalability, interoperability, and data analytics capabilities. The protocol stack analysis provides technical depth expected at the B.Tech/M.Sc. level, while the theoretical justifications (Metcalfe's Law, economies of scale) connect practical knowledge to foundational computer science principles.
