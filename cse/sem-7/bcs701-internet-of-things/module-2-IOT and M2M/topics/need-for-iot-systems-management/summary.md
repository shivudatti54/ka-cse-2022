**IoT Systems Management** is the disciplined framework of processes, protocols, and tools required to administer large-scale, heterogeneous IoT deployments throughout their operational lifecycle. It addresses five critical imperatives: (1) managing scale and heterogeneity through unified control planes and abstraction; (2) enabling remote operation via OTA configuration and firmware updates; (3) handling data overload through edge processing and aggregation; (4) enforcing security through continuous authentication, monitoring, and rapid patch deployment; and (5) governing the complete device lifecycle from provisioning to secure decommissioning. The FCAPS model (Fault, Configuration, Accounting, Performance, Security) provides the theoretical foundation, while formal modeling demonstrates the scalability advantages of centralized management. This module emphasizes that management is not optional but fundamental to achieving reliable, secure, and commercially viable IoT deployments.
