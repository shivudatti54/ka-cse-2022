Network operators serve as the critical infrastructure providers for IoT ecosystems, imposing specific technical requirements for large-scale deployments. Key requirements include: (1) Scalability—supporting massive device densities through NB-IoT, LTE-M, and LPWAN technologies, with capacity analyzed using Erlang B formulas; (2) Energy Efficiency—implementing PSM and eDRX mechanisms to achieve 10+ year battery life; (3) Network Management—integrating SNMP and NETCONF/YANG for traffic shaping, APN configuration, and device provisioning; (4) Security—deploying defense-in-depth strategies including eSIM/iSIM, TLS encryption, and secure onboarding; (5) QoS—utilizing QCI, ARP, and MBR parameters to differentiate service levels; (6) Coverage Enhancement—employing repetition coding and CE modes; and (7) Cost-Effectiveness—optimizing hardware and connectivity costs for commercially viable deployments. Students should be able to analyze IoT network requirements, evaluate technology trade-offs, and design solutions meeting operator specifications.
