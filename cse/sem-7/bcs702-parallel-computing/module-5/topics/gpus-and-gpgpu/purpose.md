# Learning Purpose: GPUs and GPGPU

**1. Why this topic matters**
General-Purpose computing on Graphics Processing Units (GPGPU) represents one of the most significant shifts in computing, transforming GPUs from specialized graphics hardware into massively parallel general-purpose processors. Understanding the evolution from fixed-function graphics pipelines to programmable shaders to CUDA marks the beginning of modern GPU computing. This topic provides the essential context for why and how GPUs became the dominant platform for parallel computation.

**2. What you will learn**
You will learn what a GPU is and how its architecture fundamentally differs from a CPU, focusing on the throughput-optimized versus latency-optimized design philosophies. You will understand the key milestones in GPU evolution (fixed-function pipelines, programmable shaders, CUDA), identify workload characteristics that benefit from GPU acceleration, and gain an overview of the CUDA platform and basic GPU programming workflow.

**3. How it connects to other topics**
This topic provides the foundation for all subsequent topics in Module 5, from GPU architectures and heterogeneous computing to CUDA threads/blocks/grids and performance optimization. It builds on the SIMD concepts from Module 1 and the GPU overview from Module 2, and establishes the context for understanding why CUDA programming differs fundamentally from CPU-based OpenMP and MPI programming.

**4. Real-world relevance**
GPGPU computing powers the AI revolution through deep learning model training, accelerates scientific discoveries in genomics and drug design, enables real-time computer vision in autonomous vehicles, and drives advances in financial modeling. The shift to GPU computing has reshaped entire industries and created a high demand for developers who understand GPU programming.
