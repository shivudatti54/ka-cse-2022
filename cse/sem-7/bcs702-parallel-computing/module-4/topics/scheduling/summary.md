This chapter examined scheduling mechanisms in OpenMP parallel programming, covering work-sharing constructs that distribute loop iterations among threads, and three primary scheduling strategies. Static scheduling preassigns iterations before execution, offering minimal overhead but potential load imbalance for irregular workloads. Dynamic scheduling allows runtime iteration acquisition, achieving better load balance at increased synchronization cost. Guided scheduling provides adaptive behavior with decreasing chunk sizes. The theoretical analysis, including complexity calculations and load balance theorems, enables informed scheduling strategy selection based on iteration characteristics, performance requirements, and hardware configurations.
