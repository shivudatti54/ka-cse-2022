# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the relationship between Branch and Bound and the theory of NP-hard problems within the framework of algorithmic power limitations.

2. Describe the three fundamental operations of Branch and Bound: branching, bounding, and pruning, with mathematical precision.

3. Prove the correctness of the pruning operation in Branch and Bound algorithms.

4. Analyze the time and space complexity of Branch and Bound, distinguishing between worst-case exponential behavior and practical performance improvements.

5. Design effective bounding functions for optimization problems, with specific attention to relaxation techniques.

6. Compare different search strategies (FIFO, LIFO, Best-First) in terms of memory requirements, solution quality, and practical efficiency.

7. Apply Branch and Bound to solve concrete instances of the 0/1 Knapsack problem, computing bounds and determining optimal solutions.

8. Evaluate how Branch and Bound reduces the decision tree size compared to exhaustive enumeration, and explain why this reduction does not change the theoretical complexity class.
