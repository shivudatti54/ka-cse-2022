This module establishes fundamental lower bounds on the time complexity of comparison-based algorithms using decision tree analysis. It provides B.Tech/MSc/MCA students with rigorous mathematical proofs demonstrating that Ω(n log n) comparisons are necessary for sorting n elements, and Θ(log n) for binary search. The content enables students to understand the theoretical limits of algorithmic computation and recognize when problems cannot be solved faster within the comparison model, which is essential knowledge for algorithm designers and computational theorists.
