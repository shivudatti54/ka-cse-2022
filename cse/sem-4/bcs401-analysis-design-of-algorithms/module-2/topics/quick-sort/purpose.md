# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the divide-and-conquer paradigm as applied to Quick Sort and distinguish it from brute force approaches
2. Implement and trace both Lomuto and Hoare partition schemes with different pivot selection strategies
3. Analyze the time complexity of Quick Sort for best, average, and worst-case scenarios using recurrence relations
4. Derive and prove the average-case O(n log n) time complexity under random pivot assumptions
5. Evaluate space complexity implications including recursion depth and tail optimization techniques
6. Compare Quick Sort with other sorting algorithms (Merge Sort, Heap Sort) based on stability, in-place nature, and performance characteristics
7. Design and implement randomized Quick Sort to achieve probabilistic guarantees against worst-case inputs
8. Apply Quick Sort appropriately in practical scenarios considering its strengths and limitations
