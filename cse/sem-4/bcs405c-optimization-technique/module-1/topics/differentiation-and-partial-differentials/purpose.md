# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Apply the rules of differentiation (power, product, quotient, chain rules) to find derivatives of algebraic, trigonometric, exponential, and logarithmic functions.

2. Compute higher-order derivatives and interpret their significance in determining the nature of critical points (maxima, minima, or points of inflection).

3. Calculate partial derivatives of functions involving two or more variables and understand their geometric interpretation.

4. Apply Young's theorem to verify equality of mixed partial derivatives under appropriate conditions.

5. Use total differentiation to find the rate of change of dependent variables when independent variables are functions of a single parameter.

6. Compute the Jacobian matrix and understand its role in transformation problems and the implicit function theorem.

7. Apply Taylor series expansion to approximate functions near a given point, which serves as foundation for optimization algorithms.

8. Solve optimization problems by identifying critical points and classifying them using the second derivative test.
