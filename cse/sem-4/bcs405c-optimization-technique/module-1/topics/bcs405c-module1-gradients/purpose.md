# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define vector-valued functions and identify their component functions for given mappings from ℝⁿ to ℝᵐ

2. Compute the Jacobian matrix for a given vector-valued function, including all first-order partial derivatives

3. Recognize that the gradient of a scalar function is the transpose of its Jacobian matrix

4. Apply the chain rule to compute derivatives of composed vector-valued functions using Jacobian matrix multiplication

5. Compute the Hessian matrix for twice-differentiable scalar functions and interpret its role in second-order optimality conditions

6. Formulate and execute gradient descent iterations for simple optimization problems

7. Explain the relationship between directional derivatives and the gradient, including the formula Dᵤf = ∇f · u

8. Analyze computational complexity considerations for gradient-based computations in practical applications
