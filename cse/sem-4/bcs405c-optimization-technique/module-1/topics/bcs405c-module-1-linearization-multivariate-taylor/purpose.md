# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the concept of linearization and its importance in approximating nonlinear functions around a given point.

2. Write the multivariate Taylor series expansion formula for functions of two or more variables up to second order.

3. Compute the gradient vector (∇f) for multivariate functions and interpret its significance in determining search directions.

4. Construct and evaluate the Hessian matrix for multivariable functions to analyze curvature.

5. Apply linearization to approximate function values at points near a known expansion point with quantifiable error bounds.

6. Use second-order Taylor series approximations to classify critical points as local minima, maxima, or saddle points.

7. Understand the role of Taylor series in optimization algorithms, particularly in Newton's method.
