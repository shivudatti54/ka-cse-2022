# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the limitations of basic gradient descent methods that led to the development of adaptive optimization algorithms like RMSprop and Adam.

2. Describe the mathematical formulation of RMSprop, including the update equations for the squared gradient cache and parameter updates.

3. Understand the concept of momentum in optimization and explain how it accelerates convergence in gradient descent.

4. Derive the complete Adam optimization algorithm, including the first and second moment estimates and bias correction.

5. Compare and contrast RMSprop and Adam with other optimization algorithms (SGD, AdaGrad) based on their learning rate adaptation strategies.

6. Identify appropriate use cases for RMSprop and Adam based on problem characteristics, computational constraints, and performance requirements.

7. Apply the mathematical formulas of RMSprop and Adam to compute parameter updates for simple gradient descent scenarios.

8. Evaluate the impact of different hyperparameter choices (β, β₁, β₂, ε, η) on the convergence behavior of adaptive optimizers.
