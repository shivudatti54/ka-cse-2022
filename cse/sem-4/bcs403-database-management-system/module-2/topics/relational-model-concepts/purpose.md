# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and differentiate between entities, entity sets, attributes, and their various types (simple, composite, single-valued, multi-valued, derived)

2. Identify and explain different types of keys including superkey, candidate key, primary key, foreign key, and unique key with their characteristics

3. Describe relationships and relationship sets, including degree and cardinality of relationships

4. Construct relational schemas from real-world scenarios and identify appropriate keys for each relation

5. Explain and apply all integrity constraints: domain, key, entity integrity, and referential integrity

6. Convert ER diagrams containing entities, attributes, and relationships into equivalent relational schemas

7. Verify whether a database state satisfies referential integrity constraints

8. Analyze and solve database design problems by selecting appropriate primary keys and foreign keys
