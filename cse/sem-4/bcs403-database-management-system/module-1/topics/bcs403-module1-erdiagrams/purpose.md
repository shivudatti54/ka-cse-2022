# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Understand the purpose and importance of ER diagrams in database design and the database development lifecycle.

2. Identify and differentiate between entities, entity sets, weak entities, and strong entities with appropriate examples.

3. Classify and illustrate various types of attributes including simple, composite, single-valued, multi-valued, and derived attributes.

4. Explain the concept of keys including primary key, candidate key, alternate key, and foreign key with their roles in ER modeling.

5. Analyze and determine cardinality ratios (1:1, 1:N, M:N) and participation constraints (total and partial) for relationships between entities.

6. Design complete ER diagrams for given problem statements by identifying entities, attributes, relationships, and constraints.

7. Apply enhanced ER modeling concepts including specialization, generalization, aggregation, and composition to represent complex scenarios.
