# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and distinguish between entity types, entity sets, and entity instances in the context of database systems.

2. Identify and classify different types of attributes including simple, composite, single-valued, multi-valued, and derived attributes.

3. Understand the concept of keys including superkey, candidate key, and primary key, and apply them to uniquely identify entities.

4. Explain and differentiate between cardinality ratios (1:1, 1:N, M:N) in entity relationships.

5. Distinguish between total participation and partial participation constraints in relationships.

6. Construct Entity-Relationship (ER) diagrams using Chen's notation for various real-world scenarios.

7. Analyze business requirements to identify entity types, attributes, and structural constraints accurately.

8. Apply the concepts of entity types and structural constraints to design database schemas for simple applications.
