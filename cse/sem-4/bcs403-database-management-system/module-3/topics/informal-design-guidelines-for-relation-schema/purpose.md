# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the concept of semantics in relation schema design and its importance in database design.

2. Identify and describe the three types of update anomalies (insertion, deletion, and modification) that occur in poorly designed relation schemas.

3. Apply informal design guidelines to evaluate and improve relation schema designs.

4. Analyze a given relation schema to detect problems such as redundancy, inappropriate grouping of attributes, and excessive null values.

5. Demonstrate how to decompose a poorly designed relation into multiple well-structured relations.

6. Understand the concept of spurious tuples and explain why lossless-join decompositions are important.

7. Design relation schemas that minimize data redundancy while maintaining data integrity and query efficiency.

8. Distinguish between good and bad relation schema designs based on the informal guidelines presented.
