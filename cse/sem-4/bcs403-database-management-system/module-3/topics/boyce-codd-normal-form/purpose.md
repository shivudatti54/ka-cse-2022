# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define Boyce-Codd Normal Form (BCNF) and explain its significance in database normalization hierarchy

2. Identify functional dependencies in a relation and determine whether they satisfy BCNF conditions

3. Find and list all candidate keys for a given relation schema with multiple functional dependencies

4. Apply the BCNF decomposition algorithm to convert a relation not in BCNF into BCNF-compliant relations

5. Verify that the decomposed relations are in BCNF by checking that all functional dependencies have superkeys as determinants

6. Explain the difference between 3NF and BCNF, and identify scenarios where a relation in 3NF may not students in BCNF

7. Analyze lossless join decomposition and verify that decomposed relations can students joined without losing original data

8. Evaluate the trade-offs between BCNF and dependency preservation in database design
