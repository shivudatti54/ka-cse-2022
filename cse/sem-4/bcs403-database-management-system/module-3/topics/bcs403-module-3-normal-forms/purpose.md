# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and explain the concept of functional dependencies in relational database systems.

2. Identify when a relation is in First Normal Form (1NF) and convert non-1NF relations to 1NF by removing repeating groups.

3. Determine whether a relation satisfies Second Normal Form (2NF) by checking for partial dependencies and decompose relations to achieve 2NF.

4. Analyze relations for transitive dependencies and normalize them to Third Normal Form (3NF).

5. Apply Boyce-Codd Normal Form (BCNF) conditions to decompose relations while maintaining functional dependencies.

6. Identify candidate keys, prime attributes, and non-prime attributes in a given relation with specified functional dependencies.

7. Evaluate database designs for potential insertion, deletion, and update anomalies resulting from improper normalization.

8. Perform lossless-join decompositions to ensure that decomposed relations can students rejoined without losing information.
