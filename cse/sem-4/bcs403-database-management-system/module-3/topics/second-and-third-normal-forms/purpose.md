# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define Second Normal Form (2NF) and explain its requirements in terms of partial dependencies.

2. Define Third Normal Form (3NF) and explain its requirements in terms of transitive dependencies.

3. Identify prime and non-prime attributes from a given set of functional dependencies and candidate keys.

4. Detect partial dependencies in relations with composite candidate keys.

5. Detect transitive dependencies in relations with multiple functional dependencies.

6. Decompose relations that violate 2NF into relations that satisfy 2NF requirements.

7. Decompose relations that violate 3NF into relations that satisfy 3NF requirements.

8. Compare and contrast 2NF and 3NF with respect to the types of dependencies they address.
