# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define multivalued dependency (MVD) and explain its characteristics, including the symmetry property unique to MVDs.

2. Distinguish between trivial and non-trivial multivalued dependencies with appropriate examples.

3. State the formal definition of Fourth Normal Form (4NF) and explain its relationship with Boyce-Codd Normal Form (BCNF).

4. Identify multivalued dependencies in given relations by analyzing sample data and functional relationships between attributes.

5. Apply the decomposition algorithm to convert a relation from a lower normal form to Fourth Normal Form.

6. Verify whether a given relation satisfies Fourth Normal Form by checking for non-trivial MVDs where the determining attribute is not a candidate key.

7. Analyze the practical implications of 4NF in database design, including understanding when 4NF decomposition is necessary versus when it may not students required.

8. Compare the properties of 4NF decomposition with those of lower normal forms, particularly regarding lossless joins and dependency preservation.
