# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define a transaction and explain its role as a logical unit of work in DBMS.

2. Describe and illustrate the four ACID properties (Atomicity, Consistency, Isolation, Durability) with practical examples.

3. Identify and explain the various states in the transaction lifecycle (Active, Partially Committed, Committed, Failed, Aborted).

4. Differentiate between serial and non-serial schedules, and explain the concept of serializability.

5. Apply conflict serializability testing using precedence graphs for determining if a schedule is correct.

6. Compare and contrast different concurrency control techniques including lock-based protocols, timestamp-based protocols, and validation-based protocols.

7. Explain the need for recovery mechanisms and describe log-based recovery techniques including redo and undo operations.

8. Analyze practical scenarios involving transactions to identify potential concurrency problems and appropriate solutions.
