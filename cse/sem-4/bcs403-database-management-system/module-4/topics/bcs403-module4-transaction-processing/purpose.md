# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define what a transaction is in the context of database management systems and explain its role in maintaining data integrity.

2. Explain the four ACID properties (Atomicity, Consistency, Isolation, Durability) and provide practical examples of each.

3. Identify and describe the various states in the transaction lifecycle (Active, Partially Committed, Committed, Failed, Aborted).

4. Analyze the problems that can occur in concurrent transaction execution, including Lost Update, Dirty Read, Non-Repeatable Read, and Phantom Read.

5. Differentiate between serial and non-serial schedules and explain the concepts of recoverable and cascadeless schedules.

6. Apply lock-based concurrency control techniques, including shared locks, exclusive locks, and the Two-Phase Locking protocol.

7. Understand timestamp-based concurrency control mechanisms and how they ensure serializability.

8. Design and analyze transaction schedules that maintain database consistency under concurrent execution.
