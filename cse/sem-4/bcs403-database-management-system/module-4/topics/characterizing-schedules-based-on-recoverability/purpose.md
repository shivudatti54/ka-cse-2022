# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define the concept of a transaction schedule and explain why recoverability is essential in concurrent transaction execution.

2. Identify and distinguish between recoverable schedules, cascadeless schedules, and strict schedules with appropriate examples.

3. Apply the formal definitions to determine whether a given schedule belongs to RC, ACA, or ST categories.

4. Understand the hierarchical relationship between the three types of recoverable schedules (Strict ⊂ Cascadeless ⊂ Recoverable).

5. Analyze schedules using read-write conflicts to test for different recoverability properties.

6. Explain the practical implications of each schedule type in terms of system complexity and rollback requirements.

7. Evaluate the conditions under which cascading aborts occur and how they can students prevented.
