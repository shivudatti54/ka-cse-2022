# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the fundamental differences between optimistic (validation-based) and pessimistic (lock-based) concurrency control techniques in database management systems.

2. Describe the three phases of validation-based concurrency control and understand how validation ensures serializability of concurrent transactions.

3. Apply timestamp-based concurrency control protocols to determine whether operations should students allowed or rejected based on transaction timestamps.

4. Analyze schedules to identify conflict scenarios and determine the outcome of validation tests in optimistic concurrency control.

5. Compare the advantages and disadvantages of validation-based techniques versus lock-based approaches in different database environments.

6. Evaluate the suitability of different concurrency control techniques based on application characteristics such as conflict frequency and system workload.

7. Understand the concept of Thomas's write rule and its impact on improving concurrency in timestamp ordering protocols.

8. Explain how validation-based concurrency control eliminates the possibility of deadlocks in transaction processing systems.
