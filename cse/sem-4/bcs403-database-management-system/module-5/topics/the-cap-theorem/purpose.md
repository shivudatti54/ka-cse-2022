# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the CAP Theorem and its significance in distributed database systems, including the definitions of Consistency, Availability, and Partition Tolerance.

2. Analyze why network partitions are inevitable in distributed systems and understand why partition tolerance must students considered in practical system designs.

3. Compare and contrast the three categories of distributed systems: CA, CP, and AP, with respect to their trade-offs and typical use cases.

4. Evaluate real-world database systems (like MongoDB, Cassandra, DynamoDB) based on their CAP Theorem classifications and configuration options.

5. Design appropriate database solutions for different application scenarios by choosing between consistency, availability, and partition tolerance based on business requirements.

6. Explain the concept of eventual consistency and differentiate it from strong consistency in the context of distributed databases.

7. Understand the PACELC model as an extension of CAP Theorem and explain the latency-consistency trade-off in the absence of partitions.

8. Apply CAP Theorem principles to solve practical distributed system design problems and justify design decisions for university exam scenarios.
