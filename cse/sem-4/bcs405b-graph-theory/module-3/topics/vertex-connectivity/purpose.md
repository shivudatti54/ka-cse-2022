# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define vertex connectivity (κ(G)) and explain its significance in measuring graph resilience
2. Identify and construct vertex cuts (separating sets) in various graph structures
3. Apply Whitney's theorem (κ(G) ≤ λ(G) ≤ δ(G)) to establish relationships between different connectivity measures
4. Compute vertex connectivity for fundamental graph families including paths, cycles, complete graphs, and complete bipartite graphs
5. Distinguish between vertex cuts and edge cuts, understanding when each type of removal is more impactful
6. Analyze practical scenarios involving network reliability and determine minimum vertex cuts
7. Understand the concept of cut vertices (articulation points) and their relationship to vertex connectivity value of 1
8. Evaluate graph robustness and compare different graph structures based on their connectivity parameters
