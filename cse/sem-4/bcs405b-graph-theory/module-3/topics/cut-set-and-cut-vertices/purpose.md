# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and identify cut-vertices (articulation points) in a given graph and explain their significance in network connectivity.

2. Define and identify bridges (cut-edges) in a graph and distinguish them from cut-vertices.

3. Explain the concept of cut-sets (edge cuts) and determine the minimum edge cut for simple graphs.

4. Apply the DFS-based algorithm to find all cut-vertices in a connected graph with step-by-step computation.

5. Understand and apply the concepts of edge connectivity (λ) and vertex connectivity (κ), and state the relationship between them.

6. Explain the block-cut graph (BC-tree) and its role in representing the biconnected components of a graph.

7. Analyze whether a given graph is biconnected (2-vertex-connected) and identify its biconnected components.

8. Apply these concepts to solve practical problems related to network reliability and graph robustness.
