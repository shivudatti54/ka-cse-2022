# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and distinguish between Eulerian circuit, Eulerian path, Euler graph, and semi-Eulerian graph with precise mathematical terminology.

2. Apply Euler's theorems to determine whether a given connected graph is Eulerian, semi-Eulerian, or neither, by analyzing vertex degrees.

3. Use Fleury's algorithm to construct an Eulerian trail in a graph, making correct decisions about bridge edges at each step.

4. Apply Hierholzer's algorithm to efficiently find an Eulerian circuit in a graph.

5. Analyze real-world problems and identify them as Eulerian path/circuit problems.

6. Understand the relationship between Eulerian and Hamiltonian concepts in graph theory.

7. Verify the correctness of Eulerian trails by ensuring all edges are traversed exactly once.
