# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define the Travelling Salesman Problem and explain its formal mathematical formulation in terms of finding minimum weight Hamiltonian cycles in complete weighted graphs.

2. Distinguish between Hamiltonian cycles and Eulerian circuits, and explain why this distinction is fundamental to understanding TSP.

3. Apply the Dynamic Programming approach (Held-Karp algorithm) to solve small TSP instances, demonstrating understanding of the state definition and recurrence relation.

4. Analyze the time and space complexities of various TSP solution methods including brute force, dynamic programming, and heuristic approaches.

5. Implement and evaluate heuristic methods such as Nearest Neighbor and 2-Opt for finding good (but not necessarily optimal) TSP solutions efficiently.

6. Explain the concept of approximation algorithms for Metric TSP and state the approximation guarantees of the MST-based and Christofides algorithms.

7. Understand the significance of TSP being NP-hard and explain why this classification motivates the study of heuristic and approximation approaches in practice.

8. Identify and describe at least three real-world applications of TSP in logistics, transportation, DNA sequencing, and manufacturing.
