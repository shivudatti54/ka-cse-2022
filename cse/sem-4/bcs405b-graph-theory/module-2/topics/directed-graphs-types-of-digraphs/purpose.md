# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define a directed graph (digraph) and distinguish between directed and undirected graphs with appropriate examples.

2. Identify and explain different types of digraphs including simple digraphs, complete digraphs, and regular digraphs.

3. Analyze and distinguish between strongly connected and weakly connected digraphs with suitable examples.

4. Recognize directed acyclic graphs (DAGs) and explain their significance in computer science applications.

5. Understand tournaments as complete oriented graphs and recall their important properties including Hamiltonian path existence.

6. Perform basic operations on digraphs including union, intersection, complement, and reversal.

7. Compute indegree and outdegree of vertices in a digraph and verify the handshaking lemma for directed graphs.

8. Apply the concept of topological sorting to solve dependency problems in DAGs.
