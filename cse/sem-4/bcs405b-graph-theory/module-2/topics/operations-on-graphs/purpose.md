# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and explain the basic operations on graphs: union, intersection, and join
2. Compute the complement of a given graph and identify self-complementary graphs
3. Perform Cartesian product, strong product, and composition operations on graphs
4. Distinguish between subgraph, spanning subgraph, and induced subgraph
5. Analyze the resulting graph properties (number of vertices, edges, connectivity) after applying operations
6. Apply graph operations to solve problems involving graph construction and analysis
7. Understand the relationship between a graph and its complement in terms of edge distribution
8. Solve exam problems involving identification and construction of graphs using various operations
