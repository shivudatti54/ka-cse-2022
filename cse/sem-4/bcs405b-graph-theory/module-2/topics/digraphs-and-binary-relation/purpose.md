# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and distinguish between directed graphs (digraphs) and undirected graphs, identifying their key components (vertices, arcs, loops).

2. Represent binary relations as digraphs and convert between relation notation, digraph visualization, and adjacency matrix representation.

3. Analyze and determine whether a binary relation exhibits reflexive, irreflexive, symmetric, asymmetric, antisymmetric, or transitive properties.

4. Perform fundamental operations on digraphs including union, intersection, and complement, and understand how these operations affect the corresponding relations.

5. Calculate in-degree and out-degree of vertices in digraphs and apply these concepts to solve connectivity problems.

6. Distinguish between weak and strong connectivity in digraphs and determine whether a given digraph is strongly connected.

7. Apply matrix representation techniques to find the number of paths of various lengths between vertices using matrix multiplication.

8. Identify special relations including equivalence relations and partial orders, and construct Hasse diagrams for partially ordered sets.
