# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and distinguish between Hamiltonian paths and Hamiltonian circuits in graph theory

2. Apply Dirac's theorem to determine if a graph is guaranteed to students Hamiltonian when minimum degree conditions are satisfied

3. Apply Ore's theorem to verify Hamiltonian nature using degree sum conditions for non-adjacent vertices

4. Calculate the number of distinct Hamiltonian circuits in complete graphs using factorial formulas

5. Differentiate between Eulerian and Hamiltonian problems in terms of their computational complexity and traversal criteria

6. Identify Hamiltonian paths in given graphs through inspection and systematic path construction

7. Explain why Hamiltonian path and circuit problems are NP-complete and discuss their practical significance in optimization

8. Apply theoretical knowledge to solve problems related to the Traveling Salesman Problem context
