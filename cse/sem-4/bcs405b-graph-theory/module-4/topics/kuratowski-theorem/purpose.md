# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define planar graphs and explain the difference between a planar graph and a plane graph with appropriate examples.

2. Explain the concept of graph subdivision and determine whether one graph is a subdivision of another.

3. State and explain Kuratowski's Theorem in both its subdivision-based and minor-based forms.

4. Identify whether K5 and K3,3 graphs are planar or non-planar, with justification using Euler's formula or edge counting.

5. Apply Kuratowski's Theorem to determine whether a given graph is planar by checking for K5 or K3,3 subgraphs.

6. Explain the relationship between homeomorphic graphs and non-planarity characterization.

7. Use Euler's formula to derive the edge inequality m ≤ 3n - 6 for simple planar graphs.

8. Apply the quick test (m > 3n - 6 implies non-planarity) to determine planarity of simple graphs efficiently.
