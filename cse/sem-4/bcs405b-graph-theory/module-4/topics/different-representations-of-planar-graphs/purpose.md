# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define planar graphs and explain the concept of planar embedding with examples.

2. Differentiate between various geometric representations of planar graphs including straight-line, radial, circular, and grid embeddings.

3. Construct and analyze combinatorial representations such as adjacency matrix, incidence matrix, adjacency list, and half-edge data structures for planar graphs.

4. Apply Euler's formula to solve problems related to vertices, edges, and faces in planar graphs.

5. Explain the concept of dual graphs and their properties, including the relationship between a planar graph and its dual.

6. Select appropriate representation methods for different types of planar graph operations and algorithms.

7. Verify whether a given graph is planar using the necessary conditions (m ≤ 3n - 6).

8. Analyze the space and time complexity of different planar graph representations for various operations.
