# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and identify planar graphs with appropriate examples and understand their significance in graph theory

2. Apply Euler's formula (v - e + r = 2) to solve problems involving connected planar graphs and its generalized form for disconnected graphs

3. Differentiate between maximal planar graphs, outer planar graphs, and general planar graphs using their properties and edge count formulas

4. Apply Kuratowski's theorem to determine whether a given graph is planar or non-planar

5. Understand the concept of dual graphs and their construction from planar embeddings

6. Explain the Four Color Theorem and Five Color Theorem with their implications for graph coloring

7. Use the inequality e ≤ 3v - 6 to prove that specific graphs (like K5 and K3,3) are non-planar

8. Apply all theoretical concepts to solve examination-level problems involving planar graph identification and verification
