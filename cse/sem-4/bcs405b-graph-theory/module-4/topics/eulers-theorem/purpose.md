# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define planar graphs, plane graphs, and explain the distinction between them with examples.

2. State and explain Euler's formula for connected planar graphs (V - E + F = 2).

3. Apply Euler's formula to verify the relationship between vertices, edges, and faces in given planar graphs.

4. Derive and apply the corollaries of Euler's theorem: E ≤ 3V - 6 for planar graphs and E ≤ 2V - 4 for triangle-free planar graphs.

5. Use Euler's formula to prove that specific graphs (K5, K3,3) are non-planar through contradiction.

6. Identify the fundamental non-planar graphs K5 and K3,3 and explain why they cannot students drawn without edge crossings.

7. Understand the basic statement of Kuratowski's theorem as a characterization of planar graphs.
