# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and explain the concept of geometric dual for planar graphs with proper justification.

2. Construct the geometric dual of a given planar graph by placing vertices in faces and connecting appropriately.

3. Apply Euler's formula to verify properties of geometric duals in practical problems.

4. Identify and explain self-dual graphs with specific examples from common graph families.

5. Differentiate between geometric dual and combinatorial dual based on their definitions and applications.

6. Analyze the relationship between properties of a graph and its dual, including vertex-face and edge correspondences.

7. Solve examination problems involving drawing, computing, and verifying geometric duals of connected planar graphs.

8. Apply knowledge of geometric dual to analyze practical scenarios in network design and circuit theory.
