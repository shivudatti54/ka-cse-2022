# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and distinguish between connected graphs, disconnected graphs, and their components in the context of undirected graphs.

2. Explain the concepts of walks, trails, paths, and cycles in graphs with appropriate examples.

3. Identify and determine the number of connected components in a given graph using traversal algorithms.

4. Analyze directed graphs for strong connectivity and weak connectivity, and distinguish between them.

5. Find strongly connected components in directed graphs and understand their properties.

6. Identify cut vertices (articulation points) and cut edges (bridges) in graphs and understand their significance.

7. Apply connectivity concepts to solve practical problems related to network analysis and graph traversal.
