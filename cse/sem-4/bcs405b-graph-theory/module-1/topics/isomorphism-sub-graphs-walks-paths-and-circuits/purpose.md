# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and explain graph isomorphism, identifying the necessary and sufficient conditions for two graphs to be isomorphic.

2. Distinguish between spanning subgraphs, induced subgraphs, and edge-induced subgraphs, providing examples of each type.

3. Classify sequences of vertices and edges as walks, trails, paths, circuits, or cycles based on vertex and edge repetition criteria.

4. Determine whether two given graphs are isomorphic by constructing an explicit bijection or by identifying invariant properties that differ.

5. Compute the distance between any two vertices, the eccentricity of a vertex, and the radius and diameter of a graph.

6. Analyze complex graphs to identify specific substructures, including paths of given lengths and cycles of various sizes.

7. Apply the concepts of isomorphism, subgraphs, and graph traversal to solve practical problems in network analysis and related fields.
