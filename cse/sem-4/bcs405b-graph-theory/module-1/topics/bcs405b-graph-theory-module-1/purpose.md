# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define a graph and explain its components (vertices and edges)

2. Distinguish between different types of graphs: simple, multi, directed, undirected, and weighted

3. Explain and apply key graph terminology including adjacency, degree, path, cycle, and connectivity

4. Verify the Handshaking Lemma and use it to solve problems

5. Identify and differentiate between special graphs: complete, bipartite, regular, cycle, and wheel graphs

6. Determine whether a given graph is connected or disconnected

7. Analyze if a graph is bipartite by checking for odd-length cycles

8. Construct graphs from various representations (adjacency list, edge set, description)
