# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and identify vertices, edges, and incidence relationships in a graph

2. Compute the degree of any vertex in a graph and identify special vertex types (isolated, pendant, even-degree, odd-degree)

3. Apply the Handshaking Lemma to solve problems involving the relationship between vertices, edges, and degrees

4. Determine whether a given sequence is graphical using the Havel-Hakimi algorithm

5. Classify and construct regular graphs, complete graphs, and complete bipartite graphs with their properties

6. Distinguish between bipartite and non-bipartite graphs based on cycle structure

7. Analyze subgraph relationships and identify spanning subgraphs and induced subgraphs

8. Apply degree-based reasoning to solve practical problems in network analysis and graph characterization
