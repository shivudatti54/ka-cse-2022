# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and distinguish between finite graphs and infinite graphs with appropriate examples and applications.

2. Identify whether a given graph is finite or infinite by examining its vertex and edge sets.

3. Define a bipartite graph and explain the requirements for vertex partition into two partite sets.

4. Determine if a graph is bipartite using the 2-coloring method and the odd cycle test.

5. Explain the characterization theorem: A graph is bipartite if and only if it contains no odd cycles.

6. Define and construct complete bipartite graphs K(m,n) and calculate their properties (number of vertices, edges, and degrees).

7. Apply the concepts of bipartite graphs to solve practical problems such as job assignment and matching problems.

8. Explain the concept of perfect matching in bipartite graphs and the condition required for its existence.
