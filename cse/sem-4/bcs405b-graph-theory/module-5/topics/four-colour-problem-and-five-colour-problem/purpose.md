# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define planar graphs and understand their properties, including Euler's formula for planar graphs.

2. Explain the relationship between map colouring and graph colouring through dual graphs.

3. Understand the statement and significance of the Five Colour Theorem and its proof approach using vertex degree arguments.

4. Understand the statement and significance of the Four Colour Theorem and the computational approach to its proof.

5. Apply Euler's formula to derive bounds on edges in planar graphs and prove that every planar graph has a vertex of degree at most 5.

6. Distinguish between the historical development of the Four and Five Colour Problems and their respective proofs.

7. Analyse simple planar graphs to determine appropriate colouring strategies and verify that the colour bounds are satisfied.

8. Connect the Four and Five Colour Problems to other fundamental concepts in graph theory, including non-planar graphs and graph homeomorphisms.
