# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and distinguish between matching, maximum matching, and perfect matching in graphs
2. Identify matchings in bipartite graphs and understand the concept of partite sets X and Y
3. Apply Hall's Marriage Theorem to determine whether an X-perfect matching exists in a bipartite graph
4. Explain the concept of augmenting paths and their role in finding maximum matchings
5. State and apply König's Theorem to establish the relationship between maximum matching size and minimum vertex cover size
6. Understand Tutte's Theorem as a characterization of perfect matchings in general graphs
7. Find maximum matchings in bipartite graphs using the augmenting path algorithm
8. Recognize practical applications of matching theory in assignment problems and resource allocation scenarios
