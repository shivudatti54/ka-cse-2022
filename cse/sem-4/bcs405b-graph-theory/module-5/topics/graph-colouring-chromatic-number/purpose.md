# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define proper graph colouring and explain its significance in graph theory
2. Understand and compute the chromatic number χ(G) for various graph families including cycles, complete graphs, and bipartite graphs
3. Explain the Four Colour Theorem and Five Colour Theorem, including their proofs and limitations
4. Compute the chromatic polynomial P(G, k) for simple graphs using direct counting and the deletion-contraction recurrence
5. Apply the greedy colouring algorithm and analyze its performance in terms of vertex ordering
6. State and apply Brooks' Theorem to determine upper bounds on chromatic numbers
7. Identify k-critical graphs and understand their role in chromatic number computation
8. Solve real-world problems (scheduling, register allocation, map colouring) by modelling them as graph colouring problems
9. Distinguish between planar and non-planar graphs in the context of colouring
10. Use the relationship between chromatic number and clique number (χ(G) ≥ ω(G)) to establish bounds
