# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define the chromatic polynomial P(G, k) and explain its significance in graph theory, understanding it as a counting function for proper vertex colorings.

2. Compute chromatic polynomials for basic graph families including complete graphs K_n, path graphs P_n, cycle graphs C_n, and complete bipartite graphs K_m,n using established formulas.

3. Apply the deletion-contraction recurrence relation P(G, k) = P(G-e, k) - P(G/e, k) to compute chromatic polynomials for arbitrary graphs through systematic reduction to simpler cases.

4. Evaluate the chromatic polynomial at specific values of k and interpret the results, including understanding when P(G, k) = 0 and what this implies about k-colorability.

5. Explain the fundamental properties of chromatic polynomials, including the fact that the leading coefficient is always 1 and the coefficients alternate in sign for connected graphs.

6. Use chromatic polynomials to determine the chromatic number χ(G) of a graph by identifying the smallest positive integer k for which P(G, k) > 0.

7. Apply Whitney's theorem concepts to understand the relationship between chromatic polynomial coefficients and graph substructure, particularly the coefficient of k^(n-1).

8. Solve exam-style problems involving computation, evaluation, and interpretation of chromatic polynomials for various graph structures.
