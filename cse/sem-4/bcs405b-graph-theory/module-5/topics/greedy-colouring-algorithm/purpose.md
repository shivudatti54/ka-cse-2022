# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the greedy colouring algorithm and its fundamental working principle of assigning the smallest available colour to each vertex.

2. Apply the greedy colouring algorithm to colour any given graph step-by-step with a specified vertex ordering.

3. Compare different vertex ordering strategies including natural ordering, descending degree ordering, smallest degree ordering, and DSATUR.

4. Implement the Welsh-Powell algorithm for graph colouring and analyze its results on various graph structures.

5. Analyze and prove the theoretical bounds on greedy colouring, including the relationship between chromatic number and maximum degree.

6. Apply Brook's Theorem to determine upper bounds on chromatic number for connected graphs that are not complete or odd cycles.

7. Evaluate whether a graph is a perfect graph and explain why greedy colouring produces optimal results for perfect graphs.

8. Design practical colouring solutions for real-world problems such as scheduling, register allocation, and frequency assignment.
