This study material explains the ARM (Advanced RISC Machine) design philosophy for B.Tech/MSc/MCA students studying embedded systems. The content covers RISC fundamentals as implemented by ARM, the Current Program Status Register (CPSR), pipeline architecture, the Thumb instruction set, and quantitative power-performance-area trade-off analysis. The purpose is to provide thorough theoretical foundation with formal proofs and numerical examples, enabling students to understand why ARM was designed this way and apply this knowledge to embedded system design and programming. Understanding ARM design philosophy is essential for working with popular ARM Cortex-M microcontrollers (STM32, TIVA, LPC) used extensively in industry.
