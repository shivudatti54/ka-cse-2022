# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the pedagogical advantages of the chalk and talk method for teaching abstract mathematical concepts like Vector Spaces

2. Design a structured 8-hour teaching plan that covers Vector Spaces and related topics with appropriate progression and depth

3. Implement the subspace test correctly to determine whether given subsets are subspaces of larger vector spaces

4. Compute linear combinations and spans of sets of vectors in various vector spaces

5. Determine whether given sets of vectors are linearly dependent or linearly independent using appropriate techniques

6. Verify that a given set forms a basis for a vector space and determine the dimension of the vector space

7. Find coordinates of vectors relative to given bases and perform change of basis calculations

8. Determine the row space, column space, and null space of a matrix, and apply the rank-nullity theorem

9. # Recognize common student difficulties in understanding Vector Space concepts and prepare appropriate responses and examples
