# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the concept of dimensionality reduction and why it is necessary in data analysis.

2. Describe the curse of dimensionality and its effects on computational efficiency and model performance.

3. Define eigenvalues and eigenvectors in the context of the covariance matrix in PCA.

4. Apply the step-by-step PCA algorithm to transform high-dimensional data to lower dimensions.

5. Calculate variance explained by each principal component and determine the number of components needed for a given threshold.

6. Interpret the results of PCA, including the meaning of principal component directions and their corresponding eigenvalues.

7. Compare PCA with other dimensionality reduction techniques and identify appropriate use cases.

8. Apply PCA to practical students problems such as image compression and data visualization.
