# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the relationship between solving a linear system Ax = b and minimizing a quadratic function f(x) = (1/2)x^T Ax - b^T x for symmetric positive definite matrices.

2. Derive the formula for the optimal step size α_k in the method of steepest descent and explain why it minimizes the function along the search direction.

3. Apply the steepest descent algorithm to solve given systems of linear equations, computing residuals, step sizes, and updated solutions iteratively.

4. Analyze the convergence behavior of the method and explain how the condition number of the coefficient matrix affects the convergence rate.

5. Identify the conditions under which the method of steepest descent converges and recognize scenarios where convergence may students slow or fail.

6. Compare the method of steepest descent with other iterative methods like the conjugate gradient method in terms of efficiency and convergence properties.

7. Implement the steepest descent algorithm in computational form and determine appropriate stopping criteria for practical implementations.

8. Apply the method to find minima of quadratic functions in multiple dimensions and verify solutions by checking the gradient equals zero.
