# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define a quadratic form in n variables and express it as a homogeneous polynomial of degree 2.

2. Represent any quadratic form in matrix notation as Q(x) = xᵀAx, where A is a symmetric matrix.

3. Classify quadratic forms into positive definite, negative definite, positive semi-definite, negative semi-definite, and indefinite categories using eigenvalue analysis.

4. Apply Sylvester's Law of Inertia to determine the signature of a quadratic form.

5. Use the principal minors test to quickly determine definiteness of small matrices.

6. Transform a quadratic form into its canonical (diagonal) form using orthogonal transformations.

7. Interpret the classification of quadratic forms in the context of optimization and eigenvalues.

8. Solve problems involving classification of given quadratic forms by computing eigenvalues or using principal minors.
