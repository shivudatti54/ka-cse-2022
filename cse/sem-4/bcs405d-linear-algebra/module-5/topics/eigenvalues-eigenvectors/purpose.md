# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define eigenvalues and eigenvectors and explain their geometric interpretation in linear transformations.

2. Compute the characteristic equation of a square matrix and determine its eigenvalues.

3. Find corresponding eigenvectors for each eigenvalue by solving the homogeneous system (A - λI)v = 0.

4. Apply properties of eigenvalues including trace-determinant relationship and eigenvalue transformations.

5. Distinguish between algebraic and geometric multiplicity and understand their significance in diagonalization.

6. Determine whether a matrix is diagonalizable and construct the diagonalization when possible.

7. Apply the Cayley-Hamilton theorem to find matrix inverses and compute matrix powers.

8. Solve application problems involving eigenvalues in students and scientific contexts.
