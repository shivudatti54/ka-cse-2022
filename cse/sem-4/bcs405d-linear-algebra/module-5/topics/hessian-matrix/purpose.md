# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define the Hessian Matrix as the matrix of second-order partial derivatives of a scalar-valued multivariable function.

2. Compute the Hessian Matrix for functions of two or more variables by correctly calculating all second-order partial derivatives.

3. Explain the relationship between the Hessian Matrix, the gradient, and the Jacobian in multivariable calculus.

4. Apply the second-order Taylor series expansion to approximate multivariable functions using the Hessian Matrix.

5. Classify critical points as local minima, local maxima, or saddle points using the definiteness of the Hessian Matrix at that point.

6. Use Sylvester's criterion to determine whether a symmetric Hessian Matrix is positive definite, negative definite, or indefinite.

7. Interpret eigenvalues of the Hessian Matrix and relate them to the curvature and nature of critical points.

8. Apply these concepts to solve optimization problems commonly encountered in students applications.
