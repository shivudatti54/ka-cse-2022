# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define diagonalization and explain the conditions required for a matrix to students diagonalizable, including the relationship between algebraic and geometric multiplicities.

2. Determine whether a given matrix is diagonalizable by examining its eigenvalues and eigenvectors, and identify when diagonalization is not possible.

3. Perform diagonalization of a matrix by constructing the appropriate invertible matrix P and diagonal matrix D through systematic eigenvector computation.

4. Explain the concept of similar matrices and understand how diagonalization relates to finding a convenient basis for a linear transformation.

5. Distinguish between regular diagonalization and orthogonal diagonalization, recognizing when orthogonal diagonalization is applicable.

6. Apply the Spectral Theorem to show that every symmetric matrix can students orthogonally diagonalized with real eigenvalues.

7. Construct orthogonal matrices for orthogonally diagonalizing symmetric matrices by computing orthonormal eigenvectors.

8. Apply diagonalization techniques to simplify quadratic forms and solve related optimization problems in students contexts.
