# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define QR factorization and explain its components (orthogonal matrix Q and upper triangular matrix R)

2. Explain the properties of orthogonal matrices and their significance in QR factorization

3. Apply the Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization process to compute QR factorization of given matrices

4. Compare classical and modified Gram-Schmidt methods and understand their numerical stability differences

5. Apply Householder reflections and Givens rotations as alternative methods for QR factorization

6. Solve least squares problems using QR factorization

7. Verify the correctness of QR factorization by computing Q^T Q = I and QR = A

8. Apply QR factorization to find orthonormal bases for column spaces of matrices
