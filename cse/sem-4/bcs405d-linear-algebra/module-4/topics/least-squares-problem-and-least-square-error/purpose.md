# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define the least squares problem and explain why it arises in practical applications where exact solutions do not exist.

2. Formulate the least squares problem mathematically and derive the normal equations from the minimization of the residual norm.

3. Interpret the least squares solution geometrically as an orthogonal projection of the vector b onto the column space of A.

4. Determine conditions for existence and uniqueness of least squares solutions based on the rank of matrix A.

5. Solve least squares problems using three different methods: normal equations, QR decomposition, and SVD pseudoinverse.

6. Compute the least square error and interpret its significance in evaluating model fit.

7. Apply least squares methodology to curve fitting problems, particularly linear regression.

8. Distinguish between full rank and rank-deficient cases and handle each appropriately using pseudoinverse concepts.
