# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and identify orthogonal vectors in ℝⁿ using the dot product criterion
2. Distinguish between orthogonal sets and orthonormal sets with appropriate examples
3. Prove that any orthogonal set of non-zero vectors is linearly independent
4. Apply the Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization process to convert any set of linearly independent vectors into an orthogonal (or orthonormal) basis
5. Compute coordinates of vectors relative to orthogonal and orthonormal bases efficiently
6. Understand the concept of orthogonal complements and their properties
7. Interpret the QR decomposition in terms of the Gram-Schmidt process
8. Apply orthogonal bases to solve least squares problems in students applications
