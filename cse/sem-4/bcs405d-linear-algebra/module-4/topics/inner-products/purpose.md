# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and identify inner products on real and complex vector spaces, verifying the axioms.

2. Compute norms induced by inner products and apply the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality to estimate or bound inner products.

3. Calculate angles between nonzero vectors in inner product spaces using the inner product and induced norm.

4. Apply the Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization process to convert any linearly independent set into an orthogonal set and subsequently normalize to obtain an orthonormal basis.

5. Determine orthogonal complements of subspaces and verify the direct sum decomposition V = W ⊕ W⊥.

6. Solve best approximation problems by finding orthogonal projections onto subspaces.

7. Distinguish between orthogonal and orthonormal sets and apply their properties in computations.
