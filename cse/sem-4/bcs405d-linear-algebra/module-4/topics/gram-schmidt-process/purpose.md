# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and explain the inner product, norm of a vector, orthogonal vectors, and orthonormal vectors with mathematical precision.

2. Apply the projection formula to find the projection of one vector onto another in ℝⁿ.

3. Execute the Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization process step-by-step to convert a set of linearly independent vectors into an orthogonal set.

4. Normalize orthogonal vectors to obtain an orthonormal basis for a subspace.

5. Verify the orthogonality of the resulting vectors using dot products.

6. Connect the Gram-Schmidt process to QR decomposition of matrices and explain how Q and R are obtained.

7. Apply the Gram-Schmidt process to solve practical problems involving orthogonal bases in students contexts.
