# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and explain the four axioms of an inner product and distinguish between real and complex inner product spaces.

2. Compute norms, distances, and angles between vectors using the appropriate inner product formulas.

3. Apply the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality to verify relationships between vectors and prove inequalities in various contexts.

4. Apply the Triangle inequality to find bounds on sums of vector norms.

5. Determine whether a given function defines a valid inner product by verifying all axioms.

6. Use the Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization process to convert any linearly independent set into an orthogonal or orthonormal basis.

7. Compute and interpret orthogonal complements of subspaces, including verifying that a vector belongs to the orthogonal complement.

8. Apply the Best Approximation Theorem to find the orthogonal projection of a vector onto a subspace and understand its properties.

9. Distinguish between orthogonal and orthonormal sets and perform normalization to obtain orthonormal vectors.

10. Apply inner product concepts to solve practical problems in least-squares approximation and coordinate transformations.
