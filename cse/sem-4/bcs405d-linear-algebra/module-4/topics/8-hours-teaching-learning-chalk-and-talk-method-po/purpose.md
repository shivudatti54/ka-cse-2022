# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and verify inner products on various vector spaces, recognizing when a function qualifies as an inner product.
2. Apply the properties of inner products to prove fundamental inequalities including Cauchy-Schwarz, triangle, and Minkowski inequalities.
3. Construct orthogonal and orthonormal bases using the Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization process for any set of linearly independent vectors.
4. Compute QR factorization of matrices and understand the relationship between orthogonal matrices and preservation of norms.
5. Determine orthogonal projections onto subspaces and derive projection matrices from orthonormal bases.
6. Formulate and solve least squares problems using normal equations and orthogonal projection techniques.
7. Apply inner product space concepts to practical problems including data fitting and solving inconsistent linear systems.
8. Interpret geometrically the concepts of orthogonality, projection, and approximation within inner product spaces.
