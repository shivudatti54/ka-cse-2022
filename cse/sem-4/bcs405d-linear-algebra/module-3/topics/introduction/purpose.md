# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define eigenvalues, eigenvectors, and eigenspaces for a square matrix, articulating their precise mathematical meaning.

2. Compute the characteristic polynomial of a matrix by evaluating det(A - λI).

3. Solve the characteristic equation to determine all eigenvalues of a given matrix.

4. Find eigenvectors corresponding to each eigenvalue by solving the homogeneous system (A - λI)v = 0.

5. Distinguish between algebraic multiplicity and geometric multiplicity of an eigenvalue, and explain their relationship.

6. Apply the trace and determinant properties to verify computed eigenvalues.

7. Interpret the geometric significance of eigenvectors as vectors that retain their direction under a linear transformation.

8. Identify cases where a matrix may have complex eigenvalues and understand when such eigenvalues arise.
