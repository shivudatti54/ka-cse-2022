# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and distinguish between algebraic multiplicity and geometric multiplicity of eigenvalues in a square matrix.

2. Explain the concept of generalized eigenvectors and their relationship to Jordan chains.

3. Construct Jordan blocks and Jordan matrices for given eigenvalues with specified multiplicities.

4. Apply the Jordan Canonical Form theorem to find the Jordan form of a given square matrix.

5. Determine whether a matrix is diagonalizable by comparing geometric and algebraic multiplicities.

6. Use the Cayley-Hamilton theorem to verify that a matrix satisfies its characteristic polynomial and simplify matrix computations.

7. Compute the minimal polynomial of a matrix and relate it to the Jordan Canonical Form.

8. Understand the invariance of eigenvalues, characteristic polynomial, and minimal polynomial under similarity transformations.
