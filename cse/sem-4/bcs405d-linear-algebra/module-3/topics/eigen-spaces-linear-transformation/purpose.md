# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define eigenvalues, eigenvectors, and eigenspaces for linear transformations and matrices, understanding their geometric interpretations.

2. Compute the characteristic polynomial of a square matrix and solve the characteristic equation to find all eigenvalues.

3. Determine the eigenspace corresponding to a given eigenvalue by finding the null space of (A - λI).

4. Distinguish between algebraic multiplicity and geometric multiplicity of eigenvalues and apply their relationship (GM ≤ AM).

5. Verify whether a given matrix is diagonalizable using the sufficient and necessary conditions involving eigenvectors.

6. Construct the diagonalization of a matrix by finding the matrix of eigenvectors P and the corresponding diagonal matrix D.

7. Apply the Cayley-Hamilton theorem to verify that a matrix satisfies its own characteristic equation and use it for computational purposes.

8. Solve applied problems involving eigen-spaces in fields such as students systems, quantum mechanics, and data analysis.
