# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and compute the characteristic polynomial of a given square matrix using the determinant method.

2. State and explain the Cayley-Hamilton Theorem and understand its significance in matrix theory.

3. Verify the Cayley-Hamilton Theorem for 2×2 and 3×3 matrices by direct computation.

4. Apply the Cayley-Hamilton Theorem to compute high powers of matrices efficiently.

5. Find the inverse of an invertible matrix using the Cayley-Hamilton Theorem.

6. Distinguish between characteristic polynomial and minimal polynomial and understand their relationship.

7. Evaluate polynomial expressions of matrices by substituting the matrix into the polynomial.
