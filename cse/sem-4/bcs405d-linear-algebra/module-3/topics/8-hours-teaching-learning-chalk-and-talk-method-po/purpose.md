# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define eigenvalues and eigenvectors and explain their physical and geometric interpretation in linear transformations

2. Compute the characteristic polynomial of a matrix and determine eigenvalues from the characteristic equation

3. Find eigenvectors corresponding to each eigenvalue and construct the eigenspaces

4. Apply the Cayley-Hamilton theorem to compute matrix powers and matrix inverses

5. Determine whether a given matrix is diagonalizable and perform diagonalization when possible

6. Classify quadratic forms as positive definite, negative definite, positive semi-definite, negative semi-definite, or indefinite

7. Apply eigenvalues and eigenvectors to solve practical engineering problems in vibration analysis, stability determination, and data science applications
