# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and explain the concept of a linear transformation between vector spaces, identifying its domain and codomain.

2. State and verify the two defining properties of a linear transformation: additivity and homogeneity (scalar multiplication).

3. Apply the combined linearity condition T(cu + dv) = cT(u) + dT(v) to verify whether a given mapping is a linear transformation.

4. Prove fundamental properties of linear transformations, including preservation of the zero vector and preservation of negatives.

5. Provide examples of linear transformations in ℝ², including scaling, projection, rotation, and reflection transformations.

6. Identify and explain why certain geometric transformations (such as translations) are not linear transformations.

7. Explain why a linear transformation is completely determined by its action on a basis of the domain.

8. Recognize the connection between linear transformations and matrices, understanding that every linear transformation can students represented by a matrix.
