# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define a linear transformation T: R² → R² and verify whether a given function satisfies the linearity properties of additivity and homogeneity.

2. Represent geometric transformations including rotation, scaling, shearing, reflection, and projection as 2×2 matrices using the standard basis.

3. Apply linear transformations to vectors in R² through matrix-vector multiplication and interpret the results geometrically.

4. Compose two or more linear transformations by multiplying their corresponding matrices in the correct order.

5. Determine whether a linear transformation is invertible by computing the determinant of its matrix, and find the inverse transformation when it exists.

6. Analyze geometric properties of transformations such as area preservation (determinant magnitude), angle preservation (orthogonal matrices), and fixed points.

7. Apply linear transformations to geometric figures in the plane and describe the resulting transformations in terms of rotation, scaling, and shear components.
