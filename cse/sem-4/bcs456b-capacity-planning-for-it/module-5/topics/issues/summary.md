# Issues in Capacity Planning for IT - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Capacity Planning**: The process of determining the IT resources required to meet current and future business demands
- **Capacity Gap**: The difference between required capacity and available capacity
- **Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)**: Complete cost of acquiring, operating, and maintaining IT resources
- **Auto-scaling**: Automated adjustment of computing resources based on demand
- **Elasticity**: The ability of cloud resources to scale up and down dynamically

## Important Formulas and Metrics

- **Utilization Rate** = (Actual Usage / Available Capacity) × 100
- **Capacity Growth Rate** = (Future Capacity - Current Capacity) / Current Capacity
- **TCO** = Capital Costs + Operating Costs + Maintenance Costs + Migration Costs

## Key Points

- Forecasting accuracy is compromised by insufficient data, rapid technological changes, and unexpected business events
- Cost issues include underutilization (15-30% typical), oversubscription penalties, and vendor lock-in
- Scalability challenges involve choosing between vertical and horizontal scaling approaches
- Technology obsolescence creates ongoing migration risks and skill gap challenges
- Organizational issues such as lack of executive support and siloed approaches impede effective capacity planning
- Cloud vs. on-premise decisions involve complex trade-offs between cost, control, security, and compliance
- Data center constraints include space limitations, power/cooling requirements, and network bandwidth
- Reactive rather than proactive approaches lead to firefighting and emergency procurement

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Ignoring hidden costs**: Only considering initial procurement costs without factoring in maintenance, energy, and support expenses
2. **Poor forecasting models**: Relying solely on linear projections without accounting for seasonal variations or business events
3. **Neglecting scalability requirements**: Purchasing fixed-capacity solutions without considering future growth
4. **Ignoring organizational factors**: Focusing only on technical solutions without addressing process and people issues

## Revision Tips

- Create a comparison chart of cloud vs. on-premise advantages and disadvantages
- Memorize the key capacity planning metrics and their formulas
- Review real-world case studies of capacity planning failures and successes
- Practice answering exam questions that require analyzing capacity scenarios and recommending solutions
- Focus on understanding the interrelationships between different capacity planning issues
