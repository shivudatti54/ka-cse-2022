# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and differentiate between fundamental capacity planning terms including capacity, performance, throughput, response time, and utilization

2. Explain resource-specific capacity measurements for CPU, memory, storage, and network components in IT infrastructure

3. Apply workload characterization concepts including service demand, workload intensity, and think time in capacity analysis

4. Interpret basic queueing theory concepts such as queues, bottlenecks, service rates, and their relationship to system performance

5. Distinguish between horizontal and vertical scalability and recommend appropriate scaling strategies for different scenarios

6. Explain the relationship between Service Level Agreements (SLAs) and capacity planning requirements

7. Analyze simple capacity scenarios to calculate resource utilization and identify bottleneck resources

8. Apply growth rate projections to determine when capacity upgrades will students required in enterprise systems
