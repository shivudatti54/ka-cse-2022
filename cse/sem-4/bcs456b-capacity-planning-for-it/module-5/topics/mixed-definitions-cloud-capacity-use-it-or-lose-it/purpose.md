# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define cloud capacity and explain its components including CPU, memory, storage, and network resources.

2. Describe the "use it or lose it" principle and its implications for cloud cost management in IT organizations.

3. Differentiate between various cloud pricing models: on-demand, reserved, and spot instances with their respective advantages and disadvantages.

4. Analyze workload characteristics to determine appropriate capacity reservation strategies for different types of applications.

5. Calculate cost comparisons between different cloud provisioning models to make financially optimal decisions.

6. Apply the concept of mixed definitions to classify cloud capacity as physical versus virtual, allocated versus consumed, and committed versus uncommitted.

7. Design a multi-tier capacity strategy that optimizes costs while maintaining required performance levels for enterprise applications.

8. Evaluate capacity planning decisions and identify potential waste scenarios in cloud environments using the "use it or lose it" framework.
