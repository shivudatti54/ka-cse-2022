# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the purpose and importance of capacity tracking tools in IT infrastructure management and capacity planning processes.

2. Differentiate between agent-based and agentless capacity tracking approaches, including their advantages, limitations, and appropriate use cases.

3. Identify and describe the key metrics tracked by capacity tracking tools for compute, storage, network, and application resources.

4. Explain the essential features of modern capacity tracking tools, including real-time monitoring, historical analysis, alerting, and reporting capabilities.

5. Apply knowledge of capacity tracking concepts to implement basic monitoring for typical IT infrastructure scenarios.

6. Analyze capacity tracking data to identify utilization trends and make informed capacity planning recommendations.

7. Understand the unique capacity tracking challenges in virtualized, cloud, and containerized environments.
