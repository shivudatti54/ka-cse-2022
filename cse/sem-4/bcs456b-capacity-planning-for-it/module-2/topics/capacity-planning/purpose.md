# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define capacity in the context of IT infrastructure and explain its significance in service delivery.

2. Differentiate between various types of capacity—processing, storage, network, and memory—and identify appropriate measurement units for each.

3. Distinguish between capacity planning (strategic, future-oriented) and capacity management (operational, current utilization).

4. Describe the capacity planning lifecycle and explain the three levels: business, service, and resource capacity planning.

5. Classify workloads as CPU-bound, I/O-bound, memory-bound, or network-bound, and determine appropriate capacity planning strategies for each type.

6. Apply various capacity planning techniques including trend analysis, benchmarking, modeling, and threshold-based approaches to solve practical problems.

7. Calculate required server, storage, and network resources given workload parameters such as concurrent users, request rates, and resource consumption per user.

8. Establish performance baselines and utilize benchmarking data to make informed capacity planning decisions.
