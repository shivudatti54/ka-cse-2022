# Buying Stuff: IT Procurement and Capacity Planning - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Make vs. Buy Decision:** Strategic analysis determining whether to develop IT capabilities internally or procure them from external vendors based on factors like cost, control, customization, and strategic importance.
- **Total Cost of Ownership (TCO):** Comprehensive cost analysis including acquisition, operational, infrastructure, upgrade, and hidden costs over the entire lifecycle of an IT solution.
- **Capital Expenditure (CapEx):** Large upfront investments in physical infrastructure, typical of traditional on-premise procurement.
- **Operational Expenditure (OpEx):** Pay-as-you-go model, typical of cloud computing services, offering flexibility and lower upfront costs.
- **Service Level Agreement (SLA):** Contract defining expected service levels including uptime guarantees, response times, and penalty structures.
- **Vendor Evaluation:** Systematic process to assess potential suppliers based on technical capability, financial stability, cost, support, security, and references.

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **TCO = Acquisition Costs + Operational Costs + Infrastructure Costs + Upgrade Costs + Hidden Costs**
- **Weighted Score = Σ (Rating × Weight)** for each vendor in evaluation matrix
- **Three Nines (99.9%) Uptime = Maximum 8.76 hours downtime per year**
- **Cloud Cost Savings = On-Premise TCO - Cloud TCO**

## Key Points

- Make decisions favor internal development when customization, control, and strategic importance are high; buy decisions favor external procurement when speed, scalability, and lower TCO are priorities.
- TCO analysis must include hidden costs like downtime, productivity loss, and compliance penalties, which are often underestimated.
- Cloud models (IaaS, PaaS, SaaS) offer different levels of responsibility transfer from customer to vendor.
- Vendor evaluation should use weighted scoring to ensure objective, documented decision-making.
- SLAs with clear penalty structures protect organizations from vendor underperformance.
- Procurement decisions must directly align with capacity planning forecasts to prevent underutilization or resource shortages.
- Hybrid approaches combining on-premise and cloud resources often provide optimal balance of control and flexibility.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Ignoring hidden costs:** Many organizations focus only on acquisition costs and underestimate operational and maintenance expenses.
- **Choosing based on lowest price only:** The cheapest option rarely provides the best value when TCO is considered.
- **Overlooking vendor financial stability:** A vendor going out of business can create significant migration costs and service disruption.
- **Not considering scalability needs:** Procurement for current requirements without future growth projections leads to premature replacement.

## Revision Tips

- Practice TCO calculations for both on-premise and cloud scenarios to understand cost drivers.
- Memorize the three cloud service models (IaaS, PaaS, SaaS) and their characteristics.
- Review sample vendor evaluation matrices to understand weighted scoring application.
- Remember key SLA metrics—three nines (99.9%) means ~8.76 hours annual downtime.
- Focus on understanding when to recommend make vs. buy decisions based on given scenarios.
