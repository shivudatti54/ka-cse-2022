# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and differentiate between performance and capacity in the context of IT infrastructure management

2. Identify and explain key performance metrics including response time, throughput, utilization, latency, availability, and error rate

3. Describe the capacity planning lifecycle and explain the importance of each phase: monitoring, analysis, modeling, and implementation

4. Analyze workload characteristics and their impact on system performance and capacity requirements

5. Perform basic calculations for system utilization, throughput, and percentile response times

6. Interpret performance monitoring data to identify bottlenecks and capacity constraints

7. Apply capacity planning principles to recommend appropriate scaling strategies (vertical and horizontal)

8. Evaluate SLA compliance using statistical methods and determine required performance improvements

9. Understand the relationship between performance baselines, capacity triggers, and proactive capacity management

10. Develop foundational skills for implementing effective performance and capacity management practices in enterprise IT environments
