# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the concept of capacity planning in IT infrastructure and its importance in meeting business requirements

2. Describe the SMART framework for setting effective capacity goals and apply it to real-world scenarios

3. Establish baseline measurements for various IT components including servers, storage, and network systems

4. Differ between performance thresholds (warning and critical) and explain their role in capacity management

5. Identify and calculate key performance indicators (KPIs) used in capacity planning such as utilization, availability, and response time

6. Align capacity goals with Service Level Agreements (SLAs) and business objectives

7. Analyze capacity requirements and set appropriate goals for different IT scenarios

8. Understand the importance of proactive versus reactive capacity management and the benefits of goal-driven planning
