# Setting Goals for Capacity - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Capacity Planning**: The process of determining IT resources required to meet current and future workload demands
- **Baseline**: Reference measurement of normal system performance used for comparison
- **Threshold**: Predefined limit that triggers alerts or corrective actions when exceeded
- **SLA (Service Level Agreement)**: Formal contract defining expected service levels between provider and customer
- **KPI (Key Performance Indicator)**: Quantifiable measure used to evaluate success in meeting objectives

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Availability** = (Total Time - Downtime) / Total Time × 100%
- **Utilization** = (Resource Used / Resource Available) × 100%
- **Growth Rate** = (Current Value - Previous Value) / Previous Value × 100%
- **Throughput** = Number of requests processed / Time unit
- **Response Time** = Service time + Wait time

## Key Points

- SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) provide a structured approach to capacity goal setting
- Baselines must account for various operational conditions including peak hours, off-peak hours, and seasonal variations
- Capacity goals should align with both technical capabilities and business requirements
- Warning thresholds (yellow) indicate potential issues requiring monitoring; critical thresholds (red) require immediate action
- KPIs such as CPU utilization, memory usage, response time, and availability are essential for measuring capacity performance
- Proactive capacity management is more cost-effective than reactive approaches
- Capacity planning involves balancing performance requirements against budget constraints to avoid over-provisioning or under-provisioning

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Setting vague or immeasurable goals without specific targets
- Confusing baseline measurements with threshold values
- Ignoring business objectives when establishing technical capacity goals
- Failing to account for growth projections and future requirements

## Revision Tips

- Memorize the SMART framework and be able to explain each characteristic with an example
- Practice calculating availability percentages and utilization rates from given data
- Review the difference between proactive and reactive capacity management approaches
- Understand how SLAs and capacity goals are interrelated in IT service management
