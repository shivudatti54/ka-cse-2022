# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the cattle versus pets analogy in IT infrastructure management and its significance in modern computing environments.

2. Distinguish between pet-based (traditional) and cattle-based (cloud-native) approaches to server and infrastructure management.

3. Identify characteristics that define each model, including naming conventions, provisioning methods, scaling approaches, and operational practices.

4. Analyze how capacity planning methodologies differ between pet and cattle environments, including forecasting techniques and resource allocation strategies.

5. Evaluate scenarios where each approach is appropriate and understand the concept of hybrid infrastructure models.

6. Apply the cattle versus pets framework to make informed decisions about infrastructure design and capacity management in enterprise environments.

7. Connect this foundational concept to modern technologies including containers, orchestration platforms, and cloud-native architectures.

8. Assess the operational, financial, and strategic implications of choosing between pet and cattle models for different types of IT workloads.
