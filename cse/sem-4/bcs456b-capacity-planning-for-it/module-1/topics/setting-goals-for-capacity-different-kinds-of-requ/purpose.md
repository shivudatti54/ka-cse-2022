# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Differentiate between business requirements, user requirements, technical requirements, and operational requirements in the context of IT capacity planning.

2. Explain the relationship between various types of capacity requirements including throughput, response time, availability, scalability, and storage requirements.

3. Apply the SMART framework to set effective and measurable capacity goals for IT services.

4. Analyze given business scenarios to identify and document capacity requirements from stated business needs.

5. Calculate appropriate capacity targets based on current baseline measurements and projected growth factors.

6. Translate qualitative requirements into quantitative capacity goals with specific thresholds and metrics.

7. Understand the importance of buffer capacity and growth margins in capacity planning decisions.

8. Evaluate capacity goals for their alignment with organizational objectives and feasibility of implementation.
