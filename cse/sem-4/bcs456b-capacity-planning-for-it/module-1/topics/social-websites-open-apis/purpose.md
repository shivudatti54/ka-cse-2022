# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Analyze the unique characteristics of social website traffic patterns and their implications for capacity planning, including the phenomenon of flash crowds and viral content effects.

2. Explain how open APIs create interdependencies between systems and describe the capacity planning challenges arising from third-party API integrations.

3. Calculate appropriate API rate limits based on total system capacity, number of consumers, and business requirements using tiered allocation strategies.

4. Evaluate the cascading effects of API dependencies in micro-service architectures and determine capacity requirements for each service in the chain.

5. Design capacity planning strategies that account for the unpredictable nature of social media-driven traffic, including caching, auto-scaling, and geographic distribution considerations.

6. Compare horizontal and vertical scaling approaches in the context of social media application requirements and determine appropriate scenarios for each.

7. Assess the cost implications of variable traffic patterns driven by social media and develop strategies for optimizing resource utilization while maintaining performance.
