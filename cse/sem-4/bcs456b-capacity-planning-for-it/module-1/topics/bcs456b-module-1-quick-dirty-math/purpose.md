# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the concept and importance of quick estimation techniques in IT capacity planning, including their advantages and limitations compared to detailed analytical methods.

2. Apply Little's Law (L = λ × W) to solve practical capacity planning problems involving arrival rates, response times, and concurrent user estimates.

3. Calculate the relationship between system utilization and response time, understanding why response time increases exponentially as utilization approaches 100%.

4. Utilize the Power of 10 (order of magnitude) approach for rough capacity estimates when precise data is unavailable.

5. Apply the 80/20 rule (Pareto principle) to simplify capacity estimation problems by focusing on critical workloads.

6. Perform quick capacity calculations for common IT components including web servers, database systems, storage, and network infrastructure.

7. Determine appropriate safety margins and scaling factors when making capacity recommendations based on quick estimation methods.

8. Evaluate when quick estimation techniques are appropriate versus when detailed analytical approaches are necessary.
