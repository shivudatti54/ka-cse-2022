# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define what constitutes a process in the context of IT capacity planning and identify its five essential components.

2. Differentiate between Business Capacity Planning, Resource Capacity Planning, and Service Capacity Planning at strategic, tactical, and operational levels.

3. Apply process modeling techniques including flowcharts, queuing theory, and simulation models to represent IT capacity planning activities.

4. Calculate and interpret key process metrics such as throughput, utilization, response time, and queue length using formulas like Little's Law.

5. Design and analyze a complete capacity planning process flow from data collection through monitoring and review.

6. Identify process bottlenecks and apply optimization techniques such as right-sizing, workload characterization, and automation.

7. Evaluate capacity planning scenarios and recommend appropriate solutions based on process analysis and performance metrics.

8. Align capacity planning processes with business objectives and ITIL framework principles for effective IT service management.
