# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Differentiate between functional, non-functional, business, and technical requirements in IT capacity planning contexts.

2. Explain the concept of workload characterization and identify different workload components.

3. Apply performance measurement techniques including response time, throughput, utilization, and latency.

4. Calculate system availability using MTBF and MTTR values.

5. Interpret resource utilization metrics for CPU, memory, storage, and network components.

6. Establish baseline measurements and define appropriate threshold values for monitoring.

7. Analyze requirements to determine capacity planning implications for IT infrastructure.

8. Evaluate the relationship between business requirements and technical capacity decisions.
