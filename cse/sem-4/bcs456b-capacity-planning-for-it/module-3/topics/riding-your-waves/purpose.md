# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define the concept of "waves" in IT capacity planning and explain how they represent demand fluctuations in IT services.

2. Identify and categorize different types of demand waves (daily, weekly, seasonal, event-driven) with examples from real-world IT scenarios.

3. Explain demand management strategies including demand shaping, demand forecasting, and their practical applications in IT environments.

4. Compare and contrast different capacity scaling approaches: vertical scaling, horizontal scaling, and elastic scaling.

5. Apply wave riding techniques such as buffer management, load balancing, and throttling to solve capacity planning challenges.

6. Design a basic capacity management solution for a given scenario, considering cost optimization and service quality requirements.

7. Analyze the relationship between demand patterns, capacity planning decisions, and business outcomes in IT service management.

8. Evaluate the effectiveness of different auto-scaling configurations for various workload patterns.
