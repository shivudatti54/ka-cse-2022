# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the role and importance of measurement in the capacity planning process for IT infrastructure.

2. Identify and describe the key metrics used in capacity planning, including utilization, throughput, response time, and availability metrics.

3. Classify different types of IT workloads (batch, interactive, periodic, growth) and explain how each type affects measurement requirements.

4. Compare and contrast various measurement techniques including direct monitoring, synthetic transaction monitoring, and statistical sampling.

5. Apply measurement data to calculate current resource utilization and project future capacity requirements using growth rate analysis.

6. Evaluate measurement challenges in modern IT environments including virtualization, cloud computing, and distributed architectures.

7. Interpret measurement data to identify capacity constraints and recommend appropriate capacity planning actions.
