# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Analyze real-world capacity planning scenarios and identify factors that affect planning in practice versus theoretical models.

2. Apply capacity calculation formulas to determine current utilization, projected growth, and timeline for capacity exhaustion.

3. Distinguish between different capacity planning strategies (lead, lag, and match) and select appropriate approaches for given scenarios.

4. Identify common mistakes in capacity planning such as ignoring growth trends, underestimating seasonal variations, and neglecting non-functional requirements.

5. Evaluate capacity requirements for different IT infrastructure components including web servers, databases, and network bandwidth.

6. Understand the organizational and human factors that impact capacity planning implementation in real-world environments.

7. Design capacity planning solutions that consider both technical requirements and practical constraints like budget, tooling, and organizational processes.

8. Recommend appropriate capacity planning responses for scenarios involving cloud migration, unexpected traffic spikes, and shared infrastructure challenges.
