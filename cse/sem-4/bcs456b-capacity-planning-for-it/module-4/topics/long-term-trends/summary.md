# Long-Term Trends in IT Capacity Planning - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Long-term capacity planning**: Strategic planning approach addressing infrastructure requirements over 3-5+ year horizons, incorporating technology evolution and business growth projections.
- **Exponential data growth**: The accelerating increase in data volume generated by organizations, driving storage and processing capacity demands.
- **Hybrid cloud architecture**: Infrastructure combining on-premises data centers with cloud services, offering flexibility and control.
- **Edge computing**: Distributed computing model placing processing resources closer to data sources to reduce latency and bandwidth consumption.
- **PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness)**: Metric measuring data center energy efficiency, calculated as total facility energy divided by IT equipment energy.

## Important Formulas and Concepts

- **Capacity growth projection**: Future capacity = Current capacity × (1 + growth rate)^years
- **Storage growth calculation**: Consider both data volume increase and retention period expansion
- **AI workload capacity**: GPU/TPU requirements for training vs. inference phases differ significantly
- **Edge capacity aggregation**: Sum of distributed edge node requirements plus inter-edge connectivity

## Key Points

- Long-term planning requires understanding technology trends beyond immediate needs
- Cloud evolution has shifted planning from static provisioning to elastic, dynamic resource management
- AI/ML workloads create new capacity challenges requiring specialized hardware
- Edge computing distributes resources but increases infrastructure complexity
- Green computing and energy efficiency are now critical planning considerations
- Hybrid architectures combining multiple approaches provide optimal flexibility
- Trend analysis combines historical data with market research and technology forecasting

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Applying linear growth projections to exponentially growing data requirements
2. Ignoring the distinct resource patterns of AI/ML workloads versus traditional applications
3. Overlooking sustainability metrics in capacity planning decisions
4. Failing to account for distributed architecture complexity in edge computing scenarios

## Revision Tips

1. Focus on understanding how each trend (cloud, AI, edge, sustainability) impacts capacity planning differently
2. Review the three worked examples to understand practical application of trend analysis
3. Memorize key metrics like PUE and understand their significance
4. Practice projecting capacity requirements using growth rate calculations
5. Understand the distinction between scalability (growth capability) and capacity (maximum load)
