# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define calibration in the context of IT capacity planning and explain its significance in ensuring measurement accuracy.

2. Distinguish between different types of calibration: instrument calibration, workload calibration, and model calibration.

3. Describe the systematic five-step calibration process and explain each step's importance.

4. Apply calibration concepts to performance monitoring tools and interpret calibration results.

5. Analyze challenges that affect calibration accuracy in dynamic IT environments.

6. Evaluate the impact of calibration on capacity planning decisions and cost optimization.

7. Design appropriate calibration procedures for simple IT monitoring scenarios.

8. Assess when calibration is needed and determine suitable calibration frequency based on system characteristics.
