# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define prototype fidelity and explain its significance in the UX design process.

2. Distinguish between low-fidelity, mid-fidelity, and high-fidelity prototypes with respect to their characteristics, purposes, and appropriate usage scenarios.

3. Identify the key factors that influence the selection of appropriate fidelity levels for different design stages and research objectives.

4. Analyze case studies to determine appropriate fidelity levels based on project constraints and research goals.

5. Compare various prototyping tools and their suitability for creating prototypes at different fidelity levels.

6. Evaluate the tradeoffs between fidelity levels in terms of time investment, resource requirements, and quality of user feedback.

7. Apply knowledge of prototype fidelity to make informed decisions throughout the UX design iteration process.
