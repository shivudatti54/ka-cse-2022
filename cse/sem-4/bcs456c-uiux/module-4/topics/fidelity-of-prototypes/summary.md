# Fidelity of Prototypes - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Prototype Fidelity:** The degree to which a prototype resembles the final product in visual detail, interactivity, and functionality
- **Low-Fidelity (Lo-Fi):** Simple, abstract representations using paper, sketches, or basic wireframes; focus on functionality and layout
- **Mid-Fidelity:** Balanced prototypes with interactive wireframes but limited visual styling; suitable for testing navigation and flows
- **High-Fidelity (Hi-Fi):** Polished, realistic prototypes with complete visual design, animations, and functional interactions

## Important Formulas and Principles

- **Fidelity Selection Principle:** Match fidelity level to research objectives—concept validation requires lower fidelity, final validation requires higher fidelity
- **Cost-Benefit Tradeoff:** Higher fidelity yields more realistic feedback but demands greater time and resource investment

## Key Points

1. Prototyping fidelity exists on a spectrum from simple paper sketches to fully functional interactive prototypes

2. Low-fidelity prototyping encourages creativity by reducing stakeholder attachment to specific solutions

3. Early-stage design decisions should be validated with lower fidelity before investing in high-fidelity development

4. High-fidelity prototypes are essential for stakeholder buy-in and investor presentations

5. The appropriate fidelity level depends on project phase, research objectives, time constraints, and audience

6. Modern tools like Figma, Adobe XD, and Balsamiq support creation at all fidelity levels

7. Testing with users at appropriate fidelity levels prevents costly redesigns in later development stages

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Using high-fidelity prototypes too early when fundamental concepts remain unvalidated
- Using low-fidelity prototypes for stakeholder presentations requiring visual design approval
- Skipping low-fidelity testing entirely and jumping directly to high-fidelity designs
- Confusing fidelity with quality—both high and low fidelity have appropriate applications

## Revision Tips

1. Create a comparison table listing all three fidelity levels with characteristics, tools, and use cases

2. Practice selecting appropriate fidelity levels for given scenario descriptions

3. Remember that fidelity progression typically follows: low → mid → high as design decisions solidify

4. Focus on the core principle: use the minimum fidelity necessary to achieve your research objectives
