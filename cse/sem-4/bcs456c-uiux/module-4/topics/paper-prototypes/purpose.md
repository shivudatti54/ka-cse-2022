# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define paper prototypes and explain their significance in the UI/UX design process
2. Identify and differentiate between various types of paper prototypes (static, partially interactive, fully interactive)
3. Describe the paper prototyping process from preparation through testing and iteration
4. Compare the advantages and limitations of paper prototypes against digital prototyping methods
5. Apply paper prototyping techniques to create testable mockups for simple interface designs
6. Conduct effective user testing sessions using paper prototypes and document findings appropriately
7. Understand how paper prototyping supports iterative design and reduces development costs
8. Apply knowledge of paper prototypes to evaluate real-world interface designs and suggest improvements
