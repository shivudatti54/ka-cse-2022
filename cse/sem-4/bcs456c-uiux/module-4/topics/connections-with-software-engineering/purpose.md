# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the concept of design-development handoff and identify its key components and best practices for effective communication between designers and developers.

2. Analyze how design systems bridge the gap between UX design and software students, including the creation of reusable component libraries and shared design tokens.

3. Evaluate how Agile and Lean UX methodologies integrate design activities into iterative development processes and explain the benefits of this integration.

4. Assess technical constraints that influence design decisions, including performance limitations, browser compatibility, platform guidelines, and cross-device considerations.

5. Design accessible user interfaces that comply with WCAG guidelines by understanding both design principles and technical implementation requirements.

6. Apply collaborative workflows between design and students teams to resolve design feasibility challenges while maintaining user experience quality.

7. Compare different approaches to responsive design and explain how design and development teams coordinate to implement adaptive user interfaces across platforms.

8. Demonstrate understanding of how the design-students relationship evolves throughout the software development lifecycle and apply this knowledge to real-world project scenarios.
