# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define prototyping and explain its role in the UI/UX design process

2. Distinguish between low-fidelity, medium-fidelity, and high-fidelity prototypes and identify appropriate use cases for each type

3. Compare and contrast various prototyping methods including paper prototyping, clickable prototypes, and Wizard of Oz testing

4. Explain the importance of prototype testing and describe common usability testing techniques

5. Identify popular prototyping tools and understand their key features and applications

6. Understand how prototyping fits within the broader user-centered design methodology and Agile development processes

7. Apply prototyping best practices to create effective prototypes that generate meaningful user feedback

8. Evaluate prototypes critically and make informed decisions about design iterations based on testing results
