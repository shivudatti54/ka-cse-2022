# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define prototype depth and breadth and explain their significance in the UX design process.

2. Distinguish between low-fidelity and high-fidelity prototypes and identify appropriate use cases for each.

3. Analyze the trade-off relationship between prototype depth and breadth in resource-constrained design projects.

4. Evaluate different prototyping approaches based on project phase, stakeholder needs, and testing objectives.

5. Apply decision-making frameworks for selecting appropriate depth-breadth configurations for various design scenarios.

6. Create prototypes that balance depth and breadth according to specific project requirements and constraints.

7. Understand how prototyping decisions impact user testing outcomes and stakeholder communication effectiveness.

8. Recognize the iterative nature of prototyping and how depth-breadth choices evolve throughout the design lifecycle.
