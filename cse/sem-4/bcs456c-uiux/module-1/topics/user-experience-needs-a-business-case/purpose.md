# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Understand why creating a business case is essential for securing UX project investments and organizational commitment.

2. Identify and explain the five key components of a comprehensive UX business case, including executive summary, problem statement, solution description, benefits quantification, and resource requirements.

3. Apply ROI calculation formulas to quantify the financial returns from UX investments and interpret the results in business terms.

4. Analyze different types of benefits—direct and indirect—that UX improvements can generate for an organization.

5. Evaluate stakeholder perspectives and align UX business cases with various organizational priorities to gain broader support.

6. Develop competitive analysis arguments that demonstrate market necessity for UX investment.

7. Create compelling evidence-based arguments using appropriate metrics and KPIs to justify UX initiatives.
