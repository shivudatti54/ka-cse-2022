# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the importance of requirement extraction in the UI/UX design process and its impact on final product quality.

2. Compare and contrast various qualitative requirement extraction methods including user interviews, focus groups, and contextual inquiry with respect to their applications, strengths, and limitations.

3. Apply quantitative methods such as surveys and card sorting to gather user requirements and translate findings into design specifications.

4. Conduct task analysis to understand user workflows and identify opportunities for improving user experience.

5. Evaluate when to employ different requirement extraction methods based on project scope, timeline, budget, and type of information needed.

6. Synthesize findings from multiple research methods into coherent user requirements, personas, and design guidelines.

7. Understand the role of stakeholder interviews in balancing user needs with business objectives and technical constraints.

8. Apply ethical considerations in user research including informed consent, privacy protection, and unbiased data collection.
