# Emotional Impact as Part of User Experience - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Emotional Design**: Design approach that considers users' emotional responses alongside functional requirements, recognizing that emotions significantly influence user behavior and product success.
- **Visceral Level**: Immediate, pre-conscious emotional reaction to design elements occurring within milliseconds of exposure.
- **Behavioral Level**: Emotional experience derived from using a product, including feelings of control, efficiency, and satisfaction.
- **Reflective Level**: Long-term emotional connection and meaning users derive from products, including identity and values alignment.
- **Micro-interactions**: Small, subtle animations or feedback responses that add personality and emotional satisfaction to user interactions.
- **Affective Heuristics**: Mental shortcuts based on emotional responses that influence decision-making beyond purely rational evaluation.

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Norman's Emotional Design Theory**: Design impacts users at three simultaneous levels—visceral (immediate), behavioral (experiential), and reflective (meaningful). All three must work in harmony for optimal success.
- **Emotional Measurement Dimensions**: Pleasure-Arousal-Dominance (PAD) model used to quantify emotional responses in UX research.

## Key Points

1. Emotions are powerful drivers of user behavior—positive emotions increase engagement while negative emotions drive abandonment.
2. The limbic system's role in decision-making means users often choose based on feeling rather than pure rationality.
3. Micro-interactions transform mundane functional moments into opportunities for emotional delight.
4. Personalization creates emotional connection by making users feel understood and valued.
5. Color psychology influences emotional responses—blue conveys trust, orange suggests energy, green represents harmony.
6. Trust is an emotional construct built through transparent communication and professional design execution.
7. Error handling demonstrates empathy and can transform negative experiences into relationship-building opportunities.
8. Cultural context significantly moderates emotional associations with visual elements.
9. Emotional design directly impacts business metrics including retention, conversion, and brand loyalty.
10. Successful products achieve excellence across usability, functionality, AND emotional appeal simultaneously.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Focusing solely on visual aesthetics (visceral level) while neglecting functional emotional experience (behavioral level).
- Assuming emotional design means adding excessive animations or entertainment that distract from functionality.
- Ignoring cultural differences when applying emotional design principles across global audiences.
- Using manipulation tactics (dark patterns) that create short-term gains but damage long-term trust.

## Revision Tips

1. Practice explaining each of Norman's three levels with specific interface examples.
2. Review case studies of applications known for excellent emotional design (Headspace, Stripe, Duolingo).
3. Memorize the relationship between color choices and emotional associations.
4. Be prepared to critique designs from an emotional perspective in exam questions.
5. Understand that emotional design is complementary to, not a replacement for, solid usability fundamentals.
