# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain Donald Norman's three levels of emotional design (visceral, behavioral, and reflective) and provide examples of how each manifests in digital interface design.

2. Analyze the psychological mechanisms through which emotions influence user decision-making and behavior in digital environments.

3. Evaluate different methods for measuring emotional responses in UX research, including their advantages, limitations, and appropriate applications.

4. Apply design strategies such as micro-interactions, personalization, and storytelling to create emotionally resonant digital experiences.

5. Assess the role of trust, transparency, and emotional security in building lasting user relationships with digital products.

6. Design information architecture and visual elements that evoke desired emotional states while maintaining usability standards.

7. Critique existing digital products based on emotional design principles, identifying strengths and areas for emotional improvement.

8. Integrate emotional design considerations with functional requirements to create holistic user experiences that satisfy both rational and emotional user needs.
