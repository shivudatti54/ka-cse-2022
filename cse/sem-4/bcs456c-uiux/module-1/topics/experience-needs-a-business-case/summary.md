# Experience Needs a Business Case - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **UX ROI**: The measurable financial return on investment for user experience design, calculated as the ratio of benefits (revenue increase, cost savings) to the costs of implementing UX improvements.

- **Business Case**: A strategic document that justifies an investment by outlining the problem, proposed solution, costs, benefits, and expected returns.

- **Stakeholder Buy-in**: The process of gaining approval and support from decision-makers (executives, investors, team leaders) for UX initiatives.

- **Usability Metrics**: Quantitative measures including task success rate, time on task, and error rate that evaluate how effectively users can accomplish goals.

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **ROI Formula**: `ROI = ((Net Benefit / Cost) × 100)%`
- **Cart Abandonment Recovery**: Revenue recovered = (Reduction in abandonment rate × Total abandoned carts × Average order value)
- **Cost Savings from UX**: Annual savings = (Reduction in support tickets × Cost per ticket) + (Reduction in churn × Customer lifetime value)

## Key Points

1. Every rupee invested in UX can yield returns of ₹10-₹100 or more, depending on implementation quality and industry context.

2. Effective business cases translate design language into business metrics that executives understand and care about.

3. Key UX metrics include task success rate, time on task, error rate, SUS score, NPS, CSAT, and conversion rate.

4. Poor UX costs organizations through increased support tickets, customer churn, lost sales, and brand damage.

5. Different stakeholders prioritize different metrics—CFO focuses on costs/revenue, CMO on satisfaction/brand, CTO on efficiency/performance.

6. A complete business case includes problem statement, baseline metrics, proposed solution, cost estimate, projected benefits, and ROI calculation.

7. Quantitative metrics (conversion rate, task time) provide numerical data, while qualitative metrics (satisfaction ratings, user feedback) provide interpretive insights.

8. Building a business case requires establishing baseline metrics before improvements and measuring changes over time.

9. Industry research from Forrester and other analysts provides benchmarks for expected ROI that can strengthen business cases.

10. The ability to build compelling business cases is an essential skill that differentiates effective UX professionals.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Presenting UX improvements as purely aesthetic rather than strategically valuable
- Using design jargon (wireframes, prototypes, personas) when communicating with business stakeholders
- Failing to establish baseline metrics before implementing improvements
- Ignoring the concerns and priorities of different stakeholders
- Overlooking indirect costs and benefits (brand reputation, competitive advantage)
- Making unsubstantiated claims without research or data backing

## Revision Tips

1. Memorize the ROI formula and practice applying it to at least three different scenarios.

2. Create a one-page summary of all key UX metrics with their definitions and ideal values.

3. Practice explaining the business value of UX in under two minutes—you'll likely need to do this in interviews.

4. Review the three examples in this module and ensure you understand how each business case was constructed.

5. Focus on understanding the connection between UX improvements and business outcomes rather than memorizing isolated facts.
