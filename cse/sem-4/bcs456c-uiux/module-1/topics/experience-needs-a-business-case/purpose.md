# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the concept of Return on Investment (ROI) in the context of user experience design and its significance in securing organizational commitment.

2. Identify and describe at least five key metrics used to measure UX success, including task success rate, time on task, error rate, SUS, NPS, and conversion rate.

3. Analyze how improvements in user experience directly impact business outcomes such as revenue, customer retention, and cost reduction.

4. Develop a basic business case for a UX improvement project, including problem identification, cost estimation, benefit projection, and ROI calculation.

5. Communicate effectively with different stakeholders (CFO, CMO, CTO) by tailoring the business case presentation to their specific priorities and concerns.

6. Distinguish between quantitative and qualitative UX metrics and explain when each type of measurement is appropriate.

7. Evaluate the business impact of poor user experience and articulate the risks associated with neglecting UX considerations in product development.

8. Apply industry research and statistics to support business cases for UX initiatives in professional settings.
