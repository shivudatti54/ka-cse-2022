# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define usability and explain its five quality components as identified by Jakob Nielsen.

2. Describe the three key dimensions of usability: effectiveness, efficiency, and satisfaction.

3. Explain the concept of user experience and understand its broader scope compared to traditional usability.

4. Differentiate between usability and user experience, including their distinct objectives, scopes, and measurement approaches.

5. Identify and describe the key stages in the user experience design process.

6. Understand common user experience metrics including SUS, NPS, CSAT, and CES.

7. Analyze real-world design scenarios through both usability and UX perspectives.

8. Appreciate the historical evolution from usability engineering to modern user experience design.
