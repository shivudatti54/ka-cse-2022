# Usability to User Experience - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Usability**: A quality attribute measuring how easily users can accomplish tasks, defined by effectiveness (goal completion), efficiency (effort required), and satisfaction (subjective comfort).

- **User Experience (UX)**: The holistic experience of a user with a product/service, encompassing all interactions before, during, and after use. Coined by Donald Norman.

- **Usability Components**: Learnability (ease of initial learning), Efficiency (speed after learning), Memorability (knowledge retention), Errors (frequency/severity), Satisfaction (subjective comfort).

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Nielsen's Usability Heuristics**: 10 principles for evaluating interface designs (visibility of system status, match between system and real world, user control and consistency, error prevention, recognition rather than recall, flexibility and efficiency, aesthetic and minimalist design, help users recognize errors, help and documentation).

- **UX Metrics**: SUS (System Usability Scale - 0-100 score), NPS (Net Promoter Score), CSAT (Customer Satisfaction), CES (Customer Effort Score).

## Key Points

- Usability focuses on task completion; UX encompasses the entire user journey and emotional response.

- Usability is measurable through objective metrics; UX requires both quantitative and qualitative assessment.

- The shift from usability to UX represents increased recognition of emotional and experiential factors in design success.

- UX design integrates multiple disciplines: interaction design, visual design, information architecture, and user research.

- User research forms the foundation of both usability engineering and UX design processes.

- The UX design process includes: research, personas, journey mapping, information architecture, interaction design, prototyping, and testing.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Confusing usability with user experience—understand that usability is a subset of UX.

- Assuming usability alone guarantees product success—a usable but emotionally disengaging product may fail.

- Overlooking the subjective, emotional aspects of user experience in favor of purely functional concerns.

## Revision Tips

1. Memorize Nielsen's five quality components with one-line definitions for each.

2. Practice distinguishing between usability and UX scenarios in exam questions.

3. Remember the three dimensions of usability: effectiveness, efficiency, satisfaction.

4. Know the key metrics and what each measures in user experience evaluation.
