# User Personas - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **User Persona**: A fictional character representing a user type, created from research data to help teams understand target users' needs, goals, and behaviors.
- **Primary Persona**: The main target user receiving primary design focus.
- **Secondary Persona**: Important users whose needs are considered but not prioritized over primary.
- **Negative Persona**: Represents non-target users to avoid designing for wrong audience.

## Important Components of a Persona

- Demographic profile (age, occupation, location, income)
- Psychographic information (interests, values, lifestyle)
- User goals and needs
- Pain points and frustrations
- Behaviors and habits
- Technical proficiency level
- Memorable quote

## Important Formulas and Process

- **Persona Creation Process**: Research → Data Analysis → Segmentation → Development → Validation → Socialization
- **Research Methods**: Interviews, surveys, observations, contextual inquiry, analytics review

## Key Points

- Personas bridge qualitative research and design decisions
- Always based on actual user research, not assumptions
- Should be specific and memorable with names and visuals
- Guide decisions for UI, navigation, content, and visual design
- Validate and update personas periodically
- Primary persona receives most design attention
- Used throughout product lifecycle, not just in initial stages

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Creating personas based on assumptions rather than research
- Making personas too generic or similar to each other
- Ignoring negative personas entirely
- Treating personas as actual users rather than archetypes
- Not sharing personas with the entire team

## Revision Tips

- Practice creating sample personas for common apps (food delivery, banking, learning)
- Memorize the 6-step persona creation process
- Remember that personas are tools for empathy, not replace actual user testing
- Focus on goals and pain points as most actionable components
- Review previous university questions on this topic for pattern
