# Ideation in UI/UX Design - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Ideation**: The creative process of generating, developing, and communicating new ideas to solve user problems in UX design
- **Design Thinking**: A human-centered problem-solving approach that includes empathy, define, ideate, prototype, and test phases
- **Double Diamond Model**: A design framework showing the process of diverging (exploring) and converging (refining) throughout the design process
- **Solutioneering**: The premature focus on solutions without fully understanding the problem—a pitfall ideation helps avoid

## Important Formulas and Techniques

- **SCAMPER**: Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to another use, Eliminate, Reverse—a structured ideation technique
- **Idea Evaluation Criteria**: Desirability (user fit), Feasibility (technical viability), Viability (business sustainability)
- **Brainstorming Rules**: Encourage wild ideas, build on others, defer judgment, stay focused, go for quantity

## Key Points

- Ideation bridges user research and prototyping in the UX design process
- The primary goal is generating quantity of ideas before focusing on quality
- Techniques include brainstorming, SCAMPER, mind mapping, worst possible idea, sketching, bodystorming, and six thinking hats
- The ideation process follows: framing challenge → generating ideas → capturing → clustering → selecting
- Deferred judgment is essential—criticism suppresses creativity
- Evaluation uses feasibility, desirability, and viability frameworks
- Mind mapping uses visual, branching representation of connected ideas
- Bodystorming involves physically acting out user scenarios

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Judging and criticizing ideas too early in the ideation process
- Confusing ideation (generating ideas) with evaluation (selecting ideas)
- Generating too few ideas due to fear of judgment or time pressure
- Skipping the research phase and jumping directly to ideation
- Focusing on obvious solutions instead of exploring innovative alternatives

## Revision Tips

- Practice applying SCAMPER to common UX problems—you'll likely face a scenario-based question
- Remember the sequence: Research → Define → Ideate → Prototype → Test
- Understand that ideation is about "how might we" questions derived from user insights
- Review the Double Diamond model to visualize where ideation fits
- Memorize the three evaluation criteria: Feasibility, Desirability, Viability
