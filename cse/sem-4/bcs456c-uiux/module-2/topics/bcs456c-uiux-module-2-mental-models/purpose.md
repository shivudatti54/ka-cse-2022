# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define mental models and explain their significance in UX design, including how users form expectations about system behavior.

2. Identify and differentiate between types of mental models (innate, learned, and analogical) with examples from digital product contexts.

3. Explain the conceptual design phase and its key elements, including product concept, feature set, information architecture, and user flows.

4. Create effective storyboards that visualize user journeys, including context, actions, thoughts, and emotional states.

5. Analyze how understanding user mental models helps in designing intuitive interfaces that reduce cognitive load.

6. Apply the storyboarding technique to identify pain points and improve user experience in design scenarios.

7. Understand the relationship between user research findings, mental models, conceptual design, and storyboarding in the UX design process.
