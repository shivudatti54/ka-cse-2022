# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the concept of design perspectives in UI/UX and their importance in creating effective digital products.

2. Differentiate between user-centered design (UCD), activity-centered design (ACD), and goal-directed design (GDD) perspectives.

3. Apply the five dimensions of UX design (Garrett's framework) to analyze and plan UI/UX projects.

4. Analyze how the systems design perspective influences enterprise application development.

5. Evaluate the role of multiple stakeholder perspectives in UI/UX design decisions.

6. Select appropriate design perspectives based on project requirements and constraints.

7. Integrate multiple design perspectives to create balanced and effective user experiences.

8. Prepare effectively for university examinations by understanding key concepts, frameworks, and their practical applications.
