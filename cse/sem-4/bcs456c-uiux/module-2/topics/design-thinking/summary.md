# Design Thinking - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Design Thinking**: A human-centered, iterative problem-solving methodology that combines empathy, creativity, and rationality to develop innovative solutions
- **Empathize**: The first stage of understanding users deeply through research and observation
- **Define**: Synthesizing research findings into a clear problem statement
- **Ideate**: Generating a wide range of creative ideas without judgment
- **Prototype**: Creating tangible, low-fidelity representations of solutions
- **Test**: Validating prototypes with real users and gathering feedback

## Important Frameworks

- **Five Stages Model**: Empathize → Define → Ideate → Prototype → Test (cyclic process)
- **Double Diamond Model**: Discover → Define (first diamond), Develop → Deliver (second diamond)
- **Divergent Thinking**: Exploring many possibilities (brainstorming phase)
- **Convergent Thinking**: Narrowing down to the best solution

## Key Points

- Design Thinking places human needs at the center of the design process
- The methodology is iterative rather than linear; stages may be revisited
- Empathy is crucial for understanding real user pain points, not assumed problems
- Ideation encourages quantity of ideas before focusing on quality
- Prototypes should be quick, inexpensive, and testable
- Failure in testing provides valuable learning for improvement
- Design Thinking was pioneered by IDEO and Stanford d.school
- The approach combines analytical thinking with creative intuition

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Skipping Empathize stage**: Jumping directly to solutions without understanding actual user needs
2. **Treating stages as strictly linear**: Real Design Thinking requires going back and forth between stages
3. **Judging ideas during Ideation**: Critiquing ideas too early kills creativity
4. **Testing with wrong audience**: Prototypes must be tested with actual target users, not stakeholders

## Revision Tips

1. Memorize the five stages in order with a mnemonic or visual association
2. Practice drawing and explaining the Double Diamond diagram
3. Prepare one detailed example covering all five stages for application questions
4. Understand why Design Thinking is preferred over conventional problem-solving in UX
5. Review empathy map components and their purpose in user research
6. Focus on key terms like "iterative," "human-centered," "prototype," and "pain points"
