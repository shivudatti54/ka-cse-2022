# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the fundamental principles of UX design including Fitts' Law, Hicks' Law, Miller's Law, and Gestalt principles, and demonstrate how they apply to interface design decisions.

2. Evaluate user interfaces using Nielsen's ten usability heuristics and identify specific violations or areas for improvement in given design scenarios.

3. Apply accessibility guidelines (WCAG) to ensure digital products are usable by people with diverse abilities, including considerations for visual, auditory, motor, and cognitive disabilities.

4. Design interfaces that maintain consistency across visual, functional, and interactive dimensions while following platform-specific conventions.

5. Implement effective feedback mechanisms that keep users informed about system status and the results of their actions.

6. Create error prevention strategies and design recovery pathways that minimize user frustration when errors occur.

7. Distinguish between affordance and mapping, and apply these concepts to make interface controls intuitive and self-explanatory.

8. Analyze given UI designs critically and recommend improvements based on established UX design guidelines and principles.
