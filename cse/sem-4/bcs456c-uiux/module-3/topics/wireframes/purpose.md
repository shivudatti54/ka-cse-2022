# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define wireframes and explain their role in the UI/UX design process.

2. Differentiate between low-fidelity, mid-fidelity, and high-fidelity wireframes and identify appropriate use cases for each type.

3. Identify and explain the essential elements that comprise an effective wireframe, including navigation, content areas, and interactive components.

4. Apply the wireframing process systematically, from research and analysis through to documentation and iteration.

5. Compare and contrast wireframes, prototypes, and mockups, understanding their distinct purposes in the design workflow.

6. Utilize wireframing tools (such as Balsamiq, Figma, or similar) to create basic wireframes for web and mobile interfaces.

7. Evaluate wireframes based on usability principles and provide constructive feedback to improve design solutions.

8. Demonstrate how wireframes facilitate communication among designers, developers, and stakeholders in a design project.
