# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define UX goals and explain their significance in the design process, distinguishing them from business goals and user interface goals.

2. Identify and explain the five key dimensions of UX: Usability, Accessibility, Desirability, Credibility, and Findability, with practical examples of each.

3. Apply the SMART framework to create effective, measurable UX goals for various design projects.

4. Describe the goal-oriented design process and explain how research, personas, scenarios, and prototyping contribute to achieving UX goals.

5. Evaluate and select appropriate metrics (SUS, NPS, CSAT, task success rate, time on task) for measuring different types of UX goals.

6. Analyze case studies and propose appropriate UX goals for specific product scenarios, considering both user needs and business objectives.

7. Understand the importance of accessibility in UX goals and explain how inclusive design benefits diverse user populations.

8. Explain how achieving UX goals contributes to business success through improved customer satisfaction, loyalty, and organizational metrics.
