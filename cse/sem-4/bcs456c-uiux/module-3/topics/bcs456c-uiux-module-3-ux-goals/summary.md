# UX Goals - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **UX Goals:** Specific, measurable objectives defining desired outcomes of user interactions with a product, focused on user-centric outcomes like task completion, satisfaction, and efficiency.

- **User-Centered Goals:** Objectives derived from user research focusing on task completion rates, error reduction, learning time, and user satisfaction.

- **Business Goals:** Objectives aligning UX outcomes with organizational success metrics such as revenue, market share, and customer retention.

- **SMART Goals:** Framework requiring goals to be Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound.

## Important Formulas and Metrics

- **System Usability Scale (SUS):** Composite score 0-100; scores above 68 indicate above-average usability
- **Net Promoter Score (NPS):** Measures loyalty from -100 to +100
- **Task Success Rate:** Percentage of users completing tasks successfully
- **Time on Task:** Duration to complete tasks (lower is better)
- **Error Rate:** Frequency of user errors during task completion

## Key Points

1. UX goals bridge user needs and business objectives, ensuring design decisions create value for both.

2. The five dimensions of UX are: Usability (ease of use), Accessibility (diverse abilities), Desirability (emotional appeal), Credibility (trust), and Findability (ease of locating information).

3. Goal-oriented design follows: Research → Personas → Scenarios → Requirements → Design → Testing → Iteration.

4. Accessibility benefits all users, not just those with disabilities, and ensures compliance with regulations.

5. Qualitative metrics (interviews, feedback) provide depth; quantitative metrics (time, rates) provide measurement.

6. Personas represent user types with specific goals, motivations, and pain points derived from research.

7. Achieving UX goals leads to business benefits: higher conversion rates, reduced support costs, improved retention.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Setting vague, immeasurable goals instead of SMART goals
- Confusing UX goals with UI goals (aesthetic preferences) or business goals (revenue targets)
- Neglecting accessibility in goal-setting
- Using only quantitative or only qualitative metrics—require both for complete picture
- Designing without user research—goals must be grounded in actual user needs

## Revision Tips

1. Memorize the five dimensions of UX and be able to explain each with an example.

2. Practice writing SMART goals for different product scenarios.

3. Remember the goal-oriented design process stages in order.

4. Know what each major metric (SUS, NPS, CSAT) measures and typical "good" scores.

5. Focus on the relationship between user goals and business goals—they should align for mutual success.
