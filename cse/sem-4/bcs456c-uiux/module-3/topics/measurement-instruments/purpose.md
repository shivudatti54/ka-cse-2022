# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. **Classify** measurement instruments into quantitative and qualitative categories, with examples of each type used in UX research.

2. **Explain** the purpose and methodology of task-based performance metrics including task completion rate, error rate, and time on task.

3. **Calculate and interpret** System Usability Scale (SUS) scores, understanding what scores above and below 68 indicate about system usability.

4. **Apply** Net Promoter Score (NPS) methodology to measure user loyalty and calculate the final NPS value from survey responses.

5. **Differentiate** between various questionnaire instruments (SUS, UEQ, SEQ, NPS) and explain when each is most appropriate to use.

6. **Analyze** the role of behavioral analytics tools in measuring user interactions with digital products and interpreting heatmaps and click data.

7. **Design** a basic measurement strategy for a given UX research scenario, selecting appropriate instruments based on research objectives and constraints.

8. **Evaluate** the strengths and limitations of different measurement instruments and explain how they can students combined for comprehensive UX evaluation.
