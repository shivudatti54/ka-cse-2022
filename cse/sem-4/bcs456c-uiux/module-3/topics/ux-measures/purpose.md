# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define usability according to ISO 9241-11 and explain its three dimensions: effectiveness, efficiency, and satisfaction.

2. Apply Nielsen's 10 usability heuristics to evaluate interface designs and identify usability problems.

3. Calculate and interpret key UX metrics including task success rate, error rate, time on task, and efficiency ratios.

4. Administer and score the System Usability Scale (SUS) and interpret the resulting scores against industry benchmarks.

5. Differentiate between qualitative (thinking aloud, journey mapping) and quantitative (A/B testing, behavioral metrics) UX evaluation methods.

6. Design and conduct basic usability tests including task selection, participant recruitment, and data collection methods.

7. Analyze UX measurement results and translate findings into actionable design recommendations.

8. Evaluate when to apply formative versus summative usability evaluation in the product development lifecycle.
