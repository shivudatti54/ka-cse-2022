# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define UX metrics and differentiate between behavioral and attitudinal metrics with examples

2. Apply the HEART framework to evaluate user experience across multiple dimensions

3. Establish SMART targets that align with business objectives and user needs

4. Design and interpret A/B tests to make data-driven design decisions

5. Select appropriate KPIs based on product type and business goals

6. Analyze metric data to identify usability problems and design improvement opportunities

7. Understand the relationship between user satisfaction, engagement, and business outcomes

8. Create measurement plans that balance quantitative data with qualitative insights
