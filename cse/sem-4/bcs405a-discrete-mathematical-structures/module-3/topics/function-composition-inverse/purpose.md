# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and distinguish between injective (one-to-one), surjective (onto), and bijective functions with appropriate examples.

2. Compute the composition of two given functions and verify the properties of function composition including associativity.

3. Determine whether a function has an inverse and construct the inverse function when it exists.

4. Apply the horizontal line test and algebraic methods to verify injectivity and surjectivity of functions.

5. Prove that the inverse of a composition follows the formula (g ∘ f)⁻¹ = f⁻¹ ∘ g⁻¹.

6. Identify permutations as bijective functions and understand their role in the symmetric group Sₙ.

7. Solve applied problems involving function composition and inverse functions in discrete mathematics contexts.
