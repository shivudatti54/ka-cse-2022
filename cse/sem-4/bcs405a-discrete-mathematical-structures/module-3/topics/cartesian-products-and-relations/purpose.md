# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and compute the Cartesian product of two or more sets, understanding that it produces ordered pairs/tuples.

2. Determine the cardinality of Cartesian products using the formula |A × B| = |A| × |B|.

3. Define a relation as a subset of the Cartesian product and identify relations between given sets.

4. Identify the domain and range of a given relation and distinguish between these concepts.

5. Analyze a relation on a set to determine whether it possesses reflexive, symmetric, transitive, or anti-symmetric properties.

6. Distinguish between equivalence relations and partial orders, and provide examples of each.

7. Compute the inverse of a relation and the composition of two relations with accuracy.

8. Apply the concepts of Cartesian products and relations to solve practical problems in computing contexts.
