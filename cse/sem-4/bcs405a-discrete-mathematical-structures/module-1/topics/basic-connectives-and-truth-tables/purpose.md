# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Distinguish between propositions and non-propositions by identifying declarative statements with definite truth values.

2. Construct truth tables for all five basic logical connectives: negation, conjunction, disjunction, implication, and biconditional.

3. Evaluate compound propositions by determining their truth values for given truth assignments of atomic propositions.

4. Classify propositions as tautology, contradiction, or contingency using truth table analysis.

5. Apply De Morgan's laws and other logical equivalences to simplify and transform logical expressions.

6. Prove logical equivalence between two propositions by comparing their truth tables.

7. Understand the concept of implication as a truth-functional connective and explain why it is only false when antecedent is true and consequent is false.

8. Apply the precedence rules of logical connectives to correctly evaluate compound propositions without parentheses.
