# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define logical equivalence and distinguish between tautology, contradiction, and contingency with appropriate examples.

2. State and apply all fundamental laws of logic including identity, domination, idempotent, commutative, associative, distributive, De Morgan's, absorption, and negation laws.

3. Prove logical equivalence between two propositions using both truth table method and algebraic manipulation.

4. Apply implication equivalence rules to convert logical statements and simplify expressions involving → and ↔ connectives.

5. Simplify complex logical expressions to their simplest form using a systematic approach with appropriate law applications.

6. Construct truth tables for propositions with multiple variables and interpret the results to determine logical relationships.

7. Verify whether a given proposition is a tautology, contradiction, or contingency through algebraic reasoning.

8. Apply logical equivalence principles to solve real-world problems in digital circuits and logical reasoning contexts.
