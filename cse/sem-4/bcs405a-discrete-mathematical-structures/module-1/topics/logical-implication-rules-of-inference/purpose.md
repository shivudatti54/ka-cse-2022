# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Understand the concept of logical implication p → q and its truth table

2. Differentiate between tautology, contradiction, and contingency

3. Identify and explain the contrapositive, converse, and inverse of implications

4. Apply modus ponens and modus tollens to derive conclusions from given premises

5. Construct valid arguments using hypothetical syllogism and disjunctive syllogism

6. Use addition, simplification, and conjunction rules in formal proofs

7. Recognize common logical fallacies (affirming the consequent, denying the antecedent)

8. Construct formal proofs by applying multiple rules of inference sequentially
