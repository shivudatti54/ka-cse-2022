# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Understand and explain the Principle of Mathematical Induction (First Form) and its intuitive analogy to falling dominoes

2. Apply the weak form of mathematical induction to prove statements about natural numbers including sum formulas, product formulas, and inequalities

3. Distinguish between weak (first) mathematical induction and strong (second) mathematical induction, and identify scenarios where each is appropriate

4. Use strong mathematical induction to prove statements involving recursively defined sequences such as Fibonacci numbers

5. Construct divisibility proofs using mathematical induction techniques commonly encountered in number theory

6. Apply the Extended Principle of Mathematical Induction to prove statements starting from any integer m (not just 1 or 0)

7. Analyze and prove statements about algorithmic complexity bounds using mathematical induction
