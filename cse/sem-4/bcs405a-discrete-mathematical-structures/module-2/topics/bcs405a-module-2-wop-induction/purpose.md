# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. State and explain the Well-Ordering Principle and its significance in discrete mathematics

2. Apply the Principle of Mathematical Induction (weak induction) to prove statements about natural numbers

3. Distinguish between weak mathematical induction and strong mathematical induction, and select the appropriate method for a given problem

4. Use the Well-Ordering Principle in proof by contradiction to establish the non-existence of counterexamples

5. Prove properties involving sums, divisibility, and inequalities using mathematical induction

6. Recognize the logical equivalence between the Well-Ordering Principle and Mathematical Induction

7. Apply strong mathematical induction to prove statements about integers and recursively defined structures

8. Identify the appropriate proof technique (WOP, weak induction, or strong induction) for different types of mathematical statements
