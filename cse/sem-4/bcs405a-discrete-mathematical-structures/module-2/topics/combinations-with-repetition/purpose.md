# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define combinations with repetition and distinguish it from combinations without repetition and permutations.

2. Apply the formula C(n + k - 1, k) to solve problems involving selection with repetition where order does not matter.

3. Use the stars and bars method to visualize and solve distribution problems and integer solution problems.

4. Convert positive integer solution problems to non-negative integer solution problems using appropriate transformations.

5. Solve real-world problems involving identical objects distributed among distinct recipients.

6. Analyze and solve problems with additional constraints by breaking them into multiple cases.

7. Verify solutions using multiple approaches including direct formula application, stars and bars visualization, and generating functions.

8. Apply combinations with repetition concepts to solve counting problems in algorithm analysis and computer science applications.
