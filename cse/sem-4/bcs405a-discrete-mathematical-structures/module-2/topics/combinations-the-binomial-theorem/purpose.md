# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define combinations and distinguish between permutations and combinations based on whether order matters.

2. Apply the combination formula C(n, r) = n!/(r!(n-r)!) to solve selection problems.

3. Utilize the symmetry property C(n, r) = C(n, n-r) and Pascal's identity C(n, r) + C(n, r-1) = C(n+1, r) in calculations.

4. State and apply the Binomial Theorem to expand (a + b)^n for any non-negative integer n.

5. Identify the general term T(r+1) = C(n, r)a^(n-r)b^r in a binomial expansion and use it to find specific terms.

6. Construct and use Pascal's Triangle to determine binomial coefficients efficiently.

7. Apply combinations and the Binomial Theorem to solve real-world counting problems in computer science contexts.
