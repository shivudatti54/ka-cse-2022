# Definitions and Examples of Particular Groups - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Group (G, \*)**: A set with binary operation satisfying closure, associativity, identity, and invertibility
- **Abelian Group**: Group where operation is commutative (a _ b = b _ a)
- **Order of Group |G|**: Number of elements in a finite group
- **Cyclic Group**: Group generated by a single element; every element is a power of the generator
- **Symmetric Group Sₙ**: Group of all permutations of n symbols; |Sₙ| = n!
- **Alternating Group Aₙ**: Subgroup of Sₙ containing even permutations; |Aₙ| = n!/2
- **Dihedral Group Dₙ**: Symmetries of regular n-gon; |Dₙ| = 2n
- **Klein Four-Group V₄**: Non-cyclic group of order 4, isomorphic to Z₂ × Z₂
- **Quaternion Group Q₈**: Non-abelian group of order 8 with unique structure

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Cayley's Theorem**: Every finite group of order n is isomorphic to a subgroup of Sₙ
- **Lagrange's Theorem**: Order of any subgroup divides order of the group
- **Euler's Totient**: |U(n)| = φ(n) where φ(n) counts integers coprime to n
- **Cyclic generators in Zₙ**: Elements relatively prime to n are generators

## Key Points

- Sₙ and Dₙ (for n ≥ 3) are non-abelian; Zₙ, U(n), and V₄ are abelian
- A₃ ≅ Z₃, D₃ ≅ S₃, V₄ ≅ Z₂ × Z₂
- Q₈ has exactly one element of order 2 and six elements of order 4
- Aₙ is a normal subgroup of Sₙ with index 2
- U(n) is cyclic iff n = 1, 2, 4, pᵏ, or 2pᵏ (p odd prime)
- GL(n,R) and SL(n,R) are infinite matrix groups under multiplication

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Confusing S₃ (6 elements) with A₃ (3 elements) — they are different
- Thinking all groups of order 8 are isomorphic; Q₈ is structurally unique
- Forgetting that U(n) uses multiplication modulo n, not addition
- Incorrectly assuming cyclic groups are the only abelian groups (V₄ is non-cyclic but abelian)

## Revision Tips

1. Practice constructing Cayley tables for small groups (Z₄, S₃, V₄)
2. Memorize the orders: |Sₙ| = n!, |Aₙ| = n!/2, |Dₙ| = 2n
3. Focus on identifying whether a group is cyclic, abelian, or non-abelian
4. Remember: Klein four-group is the smallest non-cyclic group; S₃ is smallest non-abelian
