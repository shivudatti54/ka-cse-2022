# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and explain the multiplicative group of integers modulo p (Zp\*), including its group properties and order.

2. Apply Fermat's Little Theorem and Euler's Theorem to solve modular arithmetic problems involving prime moduli.

3. Identify cyclic groups and primitive roots (generators) within Zp\*, determining the order of given elements.

4. Represent permutations using cycle notation and perform composition operations on permutations.

5. Determine the parity (even or odd) of a permutation by expressing it as a product of transpositions.

6. Explain the structure and properties of the symmetric group Sn and alternating group A_n.

7. Apply group-theoretic concepts to solve problems related to cryptographic systems and combinatorial arrangements.

8. Use Lagrange's Theorem to establish relationships between element orders and group orders in finite groups.
