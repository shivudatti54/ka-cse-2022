# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define the Klein 4-Group (V4) and identify its four elements with the group operation.

2. Construct and interpret the Cayley table for the Klein 4-Group.

3. Explain why all non-identity elements in V4 have order 2 and how this distinguishes it from cyclic groups.

4. Prove that the Klein 4-Group is isomorphic to Z2 × Z2 (direct product of two cyclic groups of order 2).

5. Identify all subgroups of V4 and determine which among them are cyclic.

6. Verify that V4 is an abelian group through its Cayley table and properties.

7. Apply the concept of Klein 4-Group to real-world examples such as rectangle symmetries.

8. Distinguish between V4 and other groups of order 4, particularly the cyclic group of order 4.
