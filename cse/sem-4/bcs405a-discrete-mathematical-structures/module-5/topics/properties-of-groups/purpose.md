# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define a group and explain the four fundamental axioms (closure, associativity, identity, and inverse)

2. Prove the uniqueness of identity element and inverse element in any group

3. Apply the cancellation laws to solve problems in group theory

4. Distinguish between abelian (commutative) and non-abelian groups with appropriate examples

5. Define and identify cyclic groups, and determine generators of a cyclic group

6. Understand the concept of order of a group and order of an element

7. Verify whether a subset forms a subgroup of a given group using subgroup criteria

8. Apply Lagrange's Theorem to determine relationships between orders of groups and subgroups

9. Understand Euler's Theorem and Fermat's Little Theorem as applications of group theory

10. Solve numerical problems related to group properties in examination context
