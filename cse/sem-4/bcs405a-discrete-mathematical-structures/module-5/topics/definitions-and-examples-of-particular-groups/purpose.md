# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define a group and explain the four group axioms with specific examples
2. Distinguish between finite and infinite groups, and determine the order of a given group
3. Identify cyclic groups and determine generators for cyclic groups of finite order
4. Construct and analyze the symmetric group Sₙ and its subgroups
5. Explain the dihedral group Dₙ as the symmetry group of regular polygons
6. Compare and contrast various particular groups including cyclic, symmetric, alternating, dihedral, Klein four, and quaternion groups
7. Apply group theory concepts to solve problems involving modular arithmetic groups (Zₙ and U(n))
8. Verify whether a given set with an operation forms a group and determine its key properties
