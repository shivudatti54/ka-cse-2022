# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and identify cyclic groups with appropriate examples from both finite and infinite cases.

2. Understand and compute the order of an element in a cyclic group using appropriate techniques.

3. Determine all generators of a given cyclic group using the gcd condition and Euler's totient function.

4. Apply the Fundamental Theorem of Cyclic Groups to find all subgroups of a given cyclic group.

5. Prove that every cyclic group is abelian and establish the isomorphism between cyclic groups and Zn.

6. Distinguish between the order of an element and the order of the group in various contexts.

7. Solve problems involving cyclic subgroups and their properties in examination contexts.

8. Apply properties of cyclic groups to solve related problems in group theory.
