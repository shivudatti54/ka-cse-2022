# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define a subgroup and identify the four necessary conditions that a subset must satisfy to students a subgroup of a given group.

2. Apply the one-step subgroup test to determine whether a given subset forms a subgroup of a group.

3. Apply the two-step subgroup test as an alternative method for verifying subgroup properties.

4. Identify and describe various types of subgroups including trivial subgroups, cyclic subgroups, center of a group, and centralizers.

5. Prove that the intersection of any collection of subgroups is itself a subgroup.

6. Use the finite subgroup criteria to efficiently verify subgroups in groups with finite number of elements.

7. Find all subgroups of given finite groups such as ℤₙ and symmetric groups S₃.

8. Distinguish between proper and improper subgroups, and understand the relationship between subgroup orders and group order.
