# Subgroups - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Subgroup**: A subset H of a group G that forms a group under the same operation. Denoted H ≤ G.
- **Four conditions for subgroup**: Non-empty (H ≠ ∅), closure, identity element in H, inverses in H.
- **Proper subgroup**: A subgroup H where H ≠ G. Denoted H < G.
- **Trivial subgroup**: {e} in any group G.
- **Cyclic subgroup ⟨a⟩**: The set {aⁿ : n ∈ ℤ} generated by element a.
- **Center Z(G)**: {a ∈ G : ag = ga for all g ∈ G}
- **Centralizer C(a)**: {g ∈ G : ga = ag}

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **One-step subgroup test**: H ≤ G iff H ≠ ∅ and for all a, b ∈ H, a \* b⁻¹ ∈ H
- **Two-step subgroup test**: H ≤ G iff H ≠ ∅, a, b ∈ H implies a \* b ∈ H, and a ∈ H implies a⁻¹ ∈ H
- **Finite subgroup test**: If H is finite, non-empty, and closed under the operation, then H ≤ G
- **Intersection theorem**: ∩ᵢ Hᵢ ≤ G whenever each Hᵢ ≤ G

## Key Points

1. Every group has at least two subgroups: {e} (trivial) and G itself (improper)
2. The one-step test combines closure, identity, and inverses into one condition
3. For finite groups, checking closure alone is sufficient to prove subgroup property
4. The intersection of subgroups is always a subgroup; union generally is not
5. In ℤ under addition, nℤ = {nk : k ∈ ℤ} is a subgroup for any positive integer n
6. The center Z(G) and centralizers C(a) are always subgroups of G
7. Subgroup generated by a set S, denoted ⟨S⟩, is the smallest subgroup containing S
8. Orders of subgroups divide the group order (Lagrange's theorem for reference)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Forgetting to check non-empty condition - assuming H has at least one element
2. Using one-step test incorrectly - remember it's a _ b⁻¹, not a _ b
3. Assuming union of subgroups is a subgroup (only true if one contains the other)
4. Confusing the subgroup notation: ≤ means subgroup, < means proper subgroup
5. Forgetting that the identity in the subgroup must be the same as in the main group

## Revision Tips

1. Practice the one-step test with multiple examples until it's automatic
2. Memorize that {e} and G are always subgroups of any group G
3. For cyclic groups ℤₙ, identify subgroups by finding divisors of n
4. When stuck, verify the four basic conditions (non-empty, closure, identity, inverses)
5. Remember: finite + closed under multiplication ⇒ subgroup (no need to check inverses separately)
