# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define Building Management Systems (BMS) and explain their role in achieving sustainable building operations and energy efficiency.

2. Identify and describe the major components of a BMS architecture, including field-level devices, automation controllers, and management-level interfaces.

3. Compare and contrast major BMS communication protocols such as BACnet, Modbus, LonWorks, and KNX, including their advantages and typical applications.

4. Analyze various energy management strategies implemented in BMS, including demand response, load shedding, optimal start/stop, and demand-controlled ventilation.

5. Evaluate the integration of Internet of Things (IoT) technologies with traditional BMS and explain how this integration enhances building intelligence and sustainability.

6. Design a basic BMS strategy for a commercial building, including appropriate sensors, control strategies, and protocol selection for specific requirements.

7. Assess the contribution of BMS to green building certification systems such as LEED and explain how these systems support sustainability objectives.
