# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Analyze the environmental impact of cloud computing, including data center energy consumption, cooling requirements, and carbon emissions.

2. Evaluate the carbon footprint of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning systems, particularly the energy costs associated with model training and inference.

3. Assess the environmental implications of Internet of Things (IoT) deployments, including device energy consumption, e-waste generation, and resource requirements.

4. Compare the environmental impact of different blockchain consensus mechanisms, particularly Proof-of-Work versus Proof-of-Stake.

5. Calculate carbon footprints and energy consumption for digital infrastructure using appropriate metrics and formulas.

6. Design and propose green computing strategies to minimize environmental impact while maintaining technological effectiveness.

7. Understand the environmental challenges and sustainability opportunities presented by emerging technologies like 5G and Big Data.

8. Apply principles of Green IT to evaluate and improve the environmental performance of technological solutions in various domains.
