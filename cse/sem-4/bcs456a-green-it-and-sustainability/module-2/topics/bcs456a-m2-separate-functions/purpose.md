# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define and explain the concept of "separate functions" in IT systems and how function proliferation impacts environmental sustainability.

2. Analyze the environmental consequences of increased separate functions, including energy consumption patterns, heat generation, and electronic waste generation.

3. Calculate and interpret utilization rates for IT resources and explain the relationship between underutilization and energy waste.

4. Evaluate the trade-offs between function specialization (performance benefits) and sustainability (resource efficiency).

5. Compare and contrast different convergence and integration strategies for reducing the environmental impact of separate functions.

6. Apply Green IT principles to design and recommend sustainable solutions for scenarios involving multiple separate functions.

7. Develop and analyze power consumption calculations for data center and enterprise computing environments.

8. Recommend appropriate strategies for managing function proliferation in various organizational contexts.
