# Green IT and Sustainability - Introduction Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Green IT (Green Computing)**: The practice of designing, manufacturing, using, and disposing of computer hardware, software, and peripherals in an environmentally sustainable manner
- **Sustainability**: Meeting present needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs
- **E-waste (Electronic Waste)**: Discarded electronic devices containing toxic materials like lead, mercury, cadmium, and chromium

## Important Facts and Statistics

- Data centers consume 1-2% of global electricity
- Only about 17% of e-waste is formally recycled
- Green IT practices can reduce IT operational costs by 30-50%

## Key Principles of Green IT

- **Energy Efficiency**: Minimizing power consumption through efficient hardware and optimized software
- **Resource Conservation**: Extending hardware lifespan and minimizing raw material usage
- **Waste Reduction**: Implementing proper e-waste management through reuse and recycling
- **Carbon Footprint Reduction**: Decreasing greenhouse gas emissions from IT operations

## Benefits of Green IT Adoption

| Category      | Key Benefits                                                                      |
| ------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Economic      | Reduced energy costs, lower hardware procurement expenses, operational efficiency |
| Environmental | Decreased carbon emissions, reduced e-waste, conservation of resources            |
| Social        | Enhanced brand reputation, employee satisfaction, regulatory compliance           |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Confusing Green IT with simple energy saving—sustainability encompasses the entire product lifecycle
2. Overlooking e-waste management when discussing Green IT
3. Ignoring the economic benefits while focusing only on environmental aspects
4. Failing to recognize that Green IT is both an environmental necessity and business opportunity

## Revision Tips

1. Remember the "3E" framework: Environment, Economics, and Ethics when studying Green IT benefits
2. Practice calculating energy savings from virtualization and other consolidation techniques
3. Review real-world case studies of successful Green IT implementations
4. Understand the connection between Green IT and broader sustainability frameworks like ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance)
5. Memorize key terms and their definitions as they form the foundation for advanced topics in subsequent modules
