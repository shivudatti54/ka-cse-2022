# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the fundamental differences between traditional electromechanical energy meters and intelligent (smart) energy metering systems.

2. Describe the multi-layered architecture of intelligent energy metering systems, including data acquisition, communication, data management, and application layers.

3. Identify and explain the key components of a smart meter and their functions in energy measurement and data transmission.

4. Compare and contrast various communication protocols used in intelligent energy metering, including DLMS/COSEM, Modbus, and IEC 61850.

5. Analyze how Internet of Things (IoT) integration enhances energy metering capabilities for sustainability and efficiency.

6. Apply data analytics techniques such as energy disaggregation and load forecasting to interpret smart meter data for decision-making.

7. Evaluate the benefits of intelligent energy metering in the context of Green IT and environmental sustainability.

8. Solve numerical problems related to time-of-use pricing calculations, energy consumption analysis, and peak load management using intelligent metering data.
