# Increased Demand for Speed and Reliability - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Computing Speed**: The rate of data processing, transfer, and delivery, measured through processor clock speed, memory access time, latency, and throughput.

- **System Reliability**: The ability of a system to perform consistently without failure, measured by Mean Time Between Failures (MTBF), Mean Time to Repair (MTTR), and availability percentages.

- **Performance-per-Watt**: A metric evaluating computational work accomplished relative to energy consumed, a key indicator of sustainable computing efficiency.

- **Green IT**: Practices aimed at reducing environmental impact of IT through energy efficiency, waste reduction, and sustainable operations.

- **Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE)**: A metric measuring data center energy efficiency, calculated as total facility energy divided by IT equipment energy.

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Availability %** = (MTBF / (MTBF + MTTR)) × 100
- **Performance-per-Watt** = Operations Completed / Energy Consumed (Watts)
- **PUE** = Total Facility Energy / IT Equipment Energy
- **Carbon Footprint** = Energy Consumption × Carbon Emission Factor

## Key Points

- The demand for speed and reliability directly increases energy consumption, creating tension with sustainability objectives.

- Performance-per-watt optimization represents the core strategy for achieving sustainable high performance.

- Virtualization and containerization enable higher resource utilization, reducing energy consumption while improving performance.

- Solid-state drives offer superior performance with lower power consumption compared to traditional hard disk drives.

- Redundancy improves reliability but increases resource consumption; optimal balance is essential for sustainability.

- Data centers consume 1-2% of global electricity, making their optimization critical for environmental sustainability.

- Predictive maintenance using AI/ML extends equipment lifespan, reducing electronic waste while maintaining reliability.

- Sustainable design often improves both performance and environmental outcomes through optimization.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Assuming that performance and sustainability are mutually exclusive; optimization frequently benefits both.

- Overlooking the importance of cooling systems in data center energy consumption.

- Implementing excessive redundancy without considering the environmental cost of idle resources.

- Ignoring the lifespan extension benefits of reliability engineering for waste reduction.

- Failing to consider the full lifecycle impact of technology decisions, including manufacturing and disposal.

## Revision Tips

- Focus on understanding the interrelationships between speed, reliability, power consumption, and environmental impact.

- Memorize key metrics and formulas, as they frequently appear in examination problems.

- Review real-world case studies of green data centers to understand practical implementations.

- Practice identifying sustainable solutions for given performance requirements in exam scenarios.

- Understand the environmental arguments for Green IT to answer conceptual questions effectively.
