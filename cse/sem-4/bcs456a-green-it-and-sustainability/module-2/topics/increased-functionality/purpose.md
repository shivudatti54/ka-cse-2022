# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the concept of increased functionality in Green IT and its importance in modern computing environments.

2. Analyze how virtualization technology improves hardware utilization and enables greater functionality while reducing environmental impact.

3. Evaluate the role of cloud computing and elastic scalability in achieving sustainable increased functionality.

4. Describe energy-efficient hardware design concepts including DVFS, SSDs, and advanced power management systems.

5. Understand the principles of green software development and how efficient code contributes to both sustainability and enhanced functionality.

6. Compare traditional IT infrastructure approaches with green IT strategies in terms of functionality and environmental impact.

7. Apply quantitative analysis to assess the benefits of green IT initiatives in given scenarios.

8. Design basic strategies for achieving increased functionality through sustainable computing practices.
