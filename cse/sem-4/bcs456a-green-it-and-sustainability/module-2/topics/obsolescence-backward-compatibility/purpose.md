# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define obsolescence and explain its various types, including planned, functional, economic, and technological obsolescence.

2. Analyze the relationship between obsolescence and backward compatibility in computing systems.

3. Evaluate the environmental impact of technological obsolescence, particularly in terms of e-waste generation and resource depletion.

4. Identify strategies for achieving backward compatibility in hardware and software design to extend product lifecycles.

5. Apply Green IT principles to minimize obsolescence and promote sustainable computing practices.

6. Assess the trade-offs between technological advancement and backward compatibility in system design decisions.

7. Recommend approaches for organizations to manage legacy systems while reducing environmental impact.

8. Compare planned obsolescence strategies with sustainable design practices and their respective impacts on society and the environment.
