# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the concept of environmental accounting in IT and its importance in modern organizations

2. Analyze the Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) for Green IT initiatives, including environmental and lifecycle costs

3. Calculate carbon footprints for IT operations using the three-scope emission framework

4. Evaluate Green IT investments using financial metrics including payback period, NPV, and IRR

5. Interpret ESG reporting requirements and their implications for IT organizations

6. Design e-waste management strategies based on circular economy principles

7. Compare on-premises versus cloud computing from both financial and environmental perspectives

8. Apply green IT principles to real-world organizational scenarios and make informed recommendations
