# Green IT: Saving IT - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Green IT**: Practices and technologies that minimize environmental impact of IT operations
- **PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness)**: Ratio of total facility energy to IT equipment energy; lower is better
- **Virtualization**: Technology enabling multiple virtual machines on single physical server
- **DVFS**: Dynamic Voltage and Frequency Scaling - adjusts power based on workload
- **E-Waste**: Electronic waste requiring proper disposal and recycling

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **PUE Calculation**: PUE = Total Facility Energy / IT Equipment Energy
- **Energy Savings**: Energy Saved = (Old Consumption - New Consumption) × Time
- **Virtualization Consolidation Ratio**: Servers Reduced = Original Servers - Virtualized Servers

## Key Points

- IT sector contributes 1-2% of global carbon emissions; data centers are major consumers
- Virtualization can reduce energy consumption by 50-80% through server consolidation
- Green data centers aim for PUE values less than 1.2
- Thin clients consume 5-15W vs traditional PCs at 100-400W
- Energy Star certified equipment is 20-30% more efficient
- Cloud computing provides energy efficiency through multi-tenancy and resource pooling
- Algorithm optimization in software development reduces CPU cycles and energy
- E-waste management involves reuse, recycling, and responsible disposal
- Hot/cold aisle containment improves cooling efficiency by 20-40%

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Confusing Green IT with just recycling e-waste - it encompasses entire IT lifecycle
- Assuming virtualization always reduces energy - poor implementation can increase costs
- Ignoring the embodied energy in hardware manufacturing
- Focusing only on power consumption without considering cooling requirements

## Revision Tips

- Memorize PUE formula and understand that lower values indicate better efficiency
- Remember three main virtualization benefits: energy savings, space savings, cooling reduction
- Review Green IT pillars: Design, Manufacturing, Use, Disposal
- Practice PUE calculation problems as these frequently appear in exams
- Know the difference between thin clients and thick clients with power consumption figures
