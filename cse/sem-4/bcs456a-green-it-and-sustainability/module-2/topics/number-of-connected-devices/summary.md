# Number of Connected Devices - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Internet of Things (IoT)**: A network of physical objects embedded with sensors, software, and connectivity capabilities that enable data collection and exchange
- **Connected Devices**: Devices that can connect to networks and exchange data, including smartphones, tablets, sensors, smart home devices, and industrial equipment
- **Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE)**: Metric measuring data center efficiency; PUE = Total Facility Energy / IT Equipment Energy
- **E-waste**: Electronic waste from discarded electrical and electronic equipment

## Important Formulas and Equations

- **Device Daily Energy (Wh)**: (Standby Power × 24) + (Active Power × Active Hours)
- **Annual Carbon Footprint (kg CO2)**: Annual Energy (kWh) × Grid Emission Factor (kg CO2/kWh)
- **Data Center Total Energy**: IT Equipment Energy × PUE

## Key Points

- Global connected devices exceeded 30 billion in 2023, projected to reach 75+ billion by 2025
- Connected devices consume energy in four ways: active power, standby power, network transmission, and manufacturing
- Data centers consume 1-2% of global electricity and require significant cooling systems
- Manufacturing accounts for 60-80% of a device's total carbon footprint
- Only about 17% of e-waste is properly recycled globally
- Edge computing can reduce data transmission energy by processing locally
- Energy harvesting and low-power design can significantly reduce device sustainability impact

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Ignoring standby power**: Many students focus only on active power consumption while overlooking vampire power
2. **Forgetting manufacturing impact**: The production phase often has larger environmental impact than usage phase
3. **Not considering end-of-life**: Device disposal and e-waste are critical sustainability factors often missed
4. **Overlooking network energy**: Data transmission infrastructure consumes substantial energy beyond the device itself

## Revision Tips

1. Focus on the three lifecycle phases: manufacturing, usage, and end-of-life when analyzing environmental impact
2. Memorize key statistics (30 billion devices, 17% e-waste recycling rate) for quick recall in exams
3. Understand PUE calculations and know that lower values indicate better efficiency
4. Review edge computing benefits as a key green IT strategy for IoT sustainability
5. Practice calculating energy consumption and carbon footprint using the formulas provided
