# Industrial Revolution

The Industrial Revolution represents a transformative historical process that fundamentally altered human-environment interactions through successive technological phases. This module examines four key industrial revolutions: First (1760-1840) characterized by steam power and mechanization; Second (1870-1914) featuring electrification and mass production; Third (1950-2000) marked by digital computing and electronics; and Fourth (Industry 4.0) involving cyber-physical systems, IoT, and AI. Theoretical frameworks including the IPAT equation, Environmental Kuznets Curve, Jevons Paradox, and Material Flow Analysis provide analytical tools for understanding industrialization's environmental impacts. The module explores how each phase increased energy consumption, resource depletion, and pollution while introducing technological solutions. Contemporary Industry 5.0 emphasizes human-machine collaboration and sustainability. Quantitative analysis demonstrates declining energy intensity over time while absolute consumption continues rising. Students learn that technological innovation alone is insufficient for sustainability; structural changes in consumption patterns, institutional frameworks, and systemic thinking are equally essential for achieving industrial systems compatible with planetary boundaries.
