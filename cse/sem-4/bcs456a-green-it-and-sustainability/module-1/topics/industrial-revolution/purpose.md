This module provides B.Tech, MSc, and MCA students with a comprehensive understanding of the Industrial Revolution's evolution and its environmental implications. It establishes theoretical foundations including the IPAT equation, Environmental Kuznets Curve, Jevons Paradox, and Industrial Metabolism concepts. Through quantitative analysis of each industrial revolution—from steam power to Industry 4.0 and emerging Industry 5.0—students develop competencies in analyzing technology-environment relationships. The module enables students to critically evaluate how successive industrial phases have shaped sustainability challenges and opportunities, preparing them to design and implement sustainable technological solutions in their professional careers.
