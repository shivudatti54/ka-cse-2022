# The Emergence of Information and Communication Technology - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Information and Communication Technology (ICT)**: The convergence of computing, telecommunications, and multimedia technologies for gathering, storing, processing, and communicating information.
- **Green IT (Green Computing)**: The practice of designing, manufacturing, using, and disposing of computer equipment in an environmentally responsible manner.
- **Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE)**: A metric measuring data center energy efficiency; PUE = Total Facility Energy / IT Equipment Energy (ideal value is 1.0)
- **E-waste**: Discarded electrical or electronic devices and their components that have reached end-of-life.
- **Dematerialization**: The replacement of physical products and processes with digital alternatives to reduce resource consumption.

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Data Center Energy Consumption**: Total Power (kW) × Operating Hours × Days = Annual Energy (kWh)
- **PUE Calculation**: PUE = Total Facility Energy Input / IT Equipment Energy Input
- **Energy Savings Percentage**: ((Original Consumption - New Consumption) / Original Consumption) × 100
- **Carbon Emission Reduction**: Energy Saved (kWh) × Emission Factor (kg CO2/kWh)

## Key Points

- ICT has evolved from large mainframes in the 1950s to billions of interconnected devices today, with over 10 billion IoT devices currently in use.
- Data centers consume approximately 1-2% of global electricity, with this percentage growing annually.
- Manufacturing a single laptop generates approximately 400 kg of carbon emissions.
- Only about 17% of global e-waste is properly recycled, with the remainder causing environmental contamination.
- Green IT strategies include: energy efficiency, extending equipment life, sustainable disposal, and virtualization.
- ICT enables sustainability through smart grids, precision agriculture, telemedicine, and digital services.
- Virtualization can reduce server energy consumption by 40% or more through improved utilization.
- The four pillars of Green IT address manufacturing, usage, data center operations, and end-of-life management.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Confusing Green IT solely with energy efficiency—remember it encompasses the entire product lifecycle.
- Ignoring the environmental impact of manufacturing when focusing only on operational energy consumption.
- Assuming that digital services are inherently green—they still require physical infrastructure and energy.
- Overlooking e-waste as a major environmental concern compared to energy consumption.
- Failing to consider the embedded energy in manufacturing new devices versus extending life of existing equipment.

## Revision Tips

1. Practice numerical problems involving PUE calculations and energy savings scenarios, as university exams frequently include such questions.
2. Create a flowchart showing the ICT lifecycle and associated environmental impacts at each stage.
3. Memorize specific statistics (e.g., percentage of e-waste recycled, data center energy percentage) as they make excellent exam answers.
4. Review real-world case studies of Green IT implementations in data centers and organizations.
5. Prepare concise definitions of all key terms, as short-answer questions are common in university examinations.
