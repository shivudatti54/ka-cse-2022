# The Agenda and Challenges Ahead - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Green IT**: The practice of designing, manufacturing, using, and disposing of computing resources in an environmentally responsible manner
- **Carbon Footprint**: Total greenhouse gas emissions caused directly and indirectly by IT operations
- **E-waste**: Discarded electrical or electronic devices and their components
- **Virtualization**: Technology that allows multiple virtual machines to run on a single physical server
- **PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness)**: Metric measuring data center energy efficiency (lower is better)
- **Virtualization**: Technology that allows multiple virtual machines to run on a single physical server

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **PUE = Total Facility Energy / IT Equipment Energy** (Ideal PUE is 1.0; good is <1.5)
- **Energy Efficiency = Useful Output / Energy Input**

## Key Points

- Green IT addresses environmental concerns through energy efficiency, sustainable manufacturing, and responsible disposal
- Data centers consume 1-2% of global electricity, with cooling being a major energy drain
- Virtualization and cloud computing significantly reduce hardware requirements and energy consumption
- E-waste contains toxic materials like lead, mercury, and cadmium that require special handling
- Key regulatory frameworks include RoHS, WEEE, Energy Star, and ISO 14001
- Major challenges include high initial costs, performance trade-offs, lack of expertise, and supply chain complexity
- Green IT provides both environmental benefits and cost savings for organizations
- The entire product lifecycle from manufacturing to disposal must be considered for true sustainability
- India has implemented e-waste management rules and promotes renewable energy adoption

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Assuming Green IT is only about reducing energy consumption—it encompasses the entire product lifecycle
- Overlooking e-waste management as a critical component of Green IT
- Ignoring the economic viability of Green IT solutions—cost savings are a significant benefit
- Underestimating the importance of proper data destruction before e-waste recycling

## Revision Tips

1. Focus on understanding the interconnection between energy efficiency, virtualization, and e-waste management

3. Review the major regulatory frameworks and their purposes
4. Practice explaining Green IT challenges and solutions in your own words
