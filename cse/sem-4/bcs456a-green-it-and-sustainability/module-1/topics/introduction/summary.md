# Introduction to Green IT and Sustainability - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Green IT/Green Computing**: The practice of designing, manufacturing, using, and disposing of computer systems in an environmentally responsible manner to minimize ecological impact
- **Sustainability**: Meeting present needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs
- **E-Waste**: Discarded electronic equipment containing hazardous materials like lead, mercury, and cadmium
- **PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness)**: A metric measuring data center energy efficiency calculated as Total Facility Power divided by IT Equipment Power

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **PUE Calculation**: PUE = Total Facility Power (kW) / IT Equipment Power (kW)
- **Energy Savings through Virtualization**: Savings = (Old Servers × Old Power) - (New Servers × New Power)
- **Server Consolidation Ratio**: The number of virtual machines that can be hosted on a single physical server

## Key Points

- IT sector accounts for approximately 2% of global carbon emissions, with data centers being major consumers of electricity
- The Triple Bottom Line of sustainability includes Environmental, Economic, and Social dimensions
- Virtualization can reduce server energy consumption by 80% or more through server consolidation
- Cloud computing enables shared resources and improved energy efficiency across organizations
- Green software engineering focuses on optimizing code to reduce CPU, memory, and network resource consumption
- Major Green IT frameworks include Green Grid, ENERGY STAR, and ISO 14001 environmental standards
- E-waste contains toxic materials requiring proper handling and disposal procedures
- Sustainable software development emphasizes efficient algorithms and minimal resource utilization

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Confusing Green IT with just energy efficiency—it encompasses e-waste, manufacturing, and lifecycle management
- Overlooking the economic benefits of Green IT implementation alongside environmental benefits
- Ignoring software optimization as a component of Green IT strategy
- Not understanding that virtualization alone is insufficient—proper planning and management are essential

## Revision Tips

- Memorize the Triple Bottom Line concept as it frequently appears in examinations
- Practice PUE calculations with different scenarios to ensure clarity
- Remember that Green IT is not just about hardware but also software and operational practices
- Review real-world examples of companies implementing Green IT strategies
- Understand the relationship between virtualization, cloud computing, and energy efficiency
