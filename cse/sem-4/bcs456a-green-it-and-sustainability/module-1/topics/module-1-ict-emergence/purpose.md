# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the historical evolution of Information and Communication Technology and its progression from mainframes to modern cloud computing and IoT devices.

2. Analyze the environmental impact of ICT throughout its lifecycle, including manufacturing, operational energy consumption, and end-of-life disposal.

3. Define Green IT and describe its key strategies for minimizing environmental impact while maintaining technological effectiveness.

4. Calculate and interpret metrics such as Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE) and energy savings from Green IT implementations.

5. Evaluate how ICT can serve as an enabler for sustainable development through applications like smart grids, precision agriculture, and digital dematerialization.

6. Identify the challenges associated with e-waste management and propose appropriate solutions for responsible electronics disposal.

7. Apply Green IT principles to real-world scenarios, including data center optimization and organizational computing infrastructure management.

8. Discuss the relationship between technological advancement and environmental sustainability in the context of modern computing practices.
