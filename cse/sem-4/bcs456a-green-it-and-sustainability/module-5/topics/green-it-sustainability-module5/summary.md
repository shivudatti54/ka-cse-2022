# Green IT and Sustainability - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Green Computing**: The practice of designing, manufacturing, using, and disposing of computing resources in an environmentally responsible manner
- **PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness)**: Metric measuring data center energy efficiency; ideal value is 1.0-1.2
- **Virtualization**: Technology allowing multiple virtual machines to run on a single physical server
- **E-Waste**: Discarded electronic equipment containing hazardous materials and recyclable components
- **Carbon Footprint**: Total greenhouse gas emissions caused directly and indirectly by an individual or organization

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **PUE Calculation**: `PUE = Total Facility Energy / IT Equipment Energy`
- **Energy Savings Formula**: `Savings = (Original Power - New Power) × Hours × Days`
- **CO2 Reduction**: `CO2 (kg) = Energy Saved (kWh) × Emission Factor (kg/kWh)`
- **Virtualization Consolidation Ratio**: `Ratio = Physical Servers / Virtual Machines`

## Key Points

1. The IT industry contributes approximately 2-3% of global carbon emissions, equivalent to the aviation industry

2. Data centers consume 1-2% of global electricity, with cooling systems accounting for 30-40% of energy use

3. Virtualization can achieve 80% energy reduction and 75% space savings

4. Green IT rests on four pillars: Green Use, Green Disposal, Green Design, and Green Manufacturing

5. E-waste contains hazardous materials (lead, mercury, cadmium) and valuable precious metals requiring proper recycling

6. Sustainable software development prioritizes algorithm efficiency, optimized code, and cloud-native architectures

7. Key certifications include ISO 14001, ENERGY STAR, EPEAT, and Green Grid membership

8. Power management features like sleep mode can reduce desktop energy consumption by 40-60%

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Confusing PUE with energy consumption**: Higher PUE means less efficient data center, not necessarily more consumption

2. **Ignoring the full lifecycle**: Consider manufacturing, transportation, use, and disposal phases when evaluating environmental impact

3. **Overlooking virtualisation challenges**: Remember security, performance isolation, and licensing considerations

4. **Assuming green IT is only about hardware**: Software efficiency significantly impacts energy consumption
