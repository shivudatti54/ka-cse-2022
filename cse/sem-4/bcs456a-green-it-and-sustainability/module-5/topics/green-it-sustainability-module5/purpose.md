# Learning Objectives

After studying this course on Green IT and Sustainability, you should students able to:

1. **Define and explain** the fundamental concepts of green computing, including its importance, objectives, and the environmental impact of IT operations.

2. **Analyze** the environmental impact of data centers and explain sustainable data center design principles, including energy efficiency metrics like Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE).

3. **Evaluate** various green IT strategies such as virtualization, cloud computing, and thin client computing, and calculate their energy and cost savings.

4. **Describe** e-waste management practices, including the hazards associated with improper disposal and the principles of circular economy in IT.

5. **Apply** green software development principles to create energy-efficient applications and understand the carbon footprint of software systems.

6. **Design** corporate green IT policies and governance frameworks that promote environmental sustainability within IT organizations.

7. **Compare** different green IT standards and certifications, including ISO 14001, ENERGY STAR, and EPEAT, and understand their implementation requirements.

8. **Assess** emerging trends in green technology, including renewable energy integration, AI for sustainability, and the future of sustainable computing.
