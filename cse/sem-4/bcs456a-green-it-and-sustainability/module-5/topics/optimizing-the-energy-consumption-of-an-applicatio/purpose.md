# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental relationship between software design decisions and application energy consumption in computing systems.

2. Analyze the components of energy consumption in modern processors, including dynamic power (P = CV²f) and static power consumption from leakage current.

3. Apply power profiling and measurement techniques to identify energy bottlenecks in application code using appropriate tools and methodologies.

4. Implement code-level optimization strategies including algorithm optimization, loop optimization, lazy evaluation, and caching to reduce computational energy requirements.

5. Evaluate architectural-level optimization approaches such as multi-threading, asymmetric processing, and cloud-based resource allocation for energy efficiency.

6. Design applications following Green Software Engineering principles, ensuring energy efficiency, hardware efficiency, and carbon efficiency throughout the software development lifecycle.

7. Compare virtualization and containerization approaches for energy optimization in server environments, including container orchestration strategies.

8. Assess the energy impact of various application designs and make informed decisions about trade-offs between performance, energy consumption, and user experience.
