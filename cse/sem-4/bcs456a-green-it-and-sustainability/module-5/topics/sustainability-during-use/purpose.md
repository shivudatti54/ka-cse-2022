# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the concept of sustainability during use in Green IT and its importance in reducing environmental impact throughout the IT lifecycle.

2. Describe energy-efficient computing techniques including Dynamic Voltage and Frequency Scaling (DVFS) and processor power states.

3. Analyze power management strategies at hardware, firmware, and operating system levels for various computing devices.

4. Evaluate the benefits of virtualization and server consolidation in improving resource utilization and reducing energy consumption.

5. Design and assess green data center practices including energy-efficient cooling, Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE) optimization, and renewable energy integration.

6. Apply green software development principles to create energy-efficient applications and algorithms.

7. Interpret Energy Star and EPEAT certifications for making environmentally conscious IT procurement decisions.

8. Calculate energy savings and efficiency improvements through practical examples involving virtualization, power management, and data center optimization.
