# Evaluating Sustainability Effects - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Sustainability Evaluation**: Systematic process of measuring environmental, economic, and social impacts of IT operations
- **Green Computing Metrics**: Quantitative measures including PUE, CUE, and WUE for assessing IT sustainability
- **Life Cycle Assessment (LCA)**: Methodology evaluating environmental impacts throughout a product's entire life cycle
- **Carbon Footprint**: Total greenhouse gas emissions expressed in CO2 equivalent (CO2e)

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness)** = Total Facility Energy / IT Equipment Energy (Ideal = 1.0)
- **Carbon Footprint** = Energy Consumption (kWh) × Carbon Emission Factor (kg CO2e/kWh)
- **Scope 1 Emissions**: Direct emissions from owned sources
- **Scope 2 Emissions**: Indirect emissions from purchased electricity
- **Scope 3 Emissions**: All other indirect emissions in value chain

## Key Points

- Sustainability evaluation operates on three pillars: environmental, economic, and social dimensions
- PUE measures data center energy efficiency; lower values indicate better performance
- Carbon footprint calculation requires understanding emission scopes (1, 2, and 3)
- LCA provides comprehensive assessment from raw materials to end-of-life
- ISO 14001 offers systematic environmental management framework
- E-waste evaluation should prioritize reuse over recycling over disposal
- Cloud computing typically shows better sustainability than traditional on-premise deployments
- Virtualization can reduce energy consumption by 60-80%

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Confusing Scope 1, 2, and 3 emissions categories in exam answers
- Assuming lower PUE always means better sustainability (context matters)
- Ignoring Scope 3 emissions which often represent largest impact portion
- Treating sustainability as purely environmental without economic and social dimensions

## Revision Tips

- Practice numerical problems on energy consumption and carbon calculations
- Memorize the three pillars and be able to provide IT-specific examples for each
- Understand the difference between evaluation frameworks (ISO 14001, GRI, LCA)
- Review real-world case studies of data center sustainability improvements
- Prepare to write short notes on any sustainability metric or framework
