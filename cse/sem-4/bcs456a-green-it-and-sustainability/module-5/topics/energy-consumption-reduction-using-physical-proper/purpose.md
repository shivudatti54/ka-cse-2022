# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the fundamental relationship between physical properties (thermal, electrical, material) and energy consumption in computing systems.

2. Analyze processor power consumption using the dynamic power equation P = CV²f and identify strategies for reducing each parameter.

3. Compare different cooling technologies (passive, active, facility-level) based on their energy efficiency and application suitability.

4. Evaluate data center energy efficiency using metrics such as PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness) and cooling coefficient of performance.

5. Assess the impact of semiconductor process technologies on leakage current and overall power consumption.

6. Design energy-efficient memory and storage systems by applying physical property optimization principles.

7. Calculate power savings from voltage and frequency scaling interventions in practical scenarios.

8. Recommend appropriate physical property optimization strategies for different computing workloads and infrastructure scales.
