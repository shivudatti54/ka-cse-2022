# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the fundamental relationship between voltage, frequency, and power consumption in CMOS circuits and its implications for runtime energy optimization.

2. Describe Dynamic Voltage and Frequency Scaling (DVFS) and its implementation through P-states in modern computing systems.

3. Compare and contrast Dynamic Power Management (DPM) approaches including timeout-based and predictive policies.

4. Analyze the trade-offs between different runtime energy optimization strategies including computational sprinting and race-to-halt approaches.

5. Evaluate the role of task scheduling and load balancing in achieving energy-efficient computing in multi-core systems.

6. Apply energy optimization concepts to solve practical problems involving power management decisions.

7. Assess the effectiveness of different runtime approaches in various computing scenarios from mobile devices to data centers.
