# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the concept of sustainable software design and its importance in reducing ICT's environmental footprint.

2. Identify and describe the four key principles of sustainable software students: energy efficiency, hardware efficiency, carbon awareness, and sustainability across the software lifecycle.

3. Analyze software code and architecture decisions to determine their environmental impact in terms of energy consumption and carbon emissions.

4. Apply green coding practices including algorithmic optimization, efficient data structures, and lazy loading techniques to reduce software energy consumption.

5. Evaluate software solutions using sustainability metrics such as energy per transaction, carbon intensity, and power usage effectiveness.

6. Design and implement software architectures that incorporate carbon-aware computing and efficient resource utilization.

7. Compare the environmental impact of different software design approaches and justify decisions based on sustainability criteria.

8. Relate sustainable software design principles to global sustainability goals and corporate environmental responsibilities.
