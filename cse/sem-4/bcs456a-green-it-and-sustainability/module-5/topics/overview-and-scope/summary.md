# Green IT and Sustainability - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Green IT (Green Computing)**: The practice of using computing resources in an environmentally responsible manner, encompassing design, manufacture, use, and disposal of IT hardware and software
- **Green Data Center**: Energy-efficient data center infrastructure using virtualisation, free cooling, and renewable energy
- **Electronic Waste (E-waste)**: Discarded electronic devices and components; one of the fastest-growing waste streams globally
- **Virtualisation**: Technology allowing multiple virtual machines to run on a single physical server

## Important Formulas and Techniques

- Virtualisation can reduce server energy consumption by **60-80%**
- ICT sector contributes approximately **2-3% of global carbon emissions**
- Annual server energy cost calculation: Power (kW) × Hours × Days × Rate (₹/kWh)
- Power management can reduce desktop energy consumption by **30-40%**

## Key Points

1. Green IT operates on three pillars: Environmental Sustainability, Economic Viability, and Social Responsibility

2. The four dimensions of Green IT are: Green Data Centers, Green Hardware, Green Software, and Green IT Governance

3. Virtualisation and cloud computing are primary strategies for reducing IT energy consumption

4. Green procurement involves selecting Energy Star certified and EPEAT rated products

5. E-waste management follows the 3R principle: Reduce, Reuse, Recycle

6. Power management techniques include sleep mode, hibernate, and processor throttling

7. Hot/cold aisle containment is a common technique for improving data center energy efficiency

8. Green IT reduces both operational costs and environmental impact simultaneously

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Confusing sleep mode with hibernate - sleep uses RAM, hibernate uses disk storage
- Assuming Green IT is only about energy efficiency - it includes e-waste and lifecycle management
- Overlooking the economic benefits of Green IT - cost savings are substantial
- Neglecting the social responsibility aspect of Green IT initiatives
