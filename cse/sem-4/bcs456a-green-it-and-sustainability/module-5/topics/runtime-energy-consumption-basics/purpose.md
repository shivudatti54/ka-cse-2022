# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the fundamental relationship between power, time, and energy consumption in computing systems.

2. Distinguish between static power consumption (leakage current) and dynamic power consumption (switching activity) in runtime energy analysis.

3. Identify the hardware, software, and system factors that influence runtime energy consumption.

4. Apply the dynamic power formula (P = αCV²f) to analyze the impact of voltage and frequency scaling on energy consumption.

5. Evaluate the energy efficiency of algorithms using execution time and power consumption data.

6. Describe various power states (C-states, P-states, S-states) and their role in energy conservation.

7. Compare different energy efficiency metrics including Performance per Watt and Energy-Delay Product.

8. Propose software-level optimizations to reduce runtime energy consumption in applications.
