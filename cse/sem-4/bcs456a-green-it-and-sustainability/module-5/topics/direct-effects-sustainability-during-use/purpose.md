# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the concept of direct effects of sustainability during the use phase of IT systems and distinguish them from indirect effects.

2. Analyze energy consumption patterns in computing systems, including active, idle, and sleep power states.

3. Apply ACPI power management states (S0-S5) to minimize energy consumption in computing devices.

4. Evaluate the effectiveness of power management techniques such as virtualization, dynamic frequency scaling, and system consolidation.

5. Interpret key sustainability metrics including PUE, DCiE, CUE, WUE, and relate them to real-world data center operations.

6. Assess the role of software design and algorithmic efficiency in reducing energy consumption during the operational phase.

7. Compare different approaches to sustainable computing including traditional on-premises infrastructure versus cloud-based solutions.

8. Calculate potential energy savings and carbon emission reductions from power management implementations.
