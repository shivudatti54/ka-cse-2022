# Green Information Technology: A Sustainable Approach - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Green IT**: The practice of designing, manufacturing, using, and disposing of computing resources in an environmentally responsible manner to minimize environmental impact
- **Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE)**: A metric measuring data center efficiency, calculated as Total Facility Energy divided by IT Equipment Energy (ideal value is 1.0)
- **Virtualization**: Technology that allows multiple virtual machines to run on a single physical server, improving resource utilization and reducing energy consumption
- **E-waste**: Electronic waste containing hazardous materials requiring special disposal and recycling procedures
- **Green Software Engineering**: Development practices focused on creating energy-efficient applications through optimized algorithms and code

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **PUE Formula**: PUE = Total Facility Energy / IT Equipment Energy
- **Server Consolidation Energy Savings**: Energy saved = (Number of servers × Power per server) - (Consolidated servers × Average utilization × Power per server)
- **Energy Cost Calculation**: Annual Energy Cost = Power (kW) × Hours of Operation × Cost per kWh

## Key Points

1. Green IT addresses environmental concerns from IT operations including energy consumption, e-waste, and resource depletion
2. The five pillars of Green IT are energy efficiency, materials management, waste reduction, green architecture, and sustainable software development
3. Data centers consume significant energy; PUE is the primary metric for measuring and improving data center efficiency
4. Virtualization enables 10:1 or higher server consolidation ratios, dramatically reducing energy consumption and cooling requirements
5. Cloud computing provides shared resource models that achieve better utilization rates than traditional on-premises infrastructure
6. E-waste contains hazardous materials (lead, mercury, cadmium, chromium) requiring certified recycling and proper disposal
7. Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) makes manufacturers accountable for end-of-life product management
8. Green software engineering focuses on energy-efficient coding practices and algorithm optimization
9. Organizations benefit economically from Green IT through reduced energy costs and improved operational efficiency
10. Green IT is essential for corporate social responsibility and regulatory compliance in modern business environments

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Confusing PUE with its inverse (remember: lower PUE means better efficiency)
2. Assuming virtualization always reduces energy (it requires proper planning and management)
3. Overlooking the embodied energy in manufacturing new equipment versus extending existing equipment life
4. Choosing the cheapest e-waste disposal option without considering environmental and legal compliance
5. Neglecting the cooling system energy which often equals or exceeds server energy consumption

## Revision Tips

1. Practice PUE calculations with different scenario values to master the formula
2. Create a comparison table of Green IT strategies with their pros and cons
3. Remember 3-4 real-world examples of companies with strong Green IT initiatives
4. Focus on understanding the lifecycle approach to Green IT rather than isolated practices
5. Review virtualization and cloud computing concepts together as they are interrelated sustainability strategies
