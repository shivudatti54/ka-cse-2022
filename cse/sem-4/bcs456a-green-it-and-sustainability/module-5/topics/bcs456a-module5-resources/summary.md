# Suggested Learning Resources for Green IT and Sustainability - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Primary Resources**: Textbooks including "Green IT: Technologies and Applications" by Murugesan and Gangadharan, which forms the core reference for university examinations.

- **Secondary Resources**: Additional books like "Sustainable IT" by Leonard and reference materials from IEEE and ACM publications.

- **Online Platforms**: Coursera, edX, NPTEL, and Udemy offer supplementary courses on Green IT and sustainability topics.

- **Research Databases**: INFLIBNET provides access to journals including IEEE Transactions, ACM Computing Surveys, and Journal of Cleaner Production.

- **Practical Tools**: JouleTrack, GreenCode Analyzer, PowerTOP, VirtualBox, VMware for hands-on learning.

## Important Formulas and Metrics

- **PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness)**: Total Facility Energy ÷ IT Equipment Energy
- **Energy Consumption Calculation**: Servers × Power Rating × Operating Hours = kWh
- **Carbon Footprint**: Energy Consumption × Emission Factor (0.7 kg CO2/kWh for India)
- **Virtualization Savings**: (1 - Virtual Servers/Physical Servers) × 100%

## Key Points

1. The university prescribed textbook should be the primary study material, with 80% of exam questions drawn from it.

2. Virtualization concepts are crucial and require both theoretical understanding and practical implementation knowledge.

3. Green IT metrics including PUE, DCiE, and Carbon Footprint are frequently tested in examinations.

4. The Green IT lifecycle (design, manufacturing, usage, disposal) is a fundamental concept.

5. Indian government initiatives like National Solar Mission are important for exam preparation.

6. International standards (ISO 14001, Energy Star, EPEAT) provide frameworks for Green IT implementation.

7. Case studies from Google, Microsoft, and IBM demonstrate real-world applications.

8. Regular reading of research papers through INFLIBNET helps stay updated with latest developments.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Relying solely on online resources without studying the prescribed textbook thoroughly.

2. Neglecting practical aspects while focusing only on theoretical concepts.

3. Failing to practice numerical problems related to energy calculations.

4. Not understanding the difference between similar Green IT metrics like PUE and DCiE.

5. Ignoring Indian context and government policies in answers.

## Revision Tips

1. Create a summary sheet of all Green IT metrics with formulas and ideal values.

2. Practice at least three numerical problems on energy efficiency calculations.

3. Review the Green IT framework diagram and memorize the key components.

4. Prepare short notes on at least five case studies from different industries.

5. Use the "First Reading, Second Understanding, Third Memorizing" approach for textbook chapters.

6. Solve previous years' university question papers to understand the pattern and important topics.

7. Form study groups to discuss and clarify doubts regarding complex concepts.
