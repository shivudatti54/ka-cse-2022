# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the concept of green cloud computing and its importance in the context of environmental sustainability and climate change mitigation.

2. Analyze the key architectural components and design principles that enable energy-efficient and sustainable cloud computing systems.

3. Evaluate the role of renewable energy sources in powering cloud data centers and describe strategies for transitioning to carbon-neutral cloud operations.

4. Compare traditional data center cooling approaches with advanced methods like liquid cooling and their impact on energy efficiency and sustainability.

5. Design basic workload scheduling strategies that consider carbon intensity and renewable energy availability across different geographic regions.

6. Assess the contribution of virtualization and containerization technologies to reducing the environmental footprint of cloud computing infrastructure.

7. Examine how artificial intelligence and machine learning techniques are applied to optimize energy consumption and improve sustainability in cloud environments.

8. Interpret key sustainability metrics including PUE, CUE, and WUE, and explain their significance in evaluating cloud data center performance.
