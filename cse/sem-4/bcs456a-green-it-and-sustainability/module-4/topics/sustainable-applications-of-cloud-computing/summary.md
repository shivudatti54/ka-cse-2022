# Sustainable Applications of Cloud Computing - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Sustainable Cloud Computing**: Designing and deploying cloud services that minimize environmental impact through energy efficiency, renewable energy use, and resource optimization while maintaining performance and cost-effectiveness

- **Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE)**: Metric measuring data center efficiency; PUE = Total Facility Energy / IT Equipment Energy. Optimal values are close to 1.0

- **Virtualization**: Technology enabling multiple virtual machines on single physical server, maximizing resource utilization from typical 15-25% to 80-90%

- **Serverless Computing**: Event-driven model where resources are allocated on-demand, eliminating idle server time and achieving high efficiency

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **PUE** = Total Facility Energy ÷ IT Equipment Energy
- **Energy Savings through Virtualization** = (Initial Servers × Power) - (Consolidated Servers × Power)
- **Carbon Emissions** = Energy Consumption × Carbon Intensity of Energy Source

## Key Points

1. Data centers consume 1-2% of global electricity, making cloud sustainability critical for environmental goals

2. Virtualization achieves 85%+ energy reduction through server consolidation and improved utilization rates

3. Geographic workload distribution can reduce carbon emissions by routing requests to renewable energy-powered data centers

4. Serverless computing eliminates idle resource consumption through event-driven, on-demand resource allocation

5. Major cloud providers (Google, Microsoft, Amazon) have committed to 100% renewable energy for their operations

6. Multi-cloud and hybrid cloud strategies enable optimization of workload placement for sustainability

7. Green cloud metrics like PUE, Carbon Efficiency, and VMER help measure and compare environmental performance

8. "Follow the sun" scheduling processes workloads during daylight hours in regions with abundant solar energy

9. Containerization technologies (Docker, Kubernetes) enable finer-grained resource allocation than traditional virtualization

10. Sustainable cloud computing offers dual benefits of reduced operational costs and decreased environmental impact

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Confusing PUE with energy consumption—PUE is a ratio, not an absolute measure
- Assuming all cloud providers have equal sustainability performance—research specific provider commitments
- Overlooking the embodied energy in hardware manufacturing when assessing cloud sustainability
- Ignoring the carbon intensity of different energy sources when comparing data center locations

## Revision Tips

1. Practice PUE calculations with different scenarios to master this frequently-tested concept

2. Remember the progression from physical servers → virtualization → containers → serverless as increasingly efficient models

3. Associate each cloud service model (IaaS, PaaS, SaaS) with different sustainability responsibilities

4. Review case studies of major cloud providers' sustainability commitments and achievements

5. Focus on understanding how each technology (virtualization, scheduling, serverless) contributes to energy efficiency
