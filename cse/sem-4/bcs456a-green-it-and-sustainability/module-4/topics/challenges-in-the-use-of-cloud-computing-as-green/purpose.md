# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the concept of cloud computing as Green IT and identify why it is often promoted as an environmentally sustainable solution.

2. Analyze the major challenges that prevent cloud computing from being automatically considered a green technology, including energy consumption, virtualization overhead, and network transmission costs.

3. Evaluate the impact of data center location and energy source selection on the carbon footprint of cloud services.

4. Describe the rebound effect in cloud computing and explain how increased efficiency can paradoxically lead to higher overall energy consumption.

5. Assess the role of virtualization in creating both benefits and challenges for sustainable cloud computing.

6. Identify the e-waste challenges associated with hardware lifecycle management in cloud data centers.

7. Apply the concepts learned to analyze whether specific cloud migration scenarios would result in actual environmental benefits.

8. Critically evaluate the claims of cloud providers regarding their sustainability practices and understand the limitations of current transparency and standardization efforts.
