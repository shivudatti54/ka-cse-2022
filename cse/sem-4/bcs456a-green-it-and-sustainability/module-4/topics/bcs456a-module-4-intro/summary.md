# Green Data Centers and Energy-Efficient Computing - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Green Data Center**: A facility designed, built, and operated to minimize environmental impact through energy-efficient hardware, renewable energy, and sustainable practices.
- **PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness)**: Metric measuring data center efficiency; PUE = Total Facility Energy / IT Equipment Energy (lower is better).
- **Virtualization**: Technology enabling multiple virtual machines on single physical server, improving utilization from 15-25% to 70-80%.
- **Free Cooling**: Using ambient temperatures to reduce cooling loads without compressor-based cooling.
- **Carbon Usage Effectiveness (CUE)**: Metric measuring carbon emissions per unit of IT equipment energy consumed.

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **PUE Calculation**: PUE = Total Facility Energy (kWh) ÷ IT Equipment Energy (kWh)
- **Energy Savings**: Annual Savings = (Old Energy - New Energy) × Hours × Days × Electricity Rate
- **Consolidation Ratio**: Number of physical servers replaced ÷ Number of virtual servers hosted

## Key Points

- Data centers consume ~200 terawatt-hours globally; energy costs typically represent 30-50% of operational expenses.
- Traditional data centers have PUE of 1.8-2.5; green data centers achieve PUE below 1.2.
- Virtualization reduces server count by 80-90% while improving utilization rates significantly.
- Free cooling can reduce cooling energy consumption by 40-80% in suitable climates.
- Major tech companies (Google, Microsoft, Apple) have committed to 100% renewable energy operations.
- Green building certifications like LEED provide frameworks for sustainable data center design.
- Storage efficiency through SSDs, deduplication, and tiering reduces energy consumption.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Confusing PUE (lower is better) with efficiency percentages (higher is better)—they are inverse relationships.
- Assuming virtualization always reduces energy—it requires proper planning and right-sizing to achieve benefits.
- Overlooking the embodied energy in hardware manufacturing when calculating total environmental impact.
- Ignoring cooling system's role; cooling typically accounts for 30-40% of non-IT energy consumption.

## Revision Tips

- Memorize the PUE formula and typical values for traditional vs. green data centers.
- Remember four pillars of green data centers: Virtualization, Free Cooling, Renewable Energy, Green Building Design.
- Practice calculating PUE with given energy values; this is a common exam question.
- Understand that virtualization's primary benefit is improved utilization, not necessarily power reduction per server.
- Review real-world examples from companies like Google and Microsoft who are leaders in green data center implementation.
