# Introduction to Green IT and Sustainability - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Green IT**: Environmentally responsible design, manufacture, use, and disposal of computing resources
- **Sustainable Computing**: Meeting present computational needs without compromising future generations' ability to meet their needs
- **PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness)**: Ratio of total facility power to IT equipment power; lower values indicate better efficiency
- **CUE (Carbon Usage Effectiveness)**: Ratio of carbon emissions to energy consumption in data centers

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **PUE** = Total Facility Power (kW) / IT Equipment Power (kW)
- **CUE** = Total Carbon Emissions (kg CO2) / IT Equipment Energy Consumption (kWh)
- Energy Consumption = Power (kW) × Time (hours)
- Annual Energy Cost = Energy Consumption (kWh) × Cost per kWh

## Key Points

- ICT sector accounts for 2-3% of global carbon emissions, comparable to aviation industry
- Three pillars of sustainability: Environmental, Economic, and Social
- Lifecycle approach covers manufacturing, transportation, usage, and disposal phases
- Virtualization can reduce server energy consumption by up to 80% through consolidation
- Green software development focuses on energy-efficient coding and algorithm optimization
- E-waste contains toxic materials requiring specialized recycling processes
- Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) holds manufacturers accountable for product end-of-life
- Cloud computing enables dynamic resource allocation reducing idle capacity

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Confusing Green IT with Green Computing - they have different scopes
- Assuming only usage phase matters - manufacturing and disposal are equally important
- Focusing only on energy efficiency without considering other environmental impacts
- Ignoring the economic viability aspect of sustainability initiatives
