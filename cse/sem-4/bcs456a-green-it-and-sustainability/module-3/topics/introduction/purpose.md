# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define Green IT and explain its significance in modern computing environments, including environmental, economic, and social dimensions.

2. Describe the environmental impact of IT infrastructure, particularly data centers and electronic waste, with specific statistics and consequences.

3. Explain the three pillars of sustainability and how they apply to information technology and computing practices.

4. Calculate and interpret key green computing metrics including Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE) and Carbon Usage Effectiveness (CUE).

5. Analyze the lifecycle approach to Green IT and identify opportunities for environmental improvement at each stage.

6. Evaluate the role of virtualization and cloud computing in reducing the carbon footprint of IT operations.

7. Discuss the principles of green software development and their practical applications in software engineering.

8. Propose effective electronic waste management strategies including prevention, reuse, recycling, and policy frameworks.
