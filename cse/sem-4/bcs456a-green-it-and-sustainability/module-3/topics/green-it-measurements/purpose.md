# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the concept of carbon footprint and its significance in Green IT, including methods for calculation and reduction strategies.

2. Define and calculate Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE) and Data Center Infrastructure Efficiency (DCiE), and interpret what these values indicate about data center efficiency.

3. Apply the performance per watt metric to evaluate energy efficiency in computing environments.

4. Describe the Energy Reuse Factor (ERF) and its role in comprehensive energy assessment of data centers.

5. Explain the Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) methodology and identify the environmental impacts considered at each phase of an IT product's life cycle.

6. Evaluate organizational Green IT maturity using established maturity models and recommend improvements.

7. Analyze e-waste metrics and their importance in sustainable IT management, including generation, collection, and recycling rates.

8. Apply measurement concepts to solve numerical problems related to energy efficiency and carbon emissions in IT infrastructure.
