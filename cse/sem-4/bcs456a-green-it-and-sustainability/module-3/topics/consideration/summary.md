# Green IT Considerations - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Green IT:** Practice of using IT resources efficiently and sustainably to minimize environmental impact
- **PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness):** Metric measuring data center energy efficiency; calculated as Total Facility Energy ÷ IT Equipment Energy
- **E-Waste:** Discarded electrical or electronic equipment requiring special disposal methods
- **Virtualization:** Technology enabling multiple virtual machines on single physical hardware
- **Total Cost of Ownership (TCO):** Complete cost of IT asset including purchase, operation, maintenance, and disposal

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **PUE** = Total Facility Energy / IT Equipment Energy (Ideal = 1.0, Good < 1.5)
- **DCiE** = (1 / PUE) × 100%
- **Energy Cost** = Power (W) × Hours × Days × Rate (₹/kWh)
- **TCO** = Purchase Cost + Operating Cost + Maintenance Cost + Disposal Cost

## Key Points

- Green IT considerations span the entire hardware lifecycle: procurement, deployment, usage, and disposal
- Server virtualization can reduce energy consumption by 60-80% through better hardware utilization
- EPEAT and Energy Star certifications are key criteria for green hardware procurement
- E-waste follows hierarchy: Reduce → Reuse → Recycle → Dispose
- RoHS restricts hazardous substances; WEEE governs electronic waste disposal
- Cloud computing sustainability depends on provider's data center efficiency and renewable energy usage
- Employee awareness and organizational policies are critical for successful Green IT implementation
- Long-term cost savings often outweigh initial Green IT investments

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Considering only initial purchase cost instead of Total Cost of Ownership
- Ignoring e-waste disposal compliance requirements
- Assuming all "green" products are equally sustainable without verifying certifications
- Overlooking the importance of employee training and policy enforcement
- Neglecting data security considerations during equipment disposal

## Revision Tips

1. Memorize the PUE formula and understand what values indicate good vs. poor efficiency
2. Remember the hardware lifecycle stages and green considerations for each
3. Review the E-waste hierarchy (Reduce, Reuse, Recycle, Dispose) and associated regulations
4. Practice calculating TCO for procurement decisions using the examples as reference
5. Understand how virtualization and cloud computing contribute to Green IT objectives
