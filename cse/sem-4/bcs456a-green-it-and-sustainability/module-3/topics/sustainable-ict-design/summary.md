# Systems Engineering for Designing Sustainable ICT - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

- **Systems Engineering**: An interdisciplinary approach that enables the realization of successful systems by defining customer needs and required functionality early in the development cycle.

- **Green ICT**: Information and communication technology designed, used, and disposed of in a manner that minimizes environmental impact.

- **Design for Environment (DfE)**: Engineering approach that incorporates environmental considerations into product design including design for disassembly, recyclability, and longevity.

- **Life Cycle Assessment (LCA)**: Systematic method to evaluate environmental impacts throughout a product's entire lifecycle from raw materials to disposal.

- **Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE)**: Metric measuring data center energy efficiency calculated as Total Facility Energy / IT Equipment Energy.

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **PUE** = Total Facility Energy (kWh) ÷ IT Equipment Energy (kWh)
- Ideal PUE = 1.0, Typical = 1.5-2.0, Green < 1.3

- **Energy Savings from Virtualization** = (1 - Virtualized Servers ÷ Original Servers) × 100%
- Server utilization typically improves from 5-15% to 60-80%

- **Carbon Footprint** = Direct Emissions + Indirect Emissions + Other Indirect Emissions

- **Annual Energy Cost** = Power Load (kW) × Hours × Rate (₹/kWh)

## Key Points

- Systems engineering applies holistic lifecycle thinking to ICT sustainability

- Green ICT design principles focus on energy efficiency, resource conservation, and pollution prevention

- The sustainable systems engineering process integrates environmental considerations in all five lifecycle phases

- PUE is the primary metric for data center energy efficiency; lower values indicate better performance

- Virtualization can reduce server count by 80% while maintaining equivalent computing capacity

- Life Cycle Assessment evaluates environmental impacts from extraction to disposal

- E-waste contains hazardous materials requiring proper disposal through certified recyclers

- Cloud computing enables better resource utilization and energy efficiency through shared infrastructure

- Green software development reduces energy consumption through efficient algorithms and optimized code

- Trade-off analysis balances cost, performance, schedule, and environmental impact

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Confusing PUE with energy efficiency—PUE measures total facility overhead, not just IT equipment efficiency

2. Ignoring the use phase in LCA—the operational energy often dominates the lifecycle environmental impact

3. Assuming virtualization always reduces energy—it depends on workload consolidation and utilization rates

4. Overlooking e-waste—improper disposal of ICT equipment creates significant environmental hazards

5. Focusing only on hardware—software optimization can achieve substantial energy savings

## Revision Tips

1. Memorize the formula and ideal values for PUE as this is frequently tested

2. Understand all five phases of the systems engineering lifecycle and sustainability integration in each

3. Know the four main stages of Life Cycle Assessment

4. Be able to explain at least three benefits of virtualization for sustainability

5. Remember that cloud computing improves efficiency through economies of scale and dynamic resource allocation

6. Review green design principles: energy efficiency, resource conservation, and pollution prevention

7. Understand Design for Environment (DfE) concepts: design for disassembly, recyclability, and longevity
