### Learning Purpose: Introduction to Stacks and Queues

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Stacks and queues are the two most fundamental linear data structures with restricted access patterns. Understanding LIFO (Last In First Out) and FIFO (First In First Out) principles is essential for algorithm design and forms the basis for many computational processes.

**2. Real-world applications:**
Stacks model function calls in program execution, undo operations in software, and expression evaluation. Queues model waiting lines, print job scheduling, and message passing in distributed systems.

**3. Connection to other topics:**
This introduction provides the conceptual framework for detailed study of stack and queue operations, their array and linked list implementations, and specialized variants like circular queues, priority queues, and deques covered in this module.
