### Learning Purpose: Double-Ended Queues

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Double-ended queues (deques) allow insertion and deletion at both ends, making them more versatile than standard queues or stacks. Understanding deques provides insight into how a single data structure can support multiple access patterns.

**2. Real-world applications:**
Deques are used in palindrome checking algorithms, implementing both stack and queue behaviors, work-stealing scheduling in parallel processing, sliding window problems, and browser history management.

**3. Connection to other topics:**
This topic generalizes the concepts of stacks (LIFO) and queues (FIFO) into a single structure, connects to doubly linked list implementation, and provides a foundation for understanding more complex data structure design.
