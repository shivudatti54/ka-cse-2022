### Learning Purpose: Applications of Stacks

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Stack applications demonstrate the versatility of the LIFO principle in solving computational problems. Understanding these applications helps students recognize stack-based solutions in algorithm design, compiler construction, and expression evaluation.

**2. Real-world applications:**
Stacks are used in function call management (call stack), expression evaluation and conversion (infix to postfix), bracket matching in compilers, undo/redo functionality in editors, browser back-button navigation, and backtracking algorithms.

**3. Connection to other topics:**
This topic bridges stack theory with practical problem-solving, connects to recursion (which uses the system call stack), expression trees in Module 4, and depth-first search in graph algorithms.
