### Learning Purpose: Operations on Circular Singly Linked Lists

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Operations on circular singly linked lists require special handling since there is no NULL terminator. Understanding how to insert, delete, and traverse in a circular structure without infinite loops is crucial for correct implementation.

**2. Real-world applications:**
Circular list operations are used in implementing round-robin schedulers, managing circular buffers, cycling through application windows, and implementing token-passing protocols in networked systems.

**3. Connection to other topics:**
This topic extends the operations learned for singly linked lists to the circular variant, connects to circular queue implementation, and reinforces pointer manipulation skills needed for tree operations in Module 4.
