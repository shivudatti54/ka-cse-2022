### Learning Purpose: One-Dimensional Arrays

**1. Why is this topic important?**
One-dimensional arrays are the simplest and most commonly used data structure in programming. They provide constant-time random access to elements and form the basis for implementing other data structures like stacks, queues, and hash tables.

**2. Real-world applications:**
One-dimensional arrays are used for storing lists of items, implementing buffers in I/O operations, representing strings in C, storing sensor readings in embedded systems, and serving as the foundation for sorting and searching algorithms.

**3. Connection to other topics:**
This topic is the foundation for two-dimensional and multidimensional arrays, and directly connects to pointer arithmetic (since array names are pointers in C). Arrays are also used to implement stacks, queues, and hash tables covered in later modules.
