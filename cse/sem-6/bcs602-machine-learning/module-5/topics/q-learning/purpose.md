# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the concept of Q-learning as a model-free, off-policy reinforcement learning algorithm and its significance in solving sequential decision-making problems.

2. Understand and interpret the Q-learning update rule, including the roles of learning rate (α), discount factor (γ), and the max operator.

3. Apply the Q-learning algorithm to solve simple problems such as grid world environments, including initializing Q-values, selecting actions, and updating the Q-table.

4. Analyze the trade-off between exploration and exploitation using ε-greedy strategy and its impact on learning performance.

5. Understand the convergence conditions of Q-learning and the factors affecting convergence such as learning rate, discount factor, and state-action space size.

6. Compare Q-learning with other reinforcement learning algorithms, particularly SARSA (on-policy vs off-policy), highlighting their respective advantages and limitations.

7. Design and implement a Q-learning solution for a given problem scenario by defining states, actions, rewards, and appropriate hyperparameters.
