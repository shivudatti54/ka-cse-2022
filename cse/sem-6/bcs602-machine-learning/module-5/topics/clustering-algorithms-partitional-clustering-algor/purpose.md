# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental principles and objectives of partitional clustering algorithms in unsupervised learning.

2. Apply the K-Means clustering algorithm to partition datasets into K clusters by minimizing within-cluster variance.

3. Implement the K-Medoids clustering algorithm and distinguish its advantages over K-Means for handling noise and outliers.

4. Analyze density-based clustering methods such as DBSCAN to identify clusters of arbitrary shape and detect outliers.

5. Evaluate grid-based clustering approaches and explain their efficiency in handling high-dimensional data.

6. Compare and contrast different clustering algorithms based on their computational complexity, scalability, and suitability for various data distributions.

7. Select appropriate clustering techniques for specific real-world applications such as customer segmentation and anomaly detection.
