# Scope of Reinforcement Learning

## Overview

Reinforcement Learning (RL) enables agents to learn optimal behavior through trial and error interactions with an environment. Its scope extends across numerous industries, transforming how machines make decisions.

## Key Applications

- **Robotics**: Industrial automation, robot manipulation, legged locomotion, assembly line optimization
- **Game Playing**: AlphaGo, video games, procedural content generation, NPC behavior
- **Autonomous Vehicles**: Self-driving cars, motion planning, traffic management, drone control
- **Recommendation Systems**: Streaming services, e-commerce, personalized content, dynamic pricing
- **Resource Management**: Cloud computing, energy grids, healthcare scheduling, supply chain optimization

## Important Concepts

- **Self-Play**: RL agents improve by playing against themselves, discovering novel strategies
- **Partial Observability**: Handling incomplete environment information in real-world scenarios
- **Exploration vs Exploitation**: Balancing trying new actions with leveraging known rewards
- **Long-term Optimization**: RL considers cumulative future rewards, not just immediate gains

## Notes

- RL requires only environment definition and reward function - highly flexible framework
- Deep RL combines reinforcement learning with deep neural networks for complex tasks
- Real-world RL often requires simulation training due to safety and cost constraints
- Multi-armed bandits represent simplified RL for online optimization problems
