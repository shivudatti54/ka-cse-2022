# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. **Define and formulate** the Multi-Armed Bandit (MAB) problem, including its mathematical representation with K arms, reward distributions, and time horizon

2. **Explain the explore-exploitation tradeoff** and articulate why it is the central challenge in bandit problems, with examples of pure exploration and pure exploitation strategies

3. **Implement epsilon-greedy algorithm** with its exploration probability parameter, understanding how to balance between random exploration and greedy exploitation

4. **Apply Upper Confidence Bound (UCB)** algorithm using its formula with confidence bounds, and explain how the exploration bonus decreases for well-explored arms

5. **Utilize Thompson Sampling** with Beta distributions for binary rewards, understanding its Bayesian approach and probabilistic exploration mechanism

6. **Classify reinforcement learning problem types** including MAB, contextual bandit, episodic RL, and partially observable RL based on information availability and decision structure
