# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Describe the structure and components of an artificial neuron (inputs, weights, bias, activation function, output)
2. Explain how an artificial neuron computes its output through weighted summation and activation
3. Compare biological neurons with artificial neurons, identifying analogies and differences
4. Describe common activation functions: step, sigmoid, tanh, and ReLU
5. Understand the mathematical formulation of a single artificial neuron
6. Explain the role of weights and bias in determining the neuron's output
