# Challenges of ANN

Summary

- ANNs face several challenges, including data quality issues, overfitting, underfitting, computational complexity, and interpretability.
- Data quality issues include noisy data, missing values, and imbalanced data.
- Overfitting and underfitting are common problems in ANN training.
- Computational complexity depends on model size, data size, and algorithm complexity.
- Interpretability is a major challenge in ANNs.

### Important Formulas, Definitions, and Theorems

- Overfitting: when a model is too complex and fits the training data too closely.
- Underfitting: when a model is too simple and fails to capture the underlying patterns in the data.
- Regularization: a technique to prevent overfitting by adding a penalty term to the loss function.
- Early stopping: a technique to prevent overfitting by stopping the training process when the model's performance on the validation set starts to degrade.

### Key Points

- ANNs require high-quality data to learn and generalize well.
- Overfitting and underfitting can be addressed using techniques such as regularization, early stopping, and dropout.
- Computational complexity is a major challenge in ANNs.
- Interpretability is a major challenge in ANNs.
- ANNs have many applications, including image recognition, natural language processing, and decision-making.
- ANNs have several advantages, including the ability to learn from data and improve their performance over time.
- ANNs have several disadvantages, including the risk of overfitting and the need for large amounts of data.
