### Learning Purpose: Advantages and Disadvantages of ANN

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the strengths and limitations of Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs) is crucial for selecting the right tool for a machine learning problem. Blindly applying ANNs can lead to inefficient use of computational resources, poor model performance, or failure to meet project requirements. This knowledge ensures informed, effective, and responsible design of ML systems.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to systematically evaluate when to use an ANN and when an alternative model might be superior. They will identify key advantages, such as the ability to learn complex non-linear patterns and handle high-dimensional data. Conversely, they will examine critical disadvantages, including high computational cost, "black box" nature, need for large datasets, and proneness to overfitting.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly connects to prior knowledge of simpler models (like linear regression or decision trees), providing a comparative framework. It also sets the stage for subsequent modules on specialized architectures (like CNNs and RNNs) and techniques designed to mitigate ANN drawbacks, such as regularization and transfer learning.

**4. Real-world applications**
This evaluation is essential across industries. For instance, ANNs are advantageous for image recognition in healthcare diagnostics but may be disadvantageous for a credit scoring application where model interpretability is legally required, making a simpler, more transparent model a better choice.
