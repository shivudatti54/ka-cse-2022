### Learning Purpose: JLabel and ImageIcon

**1. Why is this topic important?**
JLabel and ImageIcon are the primary Swing components for displaying non-editable text and images in a GUI. They are essential for creating user-friendly, informative, and visually engaging Java desktop applications, from simple text prompts and status messages to image galleries and icon-driven interfaces.

**2. Real-world applications:**
These components are used in creating form labels and field descriptions, displaying product images in inventory management tools, showing status icons and notification badges, building splash screens and about dialogs, and rendering branding elements in desktop applications.

**3. Connection to other topics:**
JLabel and ImageIcon build on Components and Containers (JLabel is a JComponent placed in containers), connect to the Swing Packages (javax.swing), and provide a foundation for understanding how other components like JButton and JToggleButton can also display text and icons through similar APIs.
