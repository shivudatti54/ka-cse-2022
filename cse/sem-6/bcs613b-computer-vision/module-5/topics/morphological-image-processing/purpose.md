# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define morphological image processing and explain its fundamental purpose in computer vision applications.

2. Describe the concept of a structuring element and explain how its size and shape affect morphological operations.

3. Perform erosion and dilation operations on binary images and explain their effects on object boundaries and size.

4. Apply opening and closing operations and explain their combined effects on image smoothing and feature restoration.

5. Explain the hit-or-miss transform and its role in pattern detection and shape analysis.

6. Design morphological algorithms for specific applications such as boundary extraction, noise removal, and gap filling.

7. Select appropriate morphological operations and structuring elements based on the requirements of a given image processing problem.
