# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental concepts of mathematical morphology and its role in image processing.

2. Perform set operations including union, intersection, complement, difference, translation, and reflection on binary image sets.

3. Define and describe the purpose and properties of a structuring element in morphological image processing.

4. Explain the geometric interpretation of translation and reflection operations on image sets.

5. Identify different types of structuring elements (square, cross, diamond) and explain their effects on morphological operations.

6. Understand the representation of binary images as sets and how this enables mathematical manipulation.

7. Apply the mathematical foundations to prepare for understanding erosion, dilation, opening, and closing operations.
