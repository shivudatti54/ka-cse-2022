# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. **Understand** the mathematical model of image degradation in the presence of noise, including the additive noise model $g(x,y) = f(x,y) + \eta(x,y)$

2. **Explain** the characteristics of different noise types (Gaussian, salt-and-pepper, impulse, and periodic noise) and their impact on image quality

3. **Apply** spatial domain filtering techniques such as arithmetic mean, geometric mean, harmonic, and contraharmonic mean filters for noise removal

4. **Apply** median filters and max/min filters for effective removal of salt-and-pepper and impulse noise while preserving edges

5. **Analyze** the effectiveness of various noise reduction methods based on noise type and image content

6. **Design** appropriate restoration filters by selecting suitable filter parameters based on noise characteristics and application requirements

7. **Implement** the Wiener filter for image restoration considering noise power spectrum and original image power spectrum

8. **Evaluate** restoration results by comparing filtered outputs using qualitative visual analysis and quantitative error metrics
