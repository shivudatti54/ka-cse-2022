# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Understand the characteristics of periodic noise and its mathematical representation in spatial and frequency domains.

2. Explain the relationship between periodic noise patterns and their corresponding locations in the frequency domain.

3. Apply the Fourier Transform to identify the frequency components associated with periodic noise in degraded images.

4. Analyze the structure and behavior of band-reject filters and notch filters for periodic noise reduction.

5. Design appropriate frequency domain filtering solutions based on the observed noise pattern in the frequency spectrum.

6. Implement band-reject filtering techniques to remove specific periodic noise components from images.

7. Evaluate the restoration quality by comparing the filtered output with the original degraded image.

8. Apply periodic noise reduction techniques to practical image restoration problems in fields such as medical imaging and remote sensing.
