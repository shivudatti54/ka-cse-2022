This module provides comprehensive coverage of parallel and distributed computing paradigms essential for cloud computing applications. Students will understand theoretical foundations including Amdahl's Law and Gustafson's Law for performance analysis, master key paradigms (data, task, pipeline parallelism), and learn distributed models (client-server, P2P, MapReduce, Actor Model, microservices). The module enables selection of appropriate architectural patterns based on workload characteristics, scalability requirements, and fault tolerance objectives, preparing students for designing and implementing cloud-native distributed systems.
