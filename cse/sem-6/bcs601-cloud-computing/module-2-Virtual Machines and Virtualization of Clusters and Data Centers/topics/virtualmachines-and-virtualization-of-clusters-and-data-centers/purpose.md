# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the architecture of virtual machines and differentiate between Type-1 and Type-2 hypervisors with respect to performance characteristics and deployment scenarios.

2. Describe the architectural components of virtual clusters and their role in aggregating physical resources into unified capacity pools.

3. Analyze the live VM migration process, including pre-copy and post-copy techniques, and evaluate their trade-offs in terms of migration time and downtime.

4. Design a Software-Defined Data Center (SDDC) architecture by integrating compute, storage, and network virtualization components.

5. Evaluate resource management strategies including CPU scheduling, memory overcommitment, and quality of service enforcement in virtualized environments.

6. Calculate resource utilization metrics, overcommitment ratios, and VM density for cluster capacity planning scenarios.

7. Compare High Availability and Fault Tolerance mechanisms and select appropriate strategies based on business continuity requirements.

8. Apply virtualization concepts to solve real-world cluster management problems involving resource allocation, migration planning, and performance optimization.
