This module provides comprehensive coverage of scalable computing principles for B.Tech/MSc/MCA students at Standard level. It establishes rigorous theoretical foundations through Amdahl's and Gustafson's Laws with mathematical proofs, enabling students to analyze scalability limits quantitatively. The curriculum addresses CAP theorem trade-offs essential for distributed system design, presents load balancing algorithms with complexity analysis, and demonstrates real-world architectural decisions through application-based assessment. Successful completion equips students to evaluate scalability approaches, select appropriate strategies for given constraints, and analyze performance implications of distributed computing decisions.
