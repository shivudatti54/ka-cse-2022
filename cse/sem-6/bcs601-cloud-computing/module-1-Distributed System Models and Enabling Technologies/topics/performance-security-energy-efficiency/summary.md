This content addresses three critical pillars of distributed system design: performance (metrics, optimization techniques, monitoring), security (CIA triad, cryptographic mechanisms, access control models, cloud-specific considerations), and energy efficiency (PUE, DVFS, VM consolidation, cooling optimization). The material incorporates rigorous mathematical formulations including Amdahl's Law for parallel processing, cryptographic complexity analysis, DVFS power models, and multi-objective optimization frameworks for balancing trade-offs between the three pillars. The content is tailored for Standard tier (B.Tech/MSc/MCA) with formal academic language, technical vocabulary, and quantitative depth appropriate for graduate-level study.
