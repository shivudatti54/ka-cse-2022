Data centers form the physical foundation of cloud computing, implementing core design principles of scalability, reliability, efficiency, and security. Modern architectures employ spine-leaf (Clos) networks that provide deterministic performance through mathematical guarantees of non-blocking operation. Key metrics include PUE for efficiency, oversubscription ratios for network performance, and fault tolerance through N+1/2N redundancy. Software-defined networking enables programmable, flexible data center operation. Understanding these principles equips students to analyze and design cloud infrastructure.
