# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. **Define** cooperating processes and distinguish them from independent processes in terms of shared resources and execution dependencies.

2. **Explain** what a race condition is and identify scenarios where race conditions can occur in concurrent process execution.

3. **Describe** the critical section problem and its three essential requirements: mutual exclusion, progress, and bounded waiting.

4. **Analyze** how synchronization hardware instructions like Test-and-Set and Compare-and-Swap provide atomic operations for implementing synchronization primitives.

5. **Apply** mutex locks to solve simple mutual exclusion problems and protect shared resources from concurrent access.

6. **Compare** the capabilities of different synchronization primitives including mutexes and semaphores, understanding when each is appropriate.

7. **Design** synchronization solutions for classic problems like the producer-consumer problem using semaphores.

8. **Evaluate** whether a given synchronization solution satisfies all three requirements of the critical section problem.
