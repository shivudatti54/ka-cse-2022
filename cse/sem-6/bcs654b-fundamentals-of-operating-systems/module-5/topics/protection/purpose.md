# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental goals and principles of protection in operating systems, including the principle of least privilege.

2. Describe the concept of protection domains and how they relate to processes and objects in a system.

3. Understand and represent protection policies using the access matrix model.

4. Differentiate between Access Control Lists (ACLs) and Capability lists, analyzing their advantages and disadvantages.

5. Explain how protection rings provide hierarchical privilege levels and their implementation in modern processors.

6. Analyze the authentication and authorization mechanisms used in operating systems for user verification and access control.

7. Interpret Unix-style file permissions and trace the access decision process for file operations.

8. Evaluate protection mechanisms in terms of security, efficiency, and manageability considerations.

9. Identify potential vulnerabilities in protection mechanisms and understand the principle of complete mediation.
