# Deploying Smart Contracts on a Blockchain

Summary

- Smart contracts are self-executing contracts with the terms of the agreement written directly into lines of code.
- The process of deploying a smart contract on a blockchain involves writing, compiling, deploying, and instantiating the contract.
- There are several platforms that allow developers to deploy smart contracts, including Ethereum, Hyperledger Fabric, and Corda.
- There are several tools that can be used to deploy smart contracts, including Truffle, Web3.js, and Fabric SDK.

### Important Formulas, Definitions, and Theorems

- Smart contract: A self-executing contract with the terms of the agreement written directly into lines of code.
- Deployment: The process of sending a smart contract to the blockchain.
- Instantiation: The process of creating an instance of a smart contract and setting its initial state.
- Gas: A unit of measurement for the computational effort required to execute a smart contract.

### Key Points

- Smart contracts are a key feature of blockchain technology.
- The process of deploying a smart contract involves several steps, including writing, compiling, deploying, and instantiating the contract.
- There are several platforms and tools that can be used to deploy smart contracts.
- Smart contract security and testing are crucial to ensure the integrity of the contract.
- Gas is an important concept in smart contract deployment, as it determines the computational effort required to execute the contract.
