**Module 3: The Structure of a Block Header**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
The block header is the critical component that ensures blockchain's core properties of immutability and security. Understanding its structure is fundamental to grasping how trust is established in a decentralized network without a central authority.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to deconstruct and explain each field within a block header (e.g., previous block hash, Merkle root, timestamp, nonce, difficulty target). They will understand how these elements work in concert to cryptographically link blocks, secure the chain's history, and facilitate the consensus mechanism, specifically Proof-of-Work.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon prior knowledge of hashing and Merkle Trees. It is the essential prerequisite for understanding mining and consensus algorithms (Module 4), as the header is what miners hash to solve the cryptographic puzzle. It also provides the foundation for exploring blockchain forks and scalability challenges.

**4. Real-world applications**
Analyzing a block header allows one to verify a transaction's inclusion in a specific block on a blockchain explorer. This skill is vital for developers building smart contracts that require verified past data (oracles) and for auditors conducting forensic analysis of blockchain transactions to ensure their validity and immutability.
