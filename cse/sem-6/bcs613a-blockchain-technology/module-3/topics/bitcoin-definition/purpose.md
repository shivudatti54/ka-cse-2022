### Learning Purpose: Module 3 - Bitcoin Definition

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding Bitcoin's definition is foundational, as it is the first and most prominent application of blockchain technology. It establishes the core principles of decentralized digital currency, providing a concrete example of how cryptographic proof replaces trusted third parties in financial transactions.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to precisely define Bitcoin as a decentralized peer-to-peer network, a protocol, and a digital asset (cryptocurrency). They will dissect its core components: the blockchain, mining, wallets, and transactions. Crucially, they will understand how these elements work together to achieve decentralization, immutability, and scarcity without a central issuing authority.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon previous modules on cryptographic hashing and distributed ledger fundamentals. Bitcoin is the practical implementation of those concepts. It also serves as the essential primer for comparing other cryptocurrencies (altcoins) and understanding more complex blockchain applications like smart contracts, which evolved from Bitcoin's innovative model.

**4. Real-world applications**
The knowledge gained is vital for engaging with the entire cryptocurrency ecosystem. It applies directly to cryptocurrency exchanges, wallets, and payment systems. Furthermore, analyzing Bitcoin provides critical insights into broader applications of decentralization, such as in supply chain provenance, digital identity, and the tokenization of real-world assets.
