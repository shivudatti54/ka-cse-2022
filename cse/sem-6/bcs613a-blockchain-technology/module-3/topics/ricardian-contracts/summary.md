# Ricardian Contracts

## Overview

Ricardian contracts are hybrid legal-technical documents that combine human-readable legal prose with machine-readable code, creating legally binding agreements that can be automatically executed and verified. Proposed by Ian Grigg, they bridge the gap between traditional legal contracts and smart contracts.

## Key Points

- **Dual Format**: Contains both legal text (human-readable) and structured data (machine-readable)
- **Cryptographic Signing**: Digitally signed by parties, creating tamper-evident agreements
- **Unique Identifier**: Hashed to create unique contract ID for reference and verification
- **Legal Binding**: Designed to be legally enforceable in traditional court systems
- **Machine Processing**: Structured format enables automated execution and compliance checking
- **Parameter Specification**: Precisely defines financial and operational parameters in both formats
- **Issuer Signature**: Cryptographically signed by contract issuer establishing authenticity

## Important Concepts

- Addresses smart contract limitation of pure code lacking legal context
- Enables traditional legal enforcement while maintaining blockchain benefits
- Combines best of both worlds: code precision and legal interpretation flexibility
- Hash serves as contract fingerprint ensuring document integrity
- Particularly useful for financial instruments and formal business agreements

## Notes

- Understand difference from pure smart contracts (includes legal prose)
- Know advantages: legal enforceability, human readability, machine execution
- Be able to explain use cases: securities, bonds, financial derivatives
- Remember cryptographic signature ensures contract authenticity and integrity
