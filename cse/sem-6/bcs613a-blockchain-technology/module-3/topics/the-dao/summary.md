# The DAO

## Summary

The DAO was a decentralized autonomous organization that was created on the Ethereum blockchain in 2016. It was designed to be a decentralized investment fund, where users could pool their resources and vote on how to invest them. However, the DAO was hacked in June 2016, resulting in the loss of millions of dollars' worth of Ether.

### Important Formulas, Definitions, and Theorems

- DAO: Decentralized Autonomous Organization
- Smart Contract: A self-executing contract with the terms of the agreement written directly into lines of code.
- Decentralized Governance: A governance structure where users can vote on how to invest the funds.

### Key Points

- The DAO was a complex smart contract that consisted of multiple components.
- The DAO had a decentralized governance structure, where users could vote on how to invest the funds.
- The DAO was designed to be autonomous, meaning that it could execute transactions without the need for human intervention.
- The DAO hack was caused by a vulnerability in the DAO's smart contract.
- The hack resulted in the loss of millions of dollars' worth of Ether.
- The DAO hack highlighted the importance of secure smart contract development and the need for thorough testing and auditing of smart contracts.
