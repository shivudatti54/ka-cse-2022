### Learning Purpose: Module 3 - The Transaction Structure

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the transaction structure is fundamental because it is the atomic unit of data that powers all blockchain operations. It dictates how value and information are securely and immutably recorded. Without a deep comprehension of its components, one cannot grasp how consensus is achieved, how smart contracts are executed, or how the integrity of the entire ledger is maintained.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will deconstruct a standard transaction to learn its core components, such as transaction inputs (UTXOs or account balances), outputs, digital signatures, transaction IDs, and gas fees. They will learn how these elements work together to create a verifiable and tamper-proof record of exchange on a distributed network.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This knowledge directly connects to previous modules on cryptography (digital signatures) and decentralization. It forms the essential building block for subsequent topics like consensus mechanisms (how transactions are validated), smart contracts (which are triggered by transactions), and blockchain security (preventing double-spending).

**4. Real-world applications**
Analyzing transaction data is crucial for developing wallets, payment systems, and blockchain explorers. It is also key for auditing trails in supply chain management, verifying asset ownership in NFTs, and understanding fee dynamics in networks like Bitcoin and Ethereum, which impact user experience and scalability.
