### Learning Purpose: Module 3 - Wallets

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Cryptocurrency wallets are the fundamental gateway for users to interact with blockchain networks. Understanding them is crucial because they are responsible for securing digital assets and managing the cryptographic keys that prove ownership. A lack of knowledge in this area is a primary cause of asset loss and security breaches in the crypto space.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core mechanics of wallets, including the differences between public addresses and private keys. They will explore various wallet types—such as software (hot) wallets (web, mobile, desktop), hardware (cold) wallets, and custodial vs. non-custodial models—analyzing their respective security trade-offs, advantages, and disadvantages.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon previous modules on public-key cryptography and transaction structures. The knowledge of how keys are generated and managed is essential for understanding how transactions are signed and broadcast to the network. It also provides the practical foundation for subsequent modules on decentralized applications (dApps) and DeFi protocols, which require wallet interaction.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied whenever an individual holds or transacts with cryptocurrency, from a simple Bitcoin transfer to participating in NFT marketplaces or staking tokens in a DeFi protocol. Choosing and using an appropriate wallet is a critical, everyday skill for any individual or business operating in the blockchain ecosystem.
