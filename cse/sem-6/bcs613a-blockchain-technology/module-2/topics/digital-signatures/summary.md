# Digital Signatures

Summary

- A digital signature is a cryptographic technique used to verify the authenticity and integrity of a digital message or document.
- Digital signatures use a combination of cryptographic algorithms and the sender's private key to generate a unique code or string of characters.
- The process of creating a digital signature involves hashing, encryption, appending, and verification.

### Important Formulas, Definitions, and Theorems

- Hash function: A one-way function that generates a unique fixed-size string of characters from the digital message or document.
- Symmetric cryptography: Uses the same key for encryption and decryption.
- Asymmetric cryptography: Uses a pair of keys, one for encryption (public key) and another for decryption (private key).

### Key Points

- Digital signatures verify the identity of the sender and ensure the integrity of the message.
- Digital signatures use a combination of cryptographic algorithms and the sender's private key.
- There are two primary types of digital signatures: symmetric and asymmetric.
- Digital signatures have numerous applications in various fields, including blockchain, e-commerce, and digital contracts.
- Cryptographic primitives used in digital signatures include hash functions, symmetric cryptography, and asymmetric cryptography.
- Digital signatures are essential in ensuring the integrity and authenticity of digital messages or documents.
