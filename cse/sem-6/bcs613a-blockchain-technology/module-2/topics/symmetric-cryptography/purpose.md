# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define symmetric cryptography and explain how it uses a single shared secret key for both encryption and decryption.
2. Describe the two main types of symmetric ciphers: block ciphers and stream ciphers, with examples.
3. Explain the operation of AES (Advanced Encryption Standard) at a high level including key sizes and rounds.
4. Analyze the role of symmetric cryptography in blockchain for data encryption, key derivation, and wallet security.
5. Compare symmetric and asymmetric cryptography in terms of speed, key management, and use cases.
6. Identify common modes of operation for block ciphers (ECB, CBC, CTR) and their security properties.
