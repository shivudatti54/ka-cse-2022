# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the architecture and working principles of Distributed Hash Tables (DHTs) in decentralized systems.
2. Describe how DHTs enable peer-to-peer data storage, lookup, and retrieval mechanisms.
3. Compare different DHT algorithms such as Chord, Kademlia, and Pastry based on routing efficiency and scalability.
4. Analyze how DHTs provide fault tolerance and load balancing in distributed networks.
5. Identify real-world applications of DHTs in blockchain ecosystems, including IPFS and BitTorrent.
6. Evaluate the security challenges and limitations of DHTs in decentralized applications.
7. Design basic DHT-based solutions for managing data in blockchain and peer-to-peer systems.
