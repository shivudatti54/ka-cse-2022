# Decentralized Applications

## Summary

Decentralized applications (dApps) are built on blockchain technology, enabling the creation of secure, transparent, and community-driven applications. dApps are characterized by decentralization, autonomy, open-source, and transparency, and offer several benefits, including security, censorship resistance, transparency, and community-driven development.

### Important Formulas, Definitions, and Theorems

- **Decentralized application (dApp)**: A application built on blockchain technology, enabling decentralization, autonomy, and transparency.
- **Smart contract**: A self-executing contract with the terms of the agreement written directly into lines of code.
- **Blockchain**: A decentralized and distributed ledger technology, enabling secure and transparent data storage.

### Key Points

- Decentralized applications are built on blockchain technology.
- dApps are characterized by decentralization, autonomy, open-source, and transparency.
- dApps offer several benefits, including security, censorship resistance, transparency, and community-driven development.
- Popular platforms for building dApps include Ethereum, Polkadot, and Solana.
- Examples of dApps include Uniswap, MakerDAO, and OpenSea.
- The development process of dApps involves the use of smart contracts and blockchain technology.
