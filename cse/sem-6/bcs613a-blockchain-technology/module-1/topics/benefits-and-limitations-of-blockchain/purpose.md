# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Identify and explain the six primary benefits of blockchain technology: decentralization, transparency, immutability, security, traceability, and cost reduction.

2. Analyze how blockchain's distributed ledger architecture eliminates single points of failure and enhances system resilience.

3. Evaluate the trade-offs between blockchain's security features and performance limitations, particularly regarding transaction throughput.

4. Compare the scalability challenges of public blockchains with private/permissioned blockchain solutions.

5. Assess the environmental and regulatory implications of different consensus mechanisms used in blockchain networks.

6. Determine appropriate use cases by analyzing when blockchain advantages outweigh its limitations compared to traditional centralized systems.

7. Critically examine the irreversibility characteristic as both a benefit (preventing fraud) and a limitation (preventing error correction).

8. Apply the decentralization-security-scalability trilemma to evaluate blockchain architecture decisions.
