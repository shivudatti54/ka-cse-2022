# Tiers of Blockchain Technology

## Overview

Blockchains are categorized into three tiers based on access permissions: public (permissionless), private (permissioned), and consortium (federated). Additionally, blockchains can be classified architecturally as Layer 1 (base protocol) or Layer 2 (scaling solutions), addressing the blockchain trilemma of decentralization, security, and scalability.

## Key Points

- **Public Blockchain**: Permissionless, fully decentralized networks like Bitcoin and Ethereum where anyone can participate
- **Private Blockchain**: Permissioned networks controlled by single organizations with restricted access and centralized governance
- **Consortium Blockchain**: Semi-decentralized networks governed by pre-selected group of organizations balancing trust and efficiency
- **Layer 1**: Base blockchain protocol providing ultimate security but facing scalability challenges
- **Layer 2**: Scaling solutions built on L1 processing transactions off-chain while leveraging L1 security
- **Blockchain Trilemma**: Difficulty achieving perfect decentralization, security, and scalability simultaneously
- **Scaling Solutions**: State channels, sidechains, and rollups (ZK-Rollups, Optimistic Rollups) increase throughput

## Important Concepts

- Choice between tiers involves trade-offs: speed vs decentralization, privacy vs transparency
- Public blockchains sacrifice performance for trustlessness
- Private blockchains achieve higher throughput but require trust in central authority
- L2 solutions enhance scalability without compromising L1 security
- Hybrid blockchains combine private operations with public audit trails

## Notes

- Know the comparison table: access, power distribution, efficiency, transparency for each tier
- Understand why L2 solutions are necessary (L1 scalability limitations)
- Be able to explain the blockchain trilemma and how different designs address it
