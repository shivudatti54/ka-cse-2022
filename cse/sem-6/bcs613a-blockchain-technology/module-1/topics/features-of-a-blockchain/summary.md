# Features of a Blockchain

## Overview

Blockchain's transformative power derives from seven core features: decentralization, immutability, transparency, security, consensus, distributed ledger architecture, and faster settlement. These features enable applications across finance, supply chain, healthcare, government, and many other domains while facing challenges in scalability, energy consumption, and regulatory uncertainty.

## Key Points

- **Decentralization**: P2P network with no central authority eliminates single points of failure and censorship risk
- **Immutability**: Cryptographic hashing makes recorded transactions extremely difficult to alter or delete
- **Transparency**: Public blockchains allow anyone to view and audit all transaction history
- **Security**: Public-key cryptography and distributed architecture protect against unauthorized access and attacks
- **Consensus Mechanisms**: PoW, PoS, and PBFT enable trustless agreement on ledger state
- **Distributed Ledger**: Replicated and synchronized across entire network of participants
- **Faster Settlement**: 24/7 operation enables transaction settlement in minutes/hours vs days

## Important Concepts

- Features interconnect - decentralization enables transparency, which builds trust
- Public vs private blockchains differ in transparency and access control
- CAP theorem impacts: blockchain chooses Consistency and Partition Tolerance
- Applications leverage specific features (e.g., immutability for supply chain tracking)
- Limitations include scalability issues, high energy consumption (PoW), and regulatory uncertainty
