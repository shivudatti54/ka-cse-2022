# Gas

## Summary

- Gas is a unit of measurement that represents the amount of computational effort required to execute a transaction or a smart contract on the Ethereum blockchain.
- Gas is needed to prevent denial-of-service (DoS) attacks on the Ethereum network.
- Gas works by requiring users to specify the amount of gas they are willing to pay for transaction or contract execution.
- Gas price is the amount of Ether (ETH) that a user is willing to pay for each unit of gas.
- Gas limit is the maximum amount of gas that a user is willing to spend on a transaction or contract execution.

### Important Formulas, Definitions, and Theorems

- Gas = unit of measurement for computational effort
- Gas price = amount of Ether (ETH) per unit of gas
- Gas limit = maximum amount of gas for transaction or contract execution

### Key Points

- Gas is used to prevent denial-of-service (DoS) attacks.
- Gas is required for every transaction and contract execution.
- Gas price determines the priority of transaction or contract execution.
- Gas limit prevents excessive gas consumption.
- Gas is refunded to the user if not used.
- Gas is used in smart contracts to execute code.
