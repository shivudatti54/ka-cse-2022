# Mining

## Summary

Mining is a crucial component of the Ethereum network, allowing it to function as a decentralized, trustless system. Miners compete to solve complex mathematical puzzles, validating transactions and creating new blocks in the process.

### Important Formulas, Definitions, and Theorems

- Ethash: a modified version of the Keccak-256 hash function used by Ethereum as its proof-of-work algorithm.
- Proof-of-work (PoW): a consensus algorithm that requires miners to solve a complex mathematical puzzle to validate transactions and create new blocks.
- Mining pool: a group of miners that work together to mine Ethereum, dividing the mining process into smaller tasks and distributing the rewards among members.

### Key Points

- Miners use powerful computers to solve complex mathematical puzzles, validating transactions and creating new blocks.
- The miner who solves the puzzle first gets to create a new block and add it to the blockchain, earning a reward in the form of Ether (ETH).
- Ethereum uses a proof-of-work (PoW) consensus algorithm, which requires miners to solve a complex mathematical puzzle to validate transactions and create new blocks.
- Miners use a variety of hardware to mine Ethereum, including CPU, GPU, and ASIC mining.
- Miners use a variety of software to mine Ethereum, including geth, ethminer, and Genoil miner.
- Mining pools are groups of miners that work together to mine Ethereum, dividing the mining process into smaller tasks and distributing the rewards among members.
