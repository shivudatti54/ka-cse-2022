### Learning Purpose: Module 4 - Currency (ETH and ETC)

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding Ethereum's native currencies, ETH and ETC, is fundamental. They are not just digital cash but the fuel that powers and secures their respective networks. This topic is crucial because it explores the real-world value and utility that make a blockchain functional, moving beyond abstract theory.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the distinct roles of ETH (Ether) as the economic engine of the Ethereum ecosystem and the historical context behind ETC (Ethereum Classic). Key learning points include their use for paying transaction fees (gas), staking in consensus mechanisms, and as a store of value. The module will also cover the philosophical and technical split that created the two separate currencies.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon previous modules on blockchain fundamentals, consensus mechanisms (like Proof-of-Work and Proof-of-Stake), and smart contracts. It provides a concrete application for these concepts, showing how cryptography, decentralization, and incentives combine to create a functional digital economy.

**4. Real-world applications**
Learners will see how ETH is used to interact with DeFi protocols, NFT marketplaces, and dApps. Analyzing ETC provides a critical case study on governance, immutability, and how communities respond to critical events. This knowledge is essential for anyone looking to develop on, invest in, or utilize the Ethereum network.
