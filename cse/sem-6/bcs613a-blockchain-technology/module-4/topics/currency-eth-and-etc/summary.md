# Currency ETH and ETC

## Overview

Ether (ETH) is Ethereum's native cryptocurrency used for transaction fees and value transfer, while Ethereum Classic (ETC) is a fork preserving the original chain after the 2016 DAO hard fork. Both share genesis blocks but diverged due to philosophical differences regarding immutability versus recovering stolen funds.

## Key Points

- **Ether (ETH)**: Native token of Ethereum, denominated in wei (1 ETH = 10^18 wei), gwei commonly used for gas prices
- **Gas Payments**: ETH pays for computational resources (gas) required for transactions and contract execution
- **Denominations**: Wei (base unit), Gwei (10^9 wei, for gas), Finney (10^15 wei), Ether (10^18 wei)
- **The DAO Hack (2016)**: Vulnerability exploited stealing ~3.6 million ETH from decentralized investment fund
- **Hard Fork Decision**: Community voted to reverse DAO hack through state change, creating philosophical split
- **Ethereum (ETH)**: Continued on forked chain with DAO hack reversed
- **Ethereum Classic (ETC)**: Maintained original chain preserving "code is law" philosophy

## Important Concepts

- ETH serves dual purpose: value transfer and computational fuel (gas)
- DAO fork highlighted immutability vs pragmatism debate in blockchain
- ETC represents commitment to immutability regardless of consequences
- Most developers, users, and infrastructure moved to ETH chain
- Denominations enable human-readable representation of varying value scales
