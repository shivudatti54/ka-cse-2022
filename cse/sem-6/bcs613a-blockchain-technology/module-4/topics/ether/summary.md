# Ether

## Summary

- Ether is the native cryptocurrency of the Ethereum blockchain.
- Ether is used to pay for transaction fees, computational services, and storage on the Ethereum network.
- Ether is decentralized, digital, and has a limited supply.
- Ether is divisible into smaller units called Wei.
- Ether is used to pay for gas, which incentivizes miners to validate transactions and secure the network.

### Important Formulas, Definitions, and Theorems

- Ether (ETH): the native cryptocurrency of the Ethereum blockchain.
- Gas: a unit of measurement that represents the amount of computational effort required to execute a transaction or smart contract on the Ethereum network.
- Wei: the smallest unit of Ether, equivalent to 10^-18 Ether.

### Key Points

- Ether is the fuel that powers the Ethereum network.
- Ether is used to pay for transaction fees, computational services, and storage.
- Ether is decentralized and digital.
- Ether has a limited supply and is divisible.
- Gas is used to prioritize transactions on the Ethereum network.
- Smart contracts use Ether to execute transactions and access services on the Ethereum network.
