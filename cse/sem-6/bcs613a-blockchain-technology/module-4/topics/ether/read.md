# Ether

## Introduction

Ether (ETH) is the native cryptocurrency of the Ethereum blockchain. It is used to pay for transaction fees, computational services, and storage on the Ethereum network. In this topic, we will delve into the world of Ether, exploring its role in the Ethereum ecosystem, its characteristics, and its various uses.

## What is Ether?

Ether is a decentralized digital currency that is not controlled by any government or financial institution. It is created through a process called mining, in which powerful computers solve complex mathematical problems to validate transactions on the Ethereum network. Ether is the fuel that powers the Ethereum network, allowing users to interact with smart contracts, send transactions, and access various decentralized applications (dApps).

## Characteristics of Ether

Ether has several characteristics that make it unique:

- **Decentralized**: Ether is not controlled by any central authority, making it resistant to censorship and tampering.
- **Digital**: Ether exists only in digital form, making it easily transferable and storable.
- **Limited supply**: The total supply of Ether is capped at 100 million, making it a scarce resource.
- **Divisible**: Ether can be divided into smaller units, called Wei, which is equivalent to 10^-18 Ether.

## Uses of Ether

Ether has several uses in the Ethereum ecosystem:

- **Transaction fees**: Ether is used to pay for transaction fees, which incentivizes miners to validate transactions and secure the network.
- **Computational services**: Ether is used to pay for computational services, such as executing smart contracts and accessing decentralized applications.
- **Storage**: Ether is used to pay for storage on the Ethereum network, which allows users to store data and interact with decentralized applications.

## Ether vs. Ethereum Classic

In 2016, the Ethereum network underwent a hard fork, resulting in the creation of Ethereum Classic (ETC). The main difference between Ether (ETH) and Ethereum Classic (ETC) is the underlying protocol and the community that supports it. Ether is the native cryptocurrency of the Ethereum network, while Ethereum Classic is a separate cryptocurrency that uses the same protocol but has a different set of rules and a smaller community.

## Gas and Ether

Gas is a unit of measurement that represents the amount of computational effort required to execute a transaction or smart contract on the Ethereum network. Ether is used to pay for gas, which incentivizes miners to validate transactions and secure the network. The gas price is set by the sender of the transaction, and it determines the priority of the transaction on the network.

## Smart Contracts and Ether

Smart contracts are self-executing contracts with the terms of the agreement written directly into lines of code. Ether is used to pay for the execution of smart contracts, which allows users to interact with decentralized applications and access various services on the Ethereum network.
