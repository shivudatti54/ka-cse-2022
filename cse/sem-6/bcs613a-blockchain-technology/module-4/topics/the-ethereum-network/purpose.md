# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, students should be able to:

1. Differentiate between various Ethereum client implementations and explain client diversity importance
2. Describe the layered architecture of the Ethereum stack and inter-layer interactions
3. Analyze the distinction between ETH and ETC with historical context
4. Evaluate the gas mechanism and its role in preventing resource exhaustion attacks
5. Compare Proof of Work versus Proof of Stake consensus mechanisms
6. Explain the world state model and account-based architecture
7. Distinguish between contract creation and message call transactions
8. Describe the EVM architecture and its Turing-completeness constraints
9. Identify key blockchain data structures and their cryptographic foundations
