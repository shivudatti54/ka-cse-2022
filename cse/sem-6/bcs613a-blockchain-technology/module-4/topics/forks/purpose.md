### Learning Purpose: Module 4 - Forks

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Forks are fundamental events in blockchain evolution, representing moments of radical change, community governance, and protocol upgrades. Understanding them is crucial because they directly impact security, consensus, and the very ownership of digital assets. They highlight the decentralized and democratic, yet sometimes contentious, nature of maintaining a public ledger.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define and differentiate between hard forks (e.g., Bitcoin Cash) and soft forks (e.g., SegWit), understanding their technical mechanisms and triggers. They will analyze the causes of forks, including protocol upgrades and philosophical disagreements, and evaluate their consequences for network security, community split, and coin creation.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon core concepts like consensus mechanisms (Proof-of-Work), decentralized governance, and cryptocurrency wallets (managing forked assets). It provides critical context for understanding blockchain scalability, Bitcoin's history, and the future development of Ethereum and other major protocols.

**4. Real-world applications**
Knowledge of forks is essential for developers contributing to protocol upgrades, investors assessing the risk and opportunity of new crypto assets, and network participants needing to navigate chain splits safely. Real-world examples include the Ethereum/Ethereum Classic split and the continuous soft forks implementing Bitcoin improvements.
