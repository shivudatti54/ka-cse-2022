# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1.  Define Knowledge Representation (KR) and explain its critical role in Artificial Intelligence.
2.  Identify and describe the four key issues in KR: Representational Adequacy, Inferential Adequacy, Inferential Efficiency, and Acquisitional Efficiency.
3.  Compare and contrast Declarative and Procedural Knowledge, listing advantages and disadvantages of each approach.
4.  Evaluate different KR formalisms (e.g., Logic, Rules, Frames) based on their properties and the core trade-offs they present.
5.  Explain the problem of representing uncertain and incomplete knowledge and why standard logic is often insufficient.
6.  Apply the concepts of ontological commitment and inferential efficiency to analyze a simple KR scenario.
