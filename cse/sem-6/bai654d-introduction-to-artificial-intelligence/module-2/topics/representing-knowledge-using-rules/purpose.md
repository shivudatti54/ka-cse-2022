### **Module 2: Representing Knowledge Using Rules**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Rule-based systems are a foundational and highly interpretable method for capturing human expertise in a form an AI can use. They are crucial for modeling the logical "if-then" reasoning that underpins many expert systems, decision-making tools, and business logic engines. Understanding rules provides a clear entry point into the broader challenge of knowledge representation.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the structure and components of rule-based systems, including facts (the knowledge base) and production rules (the inference engine). They will understand forward and backward chaining as methods for reasoning over these rules to derive new conclusions or make decisions. This includes practical skills in designing simple rule sets for a given problem.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module connects directly to Propositional and First-Order Logic (Module 1), as rules are often built upon these logical foundations. It also serves as a precursor to more complex paradigms like machine learning (later modules), providing a contrast between hand-crafted symbolic reasoning and learned statistical models. The inference mechanisms (chaining) introduce core concepts of search and reasoning.

**4. Real-world applications**
Rule-based systems are deployed in diagnostic tools (e.g., medical or technical support), automated customer service chatbots, credit approval systems, and business process management software. They excel in domains where decisions are based on clear, well-defined regulations or expert knowledge.
