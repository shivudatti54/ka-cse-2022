# What is Artificial Intelligence

## Overview

Artificial Intelligence is a branch of computer science focused on creating systems that perform tasks requiring human intelligence such as learning, reasoning, problem-solving, and perception. Modern AI primarily follows the rational agent approach, which aims to achieve optimal outcomes based on available information.

## Key Points

- **Thinking Humanly**: Cognitive modeling approach based on replicating human thought processes through cognitive science
- **Thinking Rationally**: Laws of thought approach using formal logic and mathematical reasoning for conclusions
- **Acting Humanly**: Turing Test approach requiring natural language processing, knowledge representation, reasoning, and learning capabilities
- **Acting Rationally**: Rational agent approach (dominant paradigm) where systems maximize performance measures based on perceptions
- **Knowledge Representation**: Storing world information using semantic networks, frames, production rules, and ontologies
- **Reasoning Types**: Deductive (general to specific), inductive (specific to general), and abductive (best explanation inference)
- **AI Classification**: Narrow AI for specific tasks versus General AI with human-level intelligence across domains

## Important Concepts

- The Turing Test requires NLP, knowledge representation, automated reasoning, machine learning, vision, and robotics
- AI systems combine knowledge representation with reasoning mechanisms to solve problems
- Current AI is predominantly narrow/weak AI designed for specific applications
- AI encompasses supervised learning, unsupervised learning, and reinforcement learning approaches

## Notes

- Focus on the rational agent approach as the modern AI paradigm
- Understand the four main AI definitions and how they differ in goals
- Be prepared to explain why the Turing Test alone is insufficient for defining AI
- Know real-world applications across healthcare, finance, transportation, and manufacturing domains
