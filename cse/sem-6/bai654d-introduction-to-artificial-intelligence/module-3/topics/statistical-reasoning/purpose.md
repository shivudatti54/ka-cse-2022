# Learning Purpose: Module 3 - Statistical Reasoning

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Statistical reasoning is the fundamental engine behind most modern AI systems. It provides the mathematical framework for machines to learn from data, quantify uncertainty, and make informed predictions. Without it, AI would lack the ability to reason in the face of incomplete or noisy information, which is a hallmark of real-world problems.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn core probabilistic concepts crucial for AI, including Bayes' Theorem, probability distributions, and maximum likelihood estimation. They will understand how these principles form the basis for reasoning under uncertainty, enabling them to model real-world scenarios where outcomes are not guaranteed.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module is a direct prerequisite for subsequent topics like **machine learning** (e.g., Naive Bayes classifiers, probabilistic models) and **robotics** (e.g., sensor fusion, localization). It builds upon the foundational knowledge of linear algebra and calculus from earlier modules, applying it to model stochastic (random) processes.

**4. Real-world applications**
Statistical reasoning powers a vast array of applications, including:

- **Spam Filtering:** Using Bayes' Theorem to classify emails.
- **Medical Diagnosis:** Assessing the probability of a disease given symptoms and test results.
- **Recommendation Systems:** Predicting user preferences based on past behavior.
- **Autonomous Vehicles:** Fusing sensor data to localize the vehicle and predict the actions of other drivers.
