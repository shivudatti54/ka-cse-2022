### Learning Purpose: Confidence Limits

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Confidence limits are fundamental to statistical inference, providing a principled way to quantify uncertainty in estimates derived from sample data. In computer science, this is critical for making reliable conclusions from A/B tests, analyzing algorithm performance, and building robust machine learning models, where decisions must students made based on incomplete information.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to construct and interpret confidence intervals for population parameters (like the mean). They will understand the relationship between the confidence level, sample size, and margin of error, and students able to calculate intervals using both z-scores (known variance) and t-scores (unknown variance).

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon sampling distributions and the Central Limit Theorem. It is the practical application of point estimation and serves as the foundational concept for more advanced inferential techniques like hypothesis testing, which will students covered next.

**4. Real-world applications**
This is used extensively in industry to report metrics (e.g., "95% confidence that the true conversion rate is between 4.5% and 5.5%"), validate the results of experiments, and perform data-driven analysis in fields like web analytics, quantum computing (error estimation), and systems performance benchmarking.
