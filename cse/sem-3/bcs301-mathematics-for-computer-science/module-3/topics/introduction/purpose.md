# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define statistical inference and explain how it differs from descriptive statistics
2. Distinguish between a population and a sample, and explain why sampling is necessary
3. Differentiate between parameters and statistics, and identify common examples of each
4. Describe the two main branches of statistical inference: estimation and hypothesis testing
5. Explain key terminology including sampling, bias, sampling distribution, and unbiased estimator
6. Discuss the relevance of statistical inference in computer science applications

## Why This Topic Matters for BCS301

This introduction forms the foundation of Module 3 (Statistical Inference) in the BCS301 Mathematics for Computer Science syllabus. Every subsequent topic in this module -- sampling distributions, standard error, hypothesis testing, levels of significance, confidence limits, and tests for large samples -- builds directly on the concepts introduced here.

## Exam Relevance

- **Frequently asked:** Questions on definitions (statistical inference, population, sample, parameter, statistic) appear regularly in exams.
- **Foundation for longer answers:** Most 10-mark questions on hypothesis testing or confidence intervals expect students to begin with a brief introduction to statistical inference concepts.
- **Marks allocation:** Definitions and distinctions (population vs. sample, parameter vs. statistic) are commonly worth 4-6 marks.
- **Application questions:** occasionally asks why statistical inference is important, especially in the context of computing and data analysis.

Mastering this introductory topic ensures a solid foundation for answering questions from the entire module.
