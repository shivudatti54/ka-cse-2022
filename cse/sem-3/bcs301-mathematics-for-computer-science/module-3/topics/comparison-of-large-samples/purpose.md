### Learning Purpose: Comparison of Large Samples

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Comparing large samples is a fundamental statistical task in data-driven fields. It allows us to move beyond describing single datasets and make objective, quantifiable comparisons between different groups or conditions, which is the basis for drawing meaningful conclusions from data.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to formulate and test hypotheses about the differences between two large independent samples. They will master the application of the **two-sample z-test** to determine if observed differences in sample means are statistically significant or simply due to random chance, based on the properties of the sampling distribution.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon the Central Limit Theorem, confidence intervals, and hypothesis testing from previous modules. It is a crucial prerequisite for understanding more complex inference techniques, such as ANOVA for comparing multiple groups and non-parametric tests for non-normal data.

**4. Real-world applications**
This technique is vital for A/B testing website layouts or features, comparing user engagement metrics between different app versions, analyzing performance differences between algorithms, and validating the impact of a change in a large system (e.g., comparing server response times before and after an upgrade).
