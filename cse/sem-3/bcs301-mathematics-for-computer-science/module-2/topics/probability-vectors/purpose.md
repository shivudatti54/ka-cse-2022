# Learning Purpose: Probability Vectors

**1. Importance**
This topic is fundamental as probability vectors provide the mathematical structure for representing the state of stochastic systems. They are the primary tool for quantifying uncertainty in discrete states, forming the very language of Markov chains and many other probabilistic models essential for computer science.

**2. Learning Outcomes**
Students will learn to define a probability vector, identify its key properties (non-negative entries summing to 1), and perform essential operations. This includes multiplying probability vectors by stochastic matrices to model state transitions, a core operation in Markov processes.

**3. Connections**
Probability vectors are the direct application of prior knowledge on probability distributions and linear algebra (vectors/matrices). They are the foundational building block for Markov Chains, which follow in this module, and are crucial for understanding more advanced topics like Hidden Markov Models, PageRank algorithms, and reinforcement learning.

**4. Real-World Applications**
This concept is applied everywhere state prediction is needed. Key examples include:

- **Search Engines:** Google's PageRank algorithm uses them to model web surfer behavior.
- **Weather Forecasting:** Predicting sunny/rainy days.
- **AI & Games:** Modeling user behavior or creating non-player character (NPC) AI.
- **Financial Modeling:** Predicting stock price movements or credit ratings.
