# Introduction To Stochastic Process

### 1. Why is this topic important?

Stochastic processes are fundamental for modeling systems that evolve randomly over time, which is a cornerstone of modern computer science. Understanding them is crucial for analyzing algorithms, designing networks, and building intelligent systems that must operate under uncertainty.

### 2. What will students learn?

Students will learn to define a stochastic process and identify its key components, such as state space and index set. They will build upon their knowledge of joint probability distributions and Markov chains to analyze simple stochastic processes, focusing on their probabilistic structure and evolution.

### 3. How does it connect to other concepts?

This topic is the direct application and extension of joint probability distributions (Module 2) and Markov chains. It provides the foundational framework for more advanced concepts like queueing theory, hidden Markov models (for machine learning), and random graph theory, linking probability directly to computational problems.

### 4. Real-world applications

Stochastic processes model a vast array of real-world phenomena, including the number of packets in a network router (queueing theory), the spread of diseases or information in a network (epidemiology/social networks), and the sequence of page requests in web browsing (Markov chains). They are also essential for financial modeling, speech recognition, and genomics.
