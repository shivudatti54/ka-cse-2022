# Latin Square Design

Latin-square design is an important topic in BCS301 Mathematics for Computer Science that teaches students how to control for two sources of extraneous variation in an experiment while testing a single treatment factor. This design is widely applicable in computer science experiments, such as comparing the performance of different algorithms across multiple hardware platforms and varying workload conditions, where both platform and workload act as blocking factors. Understanding Latin-square design helps students appreciate efficient experimental layouts that require fewer runs than full factorial designs while maintaining balance. Key examination skills include constructing the Latin-square layout, computing the four-way partitioning of sums of squares (SSR, SSC, SSTr, SSE), calculating the error degrees of freedom as (p-1)(p-2), and performing the F-test for treatment effects. This topic frequently appears in exams as 10-mark numerical problems.
