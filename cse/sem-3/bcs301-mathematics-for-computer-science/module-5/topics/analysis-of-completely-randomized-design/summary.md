# Analysis Of Completely Randomized Design

## Summary: Analysis of Completely Randomized Design (CRD)

### Key Points and Definitions:

- **Completely Randomized Design (CRD):** A design where all factors are randomly assigned to all experimental units, with no specific blocking or grouping.

#### Important Formulas and Concepts:

- **ANOVA Table:**
  \[
  \begin{array}{l}
  SS*{Treatments} = \sum (\bar{y}*{i.} - \bar{y})^2 \\
  MS*{Treatments} = \frac{SS*{Treatments}}{k-1} \\
  F = \frac{MS*{Treatments}}{MS*{Error}}
  \end{array}
  \]
  Where \( k \) is the number of treatments.

#### Definitions:

- **Treatment:** Each level of a factor being studied.
- **Factor:** A variable whose levels are manipulated in an experiment to observe their effect on the response variable.
- **Error (or Error Term):** The variability within each treatment group due to random variation or measurement error.

### Important Theorems and Properties:

- **CRD Assumptions:**

1. Independence of observations
2. Normality of errors
3. Homogeneity of variances

#### Summary:

Completely Randomized Design (CRD) is a foundational design in experimental studies. It ensures that the variability among treatments is due to treatment effects and not other variables, facilitating the use of ANOVA for hypothesis testing about the equality of means across different treatments. Understanding CRD's assumptions and how it sets up an ideal environment for conducting statistical analyses like ANOVA is crucial for valid conclusions in experimental designs.
