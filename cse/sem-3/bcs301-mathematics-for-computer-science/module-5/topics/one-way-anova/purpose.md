# One Way Anova

One-way ANOVA is a foundational statistical technique in BCS301 Mathematics for Computer Science that enables students to test whether the means of three or more groups differ significantly based on a single factor. This topic is essential because it forms the basis for understanding more advanced experimental designs such as two-way ANOVA, Latin-square designs, and ANCOVA. In computer science applications, one-way ANOVA is used to compare algorithm performance across multiple implementations, evaluate system response times under different configurations, and assess software quality metrics across development teams. Mastering the sum-of-squares partitioning (SST = SSB + SSW), the construction of the ANOVA table, and the F-test decision rule is critical for examinations, where numerical problems on one-way ANOVA appear frequently with 10-mark weightage.
