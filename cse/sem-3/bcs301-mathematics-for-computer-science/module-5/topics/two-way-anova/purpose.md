# Two Way Anova

Two-way ANOVA is a critical topic in BCS301 Mathematics for Computer Science that extends one-way ANOVA to study the simultaneous effect of two factors on a response variable. This technique is essential because real-world experiments in computer science often involve multiple factors, such as comparing algorithm performance across different hardware platforms, or evaluating software response times under varying user loads and network conditions. Two-way ANOVA allows students to test not only the individual (main) effects of each factor but also whether the factors interact with each other. Understanding the sum-of-squares partitioning into SSA, SSB, SSAB, and SSE, constructing the two-way ANOVA table, and interpreting multiple F-tests are frequently tested skills in examinations, often carrying 10-mark numerical problems.
