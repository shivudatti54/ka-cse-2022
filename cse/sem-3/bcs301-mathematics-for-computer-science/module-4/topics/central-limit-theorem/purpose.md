# Central Limit Theorem

### Learning Purpose: Central Limit Theorem & Confidence Limits for Unknown Mean

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is fundamental because it provides the mathematical justification for making inferences about a population from a sample. The Central Limit Theorem (CLT) guarantees that sampling distributions approach normality, which is a critical assumption underpinning nearly all parametric statistical methods used in data analysis and machine learning.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to state the Central Limit Theorem and understand its implications. They will learn how to construct and interpret confidence intervals to estimate an unknown population mean. This involves mastering the calculation of the margin of error and understanding how sample size and variability affect the interval's precision.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This knowledge builds directly upon probability distributions and sampling from Module 3. It is a prerequisite for hypothesis testing, A/B testing, and regression analysis. The CLT is the bridge that connects descriptive statistics to inferential statistics, enabling students to progress to more complex modeling techniques.

**4. Real-world applications**
These concepts are applied everywhere data-driven decisions are made. Examples include estimating average user engagement from website analytics, calculating confidence in the mean processing time of an algorithm, or determining the required sample size for a reliable machine learning model evaluation.
