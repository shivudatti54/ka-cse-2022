### Learning Purpose: Chi-square Distribution as a Test of Goodness of Fit

1.  **Why is this topic important?**
    This topic is fundamental for evaluating how well observed data fits an expected theoretical distribution. It provides a rigorous, quantitative method for validating hypotheses about categorical data, a common task in data analysis and scientific research.

2.  **What will students learn?**
    Students will learn to formulate a null hypothesis for a goodness-of-fit test, calculate expected frequencies, and compute the chi-square statistic. They will understand how to compare this statistic to a critical value from the chi-square distribution to determine whether to reject the null hypothesis, thereby assessing the fit of the model.

3.  **How does it connect to other concepts?**
    This builds directly on foundational probability distributions (Module 3) and the core concepts of hypothesis testing and p-values introduced in Statistical Inference 1. It is a specific application of the general hypothesis testing framework to categorical data.

4.  **Real-world applications**
    This test is widely used in fields like bioinformatics (testing genetic model inheritance), software development (A/B testing user engagement across categories), quality control (checking if defect rates match predictions), and market research (analyzing customer preference surveys).
