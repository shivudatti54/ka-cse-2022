# Students T Distribution

### Learning Purpose: Students 't' Distribution

**1. Why is this topic important?**
The 't'-distribution is a fundamental tool for statistical inference, particularly when working with small sample sizes or when the true population standard deviation is unknown. It corrects the inaccuracies that arise from using the standard normal (Z) distribution under these common data science conditions.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define the 't'-distribution and understand its properties, including its heavier tails and dependence on degrees of freedom. They will learn how and when to use it to construct confidence intervals and perform hypothesis tests for a population mean.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is a direct extension of the Central Limit Theorem and inference using the normal distribution. It connects to previous modules on probability and descriptive statistics and is a prerequisite for more advanced concepts like ANOVA and linear regression, which also rely on understanding different distributions.

**4. Real-world applications**
The 't'-test is used extensively to analyze experimental results, such as A/B testing website designs, evaluating the effect of a new drug in clinical trials with small cohorts, or drawing conclusions from small datasets in machine learning and business analytics.
