# **Binomial**

## Summary/Revision Notes

### Definitions and Concepts

- A **binomial distribution** models the number of successes \(X\) in `n` independent trials each with success probability \(p\).
- The **probability mass function (PMF)** for a binomial random variable \( X \) is:
  - \( P(X = k) = {n \choose k} p^k (1-p)^{n-k} \)
    where \( k \) can range from 0 to `n`.
- The **mean** of the binomial distribution is: \( \mu_X = np \).
- The **variance** is: \( \sigma_X^2 = np(1-p) \).

### Important Formulas and Identities

- Probability mass function: \( P(X = k) = {n \choose k} p^k (1-p)^{n-k} \)
- Mean formula: \( \mu_X = np \)
- Variance formula: \( \sigma_X^2 = np(1-p) \)

### Theoretical Insights

- **Bernoulli trials**: Each trial is a Bernoulli trial with two possible outcomes (success or failure).
- Binomial distribution arises when we count the number of successes in `n` such independent trials.
