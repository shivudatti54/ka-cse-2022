# Random Variables Discrete And Continuous

Learning about random variables, both discrete and continuous, is fundamental in the field of mathematics for computer science because they provide a rigorous framework for understanding uncertainty and variability inherent in many computational processes. Discrete random variables are crucial when dealing with countable outcomes, such as the number of defective items in a batch or the number of customers arriving at an ATM during peak hours, while continuous random variables are essential for modeling scenarios involving measurements like temperature fluctuations or stock price changes over time. This topic connects deeply to other concepts such as probability distributions and statistical inference, enabling the analysis and prediction of complex systems through models that account for randomness.
