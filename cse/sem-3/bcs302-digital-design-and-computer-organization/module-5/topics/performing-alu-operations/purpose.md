# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the internal architecture of an ALU, including the arithmetic circuit, logic circuit, and multiplexer-based operation selection mechanism.

2. Design and analyze ripple-carry adder circuits for implementing addition and subtraction operations in the ALU.

3. Describe how control signals (operation select bits, carry-in, result-enable) govern ALU operation selection and execution.

4. Trace the data flow through the processor datapath during ALU operations, including operand routing from registers to ALU and result storage.

5. Compute and interpret status flags (Zero, Carry, Overflow, Negative) generated by ALU operations for use in conditional branching.

6. Analyze overflow conditions for signed arithmetic operations and distinguish between signed and unsigned overflow.

7. Translate high-level assembly instructions into their corresponding register transfer sequences with specific control signal settings.

8. Evaluate the timing characteristics of ALU operations, including propagation delay implications for processor clock frequency.
