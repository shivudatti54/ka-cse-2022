Pipeline performance analysis establishes the quantitative framework for evaluating pipelined processors. A k-stage pipeline achieves theoretical maximum speedup of k when executing large instruction sequences, with efficiency approaching 100% as n→∞. The ideal throughput is one instruction per clock cycle. Three hazard types degrade performance: structural hazards from resource conflicts, data hazards (RAW, WAR, WAW) from dependencies, and control hazards from branches. Data hazards are mitigated through forwarding and stall insertion, while control hazards use branch prediction and delayed branches. The effective CPI formula integrates these factors: CPI = CPI_ideal + Σ(fraction × penalty), enabling performance comparison across pipeline designs. Modern processors employ these techniques to approximate ideal pipeline behavior.
