### Learning Purpose: Handling Multiple Devices

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Modern computing systems require the CPU to communicate with numerous peripheral devices simultaneously, each operating at vastly different speeds. Understanding how to efficiently manage these concurrent data transfers is essential for building responsive, high-performance computer systems. This knowledge forms the foundation of I/O organization and is critical for system programmers, embedded developers, and computer architects.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to analyze the CPU-I/O speed mismatch problem quantitatively and understand why synchronous I/O is inefficient. They will master interrupt-driven I/O mechanisms, including priority schemes and vector handling. Students will learn DMA operation, transfer modes, and performance analysis. The course covers comparative evaluation of techniques to select appropriate I/O management strategies based on device characteristics and system requirements.

**3. How will this knowledge be applied?**
Students will apply this knowledge to design efficient I/O subsystems, calculate system performance improvements from DMA usage, and evaluate interrupt latency in real-time systems. This understanding is essential for operating system development, device driver programming, and computer system optimization.
