# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. **Define** process scheduling and explain its role in operating system resource management, including how it enables multiprogramming and maximizes CPU utilization.

2. **Differentiate** between the three levels of scheduling: long-term (job scheduler), short-term (CPU scheduler), and medium-term scheduler, including their functions and frequency of invocation.

3. **Analyze** various scheduling criteria including CPU utilization, throughput, turnaround time, waiting time, and response time, and explain the trade-offs between them.

4. **Compare** preemptive and non-preemptive scheduling approaches, identifying scenarios where each is appropriate and the advantages and disadvantages of each.

5. **Evaluate** scheduling algorithms such as FCFS, Shortest Job First (SJF), Priority Scheduling, and Round Robin for different workload characteristics, computing metrics like waiting time and turnaround time.

6. **Explain** the concept of context switching, its overhead, and factors that affect context switch time in modern operating systems.

7. **Apply** scheduling concepts to solve numerical problems involving process execution sequences, completion times, turnaround times, and waiting times for various scheduling algorithms.

8. **Identify** the conditions that lead to starvation and explain how aging mechanisms can prevent it in priority-based scheduling systems.
