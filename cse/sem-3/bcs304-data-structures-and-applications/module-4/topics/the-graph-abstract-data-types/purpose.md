# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define the Graph Abstract Data Type (ADT) and explain its significance in modeling relationships between objects.
2. List and describe the key components of a graph, including vertices, edges, and adjacency.
3. Identify and distinguish between different types of graphs, such as directed, undirected, weighted, and complete graphs.
4. Explain the concept of degree in a graph and calculate the degree of a vertex in both directed and undirected graphs.
5. Apply the Handshaking Theorem to verify the consistency of a graph's degree sequence.
6. Analyze a graph to determine whether it is connected, bipartite, or acyclic, and justify your answer.
7. Evaluate the differences between a graph and a tree, and provide examples of each.
8. Design a simple graph algorithm using the Graph ADT, such as finding the neighbors of a vertex or checking whether two vertices are adjacent.
