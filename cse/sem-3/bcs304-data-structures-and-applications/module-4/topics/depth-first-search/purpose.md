# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define Depth-First Search (DFS) and explain its role in graph traversal.
2. List the steps involved in the recursive and iterative DFS algorithms.
3. Identify the types of edges encountered during DFS (tree, back, forward, cross) and describe their significance.
4. Explain how DFS is used for cycle detection, topological sorting, and finding connected components.
5. Analyze the time complexity of DFS for adjacency list and matrix representations.
6. Compare and contrast recursive and iterative DFS implementations.
7. Design a DFS algorithm to solve a given graph problem, such as finding articulation points or biconnected components.
8. Justify the use of DFS in various applications, including maze solving, puzzle state-space exploration, and network analysis.
