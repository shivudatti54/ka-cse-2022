# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. **Define** Breadth-First Search (BFS) and explain its level-by-level graph exploration strategy.
2. **Identify** the key components of the BFS algorithm, including the use of a queue and marking vertices as visited.
3. **Describe** the differences between BFS and Depth-First Search (DFS) in terms of data structure, exploration strategy, and application.
4. **Explain** how BFS guarantees the shortest path in unweighted graphs and provide examples to support this property.
5. **Apply** BFS to solve problems, such as finding connected components, testing bipartiteness, and broadcasting in networks.
6. **Analyze** the time and space complexity of BFS, including the impact of adjacency list and matrix representations.
7. **Compare** BFS with other graph traversal algorithms, including DFS, and determine the most suitable algorithm for a given problem.
8. **Design** a BFS algorithm to solve a specific problem, such as finding the shortest path between two vertices in an unweighted graph.
