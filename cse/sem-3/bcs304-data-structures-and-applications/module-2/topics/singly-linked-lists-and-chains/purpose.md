# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define a singly linked list and explain its basic structure.
2. Identify the time and space complexities of common operations in a singly linked list, such as insertion, deletion, and traversal.
3. Implement a singly linked list in C, including operations like insert at beginning, end, and position, delete from beginning, end, and by value, and reverse the list.
4. Explain the two-pointer technique and its applications in finding the middle element, detecting cycles, and solving other problems related to singly linked lists.
5. Analyze the trade-offs between different implementation approaches, such as using a tail pointer to optimize insertion at the end.
6. Compare and contrast the time complexities of different operations in a singly linked list, such as insertion, deletion, and search.
7. Design and implement a solution to a problem involving singly linked lists, such as merging two sorted lists or removing the nth node from the end.
8. Justify the use of singly linked lists in real-world applications, such as implementing stacks, hash table chaining, undo operations, adjacency lists, and symbol tables.
