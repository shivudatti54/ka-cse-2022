**Purpose**

Binary relations and their properties provide a formal framework for describing associations between elements in sets, which underpins many computer‑science domains such as database systems, graph algorithms, software dependencies, and formal verification. Mastery of these concepts enables you to model complex relationships, reason about orderings, and design reliable algorithms for data organization and problem solving.

**Learning Objectives**

- **Define** a binary relation on a set and represent it using set notation, matrix, or directed graph.  
- **Identify** the key properties of binary relations: reflexivity, symmetry, antisymmetry, transitivity, and completeness.  
- **Distinguish** between equivalence relations and partial orders, and give examples of each from computer‑science contexts.  
- **Apply** the reflexive, symmetric, and transitive closure algorithms to compute the smallest relation containing a given relation with a desired property.  
- **Analyze** real‑world scenarios—such as database constraints, dependency graphs, and scheduling problems—as binary relations and assess their properties.  
- **Construct** counterexamples to illustrate violations of specific properties and justify why a relation fails to satisfy them.
