# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Apply the Fundamental Counting Principle (Product Rule) to solve problems involving sequential decisions and independent choices.

2. Correctly apply the Addition Rule for mutually exclusive tasks and understand when to use product versus sum rules.

3. Compute permutations with and without repetition, distinguishing between linear and circular arrangements.

4. Compute combinations with and without repetition, and understand the relationship between permutations and combinations.

5. Solve real-world selection problems involving committees, teams, and groups by choosing the appropriate counting technique.

6. Apply the Pigeonhole Principle to prove existence results in various mathematical contexts.

7. Use the Inclusion-Exclusion Principle to count elements in unions of sets, handling overlapping cases correctly.

8. Analyze complex counting problems by breaking them into smaller, manageable cases and selecting appropriate counting strategies.
