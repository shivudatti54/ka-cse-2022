# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define recurrence relations, identify their order, and distinguish between homogeneous and non-homogeneous linear recurrence relations with constant coefficients.

2. Formulate recurrence relations from real-world problems and word problems commonly encountered in computer science applications.

3. Solve linear homogeneous recurrence relations with constant coefficients using the characteristic equation method, handling distinct real roots, repeated roots, and complex roots.

4. Solve linear non-homogeneous recurrence relations by combining the homogeneous solution with an appropriately guessed particular solution.

5. Apply iteration and substitution methods to solve first-order and simple higher-order recurrence relations.

6. Apply recurrence relation solving techniques to analyze the time complexity of recursive algorithms.

7. Verify solutions by substituting back into the original recurrence relation and checking initial conditions.

8. Recognize common patterns in recurrence relations and apply appropriate solving strategies efficiently during examinations.
