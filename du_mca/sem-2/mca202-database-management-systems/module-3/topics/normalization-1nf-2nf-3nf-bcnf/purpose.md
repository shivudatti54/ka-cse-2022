# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define normalization and explain its importance in database design, including the elimination of insertion, update, and deletion anomalies.

2. Identify and explain functional dependencies in a given relation, distinguishing between trivial, non-trivial, and transitive dependencies.

3. Apply the requirements of First Normal Form (1NF) to convert non-normalized relations into normalized form by removing repeating groups and composite attributes.

4. Analyze relations for partial dependencies and convert them to Second Normal Form (2NF) through appropriate decomposition.

5. Detect and eliminate transitive dependencies to achieve Third Normal Form (3NF), ensuring all non-key attributes depend only on the primary key.

6. Distinguish between 3NF and BCNF, identifying scenarios where BCNF violations occur even when 3NF is satisfied.

7. Perform lossless-join decomposition and evaluate whether dependency preservation is maintained in normalized relations.

8. Solve examination problems involving normalization of given schemas, including step-by-step identification of functional dependencies and normal form conversions.
