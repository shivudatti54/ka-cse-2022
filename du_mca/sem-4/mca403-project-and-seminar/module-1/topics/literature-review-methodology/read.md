# Literature Review Methodology

## Introduction
A literature review is the foundation of academic research and professional projects in computer science. It involves systematic identification, evaluation, and synthesis of existing works related to a specific research problem. For MCA students at DU, mastering literature review methodology is critical for seminar presentations, project proposals, and avoiding redundant work in emerging fields like AI/ML, cybersecurity, and blockchain.

In industry-driven projects, literature reviews help identify technological gaps, benchmark solutions, and validate research directions. For example, before developing a new intrusion detection system, researchers must analyze existing approaches in academic papers (IEEE/ACM databases) and industry whitepapers (Microsoft/Cisco reports). The methodology ensures evidence-based decision-making and methodological rigor required in NEP 2024-aligned programs.

## Key Concepts
1. **Types of Literature Reviews**
   - *Systematic Reviews*: PRISMA-guided analysis with strict inclusion/exclusion criteria
   - *Scoping Reviews*: Broad mapping of concepts in emerging fields (e.g., quantum computing)
   - *Meta-Analyses*: Statistical aggregation of results from multiple studies

2. **Five-Step Methodology**
   - **Research Question Formulation**: PICO framework (Population, Intervention, Comparison, Outcome)
   - **Search Strategy Design**: Boolean operators, database selection (Scopus, Web of Science), grey literature
   - **Quality Assessment**: JBI Critical Appraisal Tools for empirical studies
   - **Data Extraction**: Coding sheets for technical parameters (algorithms, datasets, accuracy metrics)
   - **Synthesis**: Thematic analysis using NVivo or manual affinity diagramming

3. **Tools & Metrics**
   - Reference managers: Zotero/Mendeley for citation tracking
   - Plagiarism checkers: Turnitin/iThenticate for DU project submissions
   - Bibliometric analysis: CiteSpace for co-citation networks

## Examples
**Example 1: Systematic Review of Federated Learning Architectures**
1. *Research Question*: "How do client selection strategies impact model accuracy in non-IID data scenarios?"
2. *Search Query*: ("federated learning" OR "distributed ML") AND ("client selection" OR "node sampling")
3. *Screening*: 572 initial papers → 42 relevant after applying exclusion criteria
4. *Synthesis*: Tabulate accuracy vs communication costs across 5 client selection algorithms

**Example 2: Scoping Review of NFT Security Vulnerabilities**
1. *Objective*: Map attack vectors in Ethereum-based NFT marketplaces
2. *Sources*: Smart contract audit reports + OWASP Web3 guidelines
3. *Findings*: 68% vulnerabilities stem from improper ERC-721 implementation

**Example 3: Comparative Analysis of RAG Systems**
1. *Data Extraction*: Compare retrieval accuracy of FAISS vs Pinecone for LLM augmentation
2. *Visualization*: Precision-Recall curves across 3 benchmark datasets (MS MARCO, Natural Questions)

## Exam Tips
1. Always link review type (systematic/scoping) to research objectives in viva questions
2. Memorize PRISMA flow diagram components for 5-mark diagram questions
3. Practice writing Boolean search strings using field-specific operators (e.g., ALL:(blockchain) in IEEE Xplore)
4. For case studies, emphasize quality assessment criteria like CONSORT for randomized trials
5. Use matrix frameworks (e.g., TAXONOMY vs CHRONOLOGY) when explaining synthesis methods
6. Highlight limitations: Publication bias in AI research due to industry-funded studies
7. Prepare 2-3 DU library database examples (e.g., Shodhganga for Indian theses)
