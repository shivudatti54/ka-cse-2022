# Research Presentation & Viva Defense - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts
- **Viva Voce**: Oral examination assessing research validity and candidate expertise  
- **PRISMA Framework**: Methodology for systematic literature reviews  
- **Demographic Parity Difference**: Fairness metric in ML models (ideal = 0)  
- **WCAG 2.1**: Web accessibility guidelines for inclusive slide design  

## Important Formulas and Theorems
- **Presentation Time Allocation**: T_total = 1.5×T_practice (accounting for Q&A delays)  
- **Reproducibility Score**: R = (Dockerfile + Jupyter Notebook + Dataset)/3  
- **Fairness Metric**: DPD = |TPR_group1 - TPR_group2| (Threshold < 0.05)  

## Key Points
- Structure presentations using DU's 4-pillar framework: Problem, Method, Results, Impact  
- Maintain version-controlled backups of demos (Git + DockerHub)  
- Use the "3C" response model in vivas: Clarify, Contextualize, Cite  
- All visualizations must include alt-text and colorblind-friendly palettes  
- Document all ethical approvals (DU-IEC Reference Numbers)  
- Prepare 2-minute "elevator pitch" summarizing key contributions  
- Memorize 3 critical papers from your supervisor's publications  

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Overloading slides with text (max 6 lines/slide)  
- Using unlicensed software in demos  
- Neglecting regional language capabilities in multilingual panels  
- Failing to address recent DU research contradicting your methods  

## Revision Tips
- Record mock presentations using OBS Studio and analyze filler word frequency  
- Practice with heterogeneous audiences (CS + non-CS peers)  
- Create a "viva question bank" from past DU theses in Shodhganga  
- Simulate network failures during cloud-based demos
