# AI Ethics Principles - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts
- Algorithmic Fairness: Satisfaction of statistical parity (P(Ŷ=1|A=a) = P(Ŷ=1|A=b))
- Moral Deskilling: Erosion of human ethical judgment due to AI over-reliance
- Right to Reasonable Inference: Protection against harmful data deductions (DPDP Act S.4(9))

## Important Formulas and Theorems
- Fairness Unawareness Theorem: ∃ scenarios where fairness metrics conflict (Kleinberg et al.)
- Kantian AI Principle: Maxime π → universalizable per Categorical Imperative
- Bias-Variance-Explainability Tradeoff: R(f) ≤ ē + ṽ + β (Doshi-Velez 2017)

## Key Points
- India's DPDP Act mandates algorithmic impact assessments for state AI systems
- The transparency paradox increases attack surfaces in public sector models
- Value learning requires preference elicitation from diverse populations
- Environmental costs of AI disproportionately affect Global South nations
- Constitutional AI embeds ethical principles directly in model architecture
- Red teaming must include cultural context experts for Indian deployments
- Ethical debt compounds faster than technical debt in ML systems

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
1. Confusing individual vs. group fairness metrics
2. Overlooking temporal aspects of ethical impacts
3. Treating ethics as mere constraint rather than design paradigm
4. Ignoring caste dynamics in Indian bias analyses
