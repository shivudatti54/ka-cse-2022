## Purpose & Learning Objectives

### Purpose
TCP congestion control variants are foundational to the stability, efficiency, and fairness of the modern internet. As network scale and diversity grow, understanding these mechanisms equips computer scientists to optimize application performance, troubleshoot network issues, and contribute to cutting-edge research in protocol design.

### Learning Objectives
- **Analyze** the underlying algorithms of major TCP congestion control variants (Reno, NewReno, CUBIC, BBR).
- **Compare** the performance characteristics of loss-based versus delay-based congestion control approaches.
- **Evaluate** the impact of variant selection on throughput, latency, and fairness in diverse network conditions.
- **Design** strategies to mitigate congestion collapse and improve QoS in real-world networked systems.
- **Implement** simulation experiments to observe congestion dynamics under varying traffic patterns.
- **Differentiate** between TCP's slow start, congestion avoidance, and fast recovery phases across variants.
- **Assess** emerging trends like QUIC and machine learning-based congestion control in contemporary networks.
