## Purpose and Learning Objectives

Dimensionality reduction and manifold learning are essential for transforming high‑dimensional data into compact, interpretable representations while preserving intrinsic structure, thereby enabling efficient computation, clearer visualisation, and improved model performance in real‑world computer‑science applications such as image retrieval, biomedical signal analysis, and large‑scale recommendation systems.

- **Explain** the curse of dimensionality and the rationale for projecting data onto low‑dimensional manifolds.  
- **Compare** key manifold‑learning algorithms (e.g., Isomap, Locally Linear Embedding, t‑SNE, UMAP) in terms of methodology, computational complexity, and preservation of local/global structure.  
- **Apply** dimensionality‑reduction techniques to real‑world datasets using Python libraries (scikit‑learn, UMAP, OpenTSNE) and interpret the resulting embeddings.  
- **Analyze** the impact of hyper‑parameters (neighbourhood size, perplexity, number of components) on the quality of the learned representation.  
- **Evaluate** the effectiveness of reduced representations through quantitative metrics and visualisation for downstream tasks.  
- **Design** end‑to‑end pipelines that integrate manifold learning with machine‑learning models, ensuring scalability and reproducibility.
