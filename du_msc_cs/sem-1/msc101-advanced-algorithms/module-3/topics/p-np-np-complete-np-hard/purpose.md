**Purpose**  
Understanding the classes P, NP, NP‑Complete, and NP‑Hard is essential for recognizing the theoretical limits of what can be computed efficiently and for making informed design choices when tackling real‑world computational problems. This knowledge guides practitioners in selecting exact, approximation, or heuristic methods, and underpins research areas such as cryptography, optimization, and artificial intelligence where hardness assumptions are critical.

**Learning Objectives**  
- Distinguish between the definitions of P, NP, NP‑Complete, and NP‑Hard.  
- Identify well‑known NP‑Complete problems and their reductions.  
- Analyze the implications of the P ≠ NP conjecture on algorithm tractability.  
- Construct polynomial‑time many‑one reductions to prove NP‑Completeness.  
- Evaluate approximation algorithms and heuristic strategies for NP‑Hard problems.  
- Apply complexity‑theoretic insights to select appropriate algorithmic paradigms for practical problems.  
- Prove membership of a given problem in NP‑Complete or NP‑Hard classes via rigorous reductions.
