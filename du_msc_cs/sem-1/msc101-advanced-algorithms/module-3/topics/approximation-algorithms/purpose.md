## Approximation Algorithms – Purpose and Learning Objectives

**Purpose:** Approximation algorithms supply tractable solutions to NP‑hard optimisation problems that arise in real‑world domains such as scheduling, network design, and data clustering, delivering provable performance guarantees where exact methods are impractical. Mastering these techniques equips computer scientists to balance computational feasibility with solution quality in large‑scale systems.

**Learning Objectives:**
- Identify NP‑hard optimisation problems and justify the need for approximation.  
- Explain the theoretical foundations of approximation ratios and performance guarantees.  
- Analyse classic techniques such as greedy, rounding, primal‑dual, and LP‑based methods.  
- Design approximation algorithms for given problems using appropriate strategies.  
- Apply polynomial‑time approximation schemes (PTAS/FPTAS) to obtain near‑optimal solutions.  
- Compare alternative approximation algorithms in terms of complexity, approximation factor, and practical performance.  
- Evaluate the limitations and challenges of approximation in real‑world implementations.
