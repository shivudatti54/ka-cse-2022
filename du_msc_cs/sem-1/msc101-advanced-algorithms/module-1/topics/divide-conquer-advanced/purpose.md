# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the three-step structure of the divide and conquer paradigm and identify how it applies to various algorithms

2. Analyze the Fast Fourier Transform algorithm, including its recursive structure, the Cooley-Tukey decomposition, and applications to polynomial multiplication

3. Apply Strassen's matrix multiplication algorithm to compute products with reduced multiplication complexity and understand its theoretical significance

4. Implement the closest pair of points algorithm and explain the geometric reasoning behind the strip processing step

5. Compare and contrast QuickSelect (randomized) with the Median of Medians (deterministic) algorithms for order statistics

6. Apply the Master Theorem to analyze recurrence relations arising from divide and conquer algorithms and determine asymptotic bounds

7. Evaluate when divide and conquer is appropriate versus other algorithmic paradigms such as dynamic programming, based on problem structure characteristics
