## Purpose & Learning Objectives

**Purpose**  
Bipartite matching is a cornerstone of many real‑world computer‑science applications, from assigning students to courses or jobs to scheduling rides in ridesharing platforms and allocating resources in cloud computing. It also provides the theoretical foundation for more advanced combinatorial optimization problems, such as kidney exchange, stable matchings, and network‑flow reductions. Mastering this topic equips you with algorithmic techniques to model and solve pairing problems that are pervasive across data science, operations research, and distributed systems.

**Learning Objectives**  
- Identify bipartite graphs and formulate matching problems in practical contexts.  
- Apply classic algorithms (e.g., Hungarian method, Hopcroft‑Karp) to compute maximum matchings efficiently.  
- Analyze the time and space complexities of various matching algorithms.  
- Design augmenting‑path based solutions for dynamic or weighted matching scenarios.  
- Evaluate matching quality under additional constraints such as fairness or stability.  
- Compare bipartite matching with general matching and network‑flow formulations.  
- Implement matching algorithms in code and integrate them into real‑world applications.
