# **6.4 Ch: Objectives of Identification**

## **Introduction**

In time series analysis, the objective of identification is to determine the underlying structure of the data. This involves identifying the patterns, trends, and cycles present in the time series data. The goal of identification is to create a model that can accurately forecast future values of the time series.

## **Objectives of Identification**

The objectives of identification in time series analysis are:

### **1. Pattern Recognition**

- Identify patterns in the data, such as trends, cycles, and seasonality.
- Recognize the type of pattern, such as linear, non-linear, or periodic.

### **2. Trend Estimation**

- Estimate the underlying trend in the data, such as the long-term direction of the series.
- Identify the trend as deterministic or stochastic.

### **3. Seasonality Estimation**

- Estimate the seasonality in the data, such as the cycles present in the series.
- Identify the frequency of the seasonality, such as quarterly or yearly.

### **4. Cycles Estimation**

- Estimate the cycles in the data, such as the fluctuation in the series.
- Identify the frequency of the cycles, such as daily or monthly.

## **Identification Techniques**

The following techniques are used to achieve the objectives of identification:

### **1. Visual Inspection**

- Plot the time series data to visually inspect the patterns, trends, and cycles.
- Use techniques such as time series plots, autocorrelation plots, and spectral plots to analyze the data.

### **2. Statistical Methods**

- Use statistical methods such as trend analysis, seasonality decomposition, and cycle analysis to estimate the underlying structure of the data.
- Use techniques such as linear regression, ARIMA, and spectral analysis to model the time series.

### **3. Machine Learning Methods**

- Use machine learning methods such as neural networks, decision trees, and random forests to identify patterns in the data.
- Use techniques such as time series forecasting and anomaly detection to analyze the data.

## **Example**

Suppose we have a time series dataset that represents the daily sales of a company over a year. The data may exhibit trends, cycles, and seasonality. To identify the underlying structure of the data, we can use visual inspection, statistical methods, and machine learning methods.

- Visual inspection may reveal a trend in the data, such as an increasing trend over time.
- Statistical methods may be used to decompose the trend into seasonal and cyclical components.
- Machine learning methods may be used to identify patterns in the data, such as daily and weekly cycles.

By using these techniques, we can create a model that can accurately forecast future values of the time series.
