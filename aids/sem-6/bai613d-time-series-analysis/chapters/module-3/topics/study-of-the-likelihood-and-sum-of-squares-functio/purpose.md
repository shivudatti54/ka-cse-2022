### Learning Purpose: Study of the Likelihood and Sum-of-Squares Functions

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is fundamental because the Likelihood and Sum-of-Squares (SS) functions are the core mathematical criteria for estimating parameters in time series models. Selecting the correct parameters is crucial for building models that accurately capture the underlying patterns (trend, seasonality, autocorrelation) in the data, which directly impacts forecast reliability.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn how to construct, interpret, and optimize the Likelihood Function (for ARIMA models) and the Sum-of-Squares Function (often for AR models). They will understand that the goal of estimation is to find the parameter values that maximize the likelihood or minimize the sum of squared residuals, thereby providing the "best-fit" model for the observed data.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module is the critical link between theoretical model identification (Module 2: choosing p,d,q orders) and diagnostic checking/forecasting (Module 4). The parameters estimated here define the concrete model used for all subsequent analysis. It also builds on foundational knowledge of regression and probability.

**4. Real-world applications**
These estimation techniques are applied everywhere time series forecasting is used: optimizing inventory levels in supply chain management, modeling financial volatility for risk assessment, predicting energy demand for grid management, and analyzing economic indicators for policy development.
