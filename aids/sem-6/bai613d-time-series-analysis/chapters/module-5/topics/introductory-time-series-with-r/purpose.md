### Learning Purpose: Introductory Time Series with R

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Time series data is ubiquitous, generated in fields from finance to public health. Understanding how to properly analyze this data is a critical skill for extracting meaningful trends, patterns, and forecasts. R provides a powerful, open-source ecosystem of tools specifically designed for this task, making it the industry standard for many analysts and researchers.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the foundational concepts of time series analysis, including decomposition, autocorrelation, and stationarity. They will gain practical skills in using key R packages (e.g., `stats`, `forecast`, `tsibble`) to visualize time series data, apply basic forecasting models, and interpret the results.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module builds directly on prior knowledge of statistical modeling, data visualization, and programming in R. It provides the essential groundwork for more advanced concepts like ARIMA modeling, machine learning for forecasting, and multivariate time series analysis covered in subsequent modules.

**4. Real-world applications**
The techniques learned are directly applicable to real-world problems such as predicting stock prices, forecasting product sales, analyzing economic indicators like GDP or unemployment rates, and monitoring environmental data such as temperature changes or electricity demand.
