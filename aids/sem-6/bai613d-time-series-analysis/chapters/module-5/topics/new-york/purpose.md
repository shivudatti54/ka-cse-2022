### Learning Purpose: Module 5 - New York

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Using New York City as a case study provides a tangible, real-world context for applying time series analysis techniques. Its diverse economy and vast data availability make it an ideal archetype for understanding complex, real-life temporal patterns that are rarely clean or simple, bridging the gap between theoretical models and practical application.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify, model, and forecast key economic and social indicators specific to NYC, such as tourism arrivals, subway ridership, or real estate prices. They will apply core techniques (e.g., ARIMA, decomposition, seasonal adjustment) to manage the city’s pronounced seasonality and trends, interpreting results within its unique socio-economic environment.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module synthesizes concepts from previous modules on stationarity, decomposition, and forecasting models. It challenges students to integrate their knowledge, requiring them to diagnose data issues, select appropriate models, and validate forecasts, thereby reinforcing the entire analytical workflow.

**4. Real-world applications**
The skills honed here are directly applicable to urban planning, resource allocation, traffic management, and economic policy analysis. Professionals use these same methods to forecast demand for public services, optimize energy grids, and guide investment decisions in major metropolitan areas worldwide.
