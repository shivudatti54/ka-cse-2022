### Learning Purpose: Continuous Internal Evaluation in Time Series Analysis

**1. Importance**
This topic is crucial because time series models are not "set-and-forget" tools. Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) ensures that a model remains accurate and reliable as new data arrives and underlying patterns evolve, preventing the use of outdated forecasts that can lead to poor decision-making.

**2. Learning Outcomes**
Students will learn to implement and interpret key techniques for ongoing model assessment. This includes calculating and monitoring rolling forecast errors (e.g., MAE, RMSE), using residual analysis to detect model breakdown, and establishing control limits to trigger model retraining or recalibration.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts**
CIE directly builds upon prior modules. It applies the error metrics learned in **Model Evaluation**, utilizes the diagnostic checks from **Model Identification (ARIMA)**, and is a core component of a full **Automated Forecasting Pipeline**. It is the practical implementation of maintaining model fit over time.

**4. Real-World Applications**
This process is fundamental in any dynamic environment. Examples include:
*   **Finance:** Continuously monitoring algorithmic trading model performance.
*   **Supply Chain:** Ensuring demand forecasting models adapt to sudden market shifts or disruptions.
*   **Utilities:** Maintaining the accuracy of energy load forecasting models to ensure grid stability.
