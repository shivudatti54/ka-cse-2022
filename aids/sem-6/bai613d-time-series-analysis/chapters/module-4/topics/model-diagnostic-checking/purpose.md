### **Module 4: Model Diagnostic Checking**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Diagnostic checking is a critical step in time series analysis, ensuring that a fitted model is statistically adequate and reliable for forecasting. A model that fails diagnostic tests may produce biased or inefficient forecasts, leading to poor real-world decisions. This process validates the model's assumptions, confirming that the residuals are random and contain no patterns.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to apply statistical tests (e.g., Ljung-Box test for autocorrelation, Jarque-Bera for normality) and visual tools (e.g., ACF plots of residuals, Q-Q plots) to assess a model's goodness-of-fit. They will be able to identify patterns in residuals, diagnose issues like remaining autocorrelation or heteroscedasticity, and understand the implications of these results.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds on previous lessons on model identification (Module 2) and parameter estimation (Module 3). It is the final validation step before using a model for forecasting (Module 5). The diagnostics inform whether to return to earlier stages to identify a better model, creating an iterative modeling cycle.

**4. Real-world applications**
Diagnostic checking is essential in fields like finance (validating risk models), economics (forecasting economic indicators), supply chain management (demand forecasting), and meteorology (weather prediction). It ensures that forecasts used for investment, policy, inventory, and public safety decisions are based on a robust and trustworthy model.
