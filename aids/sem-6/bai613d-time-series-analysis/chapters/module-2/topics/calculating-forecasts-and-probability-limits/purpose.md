### Learning Purpose: Calculating Forecasts and Probability Limits

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is fundamental because a forecast without a measure of its uncertainty is incomplete and often misleading. Probability limits provide a crucial confidence interval around a point forecast, quantifying the range of likely future outcomes. This allows for better risk assessment and more informed decision-making under uncertainty.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to calculate point forecasts for future values in a time series using established models (e.g., ARIMA, Exponential Smoothing). Crucially, they will also learn to compute the accompanying probability limits (forecast intervals) that define the range within which future observations are expected to fall with a certain level of confidence.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds on the model identification and estimation techniques covered in earlier modules. It requires a firm understanding of the model's structure and its residual error term to accurately calculate forecast variance. This skill is also a prerequisite for advanced topics like model comparison and evaluation, where forecast accuracy is measured.

**4. Real-world applications**
This is applied everywhere forecasting is critical: in finance to predict stock prices and assess investment risk, in supply chain management to forecast product demand and set safety stock levels, in economics to predict GDP growth, and in public health to project the spread of diseases and plan resource allocation.
