### Learning Purpose: Module 2 - Forecasting

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Forecasting is the primary practical application of time series analysis. It is a critical skill for making informed, data-driven decisions about the future. The ability to accurately predict trends, seasonal patterns, and behaviours is invaluable across numerous industries, enabling proactive strategy rather than reactive response. Understanding different forecasting models and their assumptions prevents the use of inappropriate methods that can lead to costly errors.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to build, apply, and evaluate fundamental forecasting models. This includes smoothing techniques (e.g., Moving Averages, Exponential Smoothing) and autoregressive models (e.g., ARIMA). They will understand the concepts of stationarity, decomposition, and how to measure forecast accuracy using metrics like Mean Absolute Error (MAE) and Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE). The module emphasizes selecting the right model for a given data pattern.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds on the foundational concepts from Module 1, such as identifying trends, seasonality, and stationarity. The models learned here rely on these components. Furthermore, forecasting is the practical goal that motivates earlier steps like data cleaning, visualization, and decomposition. It sets the stage for more advanced topics like multivariate forecasting and machine learning approaches applied to time series data.

**4. Real-world applications**
Forecasting is ubiquitous. Key applications include predicting product demand in supply chain management, estimating electricity load for utility companies, forecasting sales and revenue in business, predicting stock prices in finance, and anticipating patient admissions in healthcare. It is essential for effective resource allocation, inventory control, budgeting, and strategic planning.
