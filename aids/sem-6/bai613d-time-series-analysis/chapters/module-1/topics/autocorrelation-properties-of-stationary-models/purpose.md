### Learning Purpose: Autocorrelation Properties of Stationary Models

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding autocorrelation is fundamental to time series analysis because it quantifies the inherent serial dependence within data. For stationary models, these properties define the model's structure and are essential for accurate identification, estimation, and forecasting. Ignoring autocorrelation can lead to inefficient estimates and spurious results.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define and interpret the autocorrelation function (ACF) and partial autocorrelation function (PACF). They will master how to calculate these functions for foundational stationary models (e.g., AR, MA, ARMA) and, crucially, use these "fingerprints" to identify the appropriate model for a given dataset.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the critical link between the theoretical definition of stationarity (Module 1) and the practical model-building process. It provides the tools needed for model identification, which directly precedes the subsequent modules on parameter estimation and diagnostic checking (e.g., testing residuals).

**4. Real-world applications**
Analyzing autocorrelation is vital in fields like finance for modeling asset returns, in economics for forecasting GDP or inflation, and in engineering for signal processing. It allows analysts to detect patterns, such as seasonality or mean-reverting behavior, to build predictive models for future values.
