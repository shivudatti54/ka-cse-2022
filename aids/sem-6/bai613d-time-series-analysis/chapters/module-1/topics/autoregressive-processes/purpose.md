### Learning Purpose: Autoregressive Processes

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Autoregressive (AR) processes are a fundamental class of time series models. Understanding them is crucial because they provide a powerful framework for forecasting future values based solely on their own past. This concept is the cornerstone of more advanced time series models and is widely used across numerous fields.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define and identify AR processes, understand their properties (like stationarity requirements), and interpret their order (`p`) and coefficients (`φ`). They will gain practical skills in estimating AR models from data and using them to generate forecasts, forming a primary tool for univariate time series analysis.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module builds directly on the prerequisite concepts of stationarity, autocorrelation, and white noise. AR processes serve as a foundational building block for more complex models like Moving Average (MA) and ARIMA processes, which combine autoregressive and moving average components for greater modeling flexibility.

**4. Real-world applications**
AR models are applied everywhere time-dependent data exists. Key applications include forecasting economic indicators like stock prices or GDP growth, predicting weather patterns, anticipating energy demand and electricity loads, and analyzing signal processing data in engineering.
