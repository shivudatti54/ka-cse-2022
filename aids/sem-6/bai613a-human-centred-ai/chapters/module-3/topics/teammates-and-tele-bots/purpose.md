### Learning Purpose: Teammates and Tele-bots

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding how humans and AI collaborate as teammates is critical for designing future work environments. This topic moves beyond AI as a mere tool, exploring it as an interactive partner. It is essential for creating effective, safe, and trustworthy hybrid teams where humans and AI augment each other's capabilities.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core principles of Human-AI Teaming (HAT) and the specific design challenges of tele-bots (remote physical robots). This includes concepts like shared mental models, adjustable autonomy, and fluent handoff protocols to ensure seamless collaboration between human operators and robotic agents.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly on foundational HCI principles from Module 1 (like interaction design) and ethical frameworks from Module 2 (e.g., trust and transparency). It applies these concepts to a practical, physically-embodied AI context, bridging the gap between theoretical AI and its real-world deployment.

**4. Real-world applications**
Applications are vast and growing, including robotic surgery where a surgeon teams with a surgical robot, disaster response where experts remotely operate rescue bots, and manufacturing where workers supervise and collaborate with autonomous robotic systems on complex assembly tasks.
