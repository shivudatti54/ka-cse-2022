### Learning Purpose: Social Robots and Active Appliances

**1. Importance**
This topic is critical as the integration of AI into physical devices, from companion robots to smart home systems, is moving out of labs and into our daily lives. Understanding their design and impact is essential to ensure this technology is developed and deployed ethically, safely, and in a way that truly benefits humanity, rather than causing unintended social harm or exacerbating inequalities.

**2. Learning Outcomes**
Students will learn the core principles of designing embodied AI agents that interact with humans in social and physical contexts. This includes understanding different levels of autonomy (from teleoperation to full autonomy), the theory of mind for robots, and the ethical challenges specific to physical AI, such as privacy in the home, safety, and the potential for deception or emotional dependency.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts**
This module builds directly on foundational HCAI concepts learned previously. It applies principles of **trust calibration** (Module 1) and **ethical frameworks** (Module 2) to tangible, interactive systems. It also connects to future topics on **AI accountability and governance**, as the physical nature of these appliances raises unique legal and safety questions.

**4. Real-World Applications**
The concepts are immediately applicable to fields like:
*   **Elder Care:** Assistive robots providing companionship and support.
*   **Healthcare:** Surgical robots and systems for patient rehabilitation.
*   **Education:** Robots used as teaching aids or for tutoring.
*   **Smart Homes:** Autonomous appliances like vacuum cleaners and interactive voice assistants that perceive and act within their environment.
