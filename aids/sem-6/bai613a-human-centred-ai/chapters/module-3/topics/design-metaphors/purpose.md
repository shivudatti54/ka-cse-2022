# Learning Purpose: Design Metaphors

## 1. Why is this topic important?
Design metaphors are crucial in Human-Centred AI because they bridge the gap between complex technical systems and human understanding. They provide users with a familiar conceptual framework, making AI interactions more intuitive, predictable, and trustworthy. A poorly chosen metaphor can lead to confusion, misuse, or rejection of a system, while a strong one enhances usability and shapes positive user experiences.

## 2. What will students learn?
Students will learn to identify, analyze, and evaluate common AI design metaphors (e.g., agent, assistant, oracle, tool). They will develop the skill to select and apply appropriate metaphors based on user needs, context, and the AI's capabilities to design more effective and understandable user interfaces and experiences.

## 3. How does it connect to other concepts?
This topic connects directly to core HCAI principles learned previously, such as **trust**, **transparency**, and **user mental models**. It builds upon foundational UX design concepts and serves as a practical tool for implementing ethical AI guidelines. It also informs subsequent topics on prototyping and evaluating AI systems.

## 4. Real-world applications
Design metaphors are applied everywhere: the conversational "agent" in a chatbot, the predictive "oracle" in a recommendation engine, or the collaborative "assistant" in co-writing tools. Understanding metaphors allows designers to shape how users perceive and interact with AI in applications ranging from smart home devices and autonomous vehicles to clinical decision-support systems.
