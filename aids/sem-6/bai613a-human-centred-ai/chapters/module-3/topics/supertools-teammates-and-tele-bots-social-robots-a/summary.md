# **Human-Centred AI Revision Notes**

**Chapter 11: Supertools, Teammates and Tele-bots**

- Supertools: Systems that are more intelligent and capable than their human operators, often used in domains such as space exploration and scientific research.
- Characteristics:
  - Intelligent agents
  - Human-in-the-loop (HITL) control
  - Autonomous decision-making
- Examples:
  - NASA's Mars Curiosity Rover
  - The European Space Agency's Rosetta mission

**Chapter 12: Social Robots**

- Social robots: Robots designed to interact with humans in a social manner, often used in applications such as healthcare and education.
- Characteristics:
  - Human-like appearance and behavior
  - Emotion recognition and expression
  - Social learning and interaction
- Examples:
  - Pepper, a humanoid robot used in customer service
  - Jibo, a social robot designed for personal assistance

**Chapter 13: Active Appliances**

- Active appliances: Domestic robots that can perform tasks autonomously, often used in applications such as household cleaning and maintenance.
- Characteristics:
  - Autonomous decision-making
  - Human-in-the-loop (HITL) control
  - Integration with other smart devices
- Examples:
  - Roomba, a robot vacuum cleaner
  - Samsung's SmartThings, a home automation system

**Important Formulas and Definitions**

- **Intelligent Agent**: An autonomous system that perceives its environment and takes actions to achieve a goal.
- **Human-in-the-Loop (HITL)**: A control architecture where humans are involved in the decision-making process.
- **Autonomous Decision-Making**: The ability of a system to make decisions without human intervention.

**Theorems and Concepts**

- **No Free Lunch Theorem**: A fundamental principle in control theory that states that no system can be both optimal and efficient at the same time.
- **Moore's Law**: A prediction that the number of transistors on a microchip will double approximately every two years, leading to exponential increases in computing power.
