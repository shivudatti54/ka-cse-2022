### Learning Purpose: Intelligent Agents and Supertools

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding intelligent agents and supertools is crucial for designing AI that augments, rather than replaces, human capabilities. These systems are becoming central to our digital lives, and a human-centred approach ensures they are built to be collaborative, trustworthy, and aligned with human values and goals.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define and distinguish between intelligent agents (goal-oriented autonomous systems) and supertools (AI-powered tools that expand human cognitive and physical abilities). They will analyze core components like agency, perception, and action, and evaluate their design against human-centred principles such as user empowerment, transparency, and responsible oversight.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly on foundational HCAI principles from Module 1 (e.g., ethically aligned design) and concepts of trust and explainability from Module 2. It provides the practical architectural framework for implementing these principles, setting the stage for later modules on specific applications like collaborative robotics or recommendation systems.

**4. Real-world applications**
Key applications include AI co-pilots in software development (e.g., GitHub Copilot), virtual assistants (e.g., Siri, Alexa), diagnostic supertools in healthcare, and autonomous systems in manufacturing. Students will assess these real-world examples through a critical, human-centred lens.
