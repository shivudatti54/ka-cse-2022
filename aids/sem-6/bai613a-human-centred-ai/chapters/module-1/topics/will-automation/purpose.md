### Learning Purpose: Will Automation?

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Automation is a dominant force reshaping industries and society. This topic is crucial because it moves beyond a purely technical discussion to critically examine the human impact, ethical dilemmas, and societal readiness for increasingly autonomous systems. Understanding the "will" (the intent and consequences) behind automation is foundational to developing a human-centred approach, ensuring technology augments rather than replaces human potential.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to differentiate between various levels of automation and autonomy. They will analyze the socio-economic implications, including job displacement and creation, and evaluate the ethical considerations of delegating decision-making to machines. The goal is to foster critical thinking about *when* and *how* to automate responsibly.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly sets the stage for subsequent modules. It provides the essential context for discussing AI ethics (Module 2), as the decision to automate is inherently an ethical one. It also connects to designing for trust and transparency (later modules) by establishing why these principles are necessary when humans interact with autonomous systems.

**4. Real-world applications**
Applications range from examining robotic process automation (RPA) in offices and automated manufacturing lines to evaluating the societal debate around self-driving cars and autonomous weapons systems. Students will apply these concepts to assess real-world cases, weighing the benefits of efficiency against the costs to human agency and employment.
