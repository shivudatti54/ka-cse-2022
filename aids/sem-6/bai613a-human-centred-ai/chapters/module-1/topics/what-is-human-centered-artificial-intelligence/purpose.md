# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence and explain how it differs from traditional AI development approaches.

2. Identify and describe the core principles of HCAI, including human control, transparency, fairness, privacy, and accessibility.

3. Explain the human-in-the-loop paradigm and distinguish between different levels of human involvement in AI systems.

4. Discuss the importance of explainable AI (XAI) and describe techniques for making AI decisions interpretable to humans.

5. Analyze the challenges of achieving fairness in AI systems, including the conflicts between different fairness metrics.

6. Explain why trust calibration is important in human-AI interaction and how HCAI addresses both undertrust and overtrust.

7. Apply HCAI principles to evaluate real-world AI systems and identify potential improvements for human-centered design.

8. Recognize the broader implications of HCAI for society, including ethical considerations and the social responsibility of AI developers.
