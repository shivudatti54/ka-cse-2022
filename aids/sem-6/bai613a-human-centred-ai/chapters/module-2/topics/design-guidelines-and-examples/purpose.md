### Learning Purpose: Design Guidelines and Examples

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding established design guidelines is crucial for building AI systems that are truly useful, trustworthy, and ethical. Without a human-centred approach, AI can be opaque, biased, and fail to meet real user needs, leading to poor adoption and potential harm.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to apply core HCAI design principles—such as supporting human agency, ensuring transparency, and managing bias—to their own projects. They will analyze both positive and negative real-world examples to understand the practical impact of these guidelines on user experience and system success.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module builds directly on the foundational concepts of trust and ethics from Module 1. It provides the practical framework needed to implement those concepts, acting as a critical bridge to later modules on prototyping and evaluating AI systems.

**4. Real-world applications**
These guidelines are applied everywhere from conversational AI (e.g., chatbots that clearly state their limitations) and recommendation systems (e.g., providing explanations for suggestions) to high-stakes domains like healthcare and criminal justice, where mitigating bias and ensuring user control are paramount.
