# Learning Purpose: Two-Dimensional HCAI Framework

### 1. Why is this topic important?
This topic is crucial because it provides a structured model for moving beyond the simplistic "automation vs. augmentation" debate. It equips future designers and developers with the conceptual tools needed to systematically analyze and design AI systems that effectively balance human control and computer automation, ensuring technology remains a empowering partner rather than an opaque replacement.

### 2. What will students learn?
Students will learn to deconstruct and apply the two-dimensional HCAI framework. They will understand its axes: the levels of automation (from low to high) and the levels of human control (from low to high). They will practice using this model to categorize existing AI systems, identify desirable design quadrants, and conceptualize new systems that maximize both human autonomy and AI capability.

### 3. How does it connect to other concepts?
This framework integrates core concepts from Module 1, such as human-centred design principles and ethics. It serves as a practical bridge to subsequent modules on implementation, providing the analytical foundation for designing explainable AI (XAI), robust user interfaces, and ethical interaction patterns that are covered later in the course.

### 4. Real-world applications
This framework is directly applied in designing collaborative robotics, clinical decision-support systems, and adaptive educational software. For instance, it helps determine the appropriate level of autonomy for a co-bot on a factory floor or the right amount of suggestion versus final say in a medical AI diagnostic tool.
