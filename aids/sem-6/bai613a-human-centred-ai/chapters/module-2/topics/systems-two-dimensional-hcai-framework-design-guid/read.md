# Systems in Human-Centred AI

===============

## Overview

In Human-Centred Artificial Intelligence (AI), a system refers to any technology or entity that is designed to interact with humans. Systems can be simple, such as a chatbot, or complex, like a smart home. Understanding systems is crucial in designing reliable, safe, and trustworthy AI systems.

### Key Definitions

- **System**: A technology or entity designed to interact with humans.
- **Human-Centred AI**: An approach that prioritizes human needs, values, and well-being in the design and development of AI systems.
- **Reliable**: An AI system that consistently performs as expected and intended.
- **Safe**: An AI system that minimizes harm to humans and the environment.
- **Trustworthy**: An AI system that is transparent, explainable, and accountable.

## Two-Dimensional HCAI Framework

=============================

The Two-Dimensional Human-Centred AI Framework (2D-HCAI) is a comprehensive approach to designing trustworthy AI systems. The framework consists of two dimensions:

### Dimension 1: Human-Centred Design

- **Empathy**: Understanding human needs, values, and emotions.
- **Co-design**: Collaborating with humans in the design process.
- **Explainability**: Providing transparent and understandable AI decision-making processes.

### Dimension 2: Technology-Centred Design

- **Technical Feasibility**: Ensuring the technical capabilities of the AI system.
- **Robustness**: Ensuring the AI system's reliability and performance in various scenarios.
- **Security**: Ensuring the AI system's security and protection of sensitive data.

## Design Guidelines

================

### Guidelines for Human-Centred Design

- **User-Centred**: Design AI systems that meet human needs and expectations.
- **Transparency**: Provide clear and understandable AI decision-making processes.
- **Accountability**: Establish clear lines of responsibility and accountability for AI system performance.

### Guidelines for Technology-Centred Design

- **Robustness Testing**: Conduct thorough testing to ensure AI system reliability and performance.
- **Security Protocols**: Implement robust security protocols to protect sensitive data.
- **Continuous Monitoring**: Continuously monitor AI system performance and update as needed.

## Examples

==========

### Example 1: Smart Home System

- **Human-Centred Design**: A smart home system that learns users' habits and preferences to provide personalized recommendations.
- **Technology-Centred Design**: A system that integrates with various smart devices to provide seamless control and automation.

### Example 2: Healthcare Chatbot

- **Human-Centred Design**: A chatbot that provides empathetic and supportive interactions with patients, addressing their concerns and needs.
- **Technology-Centred Design**: A system that uses machine learning algorithms to provide accurate and personalized medical advice.

### Example 3: Autonomous Vehicle

- **Human-Centred Design**: An autonomous vehicle that prioritizes human safety and well-being, with features such as automatic emergency braking and lane-keeping assist.
- **Technology-Centred Design**: A system that uses advanced sensors and computer vision to navigate complex road scenarios and make informed decisions.

### Key Takeaways

- Systems in Human-Centred AI are designed to interact with humans and prioritize their needs and values.
- The 2D-HCAI Framework provides a comprehensive approach to designing trustworthy AI systems.
- Design guidelines prioritize both human-centred and technology-centred design approaches.
- Examples of Human-Centred AI systems include smart home systems, healthcare chatbots, and autonomous vehicles.
