### Learning Purpose: Trustworthy AI Systems

**1. Why is this topic important?**
As AI becomes deeply integrated into critical domains like healthcare, finance, and justice, ensuring these systems are trustworthy is paramount. An untrustworthy AI can perpetuate bias, make opaque decisions, and fail catastrophically, eroding public confidence and causing real-world harm. This topic addresses the ethical and technical pillars required to build AI that is safe, fair, and reliable for human use.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core principles of trustworthy AI, often defined by frameworks like FATE (Fairness, Accountability, Transparency, and Ethics). This includes techniques for identifying and mitigating bias, creating explainable AI (XAI) models, establishing accountability pathways, and designing for robustness and security against adversarial attacks.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the practical application of Module 1's human-centred design philosophy. It directly builds upon concepts of ethics and values by providing the technical and procedural methodologies to operationalize them. It also serves as a crucial foundation for subsequent modules on implementation, governance, and regulation.

**4. Real-world applications**
Principles of trustworthy AI are applied in developing ethical recruitment algorithms, creating explainable diagnostic tools in healthcare, building transparent loan approval systems, and ensuring the safety and reliability of autonomous vehicles and predictive policing tools.
