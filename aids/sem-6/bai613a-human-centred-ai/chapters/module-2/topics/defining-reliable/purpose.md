# Learning Purpose: Defining Reliable in Human-Centred AI

### 1. Why is this topic important?
Defining reliability is foundational to building trustworthy AI systems. In a Human-Centred AI context, reliability transcends mere technical performance; it is about creating systems that users can depend on to perform safely, consistently, and as expected in real-world scenarios. A clear, multi-faceted definition is crucial for identifying and mitigating risks, ensuring user safety, and fostering public trust in AI technologies.

### 2. What will students learn?
Students will learn to articulate a comprehensive definition of reliability that incorporates key dimensions: **robustness** (performance under changing conditions), **resilience** (ability to recover from errors), and **reproducibility** (consistent results). They will analyze how reliability is measured and the inherent trade-offs, such as between model accuracy and robustness to adversarial attacks or unexpected inputs.

### 3. How does it connect to other concepts?
This topic is intrinsically linked to core HCAI principles. Reliability is a prerequisite for **safety** and a cornerstone of **trustworthiness**. It directly influences **user experience** and **acceptance**. Furthermore, a reliable system is essential for ensuring **fairness** (as unreliable performance can disproportionately impact certain user groups) and **transparency** (as unpredictable behaviour is inherently opaque).

### 4. Real-world applications
Understanding reliability is critical for deploying AI in high-stakes domains. This includes:
*   **Healthcare:** Diagnostic algorithms must perform reliably across diverse patient data.
*   **Autonomous Vehicles:** Systems must be robust to unpredictable weather and road conditions.
*   **Finance:** Loan approval models must provide consistent, reproducible decisions.
*   **Customer Service Chatbots:** They need to handle a wide range of queries without failing catastrophically.
