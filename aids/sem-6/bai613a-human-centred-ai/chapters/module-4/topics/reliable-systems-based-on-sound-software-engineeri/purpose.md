### Learning Purpose: Module 4 - Reliable Systems Based on Sound Software Engineering Practice

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because the reliability of an AI system is foundational to its human-centredness. Without robust engineering practices, even the most ethically-aligned or innovative AI can fail, causing harm, eroding trust, and violating core principles of safety and security. This module establishes that reliability isn't an afterthought but an integral requirement built into the development lifecycle.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to apply established software engineering principles—such as rigorous testing (unit, integration), version control, continuous integration/deployment (CI/CD), and modular design—specifically to AI systems. They will understand how to create reproducible, maintainable, and verifiable AI pipelines to mitigate risks like model drift, data poisoning, and unexpected edge-case behaviour.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly operationalizes the theoretical concepts from previous modules. It connects ethical principles (Module 2) and user needs (Module 1) to practical implementation, ensuring that fairness, transparency, and accountability are not just designed but are reliably engineered into the final system. It provides the technical foundation for safe deployment (Module 5).

**4. Real-world applications**
These practices are applied in safety-critical industries like healthcare (diagnostic AI), autonomous vehicles, and financial services (credit scoring), where system failures have severe consequences. They are also essential for maintaining large-scale, consumer-facing AI products (e.g., recommendation systems, virtual assistants) to ensure consistent and secure performance.
