### Learning Purpose: Safety Culture through Business Management Strategies

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Establishing a robust safety culture is critical for the responsible development and deployment of AI systems. This topic is vital because technical solutions alone are insufficient; a proactive, organisation-wide culture, driven by management, is essential to systematically identify, mitigate, and prevent AI-related harms, ensuring trust and ethical compliance.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn how core business management strategies—such as leadership commitment, change management, risk governance frameworks, and incentive structures—can be leveraged to build and sustain a pervasive safety culture within an organisation. They will analyse how to integrate ethical AI principles into operational policies and daily practices.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module connects the technical concepts of AI risk (e.g., bias, fairness, robustness from earlier modules) to the organisational and human factors required to manage them. It builds upon governance frameworks by focusing on their practical implementation and cultural adoption, acting as the crucial bridge between policy and practice.

**4. Real-world applications**
Students will be equipped to design safety protocols, develop training programs, and advise on organisational structures that prioritise safety. This is directly applicable to roles in AI ethics auditing, product management, compliance, and leadership positions where instilling a culture of responsibility is key to mitigating real-world risks.
