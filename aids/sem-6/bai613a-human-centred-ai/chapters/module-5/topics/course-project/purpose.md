### Learning Purpose: Course Project

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This project is the capstone of your Human-Centred AI studies, moving from theory to practice. It is vital because it challenges you to integrate and apply core HCAI principles—such as fairness, transparency, and user empowerment—to a tangible problem. This experience mirrors the complex, multi-faceted nature of designing AI systems in the real world, preparing you to be a proactive and ethical practitioner.

**2. What will students learn?**
You will learn to design, prototype, and critically evaluate a human-centred AI application. This involves scoping a real-world problem, selecting appropriate technical and design methods, implementing a solution (e.g., a model, interface, or framework), and rigorously assessing its impact from both a user and ethical perspective. You will develop crucial skills in project management, problem-solving, and articulating design rationale.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This project synthesizes all preceding modules. You will directly apply concepts of ethical frameworks (Module 1), user experience (UX) design (Module 2), bias and fairness in algorithms (Module 3), and transparent, explainable AI (XAI) techniques (Module 4). It forces you to see the interconnectedness of these disciplines, demonstrating that successful HCAI requires their simultaneous consideration.

**4. Real-world applications**
The skills honed here are directly applicable to roles in AI product management, UX research, ethics auditing, and AI development. You will be equipped to build systems that are not just technically sound but are also trustworthy, accessible, and truly beneficial for end-users, whether in healthcare, finance, education, or consumer technology.
