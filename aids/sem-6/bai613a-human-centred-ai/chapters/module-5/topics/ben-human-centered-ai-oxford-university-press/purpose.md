### Learning Purpose: Module 5 - Human-Centred AI

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because it shifts the focus of AI development from purely technical performance to systems that augment human capabilities, respect user autonomy, and align with human values and ethics. It addresses the growing societal need for AI that is trustworthy, fair, and beneficial, mitigating risks like bias, opacity, and loss of control.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core principles of Human-Centred AI (HCAI), including how to design AI systems that are understandable, controllable, and transparent. They will explore frameworks for ensuring fairness, evaluating societal impact, and fostering effective human-AI collaboration.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module synthesizes concepts from AI ethics (bias, fairness), explainable AI (XAI), and interaction design. It provides the practical, ethical framework for applying technical models from previous modules (e.g., machine learning, neural networks) in a responsible and user-focused manner.

**4. Real-world applications**
HCAI principles are applied in high-stakes domains like healthcare (diagnostic AI assistants), criminal justice (risk assessment tools), autonomous vehicles, and recommendation systems, ensuring they are safe, equitable, and enhance rather than replace human decision-making.
