### Learning Purpose: Module 5 - Books 1. Shneiderman

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding Ben Shneiderman’s work is crucial because it presents a powerful, human-centred alternative to the dominant narrative of autonomous AI. It shifts the focus from simply building intelligent machines to designing reliable, safe, and trustworthy systems that amplify human capabilities. This philosophy is essential for creating ethical and effective AI that serves societal needs.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core principles of Shneiderman’s framework, particularly his “Human-Centered AI” (HCAI) model. This includes the goals of ensuring human control (supervision, accountability) and high levels of automation (performance, competence). They will analyze his eight rules for designing AI, such as ensuring reliability, safety, and transparency, to understand how to build systems that enhance human experience.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon earlier modules on AI ethics, bias, and explainability (XAI). It provides a practical, design-oriented framework for implementing those ethical principles. It also contrasts with and complements other AI paradigms, offering a concrete methodology for achieving responsible innovation and aligning AI design with human-computer interaction (HCI) best practices.

**4. Real-world applications**
Shneiderman’s principles are applied in the design of co-pilot systems, clinical decision support tools, and collaborative robotics, where AI assists rather than replaces human experts. They are also fundamental in creating governance frameworks for high-risk applications like autonomous vehicles and criminal justice, ensuring that systems remain under meaningful human control and are accountable.
