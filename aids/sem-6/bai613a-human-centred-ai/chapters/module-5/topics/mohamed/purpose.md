### Learning Purpose: Mohamed (Module 5)

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial as it examines a specific case study ("Mohamed") to ground the abstract principles of Human-Centred AI (HCAI) in a tangible, human story. It highlights the real-world societal and ethical consequences when AI systems fail to be designed with people at their core, moving the discussion from theory to impactful reality.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to analyze the technical, ethical, and systemic factors that led to a specific AI failure. They will deconstruct the case to identify where human-centred principles (e.g., fairness, accountability, transparency) were absent and propose alternative, human-centric design and governance approaches that could have prevented harm.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon prior modules on AI ethics (bias, fairness), design thinking, and policy. It serves as a synthesis point, requiring students to apply their knowledge of technical mitigations (e.g., bias audits), participatory design, and regulatory frameworks to a unified, complex scenario.

**4. Real-world applications**
Understanding this case equips students to conduct critical incident analysis in their future careers. This skill is directly applicable to roles in AI auditing, ethics consulting, policy development, and product management, enabling them to build more responsible, trustworthy, and equitable AI systems by learning from past mistakes.
