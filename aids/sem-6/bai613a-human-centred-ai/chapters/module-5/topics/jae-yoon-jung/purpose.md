**Module 5: Jae-Yoon Jung**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because Jae-Yoon Jung's research provides a critical bridge between advanced AI theory and practical, usable applications. His work emphasizes that for AI to be truly effective and beneficial, it must be designed with human needs, capabilities, and contexts at the forefront. Understanding his contributions moves us beyond purely technical performance metrics and into the realm of creating AI systems that people can actually trust, understand, and collaborate with effectively.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn about Jung's key frameworks and principles for human-centred AI (HCAI), particularly his "human-in-the-loop" (HITL) design strategies. They will explore how his approach prioritizes user control, interpretability, and the augmentation of human intelligence rather than its replacement. The module will cover specific design methodologies that ensure AI systems remain accountable and aligned with human values and goals.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon foundational concepts in HCAI (Module 1), ethics (Module 2), and explainable AI (XAI - Module 4). It provides a practical design framework that operationalizes ethical principles like fairness and transparency. Jung's HITL models also connect to earlier discussions on automation versus augmentation, showing a concrete path for implementing collaborative AI systems.

**4. Real-world applications**
Jung's principles are applied in high-stakes domains where trust and precision are paramount. This includes clinical decision-support systems where doctors and AI collaborate on diagnoses, intelligent tutoring systems that adapt to student needs, and decision-aid tools in finance or engineering that provide explainable recommendations, ensuring final human oversight and accountability.
