### Learning Purpose: Continuous Internal Evaluation

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Continuous Internal Evaluation is crucial because it shifts AI development from a one-time launch to an ongoing, adaptive process. Without it, AI systems can silently develop harmful biases, performance degradation, or misalign with user needs over time, causing real-world harm and eroding trust. This proactive approach is fundamental to creating responsible, ethical, and sustainable Human-Centred AI.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to design and implement a framework for the continuous monitoring and assessment of AI systems *after* deployment. This includes defining key performance indicators (KPIs) for fairness, accuracy, and user satisfaction; establishing feedback loops with end-users; and creating processes for iterative model retraining and improvement based on real-world performance data.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the operational culmination of earlier concepts. It directly applies principles of **Ethical AI (Module 2)** by continuously auditing for bias and **User Experience (UX) Design (Module 3)** by ensuring the system evolves with user needs. It relies on technical **Model Evaluation metrics (Module 4)** and provides the data needed for effective **Governance and Accountability (Module 6)**.

**4. Real-world applications**
This is standard practice for major tech platforms (e.g., continuously evaluating a recommendation algorithm's fairness and engagement). It is also critical in high-stakes domains like healthcare, where continuous evaluation of a diagnostic tool ensures its accuracy evolves with new medical research and diverse patient data.
