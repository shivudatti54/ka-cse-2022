### Learning Purpose: Module 5 - Chang S.

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the "Chang S." (likely referring to Shuangshuang Chang's work on participatory design) is crucial as it provides a concrete framework for moving beyond theoretical ethics. It addresses the practical challenge of how to genuinely involve diverse stakeholders in the AI design process, ensuring systems are equitable, accountable, and truly serve human needs.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the principles and methodologies of participatory design as applied to AI development. They will explore techniques for co-creating technology with users, not just for them, and analyze how this approach mitigates bias, builds trust, and leads to more robust and useful AI systems.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon prior modules on AI ethics, fairness, and bias. It takes the abstract goal of "human-centredness" and provides a practical toolkit for achieving it, connecting the *why* of ethical AI with the *how*. It also complements topics on value-sensitive design and stakeholder analysis.

**4. Real-world applications**
The principles learned are applicable across domains: from involving medical professionals and patients in designing clinical AI tools, to co-creating civic AI with community members to avoid discriminatory outcomes in public services, or engaging factory workers in the design of collaborative robots.
