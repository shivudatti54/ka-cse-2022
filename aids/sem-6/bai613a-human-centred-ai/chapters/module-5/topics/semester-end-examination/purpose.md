# Learning Purpose: Semester-End Examination

**1. Why is this topic important?**
The examination is crucial as it provides a summative assessment of your ability to synthesize and critically apply the core principles of Human-Centred AI (HCAI). It evaluates whether you can move beyond theory and demonstrate a holistic understanding of how to design, develop, and critique AI systems that are ethical, fair, and enhance human capabilities.

**2. What will students learn?**
You will learn to articulate and defend your knowledge of HCAI frameworks, ethical dilemmas, and design methodologies. The exam assesses your proficiency in analyzing case studies, identifying potential biases in AI systems, and proposing solutions that prioritize human well-being, accountability, and transparency.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This exam integrates concepts from all previous modules: ethical foundations (Module 1), UX design for AI (Module 2), bias and fairness (Module 3), and explainability and trust (Module 4). It requires you to weave these threads together, showing how they interconnect to form a cohesive approach to responsible AI development.

**4. Real-world applications**
Successfully completing this exam demonstrates you have the foundational competency to contribute to real-world HCAI projects. This includes roles in auditing AI systems for ethical compliance, designing user interfaces that foster appropriate trust, and developing governance policies that ensure AI is deployed responsibly across industries like healthcare, finance, and public services.
