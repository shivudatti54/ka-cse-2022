### Learning Purpose: Module 5 - Human-Centred AI and Sangwon Lee

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because it moves beyond the purely technical aspects of AI to focus on its human and societal impact. Understanding the principles of Human-Centred AI (HCAI) is essential for developing ethical, trustworthy, and beneficial systems that augment human capabilities rather than replace or harm them. Sangwon Lee's work provides a vital case study in applying these principles to real-world design and policy.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core tenets of HCAI, including concepts like fairness, transparency, accountability, and user empowerment. They will examine the specific contributions of researcher Sangwon Lee, analyzing his frameworks or models for designing AI that prioritizes human needs, values, and well-being throughout the development lifecycle.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds upon earlier technical foundations (e.g., algorithms, data handling) and ethical considerations (e.g., bias, privacy). It connects the abstract ethical guidelines from previous modules to concrete design methodologies, acting as a bridge between theory and practical implementation in the field of responsible AI.

**4. Real-world applications**
The principles learned are applicable across industries. Students will see how HCAI informs the design of safer autonomous vehicles, more equitable hiring algorithms, collaborative AI tools in healthcare, and explainable AI systems in finance, ensuring technology serves humanity effectively and justly.
