### Learning Purpose: Module 5 - Machine Learning (Oxford University Press 2021)

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Machine Learning (ML) is a transformative technology powering modern innovation. It enables computers to learn from data and make predictions or decisions without being explicitly programmed for every task. Understanding its principles is crucial for navigating a data-driven world and developing intelligent systems that impact nearly every industry.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the foundational concepts of ML, including key algorithms (e.g., linear regression, decision trees, k-nearest neighbours), the model training process, and the critical importance of quality data. They will explore different types of learning, such as supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds upon prior knowledge of programming, statistics, and data structures. It is the practical application of these fields, providing the tools to create predictive models and intelligent agents. It also serves as a core foundation for more advanced topics like deep learning and artificial intelligence.

**4. Real-world applications**
The applications are vast and include:
*   **Recommendation Systems:** Personalizing content on platforms like Netflix and Spotify.
*   **Predictive Analytics:** Forecasting stock trends, weather, and disease outbreaks.
*   **Computer Vision:** Enabling facial recognition and self-driving car navigation.
*   **Natural Language Processing:** Powering chatbots, translators, and voice assistants.
