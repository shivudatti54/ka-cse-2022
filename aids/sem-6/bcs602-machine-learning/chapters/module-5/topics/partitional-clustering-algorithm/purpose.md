# Learning Objectives
After studying this topic, you should be able to:
1.  Define partitional clustering and differentiate it from other clustering approaches like hierarchical and density-based clustering.
2.  Explain the step-by-step procedure of the K-Means algorithm, including initialization, assignment, and update steps.
3.  Describe the objective function (Within-Cluster Sum of Squares) that K-Means optimizes and why it is used.
4.  Identify and discuss the key challenges and limitations of the standard K-Means algorithm (e.g., choice of k, sensitivity to initialization).
5.  Apply the Elbow Method to determine a suitable number of clusters (k) for a given dataset.
6.  Explain the improvement offered by the K-Means++ initialization technique and its rationale.
