### Learning Purpose: Advantages and Disadvantages of ANN

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the advantages and disadvantages of Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs) is crucial for making informed decisions in ML project design. It ensures the correct model is selected for a given problem, balancing performance with computational cost and practical constraints, thereby avoiding inefficient or ineffective solutions.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to articulate the key strengths of ANNs, such as their ability to learn complex non-linear patterns and their adaptability. They will also identify their critical limitations, including their "black box" nature, substantial data and computational requirements, and propensity to overfit.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic connects directly to prior knowledge of model fundamentals (like linear regression from Module 2) by contrasting ANN complexity with simpler models. It also sets the stage for subsequent modules on more advanced architectures (like CNNs and RNNs in Module 5) and techniques to mitigate ANN drawbacks, such as regularization and transfer learning.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied when choosing ANNs for tasks like image recognition or fraud detection (leveraging their advantages) while being mindful of their need for explainability in fields like healthcare or finance (acknowledging their disadvantages).
