# **Chapter 2 Revision Notes (2.6-2.8)**

## **Definition and Notations**

- Bivariate data: Data that relates two variables
- Multivariate data: Data that relates more than two variables
- **n** (n): Sample size
- **x** (x): Random variable
- **y** (y): Random variable
- **r** (r): Correlation coefficient
- **μx** (μx): Mean of x
- **μy** (μy): Mean of y
- **σx** (σx): Standard deviation of x
- **σy** (σy): Standard deviation of y
- **σxy** (σxy): Covariance between x and y

## **Correlation Coefficient (r)**

- Definition: Measure of linear relationship between two variables
- Formula: `r = (σxy) / (σx * σy)`
- Properties:
  - Range: -1 to 1
  - Value of 1: Perfect positive linear relationship
  - Value of -1: Perfect negative linear relationship
  - Value of 0: No linear relationship

## **Covariance**

- Definition: Measure of linear relationship between two variables
- Formula: `σxy = Σ[(xi - μx)(yi - μy)] / (n - 1)`
- Properties:
  - Measure of spread of x and y around their means
  - Positive value: x and y tend to increase together
  - Negative value: x and y tend to decrease together

## **Multivariate Statistics**

- **Multivariate normal distribution**: Distribution with more than two variables
- **Multivariate regression**: Model that relates multiple variables to a single dependent variable
- **Principal Component Analysis (PCA)**: Method for dimensionality reduction in multivariate data

## **Important Formulas**

- **Regression equation**: `y = β0 + β1x + ε`
- **Multivariate regression equation**: `y = β0 + β1x + … + βnx + ε`

## **Theorem**

- **Multivariate normal distribution theorem**: If two variables are normally distributed, then their covariance is also normally distributed.
