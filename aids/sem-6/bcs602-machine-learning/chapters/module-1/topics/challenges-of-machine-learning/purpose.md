# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Identify and explain the major categories of challenges faced in machine learning implementations, including data-related, algorithmic, computational, and ethical challenges.

2. Distinguish between overfitting and underfitting, and describe the bias-variance tradeoff that governs model complexity selection.

3. Apply appropriate techniques for handling imbalanced datasets, including resampling methods and evaluation metric selection.

4. Analyze real-world ML failures caused by data bias and explain how to detect and mitigate such biases in training data.

5. Evaluate the tradeoffs between model interpretability and predictive performance in the context of different application domains.

6. Design monitoring strategies for production ML systems to detect and address model drift over time.

7. Critically assess ethical considerations in ML, including privacy concerns, adversarial vulnerabilities, and environmental impact of large-scale training.

8. Connect theoretical ML challenges to practical implementation decisions and explain why real-world ML projects require iterative problem-solving approaches.
