### Learning Purpose: Continuous Internal Evaluation

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) is crucial as it shifts the focus from high-stakes, end-of-term exams to an ongoing, formative assessment process. In a complex and rapidly evolving field like AI, this allows for regular feedback, helping students to iteratively improve their understanding and practical skills throughout the module, ensuring they keep pace with the material.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the purpose and mechanics of the CIE system within their course. This includes understanding the various assessment components (e.g., quizzes, programming assignments, mini-projects, presentations) and their respective weightings. They will develop strategies for effective time management and consistent engagement to successfully meet these continuous academic benchmarks.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
CIE directly connects to and reinforces all technical concepts within the AI curriculum. Each assignment or test is designed to assess comprehension of specific topics like search algorithms, machine learning, or neural networks. This iterative process ensures foundational knowledge is solid before advancing to more complex subjects, creating a cohesive and cumulative learning journey.

**4. Real-world applications**
The CIE model mirrors the agile, iterative development cycles and continuous performance monitoring inherent to real-world AI projects. It prepares students for professional environments where consistent output, regular code reviews, and iterative improvement based on feedback are standard practice for building and maintaining AI systems.
