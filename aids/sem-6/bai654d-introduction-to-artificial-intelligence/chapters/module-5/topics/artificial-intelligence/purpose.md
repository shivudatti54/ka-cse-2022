# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define artificial intelligence and distinguish between weak AI and strong AI, explaining the current state of AI technology and its limitations.

2. Explain the intelligent agent framework, describing different types of agents from simple reflex agents to learning agents and their respective characteristics.

3. Apply state-space search algorithms including breadth-first search, depth-first search, and A* search to solve classic AI problems, analyzing their completeness, optimality, and complexity properties.

4. Implement the minimax algorithm for two-player zero-sum games and describe how alpha-beta pruning improves computational efficiency.

5. Distinguish between supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning paradigms, identifying appropriate use cases and common algorithms for each.

6. Describe the architecture and training process of artificial neural networks, including forward propagation and backpropagation mechanisms.

7. Represent knowledge using logical formalisms, semantic networks, and production rules, performing inference through appropriate reasoning mechanisms.

8. Explain fundamental natural language processing concepts including tokenization, parsing, and the challenges inherent in computational language understanding.
