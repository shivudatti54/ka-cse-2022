### **Module 5: Probabilistic Reasoning in AI**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Probabilistic reasoning is a cornerstone of modern AI because it provides a mathematical framework for handling uncertainty and making rational decisions with incomplete information. The real world is noisy and unpredictable; deterministic "if-then" rules often fail. This approach allows AI systems to model real-world complexity, weigh evidence, and predict likely outcomes, which is fundamental for creating robust and intelligent applications.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core principles of representing and reasoning with uncertainty using probability theory. This includes understanding key concepts like **Bayesian Networks** (also known as Belief Networks), which graphically model the probabilistic relationships between a set of variables. They will learn how to perform inference on these networks to update beliefs given new evidence.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds upon logic and knowledge representation (Module 2-3) by moving from deterministic to probabilistic models. It is the essential theoretical foundation for subsequent topics like **Machine Learning** (e.g., Naive Bayes classifiers), **Natural Language Processing** (for word prediction), and **Computer Vision** (for object recognition under uncertainty). It connects high-level reasoning to data-driven learning.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied in a vast range of fields:
*   **Medical Diagnosis:** Systems that compute the probability of a disease given a set of symptoms.
*   **Spam Filtering:** Classifying emails as spam based on the probability of certain words appearing.
*   **Financial Risk Assessment:** Modeling market uncertainty to assess investment risks.
*   **Autonomous Vehicles:** Predicting the likely actions of other drivers and pedestrians.
