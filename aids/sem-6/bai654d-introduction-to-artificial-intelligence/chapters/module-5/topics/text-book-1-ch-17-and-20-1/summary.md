# Text Book 1: Chapter 17 and 20 1

### Overview

- Chapter 17: Learning
- Chapter 20: 1 - Expert Systems

### Key Concepts

**Learning**

- Definition: The process of acquiring knowledge and improving performance through experience and feedback
- Types of Learning:
  - Supervised Learning
  - Unsupervised Learning
  - Reinforcement Learning
- Examples: Decision Trees, Neural Networks

**Expert Systems**

- Definition: A computer program that mimics the decision-making ability of a human expert
- Components:
  - Knowledge Base
  - Inference Engine
- Types of Expert Systems:
  - Rule-Based Systems
  - Case-Based Systems

### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Definition of Learning Rate:** α (alpha) - the rate at which the weights are adjusted
- **Definition of Weight:** w - the strength of the connection between two neurons
- **Definition of Activation Function:** σ(x) - the output of the activation function, where x is the input

### Important Theorems

- **Bayes' Theorem:**
  - P(A|B) = P(A and B) / P(B)
- **No Free Lunch Theorem:**
  - No algorithm can achieve optimal performance on all tasks

### Quick Revision

- Learning:
  - Supervised Learning: learning from labeled data
  - Unsupervised Learning: learning from unlabeled data
  - Reinforcement Learning: learning from feedback
- Expert Systems:
  - Rule-Based Systems: using rules to make decisions
  - Case-Based Systems: using past cases to make decisions
