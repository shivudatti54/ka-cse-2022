### **Learning Purpose: Module 5 - Introduction to AI and Expert Systems**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Expert Systems (ES) represent one of the most successful and historically significant branches of AI. Understanding them provides a concrete foundation for how AI can capture and apply human expertise, demystifying the "how" behind intelligent systems. They are a crucial bridge between theoretical AI concepts and practical, rule-based applications.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core architecture of an expert system, including its key components: the knowledge base (storing rules and facts) and the inference engine (the processing logic). They will understand how these systems emulate human expert decision-making to solve complex problems within specific domains.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds upon earlier knowledge of knowledge representation (e.g., rules, logic) and search strategies (used by the inference engine). It serves as a critical precursor to understanding modern systems like machine learning, which can be seen as a way to automatically generate the knowledge base that expert systems traditionally required humans to build manually.

**4. Real-world applications**
Expert systems are deployed in vital fields such as medical diagnosis (e.g., MYCIN), mechanical troubleshooting, financial loan approval, and customer support chatbots. They demonstrate a timeless, practical application of AI for structured, logical problem-solving.
