### Revision Notes for Text Book 1: Chapter 4

#### Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

**Key Concepts**

- **Predicate Logic (PL)**: A formal language for representing knowledge using logical statements.
- **Knowledge Representation (KR)**: The process of representing knowledge in a formal, structured way.
- **Propositional and Predicate Logic**: Used to represent statements and relationships in KR.

**Definitions**

- **Atom**: A basic unit of knowledge, represented as a predicate and its arguments.
- **Sentence**: A well-formed expression in PL, consisting of atoms and logical operators.
- **Formula**: A logical expression involving atoms, logical operators, and quantifiers.

**Important Formulas and Theorems**

- **Tarski's Hierarchy**:
  - ∀x P(x) → P(a)
  - ∃x P(x) → P(b)
  - ∀x (P(x) → Q(x))
  - ∃x (P(x) ∧ Q(x))
- **Monadic Predicate Logic**:
  - ∀x (P(x) → Q(x)) → (∀x P(x) → ∀x Q(x))
  - ∃x (P(x) ∧ Q(x)) → (∃x P(x) ∧ ∃x Q(x))

**Important Theorems**

- **Tarski's Translation Scheme**: A method for translating PL formulas into a formal language.
- **Henkin's Completeness Theorem**: A result that establishes the completeness of PL.

**Key Points**

- PL is used to represent knowledge in KR.
- Atoms represent basic units of knowledge.
- Sentences and formulas are used to represent statements and relationships.
- Tarski's Hierarchy and Monadic Predicate Logic are important concepts in PL.
