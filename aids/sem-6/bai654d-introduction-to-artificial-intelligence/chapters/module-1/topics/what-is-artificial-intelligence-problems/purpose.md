# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define artificial intelligence from both cognitive (thinking) and behavioral (acting) perspectives, providing examples of each approach.

2. Identify and explain the four essential components required to formally represent any AI problem: initial state, goal state, operators, and path cost.

3. Distinguish between well-defined problems (with complete specifications) and ill-defined problems (with ambiguous or incomplete specifications), citing appropriate examples of each.

4. Explain the concepts of problem space and state space, and describe how they relate to each other in the context of AI problem solving.

5. Classify problems as toy problems or real-world problems, understanding the educational and practical purposes of each category.

6. Apply the formal problem representation framework to convert word problems into structured AI problem formulations.

7. Analyze the size and complexity of problem spaces to determine appropriate search strategies for different problem types.

8. Connect the study of AI problems to subsequent topics in search algorithms, establishing the conceptual foundation for informed and uninformed search techniques.
