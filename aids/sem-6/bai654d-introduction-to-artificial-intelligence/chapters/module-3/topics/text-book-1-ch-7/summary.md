### Text Book 1: Ch 7 - Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

#### Key Concepts

- **Definition of Artificial Intelligence (AI)**:
  - A branch of computer science that emphasizes the creation of intelligent machines
  - Merging of computer science, information technology, and domains such as engineering, psychology, and philosophy
- **Types of AI**:
  - Narrow or Weak AI: designed to perform a specific task
  - General or Strong AI: possesses human-like intelligence and capabilities

#### Statistical Reasoning

- **Bayes' Theorem**:
  - P(A|B) = P(A and B) / P(B)
  - Used to update probabilities based on new information
- **Conditional Probability**:
  - P(A|B) = P(A and B) / P(B)
  - P(B|A) = P(A and B) / P(A)
- **Probability Distributions**:
  - Discrete and Continuous Distributions
  - Expected Value (E(X)) and Variance (Var(X))

#### Symbolic Reasoning under Uncertainty

- **Uncertainty and Probability**:
  - Representing uncertainty in symbolic terms
  - Use of probability theory to reason under uncertainty
- **Inference and Decision Making**:
  - Using Bayesian networks and influence diagrams to reason under uncertainty
  - Decision theory and utility functions

#### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Conditional Entropy (H(X|Y))**:
  - Measures the average amount of uncertainty in X given Y
- **Joint Entropy (H(X,Y))**:
  - Measures the total amount of uncertainty in X and Y
- **Entropy (H(X))**:
  - Measures the average amount of uncertainty in X

#### Important Theorems

- **Kybernetic Theorem**:
  - A fundamental theorem in control theory and AI
  - States that the behavior of a system is determined by its initial conditions and the inputs it receives
