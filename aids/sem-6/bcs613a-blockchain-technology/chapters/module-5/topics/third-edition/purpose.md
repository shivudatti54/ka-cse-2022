### Learning Purpose: Blockchain Technology

**1. Why is this important?**
Blockchain is a foundational technology revolutionizing trust, transparency, and security in digital transactions. It underpins cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin and enables innovations in finance, supply chain, digital identity, and governance. Understanding its principles is crucial for navigating the future of the decentralized web (Web3) and its impact on various industries.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core components of blockchain (blocks, hashes, distributed ledgers, consensus mechanisms like Proof-of-Work and Proof-of-Stake), how transactions are recorded and validated, and the difference between public and private blockchains. They will also explore the concept of smart contracts and their self-executing capabilities.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic connects directly to **cryptography** (hashing, digital signatures), **distributed systems** (peer-to-peer networks), **databases** (immutable ledgers), and **economics** (incentive models, tokens). It is the practical implementation of theoretical concepts like decentralization and trustless consensus.

**4. Real-world applications**
Applications include **cryptocurrencies** (Bitcoin, Ethereum), **DeFi** (decentralized finance), **NFTs** (digital ownership), **supply chain tracking** (provenance of goods), and **secure record-keeping** (land titles, medical records).
