### Decentralization using Blockchain

- Decentralization is the process of distributing power and control among multiple entities, rather than having a single central authority.
- Blockchain technology enables decentralized decision-making by using a distributed ledger to record transactions.

### Methods of Decentralization

- **Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT):** A decentralized database that stores data in a network of computers, rather than a single central location.
- **Consensus Mechanisms:** Algorithms that enable nodes on a network to agree on the state of the ledger, such as:
  - **Proof of Work (PoW):** Requires nodes to solve complex mathematical problems to validate transactions.
  - **Proof of Stake (PoS):** Requires nodes to "stake" their own coins to validate transactions.
  - **Delegated Proof of Stake (DPoS):** A variant of PoS that allows users to vote for validators.
- **Smart Contracts:** Self-executing contracts with the terms of the agreement written directly into lines of code.

### Blockchain and Full Ecosystem

- **Blockchain Architecture:**
  - **Blocks:** Collections of transactions verified and linked together using hashes.
  - **Chains:** A series of blocks linked together using hashes, creating a permanent and unalterable record.
- **Consensus Theorem:** The concept that for a decentralized system to be secure, it must be difficult for an attacker to manipulate the ledger without being detected.

### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Hash Function:** A mathematical function that takes input data of any size and produces a fixed-size string of characters, known as a hash value.
- **Public-Key Cryptography:** A method of encryption that uses a pair of keys: a public key for encryption and a private key for decryption.

### Key Takeaways

- Blockchain technology enables decentralized decision-making and has various methods of decentralization.
- DLT, consensus mechanisms, and smart contracts are key components of a blockchain ecosystem.
- Understanding blockchain architecture, consensus theorem, and important formulas and definitions is crucial for understanding decentralized systems.
