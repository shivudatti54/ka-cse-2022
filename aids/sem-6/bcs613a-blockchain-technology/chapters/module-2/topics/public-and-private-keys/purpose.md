### **Learning Purpose: Public and Private Keys**

**1. Importance:**
This topic is fundamental because public and private keys form the bedrock of security and identity in blockchain networks. They are the cryptographic mechanism that enables users to securely own assets, authenticate transactions, and interact with decentralized applications without relying on a central authority. Understanding them is essential for grasping how trust and ownership are established in a trustless environment.

**2. Learning Outcomes:**
Students will learn the asymmetric cryptography principles behind key pairs. They will be able to define the roles of a private key (a secret for signing and granting access) and a public key (an address for receiving transactions). Crucially, they will understand the one-way relationship between them and why the private key must remain confidential.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts:**
This knowledge directly connects to creating digital signatures (Module 3), which prove ownership and authorize transactions on the blockchain. It is also foundational for understanding cryptocurrency wallets (Module 4), smart contract interaction, and the overall security model of decentralized systems.

**4. Real-World Applications:**
Beyond receiving and sending crypto assets (e.g., Bitcoin, ETH), this technology secures access to decentralized finance (DeFi) platforms, verifies identity in digital systems, and enables the secure signing of legal documents or supply chain verification processes without intermediaries.
