# **Blockchain Technology Revision Notes - Chapter 2**

## **1. Decentralization using Blockchain**

- Decentralization: A system where no single entity controls or has control over the entire network
- Characteristics of Decentralization:
  - No single point of failure
  - No central authority
  - Distributed ledger technology
- Importance of Decentralization:
  - Increased security
  - Improved transparency
  - Reduced reliance on intermediaries

## **2. Methods of Decentralization**

- **1. Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT):**
  - A decentralized ledger that records transactions across a network
  - Each node on the network has a copy of the ledger
  - Transactions are verified and added to the ledger through a consensus algorithm
- **2. Blockchain Network:**
  - A network of nodes that work together to validate and record transactions
  - Each node verifies and adds transactions to the blockchain
  - The blockchain is the public ledger that records all transactions

## **3. Blockchain and Full Ecosystem**

- **1. Consensus Algorithm:**
  - A mechanism that allows nodes on the network to agree on the validity of transactions
  - Examples: Proof of Work (PoW), Proof of Stake (PoS), Byzantine Fault Tolerance (BFT)
- **2. Smart Contracts:**
  - Self-executing contracts with the terms of the agreement written directly into code
  - Automated enforcement of terms and conditions
- **3. Cryptography:**
  - The practice of secure communication in the presence of third parties
  - Used in blockchain technology to secure transactions and control access

## **Important Formulas and Definitions:**

- **Blockchain Equation:** `Blockchain = Transactions + Consensus + Smart Contracts`
- **Decentralization Index:** `Decentralization Index = (Number of Decentralized Systems) / (Total Number of Systems)`

## **Important Theorems:**

- **Gambler's Ruin Theorem:** A theorem that describes the probability of winning or losing in a decentralized system
- **Byzantine Fault Tolerance Theorem:** A theorem that describes the conditions under which a decentralized system can be fault-tolerant
