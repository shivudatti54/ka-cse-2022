### Learning Purpose: The History of Blockchain

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding blockchain's history is crucial because it provides context for its core principles of decentralization, trustlessness, and immutability. It wasn't created in a vacuum; it emerged from decades of research in cryptography and a direct reaction to the 2008 financial crisis. Knowing this evolution prevents viewing blockchain as mere technology and instead frames it as a profound socio-economic innovation with a clear purpose.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the key milestones, from the cryptographic precursors like HashCash and Merkle Trees to the 2008 Bitcoin whitepaper by Satoshi Nakamoto. They will trace the development from Bitcoin's genesis block as "digital gold" to Ethereum's introduction of programmable smart contracts, which expanded the technology's utility far beyond cryptocurrency.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This history is the foundation for all subsequent modules. It directly connects to understanding *why* consensus mechanisms like Proof-of-Work were chosen initially (Module 2), how the desire for more functionality led to smart contracts and Ethereum (Module 3), and provides the critical context for evaluating modern scalability challenges and alternative platforms (Module 4).

**4. Real-world applications**
This historical context allows students to critically assess the legitimacy and potential of modern applications. It explains the original intent behind decentralized finance (DeFi), the evolution of non-fungible tokens (NFTs) from colored coins, and provides the rationale for using blockchain in supply chain provenance, voting systems, and digital identity.
