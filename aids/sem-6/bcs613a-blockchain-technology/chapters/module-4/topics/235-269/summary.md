# **Revision Notes: Blockchain Technology (235-269)**

## **Ethereum Blockchain Basics**

- **Definition:** A decentralized, distributed ledger technology that records transactions across a network of computers.
- **Key Characteristics:**
  - Immutable (once written, cannot be altered)
  - Transparent (all transactions are publicly visible)
  - Consensus (across the network)

## **Blockchain Structure**

- **Block:** A collection of transactions, linked to the previous block through a unique hash.
- **Chain:** A sequence of linked blocks.
- **Hash:** A unique digital fingerprint of a block or transaction.

## **Ethereum Blockchain Components**

- **Ethereum Client:** A software application that interacts with the Ethereum network (e.g., Geth, Parity).
- **Ethereum Stack:**
  - **Virtual Machine (VM):** Executes smart contracts.
  - **Gas:** A measurement of computational effort.
  - **Transaction Fee:** Compensation for network usage.

## **Consensus Algorithms**

- **Proof of Work (PoW):** Energy-intensive method (e.g., Ethereum Classic).
- **Proof of Stake (PoS):** Energy-efficient method (e.g., Ethereum 2.0).

## **Important Formulas and Theorems**

- **Hash Function:** `h(x) = SHA-256(x)`
- **Digital Signature:** `σ = (m, r, s) = (m, r, s) mod p`
- **Keccak-256 Hash Function:** Used in Ethereum's PoW algorithm.

This summary covers key concepts in the Ethereum blockchain, including its structure, components, and consensus algorithms. It's essential to review these topics before exams to demonstrate a solid understanding of blockchain technology.
