# Module 4: Ethereum Blockchain - Learning Purpose

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Ethereum is the world's leading programmable blockchain and a foundational technology for Web3. Understanding its architecture is crucial because it extends blockchain's utility far beyond simple value transfer, enabling decentralized applications (dApps), smart contracts, and entirely new economic systems that are transforming finance, business, and digital ownership.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core components that distinguish Ethereum from Bitcoin, including its account-based model, the Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM), gas fees, and consensus mechanisms (Proof-of-Work & Proof-of-Stake). They will understand how to interact with the network and the fundamental principles of writing and deploying smart contracts.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module builds directly on the foundational blockchain principles covered in earlier modules (e.g., decentralization, cryptography, consensus). It provides the essential groundwork for subsequent topics like Decentralized Finance (DeFi), Non-Fungible Tokens (NFTs), and dApp development, all of which are predominantly built on the Ethereum ecosystem.

**4. Real-world applications**
Knowledge of Ethereum is directly applicable to using and developing dApps, creating and trading NFTs, participating in DeFi protocols (lending, borrowing, yield farming), and engaging with decentralized autonomous organizations (DAOs). It is the backbone for most real-world blockchain innovation happening today.
