### Chapter 6 Revision Notes: Blockchain Technology

#### Key Concepts

- **Blockchain**: A decentralized, distributed ledger technology that records transactions across a network of computers.
- **Consensus Mechanism**: A protocol that enables nodes on a network to agree on the state of the blockchain.
- **Mining**: The process of verifying transactions and adding them to the blockchain.

#### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Hash Function**: A one-way function that takes input data of any size and produces a fixed-size string of characters (e.g., SHA-256).
- **Public-Key Cryptography**: A method of secure communication that uses pairs of keys: public and private.
- **Proof of Work (PoW)**: A consensus mechanism that requires nodes to solve a complex mathematical puzzle to validate transactions.

#### Key Definitions

- **Decentralized Network**: A network that operates without a central authority or single point of control.
- **Cryptography**: The practice of secure communication by transforming plaintext into unreadable ciphertext.
- **Smart Contracts**: Self-executing contracts with the terms of the agreement written directly into lines of code.

#### Key Terms

- **Blockchain Architecture**: The design and structure of a blockchain network.
- **Consensus Algorithm**: A protocol that enables nodes on a network to agree on the state of the blockchain.
- **Cryptographic Hash**: A digital fingerprint that identifies a piece of data.

#### Important Concepts

- **Blockchain Layers**: The different layers of a blockchain network, including:
  - Application Layer: Supports decentralized applications (dApps).
  - Network Layer: Enables communication between nodes.
  - Consensus Layer: Ensures the integrity of the blockchain.
  - Data Layer: Stores the actual data.
