# Learning Objectives
After studying this topic, you should be able to:
1.  Define a smart contract and articulate its core purpose.
2.  Describe the typical lifecycle of a smart contract from creation to execution.
3.  Explain the key characteristics (e.g., autonomy, immutability) that distinguish smart contracts from traditional code.
4.  Analyze the relationship between smart contracts and underlying cryptographic primitives like digital signatures.
5.  Compare and contrast smart contracts with traditional legal contracts across multiple dimensions (cost, speed, trust).
6.  Identify real-world applications of smart contracts and discuss their associated benefits and limitations.
