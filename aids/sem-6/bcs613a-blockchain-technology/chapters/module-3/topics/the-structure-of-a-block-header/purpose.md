### Learning Purpose: The Structure of a Block Header

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the block header is fundamental because it is the cryptographic core of every block in a blockchain. It contains the critical metadata that ensures the entire chain's immutability, security, and integrity. Without a secure header structure, the trustless and decentralized nature of blockchain would not be possible.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify and explain the six key components of a standard block header: Version, Previous Block Hash, Merkle Root, Timestamp, Difficulty Target, and Nonce. They will understand how these elements work together to form a unique digital fingerprint and enable the process of mining through cryptographic hashing.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon prior knowledge of cryptographic hashing (Module 1) and the concept of a distributed ledger (Module 2). It is a prerequisite for understanding subsequent modules on mining and consensus mechanisms (e.g., Proof-of-Work), as the header's structure is what miners compete to solve.

**4. Real-world applications**
Analyzing a block header allows one to verify transactions on a blockchain explorer (e.g., Bitcoin's Block 789,456), audit a chain's history for tampering, and understand the computational effort required to secure major networks like Bitcoin and Ethereum.
