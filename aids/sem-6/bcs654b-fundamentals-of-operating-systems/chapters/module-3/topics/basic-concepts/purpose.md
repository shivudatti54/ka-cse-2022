# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Distinguish between independent and cooperating processes and explain why synchronization is necessary for cooperating processes.

2. Define and identify race conditions in concurrent programs, explaining how unpredictable outcomes arise from improper synchronization.

3. Explain the critical section problem and its three fundamental requirements: mutual exclusion, progress, and bounded waiting.

4. Describe hardware synchronization mechanisms like test-and-set and swap instructions, and explain their role in building software synchronization primitives.

5. Differentiate between various synchronization tools including mutexes, semaphores, and monitors, and identify appropriate use cases for each.

6. Analyze the concepts of deadlock and starvation, including the necessary conditions for deadlock and strategies to prevent or recover from these situations.

7. Apply synchronization concepts to solve classical problems like the producer-consumer problem and explain how semaphores provide solutions.

8. Evaluate synchronization solutions for correctness in satisfying the critical section requirements and identify potential issues like race conditions or starvation.
