# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define what a critical section is and explain why it requires special handling in concurrent systems.

2. Identify the four sections (entry, critical, exit, remainder) in the structure of a process participating in critical section solutions.

3. Explain and distinguish between the three essential requirements: mutual exclusion, progress, and bounded waiting.

4. Analyze and trace through two-process critical section solutions to verify if they satisfy all three conditions.

5. Describe how hardware instructions like test-and-set and swap can be used to implement mutual exclusion.

6. Apply the concept of critical sections to solve practical synchronization problems like producer-consumer and bank account scenarios.

7. Compare software-based and hardware-based approaches to solving the critical section problem in terms of efficiency and complexity.

8. Evaluate different solutions to the critical section problem and determine which conditions they satisfy.
