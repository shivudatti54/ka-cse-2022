### **Learning Purpose: Scheduling Criteria**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Scheduling criteria are the metrics used to evaluate and compare the performance of different CPU scheduling algorithms. Understanding these criteria is fundamental because they define what constitutes an "efficient" scheduler. The choice of which criterion to prioritize directly impacts the user experience and overall efficiency of any computing device, from smartphones to supercomputers.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the key performance metrics for analyzing scheduling algorithms. This includes CPU utilization, throughput, turnaround time, waiting time, and response time. They will understand the trade-offs involved; for instance, maximizing throughput might increase waiting time, and optimizing for interactive systems (response time) differs from batch processing systems (turnaround time).

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the logical next step after learning about specific scheduling algorithms (e.g., FCFS, SJF, Round-Robin). The criteria provide the tools to quantitatively compare these algorithms. It also connects directly to process management (what is being scheduled) and performance analysis, forming a critical link between an algorithm's design and its practical outcome.

**4. Real-world applications**
These criteria are used by OS developers to choose the right scheduler for a system's purpose (e.g., low latency for gaming PCs, high throughput for data centers). They are also essential for sysadmins and developers to diagnose performance bottlenecks, optimize application responsiveness, and configure systems effectively.
