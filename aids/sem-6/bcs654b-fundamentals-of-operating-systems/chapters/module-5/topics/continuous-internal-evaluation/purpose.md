### Learning Purpose: Continuous Internal Evaluation

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) is crucial as it shifts the focus from high-stakes, single-point exams to a more holistic assessment of a student's progress. In a technical field like Operating Systems, this ensures that core concepts are mastered incrementally, identifying knowledge gaps early and fostering consistent engagement with complex material.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the purpose and structure of CIE within their academic framework. They will understand how various assessment tools—such as quizzes, assignments, projects, and presentations—are designed to evaluate their grasp of OS fundamentals like process management, memory allocation, and file systems continuously throughout the module.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
CIE directly connects to all technical concepts within the Operating Systems curriculum. For instance, performance on a quiz about scheduling algorithms reflects understanding of CPU management. A project on memory allocation tests the ability to apply theoretical concepts to practical problems, reinforcing the link between discrete topics.

**4. Real-world applications**
The CIE process mirrors modern agile project management and continuous integration/continuous deployment (CI/CD) practices in software development, where constant feedback is key to quality. It prepares students for a professional world where skills are continuously assessed and improved upon, not just tested in a final review.
