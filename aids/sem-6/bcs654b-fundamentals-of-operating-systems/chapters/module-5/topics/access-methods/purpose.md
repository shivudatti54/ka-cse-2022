# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Differentiate between qualitative and quantitative research methods and identify appropriate use cases for each
2. Compare attitudinal and behavioral research approaches and select the right method based on research goals
3. Plan and conduct common user research methods including interviews, surveys, usability testing, and contextual inquiry
4. Analyze research data using appropriate techniques such as affinity diagramming, thematic analysis, and statistical methods
5. Apply ethical principles in user research including informed consent, privacy protection, and minimization of harm
6. Integrate research findings into the design process to create user-centered solutions
