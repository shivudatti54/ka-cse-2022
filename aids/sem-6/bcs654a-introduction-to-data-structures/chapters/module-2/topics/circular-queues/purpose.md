# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1.  Define a circular queue and explain its need over a simple linear queue.
2.  Identify and explain the functions of the 'front' and 'rear' pointers.
3.  Trace the step-by-step execution of enqueue and dequeue operations, including pointer updates and wrap-around.
4.  Implement a circular queue in C using an array, including the isEmpty, isFull, enqueue, and dequeue functions.
5.  Derive and apply the conditions to check for empty and full states in the standard implementation.
6.  List and describe at least two practical applications of circular queues.
