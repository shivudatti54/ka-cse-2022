### **Learning Purpose: Statistical Language Model - N-gram Model**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
N-gram models are a foundational concept in statistical NLP. They represent the first practical approach to modeling language probabilistically, enabling machines to predict, generate, and understand text based on patterns learned from data. Understanding them is crucial as they are the historical and conceptual precursor to modern neural language models.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the mathematical formulation of n-grams (unigram, bigram, trigram), how to calculate sentence probabilities using the chain rule and Markov assumption, and techniques for model training (Maximum Likelihood Estimation) and smoothing (e.g., Laplace, Kneser-Ney) to handle unseen data.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic connects directly to **probability theory** (chain rule, conditional probability) and serves as the basis for more advanced models like Hidden Markov Models (HMMs) for tasks like Part-of-Speech tagging. It provides the essential groundwork for understanding the limitations that more sophisticated **neural language models** (e.g., RNNs, Transformers) were designed to overcome.

**4. Real-world applications**
*   **Autocomplete & Predictive Text:** Predicting the next most likely word in a search query or mobile keyboard.
*   **Spell Checking & Grammar Correction:** Identifying improbable word sequences as potential errors.
*   **Simple Text Generation:** Used in early chatbots and for generating basic synthetic text.
