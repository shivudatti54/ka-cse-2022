### Learning Purpose: Bigram Models

**1. Importance**
This topic is fundamental as bigrams form the simplest meaningful unit for understanding statistical relationships between words. They are the building blocks of more complex NLP models, providing a crucial first step in quantifying language patterns and predicting sequences.

**2. Student Learning**
Students will learn to calculate bigram probabilities within a corpus, understanding how these probabilities form a simple language model. They will be able to build a basic bigram model that can predict the next word in a sequence and generate new, simple text, grasping core concepts like maximum likelihood estimation (MLE) and smoothing techniques to handle unseen word pairs.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts**
Bigrams directly connect to and provide the foundation for more advanced *n*-gram models (like trigrams), and ultimately for sophisticated neural language models (e.g., RNNs, Transformers). This module bridges the gap between simple probability theory and modern NLP, illustrating the statistical principles that underpin machine understanding of language.

**4. Real-World Applications**
While simple, bigram models are used in applications like auto-complete features, basic spell checkers, and quick text generation prototypes. Understanding them is essential for recognizing the limitations that drive the development of more powerful models used in machine translation, chatbots, and voice assistants.
