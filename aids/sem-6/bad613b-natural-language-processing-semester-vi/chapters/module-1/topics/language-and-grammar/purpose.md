# Learning Objectives
After studying this topic, you should be able to:
1. Define and distinguish between key linguistic concepts: syntax, morphology, and grammar.
2. Explain the purpose and components of a Context-Free Grammar (CFG) and construct a simple parse tree for a given sentence.
3. Identify and provide examples of different types of linguistic ambiguity, such as syntactic and part-of-speech ambiguity.
4. Describe the process and importance of Part-of-Speech (POS) tagging in the NLP pipeline.
5. Recognize the significance of the Paninian framework in the history of formal grammar and its parallels to modern computational linguistics.
6. Articulate why a computational understanding of grammar is fundamental to solving core NLP tasks.
