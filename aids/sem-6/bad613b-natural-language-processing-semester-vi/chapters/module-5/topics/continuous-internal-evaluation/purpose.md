### **Learning Purpose: Continuous Internal Evaluation**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) is crucial as it shifts the focus from a single high-stakes exam to a more holistic assessment of a student's understanding and skills throughout the semester. In a complex field like NLP, this ensures steady learning, identifies gaps early, and reduces end-term pressure, fostering a deeper and more consistent engagement with the material.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to demonstrate their proficiency in NLP concepts (like sentiment analysis, text generation, or machine translation) through various formats. This includes practical coding assignments, project work, presentations, and periodic tests. They will develop not only technical skills but also soft skills like documentation, collaboration, and effective communication of their work.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
CIE inherently integrates all previous modules. A project on building a chatbot, for instance, requires knowledge of syntax (Module 1), semantics (Module 2), ML models (Module 3), and advanced techniques like transformers (Module 4). It forces students to synthesize these discrete concepts into a functional, real-world application, solidifying their end-to-end understanding.

**4. Real-world applications**
The CIE process mirrors the agile, iterative development cycles used in the tech industry. Completing smaller projects and prototypes prepares students for professional environments where continuous delivery, code reviews, and presenting results to stakeholders are standard practice, making them industry-ready.
