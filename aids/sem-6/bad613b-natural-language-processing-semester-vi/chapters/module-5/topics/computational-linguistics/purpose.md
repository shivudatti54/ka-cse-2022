### Learning Purpose: Computational Linguistics

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Computational Linguistics (CL) is the foundational engine behind modern Natural Language Processing. It is crucial because it provides the formal linguistic theories, models, and algorithms that allow machines to understand, interpret, and generate human language with structural accuracy. Without its principles, technologies like translators and voice assistants would not be possible.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core computational models used to process linguistic structure, including syntax (parsing), semantics (meaning representation), and morphology. They will explore rule-based and statistical approaches to tasks like part-of-speech tagging, context-free grammars, and semantic analysis, moving from theoretical concepts to their practical implementation.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds upon earlier knowledge of probability, machine learning, and formal language theory. It bridges the gap between pure linguistics (Module 1) and deep learning-based NLP (later modules), providing the essential grammatical and semantic understanding that neural models are built upon.

**4. Real-world applications**
The principles of CL are applied in machine translation systems, grammar checkers, search query understanding, voice-activated user interfaces (e.g., Siri, Alexa), and any application requiring precise syntactic and semantic analysis of text.
