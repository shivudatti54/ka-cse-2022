### Learning Purpose: Information Retrieval Models - Classical

**1. Importance**
This topic is foundational, as classical models form the backbone of modern search engines and information retrieval (IR) systems. Understanding these core algorithms is essential for grasping how machines process, rank, and retrieve relevant information from vast datasets, a critical function in our digital world.

**2. Learning Outcomes**
Students will learn the mechanics, advantages, and limitations of key classical models: the Boolean model (exact matching), the Vector Space Model (VSM - similarity-based ranking using term weights), and the probabilistic model (ranking by probability of relevance). They will understand how to represent queries and documents within each framework.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts**
This module builds directly on text preprocessing (tokenization, stemming) and computational linguistics. It provides the essential groundwork for subsequent study of more advanced techniques like machine learning-based ranking (e.g., Learning to Rank) and modern neural IR models.

**4. Real-World Applications**
These models are directly applied in library catalog systems, academic search engines (e.g., Google Scholar), and legacy enterprise search platforms. The principles of term weighting (tf-idf from VSM) remain ubiquitous in NLP tasks far beyond search, including text classification and summarization.
