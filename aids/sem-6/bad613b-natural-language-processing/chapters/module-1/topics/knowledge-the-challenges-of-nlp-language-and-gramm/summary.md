# Natural Language Processing (NLP) Revision Notes

### What is NLP?

- Definition: The interaction between computers and humans in natural language
- Origins: 1950s, developed from computer science, linguistics, and artificial intelligence

### Key Points

- **Knowledge Representation**: Knowledge representation is the process of representing knowledge in a machine-readable format
- **Challenges of NLP**:
  - Ambiguity and uncertainty in language
  - Limited training data and computational resources
  - Cultural and linguistic diversity
  - Contextual understanding and nuance

### Language and Grammar

- **Language Models**: Statistical models that predict the next word in a sequence
- **Grammar-Based Models**: Models that rely on grammatical rules to generate text
- **Syntax and Semantics**: Study of the structure and meaning of language
- **Morphology and Phonology**: Study of the internal structure of words and sound systems

### Processing Indian Languages

- **Script**: Devanagari, Bengali, Telugu, etc.
- **Script normalization**: Normalizing scripts to a standard format
- **Language models for Indian languages**: Training models on Indian languages data
- **Machine translation**: Translating text from one Indian language to another

### NLP Applications

- **Text Classification**: Classifying text into categories (e.g., spam vs. not spam)
- **Sentiment Analysis**: Determining the sentiment of text (e.g., positive, negative, neutral)
- **Named Entity Recognition**: Identifying named entities in text (e.g., people, places, organizations)
- **Question Answering**: Answering questions based on text
- **Chatbots and Virtual Assistants**: Conversational interfaces for customer service and more

### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Bayes' Theorem**: P(A|B) = P(A and B) / P(B)
- **Tokenization**: Breaking text into individual words or tokens
- **Stemming and Lemmatization**: Reducing words to their base form
- **Part-of-speech Tagging**: Identifying the part of speech (e.g., noun, verb, adjective) of each word

### Theorems

- **Chomsky's Generative Grammar**: A theoretical framework for understanding language structure
- **Noam Chomsky's Poverty of the Stimulus**: The idea that humans are born with an innate ability to learn language
