# **Language Modeling: Statistical Language Model**

### Overview

- **Statistical Language Modeling**: A technique used in Natural Language Processing (NLP) to model the probability distribution of language.
- **N-gram Model**: A statistical model that represents language as a sequence of n items (e.g., words, characters).

### N-gram Model

---

- **Unigram Model**: Each word is a separate probability distribution.
  - Formula: P(w) = 1/N
  - Definition: The probability of a single word occurring in the language.
- **Bigram Model**: The probability of two consecutive words.
  - Formula: P(w1, w2) = P(w1)P(w2|w1)
  - Definition: The probability of two consecutive words occurring in the language.

### Paninion Framework

---

- **Paninion**: A linguistic term referring to the combination of a word and its context.
- **Paninion Framework**: A statistical model that represents language as a sequence of paninions.

### Karaka Theory

---

- **Karaka**: A linguistic term referring to the grammatical function of a word (e.g., subject, object).
- **Karaka Theory**: A linguistic theory that represents language as a sequence of karakas.

### Important Formulas and Definitions

---

- **Probability Formula**: P(X) = P(X|A)P(A)
- **Conditional Probability Formula**: P(X|Y) = P(X, Y) / P(Y)
- **Bayes' Theorem**: P(X|Y) = P(Y|X)P(X) / P(Y)

### Important Theorems

---

- **Markov's Theorem**: A sequence of words is dependent on its previous word.
- **Independence Theorem**: Two words are independent if the probability of one word does not affect the probability of the other.
