# **Natural Language Processing Revision Notes: Textbook 1 Ch**

## **What is Natural Language Processing?**

- A branch of artificial intelligence (AI) that deals with the interaction between computers and humans in natural language.
- Encompasses several subfields, including language modeling, language understanding, sentiment analysis, machine translation, and text summarization.

## **Origins of NLP**

- Early beginnings in the 1950s and 1960s with the development of computational linguistics.
- Influence from linguistics, computer science, and cognitive psychology.
- Key milestones:
  - 1950s: First NLP programs were developed.
  - 1960s: First machine translation systems were created.
  - 1970s: First NLP textbooks were published.

## **Definitions and Theorems**

- **Definition of NLP:** The study of ways to make computers and machines understand, interpret, and generate natural language data.
- **Noam Chomsky's Generative Grammar:** A theoretical framework that describes the underlying structure of natural language.
- **Markov Chain Theorem:** A mathematical concept used to describe the probability of a sequence occurring in a language.

## **Important Formulas and Concepts**

- **Markov Chain Formula:** P(x) = P(x|y) \* P(y)
- **N-gram Calculation:** The probability of a sequence of n items occurring in a language.
- **Language Model:** A statistical model that predicts the probability of a sequence of words in a language.
