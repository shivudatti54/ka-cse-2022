### Learning Purpose: Module 5 - Paul Buhler

1.  **Why is this topic important?**
    Understanding the contributions of pioneers like Paul Buhler is crucial for appreciating the evolution of Big Data ecosystems. His work on architectural patterns, such as the Lambda Architecture, provides foundational frameworks for building robust, scalable, and fault-tolerant data processing systems. This knowledge is essential for designing modern analytics solutions.

2.  **What will students learn?**
    Students will learn the core principles of the Lambda Architecture and its components: the Batch Layer, Speed Layer, and Serving Layer. They will analyze its strengths in handling both historical and real-time data, as well as its limitations, which led to the development of newer paradigms like the Kappa Architecture.

3.  **How does it connect to other concepts?**
    This module directly builds upon previous knowledge of distributed computing (Hadoop/MapReduce) and real-time stream processing (Apache Storm, Spark Streaming). It serves as a critical bridge between batch-oriented processing and real-time analytics, showing how these concepts can be integrated into a unified architectural pattern.

4.  **Real-world applications**
    The principles derived from Buhler's work are applied in industries requiring low-latency insights combined with comprehensive historical analysis. Examples include financial fraud detection systems, real-time recommendation engines for e-commerce, and monitoring complex IoT sensor networks where both immediate alerts and long-term trend analysis are vital.
