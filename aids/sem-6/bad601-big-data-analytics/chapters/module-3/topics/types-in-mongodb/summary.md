# **Types in MongoDB**

### Key Points

- **Document**: A single data-value pair in a collection.
- **Collection**: A group of related documents. (Similar to a table in RDBMS)
- **Database**: A container for one or more collections. (Similar to a database in RDBMS)
- **Field**: A single piece of data within a document. (Similar to a column in RDBMS)
- **Type**: The data type of a field.
- ** ObjectId**: A unique identifier for each document.
- **Binary Data**: Variable-length strings, such as images and audio files.

### Data Types in MongoDB

---

- **String**: A sequence of characters.
- **Integer**: A whole number.
- **Decimal**: A floating-point number.
- **Boolean**: A true/false value.
- **Date**: A date and time value.
- **Array**: A collection of values.
- **Object**: An unordered collection of key-value pairs.

### Important Formulas and Definitions

---

- **Schema**: A definition of what data is stored in a collection.
- **NoSQL Schema**: MongoDB has a flexible schema that can change.
- **Embedded Document**: A document within a document.
- **Reference**: A field that references the \_id of another document.
- **Embedded Array**: An array of values within a document.

### Important Theorems

---

- **Dunning-Kruger Effect**: Be careful when you're not sure, it's always better to ask or verify the information.
- **Garbage In, Garbage Out (GIGO)**: The quality of the data is only as good as the source of the data.
