### **Learning Purpose: Module 2 - Introducing Hadoop**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Hadoop is the foundational open-source framework that enabled the modern era of big data. It solved the critical problem of storing and processing massive, diverse datasets across distributed clusters of commodity hardware, making large-scale analytics feasible and cost-effective for organizations worldwide. Understanding Hadoop is essential as it provides the core principles upon which many contemporary data ecosystems are built.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core components of the Hadoop ecosystem (HDFS, MapReduce, YARN) and their respective roles in distributed storage and processing. They will understand the fundamental concepts of distributed data processing and how Hadoop enables fault-tolerant, scalable computation. The module will also introduce key related projects like Hive and Pig for higher-level data interaction.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds on the big data challenges (Volume, Velocity, Variety) introduced in Module 1. It provides the architectural foundation for subsequent modules on processing frameworks (like Spark), data warehousing (Hive), and real-time systems (Kafka). Hadoop is the bedrock upon which the modern data lakehouse concept is often constructed.

**4. Real-world applications**
Hadoop is used across industries for log processing, recommendation systems (e.g., e-commerce, streaming services), fraud detection in finance, and large-scale ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) pipelines. It allows companies to analyze petabytes of customer, machine, and operational data to gain insights and drive decision-making.
