### Learning Purpose: Why not RDBMS?

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the limitations of Relational Database Management Systems (RDBMS) is crucial because the volume, velocity, and variety of Big Data often exceed the capabilities of traditional SQL-based systems. This topic establishes the fundamental "why" behind the evolution of modern Big Data technologies, preventing the misapplication of outdated tools and ensuring efficient, scalable data solutions.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify the specific technical constraints of RDBMS, such as difficulties with horizontal scaling (scaling out), schema rigidity, and poor performance with unstructured data. They will contrast the ACID compliance model of RDBMS with the BASE model often used in distributed systems and be introduced to the core principles that guide the selection of alternative data storage solutions (NoSQL, data lakes).

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This knowledge is a direct prerequisite for subsequent modules on Hadoop, NoSQL databases (e.g., MongoDB, Cassandra), and distributed computing frameworks. It provides the essential rationale for why these newer paradigms were developed, creating a foundation for understanding their architecture, trade-offs, and appropriate use cases.

**4. Real-world applications**
This is applied when choosing the right database for a project, such as selecting a scalable NoSQL database for a high-velocity social media feed instead of a traditional RDBMS, or using a data lake to store vast amounts of raw, unstructured sensor data from IoT devices before analysis.
