# Learning Purpose: Classification of Analytics

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the classification of analytics is fundamental as it provides the strategic framework for all data-driven decision-making. It helps organizations determine the right analytical approach to answer specific business questions, ensuring resources are allocated efficiently and insights are actionable.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define, distinguish, and apply the four primary types of analytics: Descriptive (what happened?), Diagnostic (why did it happen?), Predictive (what will happen?), and Prescriptive (what should we do?). They will understand the technical requirements, methodologies, and value proposition of each class.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the cornerstone of the module and the entire course. It directly connects to subsequent concepts like data mining (for predictive analytics), visualization tools (for descriptive analytics), and optimization techniques (for prescriptive analytics). It provides the vocabulary and structure for discussing more complex analytical models.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied across industries. For instance, a retail chain uses descriptive analytics to report on sales, diagnostic to understand a sales drop, predictive to forecast future demand, and prescriptive to optimize inventory levels, demonstrating the full spectrum in a single business context.
