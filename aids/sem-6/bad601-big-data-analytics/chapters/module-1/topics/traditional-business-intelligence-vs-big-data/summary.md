# Traditional Business Intelligence Vs Big Data

### Overview

- Traditional Business Intelligence (BI) refers to the collection, analysis, and presentation of data to support business decisions.
- Big Data refers to the large and complex sets of data that are difficult to process using traditional methods.

### Key Differences

- **Data Volume**:
  - Traditional BI: Relies on structured data and small to medium-sized datasets.
  - Big Data: Handles massive, unstructured, and complex datasets.
- **Data Variety**:
  - Traditional BI: Typically deals with structured data from various sources.
  - Big Data: Involves diverse, unstructured, and semi-structured data from various sources.
- **Data Velocity**:
  - Traditional BI: Relies on batch processing and near-real-time data.
  - Big Data: Requires real-time processing and high-speed data ingestion.

### Characteristics of Big Data

- **3 Vs**:
  - Volume: Large datasets
  - Variety: Unstructured and semi-structured data
  - Velocity: High-speed data ingestion
- **4 Cs**:
  - Cost: High processing costs due to complex data processing
  - Complexity: Complex data processing and analysis
  - Concurrency: Real-time processing and high-speed computation
  - Content: Unstructured and semi-structured data

### Theorems and Formulas

- **NoSQL Database Theorem**: A NoSQL database is a non-relational database that does not follow the traditional rules of relational databases.
- **Big Data Analytics Formula**: Big Data Analytics = (Volume x Variety x Velocity) / (Cost x Complexity x Concurrency x Content)

### Key Applications

- **Traditional BI**: Financial analysis, customer behavior analysis, and sales forecasting.
- **Big Data**: Predictive analytics, social media analysis, and personalized marketing.

### Quick Revision Tips

- Focus on the 3 Vs (Volume, Variety, Velocity) and 4 Cs (Cost, Complexity, Concurrency, Content) of Big Data.
- Understand the differences between Traditional BI and Big Data.
- Practice applying Big Data Analytics formulas and theorems.
