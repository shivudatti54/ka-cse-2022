# **Traditional Business Intelligence vs Big Data**

## **Introduction**

In today's fast-paced business environment, organizations rely on data to make informed decisions. Business Intelligence (BI) and Big Data are two related but distinct concepts that have evolved to meet the increasing demands of data-driven decision-making. This study material will explore the differences between Traditional Business Intelligence and Big Data, highlighting their characteristics, evolution, and definitions.

## **What is Business Intelligence?**

Business Intelligence is a set of processes, technologies, and tools used to analyze and interpret data to support business decision-making. It involves the collection, storage, and analysis of data from various sources to identify trends, patterns, and correlations.

- Characteristics of Business Intelligence:
  - Focused on historical data
  - Uses data warehouses and reporting tools
  - Relies on structured data
  - Typically uses query languages like SQL
- Examples of Business Intelligence:
  - Sales reports
  - Customer segmentation
  - Market research analysis

## **What is Big Data?**

Big Data refers to the vast amounts of structured, semi-structured, and unstructured data that organizations generate and collect from various sources. This data is characterized by its large size, complexity, and variety.

- Characteristics of Big Data:
  - Large volume
  - High velocity
  - High variety
  - Complex
- Examples of Big Data:
  - Social media posts
  - Sensor readings
  - Customer interactions
  - IoT data

## **Traditional Business Intelligence vs Big Data**

While Business Intelligence focuses on historical data to support business decision-making, Big Data encompasses a broader range of data from various sources, including social media, IoT devices, and customer interactions.

- **Key differences:**
  - **Data source:** Traditional BI relies on structured data from databases, while Big Data comes from a wide range of unstructured and semi-structured sources.
  - **Data volume:** Big Data is characterized by its large volume, whereas traditional BI typically deals with smaller datasets.
  - **Data analysis:** Traditional BI relies on query languages like SQL, whereas Big Data requires advanced analytics techniques, such as machine learning and deep learning.

## **Evolution of Business Intelligence and Big Data**

The evolution of Business Intelligence and Big Data reflects the changing nature of data and its importance in business decision-making.

- **Traditional BI:**
  - Emerged in the 1990s with the use of relational databases and query languages
  - Focused on historical data analysis
- **Big Data:**
  - Emerged in the 2000s with the increasing availability of social media data and IoT devices
  - Requires advanced analytics techniques to analyze the vast amounts of data

## **Conclusion**

Traditional Business Intelligence and Big Data are two distinct concepts that have evolved to meet the increasing demands of data-driven decision-making. While traditional BI focuses on historical data to support business decision-making, Big Data encompasses a broader range of data from various sources, including social media, IoT devices, and customer interactions. Understanding the differences between these two concepts is crucial for organizations to make informed decisions about their data strategy.
