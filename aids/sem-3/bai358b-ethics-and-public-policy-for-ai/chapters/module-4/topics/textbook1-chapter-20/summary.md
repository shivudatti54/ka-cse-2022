### Ethics and Public Policy for AI

---

- Definition: Ethics and public policy for AI refers to the development and implementation of guidelines, regulations, and standards to ensure that AI systems are designed, developed, and used in a responsible and ethical manner.

### Key Concepts

---

- **Bias and Fairness**: AI systems can perpetuate existing biases and inequalities if not designed and trained with fairness in mind.
- **Explainability and Transparency**: AI systems should be designed to provide clear explanations for their decisions and actions.
- **Accountability**: Individuals and organizations responsible for AI systems should be held accountable for any harm caused by these systems.

### Important Formulas and Theorems

---

- **Definition of Bias**: [Bias = (Actual Outcome - Predicted Outcome) / Predicted Outcome]
- **Theorem of Fairness**: [Fairness = (Predicted Outcome - Actual Outcome)^2 / Predicted Outcome^2]
- **Explainability Theorem**: [Explainability = (Predicted Outcome - Actual Outcome)^2 / Predicted Outcome^2]

### Key Theorems and Results

---

- **Theorem of Ethical AI**: [Ethical AI = (Fairness + Explainability + Accountability) / (Predicted Outcome + Actual Outcome)]
- **Result of AI Governance**: [AI Governance = (Fairness + Explainability + Accountability) / (Predicted Outcome + Actual Outcome) > 1]
