# Chapter 22: Ethics and Public Policy for AI

### Module: Innovating with Confidence: Embedding AI Governance and fairness in financial Services Risk

# Key Points

- The importance of ethics in AI development and deployment, particularly in the financial services sector
- Regulatory approaches to AI, including EU's AI White Paper and US's AI Bill of Rights
- Key concepts:
  - **Value Alignment**: ensuring AI systems align with human values and ethics
  - **Fairness, Accountability, and Transparency (FAT)**: principles for AI development and deployment
  - **Explainability**: understanding AI decision-making processes
- Theorems and Formulas:
  - **No Free Lunch Theorem**: no algorithm can perform better than the best human decision-maker for all tasks
  - **Shapley Value**: a method for attributing the value of individual contributions to a team or system

# Definitions

- **Bias**: unfairness or discrimination in AI decision-making
- **Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI)**: principles for ensuring AI systems are fair and inclusive
- **Regulatory Sandboxes**: temporary, controlled environments for testing and deploying AI systems

# Important Concepts

- **AI Governance**: frameworks for managing the development, deployment, and use of AI systems
- **Fairness Metrics**: statistical methods for evaluating AI systems' fairness and bias
- **Human Oversight**: the role of humans in reviewing and correcting AI decisions

# Quick Revision Notes

- Ethics is crucial in AI development and deployment, particularly in financial services
- Regulatory approaches to AI vary globally, with a focus on value alignment, fairness, and transparency
- Key concepts include explainability, bias, and diversity, equity, and inclusion
