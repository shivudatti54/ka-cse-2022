# Methods and Research to Translate Principles into Practices

### Chapter 9: Ethics and Public Policy for AI

#### Key Concepts

- **Translation gap**: The difference between research findings and effective implementation.
- **Principles-traditions**: The translation of abstract principles into concrete practices.
- **Theory-practice gap**: The gap between theoretical knowledge and practical application.

#### Methods for Translation

- **Reflexive practice**: A methodology that involves self-reflection and critical examination of one's own practice.
- **Participatory research**: Involves active participation of stakeholders in the research process.
- **Co-design**: A collaborative approach to designing AI systems for social good.

#### Research Approaches

- **Evaluation studies**: Focus on assessing the impact and effectiveness of AI systems.
- **Case studies**: In-depth analysis of specific AI system implementations.
- **Survey research**: Large-scale studies to gather data on public attitudes and opinions.

#### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **DeLone and McLean's Model**: A framework for evaluating information systems, including AI systems.
- ** diffusion of innovations theory**: Explains how new technologies, like AI, are adopted and implemented.

#### Key Definitions

- **Ethics**: The principles and values that guide human behavior in society.
- **Stakeholder engagement**: The process of involving various groups and individuals in the decision-making process.

#### Key Theorems and Results

- **The Trolley Problem**: A thought experiment that raises questions about AI decision-making and moral responsibility.
- **The Stanford Prison Experiment**: A study that highlights the potential risks and consequences of AI system design.
