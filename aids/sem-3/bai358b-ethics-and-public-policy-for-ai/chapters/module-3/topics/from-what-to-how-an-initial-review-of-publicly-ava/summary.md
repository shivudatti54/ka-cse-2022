## From What to How: An Initial Review of Publicly Available AI Ethics Tools

### Module: How to design AI for social good: seven essential factors

#### Key Concepts

- **AI Ethics Tools**: Software used to evaluate, improve, and implement ethical AI practices
- **Bias**: Unequal impact of AI systems on different groups, often due to data biases
- **Fairness**: Ensuring AI systems treat individuals equally and without prejudice
- **Transparency**: Providing clear explanations of AI decision-making processes
- **Accountability**: Assigning responsibility for AI system decisions and outcomes

#### Essential Factors

- **Diversity and Inclusion**: Ensuring diverse teams develop and test AI systems
- **Data Quality and Curation**: Using high-quality, representative data for AI training
- **Human Oversight and Review**: Regularly reviewing AI system decisions and outcomes
- **Explainability and Interpretability**: Developing AI systems that provide transparent explanations
- **Value Alignment**: Designing AI systems that align with human values and ethics
- **Resilience and Robustness**: Developing AI systems that can adapt to changing environments
- **Human-centered Design**: Prioritizing human needs and values in AI system design

#### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Entropy**: A measure of data uncertainty and randomness (H = - ∑ p(x) \* log₂ p(x))
- **Information Gain**: A measure of the reduction in uncertainty due to a prediction or classification (IG = H - H)

#### Publicly Available AI Ethics Tools

- **AI Now Institute's AI Ethics Toolkit**: A comprehensive framework for evaluating AI ethics
- **Microsoft's AI Ethics Principles**: A set of guidelines for responsible AI development and deployment
- **Google's AI Principles**: A set of principles for developing AI systems that prioritize fairness, transparency, and accountability

#### Revision Tips

- Focus on key concepts and essential factors
- Understand the importance of bias, fairness, transparency, and accountability
- Familiarize yourself with important formulas and theorems
- Review publicly available AI ethics tools and frameworks
