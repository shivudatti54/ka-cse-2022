# Chapter 10: Fairness, Accountability, and Transparency (FAT) in AI

### Key Concepts

- **Fairness, Accountability, and Transparency (FAT)**: essential factors in designing AI for social good
- **Bias in AI**: potential for AI systems to perpetuate existing social biases
- **Algorithmic fairness**: designing AI algorithms that minimize bias and promote fairness
- **Explainability**: ability to understand and interpret AI decision-making processes
- **Transparency**: providing clear and accessible information about AI decision-making processes

### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Bias**: a difference in the probability of a positive outcome for different groups
- **Diversity**: the presence of different groups in a population
- **Fairness metric**: a statistical measure of fairness in AI decision-making
- **Explainability metric**: a measure of the ability of an AI system to explain its decision-making process

### Theories and Frameworks

- **Equity Framework**: a framework for designing AI systems that promote equity and fairness
- **Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI) framework**: a framework for promoting diversity, equity, and inclusion in AI development

### Key Takeaways

- **FAT is essential for building trust in AI**: by prioritizing fairness, accountability, and transparency, we can build more trustworthy AI systems
- **Bias in AI can have serious consequences**: AI systems that perpetuate bias can exacerbate existing social inequalities
- **Explainability and transparency are critical for building trust in AI**: by providing clear and accessible information about AI decision-making processes, we can build more trustworthy AI systems.
