### Learning Purpose: Semester-End Examination

**1. Why is this topic important?**
The examination is a critical summative assessment designed to evaluate comprehensive understanding and practical proficiency. It validates a student's ability to synthesize and apply the entire semester's concepts under constraints, mirroring real-world project deadlines and technical challenges.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will demonstrate their mastery of key Python data science libraries (NumPy, Pandas, Matplotlib/Seaborn, Scikit-learn) by solving integrated problems. They will learn to structure their code, justify their methodological choices, and communicate results effectively, solidifying their technical and analytical skills.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This exam integrates all prior modules: data manipulation (Module 2), visualization (Module 3), statistical analysis (Module 4), and machine learning (Module 5). It requires students to connect these discrete skills into a cohesive data science workflow, from data cleaning to building and evaluating a predictive model.

**4. Real-world applications**
The skills tested are directly applicable to roles like Data Analyst or Data Scientist. The exam simulates a core industry task: receiving an ambiguous problem, leveraging the appropriate tools from a broad toolkit, and delivering a data-driven solution and insight.
