**Important Formulas and Definitions**

- **Confidence Interval**: A range of values within which a population parameter is likely to lie.
- **P-value**: The probability of observing a result at least as extreme as the one observed, assuming the null hypothesis is true.
- **Hypothesis Testing**: A procedure for making inferences about a population based on a sample of data.

**Theorems**

- **Central Limit Theorem (CLT)**: States that the sample mean of a large number of independent and identically distributed random variables will be approximately normally distributed.
- **Chebyshev's Inequality**: Provides a lower bound on the probability of observing a result within a certain range of the mean.
