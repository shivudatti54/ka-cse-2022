### Learning Purpose: Continuous Internal Evaluation

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) is a crucial pedagogical tool that shifts the focus from high-stakes, end-of-term exams to an ongoing assessment of a student's understanding and practical skills. In a hands-on field like Data Analytics with R, it ensures learners consistently engage with the material, allowing for timely feedback and targeted improvement. This process is vital for building a strong, practical foundation in R programming and analytical thinking.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to apply R programming concepts from previous modules to solve practical data analysis problems under CIE conditions. This includes data wrangling, visualization, statistical testing, and model building. They will develop the ability to think critically under time constraints and present their findings clearly, mirroring real-world analytical tasks.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module synthesizes and applies all prior technical skills (e.g., data manipulation with `dplyr`, visualization with `ggplot2`, statistical modeling) into a cohesive framework. It tests the student's integrated understanding of the entire data analytics workflow, connecting isolated techniques into an end-to-end project.

**4. Real-world applications**
The CIE simulates real-world scenarios where data analysts must efficiently derive insights from data to inform business decisions. The skills practiced are directly applicable to roles requiring regular reporting, such as creating performance dashboards, A/B test analysis, and building predictive models to solve specific business problems.
