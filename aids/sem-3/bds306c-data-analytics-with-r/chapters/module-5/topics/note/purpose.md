### Learning Purpose: Introduction to Data Visualization with ggplot2

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Effective data visualization is a cornerstone of data analytics, transforming complex results into clear, actionable insights. Mastering visualization is essential for communicating findings to both technical and non-technical stakeholders, making it a critical skill for any data professional.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the foundational principles of the `ggplot2` package, built on the Grammar of Graphics. They will construct a variety of plots (e.g., scatter plots, histograms, box plots) by mapping data to aesthetic attributes (`aes`) and adding geometric layers (`geom_*`). Key skills include customizing plots with themes, labels, and colors to enhance clarity and presentation.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon data wrangling skills from previous modules (e.g., using `dplyr`), as visualized data must first be cleaned and transformed. It serves as the essential final step in the analytics workflow, following data import, cleaning, and statistical modeling, allowing students to present their analyses effectively.

**4. Real-world applications**
These skills are applied to create dashboards, exploratory data analysis (EDA) reports, and presentation-ready graphics for business intelligence, academic research, and storytelling with data in fields like marketing, finance, and public policy.
