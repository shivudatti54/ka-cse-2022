### **Learning Purpose: Scatter Plots**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Scatter plots are a foundational tool in data analytics for visualizing the relationship between two continuous variables. They are the first and most intuitive step in identifying trends, patterns, correlations, and potential outliers within a dataset, forming the basis for more advanced statistical modeling and machine learning.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to create, customize, and interpret scatter plots using R (primarily with `ggplot2`). This includes mapping variables to aesthetics (x, y, color, size), adding trend lines (e.g., with `geom_smooth()`), and extracting meaningful insights about the strength and direction of a relationship.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly connects to correlation analysis and regression modeling (Modules 5 & 6), where scatter plots are used to visually assess assumptions and model fit. It builds upon skills in data wrangling (Module 2) to prepare data and serves as a core application of R's visualization grammar.

**4. Real-world applications**
Scatter plots are used everywhere: to analyze marketing spend vs. revenue, compare house size to price, study the relationship between patient age and blood pressure, or visualize any two measurable metrics to inform data-driven decisions.
