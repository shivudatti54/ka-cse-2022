### Learning Purpose: Line Plots in R

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Line plots are a fundamental and highly effective tool for visualizing trends over a continuous variable, most commonly time. Mastering them is crucial because they transform raw, complex data into an intuitive and easily interpretable story, revealing patterns like growth, seasonality, and anomalies that are difficult to discern from tables of numbers.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to create, customize, and interpret line plots using R, primarily with the `ggplot2` package. This includes plotting single and multiple lines on the same graph, modifying aesthetics (e.g., colors, line types, points), adding informative labels and titles, and structuring data appropriately for effective plotting.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly on previous modules: data import and cleaning ensure the data is plot-ready, while foundational `ggplot2` syntax provides the framework. Line plots are a specific application of the Grammar of Graphics, reinforcing core concepts like aesthetics and geoms. They also serve as a gateway to more advanced time series visualization and analysis.

**4. Real-world applications**
Line plots are ubiquitous in fields like finance (stock price trends), public health (tracking disease outbreaks), marketing (website traffic over time), and environmental science (temperature change analysis). The ability to create a clear, publication-quality line plot is an essential skill for any data analyst.
