# Learning Purpose: What the near future of AI could be

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the probable trajectory of AI development is crucial for creating effective, forward-looking policy. Anticipating near-future capabilities and societal impacts allows policymakers, ethicists, and technologists to proactively design governance frameworks that mitigate risks and harness benefits, rather than reacting to crises.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to analyze current AI trends to forecast plausible developments over the next 5-10 years in areas like generative AI, autonomous systems, and labor market disruption. They will evaluate the associated ethical dilemmas, policy challenges, and opportunities these advancements present, moving beyond theoretical debate to practical foresight.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic synthesizes core concepts from the entire module: ethical principles (Module 1), governance mechanisms (Module 2), and economic/social impact (Module 3). It requires applying this knowledge to construct coherent scenarios, directly linking foundational theory to practical, anticipatory strategy.

**4. Real-world applications**
This foresight is directly applicable to roles in government policy drafting, corporate AI strategy, risk assessment, and regulatory compliance. It equips students to contribute to national AI strategies, organizational ethical guidelines, and public discourse on shaping a desirable AI future.
