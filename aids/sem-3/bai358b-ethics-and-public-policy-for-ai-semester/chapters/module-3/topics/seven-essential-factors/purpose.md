### **Learning Purpose: Seven Essential Factors for Ethical AI Policy**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
The rapid integration of AI into society creates profound ethical challenges, from bias in algorithms to accountability for autonomous decisions. Without a structured framework, policymaking is reactive and inconsistent. These seven factors provide a critical, foundational checklist to proactively design, evaluate, and implement responsible and effective AI governance, ensuring technology serves the public good.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify, define, and analyze the seven essential factors (e.g., fairness, accountability, transparency, safety, privacy, human agency, and benefit) that underpin ethical AI systems. They will evaluate real and hypothetical AI policies against these criteria and learn to articulate trade-offs and synergies between them in complex scenarios.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic synthesizes core concepts from the course. It connects abstract ethical theories (Module 1: Utilitarianism, deontology) and governance models (Module 2: regulation, standards) into a concrete, actionable framework. It serves as the practical bridge between philosophical principles and their application in public policy.

**4. Real-world applications**
This framework is directly applied by policymakers drafting AI legislation (e.g., EU AI Act), auditors assessing high-risk AI systems, corporate ethics boards developing internal guidelines, and journalists/investigators scrutinizing AI deployments for public accountability.
