# Learning Purpose: Ethics and Public Policy for AI - Module 3: From What to How

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Moving from abstract ethical principles ("the what") to concrete implementation ("the how") is the critical bridge that determines whether AI is developed and deployed responsibly. Understanding this process is essential to ensure ethical guidelines translate into tangible, enforceable actions and outcomes, preventing them from remaining merely aspirational.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the mechanisms and tools for implementing AI ethics. This includes studying policy instruments (e.g., regulatory frameworks, standards, auditing processes), organizational strategies (e.g., governance structures, ethics boards, impact assessments), and technical methods (e.g., fairness toolkits, transparency protocols) used to operationalize values like fairness, accountability, and transparency.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds upon the foundational ethical principles covered in earlier modules (e.g., justice, autonomy, beneficence). It provides the practical execution layer that follows theoretical discussion, and it sets the stage for subsequent modules on law, compliance, and future policy challenges, creating a clear arc from theory to practice.

**4. Real-world applications**
Students will be equipped to design compliance strategies, develop internal governance models for tech companies, contribute to public policy drafting, and critically evaluate the effectiveness of real-world AI systems against ethical benchmarks, preparing them for roles in policy-making, corporate governance, and AI auditing.
