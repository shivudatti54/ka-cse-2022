# Learning Purpose: How to Design AI for Social Good

## 1. Why is this topic important?
This topic is crucial because AI systems are increasingly deployed in high-stakes societal domains like healthcare, criminal justice, and social services. Without intentional design, they can perpetuate existing biases, create new harms, and exacerbate inequality. Understanding how to design for social good ensures that AI development is guided by ethical principles, maximizing its potential to solve pressing human challenges and benefit all of society, not just a select few.

## 2. What will students learn?
Students will learn a practical framework for designing and evaluating AI systems through an ethical lens. This includes methodologies for identifying and mitigating biases, techniques for meaningful stakeholder engagement and participatory design, and strategies for aligning AI objectives with public welfare and fundamental human rights.

## 3. How does it connect to other concepts?
This module synthesizes core concepts from the course, directly applying prior learning on ethical frameworks (e.g., utilitarianism, deontology), algorithmic fairness, and policy mechanisms for accountability (e.g., auditing, regulation). It provides the practical culmination of how to operationalize ethical theory into responsible technical and policy design.

## 4. Real-world applications
The principles learned are directly applicable to developing systems for equitable healthcare diagnostics, fairer loan approval algorithms, unbiased hiring tools, and environmental monitoring for climate action. Students will be equipped to assess real-world case studies and contribute to projects that prioritize societal benefit.
