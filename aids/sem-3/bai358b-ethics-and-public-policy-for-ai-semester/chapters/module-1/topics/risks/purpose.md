### Learning Purpose: Module 1 - Risks

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the risks associated with AI is foundational to developing ethical and effective public policy. As AI systems become increasingly integrated into critical sectors like healthcare, finance, and justice, their potential for causing unintended harm—from perpetuating bias to threatening privacy and security—grows exponentially. This topic is crucial for preemptively identifying and mitigating these dangers to ensure AI development benefits society as a whole.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify and categorize a spectrum of AI risks, including technical (e.g., safety failures), ethical (e.g., algorithmic bias, opacity), and societal (e.g., job displacement, malicious use). They will analyze case studies to understand how these risks manifest and evaluate frameworks for assessing their likelihood and impact.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module provides the essential problem set that the entire course will address. It directly sets the stage for subsequent modules on ethical principles (which provide the values for risk mitigation), governance models (the tools for management), and policy design (the implementation of safeguards). You cannot build responsible policy without first understanding what it is you are trying to prevent.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is directly applied in roles such as AI policy analyst, risk compliance officer, and technology auditor. It is essential for conducting algorithmic impact assessments, drafting safety regulations for autonomous systems, designing corporate AI ethics guidelines, and evaluating the societal implications of deploying new AI technologies.
