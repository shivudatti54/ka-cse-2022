### Learning Purpose: Seven Essential Factors for AI Ethics & Policy

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the seven essential factors provides a structured framework for analyzing and developing ethical AI systems. It moves from abstract principles to actionable components, ensuring that AI is designed and deployed responsibly to mitigate risks like bias, privacy erosion, and lack of accountability.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify, define, and critically evaluate the seven key factors (e.g., fairness, transparency, accountability, privacy, safety, human oversight, and societal impact) that constitute a robust AI ethics framework. They will analyze how these factors interact and sometimes conflict within policy and design choices.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly on the foundational ethical principles (e.g., beneficence, justice) introduced in Module 1. It provides the critical link between those high-level principles and the practical tools, governance models, and technical mitigation strategies covered in subsequent modules.

**4. Real-world applications**
This framework is applied by policymakers to craft regulations, by auditors to conduct impact assessments, and by developers to create checklists for ethical AI design. It is used to evaluate real-world cases, such as algorithmic hiring tools, predictive policing systems, and clinical diagnostic AI, to ensure they operate ethically and equitably.
