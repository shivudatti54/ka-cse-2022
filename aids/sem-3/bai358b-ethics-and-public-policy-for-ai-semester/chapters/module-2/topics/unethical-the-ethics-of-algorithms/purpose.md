# Learning Purpose: The Ethics of Algorithms

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Algorithms increasingly shape our lives, from social media feeds and credit scores to judicial sentencing and hiring. Understanding their ethical dimensions is crucial because they can perpetuate and amplify societal biases, create opaque decision-making systems, and impact human rights, demanding responsible development and governance.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify and analyze key ethical challenges posed by algorithms, including bias, fairness, transparency (e.g., the "black box" problem), accountability, and privacy. They will be equipped with frameworks and concepts to critically assess algorithmic systems and propose ethically-informed policy responses.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly on foundational ethics from Module 1, applying philosophical principles like justice and utilitarianism to a technical context. It provides the essential groundwork for subsequent modules on specific AI policy domains (e.g., surveillance, law enforcement) and practical governance tools like regulation and auditing.

**4. Real-world applications**
Students will apply these concepts to analyze real cases, such as biased recidivism prediction tools in criminal justice, discriminatory hiring algorithms, or the ethical implications of content recommendation systems on social platforms, preparing them to evaluate and shape responsible AI policy.
