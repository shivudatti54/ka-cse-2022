### Learning Purpose: Five Risks of AI

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the risks of AI is critical for developing ethical and safe public policy. As AI systems become deeply integrated into societal infrastructure—from healthcare to criminal justice—identifying potential harms like bias, privacy erosion, and accountability gaps is essential to mitigate negative consequences and build public trust.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify, define, and analyze five core risks associated with AI, such as algorithmic bias, lack of transparency (the "black box" problem), privacy violations, safety and security vulnerabilities, and challenges in accountability and liability. They will evaluate case studies to understand the real-world impact of these risks.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds on foundational concepts from Module 1, such as core ethical principles (e.g., justice, autonomy). It provides the crucial groundwork for Module 3, where students will learn to design and critique governance frameworks and policy interventions aimed at mitigating these exact risks.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied in auditing AI systems for bias, crafting legislation like the EU AI Act, developing corporate ethical AI guidelines, and designing public sector algorithms to ensure they are fair, transparent, and accountable.
