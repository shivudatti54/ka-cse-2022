# Learning Purpose: Designing AI for Social Good

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is critical because AI's transformative power carries immense potential for both benefit and harm. Moving beyond theoretical ethics, it focuses on the practical application of principles to ensure AI actively contributes to solving societal challenges, promoting equity, and serving the public interest, rather than exacerbating existing inequalities.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn a structured framework for designing and evaluating AI systems intended for social good. This includes applying ethical principles (e.g., fairness, accountability, transparency) to technical design, practicing stakeholder engagement, and developing methodologies for impact assessment to ensure positive societal outcomes.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module builds directly on the foundational ethical principles and governance models introduced earlier. It operationalizes those concepts, connecting them to technical implementation (e.g., bias mitigation techniques) and public policy mechanisms (e.g., procurement rules for ethical AI), providing a crucial link between theory and practice.

**4. Real-world applications**
Students will examine case studies across sectors such as humanitarian aid (e.g., predictive analytics for disaster response), healthcare (e.g., diagnostic AI for underserved communities), and environmental sustainability (e.g., AI for climate change modeling and resource optimization), learning from both successful implementations and notable failures.
