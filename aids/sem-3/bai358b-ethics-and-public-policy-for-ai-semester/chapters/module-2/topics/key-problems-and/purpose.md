# Learning Purpose: Key Problems in AI Ethics and Public Policy

**1. Importance**
This topic is critical because AI systems are rapidly being integrated into high-stakes public and private sectors. Understanding the inherent ethical problems is the first step toward creating effective policies that prevent harm, protect civil liberties, and ensure AI benefits society equitably. Ignoring these issues risks reinforcing societal biases, eroding privacy, and creating unaccountable systems.

**2. Learning Objectives**
Students will learn to identify and analyze core ethical challenges, including algorithmic bias and fairness, transparency (the "black box" problem), accountability for autonomous decisions, privacy concerns in data collection, and the potential for job displacement. They will evaluate these problems through various ethical frameworks.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts**
This module directly builds on foundational concepts from Module 1 (e.g., ethical frameworks like utilitarianism and deontology). It provides the essential "problems" that the "solutions" covered later in the course—such as governance models, regulatory strategies, and technical mitigation techniques (Module 3+)—are designed to address.

**4. Real-World Applications**
This knowledge is applied in auditing AI systems for bias (e.g., in hiring or lending algorithms), designing transparent public services, drafting legislation (like the EU AI Act), and developing corporate AI ethics guidelines to ensure responsible deployment.
