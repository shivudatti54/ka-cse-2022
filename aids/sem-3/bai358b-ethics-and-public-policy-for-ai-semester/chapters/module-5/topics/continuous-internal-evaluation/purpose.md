### Learning Purpose: Continuous Internal Evaluation

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) is crucial because AI systems evolve and adapt after deployment, often in unpredictable ways. Without ongoing, embedded assessment, they can develop harmful biases, security vulnerabilities, or performance failures, leading to significant real-world harm and eroding public trust.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to design and implement ethical review processes that run concurrently with an AI system's operation. This includes mastering techniques for real-time monitoring, bias detection, impact assessment, and creating feedback loops for iterative improvement, ensuring AI remains aligned with ethical principles throughout its lifecycle.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
CIE directly builds on prior module concepts: it operationalizes **Ethical Risk Assessment** (Module 2) into a live process, relies on **Governance Frameworks** (Module 4) for structure, and uses **Audit Trails** (Module 3) for accountability. It is the practical application of proactive AI governance.

**4. Real-world applications**
This is applied in content recommendation algorithms that continuously monitor for radicalization, credit scoring systems that check for emerging demographic bias, and autonomous vehicles whose decision-making is logged and evaluated after near-miss events to improve safety protocols.
