### Learning Purpose: Module 5 - Ethics and Public Policy for AI

**1. Why is this topic important?**
As AI systems become deeply embedded in society, governing their development and deployment is critical. This topic is vital to ensure that technological advancement is aligned with human values, rights, and societal well-being, preventing harm and building public trust.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to analyze the ethical trade-offs in AI application (e.g., privacy vs. utility, fairness vs. accuracy) and evaluate different policy and regulatory approaches. They will develop the ability to formulate reasoned arguments for and against specific governance frameworks, such as the EU's AI Act or sector-specific guidelines.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module synthesizes concepts from previous modules on algorithmic bias (Module 2), privacy (Module 3), and accountability (Module 4). It requires students to apply those foundational ethical principles to the practical challenge of creating and implementing effective public policy and legal structures.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is directly applicable to roles in government policy-making, AI compliance and auditing, ethical consulting within tech firms, and legal advocacy. Students will be equipped to contribute to the creation of responsible AI strategies, conduct impact assessments, and engage in public discourse on technology regulation.
