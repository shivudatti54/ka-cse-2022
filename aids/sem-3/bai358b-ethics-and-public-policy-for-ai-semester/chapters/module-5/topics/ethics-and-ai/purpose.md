### Learning Purpose: Ethics and AI

**1. Why is this topic important?**
As AI systems become deeply integrated into society, they raise profound ethical questions concerning bias, accountability, transparency, and privacy. This topic is crucial for ensuring that technological advancement aligns with human values and public good, preventing harm and building trust in automated systems.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify and analyze key ethical challenges in AI, such as algorithmic bias and the "black box" problem. They will explore frameworks for ethical reasoning and be introduced to principles of fairness, accountability, and transparency (FAccT) to critically assess AI applications and policy proposals.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds on prior knowledge of technical AI fundamentals and public policy mechanisms. It connects the technical design of systems (Module 2) to their societal impact and the governance structures (Module 4) needed to regulate them, creating a holistic view of responsible innovation.

**4. Real-world applications**
Students will apply these concepts to evaluate real-world cases, including the use of AI in criminal justice risk assessment, hiring algorithms, autonomous vehicles, and content moderation on social media platforms, preparing them to develop and critique ethical AI policy.
