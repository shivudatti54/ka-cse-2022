### Learning Purpose: Test Component in AI Ethics & Public Policy

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Testing AI systems is a critical safeguard against deploying biased, unsafe, or unreliable technology. A robust test component within a policy framework ensures accountability, provides evidence of performance, and builds public trust by verifying that AI systems meet ethical and technical standards before real-world implementation.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define key testing methodologies (e.g., red teaming, bias audits, simulation environments), identify appropriate metrics for evaluating AI against ethical principles (e.g., fairness, transparency), and critically assess the limitations and challenges of creating comprehensive tests for complex AI systems.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the practical application of previously learned concepts. It directly connects to **AI governance** (creating enforceable standards), **risk assessment** (identifying what to test for), and **algorithmic bias** (developing audits to detect it). It operationalizes abstract ethical principles into concrete, measurable outcomes.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied in regulatory compliance (e.g., testing for adherence to the EU AI Act), development of industry standards (e.g., NIST AI Risk Management Framework), and within organizations to validate internal AI ethics guidelines before product launch, ensuring safer and more responsible deployment.
