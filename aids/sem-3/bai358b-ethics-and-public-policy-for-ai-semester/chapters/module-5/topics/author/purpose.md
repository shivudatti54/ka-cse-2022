### Learning Purpose: Module 5 - Author

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the concept of "author" is critical as AI-generated content becomes ubiquitous. It forces us to confront fundamental ethical and legal questions: Who is responsible for an AI's output? Who owns it? Clarifying authorship is essential for establishing accountability, protecting intellectual property, and maintaining trust in AI systems.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to deconstruct the traditional notion of authorship and apply it to AI-assisted and AI-generated work. They will analyze frameworks for assigning credit, responsibility, and liability across the chain of human developers, trainers, users, and the AI systems themselves. This includes examining copyright law, moral rights, and emerging policy proposals.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon prior modules concerning **accountability** (who is blamed for harm) and **transparency** (understanding how outputs are generated). It is a practical application of **value alignment**, as the policies we create around authorship will shape economic incentives and cultural norms. It also connects to future discussions on the legal **personhood** of AI systems.

**4. Real-world applications**
These concepts are immediately applicable in copyright disputes over AI-created art, determining liability for defamatory or erroneous text, and establishing standards for academic integrity and citation. Policymakers are actively grappling with these issues to create regulations that foster innovation while protecting creators and the public.
