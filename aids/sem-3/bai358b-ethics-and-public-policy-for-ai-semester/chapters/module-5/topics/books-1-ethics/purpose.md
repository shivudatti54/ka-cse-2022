### **Learning Purpose: Foundational Texts in AI Ethics**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
The ethical challenges posed by AI are not new; they are deeply rooted in long-standing philosophical debates about fairness, agency, and human values. Engaging with foundational texts provides the critical historical and theoretical context necessary to move beyond reactive, surface-level solutions. It grounds contemporary policy debates in rigorous intellectual tradition, ensuring that our frameworks are robust, thoughtful, and principled rather than merely technical or expedient.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to critically analyze core philosophical arguments from key texts (e.g., from Aristotle, Kant, utilitarians) and deconstruct how these concepts—such as justice, responsibility, and rights—directly apply to modern AI dilemmas like algorithmic bias, autonomous weapons, and privacy. The goal is to build a vocabulary of ethical reasoning that can be used to evaluate and formulate AI policies.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module is the philosophical bedrock of the entire course. It provides the essential language and logical tools that will be directly applied in subsequent modules covering specific policy areas like regulation (Module 2), governance structures (Module 3), and practical implementation (Module 4). One cannot craft effective policy without first understanding the ethical principles it aims to uphold.

**4. Real-world applications**
This learning is applied when drafting ethical guidelines for corporations, informing government legislation on AI accountability, arguing cases before regulatory bodies, or conducting ethical impact assessments for new AI systems. It empowers professionals to justify their policy positions with solid philosophical reasoning, not just technical or business considerations.
