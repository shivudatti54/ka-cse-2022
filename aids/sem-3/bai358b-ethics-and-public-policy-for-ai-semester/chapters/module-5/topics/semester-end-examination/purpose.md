### **Learning Purpose: Semester-End Examination on Ethics and Public Policy for AI**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This examination is crucial as it assesses the synthesis of core knowledge needed to responsibly guide AI development. As AI becomes deeply embedded in society, ethical missteps can cause significant harm, from perpetuating bias to threatening privacy. Understanding how to craft effective policy is essential to mitigate these risks and ensure AI benefits all of humanity, making this topic a foundational pillar for future technologists, policymakers, and citizens.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will demonstrate their mastery of key ethical frameworks (e.g., utilitarianism, deontology) and their application to AI dilemmas. They will be assessed on their ability to critically analyze policy proposals, evaluate trade-offs between innovation and regulation, and articulate well-reasoned, evidence-based arguments for governing automated systems in various sectors.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This exam integrates concepts from all previous modules. It connects technical understanding (how AI works) with philosophical principles (why ethics matter) and political mechanisms (how policy is made). It requires students to draw from law, sociology, and computer science, showcasing the interdisciplinary nature of solving real-world AI challenges.

**4. Real-world applications**
The skills tested are directly applicable to roles in AI ethics auditing, compliance, government regulation (e.g., EU AI Act), and responsible AI development within companies. It prepares students to contribute to creating fairness-aware algorithms, designing accountability frameworks for high-risk systems, and participating in public discourse on the future of AI governance.
