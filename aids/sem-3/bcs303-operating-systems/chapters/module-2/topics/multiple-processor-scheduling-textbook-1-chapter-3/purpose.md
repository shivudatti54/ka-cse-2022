Learning multiple-processor scheduling is crucial in understanding how operating systems allocate tasks among multiple processors to maximize efficiency, reduce wait times, and improve system performance. This topic matters as it directly impacts the overall responsiveness and throughput of computer systems, which is essential in modern computing applications. Real-world applications of multiple-processor scheduling include cloud computing, high-performance computing, and data centers, where multiple processors are used to process complex tasks and data.

This topic connects to other concepts in operating systems, such as process scheduling, memory management, and I/O management, as it requires careful consideration of process priorities, allocation of resources, and coordination between processors to ensure efficient and fair allocation of tasks.
