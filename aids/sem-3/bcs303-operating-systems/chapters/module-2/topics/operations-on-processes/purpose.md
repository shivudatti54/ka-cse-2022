Learning "Operations on processes" is crucial in understanding how operating systems manage multiple tasks and programs simultaneously. This topic matters because it explains how processes are created, managed, and terminated, which is essential for efficient system resource allocation and performance optimization. In real-world applications, knowledge of process operations is vital for developing efficient multitasking and resource management systems in operating systems, applications, and embedded systems. This concept also connects to other topics, such as process scheduling, synchronization, and synchronization primitives.
