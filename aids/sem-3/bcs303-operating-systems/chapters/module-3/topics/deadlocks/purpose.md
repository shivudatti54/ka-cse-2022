# Learning Objectives
After studying this topic, you should be able to:
1.  Define a distributed deadlock and explain how it differs from a centralized deadlock.
2.  List and explain the four necessary conditions for a deadlock to occur.
3.  Compare and contrast the three main strategies for handling deadlocks: prevention, avoidance, and detection/recovery, explaining why detection/recovery is most common in distributed systems.
4.  Describe how centralized and distributed deadlock detection algorithms work, including their key steps, advantages, and disadvantages.
5.  Explain the concept of a phantom deadlock, its causes, and its implications.
6.  Identify the criteria for selecting a victim process for recovery and describe the recovery actions.
