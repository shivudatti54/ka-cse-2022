### Learning Purpose: Tests of Significance

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Tests of significance are a cornerstone of statistical inference, providing a formal, quantifiable framework for making data-driven decisions. In computer science, this is critical for evaluating algorithms, validating machine learning models, analyzing A/B test results, and drawing reliable conclusions from noisy data.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the formal procedure for conducting a hypothesis test, including formulating null and alternative hypotheses, selecting a significance level (α), calculating a p-value, and making a statistical decision to reject or fail to reject the null hypothesis. They will understand the concepts of Type I and Type II errors.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly upon probability distributions and sampling theory from earlier modules. It is a foundational technique for more advanced inferential methods like confidence intervals and regression analysis. It is also a prerequisite for understanding machine learning concepts like model evaluation and statistical learning theory.

**4. Real-world applications**
This is applied extensively in industry to determine if a new feature improves user engagement (A/B testing), assess the statistical significance of a model's accuracy, validate assumptions in data analysis, and conduct performance benchmarks for hardware or software.
