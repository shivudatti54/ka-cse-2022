### Learning Purpose: Comparison of Large Samples

1. **Why is this topic important?**
   Statistical inference is fundamental for making data-driven decisions in computer science. The ability to correctly compare large samples is crucial because it allows us to generalize findings from data to broader populations, a common task in analyzing user behavior, A/B testing, and algorithm performance.

2. **What will students learn?**
   Students will learn to apply hypothesis tests (specifically, the z-test for proportions and means) to determine if observed differences between two large, independent samples are statistically significant. They will calculate test statistics, interpret p-values, and construct confidence intervals for the difference between population parameters.

3. **How does it connect to other concepts?**
   This topic builds directly upon the foundation of sampling distributions and the Central Limit Theorem covered earlier. It is a prerequisite for understanding more complex statistical methods, such as comparing small samples (t-tests) and ANOVA, which are used later in the curriculum.

4. **Real-world applications**
   This skill is directly applied in industry for A/B testing website layouts or features, comparing the accuracy of different machine learning models on large datasets, and analyzing performance metrics between two user groups or system configurations.
