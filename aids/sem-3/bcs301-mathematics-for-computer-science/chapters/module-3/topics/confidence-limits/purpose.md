### Learning Purpose: Confidence Limits

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Confidence limits are a cornerstone of statistical inference, providing a crucial measure of uncertainty around an estimate. In computer science, where data-driven decisions are paramount (e.g., A/B testing, machine learning model performance), understanding the precision and reliability of your results is essential. They move beyond a single point estimate to quantify the range in which a true population parameter is likely to lie.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to construct and interpret confidence intervals for population means and proportions. This includes understanding the underlying principles (e.g., sampling distributions, the central limit theorem), selecting the appropriate formula (z-interval vs. t-interval), and correctly interpreting the confidence level (e.g., "95% confident").

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly on probability distributions and the concept of sampling from Module 2. It is a foundational skill for more advanced statistical inference like hypothesis testing (Module 4) and is deeply connected to machine learning for evaluating model accuracy and to algorithm analysis for understanding performance metrics.

**4. Real-world applications**
This is applied to determine the error bounds in opinion polls, quantify the accuracy of an estimated average website load time, validate the results of a clinical trial, or assess the performance of a recommendation algorithm (e.g., "We are 90% confident the true conversion rate increase is between 1.5% and 3.0%").
