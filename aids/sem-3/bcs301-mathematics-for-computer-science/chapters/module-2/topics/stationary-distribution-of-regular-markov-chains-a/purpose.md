# Learning Purpose: Stationary Distribution & Absorbing States

**1. Importance**
This topic is fundamental for modeling and analyzing stochastic systems that evolve over time. Understanding long-term behavior (stationary distribution) and terminal states (absorbing states) is crucial for predicting outcomes in systems governed by probabilistic rules, a common scenario in computer science.

**2. Learning Outcomes**
Students will learn to calculate the stationary distribution of a regular Markov chain, which describes its long-run behavior regardless of the starting state. They will also learn to identify and analyze absorbing states and calculate the probability of entering these terminal states, using the fundamental matrix.

**3. Connections**
This knowledge builds directly on the previous module's concepts of joint probability distributions and Markov chain fundamentals. It provides the mathematical groundwork for more advanced topics like hidden Markov models, PageRank algorithms, and reinforcement learning, where predicting steady-state behavior is essential.

**4. Applications**
These concepts are applied in real-world systems such as:
*   **Google's PageRank:** Modeling web surfing as a Markov chain to find the stationary distribution (page importance).
*   **Queueing Theory:** Predicting long-term wait times and server loads in network traffic.
*   **Game Design:** Calculating the probability of finishing a game or reaching a specific state.
*   **Epidemiology:** Modeling the absorption of a population into a "recovered" state in disease spread.
