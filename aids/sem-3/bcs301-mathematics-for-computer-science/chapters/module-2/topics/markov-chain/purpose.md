### Learning Purpose: Markov Chains

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Markov Chains are a fundamental class of probabilistic models crucial for understanding systems that evolve randomly over time. Their importance in computer science stems from their ability to model a vast range of sequential processes where the future state depends only on the present state, not the full history. This simplifies complex stochastic problems and makes them computationally tractable.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to formally define a Markov Chain using state spaces and transition matrices. They will calculate multi-step probabilities using matrix exponentiation, classify states (transient, recurrent), and determine steady-state distributions. This provides the tools to analyze the long-term behavior of stochastic systems.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly on the joint probability distributions from earlier in the module, providing a dynamic application of those concepts. It serves as the foundational gateway to more advanced topics like Hidden Markov Models (used in machine learning and AI), Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods for sampling and estimation, and the analysis of randomized algorithms and queueing networks.

**4. Real-world applications**
Markov Chains have extensive real-world applications, including:
*   **PageRank Algorithm:** The foundation of Google's original search algorithm, modeling web surfing as a random walk.
*   **Predictive Text & Speech Recognition:** Modeling language sequences.
*   **Game Development:** For creating AI that makes probabilistic decisions.
*   **Financial Modeling:** Predicting market trends and credit ratings.
*   **Epidemiology:** Modeling the spread of diseases through a population.
