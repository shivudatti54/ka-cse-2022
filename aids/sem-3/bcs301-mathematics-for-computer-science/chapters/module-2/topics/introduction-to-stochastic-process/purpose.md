# Learning Purpose: Introduction to Stochastic Process

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Stochastic processes provide the fundamental mathematical framework for modeling systems that evolve randomly over time. This is crucial in computer science for analyzing algorithms, designing networks, and building intelligent systems that must operate under uncertainty. Understanding randomness as a dynamic process, rather than a static event, is key to many advanced applications.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define a stochastic process and identify its key components, such as the state space and index set. They will build upon their knowledge of joint probability distributions and Markov chains to analyze simple stochastic processes. Specifically, they will learn to classify processes (e.g., discrete/continuous time, discrete/continuous state space) and calculate probabilities for state transitions.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is a direct extension of joint probability distributions (Module 2), which describe multi-variable randomness, and Markov chains, a specific, memoryless type of stochastic process. It provides the foundation for subsequent topics like queueing theory, which models waiting lines, and machine learning algorithms like Hidden Markov Models and reinforcement learning.

**4. Real-world applications**
Stochastic processes model a vast array of real-world phenomena, including: the number of users on a website over time (web traffic analysis), packet routing in networks, stock price movements in financial tech (FinTech), and the predictive text algorithms on a smartphone keyboard. They are also essential for performance analysis of computer systems and databases.
