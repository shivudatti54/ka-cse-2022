### Learning Purpose: Probability Vectors

1.  **Why is this topic important?**
    Probability vectors are a foundational tool for modeling systems with a finite number of states. They provide the mathematical structure to represent and compute the likelihood of different outcomes, which is essential for analyzing probabilistic algorithms, stochastic processes, and machine learning models.

2.  **What will students learn?**
    Students will learn to define a probability vector, understand its properties (non-negative entries summing to 1), and perform key operations. This includes multiplying probability vectors by stochastic matrices, a core operation for updating state distributions in systems like Markov Chains.

3.  **How does it connect to other concepts?**
    This topic is a direct prerequisite for Markov Chains, where probability vectors represent the state distribution at a given time step. It builds directly on the concept of joint probability distributions and serves as the bridge to more advanced topics like PageRank algorithms, hidden Markov models, and Markov decision processes.

4.  **Real-world applications**
    Probability vectors are used to model real-world scenarios such as predicting weather patterns, analyzing user navigation on websites (PageRank), modeling genetic sequences in bioinformatics, and powering predictive text algorithms. They are fundamental to any system that must reason about uncertainty and state transitions.
