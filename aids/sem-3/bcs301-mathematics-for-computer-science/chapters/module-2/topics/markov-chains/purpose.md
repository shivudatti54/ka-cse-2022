### Learning Purpose: Markov Chains

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Markov chains are a fundamental tool for modeling systems that transition randomly between states, where the future depends only on the present. Their simplicity and power make them essential for analyzing stochastic processes, a core concept in computer science for handling uncertainty and randomness.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define a Markov chain using a state space and transition matrix. They will calculate multi-step probabilities, classify states (transient vs. absorbing), and determine steady-state distributions. This provides the foundation for analyzing the long-term behavior of probabilistic systems.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds on the joint probability distributions from earlier in the module, as the transition matrix is a form of conditional probability. It is a prerequisite for more advanced models like Hidden Markov Models (used in NLP and bioinformatics) and is a cornerstone of algorithms in randomized computing, queueing theory, and reinforcement learning.

**4. Real-world applications**
Markov chains have diverse applications, including predicting page rankings in web search algorithms (PageRank), modeling user behavior, text prediction and generation, financial market analysis, and simulating games of chance.
