### Learning Purpose: Review of Basic Probability Theory & Random Variables

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Probability theory is the fundamental language of uncertainty, forming the backbone of nearly every major domain in computer science. A strong grasp of these basics is essential for designing and analyzing randomized algorithms, modeling complex systems, and developing machine learning and artificial intelligence applications.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will solidify their understanding of core probability concepts, including sample spaces, events, conditional probability, and Bayes' Theorem. They will then formally define and work with both discrete and continuous random variables, learning to calculate key properties like expectation, variance, and standard deviation.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This review directly sets the stage for the rest of the module, providing the necessary tools to understand specific probability distributions (e.g., Binomial, Poisson, Normal). It is also a prerequisite for subsequent topics like statistical inference, probabilistic analysis of algorithms, stochastic processes, and data science.

**4. Real-world applications**
These concepts are used to model real-world phenomena: predicting network packet loss, assessing algorithm runtime, evaluating risk in cryptographic systems, building spam filters using Bayes' Theorem, and training machine learning models that make probabilistic predictions from data.
