### Learning Purpose: Probability Distributions

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Probability distributions are the fundamental mathematical framework for modeling uncertainty, randomness, and variability. In computer science, nearly all non-trivial systems—from network traffic and algorithm performance to machine learning and data analysis—involve stochastic processes. Understanding distributions is essential for making data-driven predictions and decisions.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define, interpret, and apply key discrete (e.g., Binomial, Poisson) and continuous (e.g., Normal, Exponential) distributions. They will master calculating probabilities, expected values, and variances. Crucially, they will learn how to select the appropriate distribution to model a given real-world scenario or dataset.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly upon core probability theory (events, random variables, expectation). It is a prerequisite for future modules on statistical inference, stochastic processes (like Markov chains), queueing theory, and machine learning, where distributions form the backbone of algorithms like Naive Bayes and Gaussian Mixture Models.

**4. Real-world applications**
Distributions are used everywhere: modeling packet arrival times in networks (Exponential), predicting user behavior on a website (Binomial), performing anomaly detection in datasets (Normal distribution), and running A/B tests to evaluate feature changes. They are the language of data-driven uncertainty.
