# **Central Limit Theorem and Confidence Limits for Unknown Mean**

## **Key Points**

- **Central Limit Theorem (CLT)**:
  - States that the distribution of the sample mean will be approximately normal, regardless of the underlying distribution of the population data.
  - As the sample size (n) increases, the sample mean will converge to the population mean (μ) with probability 1.
- **Confidence Limit for Unknown Mean**:
  - A range of values within which the true population mean is likely to lie.
  - Calculated using the sample mean (x̄), standard error (SE), and confidence level (CL).
- **Formulas**:
  - Sample mean (x̄) = (Σx_i) / n
  - Standard error (SE) = σ / √n
  - Confidence limits:
    - Lower limit = x̄ - z \* SE
    - Upper limit = x̄ + z \* SE
- **Theorems**:
  - **Chebyshev's Inequality**: E[√(Σ(x_i - μ)^2 / n)] ≤ σ / √n for any k > 0.
  - **Markov's Inequality**: P[|X - μ| ≥ kσ] ≤ 1 / k^2 for any k > 0.

## **Important Definitions**

- **Population mean**: μ (Greek letter mu)
- **Sample mean**: x̄ (x-bar)
- **Standard error**: SE (sigma-e)
- **Confidence level**: CL (e.g., 95% or 99%)
- **Z-score**: z = (x̄ - μ) / SE

## **Revision Tips**

- Remember that the CLT holds for large sample sizes (n ≥ 30).
- Use a standard normal distribution table (Z-table) to find z-scores.
- Calculate confidence limits using the sample mean and standard error.
