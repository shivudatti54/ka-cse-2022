### Semester-End Examination: Learning Purpose

**1. Why is this topic important?**
The semester-end examination is crucial as it serves as a comprehensive assessment of a student's ability to synthesize and apply the statistical concepts learned throughout the module. It validates their readiness to use these methods in advanced computer science contexts like algorithm analysis and A/B testing, where rigorous experimental design is paramount.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will demonstrate their proficiency in designing statistically sound experiments, formulating hypotheses, and applying ANOVA (One-way and Two-way) to analyze data and draw valid conclusions. They will learn to interpret p-values, F-statistics, and post-hoc tests within the constraints of an exam environment, solidifying their theoretical and practical understanding.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This exam integrates core module concepts: probability distributions (as foundations for tests), hypothesis testing, and regression analysis (as a comparative tool). It directly builds upon prior knowledge of descriptive statistics and sampling, requiring students to connect discrete topics into a cohesive workflow for data-driven decision-making.

**4. Real-world applications**
The skills assessed are directly applicable to real-world computer science problems. This includes evaluating the performance of different algorithms or hardware, testing user interface (UI) designs, analyzing the impact of network configurations, and interpreting results from machine learning model comparisons—all essential for a career in data science, AI, and software engineering.
