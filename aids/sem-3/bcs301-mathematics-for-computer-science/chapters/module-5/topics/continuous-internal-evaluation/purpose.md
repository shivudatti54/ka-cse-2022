### Learning Purpose: Continuous Internal Evaluation

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) is crucial because it provides a structured framework for collecting and analyzing data from ongoing experiments. In computer science, this is essential for A/B testing, algorithmic performance tuning, and validating system models against real-world data. It ensures decisions are data-driven and statistically sound, moving beyond one-off tests to understand performance over time.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to design and implement a CIE process. This includes formulating hypotheses, determining appropriate sampling strategies, collecting data sequentially, and applying statistical methods like sequential analysis to interpret results and make informed decisions about continuing or halting an experiment.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly upon foundational knowledge of hypothesis testing and ANOVA from this module. It connects to earlier concepts of probability distributions and regression, as CIE often involves monitoring data streams for trends. It is a practical application of statistical theory for software engineering and machine learning workflows.

**4. Real-world applications**
CIE is widely used in tech industries for continuous deployment and feature flagging, where new software features are rolled out incrementally and their impact is monitored. It is also fundamental in clinical trials, quality control in manufacturing, and any scenario requiring ongoing empirical validation.
