# **Analysis of Completely Randomized Design**

## **Definition and Assumptions**

- Completely Randomized Design (CRD) is a type of experimental design where all treatments are randomly assigned to experimental units.
- Assumptions:
  - Randomization of treatments to units
  - Independence of units
  - Normality of residuals
  - Homogeneity of variance

## **Key Formulas and Concepts**

- **Mean Square Error (MSE)**: Measure of variability of individual observations
- **Mean Square Treatment (MST)**: Measure of variability between treatments
- **Mean Square Error (MSE) = MST / (k - 1)**
- **Analysis of Variance (ANOVA)**: Statistical technique to compare means of different groups
- **F-Statistic**: Ratio of MST to MSE, used to test hypotheses about treatment effects

## **Theorems and Important Results**

- **Central Limit Theorem (CLT)**: States that sample means will be approximately normally distributed
- **Box's F-Test**: Used to test for homogeneity of variance
- **Tukey's HSD Test**: Used to compare means of different groups

## **Key Points for Revision**

- CRD is suitable for experiments with a small number of treatments
- ANOVA is used to test hypotheses about treatment effects
- MSE and MST are used to measure variability in the data
- The F-Statistic is used to test hypotheses about treatment effects
- CLT, Box's F-Test, and Tukey's HSD Test are important theorems and tests in ANOVA.
