### Learning Purpose: Continuous Internal Evaluation

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) is a crucial pedagogical tool that moves beyond a single high-stakes exam. It is important because it provides a more holistic and ongoing assessment of a student's understanding of data structures. This allows for timely intervention, helps identify gaps in knowledge, and encourages consistent engagement with the complex material throughout the module.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to demonstrate their practical and theoretical knowledge of data structures (e.g., graphs, trees, hashing) through various CIE components like quizzes, programming assignments, and presentations. They will develop the ability to apply concepts to solve problems, articulate their solutions, and receive constructive feedback to improve their skills iteratively.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
CIE acts as a feedback mechanism that connects and reinforces all previously learned concepts in the course (e.g., arrays, linked lists, stacks, queues). By being assessed on integrated topics, students learn to see the relationships between different data structures and understand their comparative advantages, leading to a more cohesive knowledge base.

**4. Real-world applications**
The process mirrors the agile development and continuous review cycles used in the software industry. Successfully completing CIE tasks cultivates essential real-world skills: consistently meeting deadlines, writing and testing functional code, collaborating with peers, and clearly presenting technical solutions—all of which are directly applicable to a career in software engineering.
