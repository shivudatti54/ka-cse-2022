# Learning Purpose: Sartaj Sahni and Susan Anderson-Freed

### 1. Why is this topic important?
This topic is crucial as it highlights the foundational contributions of two pioneers, Sartaj Sahni and Susan Anderson-Freed, to the field of data structures. Understanding their work provides historical context, showcases the evolution of key algorithms and data organization techniques, and emphasizes the importance of efficient design in solving computational problems.

### 2. What will students learn?
Students will learn about the specific data structures and algorithms pioneered or significantly advanced by these computer scientists, such as Sahni's work in data structure textbooks, NP-completeness, and parallel computing, and Anderson-Freed's contributions to the development of the efficient "splay tree" data structure. The module will cover the theoretical underpinnings and operational mechanics of these concepts.

### 3. How does it connect to other concepts?
This knowledge directly builds upon earlier modules covering fundamental data structures (like trees and graphs) and algorithm analysis (Big-O notation). It connects the theoretical principles of computational complexity and efficiency to their practical implementation, providing a deeper understanding of how to select and apply the right data structure for a given problem.

### 4. Real-world applications
The efficient data structures and algorithms developed by these pioneers are applied in numerous real-world systems. Splay trees, for instance, are used in network routers, garbage collection algorithms, and caches for their self-optimizing capabilities. Sahni's work on NP-completeness and algorithm design is fundamental to tackling complex optimization problems in logistics, scheduling, and bioinformatics.
