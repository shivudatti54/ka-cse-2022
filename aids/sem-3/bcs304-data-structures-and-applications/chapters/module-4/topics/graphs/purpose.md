# Learning Objectives
After studying this topic, you should be able to:
1. Define planar graphs and identify whether a given graph is planar or not
2. Apply Euler's formula to calculate faces, edges, or vertices in planar graphs
3. Use necessary conditions (like E ≤ 3V - 6) to quickly test for non-planarity
4. Recognize Kuratowski's graphs (K₅ and K₃,₃) and understand their role in planarity testing
5. Explain the significance of planar graphs in real-world applications
6. Construct dual graphs from given planar graphs
