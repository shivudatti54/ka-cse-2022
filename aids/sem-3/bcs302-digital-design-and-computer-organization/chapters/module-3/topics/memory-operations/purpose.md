# Learning Objectives
After completing this topic, you will be able to:
- Define a vector algebraically and geometrically, and perform vector addition and scalar multiplication.
- Calculate the magnitude (length) of a vector and normalize it to a unit vector.
- Compute the dot product of two vectors and use it to find the angle between them.
- Determine if two vectors are orthogonal (perpendicular) based on their dot product.
- Compute the projection of one vector onto another.
- Calculate the cross product of two vectors in 3-dimensional space and interpret its geometric meaning.
- Apply the properties of vector operations (commutative, associative, distributive) to simplify vector expressions.

## Prerequisites
- Understanding of basic algebraic operations and properties.
- Familiarity with the Cartesian coordinate system in 2 and 3 dimensions.
- Knowledge of the Pythagorean Theorem and basic trigonometric functions (sine, cosine).

## Real-World Applications
- Computer Graphics: Calculating lighting, reflections, and 3D object transformations.
- Physics: Representing and calculating forces, velocity, and other physical quantities.
- Engineering: Analyzing structures and solving problems in mechanics and electromagnetism.
- Machine Learning: Representing data as vectors in high-dimensional spaces for pattern recognition.
- Navigation: Calculating directions and headings using vector addition and angles.
