### 4.1 Introduction to Digital Logic

#### Definition of Digital Logic

Digital logic is a branch of mathematics that deals with the representation and manipulation of information using discrete values, typically 0s and 1s. It is the foundation of digital design and computer organization.

#### Key Characteristics of Digital Logic

- **Binary System**: Digital logic uses a binary system, where information is represented using only two values: 0 and 1.
- **Discrete Values**: Digital logic deals with discrete values, which are distinct and separate from one another.
- **Boolean Algebra**: Digital logic is based on Boolean algebra, a mathematical system that deals with logical operations.

#### Boolean Algebra

Boolean algebra is a mathematical system that deals with logical operations. It is used to represent and manipulate digital information.

- **Variables**: Boolean variables can take on two values: 0 and 1.
- **Operators**: Boolean operators include AND, OR, and NOT, which are used to combine variables.
- **Laws**: Boolean algebra has several laws, including the complement law, idempotent law, and distributive law.

#### Digital Logic Gates

Digital logic gates are the building blocks of digital circuits. They are used to perform logical operations on digital signals.

- **AND Gate**: An AND gate produces an output of 1 only if both inputs are 1.
- **OR Gate**: An OR gate produces an output of 1 if either input is 1.
- **NOT Gate**: A NOT gate produces an output that is the complement of the input.

#### Digital Logic Circuits

Digital logic circuits are composed of digital logic gates. They are used to perform more complex logical operations.

- **Sequential Logic**: Sequential logic circuits have memory and can store information.
- **Combinational Logic**: Combinational logic circuits do not have memory and only perform logical operations.

### Key Concepts

- **Binary System**: A system that uses only two values: 0 and 1.
- **Boolean Algebra**: A mathematical system that deals with logical operations.
- **Digital Logic Gates**: The building blocks of digital circuits.
- **Digital Logic Circuits**: Composed of digital logic gates, they perform more complex logical operations.

### Example Problems

1.  Design a digital logic circuit that implements an AND gate.
2.  Use Boolean algebra to simplify the expression `(A AND B) OR (C AND D)`.
3.  Design a digital logic circuit that implements a sequential logic circuit with memory.
