**Why this topic matters:**
Learning to design digital circuits using Verilog HDL and the structural style is crucial in modern digital design, as it enables students to implement complex digital systems and algorithms. This topic lays the foundation for more advanced concepts in digital design, such as behavioral and gate-level synthesis. By mastering structural design, students can optimize their digital circuits for performance, area, and power consumption. This skill is also essential for students pursuing careers in electronics engineering, computer science, and software engineering.

**Real-world applications:**
The knowledge gained from this topic has numerous real-world applications in various industries, including:

- Embedded systems: designing digital controllers, interfaces, and peripherals for applications like robotics, automotive, and consumer electronics.
- Communication systems: implementing digital signal processing, modulation, and demodulation for wireless and wired communication systems.
- Computer architecture: designing processor cores, memory interfaces, and interconnects for high-performance computing systems.

**Connection to other concepts:**
This topic connects to other concepts in digital design, such as:

- Behavioral design: learning to describe digital circuits using behavioral models, which can be used to generate netlists for structural synthesis.
- Gate-level synthesis: understanding how to optimize digital circuits for performance and area using gate-level synthesis tools.
- Digital logic: applying knowledge of digital logic gates, arithmetic circuits, and control circuits to design complex digital systems.
