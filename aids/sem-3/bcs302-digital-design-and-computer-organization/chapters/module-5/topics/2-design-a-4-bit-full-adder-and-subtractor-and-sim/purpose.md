**Designing a 4-bit Full Adder and Subtractor using Basic Gates:**

To design a 4-bit full adder and subtractor using basic gates, students can follow these steps:

1. Define the inputs and outputs for the full adder and subtractor.
2. Use logic gates (AND, OR, NOT, XOR, and XNOR) to implement the arithmetic operations.
3. Implement the carry and borrow logic for the full adder and subtractor.
4. Use truth tables to verify the correctness of the design.

**Simulation:**

To simulate the design, students can use a digital logic simulator, such as Verilog or VHDL, to model the digital circuit and verify its behavior. They can also use a hardware description language (HDL) simulator to simulate the digital circuit and test its functionality.

**Example of a 4-bit Full Adder:**

The 4-bit full adder can be designed using the following logic gates:

- A1, A2, A3, and A4 are the inputs.
- F1, F2, F3, and F4 are the sum outputs.
- C1, C2, C3, and C4 are the carry outputs.

The logic gates can be implemented as follows:

- A1, A2, A3, and A4 are connected to the inputs of the XOR gates.
- The outputs of the XOR gates are connected to the inputs of the OR gates.
- The outputs of the OR gates are connected to the outputs of the NOT gates.
- The outputs of the NOT gates are connected to the inputs of the AND gates.
- The outputs of the AND gates are connected to the outputs of the OR gates.

Similarly, a 4-bit subtractor can be designed using a combination of AND, OR, and NOT gates to implement the borrow and carry logic.
