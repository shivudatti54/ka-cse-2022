# Learning Objectives
After studying this topic, you should be able to:
1.  Describe the role of the ALU and the Control Unit within the broader von Neumann architecture.
2.  Explain each step of the fetch-decode-execute cycle, identifying the key registers involved and their specific functions.
3.  List and describe the different types of operations performed by the ALU (arithmetic, logical, shift).
4.  Define the common status flags (Zero, Carry, Overflow, Sign) and identify the conditions under which they are set.
5.  Differentiate between hardwired and microprogrammed control unit implementations, including their advantages and disadvantages.
6.  Trace the flow of data and control signals for a simple instruction (e.g., ADD) to understand the interaction between the CU, ALU, and registers.
