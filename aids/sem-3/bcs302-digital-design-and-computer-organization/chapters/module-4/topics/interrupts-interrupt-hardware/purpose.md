# Learning Objectives
After studying this topic, you should be able to:
1.  Define an interrupt and explain its necessity in a computer system, contrasting it with polling.
2.  Classify interrupts into hardware, software (traps), and exceptions, providing examples of each.
3.  Describe the complete sequence of steps involved in processing a hardware interrupt, from occurrence to resumption.
4.  Explain the role of the Interrupt Vector Table (IVT) and the concept of vectored interrupts.
5.  Differentiate between maskable and non-maskable interrupts and explain the purpose of interrupt masking.
6.  Analyze the interaction between DMA and interrupts, explaining how DMA reduces interrupt overhead.
