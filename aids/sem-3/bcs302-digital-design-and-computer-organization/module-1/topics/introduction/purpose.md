# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define digital design and explain its fundamental characteristics including binary representation and noise immunity.

2. Distinguish between digital and analog systems, articulating the advantages of digital approaches for computation and information processing.

3. Differentiate between computer architecture and computer organization, understanding the relationship between the programmer's view and hardware implementation.

4. Identify and describe the levels of design abstraction from system level down to circuit level, recognizing the purpose of each abstraction.

5. Explain the significance of Boolean algebra in digital design and its relationship to logic gate implementation.

6. Describe the key design metrics (area, performance, power) and understand the trade-offs involved in digital design optimization.

7. Trace the historical evolution of digital systems from mechanical calculators to modern integrated circuits, appreciating the technological advances that enabled modern computing.

8. Recognize how the foundational concepts introduced in this module prepare the ground for subsequent topics including Boolean algebra, logic gates, and circuit minimization.
