# **Stackelberg's Model of Duopoly**

## **Key Points**

- **Definition**: Stackelberg's model of duopoly is a game theory model that describes a situation where two firms, the leader and the follower, make decisions about their output levels and prices.
- **Leader**: The leader firm makes its decision first, and its action affects the follower firm's decision-making process.
- **Follower**: The follower firm makes its decision second, based on the leader's action.

## **Important Formulas and Definitions**

- **Game Tree**: A tree diagram that illustrates the sequence of decisions made by the leader and the follower.
- **Strategic Subgame Perfect Equilibrium (SSPE)**: A concept that describes a situation where the leader and the follower make decisions that maximize their respective payoffs, assuming the other firm's action is optimal.
- ** Stackelberg Equilibrium**: A situation where the leader and the follower make decisions that maximize their respective payoffs, given the other firm's action.

## **Key Theorems**

- **Stackelberg's Lemma**: If the leader can influence the follower's decision, then the follower's payoff is always higher than if the leader did not influence the decision.
- **First-Stage Game**: The leader's decision affects the follower's payoff, while the follower's decision does not affect the leader's payoff.

## **Important Concepts**

- **Price Competition**: The leader and the follower compete on price, with the goal of maximizing their respective sales and profits.
- **Output Competition**: The leader and the follower compete on output levels, with the goal of maximizing their respective sales and profits.
