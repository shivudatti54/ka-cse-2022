# **Illustrations: The Ultimatum Game**

**Module:** @# 16032024
**Topic:** Illustrations: The Ultimatum Game

### Key Points:

- **Definition:** The Ultimatum Game is a mathematical game theory model that illustrates how individuals make decisions when they are faced with a conflict between fairness and self-interest.
- **Gameplay:**
  - Two players, proposer and responder
  - Proposer presents a split of a sum of money (e.g. $1)
  - Responder must accept or reject the split
- **Objectives:**
  - Proposer: maximize own payoff
  - Responder: maximize own payoff and punish others

### Important Formulas, Definitions, and Theorems:

- **Proposer's Inequality:** A > (1 - ρ)A + ρB
- **Responder's Inequality:** 1 - ρ < 1
- **Threat Point:** (1 - ρ)A
- **Pareto Efficiency:** A > B
- **Prisoner's Dilemma:** A > B > (1 - ρ)A + ρB > (1 - ρ)A

### Theorem:

- ** Ultimatum Game Result:** The proposer will often offer an unfair split, resulting in a low probability of acceptance by the responder.
