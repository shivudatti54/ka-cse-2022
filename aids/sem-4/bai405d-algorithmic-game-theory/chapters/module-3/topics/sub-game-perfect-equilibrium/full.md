## Introduction

Sub-Game Perfect Equilibrium is a concept in game theory that builds upon the foundation of Nash Equilibrium. In essence, it deals with the optimal strategy for a player in a game, taking into account the optimal strategies of all other players in sub-games or sub- games within the larger game.

## Game Theory Fundamentals

Before diving into Sub-Game Perfect Equilibrium, it's essential to understand some fundamental concepts in game theory:

- **Payoff matrix**: A table that represents the payoffs for each player in a game.
- **Strategy**: A player's choice of action in a game.
- **Game**: A situation where multiple players make decisions that affect each other.

## Nash Equilibrium

In 1950, John Nash introduced the concept of Nash Equilibrium, which states that a player's optimal strategy is the one that maximizes their payoff, given the strategies of all other players.

## Sub-Game Perfect Equilibrium

Sub-Game Perfect Equilibrium is an extension of Nash Equilibrium. It assumes that the game can be divided into sub-games, where each sub-game represents a smaller game within the larger game. The optimal strategy for a player in a sub-game is the one that maximizes their payoff, given the strategies of all other players in that sub-game.

## Formulation

To formulate a Sub-Game Perfect Equilibrium, we need to:

1.  **Define the game**: Identify the game and its payoffs.
2.  **Divide the game into sub-games**: Identify the sub-games within the larger game.
3.  **Determine the optimal strategy for each sub-game**: Use game theory techniques to find the optimal strategy for each sub-game.
4.  **Combine the optimal strategies**: Combine the optimal strategies from each sub-game to find the overall optimal strategy for the entire game.

## Properties

Sub-Game Perfect Equilibrium has several important properties, including:

- **Optimality**: The optimal strategy is the one that maximizes the payoff.
- **Uniqueness**: There is only one optimal strategy.
- **Existence**: The optimal strategy exists if the game is finite and the players have a finite number of strategies.
