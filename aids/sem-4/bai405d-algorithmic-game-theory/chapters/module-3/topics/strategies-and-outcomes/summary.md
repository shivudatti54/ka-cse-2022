# Strategies and Outcomes

### Definitions and Formulas

- **Strategy**: A decision-making rule or plan to achieve a goal in a game or interaction.
- **Game Theory**: The study of mathematical models of interaction among rational decision-makers.
- **Payoff Matrix**: A table showing the possible outcomes and their associated payoffs for each player.
- **Nash Equilibrium**: A concept in game theory that describes a stable state where no player can improve their payoff by unilaterally changing their strategy.

### Key Concepts

- **Strategic Interdependence**: The idea that the outcome of a game depends on the actions (strategies) of all players.
- **Rational Choice**: The assumption that players make decisions based on their own self-interest.
- **Cooperative vs. Non-Cooperative Games**: Cooperative games involve players working together to achieve a common goal, while non-cooperative games involve players competing against each other.

### Theorems and Results

- **Pareto Optimality**: A state where no player can improve their payoff without making another player worse off.
- **Gambler's Ruin**: A theorem that describes the probability of winning or losing in a game where the player's outcome depends on chance events.
- **Minimax Theorem**: A theorem that describes the optimal strategy for a player to minimize their maximum possible loss.

### Important Outcomes

- **Dominant Strategy**: A strategy that is the best choice for a player regardless of the actions of other players.
- **Mixed Strategy**: A strategy that involves randomizing between different actions.
- **Pareto Optimal Outcome**: An outcome where no player can improve their payoff without making another player worse off.
