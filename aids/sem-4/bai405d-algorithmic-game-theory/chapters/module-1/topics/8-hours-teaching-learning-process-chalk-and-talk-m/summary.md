### Teaching-Learning Process: Chalk and Talk Method / PowerPoint Presentation

#### Introduction

- Lecture format used in traditional teaching methods
- Focus on lecturer's expertise and students' note-taking
- Pros: easy to follow, engaging for students
- Cons: limited interaction, may not accommodate diverse learning styles

#### Characteristics

- Lecture starts with introduction and ends with recap
- Students are encouraged to take notes but not actively participate
- Lecturer presents information in a straightforward manner
- Limited opportunities for questions and discussions

#### Types of Chalk and Talk Presentations

- **Traditional Chalk and Talk**: Lecturer writes on blackboard or whiteboard
- **PowerPoint Presentation**: Lecturer uses a computer presentation software

#### Advantages and Limitations

- Advantages:
  - Easy to follow
  - Engaging for students
  - Can be used for large classes
- Limitations:
  - Limited interaction between lecturer and students
  - May not accommodate diverse learning styles
  - Can be time-consuming

#### Comparison with Other Methods

| Method                  | Key Features                           | Advantages               | Disadvantages                                                    |
| ----------------------- | -------------------------------------- | ------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Chalk and Talk          | Lecturer-centered, note-taking focused | Easy to follow, engaging | Limited interaction, may not accommodate diverse learning styles |
| PowerPoint Presentation | Visual aids, lecture notes             | Engaging, easy to follow | Limited interaction, may not accommodate diverse learning styles |
