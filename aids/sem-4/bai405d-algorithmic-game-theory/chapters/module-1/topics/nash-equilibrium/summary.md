# Nash Equilibrium

- **Definition:** A Nash equilibrium is a concept in game theory where no player can improve their payoff (outcome) by unilaterally changing their strategy, assuming all other players keep their strategies unchanged.
- **Key Components:**
  - **Payoff Matrix:** A table that represents the payoffs for each player in case of different strategies.
  - **Strategy:** An action chosen by a player at a particular stage of the game.
  - **Mixed Strategy:** A probability distribution over all possible pure strategies.
- **Important Formulas:**
  - Nash Equilibrium Theorem (1950): If a game is finite, then there exists a Nash equilibrium, and it is unique.
  - Mixed Equilibrium Theorem (1951): A mixed strategy is a Nash equilibrium if and only if it is a best response to the mixed strategies of all other players.
- **Important Definitions:**
  - **Best Response:** A strategy that maximizes a player's payoff, given the strategies of all other players.
  - **Subgame Perfect Nash Equilibrium (SPNE):** A Nash equilibrium that is also a best response to the strategies of all other players at every stage of the game.
- **Important Theorems:**
  - Nash Equilibrium Theorem: States that a Nash equilibrium exists for any finite game.
  - Revelation Principle: States that a game is equivalent to a mechanism design problem, where players reveal their types (strategies).
- **Important Concepts:**
  - **Local Optimality:** A strategy is locally optimal if it maximizes the player's payoff given the strategies of all other players.
  - **Global Optimality:** A strategy is globally optimal if it maximizes the player's payoff regardless of the strategies of all other players.
