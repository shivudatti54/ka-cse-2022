# Nash Equilibrium Study Material

=====================================

## Introduction

---

In game theory, a Nash equilibrium is a state in a game where no player can improve their payoff (or outcome) by unilaterally changing their strategy, assuming all other players keep their strategies unchanged. This concept is named after John Nash, who introduced it in the 1950s.

## Definition

---

A Nash equilibrium is a stable state in a game where:

- No player can improve their payoff by changing their strategy.
- All players have rational expectations and make decisions based on the information they have.
- The equilibrium is reached through a process of trial and error, where players iteratively choose strategies and adjust based on the responses of other players.

## Key Concepts

---

- **Strategic Form**: A mathematical representation of a game, where players' strategies are represented as functions or decision trees.
- **Payoff Matrix**: A table that shows the payoffs for each player in each possible outcome of the game.
- **Mixed Strategies**: A strategy that involves choosing a random action from a set of possible actions, to avoid giving away information about one's true intentions.
- **Pareto Optimality**: A state where no player can improve their payoff without making another player worse off.

## Examples

---

### Example 1: Prisoner's Dilemma

In the Prisoner's Dilemma, two prisoners must decide whether to cooperate or defect. The payoffs are as follows:

|               | Cooperate | Defect |
| ------------- | --------- | ------ |
| **Cooperate** | 3, 3      | 0, 5   |
| **Defect**    | 5, 0      | 0, 0   |

The Nash equilibrium is (Defect, Defect), where both prisoners choose to defect and get a payoff of 0.

### Example 2: Chicken Game

In the Chicken Game, two drivers must decide whether to swerve or not. The payoffs are as follows:

|                  | Swerve | Don't Swerve |
| ---------------- | ------ | ------------ |
| **Swerve**       | 10, 10 | 0, 0         |
| **Don't Swerve** | 0, 0   | 0, 10        |

The Nash equilibrium is (Don't Swerve, Don't Swerve), where both drivers choose not to swerve and both get a payoff of 0.

## Proof of Existence

---

The Nash equilibrium can be proven to exist using the following steps:

1. Assume that there is no Nash equilibrium.
2. Show that this assumption leads to a contradiction.
3. Conclude that a Nash equilibrium must exist.

The proof involves using the concept of strategic form and payoff matrices to show that, given a set of rational players, a Nash equilibrium must exist.

## Conclusion

---

The Nash equilibrium is a fundamental concept in game theory, which helps us understand how players make decisions in strategic situations. By understanding the concept of Nash equilibrium, we can analyze and predict the behavior of players in various games and situations.
