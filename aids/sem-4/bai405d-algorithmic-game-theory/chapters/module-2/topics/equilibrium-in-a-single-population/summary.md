# **Equilibrium in a Single Population**

### Key Definitions

- **Equilibrium**: A stable state where no player can improve their payoff by unilaterally changing their strategy, assuming all other players keep their strategies unchanged.
- **Mixed Strategy**: A probability distribution over a player's available strategies, used to randomize their actions.

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Nash Equilibrium**: A state where no player can improve their payoff by unilaterally changing their strategy, assuming all other players keep their strategies unchanged.
- **Mixed Strategy Nash Equilibrium**: A Nash Equilibrium where players use mixed strategies to randomize their actions.
- **Pareto Optimality**: A state where no player can improve their payoff without making another player worse off.

### Key Points

- **Pigou-Dickersheim Theorem**: A single player can improve their payoff by randomizing their strategy if the other player is not indifferent between their payoffs.
- **Mixed Strategy Nash Equilibrium**: A necessary and sufficient condition for a Nash Equilibrium in a finite game with multiple players.
- **Minimax Theorem**: A state where the expected payoff of a mixed strategy is the minimum possible payoff (minimax) and the expected payoff of the opponent's mixed strategy is the maximum possible payoff (maximax).
- **Pareto Optimality**: A necessary condition for a Nash Equilibrium in a finite game with multiple players.

### Important Concepts

- **Strategic Subgame Perfectness**: A Nash Equilibrium in which no player can improve their payoff by changing their strategy after observing the actions of other players.
- **Correlated Equilibrium**: A state where players use correlated strategies to randomize their actions.

### Important Theorems and Results

- **Kreps-Young Theorem**: A mixed strategy is a Nash Equilibrium if and only if the opponent is indifferent between their payoffs.
- **Roberts-Aumann Theorem**: A single player can improve their payoff by randomizing their strategy if the other player is not indifferent between their payoffs.
