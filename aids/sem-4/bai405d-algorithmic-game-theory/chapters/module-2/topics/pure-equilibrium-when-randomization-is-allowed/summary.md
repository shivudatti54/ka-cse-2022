# **Pure Equilibrium when Randomization is Allowed**

### Overview

In this section, we explore the concept of pure equilibrium in strategic games where players may randomize their actions. When randomization is allowed, players can choose between a pure strategy (a specific action) and a mixed strategy (a probability distribution over actions).

### Key Points

- **Mixed Strategy Nash Equilibrium (MSNE)**: A Nash equilibrium in which players choose a mixed strategy (a probability distribution over actions).
- **Perfect Mixed Strategy**: A mixed strategy that is optimal for every player, and no player can improve their expected payoff by changing their strategy.
- **Correspondence between Pure and Mixed Strategies**: A one-to-one correspondence between pure and mixed strategies, where a mixed strategy is equivalent to a probability distribution over a set of pure strategies.
- **Formula for Expected Payoff**: The expected payoff for a player is calculated as the sum of the products of the probability of each pure strategy and the payoff for that strategy.

### Definitions and Theorems

- **Mixed Strategy**: A probability distribution over a set of actions, where each action is assigned a probability between 0 and 1.
- **Nash Equilibrium**: A state in which no player can improve their expected payoff by unilaterally changing their strategy, assuming all other players keep their strategies unchanged.
- **Borel Measurable Space**: A mathematical concept used to define mixed strategies and their expected payoffs.
- **Theorem: MSNE is a Nash Equilibrium**: A theorem that states that a mixed strategy Nash equilibrium is also a Nash equilibrium.

### Important Formulas

- **Expected Payoff Formula**: E[π] = ∑[π(i) × u(i)]
- **Correspondence Formula**: π ∼ π' if and only if π(i) = π'(i) for all i
