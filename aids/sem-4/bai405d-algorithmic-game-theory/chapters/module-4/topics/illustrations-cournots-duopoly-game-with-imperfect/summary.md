# **Illustrations: Cournot's Duopoly Game with Imperfect Information**

## **Key Points:**

- **Cournot's Duopoly Game**: A classic model of competition between two firms producing a homogeneous product.
- **Imperfect Information**: Each firm has incomplete or uncertain knowledge about the other's production level.
- **Objectives**: Each firm aims to maximize its own profit, given the uncertainty about the other firm's production.

## **Definitions:**

- **Quantity**: The quantity of the product produced by each firm.
- **Price**: The price at which the product is sold.
- **Profit**: The revenue minus the cost of production.

## **Formulas:**

- **Profit Function**: π = p(Q) - c(Q)
- **Revenue Function**: r = p(Q) \* Q
- **Cost Function**: c = c(Q)

## **Theorems:**

- **Prisoner's Dilemma Analogy**: Each firm's best response is to produce a quantity that maximizes its own profit, but this leads to a suboptimal outcome for both firms.
- **Nash Equilibrium**: The best response of each firm is to produce a quantity that is a function of the other firm's production, leading to a stable equilibrium.

## **Key Results:**

- **Monopolist Firm**: If one firm produces the entire market, it will produce the quantity that maximizes its profit.
- **Duopoly Equilibrium**: If both firms produce, the equilibrium quantity is lower than the quantity produced by a monopolist firm.
- **Imperfect Information**: The equilibrium quantity is lower than the market equilibrium quantity, as firms produce less than they would if they had perfect information.

## **Important Formulas:**

- **Cournot's Equilibrium**: Q1 = Q2 = (2 \* c'(Q)) / p'(Q)
- **Derivative of Profit Function**: π' = p' \* Q + c' \* Q

## **Revision Tips:**

- Focus on the key points and formulas.
- Understand the implications of imperfect information on the Cournot's duopoly game.
- Be able to explain the Nash equilibrium and its implications for the firms.
