# **Teaching-Learning Process: Chalk and Talk Method / PowerPoint Presentation**

## **Key Points**

- **Chalk and Talk Method**
  - An instructor lectures to students in a classroom setting.
  - Advantages: Encourages active listening, allows for Q&A and discussion.
  - Disadvantages: May not engage students with visual content.
- **PowerPoint Presentation**
  - Uses slides to convey information to students.
  - Advantages: Visual aids, easy to organize and review.
  - Disadvantages: May lead to passive learning, limited interaction.

## **Important Formulas and Definitions**

- **Bayes' Theorem**: P(A|B) = P(B|A) \* P(A) / P(B)
  - Used in updating probability estimates based on new information.
- **Normal Distribution**: A probability distribution with a Gaussian curve.
  - Used to model continuous random variables.

## **Teaching-Learning Process Theorems**

- **Fisher Information**: A measure of the amount of information obtained from a sample.
  - Used in Bayesian inference to calculate the precision of estimates.
- **Von Mises Theorem**: A theorem in probability theory that describes the behavior of statistics.

## **Motivational Examples**

- **Game Theory**: The study of strategic decision making in situations where the outcome depends on the actions of multiple individuals or parties.
  - Examples: Prisoner's Dilemma, Tragedy of the Commons.
- **Bayesian Games**: A type of game where players update their beliefs based on new information.
  - Examples: Rock-Paper-Scissors, Auctions.
