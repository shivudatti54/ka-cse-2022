# Finitely and Infinitely Repeated Prisoner's Dilemma

## Revision Notes

### Definitions and Formulas

- **Prisoner's Dilemma**: A game where two prisoners have two options: confess or remain silent. Payoffs are as follows:
  - (Confess, Confess): (3, 3)
  - (Confess, Silent): (0, 5)
  - (Silent, Confess): (5, 0)
  - (Silent, Silent): (1, 1)
- **Nash Equilibrium**: A state where no player can improve their payoff by unilaterally changing their strategy, assuming the other player's strategy remains the same.
- **Repeating Game**: A game played multiple times with the same payoffs.

### Key Concepts

- **Finite Repeated Prisoner's Dilemma**: The prisoners play the Prisoner's Dilemma multiple times, but the payoffs remain the same each time.
- **Infinitely Repeated Prisoner's Dilemma**: The prisoners play the Prisoner's Dilemma repeatedly, but the payoffs change each time. For example, the winner of the first game gets a higher payoff than the loser.
- **Pareto Optimality**: A state where no player can improve their payoff without making the other player's payoff worse.

### Theorems and Results

- **Fermat's Dilemma**: In a finite repeated Prisoner's Dilemma, the Nash Equilibrium is (Confess, Confess).
- **Folk Theorem**: In an infinitely repeated Prisoner's Dilemma, the Nash Equilibrium can be (Confess, Confess) or (Stay, Stay) if the payoffs are sufficiently high.
- **Prisoner's Dilemma Fails**: In an infinitely repeated Prisoner's Dilemma, cooperation is not always the dominant strategy, and the prisoners may not always defect.

### Strategic Concepts

- **Punishment**: A strategy that deters a prisoner from defecting.
- **Reward**: A strategy that incentivizes a prisoner to cooperate.
- **Tit-for-Tat**: A strategy where a prisoner cooperates on the first round and then mirrors the opponent's previous action.

### Important Results

- **Prisoner's Dilemma Implies No Cooperation**: In an infinitely repeated Prisoner's Dilemma, cooperation is not always the dominant strategy.
- **Pareto Optimality does not Imply Cooperation**: The Nash Equilibrium may not be Pareto Optimal in an infinitely repeated Prisoner's Dilemma.

### Conclusion

The Prisoner's Dilemma is a fundamental concept in game theory that highlights the tension between individual and collective rationality. The finitely and infinitely repeated Prisoner's Dilemma provide insights into the strategic interactions between players and the conditions under which cooperation is likely to emerge.
