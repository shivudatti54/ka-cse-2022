# Repeated Games: The Main Idea

### Overview

Repeated games are a fundamental concept in algorithmic game theory, where two or more players engage in multiple rounds of a game, with the possibility of a long-term relationship or cooperation between them.

### Key Definitions

- **Repeated game**: A game that is played multiple times, with each player making decisions based on the actions of the other players in previous periods.
- **Dynamic game**: A game where the state of the game changes over time, and the players adapt their strategies accordingly.
- **Repeated game tree**: A tree-like representation of the possible actions and outcomes of a repeated game.

### Key Formulas

- **Pareto optimality**: A state where no player can improve their payoff without making another player worse off.
- **Nash equilibrium**: A state where no player can unilaterally change their strategy to improve their payoff, assuming the other players keep their strategies unchanged.

### Theorem: Nash's 15-Player Prisoner's Dilemma

- If two players play a game of mutual cooperation, the dominant strategy for each player is to defect, resulting in a suboptimal outcome for both.
- The Nash equilibrium of the 15-player prisoner's dilemma is a mixed strategy, where each player plays the strategy of "defect with probability 1/15" to achieve a stable outcome.

### Important Theorems

- **Nash's theorem**: A game has a Nash equilibrium if and only if the game is bounded and continuous.
- **Folk theorem**: In a repeated game with a finite number of players, there exists a mixed strategy Nash equilibrium.

### Strategies for Repeated Games

- **Policymaking**: A strategy that determines the actions of a player for a given period.
- **Signaling**: A strategy that conveys information to other players about one's intentions or actions.

### Important Concepts

- **Unilateral cooperation**: A situation where one player cooperates with another player despite the latter's lack of cooperation.
- **Tit-for-tat strategy**: A strategy where a player imitates the previous action of the other player, with the option to deviate if the other player deviates.
