# Nash Equilibrium Payoffs of an Infinitely Repeated Prisoner's Dilemma

### Definition and Notations

- **Prisoner's Dilemma (PD):** A game played by two individuals, A and B, who can either cooperate (C) or defect (D).
- **Payoff Matrix:**
  | | A Defect (D) | A Cooperate (C) |
  | --- | --- | --- |
  | B Defect (D) | (R, R) | (T, P) |
  | B Cooperate (C) | (P, T) | (S, S) |

### Key Points

- **Nash Equilibrium:** A state where no player can improve their payoff by unilaterally changing their strategy, assuming the other player's strategy remains the same.
- **Infinitely Repeated PD:** The game is played multiple times, with the same players and payoff matrix.
- **Firm Threshold (FT):** The payoff threshold beyond which a player will always defect, regardless of the other player's action.
- **Perfect Deterrence:** A situation where a player is sufficiently confident that the other player will defect if they defect, and cooperate if they cooperate.

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Nash Equilibrium payoff:** The payoff a player can achieve in a Nash equilibrium, given the other player's strategy.
- **Firm Threshold formula:** `FT = (T - S) / (1 - (P / T))`
- **Perfect Deterrence formula:** `FT = (P - R) / (1 - (P / T))`

### Equilibrium Strategies

- **Tit-for-Tat:** A strategy where a player imitates the other player's previous action, with a probability of switching to a different action (e.g., always cooperate, or always defect).
- **Pawnee:** A strategy where a player always defects regardless of the other player's action.
- **Tall Tree:** A strategy where a player always cooperates regardless of the other player's action.

### Equilibrium Payoffs

- **Infinitely Repeated PD Nash Equilibrium:**
  | | A Defect (D) | A Cooperate (C) |
  | --- | --- | --- |
  | B Defect (D) | (T, T) | (T, S) |
  | B Cooperate (C) | (S, T) | (S, S) |

### Key Takeaways

- The infinitely repeated Prisoner's dilemma has multiple Nash equilibria, depending on the strategies used.
- The firm threshold and perfect deterrence concepts are crucial in understanding the dynamics of the game.
- Tit-for-tat and other cooperative strategies can lead to higher payoffs for both players, but may not guarantee a Nash equilibrium.
