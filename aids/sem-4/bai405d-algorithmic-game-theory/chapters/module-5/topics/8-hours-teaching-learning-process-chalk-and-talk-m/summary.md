# **Teaching-Learning Process: Chalk and Talk Method / PowerPoint Presentation**

### Overview

- The chalk and talk method and PowerPoint presentation are traditional teaching methods used to convey information to students.
- Both methods aim to clarify complex concepts through a lecturer's explanation and demonstration.

### Key Points - Chalk and Talk Method

- **Definition:** A teaching method where the lecturer writes on the blackboard or whiteboard and explains the concept to the students.
- **Advantages:**
  - Allows for immediate feedback and clarification.
  - Facilitates discussion and Q&A.
  - Can be used for small groups or classes.
- **Disadvantages:**
  - Limited to the lecturer's handwriting and whiteboard space.
  - May not be suitable for large classes.

### Key Points - PowerPoint Presentation

- **Definition:** A teaching method where a PowerPoint presentation is used to display slides containing text, images, and diagrams.
- **Advantages:**
  - Visual aids help to illustrate complex concepts.
  - Can be easily copied and shared.
  - Allows for additional resources and multimedia content.
- **Disadvantages:**
  - Requires technical expertise to use effectively.
  - May lead to distractions if not used correctly.

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Definition of a Game:** A mathematical model of a situation where the outcome depends on the actions of the players and their interactions.
- **Game Theory:** The study of mathematical models of strategic interactions among rational decision-makers.

### Revision Tips

- Review key concepts and definitions.
- Practice using the chalk and talk method and PowerPoint presentation to clarify complex concepts.
- Focus on understanding the advantages and disadvantages of each method.
