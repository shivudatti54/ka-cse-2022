### Learning Purpose: Sequential Compactness

1.  **Why is this topic important?**
    Sequential compactness is a fundamental concept in analysis and topology because it provides a powerful and often more intuitive criterion for compactness in metric spaces. Many deep results in analysis, such as the Extreme Value Theorem, rely on compactness, and the sequential version is frequently the most practical tool for proving them.

2.  **What will students learn?**
    Students will learn the definition of a sequentially compact set and understand that in metric spaces, sequential compactness is equivalent to the topological definition of compactness (Heine-Borel property). They will develop the skill to prove that a set is compact by showing every sequence has a convergent subsequence, and to apply this technique to solve problems.

3.  **How does it connect to other concepts?**
    This topic directly connects to and unifies previous concepts: completeness (Cauchy sequences), closed sets (limits of sequences), and the Bolzano-Weierstrass theorem in Euclidean space, which is a special case. It is a crucial precursor to studying continuous functions on compact sets and more advanced topics like functional analysis.

4.  **Real-world applications**
    The principle is used in optimization problems to guarantee the existence of a maximum or minimum (e.g., finding an optimal solution). It also underpins numerical methods where a sequence of approximations is generated, and one must show a subsequence converges to a solution.
