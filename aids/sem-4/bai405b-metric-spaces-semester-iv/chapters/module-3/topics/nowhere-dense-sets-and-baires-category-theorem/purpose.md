### Learning Purpose: Nowhere Dense Sets and Baire’s Category Theorem

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is fundamental in functional analysis and provides a powerful tool for distinguishing between "small" and "large" sets in a topological sense. Baire’s Category Theorem is a cornerstone result with profound implications across analysis, proving that complete metric spaces are not "small" and cannot be decomposed into a countable union of nowhere dense sets.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will formally define nowhere dense sets and sets of first/second category. They will understand the statement and proof of Baire’s Theorem. Crucially, they will learn to apply this theorem to prove foundational results, such as the existence of continuous, nowhere-differentiable functions and the principle of uniform boundedness.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This concept deeply connects to the earlier study of completeness, compactness, and continuity. It provides the topological framework for key proofs in functional analysis (e.g., the Open Mapping Theorem) and serves as a prerequisite for more advanced topics like Banach Space theory and the study of Polish spaces.

**4. Real-world applications**
While abstract, the theorem's logic is applied in approximation theory, the analysis of pathological functions, and even in some economic models and computer science (domain theory) to reason about convergence and the properties of spaces.
