### Exterior and Boundary Points in Metric Spaces

**1. Importance:**  
This topic is fundamental for developing a rigorous understanding of spatial relationships within a set and its environment. It provides the precise language needed to describe whether points are isolated from, on the edge of, or inside a set, which is crucial for analysis and topology.

**2. Student Learning:**  
Students will learn to formally define and identify the boundary (∂A) and exterior (Ext A) of a subset A in a metric space. They will understand that the exterior is the interior of the complement, and the boundary consists of points that are neither strictly inside nor outside A. This includes mastering the associated notations and proofs.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts:**  
This knowledge directly builds upon the prior concepts of open/closed sets, interior points, and closure. It is a essential prerequisite for understanding more advanced ideas like continuity (e.g., continuous functions can change dramatically at boundary points), connectedness, and compactness.

**4. Real-World Applications:**  
In geospatial analysis, boundary points define the precise edges of a country or a ecosystem. In computer graphics and image processing, algorithms use these concepts to detect object boundaries. In optimization, knowing the boundary of a feasible region is critical for finding optimal solutions.
