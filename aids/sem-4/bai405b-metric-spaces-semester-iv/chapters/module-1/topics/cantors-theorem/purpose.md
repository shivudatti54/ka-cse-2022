### Learning Purpose: Cantor's Theorem

**1. Importance:**
Cantor’s Theorem is a foundational result in set theory and analysis, establishing that a set's power set has a strictly greater cardinality than the set itself. This proves the existence of different "sizes" of infinity and is crucial for understanding the structure of real numbers and the concept of uncountability.

**2. Student Learning:**
Students will learn the formal statement and proof of Cantor’s Theorem, primarily through his celebrated diagonalization argument. They will develop the ability to distinguish between countable and uncountable sets and will understand why the cardinality of the continuum (like the set of real numbers) is greater than that of the natural numbers.

**3. Connections:**
This theorem is directly connected to earlier topics like set cardinality, power sets, and countable infinity. It underpins more advanced concepts in real analysis (e.g., the uncountability of $\mathbb{R}$) and serves as a precursor to the study of cardinal arithmetic, Gödel’s incompleteness theorems, and computability theory.

**4. Applications:**
While abstract, the diagonalization technique is a powerful tool with real-world applications in computer science, particularly in proving limits of computation (e.g., the undecidability of the Halting Problem). It also has philosophical implications for the nature of information and mathematical reasoning.
