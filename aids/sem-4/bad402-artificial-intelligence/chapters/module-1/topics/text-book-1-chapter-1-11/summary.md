## Artificial Intelligence

### Intelligent Agents: Agents and Environments

#### Key Points

- **Definition of Artificial Intelligence (AI)**:
  - Branch of computer science focusing on creating intelligent machines
  - Goal: Create machines that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence
- **Types of Intelligence**:
  - Narrow or Weak AI: Designed to perform a specific task
  - General or Strong AI: Designed to perform any intellectual task
- **Intelligent Agents**:
  - Autonomous entities that perceive their environment and take actions
  - Can be simple (e.g., decision-making) or complex (e.g., learning and reasoning)
- **Agents and Environments**:
  - **Agents**: The entity that perceives and interacts with the environment
  - **Environments**: The external world that the agent interacts with
  - **State**: The current situation or status of the environment
  - **Actions**: The possible actions the agent can take
  - **Reward**: The outcome or consequence of the agent's actions

#### Formulas and Definitions

- **Markov Decision Process (MDP)**:
  - M = (S, A, P, R, γ)
  - M: The MDP
  - S: The set of states
  - A: The set of actions
  - P: The transition probability function
  - R: The reward function
  - γ: The discount factor
- **Expectation**: E[X] = ∑xP(x)
- **Probability**: P(A ∩ B) = P(A)P(B|A)

#### Theorems

- **No Free Lunch Theorem**: No algorithm can perform well on all problems
- **Pac Learning Theorem**: Learning algorithms can be PAC-learnable

#### Important Concepts

- **Rational Agents**: Agents that make decisions based on rationality
- **Environmental Models**: Models of the environment that agents can use to make decisions
- **Planning**: The process of generating a sequence of actions to achieve a goal
