# **Textbook 1: Chapter 1- 1.1: Introduction to Artificial Intelligence**

## **What is Artificial Intelligence?**

Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the development of computer systems that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as:

- Learning
- Problem-solving
- Reasoning
- Perception
- Language understanding

## **Foundations of Artificial Intelligence**

AI research has its roots in the 1950s and 1960s, when computer scientists such as Alan Turing, Marvin Minsky, and John McCarthy began exploring the possibilities of creating intelligent machines.

## **Key Milestones in the History of AI**

- **1950:** Alan Turing publishes "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," a paper that proposes the Turing Test, a measure of a machine's ability to exhibit intelligent behavior equivalent to, or indistinguishable from, that of a human.
- **1956:** The Dartmouth Summer Research Project on Artificial Intelligence is established, marking the birth of AI as a field of research.
- **1960s:** AI research focuses on rule-based systems, expert systems, and machine learning.

## **Intelligent Agents: Agents and Intelligent Systems**

An **agent** is a computer program that perceives its environment and takes actions to achieve a goal. Agents can be thought of as autonomous systems that operate independently, making decisions based on their own criteria.

## **Types of Intelligent Agents**

- **Simple Reflex Agents:** These agents respond to stimuli with pre-programmed actions.
- **Model-Based Reflex Agents:** These agents use a model of their environment to make decisions.
- **Utility-Based Agents:** These agents use a utility function to evaluate options and make decisions.

**Key Concepts:**

- **Autonomy:** The ability of an agent to operate independently.
- **Perception:** The ability of an agent to sense its environment.
- **Action:** The ability of an agent to take control of its environment.

**Example:**
Consider a simple thermostat that maintains a set temperature. This thermostat can be thought of as an intelligent agent that perceives its environment (the room temperature), takes actions (turns the heating or cooling on/off), and achieves a goal (maintaining a set temperature).
