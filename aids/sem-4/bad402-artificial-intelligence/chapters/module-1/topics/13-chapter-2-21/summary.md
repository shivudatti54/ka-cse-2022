# **1.3 Chapter 2- 2.1 Revision Notes: Artificial Intelligence**

### 1.3 Intelligent Agents

- **Definition:** An intelligent agent is a computer system that perceives its environment and acts upon it to achieve a specific goal.
- **Key Characteristics:**
  - Autonomy: Ability to make decisions independently.
  - Reactivity: Ability to respond to changes in the environment.
  - Proactivity: Ability to take proactive actions to achieve goals.
  - Learning: Ability to improve performance over time.
- **Types of Intelligence:**
  - Narrow or Weak AI: Designed to perform a specific task.
  - General or Strong AI: Designed to perform any intellectual task.

### 2.1 Types of Intelligent Agents

- **Simple Reflex Agents:**
  - Respond to the current state of the environment.
  - Use a predefined set of rules to determine actions.
- **Model-Based Reflex Agents:**
  - Use a model of the environment to make decisions.
  - Consider the consequences of actions before taking them.
- **Planning Agents:**
  - Use a planning algorithm to determine the best course of action.
  - Consider multiple goals and constraints.

### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Artificial Life (ALife) Definition:** A field of study that seeks to create systems that exhibit life-like behavior.
- **Kolmogorov Complexity:** A measure of the complexity of a system.
- **MDP (Markov Decision Process) Definition:** A mathematical model of decision-making.
- **Q-Function:** A mathematical function used to represent the expected return of an action.

### Important Theorems

- **Bellman's Equation:** A mathematical equation used to solve MDPs.
- **Optimality Principle:** A fundamental principle in decision-making theory.
