### Semester-End Examination: Introduction to Group Theory

1.  **Why is this topic important?**
    This examination assesses a student's grasp of group theory, the foundational language of symmetry and algebraic structure. Proficiency here is critical for advanced studies in mathematics, computer science (e.g., cryptography, coding theory), and physics.

2.  **What will students learn?**
    Students will demonstrate their ability to apply core concepts. This includes proving whether a set and operation form a group, determining if a mapping is a homomorphism or isomorphism, finding subgroups (like cyclic subgroups), and computing cosets.

3.  **How does it connect to other concepts?**
    The exam synthesizes knowledge from across the module. It connects the definition of a group to earlier concepts like sets and operations. It also links homomorphisms to function properties and builds on number theory through concepts like the order of an element.

4.  **Real-world applications**
    The principles tested have direct applications. Group theory underpins modern cryptography (e.g., RSA algorithm), the design of error-correcting codes for reliable data transmission, and the study of molecular symmetry in chemistry.
