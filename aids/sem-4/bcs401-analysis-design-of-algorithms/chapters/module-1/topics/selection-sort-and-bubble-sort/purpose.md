# Learning Purpose: Selection Sort & Bubble Sort

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is foundational because it introduces students to fundamental sorting algorithms, which are a core concept in computer science. Understanding these simple, "brute-force" methods is a critical first step towards grasping more complex and efficient algorithms. They provide a clear, tangible introduction to core programming concepts like nested loops, array manipulation, and algorithm analysis.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the step-by-step mechanics of how Selection Sort and Bubble Sort operate. They will implement these algorithms, analyze their time and space complexity (noting that both are O(n²) in the worst and average cases), and compare them to identify key differences in their approach to sorting (e.g., swapping vs. selecting). This builds a practical understanding of algorithm correctness and efficiency.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
These algorithms serve as a baseline for comparing the efficiency of more advanced sorting techniques like Merge Sort and Quick Sort, which will be covered later. They illustrate the trade-off between simplicity and performance. The analysis of these algorithms also provides a direct application of asymptotic (Big O) notation, a key tool for evaluating algorithm efficiency.

**4. Real-world applications**
While inefficient for large datasets, their simplicity makes them practical for sorting small lists or lists that are already nearly sorted. They are also used in educational contexts, embedded systems with limited resources, and as a sub-procedure in more complex algorithms where the data size is guaranteed to be small.
