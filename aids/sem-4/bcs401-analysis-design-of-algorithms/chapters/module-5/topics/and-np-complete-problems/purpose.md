# Learning Objectives
After studying this topic, you should be able to:
1.  Define and distinguish between the complexity classes P, NP, NP-Hard, and NP-Complete using precise terminology.
2.  Explain the concept of polynomial-time reduction and its critical role in comparing the difficulty of computational problems.
3.  Describe the two-step process (verification and reduction) required to prove a problem is NP-Complete.
4.  State the Cook-Levin theorem and explain its fundamental importance in the theory of NP-Completeness.
5.  Identify and describe at least five classic examples of NP-Complete problems from different domains (e.g., logic, graph theory).
6.  Discuss the practical implications of NP-Completeness and common strategies used to address these problems when they arise in real-world applications.
