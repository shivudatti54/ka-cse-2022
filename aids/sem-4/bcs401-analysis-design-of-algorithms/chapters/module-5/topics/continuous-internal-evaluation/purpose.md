### Learning Purpose: Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE)

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Continuous Internal Evaluation is crucial as it shifts the focus from a single high-stakes exam to a more comprehensive assessment of a student's understanding throughout the module. In a complex subject like algorithm analysis, this allows for consistent feedback, helping students identify and address gaps in their knowledge of time/space complexity, design strategies, and problem-solving techniques early and often.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to consistently apply core algorithmic concepts through various CIE formats like quizzes, assignments, and mini-projects. They will develop the ability to critically analyze problems, select appropriate design paradigms (e.g., Divide & Conquer, Greedy methods), and evaluate algorithmic efficiency in a sustained manner, reinforcing a deeper and more practical understanding.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
CIE directly connects and integrates all prior modules. It requires students to synthesize knowledge of data structures (Module 2), core algorithm design techniques (Modules 3 & 4), and computational complexity (Module 1) to solve multifaceted problems, ensuring they see the subject as a cohesive whole rather than isolated topics.

**4. Real-world applications**
This process mirrors the agile, iterative development cycles used in software engineering. Regularly designing, analyzing, and refining solutions prepares students for real-world scenarios where code performance is continuously evaluated through code reviews, unit testing, and profiling, making them adept at building efficient software systems.
