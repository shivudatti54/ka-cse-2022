**Topic Overview: 2.1 to 2.6 - Database Management System**

**Why this topic matters:**
Understanding database management system concepts, including data normalization, data integrity, data security, database design, and database administration, is crucial for designing and maintaining efficient and reliable databases that support business operations. Mastering these concepts enables individuals to effectively manage and analyze data, ensuring data accuracy, consistency, and accessibility.

**Real-world applications:**
In the real world, database management system concepts have numerous applications in various industries, such as financial institutions, healthcare organizations, and e-commerce platforms. For instance, a bank may use normalized database tables to store customer information, account balances, and transaction records, while a hospital may use database management systems to store patient medical records and treatment plans.

**Connection to other concepts:**
This topic is closely connected to other database management system concepts, such as data modeling, database modeling, and database design. Understanding these concepts enables individuals to create robust and scalable database designs that support business requirements, while also integrating with other database management system concepts, such as data migration, data backup, and data recovery.
