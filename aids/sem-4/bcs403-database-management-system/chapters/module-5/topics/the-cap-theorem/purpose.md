# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the three properties of the CAP Theorem: Consistency, Availability, and Partition Tolerance, with precise definitions relevant to distributed systems.

2. Analyze why a distributed database system cannot simultaneously provide all three CAP properties, particularly during network partition events.

3. Categorize major distributed database systems (Dynamo, BigTable, Cassandra, MongoDB, PostgreSQL) into CA, CP, and AP system types based on their design choices.

4. Evaluate trade-offs between consistency and availability in the context of specific application requirements, demonstrating how different use cases demand different CAP configurations.

5. Compare the CAP Theorem with the PACELC extension and explain how latency-consistency trade-offs exist even in the absence of network partitions.

6. Design database selection strategies for different components of an application by applying CAP Theorem principles and understanding eventual consistency implications.
