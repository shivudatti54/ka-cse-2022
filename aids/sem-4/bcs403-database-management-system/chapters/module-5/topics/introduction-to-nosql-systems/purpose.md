### Learning Purpose: Introduction to NoSQL Systems

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because the exponential growth of unstructured and semi-structured data from web applications, social media, and IoT devices has challenged the limitations of traditional relational databases. Understanding NoSQL systems is essential for designing modern, scalable, and high-performance applications that can handle massive volumes of diverse data.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the fundamental concepts, types, and characteristics of NoSQL databases. This includes exploring key-value, document, column-family, and graph databases. They will understand the CAP theorem, the principles of horizontal scaling, and how the schema-less data model differs from the rigid structure of SQL.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly contrasts with previous modules on relational models (SQL), helping students understand the trade-offs between ACID compliance and BASE principles. It connects to concepts of data modeling, distributed systems, and is a foundation for learning about Big Data technologies like Hadoop and MongoDB.

**4. Real-world applications**
NoSQL databases are the backbone of many real-world applications. They power social media feeds (e.g., Facebook, Twitter), manage large-scale e-commerce product catalogs (e.g., Amazon), enable real-time analytics, and store user data in mobile apps, showcasing their critical role in the technology landscape.
