# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1.  Define unification and explain its critical role in First Order Logic inference.
2.  Execute the unification algorithm step-by-step on a pair of logical expressions, correctly applying substitutions and the occurs check.
3.  Identify whether two given expressions are unifiable and, if they are, state their Most General Unifier (MGU).
4.  Explain the consequence of omitting the occurs check in the unification algorithm.
5.  Differentiate between the simple matching used in Propositional Logic and the more powerful matching enabled by unification in First Order Logic.
6.  Describe how unification is utilized in both forward chaining and backward chaining inference methods.
