**1. Why is this topic important?**
Domain knowledge is the specific, expert information a system needs to reason about a particular problem area (e.g., medical diagnosis, financial planning). Without a formal way to represent this knowledge, an AI system cannot understand context, make informed decisions, or explain its reasoning. This topic is fundamental to moving AI beyond simple pattern matching to creating true expert systems.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the primary methods for formally representing domain knowledge, such as logical representations, semantic networks, frames, and ontologies. They will understand how to use inference engines to reason with this knowledge, drawing new conclusions from established facts. This includes practical skills in creating knowledge bases and understanding the difference between declarative and procedural knowledge.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds upon earlier studies in logic (Propositional and First-Order) and search algorithms. It provides the crucial "knowledge" component for knowledge-based agents and is a prerequisite for advanced fields like Natural Language Processing (which requires world knowledge) and the Semantic Web. It also connects to machine learning, where learned models can be integrated into a knowledge base.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied in expert systems for medical diagnosis (e.g., identifying diseases from symptoms), customer service chatbots that understand product specifics, recommendation systems that leverage user preferences, and legal software that applies case law rules to new situations.
