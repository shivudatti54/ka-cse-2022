### Learning Purpose: Quantifying in AI

**1. Importance:**  
This topic is foundational for transitioning AI from theoretical concepts to measurable, practical systems. Quantifying performance, uncertainty, and data characteristics is crucial for evaluating model efficacy, ensuring fairness, and making reliable decisions in real-world applications.

**2. Student Learning:**  
Students will learn key metrics and methods to measure AI performance, such as accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score for classification, and MSE or MAE for regression. They will also explore quantifying uncertainty through probabilities and confidence intervals, and techniques for assessing data quality and model robustness.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts:**  
This module directly builds on prior knowledge of machine learning algorithms (Module 3 & 4) and predictive modeling. It provides the essential tools needed to evaluate and compare different models, a skill critical for understanding advanced topics like optimization (hyperparameter tuning) and ethical AI (e.g., quantifying bias).

**4. Real-World Applications:**  
Quantifying is used everywhere: from calculating the accuracy of a medical diagnosis model and the error rate of a self-driving car's perception system to measuring fairness in automated hiring tools and the confidence of a financial fraud detection algorithm.
