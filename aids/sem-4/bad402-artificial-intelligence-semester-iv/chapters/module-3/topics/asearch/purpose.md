# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. DEFINE the evaluation function f(n) = g(n) + h(n) and explain what each component represents in the context of A* Search.

2. EXPLAIN the concept of admissibility in heuristic functions and describe why it is necessary for optimality in A* Search.

3. DISTINGUISH between admissibility and consistency (monotonicity) of heuristic functions with appropriate examples.

4. ANALYZE why A* Search is complete and optimal when used with an admissible heuristic function.

5. COMPUTE f(n), g(n), and h(n) values for given states in problems like the 8-puzzle or grid pathfinding.

6. EVALUATE whether a given heuristic function is admissible or consistent based on its mathematical properties.

7. DISCUSS the time and space complexity of A* Search and explain why space is typically the limiting factor in practical applications.

8. COMPARE A* Search with Greedy Best-First Search and Dijkstra's algorithm in terms of completeness, optimality, and efficiency.
