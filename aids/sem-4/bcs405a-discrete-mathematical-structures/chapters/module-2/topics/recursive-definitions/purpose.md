# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and explain fundamental graph theory concepts including vertices, edges, order, size, and degree
2. Classify graphs based on their properties (simple, complete, bipartite, regular, etc.)
3. Apply the Handshaking Lemma to solve problems involving vertex degrees and edge counts
4. Represent graphs using both adjacency matrix and adjacency list representations
5. Analyze and compare the space and time complexity of different graph representations
6. Identify special vertex types (isolated, pendant, universal) and understand their significance
7. Determine whether a given degree sequence can represent a simple graph
8. Recognize isomorphic graphs and understand the concept of graph isomorphism
