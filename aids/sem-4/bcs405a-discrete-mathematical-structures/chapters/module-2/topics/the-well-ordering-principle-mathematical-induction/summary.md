# The Well Ordering Principle – Mathematical Induction

==========================

## Definition and Theorem

---

- **The Well Ordering Principle**: Every non-empty set of positive integers contains a least element.
- **Well-Ordering Principle Theorem**: If a set of positive integers is well-ordered, then it can be put into a one-to-one correspondence with the positive integers.

## Mathematical Induction

---

- **Principle of Mathematical Induction**: A statement is true for all positive integers if:
  1. It is true for 1.
  2. If it is true for some positive integer k, then it is true for k+1.

## Important Formulas and Theorems

---

- **Well-Ordering Principle**: ∅ ∪ {1} ≠ ∅
- **Principle of Mathematical Induction**: P(1) ∀k ∈ ℕ, P(k) → P(k+1)

## Key Points

---

- The Well Ordering Principle is used to prove the validity of mathematical induction.
- Mathematical induction is a method for proving that a statement is true for all positive integers.
- The principle of mathematical induction is based on the Well Ordering Principle.
- The formula for mathematical induction is: P(1) ∀k ∈ ℕ, P(k) → P(k+1)
- The Well Ordering Principle is a fundamental concept in mathematics, used to prove the validity of mathematical induction.

## Important Definitions

---

- **Well-Ordered Set**: A set of positive integers in which every non-empty subset has a least element.
- **One-to-One Correspondence**: A bijective function between two sets.
