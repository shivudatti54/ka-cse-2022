# **Fundamental Principles of Counting: Revision Notes**

## **Introduction**

- Counting is a fundamental concept in mathematics, used to enumerate possible outcomes in various scenarios.
- Understanding the rules of sum and product, permutations, combinations, the binomial theorem, and combinations with repetition is essential for discrete mathematical structures.

## **Rules of Sum and Product**

- **Addition Rule**: If there are m ways to perform the first operation and n ways to perform the second operation, then there are m + n ways to perform both operations.
- **Multiplication Rule**: If there are m ways to perform the first operation and n ways to perform the second operation, then there are m × n ways to perform both operations.

## **Permutations**

- **Definition**: An arrangement of objects in a specific order.
- **Formula**: nPr = n! / (n-r)!
- **Example**: Permutations of 5 objects taken 3 at a time: 5P3 = 5! / (5-3)! = 5 × 4 × 3 = 60

## **Combinations**

- **Definition**: A selection of objects without regard to order.
- **Formula**: nCr = n! / (r!(n-r)!)
- **Example**: Combinations of 5 objects taken 3 at a time: 5C3 = 5! / (3!(5-3)!) = 10

## **The Binomial Theorem**

- **Definition**: A mathematical formula used to expand expressions of the form (a + b)^n.
- **Formula**: (a + b)^n = ∑[n! / (k!(n-k)!)]a^kb^(n-k) for k = 0 to n

## **Combinations with Repetition**

- **Definition**: A selection of objects with repetition allowed.
- **Formula**: nCr = (n+k-1)! / (k!(n-1)!)
- **Example**: Combinations with repetition of 5 objects taken 3 at a time with repetition: 5C(3) = (5+3-1)! / (3!(5-1)!) = 35
