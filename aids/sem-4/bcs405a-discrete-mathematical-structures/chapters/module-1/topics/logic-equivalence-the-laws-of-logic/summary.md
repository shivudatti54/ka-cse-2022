# Logic Equivalence – The Laws of Logic

### Definitions

- **Equivalence**: Two propositions are logically equivalent if they have the same truth value.
- **Logical Equivalence** (∼A ≡ B): A ≡ B, A ⊃ B, A ∧ B, A ∨ B

### Laws of Logic

- **The Law of Identity**:

* A ≡ A (Reflexivity)
* A ≡ 0 (A ≡ False)
* A ≡ 1 (A ≡ True)

- **The Law of Non-Contradiction**:

* A ≡ ¬A (Negation)

- **The Law of Excluded Middle**:

* A ≡ ¬A ∨ A (Disjunction)

- **The Law of Double Negation**:

* ¬¬A ≡ A (Double Negation)

- **The Law of Commutativity**:

* A ≡ B (Associativity)

- **The Law of Distributivity**:

* A ≡ (B ∧ C) (Distributive Law)

- **The Law of Absorption**:

* A ≡ (A ∧ B) (Absorption Law)

### Important Formulas

- De Morgan's Law:

* ¬(A ∧ B) ≡ ¬A ∨ ¬B
* ¬(A ∨ B) ≡ ¬A ∧ ¬B

- The Law of Implication:

* A ⊃ B ≡ ¬A ∨ B

### Theorems

- The Law of Equivalence is a tautology.
- The Law of Non-Contradiction is a tautology.

### Quick Revision Tips

- Understand the definitions of logical equivalence and the laws of logic.
- Practice applying the laws of logic to solve problems.
- Memorize important formulas and theorems.
