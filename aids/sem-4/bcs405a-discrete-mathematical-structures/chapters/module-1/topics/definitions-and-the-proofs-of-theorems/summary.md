# **Definitions and the Proofs of Theorems**

### Definitions

- **Theorem**: A statement that has been rigorously proved to be true.
- **Definition**: A statement that explains the meaning of a term or phrase.
- **Proposition**: A statement that can be either true or false.
- **Proof**: A sequence of arguments that demonstrate the truth of a theorem.
- **Induction**: A method of proof that involves showing a statement is true for a small number of cases, and then assuming it is true for all cases.

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Modus Ponens**: If P, then Q. If P, then Q. Therefore, Q.
- **Modus Tollens**: If P, then Q. If not Q, then not P.
- **Law of Excluded Middle**: P or not P.
- **Peirce's Law**: [P → Q] → [P ∧ Q]
- **The Fundamental Theorem of Algebra**: Every non-constant polynomial has at least one root.

### Key Point

- **Formal Proof**: A step-by-step argument that uses logical rules and definitions to prove a theorem.
- **Informal Proof**: A non-formal argument that uses logical reasoning to prove a theorem.
- **Model**: A representation of a mathematical structure that satisfies the definition of the structure.
- **Proof by Contradiction**: A method of proof that involves assuming the opposite of what is to be proved, and showing that this leads to a contradiction.
