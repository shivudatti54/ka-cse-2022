# Logical Implication – Rules of Inference

### Introduction

Logical implication is a fundamental concept in discrete mathematical structures, particularly in logic. It is a relationship between two statements that implies the truth of one statement when the other is true. In this study material, we will explore the rules of inference related to logical implication.

### Definition

Logical implication, denoted by ⇒ (implies), is a binary relation between two statements A and B, such that:

A ⇒ B is true if and only if either A is false or B is true.

Mathematically, this can be represented as:

A ⇒ B = ¬A ∨ B

where ¬A is the negation of A.

### Rules of Inference

There are several rules of inference related to logical implication. Here are some of the most important ones:

#### 1. Modus Ponens

Modus ponens is a rule of inference that states:

If A ⇒ B and A, then B.

Mathematically, this can be represented as:

1. A ⇒ B
2. A
   ∴ B

Example:

1. If it rains, the streets will be wet. (A ⇒ B)
2. It is raining. (A)
   ∴ The streets will be wet. (B)

#### 2. Modus Tollens

Modus tollens is a rule of inference that states:

If A ⇒ B and ¬B, then ¬A.

Mathematically, this can be represented as:

1. A ⇒ B
2. ¬B
   ∴ ¬A

Example:

1. If it is sunny, then the temperature will be high. (A ⇒ B)
2. The temperature is not high. (¬B)
   ∴ It is not sunny. (¬A)

#### 3. Hypothetical Syllogism

Hypothetical syllogism is a rule of inference that states:

If A ⇒ B and B ⇒ C, then A ⇒ C.

Mathematically, this can be represented as:

1. A ⇒ B
2. B ⇒ C
   ∴ A ⇒ C

Example:

1. If it is cold, then I will wear a jacket. (A ⇒ B)
2. If I wear a jacket, then the temperature will drop. (B ⇒ C)
   ∴ If it is cold, then the temperature will drop. (A ⇒ C)

### Key Concepts

- Modus ponens: A rule of inference that states: If A ⇒ B and A, then B.
- Modus tollens: A rule of inference that states: If A ⇒ B and ¬B, then ¬A.
- Hypothetical syllogism: A rule of inference that states: If A ⇒ B and B ⇒ C, then A ⇒ C.

### Conclusion

Logical implication is a fundamental concept in discrete mathematical structures, particularly in logic. The rules of inference related to logical implication, such as modus ponens, modus tollens, and hypothetical syllogism, are essential in logical reasoning and inference. By understanding these rules, you can improve your ability to reason and make logical conclusions.
