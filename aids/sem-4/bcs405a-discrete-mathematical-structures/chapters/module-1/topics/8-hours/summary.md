### Revision Notes for Fundamentals of Logic: (8 hours)

#### Introduction

- Review of mathematical structures and logical concepts
- Focus on discrete mathematical structures

#### Key Concepts

- **Sets**: A collection of unique elements, denoted as A = {a, b, c}
- **Relations**: A binary relation R between two sets A and B, denoted as R ⊆ A × B
- **Functions**: A relation between two sets A and B, denoted as f: A → B
- **Logic**: A branch of mathematics that deals with reasoning and inference

#### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Set operations**:
  - Union: A ∪ B = {a, b, c} ∪ {d, e, f} = {a, b, c, d, e, f}
  - Intersection: A ∩ B = {a, b, c} ∩ {d, e, f} = {a}
  - Difference: A \ B = {a, b, c} \ {d, e, f} = {a, b, c}
- **Relational algebra**:
  - Selection: σ (A) = A ∩ {a, b}
  - Projection: π (A) = {a, b}
  - Join: A × B
- **Function notation**:
  - f (x) = {a, b}
- **Propositional logic**:
  - ∧ (conjunction)
  - ∨ (disjunction)
  - ¬ (negation)

#### Important Theorems

- **De Morgan's laws**:
  - ¬ (A ∩ B) = ¬ A ∨ ¬ B
  - ¬ (A ∪ B) = ¬ A ∧ ¬ B
- **Distributive laws**:
  - A ∧ (B ∨ C) = (A ∧ B) ∨ (A ∧ C)
  - A ∨ (B ∧ C) = (A ∨ B) ∧ (A ∨ C)
