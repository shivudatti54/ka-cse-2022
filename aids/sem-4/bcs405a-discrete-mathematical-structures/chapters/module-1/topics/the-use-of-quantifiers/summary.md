# Quantifiers in Discrete Mathematical Structures

### Definitions

- **Quantifier**: A symbol or word that indicates the scope of a variable or expression in a mathematical statement.
- **Universal Quantifier** (∀): For all elements in a set or domain.
- **Existential Quantifier** (∃): There exists at least one element in a set or domain.

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Universal Quantification**:
  - ∀x P(x) : P(x) is true for all elements x in the domain.
- **Existential Quantification**:
  - ∃x P(x) : There exists at least one element x in the domain such that P(x) is true.
- **Quantifier Negation**:
  - ¬∀x P(x) ⇔ ∃x ¬P(x)
  - ¬∃x P(x) ⇔ ∀x ¬P(x)

### Theorems and Laws

- **Law of Excluded Middle**:
  - ∀x (P(x) ∨ ¬P(x))
- **Law of Contrapositive**:
  - ∀x (P(x) → Q(x)) ⇔ ∀x (¬Q(x) → ¬P(x))

### Important Theorems

- **Löwenheim-Skolem Theorem**:
  - If a first-order theory has a model of some infinite cardinality, then it has models of all infinite cardinalities.
- **Compactness Theorem**:
  - A set of first-order sentences has a model if and only if every finite subset of it has a model.

## Key Points for Revision

- Understand the definitions and scope of universal and existential quantifiers.
- Familiarize yourself with formulas, theorems, and laws related to quantifiers.
- Review theorems and laws, such as the Law of Excluded Middle and the Law of Contrapositive.
- Practice applying quantifiers to solve problems and prove statements.
