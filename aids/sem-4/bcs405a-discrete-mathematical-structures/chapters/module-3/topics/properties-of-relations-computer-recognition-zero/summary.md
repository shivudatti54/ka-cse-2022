# **Properties of Relations, Computer Recognition – Zero-One Matrices and Directed Graphs, Partial Orders – Hasse Diagrams, Equivalence Relations and Part**

## **Definitions and Formulas**

- **Relation**: A subset of a Cartesian product of two sets A and B, denoted as R ⊆ A × B.
- **Zero-One Matrix**: A matrix with elements that are either 0 or 1, used to represent relations.
- **Directed Graph**: A graph where each edge has a direction, representing a relation.
- **Partial Order**: A binary relation that is reflexive, antisymmetric, and transitive.
- **Equivalence Relation**: A binary relation that is reflexive, symmetric, and transitive.

## **Properties and Theorems**

- **Reflexive Property**: For all a in A, (a, a) ∈ R.
- **Antisymmetric Property**: For all a, b in A, if (a, b) ∈ R and (b, a) ∈ R, then a = b.
- **Transitive Property**: For all a, b, c in A, if (a, b) ∈ R and (b, c) ∈ R, then (a, c) ∈ R.
- **Hasse Diagram**: A graphical representation of a partial order, where elements are connected by edges if one is less than or equal to the other.
- **Equivalence Classes**: A set of elements that are equivalent to each other under an equivalence relation.
- **Pigeonhole Principle**: If n items are put into m containers, with n > m, then at least one container must contain more than one item.

## **Computer Recognition**

- **Zero-One Matrix Representation**: Relations can be represented as matrices, where 1 indicates the presence of an edge and 0 indicates its absence.
- **Directed Graph Representation**: Relations can be represented as directed graphs, where edges represent the presence of a relation between elements.
