# **Cartesian Products and Relations**

## **Definition**

- Cartesian Product of two sets A and B is denoted as A × B and is defined as:
  - A × B = {(a, b) | a ∈ A, b ∈ B}
- Cartesian Product of multiple sets can be defined recursively as:
  - A × B × C = (A × B) × C

## **Properties**

- Commutative Property: (a, b) ∈ A × B if and only if (b, a) ∈ A × B
- Associative Property: (a, (b, c)) ∈ A × B × C if and only if ((a, b), c) ∈ A × B × C
- Identity Element: ∅ × A = A × ∅ = ∅

## **Important Formulas**

- Cartesian Product of two sets:
  - |A × B| = |A| × |B|
- Cartesian Product of three sets:
  - |A × B × C| = |A × B| × |C|

## **Theorems**

- **De Morgan's Law for Cartesian Product**:
  - (∪A ∩B) = A × B (if A and B are disjoint sets)
- **Cartesian Product and Intersection**:
  - A × B ⊆ A ∩ B

## **Important Definitions**

- **Relation**:
  - A subset of the Cartesian product of two sets, i.e., R ⊆ A × B
- **Equivalence Relation**:
  - A relation R on a set A is said to be equivalence if it is reflexive, symmetric, and transitive.
