# Learning Objectives
After studying this topic, you should be able to:
1.  Define the Gradient Descent algorithm and explain its core intuition of following the steepest descent.
2.  Derive and apply the fundamental parameter update rule for Gradient Descent.
3.  Analyze the critical role of the learning rate hyperparameter and the consequences of choosing a value that is too small or too large.
4.  Compare and contrast the three main variants of Gradient Descent: Batch, Stochastic, and Mini-Batch, including their trade-offs in terms of computation, speed, and convergence.
5.  Implement a simple version of Gradient Descent (e.g., for a quadratic function) and track its progress over iterations.
6.  Explain common challenges like local minima and oscillation, and describe how enhancements like momentum help to mitigate them.
