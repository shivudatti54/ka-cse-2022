### Learning Purpose: N. Parikh

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial as it introduces students to the advanced optimization frameworks developed by N. Parikh, which are fundamental for solving large-scale, complex, and non-differentiable problems often encountered in modern data science, machine learning, and engineering. Understanding these techniques is key to moving beyond traditional gradient-based methods.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the mathematical foundations of proximal algorithms, splitting methods, and the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM). They will gain the ability to decompose complex optimization problems into simpler sub-problems and implement efficient numerical solutions for non-smooth objectives and constrained optimization.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly upon knowledge of convex optimization, linear algebra, and calculus from earlier modules. It connects deeply to machine learning (e.g., training sparse models like LASSO), signal processing (e.g., image denoising), and provides the advanced numerical tools needed to implement algorithms learned in parallel computing courses.

**4. Real-world applications**
Parikh's optimization techniques are widely applied in real-time image and video processing (e.g., MRI reconstruction), large-scale statistical data analysis (e.g., network analysis), distributed optimization in sensor networks, and training regularized regression models in artificial intelligence.
