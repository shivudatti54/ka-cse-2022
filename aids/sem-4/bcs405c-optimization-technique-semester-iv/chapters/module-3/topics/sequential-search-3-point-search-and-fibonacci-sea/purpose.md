### Learning Purpose: Sequential Search Methods (3-Point & Fibonacci)

**1. Importance:** This topic is fundamental as it introduces deterministic, single-variable search techniques that form the basis for more complex optimization algorithms. Understanding these methods is crucial for solving engineering design, economic modeling, and resource allocation problems where finding an optimum efficiently is key.

**2. Learning Outcomes:** Students will learn the underlying principles, iterative procedures, and termination criteria for both 3-point (equal interval) and Fibonacci search methods. They will be able to implement these algorithms to find the minimum of a unimodal function, compare their relative efficiency (number of function evaluations required), and understand the optimal nature of the Fibonacci search.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts:** This builds directly upon the concept of unimodality introduced earlier. It provides a concrete foundation for understanding more advanced multivariable and stochastic search techniques. The efficiency calculations (reduction ratio) also link to broader algorithmic complexity analysis.

**4. Real-World Applications:** These methods are applied wherever a process requires finding an optimal parameter value, such as minimizing production costs, optimizing the tensile strength of an alloy by determining ideal heat treatment times, or calibrating machinery for maximum efficiency.
