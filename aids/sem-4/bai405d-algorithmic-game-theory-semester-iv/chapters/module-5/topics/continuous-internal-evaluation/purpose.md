### Continuous Internal Evaluation in Algorithmic Game Theory

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) is crucial as it shifts assessment from a single high-stakes exam to an ongoing process. This allows for consistent monitoring of student understanding in a complex subject like Algorithmic Game Theory (AGT), where concepts build progressively. It encourages consistent engagement and provides timely feedback, enabling students to address knowledge gaps before moving to advanced topics.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to apply AGT concepts—like Nash equilibria, mechanism design, and price of anarchy—through regular, varied assessments. These may include quizzes, problem sets, coding assignments simulating games, and case study analyses. The process hones their ability to formulate strategic interactions mathematically, analyze outcomes, and computationally model game-theoretic scenarios.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
CIE directly connects and reinforces core module concepts. For instance, a problem set on auction theory applies optimization from Module 2. A coding assignment to simulate a routing game requires understanding equilibria (Module 1) and algorithms (Module 3). This iterative application strengthens the interdependence of theoretical foundations, algorithmic implementation, and economic reasoning.

**4. Real-world applications**
The skills developed are directly applicable to real-world systems. Analyzing continuous assessments mirrors how tech companies A/B test platform designs or how economists evaluate market mechanisms. Students learn to iteratively critique and improve systems, a vital skill for careers in digital market design, algorithmic trading, network management, and platform economics.
