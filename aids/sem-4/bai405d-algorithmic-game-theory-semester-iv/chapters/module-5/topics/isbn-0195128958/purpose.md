### Learning Purpose: Module 5 - Algorithmic Game Theory (ISBN: 0195128958)

1.  **Why is this topic important?**
    This topic is crucial because it provides the formal framework for analyzing and designing algorithms in multi-agent, self-interested environments. It bridges the gap between theoretical computer science and economics, offering tools to understand and predict the behavior of modern digital systems like online markets, social networks, and routing networks, where participants act strategically to maximize their own utility.

2.  **What will students learn?**
    Students will learn the core concepts of game theory—such as Nash Equilibrium—from a computational perspective. They will analyze the complexity of finding equilibria, study the price of anarchy (the cost of decentralization), and design incentive-compatible mechanisms (e.g., auctions) that lead to desirable outcomes despite selfish behavior.

3.  **How does it connect to other concepts?**
    This module builds directly on foundational knowledge from **Algorithm Design & Analysis** (complexity) and **Probability/Statistics**. It connects economic principles from **Microeconomics** to computational problems and uses optimization techniques from **Linear Programming** to find solutions and characterize equilibria.

4.  **Real-world applications**
    The principles learned are directly applied to the design of online ad auctions (e.g., Google, Facebook), peer-to-peer and routing networks, cryptocurrency and blockchain mechanism design, and resource allocation in cloud computing platforms.
