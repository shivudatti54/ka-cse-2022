**Learning Purpose: Strictly Competitive Games and Maximization**

**1. Importance**
This topic is fundamental as it introduces a cornerstone of strategic interaction: scenarios of pure conflict. Understanding strictly competitive (zero-sum) games provides the critical theoretical foundation for analyzing situations where one agent's gain is always another's loss, a common dynamic in many competitive environments.

**2. Student Learning**
Students will learn to formally define and identify strictly competitive games. They will master the concept of maximin strategies and security levels, understanding how a rational player can guarantee their best possible worst-case outcome. Crucially, they will learn to connect these concepts to the Minimax Theorem, which establishes the existence of a stable solution (a saddle point) in such games.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts**
This module directly builds on the knowledge of normal-form games, dominant strategies, and Nash Equilibrium from earlier modules. It provides a vital contrast to later topics like cooperative and general-sum games, highlighting how solutions and strategic reasoning differ when mutual gain is impossible.

**4. Real-World Applications**
The principles are applied in auction theory, cryptography, and national security. They are also extensively used in AI for training adversarial networks (GANs) and for developing robust strategies in competitive environments like poker or real-time strategy games.
