**Learning Purpose: Illustrations in Algorithmic Game Theory**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Illustrations are crucial for moving from abstract theoretical concepts to tangible understanding. They provide concrete examples of how game-theoretic models and algorithmic solutions operate in practice, solidifying the core principles of equilibria, mechanism design, and computational complexity.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to analyze and deconstruct canonical examples, such as the Fisher market model, bandwidth allocation auctions, and simple sponsored search auctions. They will apply solution concepts like Nash Equilibrium and compute outcomes for specific mechanisms, developing the ability to model real-world strategic interactions mathematically.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly applies foundational concepts from Modules 1 and 2, including normal-form games, equilibria analysis, and algorithmic mechanism design. It demonstrates how theoretical guarantees (e.g., price of anarchy, incentive compatibility) manifest in specific scenarios, bridging the gap between pure theory and algorithm implementation.

**4. Real-world applications**
The illustrations mirror applications in online advertising (e.g., Google's ad auction), networked resource allocation (e.g., bandwidth sharing), and electronic commerce. Understanding these models is essential for designing and critiquing the automated systems that underpin modern digital marketplaces and platforms.
