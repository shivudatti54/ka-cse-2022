### **Learning Purpose: Backward Induction**

1.  **Why is this topic important?**
    Backward induction is the fundamental algorithm for solving sequential games of perfect information. It is crucial because it provides a systematic method for predicting rational outcomes, moving beyond simple one-move games to complex, multi-stage strategic interactions.

2.  **What will students learn?**
    Students will learn to solve finite sequential games by reasoning backwards from the end of the game tree to its beginning. They will identify subgame perfect Nash equilibria (SPNE), the set of strategies that are rational at every possible point in the game, eliminating non-credible threats.

3.  **How does it connect to other concepts?**
    This concept builds directly on game trees, strategies, and Nash equilibrium. It is the practical application of the principle of **subgame perfection**. It also connects to and contrasts with concepts for solving simultaneous games (like the Nash equilibrium concept for normal form) and games with imperfect information.

4.  **Real-world applications**
    Backward induction is applied in economics (e.g., analyzing market entry), political science (e.g., legislative bargaining), computer science (for AI in games like chess), and business strategy (e.g., planning multi-stage projects or negotiations).
