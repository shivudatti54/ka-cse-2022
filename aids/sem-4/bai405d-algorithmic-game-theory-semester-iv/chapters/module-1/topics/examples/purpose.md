# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Identify and construct payoff matrices for two-player strategic games in normal form

2. Determine pure strategy Nash equilibria by finding mutual best responses in payoff matrices

3. Calculate mixed strategy Nash equilibria using the indifference principle for zero-sum and general sum games

4. Derive best response functions for continuous strategy games like Cournot competition

5. Analyze the Battle of the Sexes, Stag Hunt, and Chicken games as canonical examples of coordination and conflict

6. Explain why multiple Nash equilibria exist and discuss criteria for equilibrium selection

7. Apply game-theoretic reasoning to economic contexts including oligopoly models

8. Distinguish between dominant strategies, dominated strategies, and Nash equilibrium concepts
