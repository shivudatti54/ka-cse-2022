### Learning Purpose: Examples of Nash Equilibrium

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding specific examples of Nash equilibrium is fundamental because it transforms the abstract concept into a tangible tool. It demonstrates how strategic stability is achieved in real-world interactions, forming the bedrock for analyzing more complex economic, social, and computational systems.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify, compute, and interpret Nash equilibria in canonical games such as the Prisoner's Dilemma, Battle of the Sexes, and Cournot Duopoly. They will develop the skill to analyze the strategic choices of players and predict the stable outcomes of these interactions.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon the foundational definition of Nash equilibrium introduced previously. It provides the crucial examples needed to grasp more advanced concepts like mixed-strategy equilibria, efficiency (Pareto optimality), and the price of anarchy, which are central to the module.

**4. Real-world applications**
Examples of Nash equilibrium model countless scenarios, including oligopoly market competition, auction bidding, traffic routing, and environmental treaty negotiations. This analysis is vital for designing efficient algorithms and incentive-compatible systems in areas like online advertising and network routing.
