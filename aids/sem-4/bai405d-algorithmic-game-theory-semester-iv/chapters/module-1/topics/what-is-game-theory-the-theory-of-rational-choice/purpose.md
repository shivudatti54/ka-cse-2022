### Learning Purpose: What is Game Theory? The Theory of Rational Choice

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic provides the foundational language and core principles of game theory, a field essential for modeling strategic interactions where outcomes depend on the choices of multiple decision-makers. Understanding rational choice is crucial, as it forms the basis for predicting behavior in competitive and cooperative scenarios.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to formally define a game, including its key components: players, strategies, and payoffs. They will understand the concept of rationality and how it leads to solution concepts like the dominant strategy equilibrium. The goal is to equip students with the ability to model simple strategic situations and predict their outcomes.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This introductory module lays the groundwork for all subsequent topics. The theory of rational choice is the bedrock upon which more advanced solution concepts—like the famous Nash Equilibrium in Module 2—are built. It directly connects to analyzing auctions, bargaining, and market competition later in the course.

**4. Real-world applications**
The principles learned here apply to a vast range of real-world situations, from economics (e.g., firms setting prices) and political science (e.g., voting systems) to everyday life (e.g., negotiating or bidding on an item online). It provides a framework for making optimal decisions in any interactive environment.
