### Learning Purpose: Strategic Games

**1. Importance:**
This topic is fundamental as it provides the core building blocks for analyzing interactive decision-making. Strategic games model situations where the outcome for a participant depends not only on their own actions but also on the actions of others. Understanding these models is crucial for predicting behavior in competitive and cooperative environments, forming the bedrock of algorithmic game theory.

**2. Student Learning:**
Students will learn to formally represent strategic situations using normal-form (matrix) games. They will master key solution concepts, most notably the **Nash Equilibrium**, which predicts stable outcomes where no player can benefit by unilaterally changing their strategy. This includes understanding how to compute these equilibria in simple games and analyzing the trade-offs between different strategies.

**4. Real-World Applications:**
The principles of strategic games are applied across economics (market competition), politics (voting systems), computer science (network routing protocols, cloud resource allocation), and everyday life (bidding on eBay, social platform interactions). It offers a framework for designing smarter, more efficient systems and algorithms that must account for strategic users.
