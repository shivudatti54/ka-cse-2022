### Learning Purpose: Expert Diagnosis

**1. Importance**
This topic is crucial as it introduces students to the practical challenge of aggregating information from multiple, potentially biased, experts. It provides the mathematical foundation for designing mechanisms that incentivize truthful reporting and accurately diagnose a situation, which is a core problem in multi-agent systems and decision-making.

**2. Student Learning Outcomes**
Students will learn to model expert diagnosis as a game, design scoring rules (e.g., proper scoring rules) to incentivize truthful predictions, and apply algorithmic solutions like weighted majority or other aggregation methods. They will analyze the equilibrium properties of these mechanisms.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts**
This directly builds upon prior concepts from Mechanism Design (creating incentive-compatible systems) and Learning in Games (no-regret algorithms). It also connects to broader themes of information elicitation, Bayesian persuasion, and the computation of correlated equilibria.

**4. Real-World Applications**
The principles of expert diagnosis are applied in real-world platforms such as prediction markets (e.g., PredictIt), financial forecasting, aggregating medical diagnoses from multiple doctors, and algorithmic reputation systems used by companies like Amazon or Uber to rate service providers.
