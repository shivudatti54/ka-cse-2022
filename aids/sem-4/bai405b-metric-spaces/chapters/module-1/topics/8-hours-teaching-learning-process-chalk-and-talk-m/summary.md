# **Teaching-Learning Process: Chalk and Talk Method / PowerPoint Presentation**

## **Key Points**

### Chalk and Talk Method

- **Definition:** A traditional teaching method where the teacher speaks and writes on the board to convey information.
- **Pros:**
  - Encourages active learning
  - Allows for immediate feedback
  - Can be used for large classes
- **Cons:**
  - Limited visual aids
  - May not engage students with different learning styles

### PowerPoint Presentation

- **Definition:** A digital presentation tool used to convey information in a visually engaging way.
- **Pros:**
  - Highly engaging and interactive
  - Allows for easy navigation and organization
  - Can be shared with students remotely
- **Cons:**
  - Requires technical expertise
  - Can be distracting if not used effectively

### Comparison

- **Key Differences:**
  - Chalk and talk method: face-to-face interaction, limited visual aids
  - PowerPoint presentation: digital, interactive, visually engaging
- **When to Use:**
  - Chalk and talk method: lectures, small classes, critical thinking discussions
  - PowerPoint presentation: lectures, large classes, presentations, remote teaching

## **Important Formulas, Definitions, and Theorems**

- **Definition of a Set:** A collection of unique elements, often denoted as A = {a, b, c}.
- **Power Set:** The set of all possible subsets of a given set, denoted as P(A) = {{}, {a}, {b}, {c}, {a, b}, {a, c}, {b, c}, {a, b, c}}.
- **Union and Intersection:** Denoted as A ∪ B and A ∩ B, respectively, and represent the combination and intersection of sets.
