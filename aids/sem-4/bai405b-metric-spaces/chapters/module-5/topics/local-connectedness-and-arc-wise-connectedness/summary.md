# **Local Connectedness and Arc-Wise Connectedness**

## **Definitions and Formulas:**

- **Local Connectedness:** A space $X$ is locally connected if for every $x \in X$ and every open set $U$ containing $x$, there exists a connected open set $V$ containing $x$ such that $V \subseteq U$.
- **Arc-Wise Connectedness:** A space $X$ is arc-wise connected if every two points in $X$ can be joined by an arc (a continuous function from $[0,1]$ to $X$).
- **Heine-Borel Theorem:** A space $X$ is second-countable if it has a countable basis, i.e., a countable collection of open sets such that every open set in $X$ contains a member of the collection.
- **Hausdorff Space:** A topological space $X$ is Hausdorff if for every two distinct points $x,y \in X$, there exist disjoint open sets $U,V$ containing $x$ and $y$, respectively.

## **Theorems and Important Results:**

- **Urysohn's Lemma:** If $X$ is a normal space (Hausdorff and second-countable), then any two disjoint closed sets in $X$ can be separated by a continuous function.
- **Hahn's Extension Theorem:** If $X$ is a locally connected space and $Y$ is a normed linear space, then any continuous function from $X$ to $Y$ can be extended to a continuous function from $X$ with the topology induced from the norm on $Y$.

## **Key Points and Implications:**

- Local connectedness is equivalent to the existence of a countable basis.
- Arc-wise connectedness is equivalent to the existence of a countable basis.
- The Heine-Borel Theorem implies the existence of a countable basis in a space that is both locally connected and arc-wise connected.
- Hausdorff spaces are locally connected and arc-wise connected.

## **Implications for Metric Spaces:**

- For metric spaces, local connectedness and arc-wise connectedness are equivalent.
- Theorems such as Urysohn's Lemma and Hahn's Extension Theorem have important implications for metric spaces.
