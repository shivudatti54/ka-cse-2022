**Why this topic matters:**
Remote Invocation is crucial in Distributed Systems as it enables communication between different nodes or processes, allowing for the exchange of data and services across the network. Understanding Remote Invocation is essential for designing and developing scalable, reliable, and efficient distributed systems. It's a fundamental concept that underlies many distributed technologies and protocols.

**Real-world applications:**
Remote Invocation has numerous real-world applications, including cloud computing, grid computing, and peer-to-peer networks. It's used in various services such as web services, distributed databases, and microservices architecture. Remote Invocation also plays a vital role in IoT (Internet of Things) devices, enabling them to communicate with each other and the cloud.

**Connection to other concepts:**
Remote Invocation is closely related to other concepts in Distributed Systems, such as Request-Reply protocols, Remote Procedure Calls (RPCs), and Object Request Brokers (ORBs). Understanding these concepts is essential for designing and implementing distributed systems that can handle complex communication scenarios. Remote Invocation also builds upon other concepts like message passing, synchronization, and concurrency.
