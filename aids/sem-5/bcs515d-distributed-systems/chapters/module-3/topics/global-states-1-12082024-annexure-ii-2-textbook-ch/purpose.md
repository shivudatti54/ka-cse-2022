Learning about global states in distributed systems is crucial as it enables the synchronization and coordination of multiple processes running on independent computers, ensuring that they can operate correctly and efficiently. This topic matters because it lays the foundation for understanding how distributed systems manage concurrency, communication, and consistency in modern computing. In real-world applications, global states are essential in systems such as social media platforms, online banking, and cloud computing, where multiple users and processes interact concurrently. This concept connects to other areas, including concurrency models, synchronization primitives, and fault tolerance, making it a fundamental aspect of distributed systems design.
