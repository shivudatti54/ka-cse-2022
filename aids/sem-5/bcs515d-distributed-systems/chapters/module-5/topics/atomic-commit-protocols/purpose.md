# Learning Objectives
After studying this topic, you should be able to:
1.  Explain why atomic commit protocols are necessary for distributed transactions.
2.  Describe the roles and message flow of the Two-Phase Commit (2PC) protocol in both successful and failure scenarios.
3.  Identify and explain the "blocking" limitation of the 2PC protocol.
4.  Contrast the 2PC and Three-Phase Commit (3PC) protocols, highlighting the purpose of the additional phase in 3PC.
5.  Evaluate the practical trade-offs between 2PC and 3PC, understanding why 2PC is more prevalent in real systems.
6.  Interpret sequence diagrams illustrating the message exchanges in atomic commit protocols.
