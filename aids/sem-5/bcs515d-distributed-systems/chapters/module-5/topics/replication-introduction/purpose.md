# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define what sorting is and explain its importance.
2. Identify the two primary types of sorting: internal sorting and external sorting.
3. Describe the basic principles of bubble sort, selection sort, and insertion sort.
4. Compare and contrast the three sorting algorithms in terms of time complexity and space complexity.
5. Explain the advantages and disadvantages of each sorting algorithm.
6. Apply the sorting algorithms to solve real-world problems.
