### 1. Why is this topic important?
Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) is crucial as it shifts the focus from high-stakes, end-of-term exams to a more consistent and formative assessment process. In a technically demanding and rapidly evolving field like Computer Vision, CIE ensures students regularly engage with complex material, receive timely feedback, and can identify and address knowledge gaps early. This promotes deeper, long-term understanding of intricate concepts rather than short-term memorization.

### 2. What will students learn?
Students will learn to demonstrate their practical and theoretical knowledge of Computer Vision concepts from Module 5 (e.g., object detection, image segmentation, or CNNs) through a series of assessments. These may include quizzes, programming assignments, mini-projects, and presentations. The process will develop their problem-solving skills, ability to implement algorithms, and critically analyze results.

### 3. How does it connect to other concepts?
CIE in this module directly builds upon the foundational knowledge acquired in earlier modules (e.g., image processing, machine learning basics). Successfully completing CIE tasks requires students to synthesize these previous concepts and apply them to more advanced topics. This continuous application reinforces the interconnected nature of the curriculum, showing how core principles are used collectively to solve complex vision tasks.

### 4. Real-world applications
The skills assessed through CIE mirror the agile, iterative development cycles used in industry. Successfully completing incremental assignments prepares students for real-world scenarios such as developing and refining a computer vision model for applications in autonomous vehicles (object detection), medical imaging (image segmentation), or quality control in manufacturing. The process cultivates the consistent performance and rigorous evaluation required of a professional CV engineer.
