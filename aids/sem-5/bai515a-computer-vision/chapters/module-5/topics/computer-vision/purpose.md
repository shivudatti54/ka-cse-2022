# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define computer vision and explain its relationship with digital image processing, understanding how these fields complement each other in visual data analysis.

2. Distinguish between the three levels of vision (low-level, mid-level, high-level) and provide appropriate examples of tasks performed at each level.

3. Explain the fundamental principles of image formation using the pinhole camera model, including coordinate transformations between world, camera, and image spaces.

4. Identify and describe various feature representation techniques used in computer vision, including edges, corners, blobs, and textures.

5. Compare different object recognition approaches, from simple template matching to complex feature-based and learning-based methods.

6. Analyze computer vision pipelines and explain how individual components contribute to overall system performance.

7. Apply computer vision concepts to solve practical problems in domains such as industrial inspection, facial recognition, and video analysis.

8. Evaluate the challenges in computer vision including illumination variation, viewpoint changes, occlusion, and computational considerations.
