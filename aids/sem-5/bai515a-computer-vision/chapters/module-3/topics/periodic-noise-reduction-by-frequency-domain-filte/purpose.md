**1. Why this topic matters**: Learning periodic noise reduction by frequency domain filtering is crucial in image restoration and reconstruction, as it enables effective removal of unwanted noise patterns from images, preserving their original features and details. This technique is vital in various applications where image quality is critical, such as medical imaging, astronomy, and surveillance.

**2. Real-world applications**: Periodic noise reduction by frequency domain filtering has numerous real-world applications, including:

- Removing salt and pepper noise in medical images
- Reducing spectral noise in astronomical images
- Enhancing image quality in surveillance systems
- Restoring damaged or degraded images in various fields

**3. Connection to other concepts**: This topic is closely related to other concepts in image restoration and reconstruction, such as:

- Image filtering techniques (e.g., convolutional filters, wavelet transforms)
- Fourier analysis and transform methods (e.g., DFT, IDFT)
- Image denoising algorithms (e.g., Wiener filter, Bayesian methods)
- Machine learning-based approaches (e.g., deep learning-based image restoration)
