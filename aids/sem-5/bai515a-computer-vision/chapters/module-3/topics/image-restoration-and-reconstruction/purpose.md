### Learning Purpose: Image Restoration and Reconstruction

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Image restoration and reconstruction are fundamental to computer vision because real-world images are often degraded by noise, blur, and other imperfections during acquisition or transmission. Restoring these images to their original quality is a critical preprocessing step that directly impacts the performance of higher-level tasks like object detection and image analysis.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the mathematical models of image degradation (e.g., noise models, blur functions) and the corresponding inverse processes. They will explore fundamental techniques for noise reduction (e.g., spatial and frequency domain filters) and deblurring (e.g., inverse and Wiener filtering). The module will also cover reconstruction algorithms to form complete images from partial data, such as from projections in computed tomography.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly on the signal processing and Fourier transform foundations from earlier modules. It is a prerequisite for more advanced content like image segmentation and object recognition, as cleaner, restored images yield significantly better results in these areas. It also shares principles with deep learning-based methods using autoencoders.

**4. Real-world applications**
Applications are vast and include enhancing medical images (MRI, CT scans), restoring historical photographs and archival footage, improving astronomical images, and fixing blurry images in smartphone photography and forensic analysis.
