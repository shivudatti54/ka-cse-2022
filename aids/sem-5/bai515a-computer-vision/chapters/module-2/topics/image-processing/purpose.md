# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define digital images and explain the representation of grayscale and color images in terms of pixels, intensity values, and coordinate systems.

2. Distinguish between image enhancement and image restoration, explaining the fundamental differences in their objectives and methodologies.

3. Apply point processing operations including image negation, log transformations, power law transformations, and histogram equalization to improve image quality.

4. Implement spatial filtering operations using smoothing filters (mean, median) and sharpening filters (Laplacian, unsharp masking) on digital images.

5. Analyze the effects of different noise models on image quality and select appropriate filtering techniques for noise removal based on noise characteristics.

6. Apply morphological operations including dilation, erosion, opening, and closing for binary and grayscale image processing.

7. Compute histogram of an image, perform histogram equalization, and explain the mathematical transformation involved in redistributing intensity values.

8. Evaluate the appropriate image processing technique for a given problem scenario, considering factors such as noise type, desired output, and computational complexity.
