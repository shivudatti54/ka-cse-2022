# Revision Notes: Computer Vision - Chapter 1 (1.1)

## Introduction to Computer Vision

- Definition: The intersection of computer science and artificial intelligence that enables machines to interpret and understand visual data from images and videos.
- Brief History:
  - 1950s-60s: Image processing and feature extraction
  - 1970s-80s: Computer vision emerges as a field
  - 1990s-present: Advancements in machine learning, deep learning, and computer vision

## Image Formation

- **Photometric Image**: An image that contains information about the intensity of light reflected from objects in the scene.
- **Key Components**:
  - **Sensor**: Captures light and converts it into an electrical signal.
  - **Lens**: Focuses light onto the sensor.
  - **Camera**: Converts light into a photometric image.

## Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Resolution**: The number of pixels in an image (e.g., 640x480 pixels).
- **Dynamic Range**: The range of intensity values in an image (e.g., 12-bit dynamic range).
- **Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR)**: A measure of the ratio of signal intensity to noise intensity in an image.

## Important Theorems

- **Ray Tracing**: A method for calculating the appearance of objects in a scene by tracing the path of light rays.
- **Perspective Projection**: A method for projecting a 3D scene onto a 2D image plane.
