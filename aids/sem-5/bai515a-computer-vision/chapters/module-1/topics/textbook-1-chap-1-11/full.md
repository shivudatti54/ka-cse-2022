# Introduction to Computer Vision

---

Computer vision is a subfield of artificial intelligence (AI) that deals with the interaction between computers and the visual information of the world. It is a multidisciplinary field that combines computer science, mathematics, engineering, and cognitive psychology to develop algorithms and models that can interpret, understand, and generate visual data.

## Definition and Scope

Computer vision is a broad field that encompasses various aspects of image and video analysis, including:

- Image formation and reconstruction
- Image processing and enhancement
- Object recognition and classification
- Scene understanding and interpretation
- Image generation and synthesis

## Applications of Computer Vision

Computer vision has numerous applications in various fields, including:

- Healthcare: diagnostic imaging, disease detection, and patient monitoring
- Security: surveillance, object detection, and facial recognition
- Robotics: object recognition, tracking, and manipulation
- Autonomous vehicles: object detection, tracking, and scene understanding
- Entertainment: video games, special effects, and virtual reality

## History of Computer Vision

The field of computer vision has its roots in the 1950s and 1960s, when the first image processing algorithms were developed. In the 1970s and 1980s, the field gained momentum with the introduction of the first image recognition systems. The 1990s saw the rise of object recognition and scene understanding, while the 2000s witnessed the emergence of deep learning-based approaches.

## Modern Developments

In recent years, computer vision has experienced a significant resurgence with the advent of deep learning techniques. The introduction of convolutional neural networks (CNNs) in the 2010s revolutionized the field, enabling state-of-the-art performance in various computer vision tasks.

## Key Concepts and Techniques

### Image Formation

Image formation refers to the process of capturing visual information from the environment and converting it into a digital representation. This process involves several steps, including:

- **Light-Traffic Interaction**: The interaction between light and matter, resulting in the formation of an image.
- **Camera Modeling**: The mathematical models used to describe the behavior of cameras and image sensors.
- **Image Sampling**: The process of capturing a representative subset of pixels to form an image.

### Photometric Image

A photometric image is a two-dimensional representation of an image that captures the intensity or luminance of the visual information. It is a crucial aspect of computer vision, as it provides a quantitative measure of the visual data.
