# **Revision Notes: Chap-3 (3.1) - Image Formation**

## **What is Computer Vision?**

- Definition: The intersection of computer science and artificial intelligence that enables computers to interpret and understand visual information from images and videos.
- Brief History:
  - Early beginnings in the 1950s with the development of computer graphics and image processing.
  - 1970s-1980s: Introduction of machine vision and computer vision.
  - Modern era: Advancements in deep learning and convolutional neural networks.

## **Image Formation**

- **Photometric Image**: A representation of the intensity of light reflected by an object at each point in the image plane.
- **Camera Model**: A mathematical model that describes how light is captured and focused by a camera.
- **Camera Parameters**: Lens width, focal length, aperture, and sensor size.

## **Key Formulas and Equations**

- **Pinhole Camera Model**:
  - Image distance: `d_i = -f * d_o / f_o`
  - Object distance: `d_o = -d_i * f_o / f`
- **Perspective Projection**:
  - `x = x_o * f_o / f`
  - `y = y_o * f_o / f`

## **Important Definitions and Theorems**

- **Perspective**: A geometric concept that describes how parallel lines appear to converge at a vanishing point.
- **Projection**: A mathematical operation that maps points from one space to another.
- **Homography**: A transformation that preserves straight lines.

## **Key Terms**

- **Image size**: The number of pixels in an image.
- **Resolution**: The number of pixels per unit area in an image.
- **Noise**: Random fluctuations in an image.
