# **1.1 - 1.3: Introduction to Computer Networks**

### 1.1: Data Communications

#### Definition

Data communication refers to the process of exchanging data between two or more devices over a communication channel. This can be done through various methods, including wired and wireless connections, and involves the transmission, reception, and processing of data.

#### Types of Data Communications

- **Point-to-Point (PTP) Communications**: A direct connection between two devices, such as a phone call or an email.
- **Point-to-Multi-Point (PMP) Communications**: A connection between a single device and multiple devices, such as a broadcast signal or a multicasting service.
- **Multipoint Communications**: A connection between multiple devices, such as a video conference or a live broadcast.

#### Advantages and Disadvantages of Data Communications

Advantages:

- Enables data exchange between devices
- Facilitates communication and collaboration
- Supports various applications and services

Disadvantages:

- Requires dedicated resources and infrastructure
- Can be prone to errors and delays
- May be vulnerable to security threats and interference

#### Key Concepts

- **Data Rate**: The speed at which data is transmitted
- **Bit Rate**: The rate at which bits are transmitted
- **Baud Rate**: The rate at which bits are transmitted over a communication channel
- **Error Detection and Correction**: Methods used to detect and correct errors in data transmission

### 1.2: Networks

#### Definition

A network is a collection of interconnected devices, such as computers, printers, and servers, that communicate with each other to share resources and exchange data.

#### Types of Networks

- **Local Area Network (LAN)**: A network that spans a small geographical area, such as a home or office building.
- **Wide Area Network (WAN)**: A network that spans a large geographical area, such as a city or country.
- **Metropolitan Area Network (MAN)**: A network that spans a city or metropolitan area.
- **Wireless Network (WLAN)**: A network that uses wireless communication to connect devices.

#### Advantages and Disadvantages of Networks

Advantages:

- Enables devices to share resources and exchange data
- Supports various applications and services
- Facilitates communication and collaboration

Disadvantages:

- Requires dedicated resources and infrastructure
- Can be prone to errors and delays
- May be vulnerable to security threats and interference

#### Key Concepts

- **Network Topology**: The physical or logical arrangement of devices in a network
- **Network Protocol**: A set of rules that governs data transmission and reception in a network
- **Network Address**: A unique address assigned to a device in a network

### 1.3: Network Models

#### Definition

A network model is a conceptual representation of a network, used to describe its structure, behavior, and performance.

#### Types of Network Models

- **Physical Network Model**: A representation of a network based on its physical components, such as cables and devices.
- **Logical Network Model**: A representation of a network based on its functional components, such as protocols and services.
- **Transport Network Model**: A representation of a network based on its transport capabilities, such as data transmission and reception.

#### Advantages and Disadvantages of Network Models

Advantages:

- Helps to understand and analyze network behavior and performance
- Facilitates network design and optimization
- Supports network management and troubleshooting

Disadvantages:

- Can be complex and difficult to understand
- May not accurately represent real-world network behavior
- Requires specialized knowledge and expertise

#### Key Concepts

- ** OSI Model**: A 7-layer model that describes the structure and behavior of network communications
- **TCP/IP Model**: A 4-layer model that describes the structure and behavior of network communications
- **Network Architecture**: The overall design and organization of a network

### Key Terms

- **Network Interface**: The hardware component that connects a device to a network
- **Router**: A device that connects multiple networks together
- **Switch**: A device that connects multiple devices within a network
- **Hub**: A device that connects multiple devices within a network

### Key Concepts Summary

- Data communications enables data exchange between devices
- Networks are collections of interconnected devices that communicate with each other
- Network models are conceptual representations of networks used to describe their structure, behavior, and performance
