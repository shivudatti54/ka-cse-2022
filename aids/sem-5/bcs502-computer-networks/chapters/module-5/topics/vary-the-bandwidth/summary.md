# Vary the Bandwidth

## Revision Notes

### Module: COMPUTER NETWORKS

### Topic: Vary the Bandwidth

#### Key Points

- **Definition:** Varying bandwidth refers to the ability to adjust the amount of available bandwidth in a network to meet changing demands.
- **Importance:** Effective bandwidth management is crucial to ensure smooth network operations, prevent congestion, and maintain quality of service (QoS).
- **Types of Bandwidth:**
  - **Available Bandwidth:** The amount of bandwidth available for use at a given time.
  - **Utilized Bandwidth:** The amount of bandwidth being currently used.
  - **Allocated Bandwidth:** The amount of bandwidth allocated to a specific user or network.

### Important Formulas

- **Bandwidth Formula:** Bandwidth (B) = Throughput (T) / Latency (L)
- **Network Utilization Formula:** Network Utilization (U) = Utilized Bandwidth / Available Bandwidth

### Theorems

- **Packet Delay Theorem:** The packet delay is directly proportional to the bandwidth of the network.
- **Jain's Formula:** Jain's formula is used to calculate the throughput of a network, considering the number of users and the available bandwidth.

### Key Concepts

- **QoS (Quality of Service):** Ensures that network resources are allocated to meet specific performance requirements.
- **Traffic Management:** Techniques used to manage and control network traffic to prevent congestion and ensure QoS.
- **Bandwidth Management:** Strategies used to manage and allocate bandwidth in a network to meet changing demands.

### Revision Tips

- Understand the importance of bandwidth management in computer networks.
- Familiarize yourself with the different types of bandwidth and their definitions.
- Practice calculating bandwidth and network utilization using the relevant formulas.
