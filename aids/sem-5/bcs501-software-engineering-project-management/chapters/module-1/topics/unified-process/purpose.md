# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1.  Define a security incident and explain the importance of a structured Incident Response (IR) process.
2.  Identify and describe the four primary phases of the NIST IR lifecycle in the correct sequence.
3.  Explain the key activities and goals associated with each phase of the IR process (Preparation, Detection & Analysis, Containment/Eradication/Recovery, Post-Incident).
4.  Differentiate between short-term and long-term containment strategies.
5.  Describe the purpose and components of a final incident report and a lessons learned meeting.
6.  Articulate how digital forensic principles (e.g., chain of custody, order of volatility) are integrated into the IR process.
