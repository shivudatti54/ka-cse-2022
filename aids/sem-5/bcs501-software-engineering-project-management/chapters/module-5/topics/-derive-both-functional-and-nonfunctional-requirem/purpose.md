**Why this topic matters:**
Deriving functional and nonfunctional requirements is crucial in software engineering as it ensures that software systems meet the needs of users and stakeholders, while also being efficient, scalable, and maintainable. This topic matters because it lays the foundation for developing software systems that are both effective and efficient.

**Real-world applications:**
In the real world, deriving functional and nonfunctional requirements is essential for software development projects, such as mobile apps, web applications, and enterprise software systems. By identifying and prioritizing these requirements, developers can create software systems that meet the needs of users, improve business operations, and drive revenue growth.

**Connection to other concepts:**
This topic connects to other concepts in software engineering and project management, such as requirements gathering, design patterns, and testing methodologies. By understanding functional and nonfunctional requirements, developers can design and implement software systems that are scalable, secure, and meet the needs of users, while also ensuring that they are aligned with business goals and objectives.
