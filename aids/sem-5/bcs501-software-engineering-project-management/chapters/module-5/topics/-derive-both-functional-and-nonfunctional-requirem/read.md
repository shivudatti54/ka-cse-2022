# Deriving Functional and Non-Functional Requirements

### Introduction

In software engineering, requirements are the specifications of what the system should do and how it should behave. There are two types of requirements: functional requirements and non-functional requirements. Functional requirements describe what the system should do, while non-functional requirements describe how the system should behave and its properties.

### Functional Requirements

Functional requirements are the functional needs of the system. They describe what the system should do, how it should behave, and what inputs and outputs it should have.

#### Definition

Functional requirements are the description of the system's functions and operations.

#### Examples

- The online banking system should allow users to login and view their account balance.
- The e-commerce system should allow users to search and purchase products online.
- The healthcare system should allow doctors to view patient medical records and prescribe medication.

#### Methods for Deriving Functional Requirements

1. **User Interviews**: Conducting interviews with potential users to understand their needs and requirements.
2. **Surveys and Questionnaires**: Using surveys and questionnaires to gather information about the users' needs and requirements.
3. **Workshops and Focus Groups**: Conducting workshops and focus groups to gather feedback from potential users and stakeholders.
4. **Analysis of Existing Systems**: Analyzing existing systems to identify their strengths and weaknesses.

### Non-Functional Requirements

Non-functional requirements are the non-functional needs of the system. They describe how the system should behave and its properties.

#### Definition

Non-functional requirements are the description of the system's properties and behavior, such as performance, security, and usability.

#### Examples

- The online banking system should be accessible 24/7.
- The e-commerce system should be able to handle a high volume of transactions per day.
- The healthcare system should meet HIPAA compliance standards.

#### Methods for Deriving Non-Functional Requirements

1. **Performance Analysis**: Analyzing the system's performance and identifying any bottlenecks or areas for improvement.
2. **Security Analysis**: Analyzing the system's security and identifying any vulnerabilities or areas for improvement.
3. **Usability Testing**: Conducting usability testing to identify any issues with the system's usability.
4. **Regulatory Compliance Analysis**: Analyzing the system's compliance with regulatory requirements.

### Case Study

Let's consider a case study of a new mobile banking app.

**Functional Requirements**

- The app should allow users to login and view their account balance.
- The app should allow users to transfer funds between accounts.
- The app should allow users to pay bills online.
- The app should provide personalized financial recommendations based on user behavior.

**Non-Functional Requirements**

- The app should be accessible 24/7.
- The app should handle a minimum of 1000 concurrent users.
- The app should meet the user experience (UX) standards of the bank.
- The app should comply with PCI-DSS standards for secure payment processing.

### Conclusion

Deriving functional and non-functional requirements is an essential part of the software development process. By understanding the needs and requirements of the users and stakeholders, software engineers can design and develop a system that meets their needs and expectations.

### Key Takeaways

- Functional requirements describe what the system should do.
- Non-functional requirements describe how the system should behave and its properties.
- There are various methods for deriving functional and non-functional requirements, including user interviews, surveys, workshops, and analysis of existing systems.
- Understanding the users' needs and requirements is essential for designing and developing a system that meets their needs and expectations.
