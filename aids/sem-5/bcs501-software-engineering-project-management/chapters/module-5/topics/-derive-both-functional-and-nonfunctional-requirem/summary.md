# Deriving Functional and Nonfunctional Requirements

==============================================

### Overview

Functional and nonfunctional requirements are essential components of software engineering. This section will derive both types of requirements from a case study.

### Functional Requirements

---

- **Definition:** Functional requirements describe the desired behavior of a system.
- **Case Study:** A bank's online banking system
- **Functional Requirements:**
  - User authentication and authorization
  - Account management (create, update, delete)
  - Transaction processing (deposit, withdrawal, transfer)
  - Transaction history
  - Search and filter functionality
  - Reporting and analytics

### Nonfunctional Requirements

---

- **Definition:** Nonfunctional requirements describe the constraints and characteristics of a system.
- **Case Study:** A bank's online banking system
- **Nonfunctional Requirements:**
  - **Performance Requirements:**
    - Response time < 2 seconds
    - Throughput > 1000 transactions/minute
  - **Security Requirements:**
    - Encryption of sensitive data
    - Access control (role-based access control)
  - **Usability Requirements:**
    - Intuitive interface
    - Clear navigation
  - **Availability Requirements:**
    - Uptime > 99.99%
    - Maintenance downtime < 1 hour

### Important Formulas and Definitions

---

- **Quality Factor (QF):** A measure of the quality of a system, often used to evaluate nonfunctional requirements.
- ** reliability:** The probability that a system will operate correctly for a specified period.
- **Availability (A):** The probability that a system will be operational and functioning correctly at any given time.

### Theorems

---

- **First Law of Software Engineering:** If a system is not well-designed, it will always have bugs.
- **Second Law of Software Engineering:** No matter how well-designed, a system will always have bugs.
