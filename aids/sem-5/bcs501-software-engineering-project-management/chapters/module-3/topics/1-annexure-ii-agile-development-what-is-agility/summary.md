# Agile Development: What is Agility?

### Definitions and Key Points

- **Agility**: Ability of an organization to respond quickly to change and adapt to new requirements.
- **Agile Methodology**: Iterative and incremental approach to software development that emphasizes flexibility and collaboration.
- **Agile Manifesto**: 4 core values:
  - Individuals and interactions over processes and tools
  - Working software over comprehensive documentation
  - Customer collaboration over contract negotiation
  - Responding to change over following a plan
- **Agile Principles**: 12 principles that guide agile development, including:
  - Our highest priority is to satisfy the customer through early and continuous delivery of valuable software
  - Welcome changing requirements, even in late development
  - Deliver working software frequently, from a couple of weeks to a couple of months
  - Business people and developers must work together daily
  - Build projects around motivated individuals. Give them the environment and support they need, and trust them to get the job done.

### Important Concepts

- **Sprints**: Short iterations of development work, typically 2-4 weeks
- **User Stories**: Requirements for software development, written from the customer's perspective
- **Backlog**: Prioritized list of user stories and features to be developed
- **Daily Stand-up Meeting**: Regular meeting for team members to discuss progress and plan work

### Theories and Formulas

- **Kanban**: Visual system for managing work, emphasizing continuous flow and limiting work in progress.
- **Scrum**: Framework for agile development, including roles (Product Owner, Scrum Master, Development Team) and ceremonies (Sprint Planning, Daily Scrum, Sprint Review).

### Revision Tips

- Emphasize the importance of customer satisfaction and adaptability in agile development.
- Highlight the key principles and values of the agile manifesto.
- Familiarize yourself with common agile concepts, such as sprints, user stories, and backlogs.
