# Principles that Guide Each Framework Activity

=====================================================

## Introduction

---

This summary provides key points and formulas for the principles that guide each framework activity in Software Engineering and Project Management.

### Project Management Frameworks

---

- **Project Management Institute (PMI) Framework**:
  - Five Process Groups:
    - Initiating
    - Planning
    - Executing
    - Monitoring and Controlling
    - Closing
  - Nine Knowledge Areas:
    - Integration Management
    - Scope Management
    - Time Management
    - Cost Management
    - Quality Management
    - Resource Management
    - Communications Management
    - Risk Management
    - Procurement Management
- **Agile Framework**:
  - Iterative and incremental development
  - Emphasis on flexibility and collaboration
  - Continuous improvement and delivery
  - Key principles:
    - Individuals and interactions over processes and tools
    - Working software over comprehensive documentation
    - Customer collaboration over contract negotiation

### Software Engineering Frameworks

---

- **Waterfall Model**:
  - Linear and sequential approach
  - Phases: requirements gathering, design, implementation, testing, deployment
  - No iteration or feedback during development
- **V-Model**:
  - Phases: requirements gathering, design, implementation, testing
  - Emphasis on testing and validation
  - Iteration and feedback during development
- **Spiral Model**:
  - Iterative and incremental development
  - Phases: requirements gathering, design, implementation, testing, deployment
  - Emphasis on risk management and control

### Important Formulas and Definitions

---

- **Gantt Chart**: A bar chart that illustrates the tasks and timelines of a project
- **Kerberos**: A security protocol that provides mutual authentication and confidentiality
- **MoSCoW Method**: A prioritization technique that categorizes requirements as must-haves, should-haves, could-haves, and won't-haves

### Important Theorems

---

- **The Law of Total Variance**: A statistical principle that describes the total variance of a process
- **The Five Whys**: A method for drill-down questioning to identify the root cause of a problem

### Quick Revision Tips

---

- Focus on the key principles and processes of each framework
- Understand the importance of iteration, feedback, and continuous improvement
- Review the formulas, definitions, and theorems to reinforce your understanding
