## Software Engineering & Project Management

### 3.1: Introduction to Requirements Gathering

Requirements gathering, also known as requirements elicitation, is the process of identifying, analyzing, documenting, and refining project requirements. The primary objective of requirements gathering is to ensure that the software development project delivers value to its stakeholders.

#### Key Concepts:

- **Requirements**: Statements that define the functional and non-functional needs of the project.
- **Elicitation**: The process of gathering requirements from stakeholders.
- **Requirements Management Plan**: A document that outlines the approach for gathering, analyzing, documenting, and managing project requirements.

#### Case Study: Requirements Gathering at NASA

In the 1960s, NASA's Apollo program faced significant challenges in requirements gathering. The team encountered difficulties in defining and documenting the requirements for the lunar module. To overcome these challenges, NASA developed a structured requirements gathering approach, which included:

- Defining clear and concise requirements statements
- Identifying and documenting stakeholder needs
- Developing a requirements management plan
- Conducting regular review and updates

The structured approach enabled NASA to successfully deliver the Apollo program, demonstrating the importance of effective requirements gathering in software engineering and project management.

### 3.2: Types of Requirements

Requirements can be categorized into several types, including:

#### Functional Requirements:

- Define the functional behavior of the software system.
- Specify the inputs, processing, and outputs of the system.

#### Non-Functional Requirements:

- Define the performance, security, and usability characteristics of the software system.
- Specify the constraints on the system's behavior and performance.

#### Quality Requirements:

- Define the quality attributes of the software system.
- Specify the levels of quality that the system must meet.

#### Assumptions and Dependencies:

- Define the assumptions made about the environment and stakeholders.
- Specify the dependencies between the system and other components.

### 3.3: Requirements Gathering Techniques

Several techniques can be used to gather requirements, including:

#### Interviews:

- One-on-one conversations with stakeholders to gather information.
- Can be used to gather both functional and non-functional requirements.

#### Surveys:

- Questionnaires or surveys used to gather information from a large number of stakeholders.
- Can be used to gather both functional and non-functional requirements.

#### Workshops:

- Collaborative sessions with stakeholders to discuss and refine requirements.
- Can be used to gather both functional and non-functional requirements.

#### Observations:

- Observation of the stakeholders' behavior and interactions.
- Can be used to gather both functional and non-functional requirements.

### 3.4: Requirements Management

Requirements management involves the following steps:

#### Requirements Identification:

- Identifying and documenting the project requirements.

#### Requirements Analysis:

- Analyzing the project requirements to identify any inconsistencies or ambiguities.

#### Requirements Specification:

- Creating a detailed specification of the project requirements.

#### Requirements Review:

- Reviewing the project requirements with stakeholders to ensure that they are accurate and complete.

#### Requirements Validation:

- Verifying that the project requirements meet the stakeholders' needs.

### 3.5: Benefits of Requirements Gathering

Effective requirements gathering provides several benefits, including:

#### Reduced Risk:

- Ensures that the software development project delivers value to its stakeholders.

#### Improved Quality:

- Ensures that the software system meets the required quality attributes.

#### Increased Efficiency:

- Reduces the time and cost associated with software development.

#### Enhanced Stakeholder Satisfaction:

- Ensures that the software system meets the stakeholders' needs and expectations.

### 3.6: Challenges in Requirements Gathering

Several challenges can be encountered during requirements gathering, including:

#### Ambiguity and Uncertainty:

- Stakeholders may have unclear or conflicting requirements.

#### Lack of Information:

- Insufficient information may be available to gather accurate requirements.

#### Stakeholder Involvement:

- Stakeholders may not be involved or engaged in the requirements gathering process.

#### Communication Breakdowns:

- Communication breakdowns can occur between stakeholders and software developers.

#### Time and Cost Constraints:

- Software development projects may have limited time and budget constraints.
