# **Chapter 7: Requirements Engineering**

## **7.1: Introduction to Requirements Engineering**

### Definition

Requirements engineering is the process of defining, analyzing, documenting, and maintaining all the requirements for a software system. It involves working with stakeholders to identify and understand the needs of the project, and developing a set of requirements that will guide the development process.

### Importance of Requirements Engineering

- Ensures that the software system meets the needs of the stakeholders
- Helps to identify and mitigate risks
- Ensures that the software system is maintainable and scalable
- Allows for changes to be made to the project plan without affecting the overall scope

### Types of Requirements

- Functional requirements: describe what the software system should do
- Non-functional requirements: describe the characteristics of the software system (e.g. performance, security)
- Usability requirements: describe how the software system should be used
- Environmental requirements: describe the external factors that affect the software system

### Requirements Engineering Process

1.  **Planning**: Define the requirements engineering process and goals
2.  **Stakeholder Analysis**: Identify and analyze the stakeholders and their needs
3.  **Requirements Gathering**: Collect and document the requirements from stakeholders
4.  **Requirements Analysis**: Analyze the collected requirements and identify any inconsistencies or conflicts
5.  **Requirements Definition**: Define the requirements in a clear and concise manner
6.  **Requirements Documentation**: Document the requirements in a suitable format
7.  **Requirements Maintenance**: Maintain the requirements throughout the project lifecycle

## **7.2: Requirements Gathering Techniques**

### Definition

Requirements gathering techniques are methods used to collect and document the requirements of a software system.

### Types of Requirements Gathering Techniques

- **Interviews**: one-on-one conversations with stakeholders to gather information
- **Surveys**: questionnaires sent to a group of stakeholders to gather information
- **Focus Groups**: group discussions with stakeholders to gather information
- **Workshops**: meetings with stakeholders to gather information
- **Use Cases**: a way of documenting the interactions between the user and the software system

### Best Practices for Requirements Gathering

- **Use a variety of techniques**: use multiple techniques to gather requirements
- **Be thorough**: ensure that all stakeholders are involved in the requirements gathering process
- **Be timely**: gather requirements early in the project lifecycle
- **Be accurate**: ensure that the requirements gathered are accurate and complete

## **7.3: Requirements Analysis and Definition**

### Definition

Requirements analysis and definition is the process of analyzing the requirements gathered and defining them in a clear and concise manner.

### Types of Requirements Analysis

- **Data Analysis**: analyzing the data gathered during requirements gathering
- **Functional Analysis**: analyzing the functional requirements of the software system
- **Non-Functional Analysis**: analyzing the non-functional requirements of the software system

### Requirements Definition Techniques

- **Use Cases**: a way of documenting the interactions between the user and the software system
- **Use Case Diagrams**: a visual representation of the use cases
- **Class Diagrams**: a visual representation of the classes and objects in the software system
- **Entity-Relationship Diagrams**: a visual representation of the entities and relationships in the software system

### Best Practices for Requirements Analysis and Definition

- **Use a structured approach**: use a structured approach to analyze and define the requirements
- **Be thorough**: ensure that all requirements are analyzed and defined
- **Be accurate**: ensure that the requirements defined are accurate and complete
- **Be maintainable**: ensure that the requirements defined are maintainable throughout the project lifecycle
