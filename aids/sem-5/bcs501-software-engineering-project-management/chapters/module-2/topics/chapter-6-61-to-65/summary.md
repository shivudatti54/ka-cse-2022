# **Chapter 6: 6.1 to 6.5 Revision Notes**

### 6.1 Software Life Cycle Models

- Definition: A description of the stages involved in the development of a software system.
- Life Cycle Models:
  - Waterfall Model
  - Spiral Model
  - V-Model
  - Prototyping Model
- Advantages and Disadvantages of each model

### 6.2 Project Management Frameworks

- Definition: A structured approach to managing projects.
- Frameworks:
  - Project Management Institute (PMI) Framework
  - PRINCE2 Framework
  - Agile Framework
- Key components:
  - Initiating
  - Planning
  - Executing
  - Monitoring and Controlling
  - Closing

### 6.3 Project Management Life Cycle

- Definition: The stages involved in managing a project.
- Phases:
  - Initiating
  - Planning
  - Executing
  - Monitoring and Controlling
  - Closing
- Key activities:
  - Defining project scope
  - Identifying and scheduling tasks
  - Allocating resources
  - Monitoring progress

### 6.4 Risk Management

- Definition: The process of identifying, assessing, and mitigating risks.
- Risk Management Techniques:
  - Risk Identification
  - Risk Assessment
  - Risk Prioritization
  - Risk Mitigation
- Formula:
  - Risk = Probability x Impact

### 6.5 Quality Management

- Definition: The process of ensuring that software meets requirements and is of high quality.
- Quality Management Techniques:
  - Test-Driven Development (TDD)
  - Continuous Integration (CI)
  - Continuous Deployment (CD)
- Definition of Quality:
  - Conformance to requirements
  - Fitness for purpose
