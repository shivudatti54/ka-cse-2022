# Requirements Modeling Scenarios, Information and Analysis Classes

### 12 Hours Module: Software Engineering & Project Management

**Topic Overview**

Requirements modeling is a crucial step in software engineering that involves creating a detailed and accurate representation of the system's functional and non-functional requirements. This module will delve into the concepts of requirement analysis, scenario-based modeling, and UML models that supplement the Use Case diagram.

### Requirement Analysis

**Definition:** Requirement analysis is the process of identifying, documenting, and analyzing the functional and non-functional requirements of a system.

**Importance:** Requirements analysis is essential to ensure that the system meets the stakeholders' needs and expectations. It helps to identify potential problems and inconsistencies in the requirements, which can lead to cost savings and reduced project risks.

**Steps Involved in Requirement Analysis:**

- Identify the stakeholders and their needs
- Gather and document the requirements
- Analyze the requirements for consistency and completeness
- Identify potential problems and inconsistencies
- Prioritize the requirements based on their importance and urgency

**Example:**

Suppose we are developing an online shopping system for a retail company. The stakeholders are the customers, sales team, and management. The requirements analysis team identifies the functional requirements, such as:

- User registration and login
- Product catalog management
- Shopping cart management
- Payment processing

Non-functional requirements, such as:

- Security and data integrity
- User experience and usability
- Performance and scalability

### Scenario-Based Modeling

**Definition:** Scenario-based modeling is a technique used in requirements engineering to develop a model of the system's behavior based on real-world scenarios.

**Importance:** Scenario-based modeling helps to capture the nuances of the system's behavior and ensures that the system meets the stakeholders' needs and expectations.

**Steps Involved in Scenario-Based Modeling:**

- Identify the scenarios that the system will encounter
- Develop a model of the system's behavior for each scenario
- Analyze the scenarios for potential problems and inconsistencies
- Identify the stakeholders' needs and expectations for each scenario

**Example:**

Suppose we are developing an online banking system. The scenario-based modeling team identifies the following scenarios:

- User logs in to the system
- User initiates a transfer
- User views their account balance
- System fails due to technical issues

The team develops a model of the system's behavior for each scenario and analyzes them for potential problems and inconsistencies.

### UML Models that Supplement the Use Case Diagram

**Definition:** UML models are visual representations of the system's structure and behavior.

**Importance:** UML models provide a detailed and accurate representation of the system's functional and non-functional requirements.

**Types of UML Models:**

- Class Diagram
- Component Diagram
- Deployment Diagram
- Activity Diagram
- State Machine Diagram

**Example:**

Suppose we are developing an online shopping system. The UML team creates a class diagram that shows the relationships between the classes, such as:

- Customer
- Order
- Product
- Payment

The team also creates a component diagram that shows the relationships between the components, such as:

- Front-end component
- Back-end component
- Database component

The team uses the UML models to supplement the use case diagram and provide a detailed and accurate representation of the system's functional and non-functional requirements.
