### Learning Purpose: Distributed and Cloud Computing

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is fundamental because distributed systems form the architectural backbone of modern cloud computing. Understanding how resources are pooled, virtualized, and delivered over a network is essential for designing, deploying, and managing scalable and reliable applications in today's technology-driven world.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core principles of distributed systems—including concurrency, parallelism, and fault tolerance—and how these concepts are applied in cloud computing models (IaaS, PaaS, SaaS). They will explore the mechanisms behind virtualization, load balancing, and distributed storage that enable the cloud's agility and economies of scale.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds upon knowledge of computer networks, operating systems, and virtualization from previous semesters. It provides the foundational understanding required for subsequent topics like cloud security, big data analytics, and DevOps, which rely on distributed cloud infrastructure to function.

**4. Real-world applications**
The principles learned are applied in nearly every digital service we use today, from streaming platforms (Netflix) and social media (Facebook) to enterprise SaaS products (Salesforce) and large-scale data processing on platforms like AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud.
