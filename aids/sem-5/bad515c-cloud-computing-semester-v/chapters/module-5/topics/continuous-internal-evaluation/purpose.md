### **Learning Purpose: Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) in Cloud Computing**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) is a critical pedagogical shift from high-stakes, end-of-semester exams to a more holistic assessment of a student's ongoing progress. In a dynamic field like cloud computing, where technologies and best practices evolve rapidly, CIE ensures students consistently engage with and master practical, real-world skills throughout the semester, rather than just cramming theoretical knowledge.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn how their practical skills in cloud platforms (like AWS, Azure, or GCP) are formatively assessed through mechanisms such as module-wise quizzes, hands-on lab assignments, mini-projects (e.g., deploying a scalable web app), and presentations. They will understand the evaluation criteria for these tasks, focusing on implementation, problem-solving, cost optimization, and security.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
CIE directly connects and reinforces all core cloud concepts. An assignment on **virtual machines (Module 2)** tests Iaas understanding; a project on **serverless functions (Module 4)** assesses FaaS and event-driven architecture; a quiz on **cloud security (Module 3)** ensures foundational knowledge is solidified. It weaves discrete modules into a cohesive skill set.

**4. Real-world applications**
This process mirrors the agile, iterative development and continuous integration/continuous deployment (CI/CD) cycles used in the tech industry. Students experience a real-world workflow of being constantly evaluated on deliverables, receiving feedback, and iterating to improve—a direct preparation for their future careers in cloud engineering, DevOps, and solutions architecture.
