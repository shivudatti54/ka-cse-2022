### Learning Purpose: Connectionless & Connection-Oriented Services

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is fundamental as it explains the two primary methods of data communication that form the backbone of all computer networks. Understanding their differences is crucial for designing, implementing, and troubleshooting network applications and infrastructure.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to distinguish between connection-oriented services (like TCP), which establish a dedicated path for reliable, in-order data delivery, and connectionless services (like UDP), which send independent packets for faster, but less reliable, transmission. They will analyze the trade-offs between reliability, speed, and overhead.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This knowledge directly connects to transport layer protocols (TCP/UDP), network layer routing (IP), and quality of service (QoS). It provides the essential context needed to understand how higher-level application protocols (e.g., HTTP, VoIP, DNS) choose the appropriate service for their specific needs.

**4. Real-world applications**
This is applied whenever you use the internet: loading a web page (TCP ensures every part loads correctly), streaming video (UDP prioritizes speed over occasional packet loss), or making a DNS query (UDP for a fast, single-packet response).
