### Learning Purpose: Davie's Quality of Service Architecture

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding Davie's architecture is crucial because it provides a seminal framework for implementing Quality of Service (QoS) on the internet. As networks carry increasingly diverse traffic (e.g., video, voice, data), ensuring reliable performance for time-sensitive applications is a fundamental challenge. This topic moves beyond basic best-effort delivery, teaching the mechanisms required to manage bandwidth, control delay, and minimize jitter.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core components of Davie's QoS architecture, specifically the operation of the Differentiated Services (DiffServ) model. This includes classifying and marking packets, defining Per-Hop Behaviors (PHBs) like Expedited Forwarding (EF) and Assured Forwarding (AF), and configuring routers to manage traffic flows accordingly.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module builds directly on knowledge of the network layer (IP) and transport layer (TCP/UDP). It connects the abstract concept of QoS to practical router configuration and traffic engineering. It also contrasts with other QoS models like IntServ, providing a broader understanding of the solutions available for managing network performance.

**4. Real-world applications**
The principles learned are directly applicable to designing and maintaining modern enterprise and service provider networks. They are used to prioritize VoIP calls, ensure smooth video conferencing, guarantee bandwidth for critical business applications, and create tiered internet service offerings.
