### Learning Purpose: Module 5 - Group Projects in Computer Networks

**1. Why is this topic important?**
In the professional world, networks are never designed, implemented, or troubleshot by a single individual. This topic is crucial because it mirrors the collaborative nature of the industry, developing essential soft skills like teamwork, communication, and project management alongside technical expertise.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to apply theoretical networking concepts (e.g., subnetting, routing protocols, network security) to a tangible group project. They will gain practical experience in dividing complex tasks, collaboratively solving problems, and integrating individual components into a functional whole, simulating a real-world development cycle.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module synthesizes knowledge from all previous modules. It requires students to connect concepts like IP addressing (Module 2), switching and routing (Module 3), and application layer protocols (Module 4) to build a complete, multi-faceted network solution, reinforcing how discrete topics interrelate in a practical system.

**4. Real-world applications**
The skills practiced are directly applicable to roles such as network engineer, systems analyst, and security specialist. Students will emulate real-world scenarios like deploying a secure corporate LAN, configuring network services for a small business, or designing a scalable network infrastructure, preparing them for collaborative technical projects in their careers.
