### Learning Purpose: IPv4 Address

**1. Why is this topic important?**
IPv4 addressing is the foundational mechanism that enables devices to communicate on the Internet and most modern networks. Understanding it is critical because every networked device requires a unique IP address to send and receive data. Despite the transition to IPv6, IPv4 remains widely deployed, making proficiency in its concepts essential for any networking professional.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the structure of a 32-bit IPv4 address, including its division into network and host portions. They will master subnet mask notation, calculate network addresses, broadcast addresses, and valid host ranges. Key skills include performing subnetting to efficiently allocate address space and understand the difference between public and private IP addresses.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This knowledge directly connects to other core networking concepts. It is the practical application of the theoretical OSI and TCP/IP models at the Network Layer. Understanding IPv4 is a prerequisite for learning about routing protocols (like RIP and OSPF), Network Address Translation (NAT), firewalls, and the motivation for the development of IPv6.

**4. Real-world applications**
This skill is used daily by network administrators to design, troubleshoot, and maintain networks of all sizes. From assigning addresses in a small office to designing the subnetted architecture for a large enterprise or ISP, subnetting ensures efficient and organized use of the limited IPv4 address space. It is fundamental to implementing security policies and network segmentation.
