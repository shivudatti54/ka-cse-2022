### Learning Purpose: Guided Media

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Guided media form the physical backbone of nearly all modern wired networks. Understanding their properties, limitations, and appropriate use cases is fundamental for any network professional. It is the first step in designing, implementing, and troubleshooting the physical infrastructure that carries our data.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to differentiate between the primary types of guided media—twisted-pair cable, coaxial cable, and fiber-optic cable. They will analyze and compare their key characteristics, including bandwidth, cost, ease of installation, attenuation, and immunity to electromagnetic interference (EMI).

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This knowledge provides the physical layer (Layer 1) context for the OSI and TCP/IP models studied previously. It directly connects to subsequent topics on data transmission, signal encoding, and network topologies. The choice of media influences higher-layer protocol performance, such as data link layer error control and network layer routing efficiency.

**4. Real-world applications**
This applies directly to selecting the correct cable for a specific need: UTP for Ethernet LANs, coaxial for cable internet (DOCSIS), and fiber-optic for high-speed backbones (e.g., data centers, undersea cables, and FTTx internet connections like Verizon FIOS).
