### Learning Purpose: Introduction to Physical Layer

**1. Importance:** This topic is fundamental as the Physical Layer forms the bedrock of all network communication. It is responsible for the actual transmission and reception of raw, unstructured data bits over a physical medium. Understanding its principles is crucial for grasping how data travels across cables, radio waves, and fiber optics, making it the essential first step in understanding computer networks.

**2. Student Learning:** Students will learn the core concepts of data transmission, including signal types (digital/analog), modulation techniques, and the characteristics of various transmission media like twisted-pair copper cable, coaxial cable, fiber optics, and wireless. They will understand key terminology such as bandwidth, throughput, and latency, and how factors like attenuation and interference impact signal quality.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts:** This knowledge provides the crucial groundwork for the Data Link layer (Module 2), which builds upon these raw bits to create structured frames for reliable node-to-node delivery. It is the hardware foundation upon which all higher-layer protocols and software-defined networking logic ultimately operate.

**4. Real-World Applications:** This applies directly to choosing the right cabling for a LAN (e.g., Cat 6 vs. fiber), understanding the specs of your home Wi-Fi router (frequencies and bandwidth), and comprehending the technology behind internet infrastructure like undersea communication cables and 5G cellular networks.
