### **Learning Purpose: Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
EDA is the critical first step in any data analysis or data science project. It is the process of summarizing, visualizing, and understanding the main characteristics of a dataset *before* applying complex models. Skipping EDA often leads to flawed analyses, incorrect assumptions, and unreliable results. It is the foundation for all subsequent, more advanced work.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core techniques and mindset of EDA. This includes generating summary statistics (e.g., mean, median, standard deviation), identifying missing data and outliers, and, most importantly, creating a variety of visualizations (e.g., histograms, box plots, scatter plots) to uncover underlying patterns, trends, and relationships within the data.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
EDA is the essential bridge between raw data acquisition (Data Wrangling) and formal statistical modeling or Machine Learning (ML). The insights gained from EDA directly inform data cleaning strategies, feature selection, and the choice of appropriate models later in the data science lifecycle.

**4. Real-world applications**
EDA is universally applied across industries. Analysts use it to explore sales trends, diagnose system performance issues, understand customer behavior from web analytics, and form initial hypotheses for scientific research. It is the primary tool for generating initial, data-driven questions and insights.
