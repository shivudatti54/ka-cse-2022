### Learning Purpose: Simple Scatter Plots

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Simple scatter plots are a foundational tool in Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) because they visually reveal the relationship between two continuous variables. This immediate visual insight is crucial for identifying trends, patterns, correlations, and potential outliers before applying any complex statistical models, forming the bedrock of data-driven inquiry.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn how to construct and interpret a simple scatter plot. This includes selecting appropriate variables, plotting data points, and analyzing the resulting graph to describe the direction (positive/negative), form (linear/non-linear), and strength of a relationship. They will also learn to identify clusters and anomalies within the data.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds on understanding univariate plots (e.g., histograms from Module 3) by introducing bivariate analysis. It is a prerequisite for more advanced concepts like correlation coefficients, regression analysis, and multi-variable visualizations (e.g., using color or size for a third variable).

**4. Real-world applications**
Scatter plots are used ubiquitously across fields. Analysts use them to explore relationships like marketing spend vs. sales revenue, epidemiologists plot infection rates against time, and data scientists use them to check for feature correlations before building machine learning models.
