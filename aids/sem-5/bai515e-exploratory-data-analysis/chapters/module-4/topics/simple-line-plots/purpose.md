### **Learning Purpose: Simple Line Plots**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Simple line plots are a fundamental and powerful tool in exploratory data analysis (EDA). They are the primary method for visualizing the relationship between two continuous variables, especially over time. Understanding how to create and interpret them is crucial because they reveal trends, patterns, cycles, and outliers that are not apparent in summary statistics or tables, forming the basis for further investigation and hypothesis generation.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core principles of constructing effective line plots, including selecting appropriate axes, labeling, and titling for clarity. They will gain hands-on experience using a Python library (like Matplotlib or Seaborn) to plot a single variable (e.g., stock price) against a sequence (e.g., time). The focus will be on interpreting the visualized data to identify key characteristics such as upward or downward trends, seasonal patterns, and unexpected spikes or dips.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is a direct application of data visualization principles learned earlier. It builds upon knowledge of pandas DataFrames (for data manipulation) and serves as a prerequisite for more advanced visualizations like multi-line plots (comparing multiple categories) and time series decomposition. It is the visual counterpart to statistical concepts like correlation and trend analysis.

**4. Real-world applications**
Line plots are ubiquitous in fields that track data over intervals. Analysts use them to visualize stock market performance, economists to plot unemployment rates over months, meteorologists to display temperature changes, and businesses to track website traffic or sales figures across time, enabling data-driven decisions.
