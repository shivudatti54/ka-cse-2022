### **Learning Purpose: Sorted Arrays**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Sorting is a fundamental preprocessing step in EDA. A sorted array transforms raw, chaotic data into an ordered sequence, instantly revealing patterns, trends, and anomalies. It is the cornerstone for efficient computation of summary statistics, like medians and percentiles, and is a prerequisite for more advanced analytical techniques.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to implement and apply sorting algorithms to datasets. They will understand how sorting enables the quick identification of minimum/maximum values, ranges, and potential outliers. Crucially, they will learn to interpret the ordered data to extract meaningful insights about the distribution and central tendency of a variable.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
Sorted arrays are directly linked to calculating **summary statistics** (e.g., median, quartiles). They form the basis for **frequency distribution** tables and are essential for creating visualizations like **histograms** and **box plots**. Furthermore, sorting is a prerequisite for efficient searching and is a key concept in algorithm optimization and **Binary Search**.

**4. Real-world applications**
This skill is applied everywhere data is analyzed: identifying top-selling products in retail, finding the highest-paying job sectors in economics, analyzing patient wait times in healthcare, or detecting performance bottlenecks in computing by sorting process execution times.
