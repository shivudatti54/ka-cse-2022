# **Natural Resources: Water Resources – Availability & Quality Aspects**

## **Introduction**

Water is one of the most essential natural resources required for human survival. It is a vital component of our ecosystem and is used for various purposes such as drinking, irrigation, industry, and energy production. However, the availability and quality of water resources are becoming increasingly concerns due to various factors such as climate change, population growth, and pollution.

## **What is Water Resource?**

- Definition: A water resource is a natural or artificial source of water that is available for human consumption, agriculture, industry, or other purposes.
- Types of water resources:
  - Surface water: Rivers, lakes, reservoirs, and wetlands.
  - Groundwater: Aquifers and underground water.
  - Transboundary water: Water shared by two or more countries.

## **Availability of Water Resources**

- **Global Water Availability:** The global water availability is approximately 1.386 billion km3, which is only about 2.5% of the Earth's total water resources.
- **Water Scarcity:** Water scarcity is a major issue in many parts of the world, particularly in arid and semi-arid regions.
- **Factors Affecting Water Availability:**
  - Climate change
  - Population growth
  - Urbanization
  - Agriculture
  - Industrial activities

## **Water Quality Aspects**

- **Definition:** Water quality refers to the physical, chemical, biological, and radiological characteristics of water that make it suitable for human consumption, agriculture, industry, or other purposes.
- **Factors Affecting Water Quality:**
  - Atmospheric deposition
  - Industrial effluents
  - Agricultural runoff
  - Municipal wastewater
  - Natural disasters (e.g., floods, landslides)
- **Types of Water Pollutants:**
  - Chemical pollutants
  - Biological pollutants
  - Physical pollutants
  - Nutrient pollutants

## **Water Quality Parameters**

- **Physical Parameters:**
  - pH
  - Temperature
  - Turbidity
  - Dissolved oxygen
- **Chemical Parameters:**
  - BOD (Biochemical Oxygen Demand)
  - COD (Chemical Oxygen Demand)
  - Total dissolved solids
  - Salinity
- **Biological Parameters:**
  - Bacteria
  - Viruses
  - Fungi
  - Algae

## **Conclusion**

In conclusion, water resources are a vital component of our ecosystem, and their availability and quality are becoming increasingly concerns due to various factors. Understanding the concepts of water resources, availability, and quality aspects is essential for managing water resources sustainably and ensuring the well-being of human populations and the environment.
