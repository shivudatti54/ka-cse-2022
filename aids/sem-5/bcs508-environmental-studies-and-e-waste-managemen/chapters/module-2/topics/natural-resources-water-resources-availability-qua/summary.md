# Natural Resources: Water Resources – Availability & Quality Aspects

=====================================

## **Definitions and Key Terms**

- **Water resources**: The total amount of water available in an area, including surface water, groundwater, and atmospheric water.
- **Freshwater**: Water with a salinity of less than 35 parts per thousand (ppt).
- **Groundwater**: Water stored beneath the Earth's surface in soil and rock pores.
- **Sustainable water management**: The practice of managing water resources in a way that maintains their quality and quantity for future generations.

## **Availability Aspects**

- **Water scarcity**: The lack of sufficient water supply for human consumption, agriculture, and industry.
- **Water availability**: The total amount of water available in an area, including surface water and groundwater.
- **Water demand**: The total amount of water required by humans, agriculture, and industry.
- **Water stress**: The state of water scarcity or lack of water resources.

## **Quality Aspects**

- **Water quality**: The physical, chemical, and biological characteristics of water that make it suitable for human consumption, agriculture, and industry.
- **Water pollution**: The introduction of pollutants or contaminants into water bodies, which can harm human health and the environment.
- **Water treatment**: The process of removing pollutants and contaminants from water to make it safe for human consumption.

## **Important Formulas and Theorems**

- **Water balance equation**: The equation that describes the relationship between water inputs (precipitation, infiltration) and outputs (evaporation, runoff).
  ```
  Precipitation (P) = Evaporation (E) + Runoff (R) + Percolation (p)
  ```
- **Water quality index**: A numerical value used to measure the quality of water based on its chemical, physical, and biological characteristics.

- Understand the importance of water treatment and water quality management.
