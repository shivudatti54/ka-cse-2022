# Water Borne Diseases & Water Induced Diseases

### Definition and Importance

- Water borne diseases: diseases caused by the ingestion of contaminated water.
- Water induced diseases: diseases caused by exposure to polluted water or contaminated water environments.

### Types of Water Borne Diseases

- Bacterial diseases:
  - Cholera
  - Typhoid fever
  - Dysentery
- Parasitic diseases:
  - Schistosomiasis
  - Leishmaniasis
  - Dracunculiasis
- Viral diseases:
  - Hepatitis A
  - Norovirus
  - Rotavirus
- Fungal diseases:
  - Coccidioidomycosis
  - Histoplasmosis
  - Candidiasis

### Factors Contributing to Water Borne Diseases

- Poor sanitation and hygiene
- Contaminated water sources (e.g. rivers, lakes, wells)
- Agricultural runoff
- Industrial waste
- Climate change

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- Water quality index (WQI): WQI = (pH + Temperature + Turbidity) / 3
- Safe drinking water standards:
  - pH: 6.5-8.5
  - Turbidity: < 1 NTU
  - Bacterial contamination: < 100 CFU/100ml

### Prevention and Control Measures

- Safe water treatment and disinfection
- Improved sanitation and hygiene practices
- Education and awareness programs
- Infrastructure development (e.g. water supply systems, sewage systems)

### Important Terms

- Contaminant: substance that pollutes water
- Vector: organism that transmits disease (e.g. mosquito, flea)
- Vector-borne disease: disease transmitted by a vector
- Waterborne pathogen: microorganism that causes disease through contaminated water.
