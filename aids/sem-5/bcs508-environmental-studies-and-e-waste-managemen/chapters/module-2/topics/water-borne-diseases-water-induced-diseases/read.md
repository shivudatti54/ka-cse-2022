# **Water Borne Diseases & Water Induced Diseases**

## **Introduction**

Water borne diseases and water induced diseases are health hazards caused by the consumption of contaminated water. These diseases can be prevented by ensuring access to clean drinking water and proper sanitation facilities.

## **What are Water Borne Diseases?**

Water borne diseases are illnesses caused by the consumption of water contaminated with pathogens such as bacteria, viruses, and parasites. These pathogens can multiply in water and cause infections in the human body.

## **Types of Water Borne Diseases**

- **Bacterial Water Borne Diseases**
  - Cholera
  - Typhoid fever
  - Dysentery
- **Viral Water Borne Diseases**
  - Hepatitis A
  - Rotavirus
  - Norovirus
- **Parasitic Water Borne Diseases**
  - Schistosomiasis
  - Hookworm disease
  - Amoebiasis

## **Causes of Water Borne Diseases**

- **Poor Water Treatment**: Inadequate treatment of water can lead to the presence of pathogens.
- **Contaminated Water Sources**: Water from contaminated sources such as rivers, lakes, and wells can be contaminated with pathogens.
- **Poor Sanitation**: Poor sanitation facilities can lead to the contamination of water with human waste.

## **Examples of Water Borne Diseases**

- Cholera: A diarrheal disease caused by Vibrio cholerae bacteria, which is commonly found in contaminated water.
- Typhoid fever: A bacterial disease caused by Salmonella Typhi, which is commonly found in contaminated water.
- Dysentery: A diarrheal disease caused by Shigella bacteria, which is commonly found in contaminated water.

## **Prevention and Control**

- **Proper Water Treatment**: Ensure that water is properly treated before consumption.
- **Proper Sanitation**: Ensure that sanitation facilities are properly maintained.
- **Safe Water Sources**: Ensure that water comes from safe sources such as municipal water supply or bottled water.

## **Water Induced Diseases**

Water induced diseases are health hazards caused by the lack of safe drinking water. These diseases can be prevented by ensuring access to clean drinking water and proper sanitation facilities.

## **Types of Water Induced Diseases**

- **Dehydration**: A condition caused by the lack of sufficient water intake.
- **Water Pollution**: A condition caused by the contamination of water with chemicals, heavy metals, and other pollutants.

## **Causes of Water Induced Diseases**

- **Lack of Access to Clean Drinking Water**: The lack of access to clean drinking water can lead to dehydration and water pollution.
- **Poor Water Quality**: Poor water quality can lead to water pollution.

## **Examples of Water Induced Diseases**

- Dehydration: A condition caused by the lack of sufficient water intake, which can be prevented by drinking enough water.
- Water Pollution: A condition caused by the contamination of water with chemicals, heavy metals, and other pollutants, which can be prevented by proper waste management and water treatment.

## **Prevention and Control**

- **Access to Clean Drinking Water**: Ensure that everyone has access to clean drinking water.
- **Proper Water Treatment**: Ensure that water is properly treated before consumption.
- **Proper Sanitation**: Ensure that sanitation facilities are properly maintained.

By understanding the causes, types, and prevention strategies for water borne diseases and water induced diseases, we can work towards ensuring access to clean drinking water and proper sanitation facilities for everyone.
