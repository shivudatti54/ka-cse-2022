# Self-Study Component (SSC): Case Studies of Air Pollution Episodes

### Definitions and Notations

- **Air Pollution**: The introduction of pollutants into the atmosphere, leading to detrimental effects on the environment and human health.
- **Parameter**: A characteristic of air pollution that can be measured and analyzed.
- **Concentration**: The amount of a pollutant per unit volume of air.

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Pollutant Concentration Formula**: Concentration (C) = Mass of pollutant (M) / Volume of air (V)
- **Air Quality Index (AQI) Formula**: AQI = (C1 \* W1 + C2 \* W2 + ... + Cn \* Wn) / (W1 + W2 + ... + Wn), where C1, C2, ..., Cn are pollutant concentrations, and W1, W2, ..., Wn are weight factors.

### Key Points - Case Studies

- **Beijing 2008**: High levels of particulate matter (PM2.5) and ozone (O3) caused severe air pollution, resulting in widespread respiratory problems.
- **Donora 1948**: A severe air pollution episode in Pennsylvania, USA, caused 20 deaths and 7,000 cases of respiratory illness due to high levels of particulate matter and sulfur dioxide (SO2).
- **Delhi 2016**: High levels of PM2.5 and ozone (O3) caused widespread respiratory problems, with estimated health impacts of 1.5 billion US dollars.

### Case Study of Air Pollution Episodes

- Factors contributing to air pollution episodes include:
  - Industrial activities
  - Vehicle emissions
  - Agricultural activities
  - Natural disasters
- Mitigation strategies include:
  - Implementing emission controls
  - Promoting clean energy
  - Improving waste management
  - Educating the public on air pollution prevention
