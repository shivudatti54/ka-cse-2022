# Composition and Generation

### Definitions

- **Composition**: The process of breaking down waste into its constituent components, such as plastics, metals, and organics.
- **Generation**: The process of producing waste, including the sources, amounts, and types of waste generated.

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **The Waste Hierarchy**: An ecological principle that prioritizes waste reduction, reuse, recycling, and composting.
- **The 4Rs**: Reduce, Reuse, Recycle, Recover (a framework for waste management decisions).
- **The Waste Management Hierarchy**: A pyramid representing the preferred order of waste management strategies: prevention, reduction, reuse, recycling, recovery, and disposal.

### Key Points

- **Composition**: Importance of understanding waste composition to develop effective waste treatment and management strategies.
- **Generation**: High levels of waste generation can lead to environmental problems, such as pollution and climate change.
- **Waste Composition Analysis**: Methods for analyzing waste composition, including the "Three Rs" (Recycling, Reuse, Reduction).
- **Waste-to-Resource**: Strategies for converting waste into valuable commodities, such as biofuels, biogas, and raw materials.
- **Life Cycle Assessment (LCA)**: A methodology for evaluating the environmental impacts of products and services throughout their life cycle.

### Important Concepts

- **Waste Minimization**: Strategies for reducing the amount of waste generated, such as design for recyclability and reuse.
- **Closed-Loop Production**: A production system where materials are constantly cycled back into production, minimizing waste.
- **Zero-Waste Goal**: A target of producing no waste, achieved through a combination of waste reduction, reuse, and recycling.
