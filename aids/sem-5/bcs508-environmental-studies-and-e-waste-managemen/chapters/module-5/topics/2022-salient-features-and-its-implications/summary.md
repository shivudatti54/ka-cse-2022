# **2022 - Salient Features and its Implications**

### Environmental Studies and E-Waste Management

#### Key Points

- **Major Developments in 2022:**
  - **Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR):** Many countries, including the US, EU, and Japan, implemented or strengthened EPR policies to hold manufacturers accountable for electronic waste (e-waste).
  - **Global E-Waste Generation:** The amount of e-waste generated worldwide reached 74 million metric tons in 2022, according to the United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP).
  - **Circular Economy Policies:** Governments introduced policies to promote the recycling and reuse of e-waste, such as deposit-refund schemes and take-back programs.
  - **Green Technology Advancements:** The development of more efficient e-waste recycling technologies and the growth of sustainable materials like graphene and nanocellulose in electronics.
- **Implications:**
  - **Environmental Concerns:** The increased generation of e-waste poses significant environmental risks, including pollution, health hazards, and resource depletion.
  - **Economic Opportunities:** The recycling and reuse of e-waste can create new economic opportunities, generating revenue and jobs in the industry.
  - **Social Impacts:** The implementation of EPR policies and circular economy strategies can contribute to reduced e-waste, decreased greenhouse gas emissions, and improved public health.

### Important Formulas, Definitions, and Theorems

- **Waste Hierarchy:** The principle of minimizing waste by maximizing reuse and recycling, followed by recovery and disposal as a last resort.
- **E-Waste Generation Formula:** E-Waste (kg) = Population (in millions) \* Technology (per capita) \* Usage (per capita) \* Lifespan (years)
- **Recycling Rate Formula:** Recycling Rate (%) = (Recycled Material (kg) / Total Waste (kg)) \* 100
- **Circular Economy Theorem:** A circular economy is a regenerative system that aims to keep resources in use for as long as possible, extract the maximum value from waste and errors, and regenerate nature.
