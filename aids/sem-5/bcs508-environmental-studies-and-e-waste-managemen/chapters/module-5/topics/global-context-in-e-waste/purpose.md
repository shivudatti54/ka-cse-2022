Learning "Global context in e-waste" is crucial as it highlights the transboundary nature of electronic waste, emphasizing the need for international cooperation and regulation to mitigate its environmental and health impacts. This topic matters because e-waste is a growing concern worldwide, with millions of tons of hazardous waste being generated annually. Real-world applications include understanding e-waste export policies, illicit dumping, and the role of multinational corporations in e-waste management. This topic also connects to concepts of environmental justice, sustainability, and globalization, underscoring the need for a holistic approach to e-waste management.
