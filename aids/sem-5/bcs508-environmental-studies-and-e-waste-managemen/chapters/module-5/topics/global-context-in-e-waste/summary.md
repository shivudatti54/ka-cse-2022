# **Global Context in E-Waste**

### Definition and Importance

- E-waste: Waste generated from the disposal of electronic devices (e.g., computers, phones, televisions)
- Estimated annual global e-waste generation: 50 million metric tons (UNEP)
- Critical environmental and health concerns due to toxic materials (e.g., lead, mercury, cadmium)

### Environmental and Health Impacts

- Air and water pollution
- Soil contamination
- Human exposure to toxic chemicals
- Disruption of ecosystems and biodiversity

### Global E-Waste Management Challenges

- Lack of effective waste management infrastructure
- Insufficient regulations and policies
- Inadequate public awareness and education
- Limited access to recycling facilities

### Key Statistics and Figures

- 20% of e-waste is recycled (UNEP)
- 50% of e-waste ends up in landfills or incinerators (UNEP)
- $50 billion annual economic loss due to e-waste management challenges (UNEP)

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- Waste management hierarchy:
  - Reduce
  - Reuse
  - Recycle
  - Recover
  - Dispose
- E-waste treatment costs:
  - Recycling: $50-100 per ton
  - Incineration: $100-200 per ton
  - Landfilling: $20-50 per ton
