# E-Waste (Management and Handling) Rules

**Important Definitions and Theorems:**

- E-waste: Electronic waste generated from the disposal of electronic devices and equipment.
- Hazardous Waste: Waste that poses a risk to human health and the environment due to its toxic or radioactive properties.

**Key Points:**

- **E-Waste Management Rules 2011:**
  - Regulates the import, export, storage, transportation, and disposal of e-waste.
  - Establishes a system for segregation, treatment, and disposal of e-waste.
- **Key Provisions:**
  - Classification of e-waste into categories (hazardous and non-hazardous).
  - Requirement for manufacturers to register with the Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB).
  - Establishment of e-waste collection centers and recycling facilities.
- **Liability and Responsibility:**
  - Manufacturers and importers liable for e-waste generated by their products.
  - Responsibility for proper disposal and recycling of e-waste.
- **Penalties and Fines:**
  - Imposed for non-compliance with the rules and regulations.
  - Fines ranging from ₹5,00,000 to ₹10,00,000.

**Important Formulas and Concepts:**

- No specific formulas or concepts are required for E-waste (Management and Handling) Rules. However, understanding of waste management principles and environmental regulations is essential.
