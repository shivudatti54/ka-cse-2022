# **Self-Study Component (SSC) Revision Notes: E-Waste Management**

**Topic:** E-Waste (Management) Amendment Rules, 2023, 2024

**Textbook 1:** Chapter 6
**Textbook 2:** Chapter 2
**e-Resource:** 3

**Key Points:**

- **Definition of E-Waste:** Electronic waste generated by the disposal of electronic devices, including computers, smartphones, televisions, and other electronic equipment.
- **E-Waste Management Rules, 2023:** Amendments to the previous rules, focusing on responsible e-waste management, recycling, and prohibition of exports.
- **Key Provisions:**
  - Prohibition on export of e-waste
  - Mandatory segregation, collection, and transportation of e-waste
  - Establishment of e-waste treatment facilities
  - Creation of a national database for e-waste management
- **Importance of E-Waste Management:**
  - Environmental impact of e-waste on soil, water, and air
  - Health risks associated with e-waste disposal
  - Economic benefits of responsible e-waste management

**Important Formulas and Definitions:**

- **E-Waste Generation Rate:** The rate at which e-waste is generated per unit of GDP.
- **E-Waste Recycling Rate:** The percentage of e-waste that is recycled.
- **Circular Economy:** A system where waste is minimized and resources are utilized efficiently.

**Theorems:**

- **Zero Waste Policy:** A policy aimed at eliminating waste generation and promoting sustainable consumption patterns.
- **Polluter Pays Principle:** A principle that requires polluters to pay for the environmental damage caused by their activities.

**Quick Revision Tips:**

- Understand the key provisions and importance of e-waste management
- Familiarize yourself with the amendments and changes in the E-Waste Management Rules, 2023
- Practice identifying the key formulas, definitions, and theorems related to e-waste management
