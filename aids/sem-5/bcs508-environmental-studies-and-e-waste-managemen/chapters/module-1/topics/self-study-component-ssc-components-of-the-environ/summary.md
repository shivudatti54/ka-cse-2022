# Self-Study Component (SSC): Components of the Environment

### Definitions

- **Environmental component**: The biotic and abiotic components that affect an ecosystem.
- **Biotic components**: Living organisms that interact with each other and their environment.
  - Producers (autotrophs): Plants and algae that produce their own food.
  - Consumers (heterotrophs): Animals and fungi that consume other organisms for energy.
  - Decomposers: Microorganisms that break down dead organisms.
- **Abiotic components**: Non-living factors that affect an ecosystem.
  - Temperature
  - Precipitation
  - Light
  - Soil
  - Water

### Theories and Formulas

- **Carson's Theorem**: A small change in one component of an ecosystem can have a large impact on the entire ecosystem.
- **The Law of Reversibility**: A change in one component of an ecosystem can have a corresponding change in another component.
- **The 8-Millennium Development Goals**: A framework for sustainable development and environmental conservation.

### Important Concepts

- **Ecosystem services**: The benefits that humans receive from functioning ecosystems.
- **Biodiversity**: The variety of plant and animal species in an ecosystem.
- **Ecological footprint**: The impact of human activities on the environment.

### Key Points

- Environmental components include biotic (living) and abiotic (non-living) factors.
- Biotic components interact with each other and their environment.
- Abiotic components affect the ecosystem's physical and chemical properties.
- Small changes in one component can have a large impact on the ecosystem.
- Ecosystem services, biodiversity, and ecological footprint are important concepts in environmental studies.
