# Environmental Studies and E-Waste Management

### E-resource: 1

**Definition:** Electronic resource (E-resource) refers to digital content, such as e-books, articles, and online databases, that are accessed through digital platforms.

**Key Concepts:**

- **Green computing**: The practice of designing, manufacturing, and operating IT products and systems in an environmentally friendly manner.
- **Electronic waste (e-waste)**: Discarded electronic devices, such as computers, smartphones, and televisions, that contain hazardous materials like lead, mercury, and cadmium.
- **E-waste management**: The process of collecting, recycling, and disposing of e-waste in a responsible and environmentally friendly manner.

**Important Formulas:**

- **E-waste generation rate**: The rate at which e-waste is generated, typically expressed in pounds per person per year.
- **Recycling rate**: The percentage of e-waste that is recycled or reused.

**Definitions:**

- **Sustainability**: The practice of meeting current needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.
- **Triple bottom line**: A concept that considers not only financial and social impacts but also environmental impacts.

**Theorems:**

- **The law of diminishing returns**: As the amount of e-waste increases, the costs of recycling and disposal also increase.
- **The concept of circular economy**: A system where materials are kept in use for as long as possible, extracting the maximum value from them.

**Key Terms:**

- **Green IT**: The practice of using IT products and services in an environmentally friendly manner.
- **E-responsibility**: The responsibility of individuals, organizations, and governments to manage e-waste in a responsible and environmentally friendly manner.
