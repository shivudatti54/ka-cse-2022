# Sustainability: 17 SDG Targets and Possible Actions

## Introduction

The United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are a set of 17 goals aimed at ending poverty, protecting the planet, and ensuring peace and prosperity for all. The SDGs are built on the Millennium Development Goals (MDGs) and are designed to be achieved by 2030. The 17 SDGs are:

1. No Poverty
2. Zero Hunger
3. Good Health and Well-being
4. Quality Education
5. Gender Equality
6. Clean Water and Sanitation
7. Affordable and Clean Energy
8. Decent Work and Economic Growth
9. Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure
10. Reduced Inequalities
11. Sustainable Cities and Communities
12. Responsible Consumption and Production
13. Climate Action
14. Life Below Water
15. Life on Land
16. Peace, Justice and Strong Institutions
17. Partnerships for the Goals

In this report, we will focus on the 17 SDG targets related to sustainability, specifically SDG 7 (Affordable and Clean Energy), SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities and Communities), SDG 12 (Responsible Consumption and Production), SDG 13 (Climate Action), and SDG 14 (Life Below Water).

## SDG 7: Affordable and Clean Energy

### Target 7.1: Ensure access to reliable, modern and modern energy

- Goal: Ensure universal access to energy, improve energy efficiency, and promote clean energy technologies.
- Possible actions:
  - Invest in renewable energy sources, such as solar and wind power.
  - Improve energy efficiency in buildings and industry.
  - Develop and promote clean energy technologies, such as electric vehicles and green buildings.
  - Implement policies and regulations to ensure access to energy for all.

### Target 7.2: Increase energy efficiency

- Goal: Improve energy efficiency in buildings, industry, and transportation.
- Possible actions:
  - Implement energy-efficient technologies, such as LED lighting and insulation.
  - Promote energy-efficient transportation, such as electric vehicles.
  - Develop and implement energy-efficient building codes and standards.
  - Provide incentives for energy-efficient practices.

### Target 7.3: Support the transition to a clean energy economy

- Goal: Support the transition to a clean energy economy, including the development and use of clean energy technologies.
- Possible actions:
  - Invest in clean energy research and development.
  - Develop and implement policies and regulations to support the transition to a clean energy economy.
  - Promote clean energy technologies, such as solar and wind power.
  - Provide incentives for the adoption of clean energy technologies.

### Target 7.4: Discourage consumption and wasteful use of energy

- Goal: Discourage consumption and wasteful use of energy.
- Possible actions:
  - Implement energy-efficient practices, such as turning off lights and electronics when not in use.
  - Promote energy-efficient appliances and technologies.
  - Develop and implement policies and regulations to discourage energy waste.
  - Educate consumers about energy efficiency and conservation.

### Target 7.5: Increase energy access for all

- Goal: Increase energy access for all, including the poor and vulnerable.
- Possible actions:
  - Invest in energy access programs, such as solar home systems and energy-efficient cooking stoves.
  - Promote energy-efficient appliances and technologies.
  - Develop and implement policies and regulations to ensure energy access for all.
  - Provide incentives for energy access.

## Case Study: Rwanda's Renewable Energy Plan

Rwanda has made significant progress in increasing access to renewable energy, including solar and wind power. The country has set a goal to generate 80% of its electricity from renewable sources by 2024. To achieve this goal, Rwanda has implemented a number of policies and programs, including:

- Developing a national renewable energy policy.
- Providing incentives for the adoption of renewable energy technologies.
- Investing in renewable energy infrastructure, such as solar farms and wind turbines.
- Promoting energy-efficient practices and technologies.

Results: Rwanda has seen significant improvements in energy access, with 83% of the population now having access to electricity.
