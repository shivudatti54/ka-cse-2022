# Sustainability: 17SDG Targets and Possible Actions

### Environmental Studies and E-Waste Management

#### Overview of SDGs

- Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are a set of 17 global goals adopted by the United Nations in 2015.
- The 17 SDGs are designed to be a "blueprint to achieve a better and more sustainable future for all."

#### 17 SDG Targets

- **SDG 12: Responsible Consumption and Production**
  - Target 12.1: "Ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns"
  - Target 12.2: "Achieve sustainable consumption and production industries, including ensuring the use of sustainable materials and modalities of production and consumption"
- **SDG 6: Clean Water and Sanitation**
  - Target 6.1: "Ensure universal access to safe and affordable drinking water"
  - Target 6.2: "Ensure access to sanitation and hygiene facilities"
- **SDG 7: Affordable and Clean Energy**
  - Target 7.1: "Increase the global share of renewable energy sources in the energy mix"
  - Target 7.2: "Increase energy efficiency"
- **SDG 14: Life Below Water**
  - Target 14.1: "Conserve and sustainably use the world's oceans, seas, and marine resources"
  - Target 14.2: "Protect and restore marine ecosystems"
- **SDG 15: Life on Land**
  - Target 15.1: "Protect, restore, and promote sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems"
  - Target 15.2: "Protect marine biodiversity"
- **SDG 13: Climate Action**
  - Target 13.1: "Take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts"
  - Target 13.2: "Increase the global ambition of climate action"
- **SDG 17: Partnerships for the Goals**
  - Target 17.1: "Strengthen the means of implementation and revitalize the global partnership for sustainable development"

#### Important Formulas, Definitions, and Theorems

- **Cradle to Cradle (C2C) Design**: A design approach that aims to create products and systems that are restorative and regenerative by design.
- **Circular Economy**: An economic model that aims to reduce waste and the consumption of resources by promoting the reuse and recycling of products.
- **Life Cycle Assessment (LCA)**: A method used to assess the environmental impacts of a product or system throughout its entire life cycle.
- **System Dynamics**: A methodology used to analyze complex systems and understand the interactions between them.

#### Possible Actions

- Implement sustainable supply chain management practices
- Reduce energy consumption and greenhouse gas emissions
- Promote the use of renewable energy sources
- Implement waste reduction and recycling programs
- Support sustainable consumption patterns
- Encourage the use of sustainable materials and products.
