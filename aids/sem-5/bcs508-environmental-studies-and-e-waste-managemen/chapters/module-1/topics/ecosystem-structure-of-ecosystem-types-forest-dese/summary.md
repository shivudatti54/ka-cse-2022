# Ecosystem: Structure of Ecosystem, Types

### Structure of an Ecosystem

- **Components:**
  - Producers (autotrophs)
  - Consumers (heterotrophs)
  - Decomposers
  - Energy flow
  - Nutrient cycling
- **Levels of Organization:**
  - Individual organisms
  - Populations
  - Communities
  - Ecosystems

## Types of Ecosystems

### 1. Forest Ecosystem

- **Characteristics:**
  - High biodiversity
  - Complex food web
  - Multiple trophic levels
  - Photosynthesis and respiration
- **Importance:**
  - Supports human life and livelihoods
  - Regulates climate and weather patterns

### 2. Desert Ecosystem

- **Characteristics:**
  - Limited vegetation and biodiversity
  - High temperatures and low precipitation
  - Specialized adaptations in plants and animals
  - Unique nutrient cycles
- **Importance:**
  - Supports unique and adapted species
  - Regulates climate and weather patterns

### 3. Wetlands Ecosystem

- **Characteristics:**
  - High water tables and saturated soils
  - Unique plant and animal adaptations
  - Important habitat for biodiversity
  - Water filtration and purification
- **Importance:**
  - Supports diverse plant and animal life
  - Regulates water cycles and quality

### 4. Riverine Ecosystem

- **Characteristics:**
  - High water flow and velocity
  - Varied habitats and species
  - Nutrient cycling and sediment transport
  - Supports aquatic life
- **Importance:**
  - Supports human settlements and agriculture
  - Regulates water cycles and quality

### 5. Oceanic Ecosystem

- **Characteristics:**
  - Vast and diverse marine life
  - Complex food web and nutrient cycles
  - Important for global climate regulation
  - Supports human livelihoods and food security
- **Importance:**
  - Supports global biodiversity and ecosystem services
  - Regulates climate and weather patterns

## Important Formulas, Definitions, and Theorems

- **Carson's Law:** "In the absence of external inputs, ecosystems tend towards a stable state."
- **Trophic Cascade:** A change in one trophic level can have a ripple effect on the entire ecosystem.
- **Ecological Niche Theory:** Each species occupies a unique role in its ecosystem.
- **Biogeochemical Cycles:** The movement of nutrients and energy through ecosystems.
