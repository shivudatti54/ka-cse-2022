### Textbook 1: CH- 3 - Environmental Studies and E-Waste Management

#### Key Points

- **Definition:** E-Waste refers to electronic waste generated from discarded electronic products.
- **Types of E-Waste:**
  • Inorganic E-Waste (e.g. batteries, circuit boards)
  • Organic E-Waste (e.g. printed circuit boards, capacitors)
- **Causes of E-Waste:**
  • Obsolescence
  • Poor design
  • Lack of recycling infrastructure
- **Consequences of E-Waste:**
  • Pollution of soil and water
  • Harm to human health
  • Contribution to climate change

#### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Waste Management Hierarchy:**
  • Design for recyclability
  • Recycling
  • Reuse
  • Energy recovery
  • Final disposal
- **E-Waste Management Framework:**
  • Prevention
  • Collection
  • Transportation
  • Processing and treatment
  • Disposal

#### Key Terms

- **Recyclable materials:** Materials that can be reused or recovered
- **Recoverable materials:** Materials that can be reused or recovered with minimal processing
- **Toxic chemicals:** Chemicals that can harm human health and the environment

#### Important Concepts

- **Closed-loop production:** A production system that continuously recycles materials to minimize waste
- **Design for disassembly:** Designing products to be easily disassembled for recycling and reuse
