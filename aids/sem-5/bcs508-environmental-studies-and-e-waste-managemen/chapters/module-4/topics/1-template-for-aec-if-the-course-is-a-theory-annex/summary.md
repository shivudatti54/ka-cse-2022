### Environmental Studies and E-Waste Management

#### Waste Management Module

##### 1 TEMPLATE for AEC (if the course is a theory)

##### Annexure-IV Self-Study Component (SSC): Case studies on waste management options

##### Textbook 1: CH- 6

**Key Points:**

- **Waste Hierarchy:**
  - Reduce
  - Reuse
  - Recycle
  - Recover
  - Dispose

- **Types of Waste Management:**
  - Incineration
  - Landfilling
  - Composting
  - Recycling
  - Energy Recovery

- **Environmental Impact:**
  - Air pollution
  - Water pollution
  - Soil pollution
  - Climate change

- **Waste Management Theories:**
  - Waste minimization
  - Waste reduction
  - Waste recycling

- **Formulas:**
  - Waste generation rate: (WGR) = (W) / (t)
  - Waste reduction potential: (WRP) = (W) / (W+R)

- **Important Definitions:**
  - E-waste: Electronic waste generated from discarded electrical and electronic appliances
  - Hazardous waste: Waste that poses a threat to human health or the environment

- **Important Concepts:**
  - Waste management planning
  - Waste collection and transportation
  - Waste treatment and disposal
