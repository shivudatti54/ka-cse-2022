# Learning Objectives
After studying this topic, you should be able to:
1. Define the key components of a Turing Machine, including the tape, head, state, and transition function.
2. Explain how a Turing Machine works, including the role of the transition function and the halting state.
3. Design a Turing Machine for a given problem, including specifying the transition function and the halting state.
4. Analyze the behavior of a Turing Machine and determine whether it will accept or reject a given input string.
5. Compare and contrast the Turing Machine with other models of computation, including the Finite Automaton and the Pushdown Automaton.
6. Explain the relationship between the Turing Machine and the Church-Turing thesis.
