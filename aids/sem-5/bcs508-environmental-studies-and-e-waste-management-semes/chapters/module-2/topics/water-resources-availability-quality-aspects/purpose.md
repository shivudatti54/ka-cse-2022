### Learning Purpose: Water Resources – Availability & Quality Aspects

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Water is the planet's most critical natural resource, essential for all life and human activity. Understanding its availability and quality is fundamental to addressing global challenges like scarcity, pollution, and equitable distribution, which are exacerbated by climate change and industrial practices, including those from the electronics industry.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to analyze the global and local distribution of freshwater resources and the natural hydrological cycle. They will investigate major threats to water quality, such as contamination from industrial discharge, agricultural runoff, and improper e-waste disposal (e.g., heavy metal leaching). The module will equip them with the knowledge to interpret water quality standards and assess sustainability issues.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly connects to e-waste management by examining how toxic leachate from landfills can pollute groundwater and surface water. It also links to broader environmental studies concepts like ecosystem health, pollution control, environmental policy, and the social implications of resource management, providing a holistic view of human-environment interactions.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied in fields like environmental impact assessment, designing remediation strategies for polluted water bodies, developing sustainable water conservation policies, and advocating for responsible e-waste recycling to prevent aquifer contamination.
