### Learning Purpose: Riverine Systems & E-Waste

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Riverine systems are critical natural resources, providing water, supporting biodiversity, and enabling transportation. They are also a primary sink for pollutants, including electronic waste (e-waste). Understanding the riverine pathway of e-waste is essential to grasp its full environmental impact, as toxins from improperly disposed electronics leach into waterways, contaminating water supplies and entering the food chain.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn how e-waste enters, transports through, and impacts riverine ecosystems. They will identify key pollutants (e.g., heavy metals like lead and mercury, brominated flame retardants) and analyze their effects on aquatic life and human health. The module will also cover mitigation strategies, including policy frameworks and community-led conservation efforts for riparian zones.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic connects the principles of waste management (Module 1 core) to hydrology, ecology, and environmental chemistry. It builds upon prior knowledge of pollution and provides a critical case study for understanding the lifecycle of a pollutant, linking directly to subsequent modules on soil contamination, circular economy models, and environmental policy.

**4. Real-world applications**
Students can apply this knowledge to assess local water bodies for e-waste pollution risks, advocate for improved recycling infrastructure to prevent leaching, and contribute to watershed management plans. This understanding is vital for careers in environmental consulting, public health, waste management, and sustainability policy.
