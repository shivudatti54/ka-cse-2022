### Learning Purpose: Wetlands

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Wetlands are among the planet's most productive and vital ecosystems. They are crucial for biodiversity, water purification, flood control, and carbon sequestration. Understanding their role is fundamental to environmental conservation and sustainable resource management, directly linking to the urgent global issues of habitat loss, climate change, and water security.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define wetlands and identify their different types (e.g., marshes, swamps). They will analyze the key ecological functions and services wetlands provide and evaluate the major threats they face from human activities like urbanization, pollution, and draining for agriculture.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is intrinsically linked to other modules in E-Waste Management. Pollutants from improperly discarded electronics (e-waste) can leach heavy metals into water systems, degrading wetland health. It also connects to broader concepts like the water cycle, waste management strategies, environmental policy, and sustainable development.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge enables students to contribute to wetland conservation efforts, advocate for protective legislation, and develop more holistic e-waste disposal plans that prevent ecosystem contamination. It provides a critical foundation for careers in environmental consulting, conservation NGOs, and public policy.
