### Learning Purpose: The Impact of E-Waste on Land

**1. Importance:**  
This topic is critical because land is the primary sink for improperly disposed electronic waste (e-waste). Understanding its impact is essential, as toxins from e-waste, like lead and mercury, can leach into soil and groundwater, posing severe risks to ecosystems, food security, and human health.

**2. Student Learning:**  
Students will learn to analyze the specific processes of soil contamination and degradation caused by e-waste. They will evaluate the long-term consequences of landfilling versus sustainable alternatives like resource recovery and circular economy models, moving beyond mere disposal.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts:**  
It directly connects to prior modules on water pollution (leachate contaminating groundwater) and air quality (open burning of e-waste). It also provides the terrestrial foundation for discussing broader sustainability goals, life cycle analysis, and policy frameworks for waste management.

**4. Real-World Application:**  
This knowledge empowers students to advocate for and design better municipal e-waste collection systems, promote soil remediation techniques, and critically assess the environmental impact of landfills versus formal recycling facilities in their communities.
