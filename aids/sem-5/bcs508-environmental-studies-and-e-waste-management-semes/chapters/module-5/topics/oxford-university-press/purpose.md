### Learning Purpose: Oxford University Press & E-Waste Management

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial as it examines the role of a major global publisher, Oxford University Press (OUP), as a case study for corporate environmental responsibility. It highlights how the publishing industry, a significant consumer of resources and producer of physical goods, contributes to and can help mitigate the growing problem of e-waste through sustainable practices and policy.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to analyze the specific lifecycle of electronic products (e.g., educational tablets, online learning platforms) associated with modern publishing. They will evaluate OUP’s potential strategies for reducing e-waste, such as designing for durability, promoting digital alternatives, implementing take-back schemes, and ensuring responsible recycling, thereby understanding corporate e-waste management frameworks.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This directly connects to previous modules on life cycle assessment (LCA), extended producer responsibility (EPR), the 3Rs (Reduce, Reuse, Recycle), and sustainable product design. It applies these theoretical concepts to a real-world corporate entity, bridging the gap between environmental policy, business operations, and waste management.

**4. Real-world applications**
Students can apply this knowledge to assess the sustainability reports of other corporations, advocate for greener procurement policies in educational institutions, and develop consumer awareness about the environmental impact of the tools they use for learning. This analysis is key for future careers in corporate sustainability, environmental consultancy, and waste management policy.
