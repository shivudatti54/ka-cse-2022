### 1. Why is this topic important?
This case study is crucial as it provides a tangible, real-world example of e-waste management within a specific industry (publishing). It moves beyond abstract theory, allowing students to analyze the environmental impact of modern office technology, the challenges of responsible disposal, and the economic considerations businesses face, thereby grounding the issue of e-waste in a practical context.

### 2. What will students learn?
Students will learn to identify the primary sources of e-waste generated in a corporate office environment (e.g., computers, printers, servers). They will analyze the lifecycle of electronic equipment within a business and evaluate the financial and logistical challenges an organization like Elite Publishing House encounters when implementing a sustainable e-waste management policy.

### 3. How does it connect to other concepts?
This topic directly connects to earlier modules on the environmental impact of hazardous materials (like lead and mercury in electronics), the principles of the 3Rs (Reduce, Reuse, Recycle), and extended producer responsibility (EPR). It synthesizes these concepts into a single, applied case, demonstrating how theoretical frameworks operate—or fail—in a real business scenario.

### 4. Real-world applications
The analysis equips students with the skills to conduct e-waste audits for businesses, develop corporate social responsibility (CSR) policies focused on sustainability, and advise organizations on compliance with national e-waste management rules. It prepares them for roles in corporate sustainability, environmental consulting, and waste management.
