### Learning Purpose: Module 5 - E-Waste Management (Tata McGraw-Hill & R. Rajagopalan)

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is critically important because electronic waste (e-waste) is the world's fastest-growing waste stream, posing severe environmental and public health risks due to toxic materials like lead and mercury. Understanding its management is essential for future environmental stewards to mitigate pollution and promote a sustainable, circular economy.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the fundamental principles of e-waste management, including its classification, the global and Indian regulatory frameworks, and the technical processes of recycling and disposal. They will analyze the lifecycle of electronic products and evaluate the economic, social, and environmental dimensions of responsible e-waste handling as presented in the authoritative text.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly connects to prior knowledge of pollution control, sustainable development, and waste management principles. It applies concepts of environmental policy, corporate social responsibility (CSR), material science (recycling tech), and public health, providing a holistic view of an integrated environmental challenge.

**4. Real-world applications**
Students will be equipped to contribute to developing e-waste collection drives, advise on compliance with laws like India's E-Waste (Management) Rules, and innovate in the recycling industry. This knowledge is vital for careers in environmental consulting, corporate sustainability, public policy, and waste management entrepreneurship.
