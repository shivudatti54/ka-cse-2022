### Learning Purpose: Harrison R.M and E-Waste Management

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because it connects a seminal theoretical framework—Harrison R.M's model of waste management—to the pressing, real-world challenge of electronic waste (e-waste). E-waste is the fastest-growing waste stream globally, containing both hazardous materials and valuable resources. Understanding Harrison's principles provides a structured way to analyze and develop effective, sustainable solutions for its management, moving beyond simple disposal to a circular economy approach.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to deconstruct and apply Harrison R.M's waste management hierarchy (e.g., Reduce, Reuse, Recycle, Recover, Dispose) specifically to e-waste. They will analyze the environmental and health impacts of improper e-waste handling and evaluate strategies for each level of the hierarchy, from designing longer-lasting electronics (Reduce) to responsible recycling and recovery of precious metals.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic integrates concepts from manufacturing (green design), chemistry (hazardous material composition), economics (the value chain of recycled materials), public policy (Extended Producer Responsibility laws), and social ethics (the global flow of e-waste to developing nations). It acts as a capstone module, synthesizing prior learning into a complex, multidisciplinary issue.

**4. Real-world applications**
Students will be equipped to critically assess corporate e-waste policies, contribute to community recycling initiatives, make informed consumer choices, and understand the policy landscape governing electronic product stewardship and international waste trade.
