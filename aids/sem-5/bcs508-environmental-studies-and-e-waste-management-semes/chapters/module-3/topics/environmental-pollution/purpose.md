### Learning Purpose: Environmental Pollution

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Environmental pollution is a critical global challenge with direct consequences for ecosystem health, human well-being, and economic stability. Focusing on pollutants like heavy metals and chemicals from e-waste is paramount, as their improper disposal leads to severe soil degradation, water contamination, and air pollution, posing significant risks to all life forms.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify and classify the primary types of environmental pollutants, with a specific focus on those originating from electronic waste (e-waste). They will analyze the pathways through which these pollutants enter and move through ecosystems and evaluate their acute and long-term impacts on the environment and public health.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is a core component of environmental studies, directly linking to prior knowledge of ecosystems and biodiversity. It also provides the foundational scientific context for subsequent modules on e-waste management policies, recycling technologies, and principles of the circular economy, illustrating the cause-and-effect relationship between pollution and its solutions.

**4. Real-world applications**
Understanding this topic empowers students to critically assess local and global pollution scenarios, contribute to developing and implementing effective e-waste management strategies, and advocate for sustainable consumption and responsible electronic disposal practices within their communities and future careers.
