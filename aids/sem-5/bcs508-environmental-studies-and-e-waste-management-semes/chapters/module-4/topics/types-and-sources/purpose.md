### Learning Purpose: Types and Sources of E-Waste

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the types and sources of electronic waste (e-waste) is fundamental because it is the fastest-growing waste stream globally. Its improper disposal poses severe environmental and health risks due to toxic components like lead and mercury. Identifying where e-waste comes from and what it consists of is the critical first step toward developing effective management and recycling strategies.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to classify e-waste into major categories (e.g., IT equipment, consumer electronics, large appliances) and identify their primary sources (households, businesses, industries). They will analyze the life cycle of electronic products, from manufacture to disposal, and understand the composition of different e-waste types, including the valuable, hazardous, and rare materials they contain.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon prior knowledge of waste management principles, pollution, and resource depletion. It provides the foundational vocabulary and context for subsequent modules on e-waste recycling technologies, policy frameworks, and the economic opportunities within the circular economy. It also connects to broader themes of sustainable consumption and production.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is essential for careers in waste management, environmental policy, and corporate sustainability. It empowers students to make informed decisions as consumers, participate in or organize community e-waste collection drives, and audit e-waste generation in organizational settings to recommend better disposal practices.
