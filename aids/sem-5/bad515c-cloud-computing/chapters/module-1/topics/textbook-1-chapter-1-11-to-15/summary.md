# Scalable Computing Over the Internet: A New Frontier

### Chapter 1: Distributed System Models and Enabling Technologies

### Section 1.1: Introduction to Scalable Computing

- Scalable computing refers to the ability of a computing system to scale up or down to meet changing workload demands.
- The Internet has enabled scalable computing by providing a global network of interconnected computers.
- Cloud computing is a key enabler of scalable computing.

### Section 1.2: Characteristics of Scalable Computing

- On-demand self-service: users can provision and de-provision resources without human intervention.
- Broad network access: resources are accessible over the Internet or a private network.
- Resource pooling: resources are pooled together to provide a multi-tenant environment.
- Rapid elasticity: resources can be quickly scaled up or down to meet changing demands.
- Measured service: resources are metered and billed based on actual usage.

### Section 1.3: Types of Scalable Computing

- **Horizontal scaling**: adding more computers to a system to increase processing power.
- **Vertical scaling**: increasing the power of individual computers to increase processing power.
- **Cloud computing**: a model for delivering scalable computing resources over the Internet.

### Section 1.4: Benefits of Scalable Computing

- Cost savings: resources can be scaled up or down to match changing demands, reducing waste and excess capacity.
- Increased agility: scalable computing enables businesses to respond quickly to changing market conditions.
- Improved reliability: resources can be easily replicated and failover mechanisms can be implemented to ensure high availability.

### Section 1.5: Scalable Computing over the Internet

- The Internet enables scalable computing by providing a global network of interconnected computers.
- Cloud computing is a key enabler of scalable computing, enabling businesses to access scalable computing resources over the Internet.

### Important Formulas, Definitions, and Theorems

- **Scalability**: the ability of a system to scale up or down to meet changing workload demands.
- **Cloud computing**: a model for delivering scalable computing resources over the Internet.
- **On-demand self-service**: a characteristic of scalable computing that enables users to provision and de-provision resources without human intervention.
- **Kaplansky's Theorem**: states that a system is scalable if and only if it is homogeneous and has a uniform resource allocation.
