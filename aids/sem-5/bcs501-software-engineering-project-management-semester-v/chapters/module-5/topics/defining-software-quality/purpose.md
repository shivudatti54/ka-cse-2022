### Learning Purpose: Defining Software Quality

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding software quality is foundational because it defines the value and success of a software product. It moves beyond mere "bug-fixing" to establish a proactive framework for building reliable, efficient, and maintainable systems that meet user needs and business objectives, directly impacting customer satisfaction and project ROI.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define software quality using formal models like the ISO 25010 standard, which breaks it down into characteristics such as functional suitability, performance efficiency, compatibility, and usability. They will differentiate between validation (building the right product) and verification (building the product right) and be introduced to key metrics for quantifying quality.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the cornerstone for subsequent modules. It directly connects to requirements engineering (defining quality needs), software testing (verifying and validating quality), project management (balancing quality with scope, time, and cost), and maintenance (managing quality over the software lifecycle).

**4. Real-world applications**
These concepts are applied daily in industry to create quality assurance (QA) plans, define "Definition of Done" criteria in Agile sprints, select appropriate testing strategies, and justify technical debt decisions. It provides the shared vocabulary needed for engineers, project managers, and stakeholders to collaboratively ship high-quality software.
