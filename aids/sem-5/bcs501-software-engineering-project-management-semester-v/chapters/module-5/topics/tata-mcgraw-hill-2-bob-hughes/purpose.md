### **Learning Purpose: Module 5 - Hughes' Project Management Framework**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because Bob Hughes' framework provides a structured, practical methodology for managing software projects. It moves beyond theoretical concepts, addressing real-world challenges like changing requirements, risk management, and effective team coordination. Understanding this framework is essential for delivering software on time, within budget, and to the required quality standards.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core components of Hughes' project management approach, including techniques for project planning, scheduling, risk assessment, and resource allocation. They will understand how to define project scope, create work breakdown structures (WBS), and implement monitoring and control mechanisms to track project progress against objectives.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly connects to prior knowledge of software development life cycles (SDLC), requirements engineering, and software quality assurance. It synthesizes these technical concepts with business-oriented principles like budgeting and stakeholder management, acting as the bridge between pure software engineering and the business constraints of a project.

**4. Real-world applications**
The principles learned are directly applicable to managing any software project, from small agile teams to large-scale enterprise systems. Students will be equipped to create project plans, mitigate risks, communicate effectively with stakeholders, and use these skills as a foundation for more advanced methodologies like Agile (Scrum, Kanban) and DevOps.
