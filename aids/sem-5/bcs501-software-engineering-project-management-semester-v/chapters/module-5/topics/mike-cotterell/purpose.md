### Learning Purpose: Mike Cotterell

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial as it provides a practical, real-world perspective on software engineering and project management. Studying the work and methodologies of an industry practitioner like Mike Cotterell, a recognized expert and lecturer, grounds theoretical concepts in proven application. It highlights the importance of agile practices, clean code, and effective tool usage, which are essential for modern software development.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn about specific agile development practices, tools (such as Git and CI/CD pipelines), and the philosophical approach to software craftsmanship advocated by Cotterell. They will understand the value of test-driven development (TDD), iterative design, and maintaining high code quality to manage project complexity and reduce technical debt.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly connects to and synthesizes prior modules on Agile methodologies, version control, software testing, and project planning. It serves as a capstone example of how these separate concepts integrate into a cohesive, professional workflow, demonstrating how theoretical project management frameworks are executed in practice.

**4. Real-world applications**
The principles covered are directly applicable to professional software engineering roles. Students will be equipped to contribute effectively to agile teams, utilize modern development tools, write maintainable code, and understand the continuous delivery processes that are standard in the industry, thereby enhancing their employability and readiness for real-world projects.
