### Learning Purpose: Importance of Software Quality

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Software quality is a critical determinant of a project's success and sustainability. High-quality software reduces long-term costs, enhances security, ensures user satisfaction, and maintains a company's reputation. Conversely, poor quality can lead to catastrophic failures, financial losses, and safety risks. Understanding its importance is foundational for any software engineer or project manager.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define software quality through its key attributes: functionality, reliability, usability, efficiency, maintainability, and portability. They will explore how quality is measured and the tangible business value it delivers, moving beyond code correctness to encompass the entire user experience and product lifecycle.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the cornerstone for subsequent modules on quality assurance (QA), testing strategies, and software metrics. It connects directly to project management by showing how quality objectives influence planning, budgeting, and scheduling. It also underpins earlier concepts like requirements engineering and design principles.

**4. Real-world applications**
The principles learned apply directly to implementing QA processes, choosing appropriate testing methodologies (unit, integration, system), and justifying quality-related investments to stakeholders. This knowledge is essential for building robust, secure commercial products, critical medical systems, and reliable financial platforms.
