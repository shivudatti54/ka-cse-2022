# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define agility in the context of software engineering and explain its significance in modern software development.

2. Articulate the four values of the Agile Manifesto and explain what each value implies in practice.

3. Distinguish between agile principles and specific agile methodologies or frameworks.

4. Identify and explain the key characteristics that define an agile team.

5. Analyze the three pillars of empiricism (transparency, inspection, adaptation) and their role in agile processes.

6. Compare and contrast agile development approaches with traditional sequential methodologies.

7. Recognize common misconceptions about agility and articulate why they are incorrect.

8. Apply agile concepts to analyze real-world software development scenarios and justify agile approaches.
