# What is Agility? - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

Agility in software engineering is the ability to respond to changes quickly and effectively while continuously delivering value to customers. An agile process adapts to changing requirements even late in development, emphasizing iterative delivery, collaboration, and flexibility over rigid planning.

The Agile Manifesto, created in 2001, establishes four core values: individuals and interactions over processes and tools, working software over comprehensive documentation, customer collaboration over contract negotiation, and responding to change over following a rigid plan.

## Important Formulas and Theorems

There are no mathematical formulas in this topic. However, the conceptual framework rests on three pillars of empiricism: TRANSPARENCY (making work visible), INSPECTION (regularly reviewing progress and practices), and ADAPTATION (adjusting based on findings).

## Key Points

- Agility is a mindset and approach, not just a set of practices
- The Agile Manifesto provides values, not prescriptive rules
- Agile teams are cross-functional, self-organizing, and collaborative
- Iterative development with frequent feedback loops distinguishes agile from sequential approaches
- Empiricism—learning from observation and experimentation—underpins all agile practices
- Agility applies to teams and organizations, not individual developers alone
- Working software is the primary measure of progress in agile
- Customer collaboration is continuous throughout the project lifecycle

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Confusing agility with having no planning or documentation
- Believing agile means absence of discipline or structure
- Treating specific frameworks (Scrum, XP) as synonymous with agility itself
- Assuming agility only works for small projects
- Focusing only on practices without understanding underlying principles

## Revision Tips

For effective revision, create a comparison chart between agile and waterfall approaches, memorize the exact wording of the four Agile Manifesto values, and practice applying agile concepts to case study scenarios. Understand that agility is about principles first, then practices, and finally specific tools. Review the historical context of the Agile Manifesto to appreciate why it was created and what problems it aimed to solve.
