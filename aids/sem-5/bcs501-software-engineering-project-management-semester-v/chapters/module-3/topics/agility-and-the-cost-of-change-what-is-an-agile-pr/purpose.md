### Learning Purpose: Agility and the Cost of Change

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the cost of change is fundamental to software project success. Traditional models assume change is prohibitively expensive late in a project, but this topic challenges that by introducing Agile's core premise: that change can be managed effectively and economically through iterative development and continuous feedback, which is crucial in today's rapidly evolving markets.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define an agile process and contrast it with traditional plan-driven models (like Waterfall). They will analyze how agility reduces the cost of change through short iterations, adaptive planning, and early delivery of business value. The concept of the "cost of change curve" will be a key takeaway.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the philosophical foundation for subsequent modules on specific Agile frameworks (Scrum, XP) and practices (user stories, daily stand-ups). It directly connects to project planning and risk management, showing how Agile methods mitigate the risk of building the wrong product by embracing changing requirements.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied when choosing a project methodology. For instance, a team developing a new mobile app in a competitive market would use an Agile process to incorporate user feedback after each sprint, ensuring the final product meets real-world needs without exorbitant rework costs.
