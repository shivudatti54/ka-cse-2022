### Learning Purpose: Traditional vs. Modern Project Management Practices

**1. Why is this important?**
Understanding the evolution from traditional (e.g., Waterfall) to modern (e.g., Agile, Scrum) practices is crucial. It equips future software engineers to select the right methodology for a project's context, balancing predictability with adaptability—a core determinant of project success in the industry.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to contrast the phased, documentation-heavy approach of traditional methods with the iterative, customer-centric values of Agile frameworks. They will analyze the strengths, weaknesses, and ideal use-cases for each, enabling them to make informed managerial decisions.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic integrates with prior knowledge of software development life cycles (SDLC) and subsequent modules on quality assurance and risk management. It provides the philosophical and practical foundation for applying specific Agile practices like Scrum or Kanban.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is directly applicable in industry roles such as Project Manager, Scrum Master, or Product Owner. Students will be prepared to contribute to teams using either methodology and to lead the adoption of hybrid approaches that combine structure with flexibility.
