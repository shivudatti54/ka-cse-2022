### Module 4: Project Success and Failure

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding what drives project success or failure is fundamental for any aspiring software engineer or project manager. Many software projects exceed budgets, miss deadlines, or fail to meet user needs. By studying the common factors behind these outcomes, students learn to proactively mitigate risks, manage stakeholder expectations, and implement strategies that significantly increase the likelihood of delivering a successful project.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define and measure success using criteria beyond the traditional "triple constraint" of time, cost, and scope, including quality and customer satisfaction. They will analyze common failure factors (e.g., poor requirements, scope creep, inadequate communication) and success factors (e.g., clear objectives, user involvement, agile practices). The module will also cover techniques for risk management and early failure detection.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic synthesizes and applies all previous concepts in project management. It directly connects to requirements engineering (clear scope), scheduling & cost estimation (realistic planning), software quality assurance (meeting specifications), and team leadership (effective communication). It provides the crucial "why" behind the methodologies and processes taught throughout the course.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is directly applicable to evaluating project proposals, conducting post-mortem analyses, and creating actionable mitigation plans. Students will be equipped to champion best practices, justify process improvements, and make informed decisions to steer real-world projects away from failure and toward success, ultimately delivering greater business value.
