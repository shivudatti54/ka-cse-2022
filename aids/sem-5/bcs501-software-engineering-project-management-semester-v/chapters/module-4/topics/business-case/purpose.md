**Learning Purpose: Business Case**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
The business case is the foundational document that justifies the initiation of a software project. It is critically important because it moves development from a technical exercise to a strategic business investment. It answers the fundamental question: "Why should we spend resources on this project?" A well-constructed business case ensures projects are aligned with organizational goals, provide clear value, and have a measurable Return on Investment (ROI), thereby securing executive buy-in and funding.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core components of a formal business case, including how to identify and analyze costs, benefits (tangible and intangible), risks, and strategic options. They will develop the skills to construct a compelling financial argument using techniques like Cost-Benefit Analysis (CBA), Net Present Value (NPV), and payback period calculations. The objective is to enable them to create a document that convincingly proposes a project and defines the criteria for its success.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the crucial link between early **requirement analysis** and subsequent **project planning**. It directly informs the **project charter** and is the primary input for defining **project scope** and objectives. The financial and risk analyses within a business case provide the benchmarks against which **project success** is measured during and after closure, connecting it directly to **monitoring & control** and **project valuation** phases.

**4. Real-world applications**
Every significant software project, from a new mobile app to an enterprise system upgrade, requires a business case for approval. Product managers, business analysts, and project managers use this skill to pitch new ideas to stakeholders, secure budget, and prioritize the project portfolio. It is an essential tool for justifying IT expenditures and ensuring that software engineering efforts deliver tangible business value.
