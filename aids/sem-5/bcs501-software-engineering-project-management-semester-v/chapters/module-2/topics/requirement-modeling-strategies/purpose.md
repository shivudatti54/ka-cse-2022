### Learning Purpose: Requirement Modeling Strategies

1. **Why is this topic important?**
   Requirement modeling is a critical phase that bridges user needs and technical design. Ambiguous or incomplete requirements are a primary cause of project failure. This topic equips students with structured techniques to accurately capture, analyze, and validate what a system must do, ensuring it aligns with stakeholder expectations and provides a solid foundation for the entire development lifecycle.

2. **What will students learn?**
   Students will learn to select and apply various modeling strategies, including scenario-based (use cases), data (ER diagrams), flow-oriented (DFDs), and behavioral (state diagrams) models. They will develop the skill to create clear, unambiguous visual representations of system functionality, data, and behavior to facilitate communication between clients, users, and developers.

3. **How does it connect to other concepts?**
   This module is the direct application of the requirements elicitation techniques learned previously (Module 1). The created models serve as the essential blueprint for subsequent stages of software design (Module 3), coding, and testing. It is also intrinsically linked to project management, as a well-defined requirements model is crucial for accurate estimation, scheduling, and scope management.

4. **Real-world applications**
   These strategies are universally applied across the industry. From drawing use case diagrams for a new banking app to modeling data flows for an e-commerce checkout process or defining states for a medical device controller, these models are the standard language used by Business Analysts, Architects, and Developers to build complex software systems efficiently and effectively.
