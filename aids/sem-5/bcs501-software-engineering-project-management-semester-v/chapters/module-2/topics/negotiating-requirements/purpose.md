### **Learning Purpose: Negotiating Requirements**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Negotiating requirements is a critical skill because requirements are often conflicting, ambiguous, or resource-intensive. Failure to negotiate effectively leads to scope creep, budget overruns, project delays, and stakeholder dissatisfaction. This process ensures the final set of requirements is realistic, agreed upon, and aligned with business goals, forming a solid foundation for development.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn techniques to identify stakeholder conflicts, prioritize features using models like MoSCoW (Must-have, Should-have, Could-have, Won't-have), and conduct productive negotiation sessions. They will develop skills in communication, conflict resolution, and compromise to achieve consensus on a feasible project scope that delivers core value.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon requirements elicitation (gathering needs) and analysis (structuring needs). It is a fundamental input for project planning, estimation, and scheduling. Effective negotiation is also core to agile methodologies (e.g., sprint planning) and is essential for subsequent phases like design, development, and change management.

**4. Real-world applications**
These skills are applied daily by product owners, business analysts, and project managers. Examples include negotiating feature timelines with clients, prioritizing a product backlog with development teams, managing change requests, and making trade-offs between quality, cost, and time to meet project constraints.
