### Learning Purpose: Validating Requirements & Requirements Modeling Scenarios

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Validating requirements ensures that the documented needs of stakeholders are accurate, complete, and achievable before significant resources are invested in development. It is a critical step to prevent project failure, costly rework, and scope creep by aligning the project team and stakeholders on a shared vision.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn techniques to validate requirements (e.g., reviews, prototypes, test cases) and will master the creation of scenario-based models, such as use cases and user stories. These skills enable them to translate abstract user needs into clear, testable specifications and visual representations that guide the design and development process.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the crucial link between initial requirements elicitation (Module 1) and subsequent system design & architecture (later modules). Validated models become the foundation for creating analysis models, designing test plans, and managing project scope, directly feeding into the project management lifecycle.

**4. Real-world applications**
These techniques are applied daily by business analysts and project managers to formalize requirements for mobile apps, enterprise software, and automated systems. Creating a validated use case scenario for an e-commerce "checkout process" is a classic example, ensuring all functional steps and error conditions are agreed upon before a single line of code is written.
