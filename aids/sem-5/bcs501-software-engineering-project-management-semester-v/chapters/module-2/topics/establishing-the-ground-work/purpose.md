### Learning Purpose: Establishing the Ground Work

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Establishing the groundwork is the critical first phase of any software project. A weak foundation leads to miscommunication, scope creep, budget overruns, and ultimately, project failure. This topic is vital because it teaches the systematic processes required to define a project's vision, scope, and constraints *before* significant resources are invested, thereby de-risking the entire development lifecycle.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the key activities for project initiation, including how to formally define business needs, identify stakeholders, and establish clear objectives and success criteria. They will be introduced to creating foundational documents such as the Vision & Scope document and the initial Project Plan. The concepts of feasibility studies (technical, economic, legal) will also be covered to assess a project's viability.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module provides the essential input for all subsequent project management and engineering activities. The defined scope directly connects to **requirements engineering** (Module 3). The initial plan and success criteria set the baseline for **project scheduling, tracking, and risk management** (later modules). It is the prerequisite upon which all agile or traditional development methodologies are built.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied in the real world to initiate any software project, from a startup's new app to a large enterprise system upgrade. Product managers use these techniques to justify project funding. Developers and project managers rely on a clear scope to prevent misunderstandings with clients and ensure the final product delivers intended business value, aligning technical execution with strategic goals.
