### Learning Purpose: Understanding Requirements

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding requirements is the foundational first step in any software engineering project. Inaccurate, incomplete, or ambiguous requirements are a primary cause of project failure, leading to budget overruns, missed deadlines, and software that does not meet user needs. Mastering this topic is crucial for building the right product efficiently and effectively.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn techniques for gathering requirements (e.g., interviews, workshops), how to analyze and model them using tools like use cases and user stories, and the critical skill of documenting them in a Software Requirements Specification (SRS) document. They will also learn to validate and manage changing requirements throughout a project's lifecycle.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the direct input for system design and architecture (Module 3). It is governed by project management principles (Module 1) regarding scope, time, and cost. The clarity of requirements directly impacts subsequent phases like development, testing (verifying requirements are met), and deployment.

**4. Real-world applications**
This process is applied in every software project, from mobile apps to enterprise systems. It is used by Business Analysts, Product Managers, and Project Managers to bridge the communication gap between clients/stakeholders and the development team, ensuring a shared vision before a single line of code is written.
