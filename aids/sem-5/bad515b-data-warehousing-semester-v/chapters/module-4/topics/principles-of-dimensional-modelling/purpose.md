### Learning Purpose: Principles of Dimensional Modelling

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Dimensional Modelling is the cornerstone of building effective and performant data warehouses. It provides a simplified, intuitive structure that enables efficient data retrieval for complex business analysis. Mastering these principles is essential for designing data warehouses that translate raw data into actionable business intelligence.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core components of dimensional modelling: facts, dimensions, and their relationships. They will understand how to design schemas like Star and Snowflake, define grain, and build slowly changing dimensions (SCDs). This equips them with the practical skills to model data for analytical reporting and OLAP systems.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon prior knowledge of Entity-Relationship (ER) modelling and database normalization (from earlier semesters). It serves as the critical link between operational databases (normalized for transactions) and the data warehouse (denormalized for analysis), connecting to concepts of ETL processes, BI tools, and data mart design.

**4. Real-world applications**
These principles are applied everywhere business performance is measured. From retail (analyzing sales by store, product, and time) to finance (tracking transactions by customer and branch), dimensional models power the dashboards and reports that drive strategic decision-making in every industry.
