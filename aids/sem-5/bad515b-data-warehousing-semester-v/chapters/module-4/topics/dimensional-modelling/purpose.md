### Learning Purpose: Dimensional Modelling

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Dimensional Modelling is the cornerstone of building a data warehouse. It is crucial because it translates complex operational data into an intuitive and high-performance structure optimized for analytical queries and business intelligence (BI) reporting. Mastering this skill is essential for any data professional.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core components of dimensional modelling: facts, dimensions, and attributes. They will understand how to design schemas (like Star and Snowflake) and apply key concepts such as surrogate keys, slowly changing dimensions (SCDs), and hierarchies to create robust data models that accurately represent business processes.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon prior knowledge of Entity-Relationship (ER) modelling and relational databases from earlier semesters. It serves as the critical bridge between raw data extraction (ETL processes) and the final stage of data consumption through BI tools, dashboards, and OLAP systems.

**4. Real-world applications**
These modelling techniques are applied everywhere data-driven decisions are made. Students will be equipped to design data models for real-world scenarios such as sales analysis, customer segmentation, inventory management, and financial reporting, enabling stakeholders to easily explore and understand business trends.
