### Learning Purpose: From Requirements To Data Design

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because it forms the foundational bridge between business needs and technical implementation. A data warehouse built without a clear design, directly derived from business requirements, is likely to fail. It ensures the final data structure delivers actionable insights, supports accurate decision-making, and provides a return on investment.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the methodologies and techniques to systematically translate high-level business requirements into a concrete, logical data design. This includes processes like dimensional modeling, identifying facts and dimensions, and designing schemas (e.g., star and snowflake schemas) that are optimized for analytical querying and reporting.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This process directly applies the requirements gathering techniques from earlier modules. It is the essential prerequisite for the physical implementation steps that follow, such as extraction, transformation, loading (ETL), and the creation of cubes for Online Analytical Processing (OLAP). It ensures the technical build aligns with the strategic goals identified during the initial planning phases.

**4. Real-world applications**
This skill is applied whenever a new data mart or enterprise data warehouse is scoped and built. For example, a retail company would use these techniques to design a schema that efficiently analyzes sales (facts) by product, time, and store (dimensions) based on requirements from the marketing and sales departments.
