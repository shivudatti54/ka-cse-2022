### **Learning Purpose: The Development Phases**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the development phases is crucial because it provides a structured, proven roadmap for building a data warehouse. Without this disciplined approach, projects are prone to scope creep, budget overruns, and technical failure. This framework ensures the final system is aligned with business needs, is scalable, and delivers high-quality, trustworthy data.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the sequential stages of the data warehouse development lifecycle. This includes key phases such as project planning and requirement gathering, architectural design (conceptual, logical, physical), data modeling (e.g., star schema), ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) development, deployment, and ongoing maintenance/growth. They will understand the specific tasks and goals of each phase.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic integrates all core concepts of the module. The development phases dictate how data modeling techniques are applied, how ETL processes are designed and implemented, and how the chosen architecture (e.g., Kimball vs. Inmon) is executed. It is the practical framework that brings theoretical concepts to life.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is directly applicable to roles like Data Warehouse Architect, ETL Developer, and Business Intelligence Analyst. It is the standard methodology used across industries (finance, healthcare, retail) to build the central data repositories that power strategic reporting, dashboards, and advanced analytics, enabling data-driven decision-making.
