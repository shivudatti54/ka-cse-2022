### **Learning Purpose: Key Business Metrics Or Facts**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Key Business Metrics (or Facts) are the quantifiable core of a data warehouse. They represent the critical performance indicators an organization needs to measure. Without well-defined facts, a data warehouse cannot effectively support business intelligence, reporting, or strategic decision-making, rendering the entire system ineffective.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify, define, and categorize different types of facts (additive, semi-additive, non-additive) that form the center of a star or snowflake schema. They will understand the crucial relationship between facts and their corresponding dimensions and learn the principles of fact table granularity.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the direct application of dimensional modeling learned in Module 1. It is intrinsically linked to the concept of dimensions, which provide the context for the facts. It also lays the foundation for Online Analytical Processing (OLAP) and cube design, where these facts are aggregated and analyzed.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied when designing the core of a data warehouse for any industry. Examples include tracking sales revenue (a fact) by product, time, and store (dimensions) in retail, or analyzing average handle time (a fact) by agent and call type in a customer service center.
