# Learning Purpose: Information Packages

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Information Packages are crucial because they define the core blueprint for a data warehouse. They formally document business information requirements, serving as the definitive agreement between business users and technical developers. Understanding them prevents costly misalignment and ensures the final data warehouse delivers relevant, high-quality information for decision-making.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify and define key business dimensions (how data is analyzed) and metrics (what is measured). They will understand how to structure these components into an Information Package matrix, a foundational tool for designing fact and dimension tables in dimensional modeling.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the critical bridge between initial business requirements (Module 1) and the technical design of the data warehouse. Information Packages directly lead into the creation of Star Schemas and Snowflake Schemas (Module 3), as they provide the necessary input to model fact tables surrounded by dimension tables.

**4. Real-world applications**
This process is applied in every industry. For example, a retail company would create an Information Package for "Sales Analysis," defining dimensions like Product, Store, and Time, and metrics like Units Sold and Revenue. This ensures developers build a schema that can answer vital business questions about sales performance.
