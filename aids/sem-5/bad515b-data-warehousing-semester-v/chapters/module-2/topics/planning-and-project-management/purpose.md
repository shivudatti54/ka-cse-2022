### **Learning Purpose: Planning and Project Management for Data Warehousing**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
A data warehouse is a significant, long-term investment. Without meticulous planning and disciplined project management, projects are prone to scope creep, budget overruns, and ultimate failure. This topic provides the critical framework to ensure a data warehouse delivers its intended business value on time and within budget.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the key phases of the data warehouse project lifecycle, from initial feasibility studies and requirement gathering to design, construction, and deployment. They will be introduced to project management methodologies (e.g., Agile vs. Waterfall) tailored for data-centric projects and learn to define clear scope, create work breakdown structures, manage stakeholders, and identify/mitigate potential risks.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module acts as the strategic foundation that connects all technical components. Planning directly informs the **dimensional modeling** (Module 1) by defining business requirements. It dictates the **ETL strategy** (Module 3) by setting the project timeline and resource allocation. It also ensures the final warehouse architecture aligns with the organization's long-term **BI and analytics goals**.

**4. Real-world applications**
These skills are directly applicable to roles such as Data Warehouse Project Manager, Business Intelligence Analyst, and Data Architect. Students will be equipped to initiate a project charter, collaborate with business users to define key performance indicators (KPIs), and create a realistic project plan that manages the technical complexity of integrating data from disparate source systems.
