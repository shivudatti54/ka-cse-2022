### Learning Purpose: Requirements Not Fully Determinate

**1. Why is this topic important?**
In real-world data warehousing projects, initial requirements are often incomplete, ambiguous, or subject to change. Acknowledging this indeterminacy is critical because building a rigid warehouse for unclear needs leads to costly rework, project failure, and an inability to support evolving business intelligence. This topic teaches the agile mindset and flexible techniques necessary for success.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn strategies to manage uncertain and evolving requirements. This includes iterative development methodologies (like Agile or Spiral), techniques for prototyping, and designing flexible, scalable data models (e.g., using data vaults or conformed dimensions) that can adapt to new information without a complete rebuild.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the crucial link between theoretical design principles (Module 1) and the physical implementation of ETL processes and BI tools (later modules). It provides the practical framework for applying dimensional modeling and ETL design under realistic, non-ideal conditions where perfect information is unavailable.

**4. Real-world applications**
These principles are applied whenever businesses embark on new analytical initiatives, such as entering new markets, launching products, or dealing with regulatory changes. Data warehousing teams use these techniques to deliver value incrementally, gather feedback, and ensure the final solution truly meets the business's discovered—not just initial—needs.
