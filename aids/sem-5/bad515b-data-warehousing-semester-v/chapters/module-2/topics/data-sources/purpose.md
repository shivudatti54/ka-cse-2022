### Learning Purpose: Data Sources

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding data sources is fundamental because they are the origin points for all data flowing into a warehouse. The quality, structure, and reliability of these sources directly determine the accuracy and value of the business intelligence derived from the data warehouse. Selecting inappropriate or poor-quality sources can lead to a failed project, making this a critical first step.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify and classify different types of data sources, including operational databases (OLTP), flat files, APIs, and external data streams. They will explore the technical and business challenges associated with each, such as data formats, heterogeneity, and frequency of updates. The concept of data profiling to assess source quality will also be introduced.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the essential prerequisite for subsequent modules. It directly connects to **ETL (Extract, Transform, Load)** processes, where data is extracted from these sources. It also links to **data quality management**, **data modeling** (structuring the data), and ultimately, **data analysis and mining**, as the source data defines what can be analyzed.

**4. Real-world applications**
In the real world, analysts source data from diverse systems like CRM (Salesforce), ERP (SAP), web server logs, social media feeds, and IoT sensors. For example, a retail data warehouse integrates sales transactions (databases), customer reviews (flat files), and inventory levels (APIs) to provide a complete view of business performance.
