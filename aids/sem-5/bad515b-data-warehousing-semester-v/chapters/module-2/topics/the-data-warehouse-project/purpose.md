### Learning Purpose: The Data Warehouse Project

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because it moves from theoretical concepts to the practical execution of building a data warehouse. Understanding the project lifecycle is essential for successfully delivering a system that meets business needs, stays within budget, and is completed on time. It highlights the non-technical challenges, such as requirement gathering and project management, which are often the primary reasons for project failure.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the key phases of a data warehouse project, from initial planning and requirement analysis to design, construction, deployment, and maintenance. They will understand the roles of various stakeholders, the importance of a clear project scope, and methodologies for managing the development process to ensure alignment with business objectives.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module synthesizes prior knowledge of data modeling (Module 1), ETL processes, and OLAP cubes. It provides the framework for applying these technical skills within a structured project management context. It also connects to broader IT and business strategy concepts, showing how a data warehouse delivers value and supports decision-making.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is directly applicable to roles like Data Warehouse Project Manager, Business Analyst, or Data Architect. Students will be equipped to participate in or lead projects that centralize organizational data into a single source of truth, enabling business intelligence, reporting, and data-driven analytics that are foundational to modern enterprises.
