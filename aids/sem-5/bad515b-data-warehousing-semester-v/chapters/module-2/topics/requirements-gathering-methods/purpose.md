### Learning Purpose: Requirements Gathering Methods

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Requirements gathering is the foundational step in building a successful data warehouse. Inaccurate or incomplete requirements lead to a system that fails to meet business needs, resulting in costly rework, user dissatisfaction, and poor decision-making. This topic is crucial as it equips students with the methodologies to correctly capture and define what the business truly needs from its data assets.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core techniques for eliciting business and data requirements. This includes methods like interviews, surveys, facilitated sessions (JAD), prototyping, and analyzing existing documentation. They will understand how to identify key stakeholders, ask the right questions, and translate vague business goals into concrete, technical specifications for the data warehouse.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This process directly informs the subsequent stages of data warehousing. The gathered requirements dictate the design of the dimensional model (star/snowflake schemas), the Extract, Transform, Load (ETL) strategy, and the choice of front-end business intelligence tools. It is the critical link between business strategy (Module 1) and technical implementation (Modules 3 & 4).

**4. Real-world applications**
These methods are applied in every industry to develop systems for sales reporting, customer analytics, financial forecasting, and operational efficiency. A business analyst uses these skills to ensure a new dashboard accurately reflects a sales team's need to track performance by region and product line, preventing the development of a useless, irrelevant data product.
