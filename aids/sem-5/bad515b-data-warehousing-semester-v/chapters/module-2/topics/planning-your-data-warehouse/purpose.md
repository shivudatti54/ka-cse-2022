### Learning Purpose: Planning Your Data Warehouse

**1. Why is this topic important?**
A well-planned data warehouse is the foundation of effective business intelligence. Without a strategic plan, organizations risk building costly, inflexible systems that fail to meet business needs. This phase dictates the project's success, defining its scope, architecture, and long-term viability. It ensures alignment between technical implementation and core business objectives, maximizing ROI and preventing critical failures.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the key components of a data warehouse plan. This includes defining business requirements, selecting an appropriate architecture (e.g., Kimball vs. Inmon), identifying data sources, creating a high-level dimensional model, and establishing a project roadmap. They will understand how to conduct feasibility studies and manage stakeholder expectations.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the crucial bridge between high-level business strategy (Module 1) and the technical execution of subsequent modules. It directly informs ETL processes, data modeling, and infrastructure choices. A solid plan ensures that later technical work on extraction, transformation, and loading has a clear blueprint to follow.

**4. Real-world applications**
This planning process is used in every industry to build centralized data repositories for reporting and analytics. For example, a retail chain would use it to plan a customer analytics warehouse, integrating data from POS systems, e-commerce platforms, and loyalty programs to gain a unified view of customer behavior and optimize marketing strategies.
