### Learning Purpose: Data Quality

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Data quality is the cornerstone of any effective data warehousing initiative. Poor quality data—inaccurate, incomplete, or inconsistent—leads to flawed analytics, misguided business decisions, and a loss of trust in the data warehouse itself. Ensuring high data quality is essential for deriving reliable, actionable insights that drive strategic value.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define the key dimensions of data quality (e.g., accuracy, completeness, consistency, timeliness). They will explore common causes of data defects and the methodologies used to assess, cleanse, and monitor data throughout the ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) process to maintain its integrity.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is intrinsically linked to ETL processes, where data cleansing and transformation occur. It directly impacts downstream concepts like Business Intelligence (BI) reporting, data mining, and OLAP cubes, as the reliability of these systems is entirely dependent on the quality of the underlying data.

**4. Real-world applications**
These skills are applied in roles such as Data Analyst and ETL Developer to build trust in organizational reporting. For example, ensuring customer data is accurate for targeted marketing campaigns or that financial data is consistent for regulatory compliance.
