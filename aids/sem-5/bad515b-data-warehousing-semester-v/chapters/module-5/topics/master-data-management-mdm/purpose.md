### **Learning Purpose: Master Data Management (MDM)**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Master Data Management is a critical discipline because it ensures an organization has a single, accurate, and consistent version of its core business data (e.g., customers, products, suppliers). Without MDM, data becomes siloed and inconsistent across systems, leading to poor analytics, operational inefficiency, flawed reporting, and compliance risks.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core principles, architecture, and implementation processes of MDM. This includes understanding master data, identifying golden records, and exploring different MDM implementation styles (e.g., Consolidation, Registry, Coexistence, Transactional). They will also study the governance and stewardship models required to maintain data quality and integrity.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
MDM is a foundational pillar for a successful Data Warehouse and Business Intelligence ecosystem. It connects directly to **ETL/ELT processes** (data integration), **Data Quality** management, and **Data Governance**. It ensures the dimensional models and fact tables within the data warehouse are built on trustworthy, unified master data.

**4. Real-world applications**
MDM is applied everywhere data consistency is crucial. Real-world applications include creating a **360-degree customer view** for marketing, ensuring accurate **supply chain management**, reconciling financial data for **compliance (e.g., SOX, GDPR)**, and enabling reliable **enterprise resource planning (ERP)**.
