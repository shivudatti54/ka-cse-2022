### **Module 5 Learning Purpose: Major Features and Functions**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the core features and functions of a data warehouse is fundamental because they are the mechanisms that enable it to fulfill its primary purpose: turning raw data into actionable intelligence. These features, such as data integration, subject-orientation, and time-variance, are what distinguish a data warehouse from a standard operational database and are critical for supporting reliable business intelligence, reporting, and analytics.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify and explain the key characteristics of a data warehouse, including **Integrated, Subject-Oriented, Non-Volatile, and Time-Variant** data. They will understand the core functions that enable these features, such as the Extract, Transform, Load (ETL) process for integration and the role of dimensional modeling (star/snowflake schemas) for structuring data around business subjects.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module builds directly on prior knowledge of data models and architectures (Module 4). It provides the essential groundwork for subsequent topics like Online Analytical Processing (OLAP), data mining, and business performance management (Module 6), as these advanced techniques rely entirely on a warehouse built with these specific features and functions.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied when designing a new data warehouse to ensure it meets business reporting needs, when evaluating ETL tools for a project, or when troubleshooting why a report might be showing incorrect historical data. It is the foundational theory behind every successful BI and analytics platform in industry.
