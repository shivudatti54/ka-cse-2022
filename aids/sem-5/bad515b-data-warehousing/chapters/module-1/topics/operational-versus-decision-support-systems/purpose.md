**Why this topic matters:**
This topic is crucial as it highlights the distinction between Operational and Decision-Support Systems, emphasizing the need for organizations to understand the limitations and capabilities of each type of system to make informed strategic decisions. Recognizing the differences between operational and decision-support systems can help organizations avoid failures and improve overall performance.

**Real-world applications:**
Operational systems are commonly used for day-to-day operations, such as managing inventory, scheduling, and customer service, whereas decision-support systems are employed for strategic planning, risk analysis, and forecasting. Real-world applications of this distinction can be seen in industries like healthcare, finance, and supply chain management.

**Connection to other concepts:**
Understanding the differences between operational and decision-support systems is essential in conjunction with other concepts, such as data warehousing, business intelligence, and data governance. By recognizing the importance of each system, organizations can develop a more comprehensive strategy for leveraging data to drive business success.
