## **Escalating Need for Strategic Information**

### Introduction

In today's fast-paced and data-intensive business environment, organizations are under increasing pressure to make informed decisions that drive growth, profitability, and competitiveness. Strategic information, which is defined as information that is relevant to a company's overall business strategy and goals, plays a critical role in achieving these objectives. However, the ability to extract insights and meaning from complex data has become a significant challenge for many organizations.

### Historical Context

The need for strategic information has been around for decades, but the pace and scale of data generation have accelerated dramatically in recent years with the advent of the internet, mobile devices, and big data technologies. As a result, organizations have struggled to keep up with the increasing volume, velocity, and variety of data.

In the early days of data warehousing, the focus was on creating centralized repositories that stored and provided access to historical data. However, this approach had several limitations, including:

- **Inability to support real-time decision-making**: Data was typically updated at regular intervals, making it difficult to support timely decision-making.
- **Limited ability to analyze complex data**: Traditional data warehousing solutions were not designed to handle complex, unstructured data.
- **Insufficient attention to business intelligence**: Data warehousing was often viewed as a technical exercise, rather than a strategic business initiative.

### Failures of Past Decision-Support Systems

Many organizations have invested heavily in decision-support systems (DSS) that promised to provide strategic insights and support decision-making. However, these systems often failed to deliver, due to a range of issues including:

- **Lack of business understanding**: DSS were often designed and implemented without a deep understanding of the business problem or requirements.
- **Insufficient data quality**: Poor data quality and integrity issues led to inaccurate or incomplete results.
- **Inability to support real-time decision-making**: DSS were often slow to respond to changing business conditions.

### Modern Developments

In recent years, there has been a significant shift towards more flexible, scalable, and user-friendly data warehousing solutions that can support strategic decision-making. Some of the key modern developments include:

- **Cloud-based data warehousing**: Cloud-based data warehousing solutions offer greater scalability, flexibility, and cost-effectiveness.
- **Big data analytics**: The increasing availability of big data technologies, such as Hadoop and Spark, has enabled organizations to analyze complex, unstructured data.
- **Business intelligence and analytics tools**: Advances in business intelligence and analytics tools have improved the ability to extract insights and meaning from data.

### Key Concepts

- **Strategic information**: Information that is relevant to a company's overall business strategy and goals.
- **Data warehousing**: A centralized repository that stores and provides access to historical data.
- **Business intelligence**: The use of data and analytics to support decision-making and improve organizational performance.
- **Big data**: Large, complex datasets that are difficult to process using traditional data warehousing solutions.

### Real-World Examples

- **Retail**: A retail company uses a cloud-based data warehousing solution to analyze sales data and optimize inventory management.
- **Finance**: A financial services company uses big data analytics to identify new business opportunities and improve risk management.
- **Manufacturing**: A manufacturing company uses business intelligence tools to optimize production planning and improve product quality.
