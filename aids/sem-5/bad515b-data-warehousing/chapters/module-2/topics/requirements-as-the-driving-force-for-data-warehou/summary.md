# **Requirements as the Driving Force for Data Warehousing**

## **Introduction**

- Data warehousing is a process of designing, building, and maintaining a shared repository of data to support business intelligence and decision-making.
- Requirements play a crucial role in driving the data warehousing process.

## **Key Requirements**

- **Business Requirements**
  - Identify business needs and objectives
  - Define data entities and attributes
  - Determine data volume and frequency of updates
- **Technical Requirements**
  - Data integration and data quality
  - Performance and scalability
  - Security and access control
- **User Requirements**
  - Define user needs and expectations
  - Determine reporting and analysis capabilities

## **Data Design**

- **Entity-Relationship Modeling (ERM)**
  - Define the structure and relationships between data entities
  - Identify key attributes and relationships
- **Star and Snowflake Schemas**
  - Star schema: fact table with dimensions
  - Snowflake schema: fact table with multiple dimensions

## **Architectural Plan**

- **Data Warehouse Architecture**
  - centralized vs. distributed architecture
  - data mart vs. data warehouse
- **Data Warehousing Models**
  - logical vs. physical data models

## **Data Storage Specifications**

- **Data Storage Types**
  - relational databases
  - data warehouses
  - data marts
- **Data Storage Technologies**
  - storage area networks (SANs)
  - network-attached storage (NAS)
  - cloud storage

## **Information Delivery Strategy**

- **Data Delivery Methods**
  - reporting and analysis tools
  - data visualization and reporting tools
  - data mining and predictive analytics
- **Information Delivery Channels**
  - intranet and extranet
  - web portals and mobile devices

## **Important Formulas and Definitions**

- **Business Intelligence (BI)**
  - definition: using data to support business decision-making
  - process: data warehousing, data analysis, and reporting
- **Data Warehousing Maturity Model (DWMM)**
  - definition: a framework for evaluating data warehousing maturity
  - stages: strategic planning, data warehousing, business analysis, and implementation

## **Theorems and Concepts**

- **Data Integration Theorem**
  - the law of data integration: "the quantity of data is inversely proportional to the quality of data"
- **Data Quality Theorem**
  - the law of data quality: "the quality of data is directly proportional to the quantity of data"
