# **Who Will Use The Information? Information Delivery**

## **Introduction**

In the realm of data warehousing, delivering accurate and relevant information to the right users is crucial for informed decision-making. The question of who will use the information and how it will be delivered is a critical aspect of the data warehousing process. In this chapter, we will delve into the importance of information delivery, historical context, and modern developments in this area.

## **Historical Context**

The concept of information delivery has been around for decades, but the advent of data warehousing and business intelligence (BI) has brought it to the forefront. In the early days of data warehousing, information delivery was primarily focused on technical analysts and IT professionals. However, as the technology evolved, it became clear that end-users, such as business stakeholders and managers, needed access to accurate and timely information to make informed decisions.

## **The Importance of Information Delivery**

Information delivery is critical for several reasons:

1.  **Informed Decision-Making**: Accurate and relevant information enables users to make informed decisions, which can lead to improved business outcomes.
2.  **Increased Productivity**: By providing users with the information they need, organizations can reduce the time and effort required to gather data, leading to increased productivity.
3.  **Better Collaboration**: Information delivery can facilitate collaboration among teams and stakeholders, leading to better communication and decision-making.

## **Types of Information Delivery**

There are several types of information delivery, including:

1.  **Self-Service BI**: Allowing users to access and analyze data without IT assistance.
2.  **Reports and Dashboards**: Providing pre-built reports and dashboards for users to access and analyze data.
3.  **Data Visualization**: Using visualizations, such as charts and graphs, to present complex data in a clear and concise manner.
4.  **Interactive Analytics**: Allowing users to interact with data in real-time, using tools such as query interfaces and ad-hoc analysis.

## **Modern Developments**

In recent years, there have been several modern developments in information delivery, including:

1.  **Cloud-Based BI**: Cloud-based BI solutions, such as Amazon QuickSight and Google Data Studio, provide users with easy access to data and analytics.
2.  **Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML)**: AI and ML are being used to improve data visualization, predictive analytics, and automated reporting.
3.  **Mobile Devices**: Mobile devices, such as smartphones and tablets, are providing users with access to data and analytics on-the-go.

## **Case Studies**

Several organizations have implemented successful information delivery solutions, including:

1.  **The Coca-Cola Company**: Coca-Cola used a self-service BI solution to provide employees with access to data and analytics, leading to improved decision-making and increased productivity.
2.  **The Walmart Corporation**: Walmart used a data visualization solution to present complex data in a clear and concise manner, leading to improved sales forecasting and inventory management.

## **Applications**

Information delivery has numerous applications across various industries, including:

1.  **Retail**: Providing retailers with access to real-time sales data and analytics, enabling them to make informed decisions about inventory management and marketing.
2.  **Finance**: Providing financial institutions with access to accurate and timely financial data, enabling them to make informed decisions about lending and risk management.
3.  **Healthcare**: Providing healthcare professionals with access to accurate and timely patient data, enabling them to make informed decisions about patient care and treatment.

## **Conclusion**

Information delivery is a critical aspect of data warehousing, enabling users to access accurate and relevant information to make informed decisions. The history of information delivery has evolved over time, from technical analysts to end-users, and continues to evolve with modern developments such as cloud-based BI and AI/ML. Case studies and applications demonstrate the importance of information delivery across various industries.
