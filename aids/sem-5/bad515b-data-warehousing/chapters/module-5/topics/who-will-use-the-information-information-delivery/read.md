# **Who Will Use The Information? Information Delivery**

## **Introduction**

In the context of data warehousing, information delivery refers to the process of making data available to the right people at the right time to support informed decision-making. In this topic, we will discuss the importance of information delivery and the various methods used to make data accessible to stakeholders.

## **Definition of Information Delivery**

Information delivery is the process of presenting data in a format that is easily understandable and usable by the intended audience. It involves the creation, dissemination, and maintenance of data products that meet the needs of users.

## **Types of Users**

There are several types of users who are stakeholders in an organization's data warehousing initiative:

- **Business Users**: These are individuals who need data to support their business activities, such as sales, marketing, or finance.
- **Analysts**: These are individuals who analyze data to support business decisions, such as data analysts or business intelligence analysts.
- **Executives**: These are senior-level executives who make strategic decisions based on data insights.

## **Importance of Information Delivery**

Information delivery is critical to the success of a data warehousing initiative because it ensures that users have access to the data they need to support informed decision-making. When data is delivered in a format that is easy to understand and usable, users are more likely to:

- **Act on data insights**: Users are more likely to act on data insights if the data is presented in a format that is easy to understand.
- **Make informed decisions**: Users are more likely to make informed decisions if they have access to accurate and timely data.

## **Methods of Information Delivery**

There are several methods of information delivery, including:

- **Data Mining**: This involves extracting insights from data using specialized software and techniques.
- **Reporting**: This involves presenting data in a format that is easy to understand, such as reports or dashboards.
- **Data Visualization**: This involves presenting data in a visual format, such as charts or graphs.
- **Data Sharing**: This involves making data available to multiple stakeholders at the same time.

## **Key Concepts in Information Delivery**

- **Data Product**: A data product is a specific presentation of data that is designed to meet the needs of a specific user group.
- **Data Warehouse**: A data warehouse is a centralized repository of data that is designed to support information delivery.
- **ETL (Extract, Transform, Load)**: ETL is a process of extracting data from multiple sources, transforming it into a standardized format, and loading it into a data warehouse.

## **Best Practices in Information Delivery**

- **Know Your Users**: Understand the needs and preferences of your users to design data products that meet their needs.
- **Use Simple Language**: Use simple language and avoid technical jargon to ensure that data is accessible to users.
- **Provide Context**: Provide context to help users understand the data and its relevance to their business activities.

By understanding the importance of information delivery and the various methods used to make data accessible to stakeholders, organizations can ensure that their data warehousing initiative is successful and provides value to users.
