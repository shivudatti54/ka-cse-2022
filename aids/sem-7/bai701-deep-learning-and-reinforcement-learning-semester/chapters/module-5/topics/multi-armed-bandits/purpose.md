# Learning Purpose: Multi-Armed Bandits

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Multi-Armed Bandits (MAB) provide a fundamental framework for understanding the core trade-off in reinforcement learning: exploration versus exploitation. This is a critical concept for building intelligent systems that must learn from and adapt to uncertain environments, making it a cornerstone of efficient decision-making algorithms.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the formal definition of the MAB problem and analyze strategies like ε-greedy, Upper Confidence Bound (UCB), and Thompson Sampling. They will understand how these algorithms balance exploring new options with exploiting known rewards to maximize cumulative gain and evaluate their performance using measures like regret.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic serves as a simplified introduction to key RL concepts like value estimation, policies, and regret before moving on to more complex, full Markov Decision Processes (MDPs). It directly builds upon probability and statistics from earlier modules and provides the foundational exploration-exploitation dilemma that is central to advanced deep RL.

**4. Real-world applications**
MAB algorithms are deployed in numerous real-world systems, including optimizing website A/B testing, personalizing news article recommendations, clinical trial design, and dynamic pricing models, making them one of the most widely applied RL concepts in industry.
