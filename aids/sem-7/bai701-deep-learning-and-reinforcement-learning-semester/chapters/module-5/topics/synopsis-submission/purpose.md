### **Learning Purpose: Synopsis Submission**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Submitting a well-structured synopsis is a critical academic and professional skill. It forces the articulation of a complex project's core idea, methodology, and expected outcomes into a concise proposal. This process is foundational for securing approval, guiding research, and effectively communicating technical plans to stakeholders, mirroring real-world grant or project proposal writing.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to formulate a clear research question for a deep or reinforcement learning project. They will develop the ability to outline a chosen model architecture (e.g., CNN, RNN, DQN), define a suitable dataset and evaluation metrics, and propose a realistic timeline. The focus is on structuring ideas and justifying technical choices concisely.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This task integrates all prior module concepts. Students must apply knowledge of neural network fundamentals, optimization algorithms, and specific RL or DL paradigms (e.g., policy gradients, Q-learning, CNNs) to justify their model choice. It also connects to practical considerations like data preprocessing, computational requirements, and ethical implications of their proposed model.

**4. Real-world applications**
The skill of writing a project synopsis directly translates to creating technical proposals in industry roles, such as an ML Engineer proposing a new model to a product team, a Research Scientist applying for funding, or a Data Scientist outlining a proof-of-concept project to management. It is the bridge between theoretical knowledge and executable project planning.
