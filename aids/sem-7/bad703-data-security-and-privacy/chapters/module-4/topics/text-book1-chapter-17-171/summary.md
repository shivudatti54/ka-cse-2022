# **Text Book 1: Chapter 17.1 - Data Security and Privacy**

## **Key Points**

### Definitions

- **Data Security**: The practice of protecting digital data from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction.
- **Data Privacy**: The control over who can access, use, or share personal information.

### Theorems

- **Cantor's Theorem**: A fundamental principle in set theory, demonstrating the uniqueness of a function.
- **Bell's Theorem**: A theorem in quantum mechanics, illustrating the fundamental limits of quantum description.

### Formulas

- **Data Encryption**: E(X) = C, where E(X) is the encrypted message, C is the ciphertext, and X is the plaintext.
- **Data Decryption**: D(C) = X, where D(C) is the decrypted message, C is the ciphertext, and X is the plaintext.

### Important Concepts

- **Authentication**: The process of verifying the identity of a user or system.
- **Authorization**: The process of granting or denying access to resources based on user identity and permissions.
- **Data Anonymization**: The process of removing personally identifiable information (PII) from data to protect privacy.

### Security Measures

- **Firewalls**: Network security systems that monitor and control incoming and outgoing network traffic.
- **Encryption**: The process of converting plaintext into ciphertext to protect data from unauthorized access.
- **Access Control**: The process of regulating who can access resources and data.
