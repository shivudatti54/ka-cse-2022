# Data Security and Privacy

==========================

## Key Concepts

---

- **Data Security**: The practice of protecting data from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction.
- **Data Privacy**: The protection of personal information from unauthorized access, use, or disclosure.

## Important Formulas and Definitions

---

- **Data Encryption**:
  - Formula: `C = E(K, M)`, where `C` is the ciphertext, `E` is the encryption function, `K` is the key, and `M` is the plaintext.
  - Definition: The process of converting plaintext into unreadable ciphertext using an encryption algorithm and a secret key.
- **Data Decryption**:
  - Formula: `M = D(K, C)`, where `M` is the plaintext, `D` is the decryption function, `K` is the key, and `C` is the ciphertext.
  - Definition: The process of converting ciphertext back into plaintext using a decryption algorithm and a secret key.
- **Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability (CIA)**:
  - **Confidentiality**: The protection of data from unauthorized access.
  - **Integrity**: The protection of data from unauthorized modification or destruction.
  - **Availability**: The protection of data from unauthorized denial of access.

## Important Theorems

---

- **Data Privacy Theorem**: If a data processing system is designed to protect the privacy of individuals, it will also protect the confidentiality of the data.
- **Data Security Theorem**: If a data processing system is designed to protect the confidentiality and integrity of data, it will also ensure the availability of the data.
