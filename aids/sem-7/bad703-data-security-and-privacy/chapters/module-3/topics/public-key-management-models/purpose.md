### Learning Purpose: Public-Key Management Models

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Public-key cryptography is the foundation of secure digital communication. However, the security of the entire system hinges on how public keys are managed, distributed, and trusted. Understanding management models is critical because a compromised or mismanaged key can lead to catastrophic data breaches, identity theft, and a complete breakdown of trust in digital systems.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core models for establishing trust in public keys. This includes the centralized Certificate Authority (CA) model used in web PKI (HTTPS), the decentralized Web of Trust (WoT) model, and emerging decentralized models like blockchain-based systems. They will understand the processes of key generation, distribution, revocation, and the role of digital certificates.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon the fundamental concepts of public-key cryptography (Module 2). It is the essential link between theoretical cryptography and its practical application in protocols like SSL/TLS, secure email (PGP/GPG), and digital signatures. It also connects to broader themes of identity management, trust models, and blockchain technology.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied every time a user visits a secure website (verified by a CA), sends a cryptographically signed email, or uses a government-issued digital ID. It is also fundamental for IT professionals who manage organizational PKI, issue certificates for internal servers, and ensure secure authentication and data integrity.
