### **Learning Purpose: The Security of RSA & Diffie-Hellman Key Exchange**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
These algorithms are the bedrock of modern secure communication. Understanding their security is fundamental to grasping how trust and confidentiality are established online, from HTTPS connections to virtual private networks (VPNs). It moves beyond abstract theory to the practical protocols protecting everyday digital interactions.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core cryptographic principles behind RSA (a public-key system for encryption and digital signatures) and the Diffie-Hellman key exchange (a method for securely establishing a shared secret over a public channel). They will analyze the mathematical problems that provide their security (integer factorization and the discrete logarithm problem) and evaluate their strengths and vulnerabilities against modern threats, including brute-force and quantum computing attacks.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon foundational concepts of symmetric vs. asymmetric cryptography. It is the critical link that explains *how* a secure session key is generated and exchanged before being used with faster symmetric ciphers (like AES). It also connects forward to topics on authentication (digital certificates), protocol security (e.g., TLS/SSL), and post-quantum cryptography.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied in nearly every secure online protocol. RSA is used for securing web traffic (HTTPS), email (PGP/GPG), and code signing. Diffie-Hellman is a key component of the security protocols for Wi-Fi (WPA2/3), SSH, and IPsec VPNs, ensuring that even if a communication is intercepted, the session key remains secret.
