# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define public key cryptanalysis and explain its role in evaluating cryptographic security
2. Describe the mathematical foundations of attacks on RSA, particularly factorization-based attacks
3. Analyze and explain timing attacks and their relationship to side-channel security
4. Evaluate chosen ciphertext attacks (CCA1 and CCA2) and their impact on RSA security
5. Apply Wiener's attack algorithm to recover small private exponents
6. Understand Hastad's broadcast attack and the Chinese Remainder Theorem's role in breaking low-exponent RSA
7. Compare different cryptanalytic techniques and their computational complexity
8. Design appropriate countermeasures against major public key cryptanalytic attacks
