# **Chapter 2: Data and Sampling Distributions**

## **2.1 Introduction**

In this chapter, we will explore the concept of data and sampling distributions, which are fundamental to statistical machine learning. We will discuss the importance of random sampling, selection bias, and the sampling distribution of a statistic.

## **2.2 Random Sampling**

Random sampling is a process of selecting a subset of data from a larger population, where every individual in the population has an equal chance of being selected. The key characteristics of random sampling are:

- **Independence**: Each observation is selected independently of the others.
- **Uniformity**: Every individual in the population has an equal chance of being selected.
- **Non-bias**: The sample is representative of the population.

**Example:** A researcher wants to study the average height of adults in a city. She randomly selects 1000 adults from the city and measures their heights. The sample is representative of the population, and every adult in the city has an equal chance of being selected.

## **2.3 Selection Bias**

Selection bias occurs when the sample is not representative of the population due to unequal selection of individuals. There are several types of selection bias:

- **Non-response bias**: Some individuals in the population are not included in the sample.
- **Self-selection bias**: Individuals in the population select themselves into the sample.
- **Non-coverage bias**: Some individuals in the population are not included in the sample due to incomplete sampling.

**Example:** A researcher wants to study the average income of students in a university. However, he only surveys students who are registered in a particular course. This sample is biased because it excludes students who are not registered in the course.

## **2.4 Sampling Distribution of a Statistic**

The sampling distribution of a statistic is a distribution of the statistic obtained by sampling from a population. The key characteristics of a sampling distribution are:

- **Central Limit Theorem (CLT)**: The sampling distribution of a statistic follows a normal distribution as the sample size increases.
- **Standard Deviation**: The standard deviation of the sampling distribution is the standard deviation of the population divided by the square root of the sample size.

**Example:** A researcher wants to estimate the population mean using a sample of size 30. The population mean is 100, and the population standard deviation is 10. The sampling distribution of the sample mean is a normal distribution with a mean of 100 and a standard deviation of 10 / sqrt(30).

- Selection bias
- Sampling distribution of a statistic
- Central Limit Theorem (CLT)
- Standard Deviation
