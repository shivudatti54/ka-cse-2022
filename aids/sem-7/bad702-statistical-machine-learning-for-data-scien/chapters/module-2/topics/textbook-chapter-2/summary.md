**Statistical Machine Learning for Data Science**
**Chapter 2: Data and Sampling Distributions**
=====================================================

### Key Points

- **Random Sampling**:
  - A method of selecting a sample from a population without replacement or bias.
  - Ensures that every observation in the population has an equal chance of being selected.
- **Selection Bias**:
  - A type of bias that occurs when the sample is not representative of the population.
  - Can occur due to non-random selection of samples.
- **Sampling Distribution of a Statistic**:
  - The distribution of a statistic (e.g. mean, proportion) obtained from multiple samples.
  - Can be used to make inferences about the population.
- **The Central Limit Theorem (CLT)**:
  - States that the sampling distribution of the mean is approximately normal, even if the population distribution is not normal.
  - Provides a foundation for statistical inference.

### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Standard Error (SE)**: The standard deviation of the sampling distribution of a statistic.
- **Confidence Interval**: A range of values within which a population parameter is likely to lie.
- **Margin of Error (ME)**: The maximum difference between the sample statistic and the true population parameter.

### Important Theorems

- **The Law of Large Numbers (LLN)**: States that the average of the results obtained from a large number of trials will be close to the expected value.
- **The Central Limit Theorem (CLT)**: States that the sampling distribution of the mean is approximately normal, even if the population distribution is not normal.

- Recognize the differences between selection bias and other types of bias.
- Apply the Central Limit Theorem to make inferences about population parameters.
