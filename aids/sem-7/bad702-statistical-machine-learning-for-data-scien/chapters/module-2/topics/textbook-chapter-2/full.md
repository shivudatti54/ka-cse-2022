## **Random Sampling**

Random sampling is the process of selecting a subset of data from a larger population, where every individual has an equal chance of being chosen. This method ensures that the sample is representative of the population, minimizing bias and ensuring generalizability.

## **Types of Random Sampling**

1. **Simple Random Sampling**: Each individual in the population has an equal chance of being selected.
2. **Stratified Random Sampling**: The population is divided into subgroups, and random sampling occurs within each subgroup.
3. **Cluster Sampling**: The population is divided into clusters, and random sampling occurs within each cluster.

## **Selection Bias**

Selection bias occurs when the sample is not representative of the population, often due to non-random selection. This can lead to inaccurate conclusions and flawed decision-making.

## **Types of Selection Bias**

1. **Non-Response Bias**: Respondents are not representative of the non-respondents.
2. **Refusal Bias**: Respondents who refuse to participate are not representative of the remaining respondents.
3. **Self-Selection Bias**: Respondents select themselves into the sample, often based on self-interest.

## **Sampling Distribution of Statistics**

The sampling distribution of a statistic is the distribution of the statistic over multiple samples from the same population. This distribution is used to estimate the population parameter, providing a measure of uncertainty.

## **Types of Sampling Distributions**

1. **Sampling Distribution of the Mean**: The distribution of the sample mean.
2. **Sampling Distribution of the Proportion**: The distribution of the sample proportion.
3. **Sampling Distribution of the Standard Deviation**: The distribution of the sample standard deviation.

**Example:**
Suppose we want to estimate the average height of a population using simple random sampling. We select a sample of 100 individuals and calculate the sample mean height of 175 cm. We then use the sampling distribution of the mean to estimate the population mean height.

**Case Study:**
A company wants to estimate the average salary of all employees in the organization. They conduct a survey of 500 employees and calculate the sample mean salary of $50,000. Using the sampling distribution of the mean, they estimate the population mean salary to be $50,000 ± $1,000.

## **Applications**

1. **Social Science Research**: Random sampling is used to estimate population parameters in social science research.
2. **Business Decision-Making**: Selection bias is avoided by using random sampling to estimate population parameters in business decision-making.
3. **Medical Research**: The sampling distribution of statistics is used to estimate population parameters in medical research.
