# Revision Notes: Creating Visualizations for Satisfaction Level and Repeat Purchases

===========================================================

### Introduction

This topic focuses on creating visualizations to explore the relationship between satisfaction level and repeat purchases.

### Key Points

#### Data Visualization

- Bar plots and stacked bar charts can be used to visualize the relationship between satisfaction level and repeat purchases.
- These visualizations can help identify patterns, trends, and correlations.

#### Satisfaction Level

- Satisfaction level is a categorical variable (e.g., 1-5 scale) used to measure customer satisfaction.
- Commonly used satisfaction scales include:
  - Likert scale (1-5)
  - Numerical rating scale (1-5)
  - Semantic differential scale (1-7)

#### Repeat Purchases

- Repeat purchases refer to the number of times a customer buys a product or service.
- This variable can be categorical (e.g., one-time, repeat) or numerical (e.g., frequency of purchases).

#### Formulas and Definitions

- **Correlation Coefficient (r)**: measures the strength and direction of the linear relationship between two variables.
  - Formula: r = Σ[(xi - x̄)(yi - ȳ)] / sqrt(Σ(xi - x̄)² \* Σ(yi - ȳ)²)
- **Regression Analysis**: a statistical method to model the relationship between a dependent variable (e.g., repeat purchases) and one or more independent variables (e.g., satisfaction level).

#### Important Concepts

- **Correlation vs. Causation**: correlation does not imply causation.
- **Visual Inspection**: inspecting visualizations to identify patterns and trends in the data.

### Key Theorems

- **Central Limit Theorem**: states that the distribution of sample means approaches a normal distribution as the sample size increases.
- **Linearity Assumption**: assumes that the relationship between variables is linear.

### Revision Tips

- Focus on understanding the concepts and formulas.
- Practice creating visualizations and interpreting results.
- Review statistical concepts, such as correlation and regression analysis.
