# **And Calculate the Sample Mean for Each**

- **Definition:** The sample mean, also known as the arithmetic mean, is a measure of central tendency that represents the average value of a dataset.
- **Formula:** $\bar{x} = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i$
  - $\bar{x}$: sample mean
  - $x_i$: individual data points
  - $n$: number of data points
- **Theorem:** The sample mean is an unbiased estimator of the population mean, i.e., $E(\bar{x}) = \mu$, where $\mu$ is the population mean.
- **Importance:** The sample mean is used in many statistical techniques, including hypothesis testing and confidence intervals.

**Key Points:**

- The sample mean is a widely used measure of central tendency.
- It is calculated by summing all data points and dividing by the number of data points.
- The sample mean is an unbiased estimator of the population mean.
