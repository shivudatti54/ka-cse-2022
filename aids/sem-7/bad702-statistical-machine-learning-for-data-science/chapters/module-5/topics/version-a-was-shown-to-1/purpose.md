# Learning Purpose: Module 5, Version A/B Testing

**1. Why is this topic important?**
A/B testing is a fundamental experimental technique for making data-driven decisions. It is critically important for optimizing user experiences, marketing campaigns, and product features in tech, e-commerce, and numerous other industries. Understanding its statistical underpinnings ensures tests are valid, results are reliable, and conclusions are actionable, preventing costly misinterpretations of data.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core principles of designing and analyzing A/B tests. This includes formulating a testable hypothesis, determining appropriate sample sizes, randomizing samples, and calculating key metrics. Crucially, they will learn to perform statistical inference—using hypothesis tests (e.g., t-tests, chi-squared tests) and calculating p-values and confidence intervals—to determine if observed differences between groups are statistically significant or due to random chance.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly applies concepts from probability theory (distributions, chance) and statistical inference (hypothesis testing, confidence intervals) covered in foundational modules. It is a practical application of the experimental design principles that underpin all causal inference and is a precursor to more advanced techniques like multi-armed bandits and causal ML.

**4. Real-world applications**
This is applied everywhere online: testing website layouts (Version A vs. B) to increase click-through rates, evaluating email subject lines to improve open rates, and comparing algorithms to enhance user engagement. It is a standard practice for data scientists and product analysts to guide business strategy and innovation.
