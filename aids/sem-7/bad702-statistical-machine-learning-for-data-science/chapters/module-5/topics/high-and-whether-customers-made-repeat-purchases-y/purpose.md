### **Learning Purpose: Predicting Customer Repeat Purchases**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding customer repeat purchase behavior is a cornerstone of business growth and customer relationship management (CRM). Predicting this binary outcome (Yes/No) is a classic and high-value application of statistical machine learning. It directly impacts profitability, as retaining an existing customer is often far less expensive than acquiring a new one.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to frame a business question as a binary classification problem. They will apply and evaluate key classification algorithms like Logistic Regression, Decision Trees, and potentially more advanced ensemble methods suitable for this task. The focus will be on the entire pipeline: from data preparation and feature engineering to model interpretation, evaluating performance using metrics like accuracy, precision, recall, and ROC curves.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic integrates core concepts from probability (log-odds in Logistic Regression), model evaluation (confusion matrices, cross-validation from Module 4), and feature selection. It serves as a practical bridge between foundational statistical learning and more complex models, reinforcing the importance of choosing the right tool for a specific predictive task.

**4. Real-world applications**
The principles learned are directly applicable in e-commerce for personalized marketing, loyalty program design, and churn prediction. Beyond retail, the same binary classification framework is used in finance for credit scoring (default/not default), in healthcare for disease diagnosis (diseased/healthy), and in countless other domains where a yes/no prediction is required.
