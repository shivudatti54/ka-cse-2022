# Learning Purpose: Module 5 - Generalized Linear Models

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Generalized Linear Models (GLMs) are a critical extension of linear regression, providing the foundational framework for modeling a wide variety of data types beyond the normal distribution. They are essential for data scientists because real-world data (e.g., counts, proportions, binary outcomes) rarely meets the strict assumptions of ordinary least squares, and GLMs offer a principled, unified approach for these scenarios.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core components of GLMs: the random component (exponential family distributions), the systematic component (linear predictor), and the link function connecting them. They will understand how to implement and interpret specific models like Logistic Regression for classification and Poisson Regression for count data.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds upon the linear models and maximum likelihood estimation covered in earlier modules. It establishes a crucial theoretical bridge to more complex nonlinear and deep learning methods, providing the statistical grounding necessary for advanced machine learning.

**4. Real-world applications**
GLMs are applied everywhere: predicting customer churn (logistic regression), forecasting the number of product sales (Poisson regression), determining insurance premiums (Gamma regression), and analyzing A/B test results. Their interpretability makes them a preferred tool for inference in fields like healthcare, finance, and marketing.
