### Module 5: Strategies for Imbalanced Data

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Imbalanced datasets, where one class significantly outnumbers others, are ubiquitous in real-world data science (e.g., fraud detection, disease diagnosis). Standard algorithms often fail by naively favoring the majority class, leading to poor predictive performance on the critical minority class and misleadingly high accuracy scores.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn practical strategies to address class imbalance. This includes data-level methods like oversampling (SMOTE) and undersampling, algorithm-level approaches such as cost-sensitive learning, and model evaluation techniques like Precision-Recall curves and F1-score, moving beyond simple accuracy.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds on core concepts of classification, model evaluation metrics, and bias-variance trade-offs. It directly applies and modifies techniques learned in previous modules, emphasizing that a model's performance is context-dependent and must be measured appropriately for the problem at hand.

**4. Real-world applications**
These strategies are critical for building effective models in domains like financial fraud detection, rare medical condition screening, manufacturing defect identification, and customer churn prediction, where correctly identifying the rare events has immense value.
