### Learning Purpose: Confidence Intervals

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Confidence intervals are a fundamental concept in statistical inference, providing a range of plausible values for an unknown population parameter (like a mean or proportion). They are crucial for quantifying the uncertainty inherent in estimates derived from sample data, which is a cornerstone of robust data science and machine learning.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to calculate confidence intervals for means and proportions, interpret their practical meaning (e.g., "We are 95% confident..."), and understand the relationship between interval width, sample size, and confidence level. This moves beyond point estimates to a more complete and truthful representation of what the data reveals.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly on the sampling distributions and Central Limit Theorem covered in Module 1. It is a prerequisite for understanding key machine learning concepts like the uncertainty in model coefficients and predictions, which leads directly to hypothesis testing (e.g., A/B testing) and Bayesian inference.

**4. Real-world applications**
Confidence intervals are used everywhere data-driven decisions are made: to report margin of error in political polls, compare the average performance of two website layouts in A/B testing, estimate the true mean sales after a marketing campaign, and quantify the uncertainty in a model's forecast.
