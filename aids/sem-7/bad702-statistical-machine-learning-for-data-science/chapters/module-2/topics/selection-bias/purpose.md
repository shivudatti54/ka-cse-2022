# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define selection bias and explain how it differs from random sampling error in terms of both mechanism and behavior as sample size increases.

2. Identify and describe at least five different types of selection bias (sampling bias, survivorship bias, recall bias, attrition bias, confirmation bias) with concrete examples of each.

3. Analyze real-world scenarios to determine whether selection bias is present and explain the specific mechanism causing the bias.

4. Apply appropriate statistical techniques including weighting adjustments and propensity score methods to correct for selection bias when applicable.

5. Evaluate the impact of selection bias on the validity of statistical conclusions, distinguishing between internal and external validity.

6. Design appropriate sampling strategies (simple random, stratified, cluster sampling) to minimize selection bias in data collection.

7. Recognize the connection between selection bias and broader data science applications, including machine learning model fairness and algorithmic bias.
