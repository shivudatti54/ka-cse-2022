### **Module 2: Chi-square**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
The Chi-square test is a cornerstone of statistical analysis, providing a simple yet powerful method to detect relationships between categorical variables. In data science, understanding these associations is crucial for feature selection, model validation, and deriving insights from non-numerical data like survey responses, demographic information, or user behavior categories.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to formulate hypotheses for independence and goodness-of-fit tests. They will calculate the Chi-square statistic, determine its significance using p-values and degrees of freedom, and correctly interpret the results. The module will also cover the assumptions and limitations of the test to prevent its misuse.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly on foundational concepts of probability, hypothesis testing, and p-values. It is a key tool for evaluating the performance of classification models (e.g., comparing expected vs. actual outcomes) and is a prerequisite for more advanced techniques like feature selection algorithms and causal inference analysis in observational studies.

**4. Real-world applications**
Chi-square tests are widely applied in A/B testing to compare user engagement across different groups, in market research to analyze demographic preferences, and in healthcare to study the link between risk factors (like smoking) and health outcomes (like disease incidence). It is a fundamental tool for making data-driven decisions based on categorical data.
