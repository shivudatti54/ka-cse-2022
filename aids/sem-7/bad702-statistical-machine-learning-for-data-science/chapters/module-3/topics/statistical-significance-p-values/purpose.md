# Learning Purpose: Statistical Significance & p-values

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding statistical significance and p-values is fundamental for data scientists to make valid inferences from data. These concepts form the cornerstone of hypothesis testing, allowing practitioners to separate genuine effects from random noise and quantify the strength of evidence against a null hypothesis, which is critical for robust, reliable analysis.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the formal definition of a p-value and the principles of null hypothesis significance testing (NHST). They will understand how to calculate and interpret p-values, set a significance level (α), and distinguish between statistical significance and practical importance. The module will also cover common pitfalls, such as misinterpretation and p-hacking.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds on probability distributions and sampling from earlier modules. It is a prerequisite for more advanced inferential techniques like confidence intervals, power analysis, and multiple testing corrections (e.g., Bonferroni, FDR). It is also the foundation for interpreting results from machine learning models, including A/B tests and feature significance.

**4. Real-world applications**
This is applied in nearly every data-driven field: to validate the results of an A/B test for a website change, to determine if a new drug is effective in clinical trials, to identify significant predictors in a regression model, and to assess the impact of a new marketing campaign on sales revenue.
