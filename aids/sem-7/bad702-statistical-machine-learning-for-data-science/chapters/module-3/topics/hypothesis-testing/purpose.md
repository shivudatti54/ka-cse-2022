### Learning Purpose: Hypothesis Testing

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Hypothesis testing is a cornerstone of statistical inference, providing a rigorous framework for making data-driven decisions. In data science, it is crucial for validating assumptions, comparing models, and determining if observed patterns or results are statistically significant or merely due to random chance.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the formal procedure for conducting hypothesis tests, including formulating null and alternative hypotheses, selecting appropriate tests (e.g., t-test, chi-square), calculating p-values, and interpreting results. They will understand Type I/II errors and the concept of statistical significance and power.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon probability theory and sampling distributions from earlier modules. It is a foundational skill for A/B testing, experimental design, and model evaluation, which are covered later in the curriculum. It also provides the statistical backbone for validating machine learning model performance and feature significance.

**4. Real-world applications**
Hypothesis testing is applied extensively in real-world scenarios such as:
*   **A/B Testing:** Comparing the effectiveness of two website layouts or marketing campaigns.
*   **Medical Trials:** Determining if a new drug has a significant effect compared to a placebo.
*   **Quality Control:** Checking if a manufacturing process meets specified standards.
