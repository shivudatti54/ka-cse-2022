### Learning Purpose: t-tests

**1. Why is this topic important?**
t-tests are a cornerstone of statistical inference, providing a formal method to test hypotheses and draw conclusions about population means from sample data. In data science, they are a fundamental tool for validating assumptions, comparing models, and determining the statistical significance of findings, which is critical for making data-driven decisions.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the theory behind and application of the three main types of t-tests: one-sample, independent two-sample, and paired two-sample. They will understand how to formulate null and alternative hypotheses, calculate test statistics, interpret p-values, and understand confidence intervals. The module will also cover the assumptions underlying the tests and how to check for them.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly on probability distributions, specifically the t-distribution from Module 2. It provides the practical, inferential groundwork for more advanced machine learning concepts like A/B testing, experimental design, and feature selection, where determining a difference's significance is crucial before building a model.

**4. Real-world applications**
t-tests are extensively used for A/B testing in marketing and tech to compare user engagement between two webpage versions. They are also vital in scientific research to validate the efficacy of a new drug versus a placebo and in business analytics to compare sales performance before and after a new strategy is implemented.
