### Learning Purpose: Exploring Two or More Variables

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding relationships between multiple variables is fundamental to data science. Real-world data is inherently multivariate, and simple univariate analysis provides an incomplete picture. This topic is crucial for identifying complex patterns, interactions, and potential confounding factors that drive accurate modeling and insightful analysis.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to visualize relationships using scatterplot matrices, and calculate and interpret correlation coefficients for numerical variables. They will explore techniques for analyzing categorical-numerical relationships through side-by-side box plots and will be introduced to the concept of contingency tables for categorical-categorical analysis.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module builds directly on foundational univariate analysis (summary statistics, distributions). It provides the essential groundwork for subsequent modules on feature engineering (selecting relevant variables) and advanced modeling techniques like linear and logistic regression, which fundamentally model the relationship between multiple input variables and an output/target variable.

**4. Real-world applications**
This skill is applied in countless domains: analyzing customer demographics and spending habits (retail), studying the effect of drug dosage and patient age on outcomes (healthcare), and understanding the relationship between economic indicators and market performance (finance). It is the first step toward predictive modeling.
