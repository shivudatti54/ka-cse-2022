# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the concept of framing and its role in the data link layer of the OSI model
2. Describe the components of a typical frame structure including header, data, and trailer
3. Differentiate between character-oriented (byte stuffing) and bit-oriented (bit stuffing) framing techniques
4. Apply bit stuffing algorithm to a given data sequence and verify the stuffed output
5. Apply byte stuffing algorithm to a given payload containing special characters
6. Analyze the advantages and disadvantages of different framing methods
7. Identify frame components in protocol analyzers and network captures
8. Understand the concept of frame synchronization and transparency in data communication
9. Explain the purpose and operation of the Frame Check Sequence (FCS) in error detection
10. Compare framing in different protocols such as HDLC, PPP, and Ethernet
