# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the fundamental characteristics of UDP and its role in the Internet Protocol Suite.

2. Describe the UDP header structure and the purpose of each field in the header.

3. Compare UDP with TCP, highlighting the advantages and disadvantages of each protocol.

4. Identify the types of applications that typically use UDP and explain why.

5. Understand the concept of connectionless communication and its implications for data delivery.

6. Explain the checksum mechanism used in UDP for error detection.

7. Calculate UDP efficiency and understand the overhead involved in UDP communication.

8. Recognize well-known UDP port numbers and their associated applications.
