# Data communications encompasses the exchange of digital data between devices through defined channels, comprising five fundamental components: message, sender, receiver, transmission medium, and protocols. Transmission modes include simplex (unidirectional), half-duplex (alternating bidirectional), and full-duplex (simultaneous bidirectional). Signal encoding schemes such as NRZ and Manchester encoding facilitate digital data transmission with synchronization properties. Performance evaluation relies on bandwidth, latency, throughput, and the bandwidth-delay product. The Nyquist theorem (C = 2B log₂ M) and Shannon-Hartley theorem (C = B log₂(1 + S/N)) establish theoretical channel capacity limits. Error detection mechanisms include parity checking and CRC polynomial coding. These concepts collectively provide the analytical framework necessary for designing and evaluating data communication systems.
