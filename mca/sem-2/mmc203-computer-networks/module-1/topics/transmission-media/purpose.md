# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Classify transmission media into guided (bounded) and unguided (unbounded) categories with examples of each type.

2. Compare and contrast the characteristics of twisted pair, coaxial, and optical fiber cables including their bandwidth, distance limitations, cost, and susceptibility to interference.

3. Explain the working principles and applications of unguided transmission media including radio waves, microwaves, infrared, and satellite communication.

4. Describe the key transmission characteristics such as bandwidth, attenuation, crosstalk, noise, and propagation delay that affect signal quality.

5. Differentiate between single-mode and multi-mode optical fiber based on core diameter, propagation characteristics, and suitable applications.

6. Analyze given scenarios to select appropriate transmission media based on requirements like distance, bandwidth, cost, and environmental conditions.

7. Understand transmission types (simplex, half-duplex, full-duplex) and their practical applications in communication systems.

8. Apply the knowledge of transmission media to design basic network infrastructure considering practical constraints and requirements.
