# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the concept of packet switching and its advantages over circuit switching in data communication networks.

2. Describe the structure of a packet, including the header, payload, and trailer components with their respective functions.

3. Distinguish between datagram packet switching and virtual circuit packet switching based on connection-oriented vs. connectionless communication.

4. Analyze the routing mechanisms in both datagram and virtual circuit switching, explaining how packets are forwarded in each approach.

5. Compare the advantages and disadvantages of datagram and virtual circuit switching in terms of reliability, overhead, QoS, and failure handling.

6. Identify real-world examples of each packet switching type, including IP, X.25, Frame Relay, and ATM networks.

7. Evaluate the performance characteristics of both switching techniques under different network conditions such as congestion and failure scenarios.

8. Apply the concepts of packet switching to understand how data flows through the Internet and other packet-switched networks.
