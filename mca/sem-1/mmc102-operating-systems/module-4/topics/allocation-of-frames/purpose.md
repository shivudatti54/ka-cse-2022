# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define frames and explain the concept of frame allocation in paged virtual memory systems.

2. Describe and differentiate between equal allocation, proportional allocation, and priority-based allocation strategies for frames.

3. Calculate frame allocations using both equal and proportional allocation methods with given numerical values.

4. Explain the working set model and its relationship to frame allocation and thrashing prevention.

5. Distinguish between global and local page replacement strategies and their implications for frame allocation.

6. Analyze the conditions that lead to thrashing and explain how proper frame allocation prevents it.

7. Evaluate the advantages and disadvantages of different frame allocation strategies in various scenarios.

8. Apply frame allocation concepts to solve numerical problems and design memory management solutions.
