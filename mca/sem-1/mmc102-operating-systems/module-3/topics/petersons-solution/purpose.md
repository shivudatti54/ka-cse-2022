# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define the critical section problem and explain its three fundamental requirements: mutual exclusion, progress, and bounded waiting.

2. Describe the purpose and significance of Peterson's Solution in the context of process synchronization in operating systems.

3. Identify and explain the two shared variables (`turn` and `flag[]`) used in Peterson's Solution and their respective roles.

4. Trace through the execution of Peterson's Solution for various scenarios to demonstrate how mutual exclusion is achieved.

5. Analyze how Peterson's Solution satisfies each of the three requirements for a correct solution to the critical section problem.

6. Evaluate the limitations of Peterson's Solution, including busy waiting and the assumption of atomic operations.

7. Compare software-based solutions like Peterson's Algorithm with hardware-based synchronization primitives.
