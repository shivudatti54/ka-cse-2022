# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the producer-consumer problem and implement its solution using semaphores, including the roles of full, empty, and mutex semaphores.

2. Analyze the readers-writers problem and differentiate between reader-priority, writer-priority, and fair solutions, understanding the trade-offs involved.

3. Describe the dining philosophers problem and identify deadlock conditions, evaluating solutions that prevent deadlock through resource hierarchy or limited concurrency.

4. Solve the sleeping barber problem using semaphore-based synchronization, understanding the interaction between customer and barber processes.

5. Identify race conditions in concurrent programs and explain how synchronization primitives prevent such conditions.

6. Understand the concepts of critical sections, mutual exclusion, progress, and bounded waiting in the context of process synchronization.

7. Compare and contrast different synchronization mechanisms including semaphores, monitors, and condition variables for solving classical problems.

8. Evaluate the efficiency and fairness characteristics of different solutions to the classical synchronization problems.
