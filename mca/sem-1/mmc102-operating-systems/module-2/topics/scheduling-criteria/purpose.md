# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and explain each of the major scheduling criteria including CPU utilization, throughput, turnaround time, waiting time, and response time.

2. Calculate CPU utilization, throughput, turnaround time, waiting time, and response time for given process schedules using appropriate formulas.

3. Analyze how different scheduling algorithms (FCFS, SJF, Round-Robin, Priority) perform against various scheduling criteria.

4. Compare and contrast scheduling criteria in the context of different system types including batch, interactive, and real-time systems.

5. Evaluate trade-offs between competing scheduling objectives and explain why optimizing one criterion may degrade others.

6. Apply scheduling criteria knowledge to select appropriate algorithms for specific operating scenarios and workload characteristics.

7. Identify potential problems in scheduling such as starvation, priority inversion, and convoy effects that arise from particular criterion optimizations.
