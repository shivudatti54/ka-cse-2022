# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. DEFINE process scheduling and explain its role in managing CPU resources among competing processes

2. DISTINGUISH between long-term, short-term, and medium-term schedulers and describe their specific functions in the scheduling hierarchy

3. ANALYZE the various scheduling criteria including CPU utilization, throughput, turnaround time, waiting time, and response time, and explain their significance in system performance evaluation

4. COMPARE fundamental scheduling algorithms including FCFS, SJF, Priority, and Round Robin based on their performance characteristics and suitable applications

5. APPLY scheduling algorithms to solve numerical problems involving waiting time, turnaround time, and average performance metrics

6. EXPLAIN the convoy effect in FCFS and analyze how it impacts system efficiency, particularly with mixed CPU-bound and I/O-bound processes

7. EVALUATE the trade-offs between different scheduling algorithms and determine appropriate algorithm selection for specific system requirements and workload characteristics
