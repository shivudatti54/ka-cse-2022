# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental differences between thread scheduling and process scheduling in operating systems

2. Distinguish between user-level threads and kernel-level threads, including their respective scheduling mechanisms and trade-offs

3. Analyze and apply the five key scheduling criteria: CPU utilization, throughput, turnaround time, waiting time, and response time

4. Compare and contrast various thread scheduling algorithms including FCFS, SJF, Priority Scheduling, Round Robin, and Multilevel Feedback Queue

5. Solve numerical problems involving waiting time and turnaround time calculations for different scheduling algorithms

6. Explain the challenges and solutions for thread scheduling in multi-processor and multi-core systems including processor affinity, load balancing, and NUMA awareness

7. Describe real-time scheduling concepts including Rate Monotonic Scheduling and Earliest Deadline First scheduling

8. Evaluate the suitability of different scheduling algorithms for specific application scenarios (interactive systems, batch processing, real-time systems)
