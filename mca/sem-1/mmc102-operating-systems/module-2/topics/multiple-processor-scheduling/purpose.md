# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define multiple processor scheduling and explain its significance in modern computing environments compared to uniprocessor scheduling.

2. Classify multiprocessor architectures into SMP, AMP, and clustered multiprocessing, and describe the distinguishing characteristics of each.

3. Compare and contrast major multiprocessor scheduling approaches including load sharing, gang scheduling, and dedicated processor assignment with respect to their advantages and limitations.

4. Explain the concept of processor affinity and analyze its impact on system performance with respect to cache behavior.

5. Describe load balancing techniques and understand the trade-offs involved between maintaining processor affinity and achieving balanced processor utilization.

6. Identify the challenges introduced by multiprocessor systems including cache coherence, migration costs, and NUMA effects, and explain how schedulers address these issues.

7. Apply scheduling concepts to analyze practical scenarios and determine appropriate scheduling strategies for different workload characteristics.
