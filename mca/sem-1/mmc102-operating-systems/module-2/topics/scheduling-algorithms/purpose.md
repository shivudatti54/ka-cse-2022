# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and explain CPU scheduling, including the roles of long-term, short-term, and medium-term schedulers.

2. Identify and describe key scheduling criteria such as CPU utilization, throughput, turnaround time, waiting time, and response time.

3. Analyze and compare First-Come-First-Served (FCFS), Shortest Job First (SJF), Priority Scheduling, and Round Robin scheduling algorithms.

4. Calculate waiting times, turnaround times, and average waiting times for various scheduling algorithms using numerical examples.

5. Distinguish between preemptive and non-preemptive scheduling and explain the implications of each type.

6. Explain Multilevel Queue and Multilevel Feedback Queue scheduling algorithms and their advantages over simpler algorithms.

7. Evaluate the performance of different scheduling algorithms under various workload conditions and identify appropriate use cases.

8. Discuss practical considerations and limitations of scheduling algorithms, including the convoy effect, starvation, and the challenge of unknown CPU burst times.
