**Learning Purpose: Defining Software Quality**

1.  **Why is this topic important?**
    Defining software quality is the foundational step in building reliable, efficient, and valuable software. Without a clear, shared definition, teams cannot measure progress, manage customer expectations, or objectively assess the success of a project. It shifts quality from a vague concept to a set of measurable, actionable criteria.

2.  **What will students learn?**
    Students will learn the multi-faceted nature of software quality, moving beyond "it works" to encompass attributes like correctness, reliability, usability, and maintainability (e.g., ISO 25010 standard). They will explore how to create concrete, verifiable Quality Attribute Requirements that serve as benchmarks for the entire development lifecycle.

3.  **How does it connect to other concepts?**
    This topic is intrinsically linked to requirements engineering (defining what "good" means) and forms the basis for subsequent topics like verification & validation (testing against the definition), software metrics (measuring the definition), and project management (planning and budgeting for quality activities).

4.  **Real-world applications**
    This knowledge is directly applied when creating a "Definition of Done," establishing acceptance criteria with stakeholders, selecting appropriate testing strategies, and justifying technical debt decisions. It is the critical first step in building a culture of quality in any software development team.
