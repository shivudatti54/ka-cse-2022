### Learning Purpose: Pressman's Software Engineering Principles

**1. Importance:**  
This topic is crucial as it introduces the foundational frameworks, processes, and best practices defined by Roger Pressman, a seminal author in software engineering. Understanding these principles is vital for implementing disciplined, predictable, and high-quality software development, which is the industry standard for successful projects.

**2. Learning Outcomes:**  
Students will learn the key phases of Pressman's software development life cycle (SDLC), including communication, planning, modeling, construction, and deployment. They will understand essential process models like waterfall, incremental, and agile, and grasp core concepts such as software requirements, design principles, testing strategies, and project management techniques.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts:**  
This module directly builds upon earlier knowledge of programming and systems analysis. It provides the managerial and procedural framework that connects coding (Module 3) and quality assurance (Module 4), showing how technical work is organized within a structured project lifecycle. It also sets the stage for studying advanced topics like Agile and DevOps.

**4. Real-World Applications:**  
These principles are applied globally to manage complex software projects in industries from finance to healthcare. Students will be equipped to create project plans, estimate costs, manage teams, and ensure quality control—skills directly applicable to their roles as future developers, project managers, and quality engineers.
