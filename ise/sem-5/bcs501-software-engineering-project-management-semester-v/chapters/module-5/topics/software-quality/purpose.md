### Learning Purpose: Software Quality

**1. Importance:**  
This topic is critical because software quality directly determines a system's reliability, security, and user satisfaction. In an industry where failures can lead to significant financial loss, safety risks, or reputational damage, understanding how to build and maintain high-quality software is a fundamental professional responsibility. It moves the focus from merely "getting code to work" to creating robust, maintainable, and trustworthy products.

**2. Learning Outcomes:**  
Students will learn the core principles, standards (like ISO 25010), and models of software quality. They will be equipped with techniques for quality assurance (QA), including code reviews, static analysis, and the distinct roles of Quality Assurance (process-focused) and Quality Control (product-focused). The module will also cover testing strategies, metrics for measuring quality, and the cost implications of quality throughout the software lifecycle.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts:**  
Software quality is the culmination of all prior software engineering concepts. It deeply connects to requirements engineering (building the right product), design principles (creating a maintainable architecture), and coding standards (writing clean code). It is also intrinsically linked to project management, as quality goals must be planned, tracked, and managed within scope, budget, and schedule constraints.

**4. Real-World Applications:**  
These concepts are applied everywhere from developing safety-critical systems in aerospace and medical devices to ensuring the scalability and security of consumer web applications. Students will see how quality processes are implemented in Agile/DevOps environments through continuous integration and automated testing pipelines, ensuring quality is "built-in" rather than "tested-in" at the end.
