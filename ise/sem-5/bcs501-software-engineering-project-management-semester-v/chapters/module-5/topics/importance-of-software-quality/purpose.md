### Learning Purpose: Importance of Software Quality

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding software quality is fundamental because it directly impacts user satisfaction, security, operational costs, and brand reputation. Neglecting quality can lead to catastrophic system failures, financial losses, and damage to an organization's credibility. This topic establishes why quality is a core business objective, not just a technical afterthought.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define software quality attributes like reliability, usability, performance, and maintainability. They will explore the tangible consequences of poor quality and the cost-effectiveness of early defect detection. The module introduces core activities for achieving quality, including quality assurance (QA) processes, testing methodologies, and the role of standards.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This concept is the "why" behind many other topics. It connects directly to requirements engineering (clear, testable requirements), software design (for maintainability), testing techniques (the "how" of verification), and project management (budgeting for QA, risk management). It justifies the processes learned throughout the curriculum.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied in every industry, from developing safety-critical systems in aviation and healthcare to ensuring e-commerce platforms are reliable and secure. Students will see its application in creating QA plans, defining acceptance criteria, performing root cause analysis on defects, and measuring customer satisfaction to drive continuous improvement.
