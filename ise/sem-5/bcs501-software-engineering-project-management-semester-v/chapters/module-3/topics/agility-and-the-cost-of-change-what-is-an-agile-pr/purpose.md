### Learning Purpose: Agility and the Cost of Change

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding agility and the cost of change is fundamental because traditional software development often fails in dynamic environments. This topic is crucial for appreciating why modern projects demand flexible, iterative approaches to manage uncertainty, reduce risks, and deliver value to customers faster.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core principles of an agile process, contrasting it with traditional plan-driven models (like Waterfall). They will analyze how the cost of change escalates over time in a traditional project versus how agile methods aim to flatten this curve through iterative development, continuous feedback, and adaptive planning.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This concept is the foundation for all subsequent agile practices. It directly connects to prior topics on traditional project management (like the Waterfall model) and sets the stage for learning specific agile frameworks (Scrum, XP, Kanban), user stories, sprints, and continuous integration covered later in the module.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is directly applicable in the industry for managing software projects of any scale. Students will be equipped to advocate for and participate in agile teams, helping organizations respond to changing market needs, improve product quality, and increase customer satisfaction through incremental delivery.
