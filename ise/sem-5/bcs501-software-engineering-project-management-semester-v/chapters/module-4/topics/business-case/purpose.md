### Learning Purpose: The Business Case

**1. Importance:**
The business case is the foundational document that justifies the initiation of a software project. It is critical because it moves the conversation from technical possibility to strategic value, ensuring that projects align with organizational goals and deliver a tangible return on investment. Understanding how to create and evaluate a business case prevents wasted resources on projects that lack a clear purpose or financial viability.

**2. Learning Outcomes:**
Students will learn to define the core components of a robust business case, including problem statement, analysis of alternatives, cost-benefit analysis (CBA), and success criteria. They will develop the skills to quantitatively and qualitatively argue for a project's value, assessing its feasibility from both a business and technical perspective.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts:**
This topic is the crucial first step in the project lifecycle, directly feeding into project selection, chartering, and requirements gathering (covered in Module 2). It integrates with financial concepts from project cost management and relies on the stakeholder analysis and risk identification techniques taught in earlier modules.

**4. Real-World Application:**
In industry, a business case is a mandatory deliverable to secure project funding and executive sponsorship. Graduates will use this skill to pitch new product ideas, propose system upgrades, or justify internal tool development, making them valuable contributors to strategic decision-making.
