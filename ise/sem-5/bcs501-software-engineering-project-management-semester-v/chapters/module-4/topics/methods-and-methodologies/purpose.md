### Learning Purpose: Methods and Methodologies

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding methods and methodologies is crucial because they provide the structured frameworks and processes necessary to manage software development complexity. Selecting an inappropriate methodology can lead to project failure, budget overruns, and missed deadlines. This knowledge equips future engineers to choose and adapt the right approach to a project's specific context, team, and goals.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to distinguish between various methodologies, from traditional, plan-driven models like Waterfall to adaptive, agile frameworks like Scrum and Kanban. They will understand the principles, practices, advantages, and limitations of each. The module also covers how to tailor a methodology and integrate supportive methods such as version control, CI/CD, and testing strategies into a cohesive development process.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the practical application of prior learning in requirements engineering, software design, and coding. It directly connects to project management concepts like scheduling, risk management, and team leadership. The chosen methodology dictates how these other concepts are implemented and coordinated throughout the project lifecycle.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied daily in the tech industry to structure teamwork, manage product backlogs, conduct sprints, and ensure continuous delivery. Whether developing a mobile app in a startup using Agile or a safety-critical system in aerospace using a V-Model, methodologies are the engine of professional software development.
