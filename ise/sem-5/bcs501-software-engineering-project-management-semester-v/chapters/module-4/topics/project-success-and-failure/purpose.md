# Learning Purpose: Project Success and Failure

### 1. Why is this topic important?

Understanding what defines project success and failure is fundamental in software engineering and project management. Many high-profile projects exceed budgets, miss deadlines, or fail to meet user needs, leading to significant financial losses and reputational damage. This topic is crucial because it equips future engineers and managers with the foresight to avoid common pitfalls and implement strategies that dramatically increase the likelihood of delivering a successful project.

### 2. What will students learn?

Students will learn to define project success beyond the traditional "Iron Triangle" of scope, time, and cost, incorporating factors like customer satisfaction and strategic business value. They will analyze common causes of failure, such as poor requirements gathering, scope creep, and inadequate risk management. Furthermore, they will study frameworks and best practices for establishing clear success criteria, effective communication, and robust change control processes.

### 3. How does it connect to other concepts?

This topic is the culmination of prior modules on project planning, scheduling, risk management, and software development methodologies (like Agile and Waterfall). It directly applies these concepts by evaluating how effective planning, risk mitigation, and choosing the right methodology contribute to ultimate success or lead to failure, providing a holistic view of the project lifecycle.

### 4. Real-world applications

This knowledge is directly applicable to roles such as Project Manager, Product Owner, and Business Analyst. Students will be able to critically evaluate project charters, define measurable Key Performance Indicators (KPIs), conduct post-mortem analyses to learn from failed projects, and champion practices that align development work with overarching business goals in any tech organization.
