### Learning Purpose: Validating Requirements & Requirements Modeling Scenarios

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Validating requirements ensures that the documented needs of stakeholders are accurate, complete, and feasible before significant resources are invested in development. It is a critical quality gate to prevent project failures, costly rework, and scope creep. Modeling these requirements with scenarios (e.g., use cases, user stories) provides a clear, unambiguous blueprint for both developers and clients, bridging the communication gap between technical and non-technical stakeholders.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn techniques to validate requirements through reviews, prototypes, and feasibility analysis. They will also learn to create and apply scenario-based models, such as use case diagrams and narratives, to visually represent system functionality from a user's perspective. This equips them to translate abstract user needs into concrete, testable specifications.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the crucial link between initial requirements elicitation (Module 1) and subsequent system design (Modules 3/4). Validated and well-modeled requirements directly inform architectural decisions, detailed design, testing strategies, and serve as the foundation for the entire project management lifecycle, including scope and change control.

**4. Real-world applications**
These skills are applied daily by business analysts and project managers to create contractually sound specification documents, secure stakeholder sign-off, and guide agile sprint planning. For example, modeling a "Place Order" scenario for an e-commerce app clarifies functionality for developers and allows the client to visualize the final product, ensuring alignment and reducing the risk of misunderstandings.
