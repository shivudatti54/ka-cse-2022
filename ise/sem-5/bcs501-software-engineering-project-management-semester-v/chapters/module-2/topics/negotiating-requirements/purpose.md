### Learning Purpose: Negotiating Requirements

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Negotiating requirements is a critical skill because software projects often involve conflicting stakeholder needs, limited resources, and changing priorities. Failure to manage these negotiations effectively can lead to scope creep, budget overruns, project failure, and damaged client relationships. This topic equips future engineers with the soft and hard skills needed to align project goals with practical constraints.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn techniques for identifying key stakeholders, eliciting their true needs, and prioritizing requirements. They will explore negotiation strategies, conflict resolution, and the art of creating win-win agreements. This includes understanding how to use tools like MoSCoW prioritization and build negotiation into agile and traditional development cycles.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon prior knowledge of **requirement elicitation** (Module 1) and is fundamental to subsequent modules on **project planning, scheduling, and risk management**. Effective negotiation ensures the requirements baseline feeding into these processes is realistic and agreed upon, making it a linchpin between analysis and execution.

**4. Real-world applications**
These skills are applied daily by business analysts, product owners, and project managers. They are used to refine product backlogs in Agile sprints, settle feature debates with clients, manage change requests, and secure necessary resources from upper management, ensuring project viability and success.
