### Learning Purpose: Module 5 - Group Projects

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Collaboration is a fundamental skill in the IT industry. Networks are inherently collaborative systems, and designing, configuring, or troubleshooting them is almost always a team effort. This topic is crucial as it mirrors real-world work environments, preparing students for the interpersonal and project management challenges they will face in their careers.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to apply theoretical network concepts to a practical, multi-faceted project. This includes dividing complex tasks (like designing a network for a small business), collaborating on configuration, conducting tests, and presenting results. They will develop crucial soft skills in communication, task delegation, and collective problem-solving.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module synthesizes knowledge from all previous modules. Students will directly use concepts from network design (topologies, IP addressing), protocols (TCP/IP, routing), switching and routing configurations, and network security. It forces an integrated understanding rather than seeing topics as isolated units.

**4. Real-world applications**
This group work directly simulates the workflow of network engineers, system administrators, and security analysts. Whether deploying a corporate LAN, securing a network perimeter, or optimizing performance for a data center, these tasks are carried out by teams. This experience is invaluable for any role in IT infrastructure.
