### Learning Purpose: Guided Media

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Guided media form the physical backbone of nearly all wired networks. Understanding their properties, limitations, and appropriate use cases is fundamental for any network professional. It is the essential first step in designing, implementing, and troubleshooting the physical layer of a network infrastructure.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify and compare the primary types of guided transmission media: twisted-pair cable, coaxial cable, and fibre-optic cable. They will analyze key characteristics such as bandwidth, attenuation, susceptibility to interference, and cost. This knowledge will enable them to evaluate and select the most suitable medium for a given network scenario based on technical requirements.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly links to the OSI and TCP/IP models, as guided media operate at the physical layer (Layer 1). The choice of media influences data link layer protocols (Layer 2) and higher-level concepts like network performance, latency, and security. It provides the tangible foundation for understanding abstract networking concepts like signalling and data encoding.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied in designing Local Area Networks (LANs) using twisted-pair Ethernet cables, deploying high-speed internet via fibre-to-the-home (FTTH) connections, and setting up cable television systems using coaxial infrastructure. It is crucial for careers in network administration, cabling installation, and telecommunications engineering.
