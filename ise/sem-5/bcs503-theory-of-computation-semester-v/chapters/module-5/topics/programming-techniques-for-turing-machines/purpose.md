# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. **Understand the basic structure of a Turing machine**: Know how the machine works, including the tape, states, and transition rules.
2. **Design simple Turing machines**: Be able to design Turing machines for simple problems, such as accepting regular languages.
3. **Understand the extensions to the basic Turing machine model**: Know the advantages and disadvantages of each extension, including multi-tape Turing machines, non-deterministic Turing machines, and universal Turing machines.
4. **Compare and contrast different Turing machine models**: Be able to compare and contrast the different models, including their computational power and limitations.
5. **Apply programming techniques to Turing machines**: Be able to apply programming techniques, such as sequential execution, conditional statements, loops, and subroutines, to Turing machines.
6. **Analyze the computational power of Turing machines**: Be able to analyze the computational power of Turing machines, including their ability to solve problems and recognize languages.
