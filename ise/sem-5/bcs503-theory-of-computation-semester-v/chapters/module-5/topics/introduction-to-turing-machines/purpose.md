# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define the basic components of a Turing machine, including the tape, head, state, and transition function.
2. Explain the operations of a Turing machine, including reading, writing, moving, and halting.
3. Construct a simple Turing machine for a given language, such as accepting all strings of 1s.
4. Understand the difference between deterministic and non-deterministic Turing machines.
5. Explain the purpose of the halting problem in the context of Turing machines.
6. Describe the relationship between Turing machines and the Church-Turing thesis.
