### Learning Purpose: Semester-End Examination

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This examination is a critical assessment tool designed to evaluate a student's comprehensive understanding of the core principles, architectures, and challenges of distributed systems. Successfully navigating this exam demonstrates the ability to synthesize and apply complex concepts, proving readiness to design, analyze, and troubleshoot real-world distributed applications.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will consolidate their knowledge by reviewing and integrating key concepts from the entire module. This includes demonstrating proficiency in communication protocols (RPC, message passing), synchronization (logical & physical clocks, mutual exclusion), consistency models, replication, fault tolerance, and distributed file systems. The exam assesses both theoretical knowledge and problem-solving skills.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
The exam inherently connects all previous topics. For instance, solving a problem on consensus algorithms requires understanding both communication (how nodes talk) and fault tolerance (handling node failures). It forces students to see the interdependencies between isolation (transactions), performance (caching), and reliability (replication) that are fundamental to any distributed architecture.

**4. Real-world applications**
The skills tested are directly applicable to building and managing the scalable, reliable systems that power modern technology. This includes cloud computing platforms (AWS, Azure), large-scale data storage (Google File System, Hadoop), content delivery networks (CDN), blockchain networks, and any microservices-based application, where coordination among independent components is paramount.
