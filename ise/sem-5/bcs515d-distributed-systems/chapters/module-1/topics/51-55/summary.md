# **5.1-5.5: Characterization of Distributed Systems**

### 5.1: Introduction to Distributed Systems

- A distributed system is a collection of independent computers that appear to be a single, coherent system.
- Characteristics:
  - Decentralization
  - Autonomy
  - Distribution

### 5.2: Types of Distributed Systems

- **Homogeneous Distributed System**: All nodes have the same capabilities and resources.
- **Heterogeneous Distributed System**: Nodes have different capabilities and resources.
- **Homogeneous Heterogeneous Distributed System**: Combination of both.

### 5.3: Components of a Distributed System

- **Process**: A program in execution.
- **Computer**: A physical machine or processor.
- **Network**: A communication infrastructure.

### 5.4: Communication in Distributed Systems

- **Communication**: The exchange of information between nodes.
- **Message Passing**: One node sends a message to another node.
- **Synchronization**: Coordination of actions among nodes.

### 5.5: Evaluation of Distributed Systems

- **Availability**: The likelihood that a service is accessible when needed.
- **Performance**: The efficiency of a system in completing tasks.
- **Scalability**: The ability of a system to handle increasing workloads.

### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Load Balancing**: The process of distributing workload across multiple nodes.
- **Partitions**: A division of a set of nodes into disjoint subsets.

### Important Theorems

- **Aloha Theorem**: The probability of collisions in a multiple access protocol.
- **Thompson's Theorem**: A bound on the probability of failure in a distributed algorithm.
