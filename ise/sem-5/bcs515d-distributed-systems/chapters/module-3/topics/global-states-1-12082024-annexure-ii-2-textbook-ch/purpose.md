Learning about global states in distributed systems is crucial as it enables the synchronization of multiple processes running on different nodes, ensuring that all processes are aware of the current state of the system. This knowledge matters as it provides a foundation for designing and implementing fault-tolerant and scalable distributed systems. In real-world applications, understanding global states is essential for building systems like distributed databases, cloud computing platforms, and social media networks. It also connects to other concepts, such as concurrency control, consistency models, and synchronization primitives.
