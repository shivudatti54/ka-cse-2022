# **Project Evaluation: Chapter 1 Summary**

## **Important Definitions and Formulas**

- **Evaluation**: The process of assessing the value and performance of a project.
- **Cost-Benefit Analysis (CBA)**: A method of evaluating the value of a project by comparing its costs and benefits.
- **Cost-Benefit Ratio (CBR)**: CBR = Total Benefit - Total Cost
- **Net Present Value (NPV)**: NPV = ∑(CFt / (1 + r)^t)
- **Payback Period (PBP)**: PBP = Time taken for the project to recover its initial investment.

## **Cost-Benefit Evaluation Techniques**

- **Incremental Cost-Benefit Analysis**: Evaluates the project's benefits and costs at the incremental level (i.e., the additional benefit and cost of each unit produced).
- **Marginal Cost-Benefit Analysis**: Evaluates the project's benefits and costs at the marginal level (i.e., the additional benefit or cost of each additional unit produced).

## **Risk Evaluation Techniques**

- **Risk Matrix**: A table that plots the probability of a risk against its potential impact.
- **Sensitivity Analysis**: Analyzes how changes in project assumptions affect the outcome.
- **Expected Value**: EV = ∑(x \* P(x)), where x is the outcome and P(x) is its probability.

## **Other Key Points**

- **Evaluation Criteria**: Identify the criteria to be used for project evaluation, such as cost, schedule, and quality.
- **Evaluation Methods**: Choose the evaluation method that best suits the project, such as cost-benefit analysis or risk analysis.
- **Communication**: Communicate the evaluation results to stakeholders to ensure that they are understood and acted upon.
