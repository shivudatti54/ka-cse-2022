# **Empirical Estimation Models**

## **Definition**

Empirical estimation models are methods used to estimate the effort required for a software development project based on historical data and past experiences.

## **Key Points**

- **Pareto Law**: 20% of causes produce 80% of effects, and 20% of effects require 80% of effort.
- **Leakage**: 10-20% of effort is lost due to changes in requirements, scope, and other factors.
- **Goods and Services Method**: estimates effort based on the cost of goods and services required to deliver the project.
- **Resource-Usage Estimation**: estimates effort based on the resources required to deliver the project.
- **Covington's Law**: 15% of the project's work is done by 25% of the team.
- **Three-Point Estimation**: estimates effort by using a three-point range (optimistic, most likely, and pessimistic).

## **Important Formulas**

- **Pareto Law Formula**: E = 2B^1.3
- **Goods and Services Method Formula**: E = (TC \* f) / (s \* p)
- **Three-Point Estimation Formula**: E = (OP + MP + LP) / 3

## **Theorems**

- **The Law of Diminishing Returns**: as the size of the project increases, the rate of increase in effort decreases.
- **The Law of Increasing Returns**: as the size of the project increases, the rate of increase in effort increases.

## **Revision Tips**

- Review the Pareto Law and its application in empirical estimation.
- Practice using the Goods and Services Method and Resource-Usage Estimation.
- Understand the concept of Leakage and its impact on project effort.
- Learn to apply Three-Point Estimation and its three-point range.
