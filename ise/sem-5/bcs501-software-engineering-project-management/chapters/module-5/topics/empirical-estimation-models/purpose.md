**Importance of Empirical Estimation Models:**

1. **Practical Problem-Solving**: Empirical estimation models are crucial in software engineering and project management as they provide a data-driven approach to estimating project duration, cost, and resources, helping teams make informed decisions and mitigate risks.
2. **Real-World Applications**: These models are widely used in various industries, such as IT, finance, and construction, to estimate project timelines, allocate resources, and track progress, ensuring that projects are completed on time, within budget, and to the required quality standards.
3. **Connection to Other Concepts**: Empirical estimation models are closely related to other concepts in software engineering and project management, such as project planning, risk management, and quality assurance, as they provide a foundation for creating realistic project plans and identifying potential risks and opportunities for improvement.

**Why this topic matters**: Learning empirical estimation models is essential for software engineers and project managers to develop practical skills in estimating project duration and cost, which is critical for delivering successful projects on time and within budget.
