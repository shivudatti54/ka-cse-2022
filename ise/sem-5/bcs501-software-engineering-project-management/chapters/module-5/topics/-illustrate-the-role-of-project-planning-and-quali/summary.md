# **Project Planning and Quality Management in Software Development**

**Definition:** Project planning and quality management are crucial aspects of software development that ensure the delivery of high-quality software products on time and within budget.

**Key Points:**

- **Project Planning:**
  - Identifies project goals, scope, timeline, and resources
  - Develops project charter, business case, and feasibility study
  - Creates project schedule and budget
  - Allocates tasks and assigns team members
  - Monitors progress and tracks changes
- **Quality Management:**
  - Defines quality standards and metrics
  - Develops quality plans and procedures
  - Conducts risk assessments and defect prevention
  - Implements testing and validation techniques
  - Maintains defect tracking and reporting

**Formulas and Theorems:**

- **Project Management Institute (PMI) Triangular Model:** Scope, Time, Cost (STC)
- **Critical Path Method (CPM):** Calculates project duration and earliest/late start/finish times
- **Pert Diagram:** Visual representation of project tasks and dependencies
- **Cobb's Law:** States that the number of defects per unit of code remains constant over time

**Important Definitions:**

- **Modularity:** Breaking down a system into independent, reusable components
- **Reusability:** Creating software components that can be reused in multiple projects
- **Design Patterns:** Solutions to common software design problems

**Revision Tips:**

- Review project planning techniques, such as Gantt charts and resource allocation
- Study quality management methodologies, including Agile and Waterfall
- Practice calculating project duration and estimating effort using CPM andPERT diagrams
- Familiarize yourself with quality metrics, such as defect density and test coverage
