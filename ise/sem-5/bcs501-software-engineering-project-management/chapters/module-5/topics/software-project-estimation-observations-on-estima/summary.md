# **Software Project Estimation: Observations on Estimation**

## **Definitions and Concepts**

- **Estimation**: The process of predicting the effort, cost, or duration required to complete a software project.
- **Uncertainty**: The inherent unpredictability of the project's characteristics.
- **Pareto Principle**: 80% of the effort usually comes from 20% of the problems.

## **Estimation Techniques**

- **Parametric Estimation**: Uses historical data and formulas to predict effort, cost, or duration.
- **Singleton Estimation**: Estimates effort based on the number of lines of code.
- **Three-Point Estimation**: Provides a range of possible effort, cost, or duration.
- **Expert Judgment Estimation**: Estimates effort based on the experience and expertise of team members.

## **Important Formulas and Theorems**

- **Pareto Law**: Effort is proportional to the square of the size of the problem.
- **Hofstadter's Law**: It always takes longer than you expect, even when you take into account Hofstadter's Law.
- **The 100-Point Estimation Scale**: A simple scale used to estimate the size of the problem.

## **Observations on Estimation**

- **Estimation is a subjective process**: Effort, cost, and duration estimates are always uncertain.
- **Estimation should be based on historical data**: Use data from similar projects to inform your estimates.
- **Estimation should take into account uncertainty**: Use techniques like risk management and sensitivity analysis to account for uncertainty.
- **Estimation should be reviewed and refined**: Regularly review and refine your estimates as the project progresses.

## **Key Takeaways**

- Estimation is a complex and uncertain process.
- Historical data and expert judgment can inform estimation.
- Uncertainty should be accounted for in the estimation process.
- Estimation should be reviewed and refined regularly.
