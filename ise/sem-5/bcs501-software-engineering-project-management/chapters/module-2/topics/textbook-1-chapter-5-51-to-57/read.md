# **Textbook 1: Chapter 5: 5.1 to 5.7**

## **5.1: Introduction to Software Requirements**

### Definition

Software requirements are the specifications that describe the desired functionality of a software system.

### Importance

Software requirements are crucial in determining the feasibility, viability, and success of a software project.

### Example

A software company develops an e-commerce platform for online shopping. The software requirements include:

- User authentication and authorization
- Product catalog management
- Payment gateway integration

### Key Concepts

- Functional requirements: describe the desired functionality of the software
- Non-functional requirements: describe the constraints and limitations of the software
- User requirements: describe the needs and expectations of the users

### Study Tips

- Read the software requirements carefully to understand the desired functionality
- Identify the functional and non-functional requirements
- Analyze the user requirements to understand their needs and expectations

## **5.2: Types of Software Requirements**

### Definition

There are two types of software requirements: functional and non-functional.

### Functional Requirements

---

Functional requirements describe the desired functionality of the software.

### Non-Functional Requirements

---

Non-functional requirements describe the constraints and limitations of the software.

### Example

A software company develops a mobile app for tracking fitness goals. The functional requirements include:

- Tracking workout data
- Setting goals and targets

The non-functional requirements include:

- Security: the app should be secure and protect user data
- Performance: the app should be fast and responsive

### Key Concepts

- Functional requirements: describe the desired functionality of the software
- Non-functional requirements: describe the constraints and limitations of the software
- User requirements: describe the needs and expectations of the users

### Study Tips

- Read the functional and non-functional requirements carefully to understand the desired functionality
- Identify the requirements that are essential for the software
- Prioritize the requirements based on their importance and feasibility

## **5.3: Requirements Elicitation**

### Definition

Requirements elicitation is the process of gathering and capturing the requirements of a software system.

### Methods

---

There are several methods of requirements elicitation, including:

- Interviews: one-on-one discussions with stakeholders
- Surveys: questionnaires to gather information from a large group of people
- Focus groups: group discussions to gather information from a small group of people
- Observations: observing users and stakeholders to gather information

### Example

A software company develops a software system for managing inventory. The requirements elicitation process involves:

- Conducting interviews with inventory managers
- Conducting surveys to gather information from a large group of people
- Conducting focus groups to gather information from a small group of people

### Key Concepts

- Requirements elicitation: the process of gathering and capturing the requirements of a software system
- Methods: techniques used to gather requirements
- Stakeholders: people who are impacted by the software system

### Study Tips

- Use a combination of methods to gather requirements
- Gather requirements from multiple stakeholders
- Document the requirements carefully

## **5.4: Requirements Analysis**

### Definition

Requirements analysis is the process of analyzing and reviewing the requirements of a software system.

### Techniques

---

There are several techniques used in requirements analysis, including:

- Data flow diagrams: visual representations of the data flow through the system
- Use cases: descriptions of the interactions between the users and the system
- State transition diagrams: visual representations of the states of the system
- Decision tables: tables used to evaluate the options and choose the best solution

### Example

A software company develops a software system for managing orders. The requirements analysis process involves:

- Creating a data flow diagram to visualize the data flow
- Creating use cases to describe the interactions between the users and the system
- Creating a state transition diagram to visualize the states of the system

### Key Concepts

- Requirements analysis: the process of analyzing and reviewing the requirements of a software system
- Techniques: methods used to analyze and review requirements
- Data flow diagrams: visual representations of the data flow through the system

### Study Tips

- Use a combination of techniques to analyze and review requirements
- Use visual representations to help understand the requirements
- Document the analysis carefully

## **5.5: Requirements Engineering**

### Definition

Requirements engineering is the process of applying techniques and tools to analyze, design, and document the requirements of a software system.

### Techniques

---

There are several techniques used in requirements engineering, including:

- Requirements modeling: creating models of the requirements
- Requirements validation: verifying that the requirements meet the requirements of stakeholders
- Requirements traceability: tracing the requirements back to their source

### Example

A software company develops a software system for managing projects. The requirements engineering process involves:

- Creating a requirements model to visualize the requirements
- Validating the requirements to ensure they meet the requirements of stakeholders
- Tracing the requirements back to their source to ensure completeness and accuracy

### Key Concepts

- Requirements engineering: the process of applying techniques and tools to analyze, design, and document the requirements of a software system
- Techniques: methods used to analyze, design, and document requirements
- Requirements modeling: creating models of the requirements

### Study Tips

- Use a combination of techniques to analyze, design, and document requirements
- Use visual representations to help understand the requirements
- Document the requirements carefully

## **5.6: Requirements Management**

### Definition

Requirements management is the process of managing and controlling the requirements of a software system throughout its life cycle.

### Techniques

---

There are several techniques used in requirements management, including:

- Requirements management plans: documents that outline the requirements management process
- Requirements management tools: tools used to manage and control requirements
- Requirements management methodologies: approaches used to manage and control requirements

### Example

A software company develops a software system for managing inventory. The requirements management process involves:

- Creating a requirements management plan to outline the requirements management process
- Using requirements management tools to manage and control requirements
- Applying a requirements management methodology to manage and control requirements

### Key Concepts

- Requirements management: the process of managing and controlling the requirements of a software system throughout its life cycle
- Techniques: methods used to manage and control requirements
- Requirements management plans: documents that outline the requirements management process

### Study Tips

- Use a combination of techniques to manage and control requirements
- Use requirements management plans and tools to manage and control requirements
- Apply a requirements management methodology to manage and control requirements

## **5.7: Requirements Review**

### Definition

Requirements review is the process of reviewing and verifying the requirements of a software system to ensure they are complete, accurate, and consistent.

### Techniques

---

There are several techniques used in requirements review, including:

- Requirements review checklists: lists of questions used to verify requirements
- Requirements review tables: tables used to evaluate the completeness and accuracy of requirements
- Requirements review meetings: meetings used to review and verify requirements

### Example

A software company develops a software system for managing orders. The requirements review process involves:

- Creating a requirements review checklist to verify the completeness and accuracy of requirements
- Conducting a requirements review meeting to review and verify the requirements
- Using a requirements review table to evaluate the completeness and accuracy of requirements

### Key Concepts

- Requirements review: the process of reviewing and verifying the requirements of a software system to ensure they are complete, accurate, and consistent
- Techniques: methods used to review and verify requirements
- Requirements review checklists: lists of questions used to verify requirements

### Study Tips

- Use a combination of techniques to review and verify requirements
- Use requirements review checklists and tables to verify requirements
- Conduct a requirements review meeting to review and verify requirements
