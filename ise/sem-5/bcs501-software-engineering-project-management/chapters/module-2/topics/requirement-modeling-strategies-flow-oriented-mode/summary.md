# **Requirement Modeling Strategies: Flow-Oriented Modeling**

## **Definitions and Theorems**

- **Flow-Oriented Modeling**: A requirement modeling strategy that focuses on the flow of activities, processes, and interactions between actors in a system.
- **Flow Diagram**: A visual representation of the flow of activities and interactions between actors.
- **Flow Process Model**: A flow-oriented model that describes the sequence of activities and interactions between actors.

## **Key Points**

- **Components of a Flow Diagram**:
  - Actors
  - Activities
  - Interfaces
  - Data Flows
- **Types of Flow Diagrams**:
  - Sequential Flow Diagram
  - Concurrent Flow Diagram
  - Loop Flow Diagram
- **Flow Process Model Characteristics**:
  - Describes the sequence of activities and interactions between actors
  - Focuses on the flow of activities and interactions
  - Used to identify and model business processes

## **Importance and Applications**

- **Identify and Model Business Processes**: Flow-oriented modeling is used to identify and model business processes, which helps in understanding the requirements of a system.
- **Improve System Design and Development**: By understanding the flow of activities and interactions between actors, flow-oriented modeling helps in improving system design and development.
- **Support Requirements Management**: Flow-oriented modeling supports requirements management by providing a clear understanding of the requirements of a system.

## **Key Formulas and Notations**

- **Activity Diagram**: A flow diagram that represents the activities and interactions between actors.
- **State Machine Model**: A flow-oriented model that describes the state transitions of an actor.
