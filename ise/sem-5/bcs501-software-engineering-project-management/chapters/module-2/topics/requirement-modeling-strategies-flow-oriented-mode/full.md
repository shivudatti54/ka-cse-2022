# **Requirement Modeling Strategies: Flow Oriented Modeling**

## **Introduction**

Requirement modeling is a crucial activity in software engineering that involves capturing, specifying, and documenting the requirements of a software system. The goal of requirement modeling is to create a clear understanding of the system's functional and non-functional requirements, enabling effective communication among stakeholders, designers, developers, and testers. In this module, we will delve into the world of requirement modeling strategies, with a focus on flow-oriented modeling.

## **Historical Context**

The concept of requirement modeling dates back to the 1960s, when the first software development methodologies emerged. In the 1970s and 1980s, requirement modeling became a formal part of software engineering, with the introduction of structured analysis and design techniques.

In the 1990s, the use of object-oriented analysis and design (OOAAD) became widespread, leading to the development of object-oriented requirement modeling (OORM). OORM emphasized the use of objects and relationships to model the system's functional and non-functional requirements.

## **Flow Oriented Modeling**

Flow oriented modeling is a requirement modeling approach that focuses on the flow of actors, activities, and data within the system. This approach is also known as flow-based modeling or activity-based modeling.

## **Key Characteristics**

1.  **Focus on Process**: Flow oriented modeling emphasizes the process by which the system operates, rather than just its components.
2.  **Actor-Centered**: The approach focuses on the actors involved in the system, such as users, operators, and administrators.
3.  **Activity-Based**: The modeling process involves identifying the activities that act on the system's data.
4.  **Data Flow**: The approach models the flow of data between actors, activities, and other system components.

## **Benefits**

1.  **Improved Communication**: Flow oriented modeling facilitates effective communication among stakeholders by emphasizing the system's functional and non-functional requirements.
2.  **Increased Understanding**: The approach provides a clear understanding of the system's behavior and interactions, enabling designers and developers to create a more accurate model of the system.
3.  **Reduced Complexity**: By focusing on the system's process, flow oriented modeling can help to simplify complex systems and reduce the risk of errors.

## **Applications**

1.  **Business Process Reengineering**: Flow oriented modeling is commonly used in business process reengineering to identify and improve business processes.
2.  **System Design**: The approach is applied in system design to model the interactions between system components and stakeholders.
3.  **Requirements Gathering**: Flow oriented modeling is used in requirements gathering to identify and document the functional and non-functional requirements of a system.

## **Example**

Suppose we are developing a banking system that allows customers to check their account balances. Using flow oriented modeling, we can model the following activities:

- **Actor**: Customer
- **Activity**: Check balance
- **Data Flow**: Customer's account number → Bank's account information → Customer's balance

## **Case Study**

A software company is developing a new e-commerce platform for an online retailer. The company uses flow oriented modeling to model the following activities:

- **Actor**: Customer
- **Activity**: Place order
- **Data Flow**: Customer's product selection → Order summary → Payment processing → Order confirmation

The flow oriented model reveals the following benefits:

- **Improved Communication**: The model ensures that all stakeholders, including customers, sales team, and logistics team, understand the system's functional and non-functional requirements.
- **Increased Understanding**: The model provides a clear understanding of the system's behavior and interactions, enabling designers and developers to create a more accurate model of the system.
- **Reduced Complexity**: The model simplifies complex processes, reducing the risk of errors and improving overall system performance.
