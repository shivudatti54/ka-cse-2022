# **Personal and Team Process Models Revision Notes**

**Chapter 1: Personal and Team Process Models**

### 1.1 Introduction to Process Models

- Process models describe the sequence of activities involved in software engineering and project management.
- Types of process models: formal, informal, and semi-formal.
- Importance of process models: ensures consistency, efficiency, and quality.

### 1.2 Types of Process Models

- **Formal Process Models**:
  - Define every activity and its input/output parameters.
  - Use mathematical notation and formal language.
  - Examples: AADL (Architecture Analysis and Design Language), BPMN (Business Process Model and Notation).
- **Informal Process Models**:
  - Describe the process in a general way.
  - Use natural language and diagrams.
  - Examples: SWMM (Software Management Method), RUP (Rapid Upper Model).
- **Semi-formal Process Models**:
  - Use a mix of formal and informal notation.
  - Examples: UML (Unified Modeling Language), DoDAF (Department of Defense Acquisition Framework).

### 1.3 Key Process Models

- **Waterfall Model**:
  - Linear and sequential development process.
  - Phases: requirements, design, implementation, testing, deployment.
- **V-Model**:
  - Parallel development process.
  - Phases: specification, design, implementation, testing.
- **Agile Model**:
  - Iterative and incremental development process.
  - Emphasizes flexibility and customer involvement.

### 1.4 Process Modeling Techniques

- **Activity Diagrams**:
  - Represent activities and their relationships.
  - Used for informal process modeling.
- **State Machine Diagrams**:
  - Represent states and transitions.
  - Used for formal process modeling.
- **Flowcharts**:
  - Represent processes using a sequence of steps.
  - Used for informal process modeling.

### 1.5 Notation and Symbolism

- **Process Notation**:
  - Used to represent processes and activities.
  - Examples: arrows, boxes, and labels.
- **State Notation**:
  - Used to represent states and transitions.
  - Examples: rectangles, arrows, and labels.

### 1.6 Case Study: A Project Management Approach

- **Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC)**:
  - A traditional approach to software development.
  - Phases: initiation, planning, execution, monitoring and control, closure.

### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Gantt Chart**: a bar chart that shows the duration of each task.
- **Critical Path Method**: a technique for identifying the longest path through a project network.
- **Earned Value Management (EVM)**: a technique for measuring project performance.
