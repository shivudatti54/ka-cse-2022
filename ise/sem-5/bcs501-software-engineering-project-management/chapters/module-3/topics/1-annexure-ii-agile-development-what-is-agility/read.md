# **Agility Study Material**

**Module: 10 hours**
**Topic: 1 Annexure II Agile Development: What is Agility?**

## **Introduction**

Agility is a software development methodology that emphasizes flexibility, collaboration, and rapid delivery. In this module, we will explore the concept of agility, its history, key principles, and benefits.

## **What is Agility?**

Agility is a software development approach that emphasizes flexibility, adaptability, and collaboration. It was first introduced in the 2001 Agile Manifesto, which values individuals and interactions over processes and tools.

## **Key Principles of Agility**

- **Individuals and Interactions**: Agility emphasizes the importance of individuals and interactions over processes and tools.
- **Working Software**: Agility prioritizes the delivery of working software over comprehensive documentation.
- **Customer Collaboration**: Agility encourages collaboration between customers and developers to ensure that the software meets the customer's needs.
- **Responding to Change**: Agility is flexible and responsive to change, allowing it to adapt to new requirements and priorities.

## **History of Agility**

Agility has its roots in the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) of the 1960s and 1970s. However, it wasn't until the 1990s that the Agile movement began to take shape. In 2001, the Agile Manifesto was published, which outlined the core principles of agility.

## **Characteristics of Agile**

- **Iterative Development**: Agility uses iterative development, where the software is developed in short cycles (iterations) with continuous feedback from customers.
- **Incremental Delivery**: Agility delivers software in increments, with each iteration building on the previous one.
- **Flexibility**: Agility is flexible and responsive to change, allowing it to adapt to new requirements and priorities.
- **Collaboration**: Agility encourages collaboration between customers, developers, and stakeholders to ensure that the software meets the customer's needs.

## **Benefits of Agility**

- **Faster Time-to-Market**: Agility allows for faster time-to-market, as software is delivered in shorter cycles.
- **Improved Quality**: Agility emphasizes the delivery of working software, which leads to improved quality and fewer defects.
- **Increased Customer Satisfaction**: Agility encourages collaboration between customers and developers, which leads to increased customer satisfaction.
- **Reduced Risk**: Agility reduces risk by allowing for flexibility and adaptability in response to change.

## **Conclusion**

Agility is a software development methodology that emphasizes flexibility, collaboration, and rapid delivery. Its key principles, such as individuals and interactions, working software, customer collaboration, and responding to change, make it an effective approach to software development. By understanding the characteristics and benefits of agility, software developers and organizations can adopt this approach to improve the quality, speed, and satisfaction of their software delivery.

## **Key Terms**

- **Agile Manifesto**: A document that outlines the core principles of agility.
- **Iterative Development**: A software development approach that uses short cycles (iterations) with continuous feedback from customers.
- **Incremental Delivery**: A software delivery approach that delivers software in increments, with each iteration building on the previous one.
- **Flexibility**: The ability of agility to adapt to new requirements and priorities.
- **Collaboration**: The practice of working together between customers, developers, and stakeholders to ensure that the software meets the customer's needs.
