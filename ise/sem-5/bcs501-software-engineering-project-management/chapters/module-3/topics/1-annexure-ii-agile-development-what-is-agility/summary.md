# Agile Development: What is Agility?

### Key Definitions

- **Agility**: Ability to quickly adapt to change, be flexible and innovative.
- **Agile Methodology**: An iterative and incremental approach to software development that focuses on delivering value quickly and responding to change.
- **Agile Project Management**: A dynamic and adaptive approach to managing projects that involves frequent collaboration, continuous improvement, and rapid delivery.

### Key Principles

- **Individuals and Interactions**: People and communication are more important than processes and tools.
- **Working Software**: Working software is more important than comprehensive documentation.
- **Customer Collaboration**: Collaboration with customers and stakeholders is essential for delivering value.
- **Responding to Change**: Agility involves responding quickly to change and adapting to new requirements.

### Agile Concepts

- **Sprints**: Short iterations (usually 2-4 weeks) that involve a set of tasks and deliverables.
- **Backlog**: A prioritized list of features or tasks that need to be developed.
- **Product Owner**: A person responsible for prioritizing and maintaining the backlog.
- **Scrum Team**: A team that works together to deliver a sprint's worth of work.

### The Agile Manifesto

- **Individuals and Interactions**: We value people and communication over processes and tools.
- **Working Software**: We value working software over comprehensive documentation.
- **Customer Collaboration**: We value collaboration with customers and stakeholders over contract negotiation.
- **Responding to Change**: We value responding to change over following a plan.

### Important Formulas/Definitions

- **Agile Velocity**: The amount of work completed in a sprint.
- **Cohesion**: The degree to which team members work together effectively.
- **Collaboration**: The degree to which team members and stakeholders work together effectively.

### Important Theorems

- **The Agile Triangle**: A triangle that represents the relationship between speed, quality, and cost in agile projects.
- **The Scrum Framework**: A framework for managing agile projects that involves the Scrum Team, the Product Owner, and the Customer.

### Summary

Agility is the ability to quickly adapt to change and be flexible and innovative. Agile methodology involves an iterative and incremental approach to software development that focuses on delivering value quickly and responding to change. Agile project management involves a dynamic and adaptive approach to managing projects that involves frequent collaboration, continuous improvement, and rapid delivery.
