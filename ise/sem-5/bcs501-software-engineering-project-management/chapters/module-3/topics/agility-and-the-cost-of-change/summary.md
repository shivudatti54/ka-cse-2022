# Agility and the Cost of Change

### Overview

- Agility: an iterative and adaptive approach to software development that emphasizes flexibility and rapid delivery
- Cost of Change: the additional costs incurred when changing existing requirements or scope

### Key Concepts

- **Agile Methodologies**:
  - Definition: iterative and incremental development approach
  - Examples: Scrum, Kanban, Lean
- **Cost of Change**:
  - Definition: additional costs incurred when changing existing requirements or scope
  - Formula: C(ΔQ) = (Q0 \* ΔP) / (1 - ΔP)
    - C(ΔQ): cost of change
    - Q0: original quality
    - ΔP: percentage change in project scope
- **The Law of Diminishing Returns**:
  - Definition: the additional cost of change decreases as the project scope changes
  - Theorem: C(ΔQ) = Q0 \* ln(1 + ΔP)
- **The Economics of Change Management**:
  - Definition: the study of the costs and benefits of change management
  - Key principles:
    - Minimize disruption to existing processes
    - Gradually introduce changes to minimize costs

### Important Formulas

- C(ΔQ) = (Q0 \* ΔP) / (1 - ΔP)
- C(ΔQ) = Q0 \* ln(1 + ΔP)
- C(M) = Q0 \* (1 - (1 + ΔP)^(-n))

### Definitions

- **Scope**: the set of requirements for a project
- **Quality**: the degree to which a project meets its requirements
