# **Chapter 4: 4.1 to 4.3 Revision Notes**

### 4.1: Introduction to Project Management

- Project management is the application of knowledge, skills, and techniques to execute a project successfully.
- Project management involves planning, organizing, leading, and controlling a project.
- Project management is a discipline that uses scientific and engineering principles to manage projects.

### 4.2: Project Life Cycle

- The project life cycle consists of five phases:
  - Initiation
  - Planning
  - Execution
  - Monitoring and Control
  - Closure
- Each phase has specific tasks and deliverables.

### 4.3: Project Management Frameworks

- **Agile Framework**: emphasizes flexibility, collaboration, and iterative development.
- **Waterfall Framework**: emphasizes sequential development, with each phase completed before moving on to the next one.
- **Hybrid Framework**: combines elements of Agile and Waterfall frameworks.

### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Project Management Matrix**: a tool used to evaluate and prioritize project tasks.
- **Earned Value Management (EVM)**: a methodology used to measure project performance and progress.
- **Critical Path Method (CPM)**: a technique used to determine the minimum duration required to complete a project.

### Important Theorems and Concepts

- **The Law of Diminishing Returns**: states that the marginal benefit of a project decreases as its scope increases.
- **The Project Management Triangle**: a model that illustrates the relationship between scope, time, and cost in project management.

### Key Terms

- **Project scope**: the set of activities and deliverables that are included in a project.
- **Project schedule**: the plan for the sequence and timing of project activities.
- **Project budget**: the estimated cost of a project.
