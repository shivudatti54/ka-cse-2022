### Learning Purpose: Water Resources – Availability & Quality Aspects

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Water is a fundamental, life-sustaining resource facing immense pressure from pollution, over-exploitation, and climate change. Understanding its availability and quality is critical for human health, ecosystem stability, and sustainable development, forming the foundation for effective environmental management.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will analyze the global and local distribution of freshwater resources and the factors affecting their availability. They will learn to identify major pollutants, their sources (including from e-waste leaching), and evaluate key water quality parameters. This includes studying the implications for public health and the environment.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly connects to the overarching subject of e-waste management, as improper disposal can lead to heavy metals and toxins leaching into and contaminating water supplies. It also integrates concepts from climate science (droughts/floods), public health (water-borne diseases), and policy (water conservation regulations).

**4. Real-world applications**
Students will be equipped to assess water sustainability in communities, contribute to water conservation initiatives, and understand the critical link between waste management policies (like e-waste recycling) and the protection of vital water resources from industrial and chemical pollution.
