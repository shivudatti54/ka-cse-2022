# Learning Purpose: Water Borne & Water Induced Diseases

**1. Importance:**
This topic is critically important as unsafe water and poor sanitation are among the world's leading causes of disease and mortality, particularly in vulnerable communities. Understanding these diseases is fundamental to public health, environmental justice, and sustainable development, directly linking environmental quality to human wellbeing.

**2. Student Learning Outcomes:**
Students will learn to differentiate between water-borne (caused by pathogenic microorganisms, e.g., cholera, typhoid) and water-induced (caused by chemical contaminants or water-related vectors, e.g., fluorosis, malaria) diseases. They will analyze the causative agents, transmission pathways, symptoms, and preventive measures, connecting them to broader issues of water pollution and waste management.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts:**
This topic directly connects to prior knowledge of water pollution sources (from Module 1), where improper e-waste disposal can leach heavy metals and chemicals into water supplies, causing water-induced diseases. It also provides a crucial link to subsequent modules on waste management solutions, highlighting how effective e-waste and general waste handling can prevent water contamination and protect public health.

**4. Real-World Applications:**
This knowledge is essential for careers in public health policy, environmental consulting, and community advocacy. Students will be equipped to design and evaluate public awareness campaigns, contribute to water safety and sanitation projects, and understand the health impacts of environmental degradation, making them informed citizens and effective practitioners.
