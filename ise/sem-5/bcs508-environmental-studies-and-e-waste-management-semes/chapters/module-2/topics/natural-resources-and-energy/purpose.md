# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1.  Differentiate between renewable and non-renewable energy resources with clear examples.
2.  Explain the extraction, conversion, and use of major fossil fuels (coal, oil, natural gas) and their primary environmental impacts.
3.  Describe the process of generating nuclear energy and evaluate its major advantages and challenges.
4.  Identify and explain the working principles of at least five different renewable energy alternatives (solar, wind, hydro, biomass, geothermal).
5.  Compare and contrast different energy sources based on criteria such as availability, environmental impact, cost, and reliability.
6.  Articulate the concept of 'energy transition' and its importance for climate change mitigation and sustainable development.
