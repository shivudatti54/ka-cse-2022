# Learning Purpose: Module 5 - E-Waste Management (R. Rajagopalan)

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is critically important because electronic waste (e-waste) is the world's fastest-growing waste stream, posing severe environmental and health risks due to toxic materials like lead and mercury. Understanding its management is essential for developing sustainable solutions and fostering responsible consumer behavior in our technology-driven society.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the fundamental concepts of e-waste, including its sources, composition, and the associated environmental hazards. They will explore formal and informal recycling processes, the legal and policy frameworks governing e-waste management in India, and the socio-economic aspects of the recycling chain, as outlined by author R. Rajagopalan.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly connects to earlier studies on waste management, pollution control, and sustainable development. It applies principles of life cycle assessment (from product design to disposal), environmental law, and social responsibility, providing a holistic view of how consumption patterns impact ecological systems and human health.

**4. Real-world applications**
Knowledge from this module can be applied to careers in corporate sustainability, waste management policy, and environmental consulting. Students will be equipped to advocate for and implement better recycling programs, make informed choices as consumers, and understand the role of extended producer responsibility (EPR) in creating a circular economy.
