### Learning Purpose: Environmental Studies and E-Waste Management (Elite Publishing House)

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial as it examines a specific, real-world case of corporate e-waste mismanagement. It highlights the severe environmental and social consequences of improper electronic disposal, moving beyond abstract concepts to a tangible example. Understanding such a case is vital for fostering corporate accountability and informed citizenship.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will analyze the "Elite Publishing House" case study to identify the root causes, key stakeholders, and multifaceted impacts (soil/water pollution, health hazards) of the e-waste incident. They will learn to evaluate the ethical and legal breaches involved and propose sustainable e-waste management strategies for corporations.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This case directly connects to earlier modules on the life cycle of electronic products, waste management policies (e.g., E-Waste Management Rules), and the principles of Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR). It synthesizes concepts of environmental law, corporate social responsibility, and circular economy models.

**4. Real-world applications**
The insights gained are directly applicable to roles in corporate sustainability, environmental auditing, policy advocacy, and waste management consultancy. Students will be equipped to develop compliance strategies, conduct impact assessments, and advocate for responsible e-waste handling practices in any organizational context.
