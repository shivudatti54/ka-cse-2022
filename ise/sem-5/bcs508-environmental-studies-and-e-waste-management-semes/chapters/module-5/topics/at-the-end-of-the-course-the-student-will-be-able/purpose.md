### Learning Purpose: E-Waste Management

**1. Why is this topic important?**
The rapid growth of technology creates a massive stream of electronic waste (e-waste), one of the world's fastest-growing waste categories. Improper disposal releases hazardous materials like lead and mercury, posing severe risks to human health and the environment. Understanding its management is critical for building a sustainable future.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define e-waste, identify its sources and composition, and analyze the environmental and health impacts of its improper disposal. They will evaluate various management strategies, including the 3Rs (Reduce, Reuse, Recycle), extended producer responsibility (EPR), and formal vs. informal recycling sectors.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic integrates core environmental studies concepts like pollution, resource depletion, life-cycle analysis, and sustainable development. It connects to social sciences by examining the socioeconomic dimensions of the informal recycling sector and to policy studies through an analysis of national and international e-waste regulations.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge empowers students to make responsible personal choices regarding electronics consumption and disposal. It prepares them for roles in environmental compliance, waste management policy, corporate sustainability, and green technology design, enabling them to develop and implement effective e-waste solutions.
