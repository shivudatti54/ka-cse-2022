### Learning Purpose: Harrison R.M & E-Waste Management

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because Harrison R.M. is a leading authority in environmental chemistry, particularly on the fate and impact of hazardous substances from e-waste. His research provides the scientific foundation for understanding how toxic heavy metals and chemicals leach from discarded electronics into soil, water, and air, posing significant risks to ecosystems and human health globally.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to analyze the key findings from Harrison’s work on the chemical processes of e-waste pollution. They will evaluate the pathways of pollutant release and transport, assess the associated environmental and health risks, and critique the scientific methodologies used to monitor and quantify this contamination.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This directly connects to prior modules on toxicology, waste management systems, and environmental policy. It applies core principles of chemistry and earth science to a real-world problem, bridging the gap between theoretical environmental studies and practical, evidence-based research on pollution.

**4. Real-world applications**
Understanding this research is essential for developing effective e-waste recycling protocols, shaping public health policies, designing safer electronic products, and improving remediation strategies for contaminated sites, making it highly relevant for future environmental scientists and policymakers.
