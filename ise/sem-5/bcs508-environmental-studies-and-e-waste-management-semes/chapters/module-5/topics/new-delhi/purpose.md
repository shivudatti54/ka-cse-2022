### Learning Purpose: New Delhi in E-Waste Management

**1. Why is this topic important?**
New Delhi is a critical global case study, serving as a major hub for both the generation and informal processing of electronic waste. Studying its challenges provides a stark, real-world example of the severe environmental and human health consequences of unregulated e-waste disposal, highlighting the urgent need for sustainable management solutions.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will analyze the socio-economic drivers behind Delhi's extensive informal recycling sector, including the roles of scrap dealers and the associated health risks for workers. They will evaluate the environmental impact on local ecosystems and assess the effectiveness of existing policies and initiatives aimed at formalizing and improving the e-waste management system.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly connects to previous modules on life cycle assessment (tracking a product's end-of-life), the global e-waste trade (India is a major recipient), and the principles of a circular economy. It applies theoretical concepts of sustainable waste management, policy frameworks, and environmental justice to a complex, real-world scenario.

**4. Real-world applications**
Understanding Delhi's situation equips students to critically evaluate e-waste management strategies in their own regions, advocate for responsible consumer disposal, and contribute to designing safer, more efficient formal recycling systems that protect both the environment and human health.
