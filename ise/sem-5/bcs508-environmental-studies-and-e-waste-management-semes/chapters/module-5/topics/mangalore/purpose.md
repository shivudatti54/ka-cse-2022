### Learning Purpose: Mangalore's Role in E-Waste Management

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because Mangalore is a significant case study of a rapidly urbanizing Indian city facing the dual challenges of growing electronic consumption and inadequate e-waste disposal systems. Studying it highlights the direct environmental and public health consequences of improper e-waste handling in a specific, relatable context, moving the problem from an abstract global issue to a tangible local one.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn about Mangalore's specific e-waste generation patterns, the existing formal and informal recycling sectors within the city, and the associated environmental impacts on its local ecosystem, such as soil and water contamination. They will also analyze the socio-economic dynamics of the informal waste-picking economy and evaluate the effectiveness of current local policies and proposed management strategies.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly connects to earlier modules on the global e-waste problem, life cycle assessment of electronics, and policy frameworks. It applies these broader concepts to a micro-level, real-world scenario, bridging the gap between theoretical knowledge of waste management principles and their practical, ground-level implementation and challenges in an urban Indian setting.

**4. Real-world applications**
Understanding Mangalore's situation equips students to conduct community-level e-waste audits, design targeted awareness campaigns for consumers and businesses, and propose context-specific improvements to municipal waste collection systems. This knowledge is essential for future roles in urban planning, corporate sustainability (EPR compliance), and environmental consultancy focused on developing localized waste management solutions.
