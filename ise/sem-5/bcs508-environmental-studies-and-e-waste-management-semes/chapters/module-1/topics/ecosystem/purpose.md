# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1.  Define an ecosystem and identify its biotic and abiotic components.
2.  Differentiate between producers, consumers, and decomposers and explain their roles.
3.  Describe the flow of energy through an ecosystem, applying the 10% law.
4.  Explain the concept of nutrient cycling and its importance.
5.  Compare and contrast food chains and food webs.
6.  Interpret ecological pyramids (numbers, biomass, energy) and explain their shapes.
7.  Identify different types of ecological interactions (e.g., predation, mutualism).
