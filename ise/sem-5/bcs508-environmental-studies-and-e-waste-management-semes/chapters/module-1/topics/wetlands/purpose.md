### Learning Purpose: Wetlands

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Wetlands are among the planet's most productive and vital ecosystems. They are crucial for biodiversity, water purification, flood control, and carbon sequestration, directly combating climate change. Understanding their role is fundamental to environmental conservation and sustainable resource management, especially as they face severe threats from urbanization and pollution.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define wetlands and identify their different types (e.g., marshes, swamps). They will analyze the key ecological functions wetlands provide and the major threats leading to their degradation. This knowledge will be applied to understand the importance of conservation policies and sustainable practices.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly connects to previous modules on ecosystems and biodiversity. It provides a critical link to upcoming concepts in e-waste management, as wetland health can be severely impacted by leached heavy metals and toxins from improperly disposed electronic waste, illustrating the intersection of natural and human-made environmental systems.

**4. Real-world applications**
Students will be equipped to engage in real-world wetland conservation efforts, assess the environmental impact of development projects, and understand how responsible e-waste recycling can protect these fragile ecosystems from contamination. This knowledge is essential for future roles in policy-making, environmental advocacy, and sustainable business practices.
