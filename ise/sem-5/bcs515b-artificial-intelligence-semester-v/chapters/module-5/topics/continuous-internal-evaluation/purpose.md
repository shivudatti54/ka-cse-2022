# Learning Purpose: Continuous Internal Evaluation in AI

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) is crucial because it shifts assessment from a single high-stakes exam to an ongoing process. In a rapidly evolving field like AI, this allows for a more accurate and holistic measurement of a student's understanding, practical skills, and ability to apply concepts incrementally, mirroring the iterative nature of AI development itself.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to demonstrate their proficiency through a series of structured activities. This includes implementing and refining small-scale AI models, critically analyzing case studies, and presenting solutions—all of which develop not only technical competency but also soft skills like documentation, collaboration, and effective communication.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
CIE directly connects theoretical knowledge from all previous modules (e.g., Machine Learning, Neural Networks, NLP) to practical application. It reinforces learning by requiring students to continuously synthesize and apply these concepts in mini-projects and assignments, ensuring a deeper, more integrated understanding of the AI curriculum.

**4. Real-world applications**
This evaluation model mirrors the agile development cycles and continuous integration/continuous deployment (CI/CD) pipelines used in the tech industry. It prepares students for professional environments where projects are assessed iteratively through sprints, code reviews, and milestone-based deliverables, rather than a single final product.
