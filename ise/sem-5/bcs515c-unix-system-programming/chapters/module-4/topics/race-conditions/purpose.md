# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1.  Define clickjacking (UI redress attack) and explain its core mechanics and security impact.
2.  Identify the primary purpose and function of the `X-Frame-Options` HTTP response header.
3.  Differentiate between the `DENY`, `SAMEORIGIN`, and `ALLOW-FROM` directives of the `X-Frame-Options` header.
4.  Explain the role of the `Content-Security-Policy` header's `frame-ancestors` directive as a modern defense against clickjacking.
5.  Compare and contrast the `X-Frame-Options` and `CSP frame-ancestors` approaches, including their browser support and capabilities.
6.  Evaluate the limitations of client-side JavaScript framebusting techniques and explain why server-set headers are superior.
