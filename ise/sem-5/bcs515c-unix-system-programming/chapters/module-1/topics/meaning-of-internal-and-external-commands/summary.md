# **Meaning of Internal and External Commands**

### Overview

- In UNIX, commands are the building blocks of a script or a program.
- Commands can be categorized into two types: internal and external commands.

### Internal Commands

---

- Internal commands are built-in to the shell and are executed directly by the shell.
- Examples:
  - cd (change directory)
  - pwd (print working directory)
  - exit (exit the shell)
- Internal commands are executed more quickly than external commands.

### External Commands

---

- External commands are stored in a file or on disk and are executed by the shell.
- Examples:
  - ls (list files and directories)
  - grep (global regular expression print)
  - sort (sort lines of input)
- External commands are executed more slowly than internal commands.

### Key Differences

---

- Internal commands are faster and more efficient.
- External commands are more flexible and can be reused.
- Internal commands are built-in to the shell, while external commands are separate files.

### Important Formulas, Definitions, and Theorems

---

- No specific formulas or definitions relevant to internal and external commands.
- Definition: A command is a sequence of characters that instructs the shell to perform a specific task.
- Theorem: The shell can execute both internal and external commands to achieve a desired outcome.
