# Image Restoration And Reconstruction
### Learning Purpose: Image Restoration and Reconstruction

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Image Restoration and Reconstruction is a foundational pillar of computer vision because real-world images are invariably corrupted by noise, blur, and other degradations during acquisition or transmission. This topic is crucial for recovering a high-quality, original image from its degraded observation, which is a prerequisite for accurate high-level tasks like object recognition and medical diagnosis. It teaches the mathematical principles of modeling degradation and building inverse processes to counteract it.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the fundamental noise models (e.g., Gaussian, salt-and-pepper) and degradation functions (e.g., motion blur). They will explore core restoration techniques including spatial filters (mean, median, Wiener) and frequency domain filtering. The module will also cover reconstruction concepts from projections, introducing them to the foundational principles behind computed tomography (CT) scans.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module builds directly on the signal processing and Fourier transform knowledge from earlier modules. It provides the cleaned, enhanced images required for successful segmentation (Module 4) and object recognition (Module 5). Conversely, it relies on a precise understanding of image formation and sampling covered in Module 1 and 2.

**4. Real-world applications**
Applications are vast and critical: enhancing astronomical images, restoring old photographs and archived films, improving medical imaging (MRI, CT scan clarity), removing noise from surveillance footage, and correcting artefacts in satellite and geographical imaging systems.
