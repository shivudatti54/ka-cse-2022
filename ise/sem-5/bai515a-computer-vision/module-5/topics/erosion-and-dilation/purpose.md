# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define morphological processing and explain the role of a structuring element.
2. Describe the fundamental operation of erosion, both mathematically and intuitively, including its effect on binary images.
3. Describe the fundamental operation of dilation, both mathematically and intuitively, including its effect on binary images.
4. Differentiate between erosion and dilation based on their purposes, effects, and the conditions that govern their output.
5. Manually compute the result of applying erosion or dilation with a given structuring element to a small binary image matrix.
6. Identify real-world image processing problems where erosion or dilation would students an appropriate solution (e.g., noise removal, gap filling).
