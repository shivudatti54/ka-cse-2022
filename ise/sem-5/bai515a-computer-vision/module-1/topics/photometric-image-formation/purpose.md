# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the fundamental principles of light interaction with surfaces, including diffuse and specular reflection models
2. Describe the complete process of photometric image formation from scene radiance to digital pixel values
3. Differentiate between radiometric and photometric quantities and understand their relationships
4. Apply the image irradiance equation to analyze how various camera parameters affect image formation
5. Understand the role of the camera response function and Bayer pattern in digital image capture
6. Identify applications of photometric principles in computer vision tasks such as photometric stereo and shape from shading
