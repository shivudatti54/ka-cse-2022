# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Explain the concept of Fourier Transform and its significance in transforming images from spatial to frequency domain

2. Differentiate between spatial domain and frequency domain representations of images and describe their respective applications

3. Apply the 2D Fourier Transform to images and interpret the resulting frequency spectrum, including DC component and frequency distribution

4. Analyze the computational advantages of Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) over direct Discrete Fourier Transform (DFT) implementation

5. Implement frequency domain filtering operations including ideal, Butterworth, and Gaussian low-pass and high-pass filters

6. Utilize the convolution theorem to perform filtering operations efficiently in the frequency domain

7. Design appropriate frequency domain filters for applications such as image smoothing, edge enhancement, and noise reduction
