# **Chap-3 (3.1) Computer Vision Revision Notes**

## **Definition and Brief History**

- Computer Vision (CV): branch of Artificial Intelligence (AI) that enables computers to interpret and understand visual data from images and videos.
- Brief History:
  - 1950s: Early work on computer vision, including image processing and feature extraction.
  - 1960s-1970s: Development of image capture devices and image processing algorithms.
  - 1980s-present: Advancements in computer vision with the development of machine learning and deep learning techniques.

## **Image Formation**

- **Photometric Image**: representation of light intensity at each pixel in an image.
- Image Formation:
  - **Camera**: converts light into electrical signals, which are then processed into an image.
  - **Sensor**: captures light and converts it into an electrical signal.
  - **Lens**: focuses light onto a sensor or film.

## **Key Formulas and Definitions**

- **Intensity **(I):\*\* light intensity at a given point in an image.
- **Radiance **(L):\*\* light intensity per unit solid angle at a given point in an image.
- **Viewing Angle **(α):\*\* angle between the line of sight and the normal to the surface.

## **Theorems and Concepts**

- **Ray Tracing**: method for calculating the behavior of light as it interacts with objects in a scene.
- **Shading**: technique for determining the color and appearance of objects in an image.
- **Perspective**: technique for creating a 2D representation of a 3D scene.

## **Important Equations**

- **Radiance Equation**: L = I \* cos(α)
- **Viewing Angle Equation**: θ = arctan(1/r)
