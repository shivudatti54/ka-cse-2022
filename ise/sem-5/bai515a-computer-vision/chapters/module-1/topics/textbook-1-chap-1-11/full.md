## **Introduction: What is Computer Vision?**

### 1.1: What is Computer Vision?

Computer vision is a branch of artificial intelligence (AI) that enables computers to interpret and understand visual information from the world around us. It is a field that deals with the interaction between computers and visual data, which can be captured through various means such as cameras, microscopes, and other sensors.

### Historical Context

The concept of computer vision has been around for several decades, but it wasn't until the 1980s that the field began to take shape. The introduction of the first commercial computer vision system, the PICK (Programmable Image Capture) system, marked the beginning of the field. This system was capable of detecting and identifying objects on a production line.

In the 1990s, the field of computer vision experienced a significant boost with the introduction of the first computer vision conferences and the development of new algorithms and techniques. The 2000s saw the rise of modern computer vision, with the introduction of deep learning techniques and the development of large-scale computer vision datasets.

### Image Formation

Before we can understand what computer vision is, we need to understand how images are formed. An image is a two-dimensional representation of a three-dimensional scene, which can be captured through various means such as cameras and microscopes.

#### Photometric Image

A photometric image is a type of image that represents the amount of light that falls on a surface. It is a measure of the intensity of the light and is typically represented in units such as pixels, which are small squares of the image.

##### Image Formation Process

The process of forming a photometric image can be described as follows:

1. **Light Emission**: Light is emitted from a source, such as the sun or a light bulb.
2. **Reflection**: The light is reflected off a surface, such as a ball or a book.
3. **Transmission**: The light is transmitted through a medium, such as air or a medium with a certain optical density.
4. **Detection**: The light is detected by a sensor, such as a camera or a photodetector.

The detected light is then converted into an electrical signal, which is processed by a computer to create an image.

### Applications of Computer Vision

Computer vision has a wide range of applications, including:

- **Object Recognition**: Detecting and identifying objects in images.
- **Image Segmentation**: Dividing an image into its constituent parts.
- **Tracking**: Following the movement of objects over time.
- **Facial Recognition**: Identifying individuals based on their facial features.
- **Self-Driving Cars**: Using computer vision to navigate and control a vehicle.

### Case Study: Object Recognition

One of the most common applications of computer vision is object recognition. In this case study, we will explore how to detect and identify objects in an image using a computer vision system.

#### Object Recognition System

The object recognition system consists of the following components:

- **Camera**: Captures the image of the object.
- **Image Processing**: Enhances and preprocesses the image.
- **Feature Extraction**: Extracts features from the image.
- **Classification**: Classifies the object based on the extracted features.

The system uses a deep learning algorithm, such as convolutional neural networks (CNNs), to classify the object.
