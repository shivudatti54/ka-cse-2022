# **Revision Notes: Computer Vision - Chap 1 (1.1)**

### What is Computer Vision?

- Computer vision is the field of study that enables computers to interpret and understand visual data from images and videos.
- It involves developing algorithms and models to process and understand visual information.

### A Brief History of Computer Vision

- 1950s: First computer vision research began with the development of image processing techniques.
- 1960s-1970s: Early computer vision systems were developed for industrial applications, such as inspection and robotics.
- 1980s-present: Advancements in computer vision have led to applications in fields like security, healthcare, and autonomous vehicles.

### Image Formation

- **Photometric Image**: An image that represents the amount of light that hits a sensor or camera.
- **Radiometric Image**: An image that represents the actual amount of light that hits a sensor or camera.

### Key Formulas and Definitions

- **Intensity**: The amount of light that hits a sensor or camera.
- **Radiance**: The amount of light that falls on a surface.
- **Illuminance**: The amount of light that falls on a surface per unit area.

### Important Theorems

- **The Radon Transform**: A mathematical tool used to reconstruct images from projection data.
- **The Fourier Transform**: A mathematical tool used to analyze and process images.

### Key Concepts

- **Image acquisition**: The process of capturing images using cameras or sensors.
- **Image processing**: The application of algorithms and techniques to enhance, restore, or modify images.
