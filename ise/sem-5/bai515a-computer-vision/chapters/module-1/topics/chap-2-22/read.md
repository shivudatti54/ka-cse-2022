## Introduction

---

In computer vision, an image is the representation of the light or radiation reflected from an object. Photometric images are a crucial aspect of computer vision, as they provide a measure of the amount of light that is reflected from an object.

## Photometric Image

---

A photometric image is a 2D representation of the amount of light that is reflected from an object. It is a measure of the intensity of the light that is reflected, and it can be used to determine the properties of the object, such as its color, texture, and shape.

### Characteristics of Photometric Image

- **Intensity**: The amount of light that is reflected from an object, measured in units of watts per square meter (W/m²).
- **Radiance**: The amount of light per unit solid angle that is emitted or reflected from an object, measured in units of watts per steradian (W/str).
- **Illuminance**: The amount of light that falls on a surface, measured in units of lux (lx).

### Types of Photometric Images

- **Raw Image**: The raw data from a camera sensor, which contains both photometric and non-photometric information.
- **Linear Image**: An image that has been processed to remove non-photometric information, resulting in a linear relationship between the pixel values and the photometric quantity.
- **Logarithmic Image**: An image that has been processed to reduce the dynamic range of the image, resulting in a logarithmic relationship between the pixel values and the photometric quantity.

### Applications of Photometric Image

- **Image Segmentation**: Photometric images can be used to segment objects in an image based on their intensity values.
- **Object Recognition**: Photometric images can be used to recognize objects in an image based on their intensity values and other features.
- **Image Enhancement**: Photometric images can be used to enhance the quality of an image by adjusting the intensity values of the pixels.

### Example

Consider an image of a sky with a few clouds. The photometric image of this image would show a range of intensity values, with the sky being darker than the clouds. By analyzing this photometric image, we can determine the properties of the sky and the clouds, such as their color, texture, and shape.

## Key Concepts

---

- **Intensity**: The amount of light that is reflected from an object.
- **Radiance**: The amount of light per unit solid angle that is emitted or reflected from an object.
- **Illuminance**: The amount of light that falls on a surface.
- **Raw Image**: The raw data from a camera sensor.
- **Linear Image**: An image that has been processed to remove non-photometric information.
- **Logarithmic Image**: An image that has been processed to reduce the dynamic range of the image.

## Conclusion

---

Photometric images are a crucial aspect of computer vision, as they provide a measure of the amount of light that is reflected from an object. By analyzing photometric images, we can determine the properties of the objects in an image, such as their color, texture, and shape. Understanding photometric images is essential for applications such as image segmentation, object recognition, and image enhancement.
