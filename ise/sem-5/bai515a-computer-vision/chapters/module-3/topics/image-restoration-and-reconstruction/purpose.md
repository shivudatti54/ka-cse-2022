### Learning Purpose: Image Restoration and Reconstruction

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Image Restoration and Reconstruction is a fundamental pillar of computer vision, crucial for recovering a high-quality original image from its degraded version. In real-world applications, images are often corrupted by noise, blur, or other artifacts during acquisition or transmission. This topic provides the mathematical and algorithmic foundation to reverse these degradations, which is essential for ensuring the reliability of downstream vision tasks.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the primary causes of image degradation (e.g., noise, motion blur) and the corresponding mathematical models. They will explore and implement core restoration techniques, including spatial and frequency domain filtering (e.g., Wiener filter), and algorithms for noise reduction and deblurring. The module also covers reconstruction concepts from image projections, forming a basis for computed tomography (CT) scans.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds upon the prior knowledge of image processing (Module 2), utilizing concepts like convolution, the Fourier transform, and image filtering. It provides the enhanced image quality necessary for successful performance in subsequent modules on high-level tasks like object detection (Module 4) and image segmentation (Module 5).

**4. Real-world applications**
The techniques learned are critical in medical imaging (MRI, CT scan reconstruction), astronomical image enhancement, forensic analysis (e.g., license plate recognition from blurry footage), and restoring archived photographs and historical documents.
