**Subject:** COMPUTER VISION
**Module:** Module 3
**Topic:** Segmentation by Region Growing & Region Splitting & Merging

---

1.  **Importance:** This topic is crucial because segmentation is a foundational step in computer vision, transforming raw pixels into meaningful regions that represent objects or parts of objects. Region-based techniques are particularly important for their ability to produce connected segments with closed boundaries, which is essential for subsequent tasks like object recognition, scene interpretation, and image measurement.

2.  **Learning Outcomes:** Students will learn the underlying principles of region growing, splitting, and merging. They will understand how to implement algorithms that group pixels based on similarity criteria (e.g., intensity, texture) and how to manage the trade-offs between over-segmentation and under-segmentation. The goal is to move from a pixel-level to a region-level representation of an image.

3.  **Connection to Other Concepts:** This knowledge builds directly upon low-level image processing concepts like pixel connectivity and similarity metrics. It serves as a critical prerequisite for more advanced topics such as feature extraction, object detection, and 3D scene reconstruction, providing the structured data these higher-level processes require.

4.  **Applications:** These segmentation methods are widely applied in medical imaging (e.g., isolating tumors in MRI scans), remote sensing (e.g., identifying land use in satellite imagery), autonomous driving (e.g., detecting road regions), and content-based image retrieval.
