**Why this topic matters:**
Learning Boundary Preprocessing (Boundary Following & Chain Codes) is crucial in Computer Vision as it enables the detection and analysis of image boundaries, which is essential for various applications. By understanding how to process and represent boundaries, researchers and developers can develop more accurate and efficient algorithms for tasks such as object recognition, segmentation, and feature extraction.

**Real-world applications:**
Boundary Preprocessing has numerous real-world applications, including image and video processing, robotics, and computer-aided design (CAD). For instance, boundary detection is used in automatic image segmentation, object recognition, and quality control systems. It is also used in medical imaging to detect tumors, bones, and other anatomical structures.

**Connection to other concepts:**
Boundary Preprocessing is closely related to other concepts in Morphological Image Processing, such as Erosion and Dilation, Opening and Closing, and Hit-or-Miss Transformations. Understanding the principles of boundary preprocessing is essential to grasp these concepts and to develop more sophisticated image processing algorithms. Additionally, boundary preprocessing is a fundamental step in other image processing techniques, such as image segmentation, feature extraction, and object recognition.
