# **Switching: Packet Switching and its types**

## **Introduction**

- Switching is a technique used in computer networks to forward packets between networks.
- It is a critical component of modern computer networks.

## **Packet Switching**

- Packet switching is a technique where data is divided into small packets and transmitted over a network.
- Each packet is assigned a header that contains the destination IP address and other relevant information.

## **Types of Packet Switching**

- **Store-and-Forward Switching**
  - Data is stored in a buffer before being transmitted
  - Switching occurs at the destination network
- **Cut-Through Switching**
  - Data is processed before being stored in a buffer
  - Switching occurs at intermediate nodes
- **Virtual Circuit Switching**
  - Connection is established between sender and receiver before data transmission
  - Switching occurs at the destination network
- **Connectionless Switching**
  - Connection is not established before data transmission
  - Switching occurs at intermediate nodes

## **Theorems and Formulas**

- **Theorem: Network Capacity**
  - Total network capacity = sum of individual link capacities
- **Theorem: Packet Transmission Time**
  - Minimum packet transmission time = minimum of hop times along the shortest path

## **Important Definitions**

- **Hop Time**: time it takes for a packet to traverse one hop (i.e., one link) in the network
- **Packet Loss**: loss of a packet during transmission or routing
- **Jitter**: variation in packet transmission time
- **Packet Delay**: delay in packet transmission or routing

## **Revision Tips**

- Understand the different types of packet switching and their characteristics.
- Know the theorems and formulas related to packet transmission and network capacity.
- Be able to explain the concepts of hop time, packet loss, jitter, and packet delay.
