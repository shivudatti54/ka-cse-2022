**Why this topic matters:**
Controlled Access is crucial in computer networks as it ensures efficient and secure data transmission by limiting the access of multiple devices to a shared medium, thereby preventing collisions and ensuring data integrity. This concept is vital in preventing network congestion and promoting fair sharing of bandwidth.

**Real-world applications:**
In real-world scenarios, Controlled Access is widely used in wireless local area networks (WLANs) such as Wi-Fi, where access is restricted to authorized devices to prevent unauthorized access and data breaches. It is also employed in cable networks, such as cable television and broadband, to manage data transmission and prevent signal interference.

**Connection to other concepts:**
Controlled Access is closely related to other concepts in computer networks, such as error detection and correction, as it relies on techniques like block coding and cyclic codes to detect and correct errors in transmitted data. Additionally, it shares connections with congestion control mechanisms, as it aims to prevent network congestion by limiting the number of devices that can access the network at the same time.
