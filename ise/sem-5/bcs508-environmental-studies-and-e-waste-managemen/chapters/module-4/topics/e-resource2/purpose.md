# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1.  Explain the hydrological cycle and its significance as the foundation of all water resources.
2.  Differentiate between surface water and groundwater resources, and identify the key challenges associated with each.
3.  Analyze the water demands of the major use sectors (agricultural, domestic, industrial, environmental) and the conflicts that arise between them.
4.  Evaluate the major challenges in water resources management, including scarcity, pollution, over-exploitation, and the impacts of climate change.
5.  Describe the principles and strategies of sustainable water management, including Integrated Water Resources Management (IWRM), demand management, and pollution control.
6.  Discuss the role of policy, community participation, and technology in achieving effective and equitable water governance.
