**Why this topic matters:**
Learning about waterborne diseases and water-induced diseases is crucial as it highlights the significant impact of contaminated water on human health, particularly in developing countries where access to clean water is limited. Understanding the causes, symptoms, and prevention measures of these diseases can help individuals make informed decisions to protect their health and the environment.

**Real-world applications:**
This topic has real-world applications in water resource management, public health, and environmental conservation. It informs policies and strategies for safe drinking water supply, wastewater treatment, and water pollution control, ultimately reducing the burden of waterborne diseases on communities worldwide.

**Connection to other concepts:**
The study of waterborne diseases and water-induced diseases connects to other concepts in environmental studies, such as water quality management, ecosystem health, and sustainable development. It also intersects with concepts like climate change, where increased temperatures and extreme weather events can contaminate water sources, exacerbating the risk of waterborne diseases.
