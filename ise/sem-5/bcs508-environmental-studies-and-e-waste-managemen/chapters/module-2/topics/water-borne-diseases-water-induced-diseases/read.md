# Water Borne Diseases & Water Induced Diseases

### Introduction

Water is essential for human survival, but contaminated water can lead to various health problems. Waterborne diseases and water-induced diseases are caused by the presence of pathogens, chemicals, or physical contaminants in drinking water. In this section, we will discuss the causes, effects, and examples of waterborne diseases and water-induced diseases.

### Definition

- Waterborne diseases: Diseases caused by the consumption of contaminated water, leading to the presence of pathogens, such as bacteria, viruses, or parasites, in the body.
- Water-induced diseases: Diseases caused by the exposure to contaminated water, but not necessarily through consumption, such as skin irritation, eye infections, or respiratory problems.

### Causes of Waterborne Diseases

The following factors contribute to the spread of waterborne diseases:

- **Contaminated Water Sources**: Rivers, lakes, and groundwater can be contaminated with human waste, animal waste, or industrial waste.
- **Poor Water Treatment**: Inadequate or ineffective treatment of water can lead to the presence of pathogens.
- **Lack of Sanitation**: Insufficient or poor sanitation can lead to the disposal of human waste into water sources.
- **Climate Change**: Climate change can alter water chemistry, making it more susceptible to contamination.

### Examples of Waterborne Diseases

- **Cholera**: Caused by the bacterium Vibrio cholerae, which is found in contaminated water.
- **Typhoid Fever**: Caused by the bacterium Salmonella Typhi, which is found in contaminated water.
- **Dysentery**: Caused by the bacterium Shigella dysenteriae, which is found in contaminated water.
- **Leptospirosis**: Caused by the bacterium Leptospira, which is found in contaminated water.

### Causes of Water-Induced Diseases

The following factors contribute to the spread of water-induced diseases:

- **Physical Contaminants**: Chemicals, heavy metals, or other physical contaminants in water can cause skin irritation, eye infections, or respiratory problems.
- **Chemical Contaminants**: Chemicals in water, such as pesticides, herbicides, or industrial chemicals, can cause a range of health problems.
- **Inadequate Water Quality**: Poor water quality can lead to the presence of pathogens, physical contaminants, or chemical contaminants.

### Examples of Water-Induced Diseases

- **Skin Irritation**: Caused by the presence of physical contaminants, such as sand or sediment, in water.
- **Eye Infections**: Caused by the presence of physical contaminants, such as sand or sediment, in water, or chemical contaminants, such as pesticides or herbicides.
- **Respiratory Problems**: Caused by the inhalation of chemical contaminants, such as particulate matter or gases, in water.

### Prevention and Control Measures

Preventing and controlling waterborne and water-induced diseases requires a multi-faceted approach:

- **Source Protection**: Protecting water sources from contamination through proper management and regulation.
- **Water Treatment**: Ensuring adequate and effective treatment of water to remove pathogens and physical and chemical contaminants.
- **Sanitation and Hygiene**: Promoting proper sanitation and hygiene practices to prevent the disposal of human waste into water sources.
- **Public Education**: Educating the public about the risks of waterborne and water-induced diseases and the importance of proper water treatment and use.

By understanding the causes, effects, and examples of waterborne diseases and water-induced diseases, we can take steps to prevent and control these health problems, ensuring safe and clean drinking water for everyone.
