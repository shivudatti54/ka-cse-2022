# Water Borne Diseases & Water Induced Diseases

### Overview

Waterborne diseases are illnesses caused by the consumption of contaminated water, while water-induced diseases are conditions caused by the interaction of water with the human body.

### Key Points

#### Waterborne Diseases

- **Definition:** Diseases caused by the consumption of contaminated water, typically requiring a vector (e.g. waterborne pathogens, chemicals) to survive and thrive.
- **Examples:**
  - Cholera
  - Typhoid fever
  - Dysentery
  - Hepatitis A
- **Causes:**
  - Bacteria (e.g. Escherichia coli, Salmonella)
  - Viruses (e.g. Rotavirus, Norovirus)
  - Parasites (e.g. Giardia, Cryptosporidium)
  - Chemical contaminants (e.g. heavy metals, pesticides)
- **Risk Factors:**
  - Poor sanitation and hygiene
  - Insufficient water treatment
  - Contaminated water sources (e.g. wells, rivers)

#### Water Induced Diseases

- **Definition:** Conditions caused by the interaction of water with the human body, including physical and chemical changes.
- **Examples:**
  - Skin infections (e.g. swimmer's itch, contact dermatitis)
  - Respiratory problems (e.g. asthma, bronchitis)
  - Gastrointestinal issues (e.g. acid reflux, stomach ulcers)
  - Allergic reactions (e.g. hives, itching)
- **Causes:**
  - Physical factors (e.g. temperature, pH, pressure)
  - Chemical factors (e.g. dissolved gases, ions)
  - Biological factors (e.g. microorganisms, allergens)
- **Risk Factors:**
  - Prolonged exposure to contaminated water
  - Poor water quality
  - Underlying medical conditions

### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Coliform Index:** A measure of the number of coliform bacteria present in water, used to assess water quality.
- **Pfizer coefficient:** A factor used to calculate the risk of waterborne disease transmission.
- **Water Quality Index (WQI):** A numerical rating system used to evaluate the quality of drinking water.

### Theorems and Principles

- **Hygiene Hypothesis:** The idea that exposure to microorganisms in childhood may help develop immune systems and prevent allergies.
- **Public Health Principle:** The concept that individual actions can contribute to collective health outcomes.
