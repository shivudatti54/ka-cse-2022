# **Natural Resources: Water Resources – Availability & Quality Aspects**

## **Key Concepts**

- **Definition:** Water is a vital natural resource essential for human survival, industry, and ecosystem health.
- **Availability:** Water resources are limited, and their availability is affected by factors like precipitation, evaporation, and infiltration.
- **Quality:** Water quality is influenced by factors like climate, geology, land use, and human activities.

## **Water Resources Classification**

- **Renewable:** Surface water (rivers, lakes, reservoirs) and groundwater
- **Non-renewable:** Stored water in glaciers and ice caps

## **Water Cycle and Availability**

- **Water Balance:** M = P + Q - R - E (where M = moisture input, P = precipitation, Q = evaporation, R = runoff, E = evapotranspiration)
- **Water Availability Index (WAI):** WAI = (P - R) / (P + R)

## **Factors Affecting Water Quality**

- **Physical Factors:**
  - Temperature
  - pH
  - Turbidity
- **Chemical Factors:**
  - pH
  - Hardness
  - Salinity
  - Nutrient levels
- **Biological Factors:**
  - Bacteria
  - Algae
  - Fungi

## **Important Formulas and Theorems**

- **Darcy's Law:** Q = -k \* A \* (ΔP / Δx) (where Q = flow rate, k = hydraulic conductivity, A = cross-sectional area, ΔP = pressure difference, Δx = distance)
- **Henry's Law:** C = k \* P (where C = concentration, k = Henry's law constant, P = pressure)

## **Revision Tips**

- Understand the water cycle and its impact on water availability.
- Recognize the factors affecting water quality and their impact on human health and ecosystems.
- Apply formulas and theorems to analyze water resources and quality.
