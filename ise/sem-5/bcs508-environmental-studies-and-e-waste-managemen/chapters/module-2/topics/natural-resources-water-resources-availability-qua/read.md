# **Natural Resources: Water Resources – Availability & Quality Aspects**

## **Introduction**

Water is a vital component of the Earth's ecosystem, essential for human survival, agriculture, and industry. However, the availability and quality of water resources are increasingly being threatened by various environmental, social, and economic factors. This study material provides an overview of the availability and quality aspects of water resources.

## **Definition of Water Resources**

- Water resources refer to the natural systems and sources that supply freshwater to humans and ecosystems.
- These include rivers, lakes, reservoirs, groundwater, and wetlands.

## **Availability of Water Resources**

### Factors Affecting Water Availability

- **Geographical Location**: Mountains, hills, and valleys can block or redirect water flow, reducing availability.
- **Climate Change**: Changes in temperature and precipitation patterns affect water availability.
- **Demands on Water**: Agriculture, industry, and urbanization increase water demands.
- **Water Loss**: Evaporation, transpiration, and leakage can lead to water loss.

### Types of Water Resources

- **Surface Water**: Rivers, lakes, reservoirs, and wetlands.
- **Groundwater**: Underlying aquifers and wells.
- **Freshwater**: Potable and non-potable water sources.

### International and Local Water Resources

- **International Water Resources**: Shared by countries across borders (e.g., Nile River).
- **Local Water Resources**: Managed at the local level (e.g., municipal water supply).

## **Quality of Water Resources**

### Factors Affecting Water Quality

- **Pollution**: Industrial, agricultural, and domestic waste can contaminate water.
- **Human Activities**: Deforestation, urbanization, and climate change can alter water cycles.
- **Geological Factors**: Rock type, soil composition, and groundwater flow can affect water quality.

### Types of Water Pollution

- **Physical Pollution**: Sediment, silt, and debris can reduce water clarity.
- **Chemical Pollution**: Nutrient, bacterial, and toxic pollutants can harm aquatic life.
- **Biological Pollution**: Algal blooms, invasive species, and diseases can affect water quality.

### Water Quality Indicators

- **pH**: Measures acidity or alkalinity.
- **Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)**: Measures total minerals and salts.
- **Bacterial Coliform**: Measures fecal contamination.

## **Case Studies**

- **Chernobyl Nuclear Disaster (1986)**: Radioactive contamination of water sources.
- **Bhopal Gas Tragedy (1984)**: Toxic gas release into groundwater.
- **Yukon River (Canada)**: Oil spill and water quality decline.

## **Conclusion**

Water resources are crucial for human survival and ecosystem health. However, availability and quality are threatened by various environmental, social, and economic factors. Understanding the factors affecting water availability and quality is essential for sustainable management and conservation of water resources.

**Key Concepts**

- Availability: Refers to the amount of water available for human use.
- Quality: Refers to the purity and suitability of water for human consumption, agriculture, and industry.
- Factors affecting water availability: Geographical location, climate change, demands on water, and water loss.
- Factors affecting water quality: Pollution, human activities, geological factors, and types of water pollution.
- Water quality indicators: pH, TDS, and bacterial coliform.
