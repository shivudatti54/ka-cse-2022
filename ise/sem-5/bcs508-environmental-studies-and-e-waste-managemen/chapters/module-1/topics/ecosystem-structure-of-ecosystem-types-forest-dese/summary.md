# **Ecosystem: Structure of Ecosystem, Types**

### Definition:

An ecosystem is a complex network of living organisms (biotic factors) interacting with each other and their environment (abiotic factors).

### Structure of Ecosystem:

- **Producers (Autotrophs)**: Organisms that produce their own food, such as plants and algae.
- **Consumers (Heterotrophs)**: Organisms that consume other organisms or organic matter, such as animals and decomposers.
- **Decomposers**: Organisms that break down dead organic matter, such as bacteria and fungi.
- **Energy Flow**: Energy flows from producers to consumers through food chains and food webs.
- **Nutrient Cycling**: Nutrients are recycled through ecosystems through decomposition and nutrient uptake by organisms.

### Types of Ecosystems:

#### 1. Forest Ecosystem

- Characterized by dense vegetation, complex food webs, and high biodiversity.
- Examples: Tropical rainforests, temperate forests.

#### 2. Desert Ecosystem

- Characterized by limited vegetation, low rainfall, and high temperatures.
- Examples: Sahara Desert, Mojave Desert.

#### 3. Wetlands Ecosystem

- Characterized by standing water, saturated soils, and unique plant and animal communities.
- Examples: Mangrove forests, marshes, and swamps.

#### 4. Riverine Ecosystem

- Characterized by flowing water, aquatic plants, and diverse animal life.
- Examples: Freshwater rivers, estuaries, and deltas.

#### 5. Oceanic Ecosystem

- Characterized by vast bodies of saltwater, diverse marine life, and complex food webs.
- Examples: Coral reefs, open ocean, and deep-sea ecosystems.

### Important Formulas and Theorems:

- **Carson's Law**: Energy flow is proportional to the number of trophic levels.
- **Trophic Cascade**: Changes in one trophic level can have cascading effects on subsequent levels.
- **Logistic Growth**: Population growth is limited by available resources and environmental factors.

### Key Terms:

- **Biotic Factors**: Living organisms in an ecosystem.
- **Abiotic Factors**: Non-living components of an ecosystem, such as light, temperature, and water.
- **Ecosystem Services**: The benefits humans receive from ecosystems, such as clean air and water, and climate regulation.
