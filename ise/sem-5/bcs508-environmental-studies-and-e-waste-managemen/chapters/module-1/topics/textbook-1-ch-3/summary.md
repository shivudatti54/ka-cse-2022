### Textbook 1: CH- 3 Revision Notes

#### Environmental Studies and E-Waste Management

#### Module: Ecosystem and Sustainability

**Key Concepts:**

- **Ecosystem Services**: The benefits humans receive from functioning ecosystems, including provision of fresh water, climate regulation, and air and water purification.
- **Sustainable Development**: Economic growth that meets the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.
- **Circulating Economy**: An economy that keeps resources in use for as long as possible, extracts the maximum value from them, and recovers and regenerates materials at the end of their service life.

**Important Formulas:**

- **Carson Rule**: A method for estimating the rate of pollution: `∂C/∂t = -kC`
- **Half-Life Formula**: `t_{1/2} = \frac{ln(2)}{k} = \frac{0.693}{k}`
- **Environmental Impact Assessment**: `EIA = \frac{A}{V} \times \frac{U}{D} \times f \times S \times T`

**Definitions:**

- **E-Waste**: Electronic waste generated from the disposal of electronic devices such as computers, phones, and televisions.
- **Circular Economy**: An economic system that aims to keep resources in use for as long as possible, extract the maximum value from them, and recover and regenerates materials at the end of their service life.
- **Carbon Footprint**: The amount of greenhouse gases released into the atmosphere as a result of human activities.

**Theorems:**

- **The Law of Conservation of Energy**: Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only converted from one form to another.
- **The Law of Conservation of Mass**: Matter cannot be created or destroyed, only converted from one substance to another.
