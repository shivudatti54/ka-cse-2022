# **Sustainability: 17SDG Targets and Possible Actions**

## **Introduction**

The United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are a set of 17 goals aimed at ending poverty, protecting the planet, and ensuring peace and prosperity for all. The 17th goal, "Take action to eliminate all forms of violence against all people and ages," is not the focus of this report. Instead, we will delve into the 17th SDG target that is relevant to our module, "Environmental Studies and E-Waste Management": "Take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts."

## **Historical Context**

The concept of sustainability has been around for decades, but the modern understanding of the term gained momentum in the 1970s and 1980s, with the publication of books such as "The Limits to Growth" (Meadows et al., 1972) and "Ecological Economics" (Georgescu-Roegen, 1971). The SDGs were adopted in 2015, building upon the Millennium Development Goals (MDGs) and the Paris Agreement (2015). The 17th SDG target was included to address the urgent need to combat climate change and its impacts.

## **17th SDG Target: 13.1**

**Goal 13.1:**
"Take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts by reducing greenhouse gas emissions through a process of watching, learning, adopting, scaling-up, and disseminating effective and replicable practices."

**Possible Actions:**

### 1. Reduce Greenhouse Gas Emissions

- Transition to renewable energy sources
- Improve energy efficiency
- Electrify transportation
- Carbon capture and storage

### 2. Adapt to the Impacts of Climate Change

- Early warning systems for extreme weather events
- Climate-resilient infrastructure
- Climate-smart agriculture
- Ecosystem-based adaptation

### 3. Promote Sustainable Land Use

- Sustainable agriculture practices
- Reforestation and afforestation
- Agroforestry
- Climate-resilient water management

### 4. Support Climate Change Education and Awareness

- Climate change education in schools
- Public awareness campaigns
- Climate change research and development
- Climate change policy and governance

### 5. Encourage Climate Change Governance

- Climate change policies and laws
- International cooperation and agreements
- Climate change monitoring and reporting
- Climate change finance and funding

**Case Studies:**

- **Costa Rica's Renewable Energy Transition:** Costa Rica has made significant progress in transitioning to renewable energy, with over 90% of its electricity coming from hydroelectric power.
- **California's Greenhouse Gas Emissions Reduction:** California has implemented a range of policies to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, including a cap-and-trade system and a goal of 80% reduction by 2050.
- **The Green Climate Fund:** The Green Climate Fund is an international fund that provides funding for climate change mitigation and adaptation projects in developing countries.

**Applications:**

- **Carbon Pricing:** Carbon pricing is a policy tool that puts a price on carbon emissions, providing a financial incentive for companies and individuals to reduce their emissions.
- **Sustainable Supply Chains:** Companies can reduce their greenhouse gas emissions by implementing sustainable supply chain practices, such as reducing transportation emissions and promoting renewable energy.
- **Climate-Resilient Agriculture:** Climate-resilient agriculture practices, such as agroforestry and conservation agriculture, can help farmers adapt to climate change and reduce their greenhouse gas emissions.

## **Conclusion:**

The 17th SDG target, "Take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts," is a critical goal that requires immediate attention and action. By reducing greenhouse gas emissions, adapting to the impacts of climate change, promoting sustainable land use, supporting climate change education and awareness, and encouraging climate change governance, we can help mitigate the worst effects of climate change and create a more sustainable future for all.
