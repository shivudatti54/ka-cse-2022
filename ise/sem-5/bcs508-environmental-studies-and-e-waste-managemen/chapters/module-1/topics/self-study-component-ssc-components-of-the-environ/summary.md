# **Self-Study Component (SSC): Components of the Environment**

**Key Points:**

- **Biological Components:**
  - Living organisms (plants, animals, microorganisms)
  - Interactions between organisms and their environment
  - Ecosystems (biotic components: producers, consumers, decomposers)
- **Physical Components:**
  - Soil
  - Water
  - Air
  - Climate
  - Topography
- **Chemical Components:**
  - Nutrients
  - Pollutants
  - Gases (oxygen, carbon dioxide, etc.)
  - Minerals
- **Human Components:**
  - Human population
  - Technology
  - Economic activities

**Important Formulas, Definitions, and Theorems:**

- **Carson's Ecosystem Theory:** "All living things are interconnected and interdependent"
- **Sustainability:** "Meeting the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs"
- **Environmental Carrying Capacity:** Formula: C = P x R x T, where C = carrying capacity, P = population, R = resource utilization, T = technology
- **Pollution Index:** Formula: PI = (C1 + C2 + ... + Cn) / n, where PI = pollution index, C1, C2, ..., Cn = pollution levels of different components

**Revision Tips:**

- Review the relationships between biological, physical, chemical, and human components of the environment
- Understand the concepts of ecosystem, sustainability, and environmental carrying capacity
- Practice calculating pollution index and environmental carrying capacity
