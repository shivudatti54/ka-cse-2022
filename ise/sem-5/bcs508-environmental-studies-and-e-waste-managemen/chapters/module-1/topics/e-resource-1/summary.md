## Revision Notes

### Key Terms and Definitions

- **E-resource**: Electronic waste or electronic recyclable material.
- **E-waste**: Discarded electronic devices, parts, and materials from consumption and disposal.
- **Recyclable materials**: Materials that can be reused or processed to produce new products.
- **Closed-loop recycling**: A process where materials are continually cycled back into production.

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Waste Management Hierarchy**:
  1. Reduce
  2. Reuse
  3. Recycle
  4. Recover
  5. Dispose
- **Eco-Indicator 26**: A measure of the environmental impact of electronic waste.

### Key Concepts

- **Characteristics of E-waste**:
  - Toxic substances (e.g., lead, mercury, cadmium)
  - Electronic components (e.g., batteries, circuit boards)
  - Non-electronic materials (e.g., plastics, metals)
- **E-waste Generation**:
  - Factors: consumer behavior, technological advancements, e-waste management practices
- **E-waste Disposal**:
  - Landfilling
  - Incineration
  - Recycling

### Important Theories and Models

- **Circular Economy**: A production and consumption system that aims to keep resources in use for as long as possible.
- **Triple Bottom Line**: A framework that considers economic, social, and environmental impacts of business operations.

### Quick Revision Tips

- Review the waste management hierarchy and its applications.
- Understand the characteristics and types of e-waste.
- Familiarize yourself with eco-indicators and their significance.
- Practice identifying and disposing of e-waste responsibly.
