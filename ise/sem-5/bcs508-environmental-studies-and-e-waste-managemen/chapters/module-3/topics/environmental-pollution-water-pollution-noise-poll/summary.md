# Environmental Pollution: Water Pollution, Noise Pollution, Air Pollution

### Water Pollution

- **Sources:**
  - Industrial effluent
  - Agricultural runoff
  - Domestic sewage
  - Chemical contamination
- **Impacts:**
  - Harm to aquatic life
  - Water scarcity
  - Increased risk of water-borne diseases
  - Economic losses
- **Preventive Measures:**
  - Treatment and disposal of waste
  - Implementation of pollution control norms
  - Public awareness and education
  - Waste management strategies
- **Public Health Aspects:**
  - Increased risk of water-borne diseases (e.g. cholera, typhoid)
  - Long-term effects on human health (e.g. cancer, neurological damage)

### Noise Pollution

- **Sources:**
  - Traffic noise
  - Industrial noise
  - Recreational noise (e.g. music, construction)
- **Impacts:**
  - Hearing loss
  - Stress and anxiety
  - Sleep disturbance
  - Decreased cognitive function
- **Preventive Measures:**
  - Noise regulations and guidelines
  - Use of noise-reducing materials
  - Sound barriers and noise abatement measures
  - Public awareness campaigns
- **Public Health Aspects:**
  - Long-term effects on hearing loss and cognitive function
  - Increased risk of mental health problems (e.g. depression, anxiety)

### Air Pollution

- **Sources:**
  - Fossil fuel combustion (e.g. coal, oil, gas)
  - Industrial processes
  - Agricultural burning
  - Transportation (e.g. vehicles, aviation)
- **Impacts:**
  - Respiratory problems (e.g. asthma, lung cancer)
  - Cardiovascular disease
  - Premature mortality
  - Neurological damage
- **Preventive Measures:**
  - Energy efficiency and renewable energy sources
  - Emissions control technologies
  - Waste management and recycling
  - Public awareness and education
- **Public Health Aspects:**
  - Long-term effects on respiratory and cardiovascular health
  - Increased risk of premature mortality and morbidity

Key Formulas and Definitions:

- **BOD (Biochemical Oxygen Demand)**: measure of the amount of oxygen required by microorganisms to break down organic matter in wastewater
- **NOx (Nitrogen Oxides)**: pollutants emitted by fossil fuel combustion and industrial processes
- **PM2.5 (Particulate Matter)**: fine particles < 2.5 μm in diameter, affecting respiratory and cardiovascular health

Key Theorems and Concepts:

- **Anthropogenic pollution**: pollution caused by human activities
- **Ecosystem services**: essential services provided by nature, including clean air and water
- **Sustainable development**: balance between economic growth, social welfare, and environmental protection
