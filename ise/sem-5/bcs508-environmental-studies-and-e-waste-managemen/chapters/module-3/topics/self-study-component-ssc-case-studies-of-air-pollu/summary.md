# **Self-Study Component (SSC) Revision Notes: Air Pollution Episodes**

### Definition and Importance of Air Pollution

- Air pollution refers to the contamination of air by pollutants that can cause harm to human health and the environment.
- It is a major environmental issue that affects the quality of life and health of individuals and communities.

### Types of Air Pollutants

- Primary pollutants: emitted directly into the air, e.g., particulate matter, sulfur dioxide, nitrogen oxides.
- Secondary pollutants: formed in the air through chemical reactions, e.g., ground-level ozone, smog.

### Case Studies of Air Pollution Episodes

- **Beijing Air Pollution Episode (2013)**:
  - Caused by high levels of particulate matter (PM2.5) and sulfur dioxide (SO2).
  - Resulted in significant health impacts, including respiratory problems and cardiovascular disease.
- **London Smog (1952)**:
  - Caused by a combination of pollution from coal-fired power plants, vehicles, and industrial activities.
  - Led to widespread illness and death, with estimated mortality rates of 12,000 people.

### Health Impacts of Air Pollution

- Respiratory problems, including asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).
- Cardiovascular disease and stroke.
- Cancer and neurodegenerative diseases.

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **APHA (Air Pollutant Emission Standards Act) Formula**:
  - APHA = (PM2.5 + NOx + SO2) / 1000
- **Aeolian Formulation**:
  - wind speed (m/s) = (PM2.5 concentration (μg/m³) x 1.08) / (1.08 x 10^(-9))
