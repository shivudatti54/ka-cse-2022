# **Global Context in E-Waste**

### Definitions and Key Terms

- E-waste: Electronic waste generated from the disposal of electronic devices
- Electronic waste management: The process of managing and disposing of e-waste in an environmentally responsible manner
- Sustainable development: The process of meeting the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs

### Environmental Impact of E-Waste

- Toxic substances: Lead, mercury, cadmium, and other hazardous materials are released into the environment through e-waste disposal
- Pollution: E-waste can contaminate soil, air, and water, posing health risks to humans and wildlife
- Climate change: E-waste contributes to greenhouse gas emissions through the production and disposal of electronic devices

### Global E-Waste Statistics

- Estimated global e-waste generation: 50 million metric tons per year (2020)
- Top e-waste generating countries: China, USA, Japan, and South Korea
- E-waste disposal methods: landfilling, incineration, and recycling

### International Frameworks and Regulations

- Basel Convention: An international treaty regulating the transboundary movement of hazardous waste, including e-waste
- RoHS Directive: A European Union directive restricting the use of certain hazardous substances in electrical and electronic equipment
- Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment (WEEE) Directive: A European Union directive requiring the recycling and disposal of e-waste

### Theories and Formulas

- The Waste Hierarchy: A framework for managing waste, prioritizing prevention and reuse over disposal
- The 3Rs: Reduce, Reuse, Recycle
- The Circular Economy: A model for sustainable production and consumption, reducing waste and promoting resource efficiency

### Important Formulas

- Total worldwide e-waste (2020): 50 million metric tons/year = 4.4 billion metric tons (estimated)
- E-waste generation rate: 1.4% of global GDP
