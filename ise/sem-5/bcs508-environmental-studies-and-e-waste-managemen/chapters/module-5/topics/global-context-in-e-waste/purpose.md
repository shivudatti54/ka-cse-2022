**Why this topic matters:**
Learning about global context in e-waste is crucial as it highlights the devastating environmental and health impacts of electronic waste on a global scale, emphasizing the need for sustainable practices and responsible consumption. The topic matters as it underscores the interconnectedness of global issues, such as pollution, climate change, and human rights.

**Real-world applications:**
The global context of e-waste has significant real-world implications, including international trade agreements, consumer behavior, and government policies. Understanding these factors can inform strategies for e-waste management, such as designing more sustainable products, establishing recycling infrastructure, and promoting extended producer responsibility.

**Connection to other concepts:**
The global context of e-waste is intricately linked to other environmental concepts, such as the circular economy, sustainable development, and environmental justice. By examining the global context of e-waste, students can better appreciate the complex relationships between human behavior, technological innovation, and environmental outcomes, ultimately informing more effective solutions for e-waste management and sustainable development.
