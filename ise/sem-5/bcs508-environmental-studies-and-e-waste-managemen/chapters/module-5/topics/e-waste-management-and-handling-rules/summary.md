# E-Waste (Management and Handling) Rules

### Overview

- The E-Waste (Management and Handling) Rules, 2011, is an environmental regulation in India aimed at managing electronic waste (e-waste).
- The rules provide guidelines for the collection, segregation, transportation, storage, treatment, and disposal of e-waste.

### Key Points

- **Definition of E-Waste**: Electronic waste refers to waste generated from the disposal of electronic devices such as computers, mobile phones, televisions, and other electronic equipment.
- **Types of E-Waste**: Hazardous e-waste, non-hazardous e-waste, and special e-waste.
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Manufacturers: Design and manufacture products with recyclability in mind.
  - Consumers: Return used products to manufacturers or authorized recycling centers.
  - Municipalities: Establish e-waste collection centers and transport e-waste to treatment facilities.
  - Treatment Facilities: Ensure proper handling, storage, and disposal of e-waste.
- ** e-Waste Segregation**:
  - Hazardous e-waste: Batteries, fluorescent lamps, and other toxic components.
  - Non-hazardous e-waste: Non-toxic and non-hazardous components such as metals and plastic.
- **Certification**: Manufacturers must obtain certification from the Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) for designing and manufacturing e-waste management systems.

### Important Formulas, Definitions, and Theorems

- **Definition of Environmental Impact**: The impact of human activities on the environment.
- **Theorem of Environmental Justice**: The principle that everyone has the right to a healthy environment, regardless of their economic or social status.

### Key Acts and Regulations

- **The Environmental (Protection) Act, 1986**: Provides the framework for environmental protection in India.
- **The Hazardous Waste (Management, Handling, Reclamation and Transboundary Movement) Rules, 2013**: Regulates the handling and disposal of hazardous waste.

### Important Provisions

- **E-Waste Registration**: Manufacturers must register with the CPCB and obtain a registration certificate for designing and manufacturing e-waste management systems.
- **E-Waste Labeling**: Manufacturers must affix a label to products indicating the e-waste management practices used.
