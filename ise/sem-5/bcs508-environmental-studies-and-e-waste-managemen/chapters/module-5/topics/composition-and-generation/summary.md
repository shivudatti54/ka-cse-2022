# Composition and Generation of E-Waste

### Definition and Scope

- Definition: E-waste, electronic waste, refers to discarded electronic devices and their parts.
- Scope: Includes a wide range of electronic products, such as computers, smartphones, televisions, and appliances.

### Composition of E-Waste

- **Materials Composition:**
  - Metals: copper, gold, silver, lead, mercury
  - Non-metals: plastics, glass, ceramics
  - Organic materials: batteries, textiles
- **Waste Streams:**
  - Hazardous waste (e.g., batteries, electronics with hazardous materials)
  - Bulk waste (e.g., discarded devices, packaging materials)
  - Recyclable materials (e.g., metals, plastics)

### Generation of E-Waste

- **Factors Contributing to E-Waste Generation:**
  - Rapid obsolescence of electronic devices
  - Increasing demand for technology and consumption
  - Lack of effective waste management infrastructure
- **E-Waste Generation Rates:**
  - Global: approximately 50 million metric tons per year
  - Regional: varies depending on country and region

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Waste Management Hierarchy:**
  1.  Reduce
  2.  Reuse
  3.  Recycle
  4.  Recover
  5.  Dispose
- **Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) Formula:**
  Total Energy Use = Energy Use in Production + Energy Use in Use + Energy Use in Disposal

### Key Concepts

- **Design for Recyclability (DfR):** design of products and systems that facilitate easy recyclability and reuse.
- **Closed-Loop Production:** production of new products from recycled materials.

### Important Terms

- **Eco-friendly:** products or processes that minimize harm to the environment.
- **Sustainable consumption:** consumption patterns that minimize waste and promote the efficient use of resources.
