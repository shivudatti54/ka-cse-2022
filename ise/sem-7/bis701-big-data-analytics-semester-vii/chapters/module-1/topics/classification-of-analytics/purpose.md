### Learning Purpose: Classification of Analytics

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the classification of analytics is fundamental because it provides the strategic framework for all data-driven decision-making. It moves beyond simply processing data to asking the right questions at the right time, guiding what data to collect, which tools to use, and how to interpret the results. This classification ensures analytical efforts are aligned with specific business goals, from optimizing current operations to predicting future trends.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to distinguish between the four primary types of analytics: Descriptive (what happened?), Diagnostic (why did it happen?), Predictive (what will happen?), and Prescriptive (what should we do?). They will understand the unique purpose, common techniques (e.g., reporting, regression, machine learning, optimization), and the increasing business value and complexity associated with each level.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the cornerstone that connects core technical skills like data mining, SQL, statistics, and machine learning to their strategic application. It shows how these techniques are not used in isolation but are purposefully selected based on the problem. It directly builds on data preprocessing and feeds into advanced topics like model deployment and business intelligence.

**4. Real-world applications**
Applications are vast: **Descriptive** (dashboarding sales performance), **Diagnostic** (analyzing root cause of a supply chain failure), **Predictive** (forecasting customer churn or equipment failure), and **Prescriptive** (recommending optimal inventory levels or personalized marketing actions).
