### Learning Purpose: Why Big Data?

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the 'why' behind Big Data is foundational. It moves beyond the technical 'how' and 'what' to establish the critical business and societal imperatives driving the field. This context is essential for students to appreciate the immense strategic value of data and why organizations invest heavily in analytics infrastructure and talent.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core drivers and characteristics (Volume, Velocity, Variety, Veracity, Value) that define Big Data. They will analyze the limitations of traditional data processing systems and identify the specific technological and market shifts that created the need for new Big Data paradigms and tools.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic sets the stage for the entire course. It provides the necessary context for subsequent modules on specific technologies (like Hadoop, Spark), storage solutions (HDFS, NoSQL databases), and analytical techniques (machine learning, predictive modeling). It directly links to business intelligence, data science, and cloud computing.

**4. Real-world applications**
Students will explore applications across industries: personalized recommendations in e-commerce, real-time fraud detection in finance, predictive maintenance in manufacturing, and genomic sequencing in healthcare. This demonstrates how Big Data solves real problems and creates tangible value.
