### Learning Purpose: NoSQL

**1. Why is this topic important?**
As the volume, velocity, and variety of data continue to explode, traditional relational databases often struggle with scalability and flexibility. NoSQL databases are fundamentally important because they are designed to handle this modern "big data," enabling organizations to store and process massive, unstructured, or rapidly changing datasets efficiently. Understanding NoSQL is essential for building scalable, high-performance applications.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core principles of NoSQL, including its schema-less design. They will explore the four primary data models: key-value, document, column-family, and graph databases. The module will cover the CAP theorem and how it influences database design for distributed systems, providing a contrast to traditional SQL-based approaches.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon foundational knowledge of databases and data structures from earlier semesters. It connects to distributed systems, cloud computing (where NoSQL is often deployed), and data engineering pipelines. Understanding NoSQL provides a crucial counterpart to SQL, offering a complete picture of modern data storage strategies.

**4. Real-world applications**
NoSQL databases power many real-world applications we use daily. They are the backbone of social media feeds (e.g., Facebook uses Cassandra), e-commerce product catalogs, real-time analytics platforms, and IoT data collection. Learning NoSQL equips students with skills directly applicable to building these scalable, modern systems.
