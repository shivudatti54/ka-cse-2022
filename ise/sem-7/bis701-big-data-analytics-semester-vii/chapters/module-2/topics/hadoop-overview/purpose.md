# Learning Objectives
After studying this topic, you should be able to:
1.  Define a thread and differentiate it from a process, listing the components they share and those they do not.
2.  Explain at least four advantages of using a multithreaded approach in program design.
3.  Compare and contrast the three primary multithreading models (Many-to-One, One-to-One, Many-to-Many), including their advantages and disadvantages.
4.  Distinguish between user-level and kernel-level thread libraries and provide an example of each.
5.  Identify why context switching between threads is faster than between processes.
6.  Recognize the main challenge (synchronization) that arises from multithreading and name common mechanisms used to address it.
