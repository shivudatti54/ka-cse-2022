### Learning Purpose: RDBMS vs. Hadoop

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the fundamental differences between RDBMS (Relational Database Management Systems) and Hadoop is crucial because the choice between these two paradigms dictates how an organization stores, processes, and analyzes its data. Selecting the wrong technology can lead to inefficiency, high costs, and an inability to gain valuable insights. This knowledge is essential for designing modern, scalable data architectures.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to compare and contrast RDBMS and Hadoop across key dimensions, including data structure (structured vs. semi/unstructured), schema requirements (schema-on-write vs. schema-on-read), scalability (vertical vs. horizontal), and ideal use cases (e.g., OLTP vs. batch processing). They will evaluate which technology is best suited for a given business problem.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the foundation for understanding the broader big data ecosystem. It connects directly to subsequent concepts like data warehousing, ETL processes, and modern hybrid architectures (like Lambda or Kappa), which often integrate both relational databases and Hadoop-like frameworks to handle diverse data workloads.

**4. Real-world applications**
This comparison is applied when choosing a platform for tasks like building a traditional financial transaction system (RDBMS) versus analyzing massive volumes of social media logs or sensor data for predictive maintenance (Hadoop). It is a key decision point in industries like finance, e-commerce, and telecommunications.
