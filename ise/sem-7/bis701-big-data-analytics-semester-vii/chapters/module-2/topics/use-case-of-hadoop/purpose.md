### **Learning Purpose: Use Case of Hadoop**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding Hadoop use cases is crucial because it moves beyond abstract theory to demonstrate *how* and *why* this foundational technology is applied to solve real-world, large-scale data problems. It provides the critical link between Hadoop's architectural components and its tangible business value, justifying its role in the modern data ecosystem.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify common industry patterns where Hadoop is the optimal solution, such as large-scale data warehousing, log processing, and sentiment analysis. They will analyze specific case studies to understand the problem Hadoop solved, the architecture deployed, and the business outcome achieved, moving from a conceptual understanding to applied knowledge.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly integrates concepts from Module 1 (Hadoop architecture, HDFS, MapReduce) and connects to other Big Data technologies like Spark, Hive, and HBase. It demonstrates how these components work together in a pipeline. Furthermore, it lays the groundwork for future modules on analytics, machine learning, and real-time processing by establishing the batch-oriented foundation they often build upon.

**4. Real-world applications**
The applications are vast and cross-industry. Students will see its use in **recommendation systems** (e.g., Netflix, Amazon), **fraud detection** in banking, **genomic sequencing** in healthcare, and analyzing **social media sentiment** for brand management. This demonstrates Hadoop's pivotal role in enabling data-driven decision-making.
