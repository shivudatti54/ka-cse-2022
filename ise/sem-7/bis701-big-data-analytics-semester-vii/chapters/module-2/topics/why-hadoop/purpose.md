### Learning Purpose: Why Hadoop?

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because Hadoop provides the foundational framework for processing and storing massive datasets that traditional systems cannot handle. Understanding "Why Hadoop?" explains the shift from centralized to distributed computing, which is the bedrock of modern big data analytics. It is essential knowledge for any data professional working with large-scale, unstructured data.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core problems Hadoop was designed to solve: storing (`HDFS`) and processing (`MapReduce`) vast amounts of data across clusters of computers. They will understand its key characteristics—scalability, fault tolerance, and cost-effectiveness—and why these made it a revolutionary technology in the big data landscape.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module connects directly to prior knowledge of data storage limitations and sets the stage for subsequent technologies. Hadoop is the precursor and often the underlying engine for more recent tools like `Spark`, `Hive`, and `HBase`. Understanding Hadoop's architecture is fundamental to grasping the entire big data ecosystem.

**4. Real-world applications**
Hadoop is applied in numerous real-world scenarios. Major companies use it for log processing, recommendation systems (e.g., e-commerce, streaming services), fraud detection in finance, and large-scale ETL (Extract, Transform, Load) processes. It enables analytics on diverse data types, from social media feeds to scientific sensor data.
