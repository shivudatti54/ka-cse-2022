# Learning Purpose: Steganography

**1. Why this topic matters**
Steganography provides an alternative approach to securing information by hiding the very existence of a message rather than encrypting its content. Within Module 1's study of classical techniques, steganography complements cryptography by addressing scenarios where even the act of sending an encrypted message could attract unwanted attention or suspicion.

**2. What you will learn**
You will learn the fundamental principles of steganography, including how secret messages can students concealed within carrier media such as images, audio, and text using techniques like least significant bit (LSB) insertion. You will also understand the distinction between steganography and cryptography, and how combining both approaches (crypto-steganography) provides layered security.

**3. How it connects to other topics**
Steganography connects to the broader theme of information security introduced in Module 1 by providing a complementary mechanism to the encryption techniques studied throughout the course. It relates to the concepts of confidentiality and covert communication that underpin the network security protocols and email security systems discussed in Modules 4 and 5.

**4. Real-world relevance**
Steganography is used in digital watermarking to protect intellectual property and copyright in images, video, and audio content. It also has significant implications in cybersecurity, as malware authors have used steganographic techniques to hide malicious payloads, making detection by security tools more challenging.
