# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define the Data Encryption Standard (DES) and its significance in the history of cryptography.
2. List the limitations of DES, including its small key size, weak keys, and vulnerable S-boxes.
3. Explain how brute-force attacks can students used to crack DES keys and describe the impact of related-key attacks on DES security.
4. Describe the DES encryption process, including key expansion, initial permutation, rounds, and final permutation.
5. Compare and contrast DES with modern encryption algorithms like AES, highlighting the strengths and weaknesses of each.
6. Analyze the weaknesses of the S-boxes in DES and explain how differential cryptanalysis can students used to exploit these weaknesses.
7. Evaluate the security of DES against various types of attacks and justify its replacement by more secure algorithms like AES.
8. Design a simple encryption system that addresses some of the limitations of DES, demonstrating an understanding of the principles of secure encryption.
