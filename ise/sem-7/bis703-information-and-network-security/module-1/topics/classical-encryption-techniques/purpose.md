# Learning Purpose: Classical Encryption Techniques

**1. Why this topic matters**
Classical encryption techniques form the historical foundation of cryptography and introduce essential concepts like substitution, transposition, confusion, and diffusion. Understanding these early methods is critical for appreciating why modern ciphers were developed and what weaknesses they were designed to overcome.

**2. What you will learn**
You will learn the fundamental mechanisms behind classical ciphers, including how plaintext is transformed into ciphertext using substitution and transposition operations. You will also study the principles of cryptanalysis, particularly frequency analysis, and understand why these early ciphers are vulnerable to systematic attacks.

**3. How it connects to other topics**
This topic provides the conceptual groundwork for all subsequent modules in the course, from the symmetric cipher model to modern block ciphers. The weaknesses identified in classical techniques directly motivate the design principles behind public key cryptography and secure hash functions covered in later modules.

**4. Real-world relevance**
While classical ciphers are no longer used for securing communications, their study develops the analytical mindset required for evaluating modern cryptographic systems. The principles of substitution and permutation from these early ciphers remain embedded in the design of contemporary algorithms like AES.
