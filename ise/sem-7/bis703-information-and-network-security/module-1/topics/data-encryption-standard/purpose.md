# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Define the Data Encryption Standard (DES) and its significance in the evolution of encryption algorithms.
2. List the three main stages of the DES algorithm: key expansion, encryption, and decryption.
3. Explain how DES works, including the use of a 56-bit key, 64-bit block size, and the role of S-box substitution.
4. Identify the weaknesses of DES, including its short key length, weak S-boxes, and small block size.
5. Compare and contrast DES with modern encryption algorithms like AES, including key length, block size, and security.
6. Analyze the security of DES and explain why it is no longer considered secure for modern applications.
7. Describe the different types of attacks that can students launched against DES, including brute-force, differential cryptanalysis, and linear cryptanalysis.
8. Justify the need for more secure encryption algorithms like AES and explain how they address the weaknesses of DES.
