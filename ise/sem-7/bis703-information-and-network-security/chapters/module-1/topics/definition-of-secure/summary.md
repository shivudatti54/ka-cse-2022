# **Definition of Secure**

### Overview

This section provides a concise summary of the definition of secure in the context of information and network security.

### Key Points

- **Definition of Secure**: A system or network is considered secure if there is a low probability of unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction of the system or its data.
- **Secure System**: A system that is designed, implemented, and operated to protect its confidentiality, integrity, and availability from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction.
- **Security Metrics**: Measures used to evaluate the effectiveness of a system's security controls, such as:
  - Confidence level
  - Probability of breach
  - Mean time to detect (MTTD)
  - Mean time to respond (MTTR)

### Important Formulas, Definitions, and Theorems

- **Boltzmann's Law**: The probability of a system being in a particular state is directly proportional to the Boltzmann constant.
- **Shannon's Entropy**: A measure of the randomness or uncertainty of a system's output.
- **Secure Sockets Layer/Transport Layer Security (SSL/TLS)**: A cryptographic protocol used to secure online communications.

### Key Concepts

- **Security Controls**: Measures implemented to prevent or detect security breaches, such as firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and access controls.
- **Vulnerability Management**: The process of identifying, classifying, and remediating vulnerabilities in a system.
- **Risk Management**: The process of identifying, assessing, and mitigating risks to a system or organization.

### Important Terms

- **Confidentiality**: The protection of sensitive information from unauthorized access or disclosure.
- **Integrity**: The accuracy and completeness of a system's data.
- **Availability**: The ability of a system to operate and provide services when needed.
