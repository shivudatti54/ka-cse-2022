## **Introduction**

The Election of 1876 was a pivotal moment in American history, marked by widespread voter fraud and disputed results. In an effort to ensure the legitimacy of the election, the government employed various ciphers to secure the counting of ballots. This study will explore the ciphers used in the Election of 1876, their purpose, and the impact of cryptanalysis on the outcome of the election.

## **Background of the Election of 1876**

The Election of 1876 was a presidential election between Rutherford B. Hayes (Republican) and Samuel J. Tilden (Democrat). The election was marked by allegations of voter suppression, ballot stuffing, and other forms of electoral malfeasance. The Electoral Commission, established by Congress, was tasked with resolving the disputed electoral votes from four southern states: Florida, Louisiana, South Carolina, and Oregon.

## **The Compromised Election of 1876**

The election was marred by controversy, with both parties accusing each other of electoral irregularities. The Democratic Party claimed that the Republican Party had engaged in widespread voter suppression, while the Republican Party alleged that the Democrats had engaged in ballot stuffing. The Electoral Commission ultimately awarded the presidency to Rutherford B. Hayes, but the decision was widely unpopular and contributed to the end of Reconstruction.

## **Ciphers Used in the Election of 1876**

In an effort to ensure the integrity of the election, the government employed various ciphers to secure the counting of ballots. The most notable cipher used was the **Buchanan Cipher**, also known as the **Morse Code Cipher**. This cipher used a series of dots and dashes to represent letters and numbers, making it difficult to decipher without the key.

**Key Concepts:**

- **Buchanan Cipher:** A Morse Code Cipher used to secure the counting of ballots in the Election of 1876.
- **Morse Code:** A system of encoding letters and numbers using a series of dots and dashes.
- **Key:** A secret code used to decipher the encrypted message.

## **Cryptanalysis and the Election of 1876**

In the late 19th century, the development of cryptanalysis, the practice of breaking codes and ciphers, was still in its infancy. However, a young cryptanalyst named William Friedman, who would later become a renowned cryptologist, was tasked with deciphering the Buchanan Cipher. Friedman's efforts ultimately led to the discovery of a hidden key, which revealed the true outcome of the election.
