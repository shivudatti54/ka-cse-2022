# **Ciphers of the Election of 1876**

## **Overview**

The Election of 1876 was a pivotal event in American history, marred by controversy and manipulation. This topic explores the cryptographic techniques used to conceal and manipulate the election results.

## **Key Points**

- **The Compromised Election**
  - 1876 US presidential election between Rutherford B. Hayes and Samuel J. Tilden
  - Controversy over electoral votes in Florida, Louisiana, and South Carolina
- **Cryptographic Techniques**
  - Vigenère cipher
  - Caesar cipher
  - Steganography
- **Key Formulas and Definitions**
  - Vigenère cipher: `C = (P + K) mod 26`, where `C` is the ciphertext, `P` is the plaintext, and `K` is the keyword
  - Caesar cipher: `C = P + k`, where `C` is the ciphertext, `P` is the plaintext, and `k` is the shift
- **Theorem:**
  - "The cipher is only as strong as its weakest key" (Aesop's fable)
- **Important Events**
  - 3rd Continental Congress (1774) uses steganography to hide documents
  - 1876 election results manipulated through postal vote tampering

## **Revision Tips**

- Review the Vigenère and Caesar ciphers, including key formulas and weaknesses
- Understand the significance of the election and its impact on American history
- Familiarize yourself with steganography and its applications
- Practice decrypting ciphertext using the formulas and techniques discussed
