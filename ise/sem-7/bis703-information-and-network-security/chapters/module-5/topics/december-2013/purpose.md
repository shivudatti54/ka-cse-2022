**Learning Purpose: Analyzing the December 2013 Target Data Breach**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because the December 2013 Target breach is a landmark case study in cybersecurity. It demonstrates the catastrophic real-world consequences of security failures, highlighting the importance of robust network defense, third-party risk management, and proactive threat detection. Analyzing this event provides invaluable lessons on the tactics of attackers and the systemic vulnerabilities they exploit.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the technical chain of events that led to the breach, including the compromise of a third-party vendor, lateral movement within the network, and exfiltration of data. They will analyze the specific security failures, such as inadequate network segmentation and insufficient monitoring, and identify the key mitigations that could have prevented or limited the damage.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This case directly connects to core concepts across the curriculum: **risk management** (assessing third-party risk), **network security** (segmentation, firewall policies), **intrusion detection** (monitoring for anomalous activity), **incident response** (containment and communication), and the **human element** in security (social engineering, security awareness).

**4. Real-world applications**
Understanding this breach prepares students to design more secure network architectures, implement effective monitoring strategies, develop comprehensive incident response plans, and critically evaluate the security posture of partners and supply chains—skills directly applicable to roles in cybersecurity analysis, network engineering, and risk compliance.
