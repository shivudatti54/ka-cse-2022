# Learning Purpose: Wiley 2

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is critical as it delves into advanced cryptographic protocols and secure communication mechanisms that form the backbone of trusted digital transactions. Understanding these concepts is essential for protecting sensitive data from increasingly sophisticated cyber threats in our interconnected world.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to analyze and apply core principles of modern cryptography, including public-key infrastructure (PKI), cryptographic hash functions, and digital signatures. They will also explore protocols for secure email, web browsing (SSL/TLS), and network access, understanding their strengths and potential vulnerabilities.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds upon foundational knowledge of symmetric and asymmetric encryption from earlier modules. It provides the practical application layer for theoretical concepts, connecting cryptography to network security (e.g., securing TCP/IP layers) and access control mechanisms, creating a holistic view of the security landscape.

**4. Real-world applications**
These principles are applied daily in securing online banking, e-commerce transactions, and confidential corporate communications. Students will gain the skills to implement and manage security for web servers, verify digital signatures on software updates, and understand the trust models behind secure messaging apps and virtual private networks (VPNs).
