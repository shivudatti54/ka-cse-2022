# Texas Hold'em Poker Revision Notes

=====================================

## Definitions

---

- **Texas Hold'em Poker**: A community card poker game played with a standard deck of 52 cards.
- **Hand**: The combination of five cards held by a player.
- **Community Cards**: Five cards shared by all players, laid out face-up on the table.
- **Private Cards**: Cards held by each player.

## Key Concepts

---

- **Ante**: The mandatory bet made by all players before the cards are dealt.
- **Blind**: A forced bet made by two players before the cards are dealt (Small Blind and Big Blind).
- **Call**: Matching the current bet.
- **Raise**: Increasing the current bet.
- **Fold**: Giving up one's hand and forfeiting any chance of winning the pot.
- **Showdown**: The final stage of the game, where each player reveals their hand and the highest hand wins.

## Formulas

---

- **Expected Value (EV)**: The average return on investment, calculated as (probability of winning x payout) - (probability of losing x cost). EV = EV(P, W, L) = (P x W) - (L x (1-P)), where P = probability of winning, W = winnings, L = losses.
- **Pot Odds**: The ratio of the current pot size to the cost of calling. If the pot odds are greater than the EV, it's considered a good call.

## Theorems

---

- **The Law of Large Numbers (LLN)**: As the number of trials (hands) increases, the observed frequency of an event approaches its theoretical probability.
- **The Second Law of Thermodynamics**: In a zero-sum game, the total amount of energy (or chips) remains constant, but the distribution of energy becomes more dispersed and random over time.

## Important Concepts in Game Theory

---

- **Nash Equilibrium**: A stable state where no player can improve their payoff by unilaterally changing their strategy, assuming all other players keep their strategies unchanged.
- **Mixed Strategies**: A combination of different actions, where each action is played with a certain probability.

## Strategies

---

- **Aggressive Play**: Betting and raising to build the pot and intimidate opponents.
- **Passive Play**: Calling and folding to conserve chips and wait for favorable situations.
- **Bluffing**: Betting or raising without a strong hand to deceive opponents and manipulate the pot size.

## Important Terms

---

- **All-In**: Betting all remaining chips.
- **Continuation Bet (CB)**: Betting on the flop or turn after raising preflop.
- **Check-Raise**: Checking and then raising to control the pot and build the bet.

## Additional Notes

---

- **Table Image**: The perception of a player's playing style and aggression level, which can affect how opponents react to them.
- **Reading Opponents**: Observing and understanding an opponent's behavior and body language to make informed decisions.
- **Table Dynamics**: The interactions and relationships between players at the table, which can influence the game's flow and outcome.
