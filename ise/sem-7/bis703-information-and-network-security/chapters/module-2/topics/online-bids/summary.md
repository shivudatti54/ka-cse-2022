# **Online Bids**

**Definition:** Online bids refer to the electronic submission of bids for goods or services through an online platform.

**Key Points:**

- **Types of Online Bids:**
  - Electronic Bidding Systems (EBS)
  - E-commerce platforms
  - Online auctions
- **Benefits:**
  - Increased transparency and accountability
  - Reduced bid processing time
  - Cost savings
  - Improved accessibility for bidders
- **Security Measures:**
  - Authentication and authorization protocols
  - Encryption techniques
  - Secure communication channels
  - Auditing and logging mechanisms
- **Threats:**
  - Eavesdropping and tampering
  - Identity theft and impersonation
  - Malware and phishing attacks
  - Denial of Service (DoS) and Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks

**Formulas and Theorems:**

- **Bayes' Theorem:** P(A|B) = P(A and B) / P(B)
- **Probability of Error:** P(E) = 1 - P(No Error)
- **Confidence Interval:** X ± (Z \* σ / √n)

**Important Terms:**

- **Probability of Error:** The probability that an online bid system will produce an incorrect result.
- **Authentication:** The process of verifying the identity of a bidder or system user.
- **Authorization:** The process of granting access to a system or resource based on a user's identity.
- **Encryption:** The process of converting plaintext data into unreadable ciphertext.
