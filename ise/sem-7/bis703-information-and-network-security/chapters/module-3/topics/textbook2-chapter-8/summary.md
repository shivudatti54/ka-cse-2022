# Textbook: 2-Chapter 8

### Information and Network Security

### Key Points

- **Definition of Information Security**: The practice of protecting information from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction.
- **Key Concepts**:
  - Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability (CIA)
  - Security Control Objectives
  - Risk Management
  - Vulnerability Management
  - Threats and Vulnerabilities
- **Security Policies and Procedures**:
  - Organization's security policies
  - Security procedures
  - Incident response plan
- **Security Standards and Regulations**:
  - ISO 27001
  - PCI-DSS
  - HIPAA
  - GDPR

### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **CIA Triad**:
  - Confidentiality: Protecting sensitive information from unauthorized access
  - Integrity: Ensuring data is accurate and not modified without authorization
  - Availability: Ensuring data is accessible when needed
- **Vulnerability Management**:
  - Vulnerability scanning
  - Patch management
  - Configuration management
- **Risk Management**:
  - Risk assessment
  - Risk mitigation
  - Risk prioritization

### Important Theorems

- **Thermodynamic System**: A system that can exchange matter and energy with its surroundings.
- **Information Theory**: The study of information as a mathematical concept.

### Revision Tips

- Review security policies and procedures
- Familiarize yourself with security standards and regulations
- Practice vulnerability management and risk assessment
