### **Learning Purpose: Analysing a Simple Protocol**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Protocols are the fundamental rules governing all digital communication. A flaw in a protocol's design can lead to catastrophic security failures, regardless of how well it is implemented. Analysing a simple protocol teaches the critical mindset required to deconstruct and question communication flows, identifying potential vulnerabilities like eavesdropping, replay attacks, and spoofing before they are exploited in real-world systems.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn a structured method to analyse a protocol's security properties. They will identify the entities involved, the messages exchanged, and the assumptions made. Using threat modelling techniques, they will pinpoint weaknesses such as a lack of confidentiality, integrity, authentication, or non-repudiation. This practical skill transforms them from passive users to active evaluators of security mechanisms.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds upon cryptographic primitives learned earlier (e.g., hashing, encryption) by applying them to achieve security goals within a communication sequence. It provides the essential foundation for understanding and critiquing more complex, real-world protocols like TLS, SSH, and WPA2, which are combinations of simpler constructs. It also introduces core principles of secure design.

**4. Real-world applications**
This skill is vital for security analysts, penetration testers, and network engineers. It is used to audit new protocol designs, assess the security of IoT device communications, understand the weaknesses in legacy systems (e.g., WEP), and is the first step in developing robust, secure applications and network infrastructures.
