### Revision Notes for Chapter 10 of Information and Network Security Textbook

#### Module: 10 hours

Here's a concise summary of key points for Chapter 10:

- **Key Concepts**
  - Threats
  - Vulnerabilities
  - Attackers (Types: Insider, Outdoors, and External)
  - Phishing
  - Social Engineering
  - Malware
  - Trojan Horses
  - Spyware
  - Rootkits
  - Denial of Service (DoS) and Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS)

- **Threat Models**
  - ADAM (Adversary, Data, Asset, Motivation, Opportunity, and Ability)
  - STRIDE (Spoofing, Tampering, Repudiation, Denial of Service, Elevation, and Malicious activity)

- **Cryptographic Techniques**
  - Symmetric and Asymmetric Encryption
  - Hash Functions (SHA-256, MD5)
  - Digital Signatures (RSA, DSA)
  - Certificate Authority (CA)
  - Public Key Infrastructure (PKI)

- **Network Security**
  - Firewalls
  - Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS) and Prevention Systems (IPS)
  - VPNs
  - Network Segmentation
  - Access Control Lists (ACLs)

- **Important Formulas and Theorems**
  - Shannon's Entropy Formula
  - RSA Algorithm
  - Diffie-Hellman Key Exchange

- **Important Definitions**
  - Zero-Day Exploit
  - SQL Injection
  - Cross-Site Scripting (XSS)
  - Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) Attack
