# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the importance of digital logic design in modern computing and electronic systems.

2. Convert numbers between binary, decimal, hexadecimal, and octal number systems.

3. Apply Boolean algebra theorems and laws to simplify logical expressions.

4. Describe the operation of all basic logic gates (AND, OR, NOT, NAND, NOR, XOR, XNOR) and draw their symbols.

5. Construct truth tables for given Boolean functions or logic gates.

6. Explain the concept of universal gates and implement Boolean functions using only NAND or only NOR gates.

7. Apply De Morgan's theorems to transform and simplify Boolean expressions.

8. Convert Boolean functions to their canonical Sum of Products (SOP) and Product of Sums (POS) forms.
