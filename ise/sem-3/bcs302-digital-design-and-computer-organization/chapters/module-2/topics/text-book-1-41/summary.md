### Digital Design and Computer Organization

### 4.1 Introduction to Digital Logic

#### Key Concepts

- **Binary System**: base-2 number system using only 0s and 1s
- **Boolean Algebra**: mathematical operations on logical expressions
- **Logic Gates**: digital circuit components that perform logical operations

#### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Complement Law**: $\overline{A} \cdot A = 0$, $\overline{A} + A = 1$
- **De Morgan's Law**: $\overline{A+B} = \overline{A} \cdot \overline{B}$, $\overline{A \cdot B} = \overline{A} + \overline{B}$
- **OR Gate**: $\overline{A+B}$
- **AND Gate**: $\overline{A \cdot B}$
- **NOT Gate**: $\overline{A}$

#### Important Theorems

- **De Morgan's Theorem**: states the relationship between Boolean algebra and logic gates
- **Karnaugh Map**: a method for simplifying Boolean expressions

#### Key Terms

- **Digital Circuit**: a circuit that uses logic gates to perform digital operations
- **Digital Signal**: a discrete-time signal represented by a sequence of binary values

#### Formulae

- **NAND Gate**: $F(A,B) = \overline{A+B}$
- **NOR Gate**: $F(A,B) = \overline{A \cdot B}$
