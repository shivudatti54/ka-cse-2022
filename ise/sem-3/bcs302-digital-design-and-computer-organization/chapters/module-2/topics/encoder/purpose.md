# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define an encoder and explain its role in digital systems as the inverse operation of a decoder

2. Analyze the truth table of a binary encoder and derive Boolean expressions for output lines

3. Design a simple binary encoder (2^n to n) using OR gates based on given specifications

4. Explain the limitation of binary encoders when multiple inputs are active simultaneously

5. Describe the operation of a priority encoder and understand how it resolves conflicting input signals

6. Interpret priority encoder truth tables including don't care conditions and valid output indicators

7. Identify practical applications of encoders in real-world digital systems such as keyboards and address selection

8. Compare and contrast binary encoders and priority encoders with respect to their handling of simultaneous inputs
