# Learning Purpose: Sequential Circuits

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Sequential circuits are the fundamental building blocks for creating memory and stateful behavior in digital systems. Unlike combinational circuits, their output depends on both current inputs and past history, which is essential for building complex computational devices like computers, smartphones, and controllers that perform sequenced operations over time.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to analyze and design basic sequential elements, primarily flip-flops (SR, D, JK, T) and latches. They will understand the concepts of clock signals, synchronization, and state tables/diagrams. This knowledge culminates in designing fundamental sequential systems like registers and counters.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds upon the combinational logic learned in Module 1. Students will combine these concepts to create Finite State Machines (FSMs), a core model of computation. This is a critical prerequisite for understanding the design of central processing units (CPUs), memory units, and complex digital systems in subsequent modules.

**4. Real-world applications**
Sequential circuits are ubiquitous in digital electronics. They form the core of computer memory (RAM, registers), microprocessors, communication systems (counters for data packets), and everyday devices like digital clocks, traffic light controllers, and appliance timers.
