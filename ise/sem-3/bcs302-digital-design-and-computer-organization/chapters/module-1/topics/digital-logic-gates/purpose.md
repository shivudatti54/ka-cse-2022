# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Identify and describe the seven basic logic gates: NOT, AND, OR, NAND, NOR, XOR, and XNOR along with their symbols and logical behaviors.

2. Construct truth tables for all basic logic gates with two or more inputs, demonstrating the output for every possible input combination.

3. Write Boolean expressions for gate outputs and convert between Boolean expressions, truth tables, and gate circuit diagrams.

4. Explain why NAND and NOR gates are called universal gates and demonstrate their ability to implement any Boolean function.

5. Apply De Morgan's theorems to simplify and transform Boolean expressions and to convert between different gate implementations.

6. Analyze combinational logic circuits to determine their Boolean expressions and truth tables, including circuits with multiple cascaded gates.

7. Design simple logic circuits using basic gates to implement given Boolean functions or practical specifications.

8. Understand the practical considerations of logic gates including propagation delay, fan-in, and fan-out in digital circuit design.
