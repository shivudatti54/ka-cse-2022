# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental differences between digital and analog systems, including the advantages of digital representation in terms of noise immunity and reliability.

2. Perform conversions between binary, decimal, octal, and hexadecimal number systems, understanding the positional value system and bit significance.

3. Apply the three basic Boolean operations (AND, OR, NOT) and describe how they map to physical logic gates used in digital circuit implementation.

4. Analyze and simplify Boolean expressions using fundamental Boolean algebra theorems, including De Morgan's theorems and the consensus theorem.

5. Differentiate between combinational and sequential logic circuits, explaining why memory elements are essential for certain digital operations.

6. Design and interpret truth tables for given Boolean functions, understanding the relationship between algebraic expressions, truth tables, and logic gate implementations.

7. Recognize the importance of digital design in modern computing and its connection to advanced topics like computer architecture, embedded systems, and VLSI design.
