# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Distinguish between analog and digital signals, explaining why digital systems offer advantages in noise immunity, signal reproduction, and information processing.

2. Convert numbers between binary, decimal, and hexadecimal representations accurately, understanding positional notation and the mathematical basis of number base conversion.

3. Perform binary arithmetic operations including addition, subtraction (using two's complement), and multiplication, demonstrating proper carry and borrow propagation.

4. Explain the concept of logic levels in digital circuits, defining voltage ranges for logic 0 and logic 1 and calculating noise margins for digital circuits.

5. Differentiate between combinational and sequential logic circuits, identifying examples of each and understanding how memory elements enable sequential behavior.

6. Apply the two's complement representation for representing signed numbers in digital systems, performing negation and arithmetic operations correctly.

7. Analyze the characteristics of major logic families (TTL and CMOS), understanding their relative advantages and typical applications in digital design.

8. Connect theoretical concepts to practical digital systems, recognizing how fundamental digital design principles manifest in real-world computing devices.
