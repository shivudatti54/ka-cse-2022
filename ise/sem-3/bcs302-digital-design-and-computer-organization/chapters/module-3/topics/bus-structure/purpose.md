# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define what a computer bus is and explain its fundamental role in system interconnection

2. Identify and describe the three main types of bus lines: data lines, address lines, and control lines, along with their specific functions

3. Explain the concept of bus width and calculate theoretical bus bandwidth given bus frequency and width specifications

4. Distinguish between synchronous and asynchronous bus timing modes, including their respective advantages and disadvantages

5. Describe the need for bus arbitration and compare different arbitration methods including daisy-chain, centralized, and distributed approaches

6. Explain the hierarchical bus structure used in modern computers, including system buses, expansion buses, and I/O buses

7. Relate address bus width to maximum addressable memory capacity in computer systems

8. Analyze how bus architecture decisions impact overall system performance and design trade-offs
