A digital counter is a circuit that counts the number of clock pulses and produces a binary output. The 4:1 and 8:1 digital circuits are types of digital counters that have a specific number of counting stages.

## **Working Principle**

A 4:1 digital counter consists of four counting stages, each with a flip-flop that stores the count value. The flip-flops are connected in series, and each stage is triggered by a clock pulse. The output of each stage is fed into the next stage, creating a cascaded effect.
