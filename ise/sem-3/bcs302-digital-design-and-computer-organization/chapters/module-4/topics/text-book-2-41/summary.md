# **Text Book 2: 4.1 - Digital Design and Computer Organization**

## **Revision Notes**

### Definitions

- **Logic Gate**: A fundamental building block of digital circuits that performs a specific logical operation on one or more input signals.
- **Digital Circuit**: An electronic circuit that uses Boolean logic and electronic switches to process and transmit information as a series of binary digits (0s and 1s).
- **Combinational Circuit**: A digital circuit that produces an output based on a combination of inputs, without any feedback or memory elements.

### Key Concepts

- **Logic Gates**: AND, OR, NOT, NAND, NOR, XOR, XNOR
- **Digital Circuit Truth Tables**: A table used to represent the output of a digital circuit for different input combinations.
- **Karnaugh Map**: A graphical tool used to simplify Boolean expressions and design digital circuits.
- **Flip-Flop**: A digital circuit that can store a binary digit (0 or 1) and remember its state.

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Boolean Algebra**: Laws and operations for manipulating Boolean expressions, including:
  - Laws of Identity, Commutativity, Associativity, and Distributivity
  - Laws of De Morgan and Boolean Algebra
- **Karnaugh Map Theorem**: A method for simplifying Boolean expressions by grouping adjacent cells in the Karnaugh map.

### Important Theorems

- **De Morgan's Law**: A fundamental theorem in Boolean algebra that states: $\overline{AB} = \overline{A} + \overline{B}$ and $\overline{A+B} = \overline{A}\cdot\overline{B}$
- **Theorem of Transitivity**: A theorem that states: If A implies B and B implies C, then A implies C.

### Important Formulas

- **Truth Table Formula**: A formula that represents the output of a digital circuit based on its input combinations.
- **Equation of a Digital Circuit**: A formula that represents the output of a digital circuit in terms of its input variables and logical operations.
