# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental difference between polling and interrupt-driven I/O, and justify why interrupts are preferred in modern computer systems.

2. Describe the role and functions of an interrupt controller in managing multiple interrupt sources from various peripheral devices.

3. Identify and explain the different types of interrupt signals, including level-triggered, edge-triggered, maskable, and non-maskable interrupts.

4. Trace through the complete interrupt handling sequence from initial interrupt request to resumption of the interrupted program.

5. Explain how interrupt priority resolution works using hardware mechanisms like priority encoders and daisy chaining.

6. Understand the purpose and structure of interrupt vector tables in mapping interrupt requests to appropriate service routines.

7. Analyze the concept of interrupt latency and its significance in real-time computing applications.

8. Apply knowledge of interrupt hardware to explain how operating systems achieve efficient device management and multitasking.
