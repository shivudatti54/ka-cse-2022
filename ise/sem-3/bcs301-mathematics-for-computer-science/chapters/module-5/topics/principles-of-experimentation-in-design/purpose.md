**Why this topic matters:**
Learning the principles of experimentation in design is crucial in mathematics for computer science as it enables students to quantify uncertainty and make data-driven decisions in various fields, including software development, data analysis, and artificial intelligence. This topic matters because it provides a framework for testing hypotheses, analyzing data, and optimizing system performance. By mastering experimentation principles, students can develop a systematic approach to problem-solving and improve the accuracy of their results.

**Real-world applications:**
The principles of experimentation in design have numerous real-world applications, including:

- Software development: Test-driving code, iterating on designs, and optimizing algorithms
- Data analysis: Identifying trends, patterns, and correlations in large datasets
- Artificial intelligence and machine learning: Developing and evaluating predictive models
- Quality control: Analyzing manufacturing processes and identifying areas for improvement
