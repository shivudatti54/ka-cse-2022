# **Principles of Experimentation in Design**

### Definitions and Key Concepts

- **Experimentation**: A systematic process of designing, conducting, analyzing, and interpreting experiments to answer research questions.
- **Design of Experiments (DOE)**: A methodology for planning and conducting experiments to understand the relationship between variables.
- **Randomization**: A technique to minimize bias and ensure that each observation is independent.

### Principles of Experimentation

- **Replication**: Conduct multiple trials to ensure reliable results.
- **Control**: Include a control group to establish a baseline and isolate the effect of the variable being tested.
- **Randomization**: Randomly assign subjects or treatments to minimize bias.
- **Blinding**: Conceal treatments or outcomes to reduce bias.

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Confidence Interval (CI)**: A range of values within which the true population parameter is likely to lie.
- **Margin of Error (ME)**: The difference between the sample mean and the true population mean.
- **Standard Error (SE)**: A measure of the variability of the sample mean.
- **ANOVA (Analysis of Variance)**: A statistical technique to analyze variance between groups and within groups.

### Theorems

- **Law of Large Numbers (LLN)**: The average of the results obtained from a large number of trials will converge to the true population mean.
- **Central Limit Theorem (CLT)**: The distribution of the sample mean will be approximately normal for large sample sizes.

### Important Formulas

- **t-statistic**: A measure of the difference between the sample mean and the population mean, divided by the standard error.
- **F-statistic**: A measure of the ratio of the variance between groups to the variance within groups.
- **P-value**: The probability of observing the results (or more extreme results) assuming the null hypothesis is true.

### Revision Tips

- Understand the concepts of replication, control, randomization, and blinding.
- Learn the formulas and theorems mentioned above.
- Practice calculating confidence intervals, margin of error, standard error, t-statistic, F-statistic, and P-value.
- Review the ANOVA technique and its applications.
