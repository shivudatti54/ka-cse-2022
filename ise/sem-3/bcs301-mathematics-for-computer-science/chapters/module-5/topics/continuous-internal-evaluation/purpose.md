### Module 5: Continuous Internal Evaluation

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because it moves beyond single experiments, teaching you how to manage, analyze, and draw valid conclusions from a continuous stream of data. This mirrors real-world R&D and tech environments like A/B testing platforms or quality control systems, where data arrives sequentially and decisions must be made iteratively.

**2. What will students learn?**
You will learn to design and analyze a series of experiments, manage the statistical challenges of repeated testing (like the multiple comparisons problem), and apply sequential analysis techniques. This includes using control charts and understanding how to adjust significance levels to maintain overall validity.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This builds directly on the foundational ANOVA framework from this module, applying its principles to a dynamic context. It also connects to core concepts of probability, hypothesis testing, and regression, requiring you to synthesize these tools to solve complex, ongoing problems rather than isolated ones.

**4. Real-world applications**
This is directly applicable to continuous deployment cycles in software development (e.g., testing new features), automated quality assurance in manufacturing, and clinical trials with adaptive designs. It provides the statistical rigor needed to make reliable, data-driven decisions in fast-paced, iterative environments.
