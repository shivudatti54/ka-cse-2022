Analysis of completely randomized design (CRD) is crucial in mathematics for computer science as it provides a fundamental framework for understanding the effects of variables on a phenomenon, enabling data-driven decision making in various fields.

1. Why this topic matters: This topic matters because it helps researchers and scientists to design and analyze experiments, identify the relationships between variables, and make informed conclusions about the efficacy of treatments or interventions.

2. Real-world applications: CRD has numerous real-world applications in fields such as medicine, agriculture, engineering, and economics, where understanding the effects of different variables on outcomes is critical for optimizing processes and improving results.

3. How it connects to other concepts: CRD is closely related to other statistical concepts such as ANOVA (Analysis of Variance) and regression analysis, as it provides the foundation for these advanced techniques. It also connects to other areas of mathematics, such as probability theory and optimization, as it involves the analysis of experimental data and the estimation of model parameters.
