# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define statistical inference and explain its role in converting sample data into meaningful conclusions about populations

2. Distinguish between population and sample, and explain why we rarely measure entire populations directly

3. Differentiate between population parameters (such as μ, σ², p) and sample statistics (such as x̄, s², p̂)

4. Identify and explain the two major categories of statistical inference: estimation and hypothesis testing

5. Understand the concept of sampling variability and explain why different samples yield different sample statistics

6. Explain the importance of random sampling in producing valid statistical inferences

7. Apply the concepts of parameters and statistics to real-world scenarios involving data analysis and decision-making
