### Learning Purpose: Simple Sampling of Attributes

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding simple sampling of attributes is fundamental to making data-driven decisions in computer science. It provides the statistical foundation for analyzing a population based on a smaller, manageable subset of data, which is crucial when dealing with large datasets that are common in the field.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn how to define a population and select a simple random sample. They will understand how to calculate and interpret key sample statistics (like sample proportion) and use them to estimate population parameters. This includes grasping the inherent variability in sampling and the concept of a sampling distribution.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly on probability theory (Module 2) and provides the essential groundwork for more advanced statistical inference, such as constructing confidence intervals and hypothesis testing (later in Module 3). It is a prerequisite for understanding machine learning concepts like training/test splits and A/B testing.

**4. Real-world applications**
This technique is applied everywhere data is used. Examples include estimating the proportion of users who will click on an ad (A/B testing), calculating the error rate in a batch of software code through testing, and predicting election results from exit polls. It is the first step in any statistical analysis of large-scale data.
