### Learning Purpose: Confidence Limits

1.  **Why is this topic important?**
    Confidence limits are a fundamental concept in statistical inference, providing a crucial measure of uncertainty. In computer science, algorithms and data-driven decisions are rarely based on absolute certainty. Understanding confidence limits is essential for quantifying the reliability of estimates, model predictions, and A/B test results, ensuring robust and interpretable conclusions from data.

2.  **What will students learn?**
    Students will learn to calculate and interpret confidence intervals for population parameters, such as means and proportions. They will understand the relationship between confidence levels, interval width, and sample size. The core learning objective is to correctly articulate what a confidence interval implies about an estimated parameter.

3.  **How does it connect to other concepts?**
    This topic builds directly on the sampling distributions and the Central Limit Theorem covered earlier. It is a foundational pillar for subsequent concepts in Module 4, including hypothesis testing, which uses interval estimates to make decisions. It is also intrinsically linked to point estimation and margin of error.

4.  **Real-world applications**
    Confidence intervals are used everywhere data is analyzed. Key applications include determining the error margins in political polls, validating the performance of machine learning models, analyzing the results of website A/B tests to guide product development, and quantifying uncertainty in scientific and market research studies.
