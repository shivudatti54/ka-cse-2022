### Learning Purpose: Levels of Significance

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Levels of significance (alpha, α) are the cornerstone of hypothesis testing. They provide a pre-defined, objective standard for making decisions about statistical evidence, preventing arbitrary conclusions and forming the basis for scientific and data-driven reasoning in computer science.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define the level of significance as the probability of rejecting a true null hypothesis (Type I error). They will understand how to select an appropriate α value (e.g., 0.05, 0.01) and use it to determine statistical significance by comparing it to a p-value, leading to a clear "reject" or "fail to reject" decision for a hypothesis.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This concept directly builds upon the fundamentals of probability, null and alternative hypotheses, and p-values from earlier in the module. It is a prerequisite for understanding more advanced inference techniques like confidence intervals, statistical power, and is essential for evaluating machine learning model performance and A/B testing results.

**4. Real-world applications**
Setting significance levels is critical for making reliable decisions based on data. Applications include determining if a new algorithm is truly faster (α=0.05), validating that a feature update improves user engagement, or assessing the results of network intrusion detection systems, ensuring findings are not due to random chance.
