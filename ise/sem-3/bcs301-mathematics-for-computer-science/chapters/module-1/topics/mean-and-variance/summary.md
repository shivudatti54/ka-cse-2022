# Mean and Variance

### Definitions

- **Mean**: The average value of a random variable, calculated as the sum of all possible values divided by the number of values.
  - Formula: μ = E(X) = ∑xP(X=x)
- **Variance**: A measure of the spread or dispersion of a random variable from its mean.
  - Formula: σ² = E((X - μ)²) = ∑(x - μ)²P(X=x)

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Linearity of Expectation**: E(aX + b) = aE(X) + b
- **Law of Iterated Expectation**: E(E(X|Y)) = E(X)
- **Chebyshev's Inequality**: P(|X - μ| ≥ kσ) ≤ 1/k²
- **Central Limit Theorem (CLT)**: The distribution of the sample mean converges to a normal distribution as the sample size increases.

### Key Properties

- **Variance and Standard Deviation**: σ = √var(X), SD(X) = σ
- **Skewness and Kurtosis**: Measures of the shape of a distribution
- **Normal Distribution**: A distribution with a mean and variance, often approximated by a bell curve.

### Important Concepts

- **Expectation**: A measure of the central tendency of a random variable
- **Probability Density Function (PDF)**: A function that describes the probability of a random variable taking on a particular value
- **Conditional Expectation**: The expected value of a random variable given a condition or constraint.
