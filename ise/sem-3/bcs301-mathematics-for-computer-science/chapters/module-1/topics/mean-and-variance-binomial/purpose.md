### Learning Purpose: Mean, Variance, and the Binomial Distribution

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the mean and variance of a distribution is fundamental to quantifying the expected outcome and variability of any random process. The binomial distribution, specifically, is a cornerstone for modeling scenarios with binary outcomes (success/failure) prevalent in computer science, such as network reliability, error-correcting codes, and A/B testing.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to calculate the mean (expected value) and variance for discrete probability distributions. They will then apply this to the binomial distribution, defining its parameters (`n` trials, `p` probability of success) and mastering the formulas `μ = np` and `σ² = np(1-p)` to describe its center and spread.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly upon core probability rules (Module 1). It provides the essential foundation for understanding more complex distributions like Poisson and Normal (Gaussian) distributions (later modules). Mastery of these concepts is critical for upcoming topics in statistics, machine learning (e.g., classification), and algorithm analysis.

**4. Real-world applications**
These tools are used to predict the likelihood of a certain number of packet losses in a data stream, model the number of bugs found in a code review, analyze the results of a quality control test on hardware, and estimate user click-through rates on a website, making them indispensable for data-driven decision-making.
