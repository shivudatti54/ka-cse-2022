### Learning Purpose: Probability Mass & Density Functions, and Mathematical Expectation

**1. Importance**
This topic is fundamental because it provides the mathematical language to describe and analyze uncertain events, which is the cornerstone of probabilistic modeling in computer science. Understanding these functions is essential for quantifying randomness and making data-driven predictions.

**2. Student Learning**
Students will learn to define, interpret, and differentiate between Probability Mass Functions (PMFs) for discrete random variables and Probability Density Functions (PDFs) for continuous ones. They will calculate and apply the concept of mathematical expectation (expected value) to find the average outcome of a probabilistic process, including the expectation of functions of random variables.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts**
This knowledge directly builds upon foundational probability (Module 1) and is a prerequisite for upcoming topics like key distributions (e.g., Binomial, Normal, Poisson), variance, law of large numbers, and machine learning algorithms. It provides the tools needed to formalize stochastic processes.

**4. Real-World Applications**
These concepts are applied to model real-world systems everywhere: from calculating expected algorithm runtime and complexity analysis, to powering recommendation systems, risk assessment in cybersecurity, statistical analysis of data, and training models in artificial intelligence.
