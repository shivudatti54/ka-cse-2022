# Probability Distributions: Review of Basic Probability Theory

### 1. Introduction

Probability theory is the mathematical framework used to analyze random events and make predictions based on uncertainty. In this module, we will review the basic concepts of probability theory and its application in probability distributions.

### 2. Basic Probability Theory

#### 2.1 Definitions

- **Random Experiment**: An event that produces a set of possible outcomes, some of which may be favorable or unfavorable.
- **Sample Space**: The set of all possible outcomes of a random experiment.
- **Event**: A subset of the sample space that contains one or more favorable outcomes.
- **Probability**: A measure of the likelihood of an event occurring, ranging from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain).

#### 2.2 Basic Probability Concepts

- **Mutually Exclusive Events**: Events that cannot occur simultaneously.
- **Dependent Events**: Events where the occurrence of one event affects the probability of another event.
- **Independent Events**: Events where the occurrence of one event does not affect the probability of another event.
- **Conditional Probability**: The probability of an event occurring given that another event has occurred.

### 3. Probability Rules

#### 3.1 Probability Rules

- **Complementary Rule**: The probability of an event occurring is equal to 1 minus the probability of the event not occurring.
- **Addition Rule**: The probability of the union of two or more mutually exclusive events is the sum of their individual probabilities.
- **Multiplication Rule**: The probability of the intersection of two or more independent events is the product of their individual probabilities.

### 4. Types of Probability Distributions

#### 4.1 Discrete Probability Distributions

- **Uniform Distribution**: Each possible outcome has a probability equal to 1 divided by the number of outcomes.
- **Bernoulli Distribution**: A discrete probability distribution that models a random variable that can take on two values, 0 and 1, with a fixed probability of success.
- **Binomial Distribution**: A discrete probability distribution that models the number of successes in a fixed number of independent trials, each with a constant probability of success.

#### 4.2 Continuous Probability Distributions

- **Uniform Distribution**: A continuous probability distribution where each possible value has a probability equal to 1 divided by the range of values.
- **Normal Distribution**: A continuous probability distribution where the probability density function is symmetric about the mean and the variance is small.
- **Exponential Distribution**: A continuous probability distribution where the probability density function is proportional to the exponential function.

### 5. Key Concepts

- **Mean**: The expected value of a random variable, which represents the long-run average value of the variable.
- **Variance**: A measure of the spread or dispersion of a random variable, which represents how much the variable deviates from its mean.
- **Standard Deviation**: The square root of the variance, which represents the amount of variation or dispersion of a random variable.
- **Mode**: The value that appears most frequently in a probability distribution.

### 6. Applications of Probability Theory

- **Random Sampling**: A method of selecting a sample from a population to make inferences about the population.
- **Decision Making**: A method of making decisions based on probability theory to minimize risk and maximize gains.
- **Simulation**: A method of approximating the behavior of a complex system by generating random samples from a probability distribution.

By mastering the concepts and rules of probability theory, you can develop a solid foundation in probability distributions and apply them to real-world problems in computer science.
