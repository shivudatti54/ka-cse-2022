# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and compute the expected value (mean) of discrete and continuous random variables using their probability distributions.

2. Apply the linearity property of expectation to simplify calculations involving linear combinations of random variables.

3. Use the Law of the Unconscious Statistician (LOTUS) to find the expectation of functions of random variables without deriving their distributions.

4. Calculate and interpret variance and standard deviation as measures of dispersion around the expected value.

5. Compute moments (raw and central) of probability distributions and understand their role in characterizing distributions.

6. Determine covariance and correlation coefficients to measure the linear relationship between two random variables.

7. Apply conditional expectation and the law of total expectation in solving multi-variable probability problems.

8. Interpret expected value as a long-run average and apply it to practical decision-making scenarios in fields like business and engineering.
