# **Covariance and Correlation**

### Definitions

- **Covariance**: A measure of how much two random variables vary together. It calculates the average of the product of the deviations from the mean.
- **Correlation**: A measure of the strength and direction of the linear relationship between two random variables.
- **Coefficient of Correlation (ρ)**: A value between -1 and 1 that represents the strength and direction of the linear relationship.

### Formulas

- **Covariance Formula**: $Cov(X, Y) = E[(X - E(X))(Y - E(Y))]$
- **Correlation Formula**: $ρ = \frac{Cov(X, Y)}{\sigma_X \sigma_Y}$, where $\sigma_X$ and $\sigma_Y$ are the standard deviations of X and Y, respectively.

### Theorems

- **Bionomial Theorem**: If $X$ and $Y$ are independent random variables, then $Cov(X, Y) = 0$.
- **Independence Theorem**: If $ρ = 0$, then $X$ and $Y$ are independent.

### Properties

- **Symmetry**: $Cov(X, Y) = Cov(Y, X)$
- **Linearity**: $Cov(aX + b, Y) = aCov(X, Y)$
- **Homogeneity**: $Cov(kX, Y) = kCov(X, Y)$

### Important Concepts

- **Positive Correlation**: $ρ > 0$ indicates a positive linear relationship between X and Y.
- **Negative Correlation**: $ρ < 0$ indicates a negative linear relationship between X and Y.
- **Perfect Correlation**: $ρ = 1$ or $ρ = -1$ indicates a perfect linear relationship between X and Y.

### Quick Revision Tips

- Remember that correlation is a measure of the strength and direction of the linear relationship between two random variables.
- Use the covariance formula to calculate the covariance between two random variables.
- Recognize the properties of covariance, such as symmetry, linearity, and homogeneity.
