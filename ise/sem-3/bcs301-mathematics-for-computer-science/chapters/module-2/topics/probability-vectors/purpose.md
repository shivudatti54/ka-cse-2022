### Learning Purpose: Probability Vectors

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Probability vectors are the fundamental building blocks for modeling systems with discrete states. They are essential for quantifying uncertainty in computer science applications, from designing algorithms to training machine learning models.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to represent a probability distribution over a finite set of states as a vector. They will master operations like updating state probabilities, calculating subsequent state distributions using transition matrices, and verifying that a vector is stochastic (i.e., its entries are non-negative and sum to 1).

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This concept is a direct prerequisite for understanding Markov Chains, where a probability vector represents the state of the system at a given time step. It relies on prior knowledge of basic probability (events, distributions) and linear algebra (vectors, matrix multiplication).

**4. Real-world applications**
Probability vectors power PageRank algorithms, model user behavior in recommendation systems, predict weather patterns, and describe the state of systems in robotics, finance, and queueing theory for network traffic analysis.
