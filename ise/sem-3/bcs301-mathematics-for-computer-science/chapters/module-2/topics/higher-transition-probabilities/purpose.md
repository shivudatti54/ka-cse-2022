### Learning Purpose: Higher Transition Probabilities

**1. Importance:**  
This topic is fundamental for analyzing the mid-to-long-term behavior of systems that evolve randomly over time. Understanding how to compute multi-step transitions is crucial for predicting future states and determining stable, long-run distributions.

**2. Student Learning:**  
Students will learn to compute the probability of transitioning between states in multiple steps using the Chapman-Kolmogorov equations and matrix exponentiation. They will understand that the `n`-step transition matrix is the `n`-th power of the one-step transition matrix, a key insight into the structure of Markov chains.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts:**  
This concept directly builds on the foundational knowledge of one-step transition probabilities and state diagrams from earlier lessons. It is a prerequisite for understanding limiting and stationary distributions, which describe the chain's equilibrium behavior, a core topic later in the module.

**4. Real-World Applications:**  
Higher transition probabilities are used to model and predict behavior in numerous fields, such as forecasting weather patterns over several days, evaluating the long-term reliability of hardware systems, predicting page rankings in web surfing (Google's PageRank), and modeling genetic sequences in bioinformatics.
