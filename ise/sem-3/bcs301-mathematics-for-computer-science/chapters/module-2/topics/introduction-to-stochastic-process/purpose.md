### Learning Purpose: Introduction to Stochastic Process

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Stochastic processes provide the fundamental mathematical framework for modeling systems that evolve randomly over time. Understanding them is crucial for computer science, as they are essential for analyzing algorithms, designing networks, and building intelligent systems that must operate under uncertainty.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the formal definition of a stochastic process and its key components: the state space and index set (often time). They will explore different types, focusing primarily on Markov Chains, which are memoryless processes where the future state depends only on the present state. This builds directly on the joint probability distributions from earlier modules.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is a direct application and extension of joint probability distributions (Module 2). It serves as the foundational bridge to more advanced concepts like queuing theory, graph analysis (PageRank), machine learning (Hidden Markov Models), and randomized algorithms.

**4. Real-world applications**
Stochastic processes model a vast range of real-world phenomena, including:

- **Computer Networks:** Packet arrival times and network queue lengths.
- **Algorithms:** Performance analysis of randomized algorithms.
- **Artificial Intelligence:** Speech recognition, robot localization, and reinforcement learning.
- **Data Science:** Forecasting stock prices and analyzing user behavior sequences.
