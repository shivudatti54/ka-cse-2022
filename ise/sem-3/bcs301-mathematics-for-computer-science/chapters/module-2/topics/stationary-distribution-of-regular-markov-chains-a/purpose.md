### **Learning Purpose: Stationary Distribution & Absorbing States**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding stationary distributions and absorbing states is fundamental to analyzing the long-term behavior of systems modeled by Markov chains. It allows us to answer critical questions about stability, predict outcomes over time, and identify "trapping" states from which a system cannot escape. This is a cornerstone of stochastic modeling in computer science.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to calculate the stationary (or steady-state) distribution of a regular Markov chain, which represents the system's long-term probabilities. They will also learn to identify and analyze absorbing states, calculate the probability of absorption, and determine the expected time to reach such states using fundamental matrix methods.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon prior knowledge of state classification, transition matrices, and the Chapman-Kolmogorov equations. It provides the crucial toolkit needed before advancing to more complex models like hidden Markov models (used in machine learning and data sequencing) and Markov chain Monte Carlo methods (used for sampling and randomized algorithms).

**4. Real-world applications**

- **PageRank Algorithm:** Models the web as a Markov chain to find the stationary distribution, which determines a page's importance.
- **Queueing Theory:** Predicts long-term wait times and system load in network routers and processors.
- **Absorbing States:** Used in reliability engineering to model system failure and in game theory to model terminal outcomes (e.g., win/loss states).
