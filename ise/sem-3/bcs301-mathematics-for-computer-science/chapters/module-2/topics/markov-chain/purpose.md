# Learning Purpose: Markov Chains

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Markov Chains are a fundamental stochastic process essential for modeling systems that evolve randomly over time. Their importance lies in their ability to provide a mathematical framework for predicting the future state of a system based solely on its present state, making them a powerful tool for analyzing real-world scenarios with inherent uncertainty.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define a Markov chain using its state space and transition probability matrix. They will analyze the properties of chains, including states (transient/recurrent) and long-term behaviors like steady-state distributions. This includes performing calculations to predict future states and understand equilibrium.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon the joint probability distributions and conditional probability covered earlier in the module. It is a foundational concept for more advanced topics like Hidden Markov Models (used in machine learning and AI) and Markov Decision Processes (used in reinforcement learning and optimization).

**4. Real-world applications**
Markov Chains have diverse applications, including predicting weather patterns, modeling board games, powering Google's PageRank algorithm for web search, analyzing financial market behaviors, and developing predictive text and speech recognition systems.
