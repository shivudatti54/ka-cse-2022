### Learning Purpose: Joint Probability Distribution for Two Discrete Random Variables

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding joint probability distributions is fundamental to modeling systems where multiple random variables interact. It provides the mathematical framework for analyzing the relationship and dependence between variables, which is a cornerstone of probabilistic reasoning in computer science.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define joint probability mass functions (PMF), calculate joint, marginal, and conditional probabilities, and determine the independence of two discrete random variables. They will also be introduced to expectation and covariance for these variables.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon core probability theory (Module 1) and is a essential prerequisite for understanding multivariate distributions, Bayesian networks, and, crucially, Markov Chains. The concept of conditional probability and dependence is the very foundation upon which state transitions in a Markov Chain are built.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied in numerous CS fields: modeling correlated events in networks, designing spam filters using Naive Bayes classifiers, analyzing algorithm performance with dependent inputs, and processing data in machine learning features.
