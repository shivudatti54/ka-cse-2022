# Joint Probability Distribution for Two Discrete Random Variables

===========================================================

### Definitions and Notations

- **Joint Probability Distribution (JPD)**: A probability distribution that describes the probability of two or more discrete random variables occurring together.
- **Random Variables (X, Y)**: Discrete or continuous variables that can take on different values.
- **Joint Probability Mass Function (PMF)**: P(X=x, Y=y) = P(X=x, Y=y)

### Formulas and Theorems

- **Joint Probability Formula**:
  P(X=x, Y=y) = P(X=x)P(Y=y|X=x) (for continuous variables)
  P(X=x, Y=y) = P(X=x)P(Y=y|X=x) if X is discrete and Y is discrete
  P(X=x, Y=y) = P(X=x)P(Y=y) if X and Y are discrete (for independent events)
- **Law of Total Probability**: P(A) = ∑ P(A|X=x)P(X=x)
- **Independence Theorem**: If X and Y are independent, then P(X=x, Y=y) = P(X=x)P(Y=y)

### Key Points

- A joint probability distribution describes the probability of two or more discrete random variables occurring together.
- The joint probability mass function represents the joint probability of two discrete random variables.
- The law of total probability and independence theorem are useful in calculating joint probabilities.
- Joint probability distributions can be used to model complex systems and events.

### Important Formulas

- P(X=x, Y=y) for continuous variables: P(X=x)P(Y=y|X=x)
- P(X=x, Y=y) for discrete X and Y: P(X=x)P(Y=y|X=x)
- P(X=x, Y=y) for independent X and Y: P(X=x)P(Y=y)
- Law of Total Probability: P(A) = ∑ P(A|X=x)P(X=x)
