# **Central Limit Theorem and Confidence Limits for Unknown Mean**

## **Key Points**

### Definitions

- **Central Limit Theorem (CLT)**: States that the distribution of sample means approaches a normal distribution as the sample size increases, regardless of the population distribution.
- **Confidence Interval**: A range of values within which a population parameter is likely to lie with a specified level of confidence.

### Formulas

- **CLT Formula**: $\bar{X} \sim N(\mu, \sigma^2/n)$, where $\bar{X}$ is the sample mean, $\mu$ is the population mean, $\sigma^2$ is the population variance, and $n$ is the sample size.
- **Confidence Interval Formula**: $(\bar{X} - z_{\alpha/2} \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}, \bar{X} + z_{\alpha/2} \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}})$, where $z_{\alpha/2}$ is the z-score corresponding to the desired confidence level.

### Theorems

- **Chebyshev's Inequality**: $P(|\bar{X} - \mu| \geq k) \leq \frac{\sigma^2}{k^2}$, where $\bar{X}$ is the sample mean, $\mu$ is the population mean, $\sigma^2$ is the population variance, and $k$ is a positive constant.
- **Decision Theory**: The optimal decision rule is to reject the null hypothesis if the test statistic exceeds a certain critical value.

### Important Concepts

- **Sample Size**: The number of observations in a sample.
- **Population Variance**: The average of the squared differences from the mean.
- **Standard Error**: The standard deviation of the sampling distribution of the sample mean.
- **Z-Score**: A measure of how many standard deviations an element is from the mean.

### Important Constants

- **Alpha**: The significance level of the test, usually set to 0.05.
- **Z-Score Table**: A table of z-scores corresponding to specific confidence levels.

### Key Concepts to Remember

- The CLT states that the distribution of sample means approaches a normal distribution as the sample size increases.
- Confidence intervals provide a range of values within which a population parameter is likely to lie with a specified level of confidence.
- The formula for the confidence interval involves the sample mean, standard error, and z-score.
