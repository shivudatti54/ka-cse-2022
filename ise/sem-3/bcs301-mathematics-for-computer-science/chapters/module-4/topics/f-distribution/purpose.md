# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define the F-distribution and explain its mathematical relationship to chi-square distributions
2. Identify the key properties of F-distribution including domain, skewness, and dependence on degrees of freedom
3. Calculate the F-statistic for comparing two population variances and interpret the results
4. Use F-tables to find critical values for hypothesis testing at specified significance levels
5. Apply F-distribution in one-way ANOVA for testing equality of multiple group means
6. Understand the connection between F-distribution and Student's t-distribution
7. Apply F-distribution in regression analysis for testing overall model significance
8. Verify the assumptions underlying valid F-tests and recognize their importance in statistical inference
