# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Distinguish between population parameters and sample statistics, understanding that statistics are random variables while parameters are fixed constants.

2. Explain the concept of simple random sampling and the i.i.d. (independently and identically distributed) assumption.

3. Derive and interpret the expected value and variance of the sample mean, understanding its properties as an unbiased estimator.

4. Compute and interpret the sample variance, understanding why (n-1) degrees of freedom provide an unbiased estimator of population variance.

5. Calculate the standard error of the mean and explain its interpretation as a measure of precision.

6. Describe the sampling distribution of the sample mean for normal populations, including the specific normal distribution it follows.

7. Apply the chi-square distribution to characterize the sampling distribution of the sample variance.

8. Determine appropriate sample sizes for achieving desired precision in estimation problems.
