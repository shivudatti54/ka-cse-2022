# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. DEFINE the Student's t-distribution and explain its relationship to the standard normal distribution

2. IDENTIFY the parameter degrees of freedom and explain how it affects the shape of the t-distribution

3. CALCULATE the t-statistic for one-sample, two-sample independent, and paired t-tests

4. CONSTRUCT confidence intervals for population means when the population standard deviation is unknown

5. PERFORM hypothesis tests using the t-distribution and interpret the results in context

6. DISTINGUISH between situations requiring one-tailed and two-tailed t-tests

7. APPLY the appropriate degrees of freedom formula based on the experimental design

8. EXPLAIN the assumptions underlying t-tests and recognize when they are violated
