# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. EXPLAIN THE CONCEPT OF STORAGE HIERARCHY AND ITS IMPORTANCE IN COMPUTER SYSTEMS, INCLUDING THE TRADE-OFFS BETWEEN SPEED, COST, AND CAPACITY AT EACH LEVEL.

2. DESCRIBE THE PHYSICAL AND LOGICAL STRUCTURE OF MAGNETIC DISKS, INCLUDING TRACKS, SECTORS, CYLINDERS, AND LOGICAL BLOCK ADDRESSING.

3. ANALYZE AND COMPARE DIFFERENT DISK SCHEDULING ALGORITHMS (FCFS, SSTF, SCAN, C-SCAN, LOOK) BY CALCULATING TOTAL HEAD MOVEMENT AND EVALUATING THEIR PERFORMANCE CHARACTERISTICS.

4. EVALUATE THE THREE PRIMARY FILE ALLOCATION METHODS (CONTIGUOUS, LINKED, INDEXED) IN TERMS OF ACCESS TIME, SPACE EFFICIENCY, AND SUITABILITY FOR DIFFERENT WORKLOADS.

5. EXPLAIN HOW OPERATING SYSTEMS MANAGE FREE SPACE USING VARIOUS TECHNIQUES INCLUDING BIT VECTORS, LINKED LISTS, AND GROUPING METHODS.

6. APPLY CONCEPTS OF FILE SYSTEMS AND STORAGE MANAGEMENT TO SOLVE PRACTICAL PROBLEMS INVOLVING DISK ALLOCATION AND REQUEST SCHEDULING.

7. UNDERSTAND THE RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN STORAGE MANAGEMENT AND OTHER OPERATING SYSTEM FUNCTIONS SUCH AS MEMORY MANAGEMENT AND PROCESS SCHEDULING.

8. ANALYZE REAL-WORLD STORAGE SOLUTIONS AND SELECT APPROPRIATE TECHNIQUES BASED ON SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS AND PERFORMANCE CONSTRAINTS.
