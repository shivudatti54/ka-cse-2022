# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the various directory structure models including single-level, two-level, hierarchical, acyclic graph, and general graph structures, with their respective advantages and limitations.

2. Describe how directories are implemented using linear lists and hash tables, analyzing the time complexity of different lookup operations.

3. Understand the physical organization of disks including tracks, sectors, cylinders, and the concept of sector interleaving.

4. Analyze different disk scheduling algorithms (FCFS, SSTF, SCAN, C-SCAN, LOOK) and calculate the total head movement for given request sequences.

5. Explain free space management techniques including bit vectors, linked lists, grouping, and counting methods.

6. Understand disk partitioning and the role of partition tables in boot processes and operating system installation.

7. Apply your knowledge to solve numerical problems involving directory operations, disk allocation, and free space management.

8. Evaluate the trade-offs between different directory and disk structure implementations in terms of performance, complexity, and practical usage.
