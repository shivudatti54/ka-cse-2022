# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the physical structure of magnetic disks including platters, tracks, sectors, cylinders, and the relationship between these components.

2. Calculate average disk access time by considering seek time, rotational latency, and transfer time components.

3. Compare and contrast different disk scheduling algorithms including FCFS, SSTF, SCAN, C-SCAN, and LOOK with respect to performance and fairness.

4. Apply the SCAN and C-SCAN algorithms to determine the service order of disk I/O requests given a specific queue and initial head position.

5. Describe the purpose and operation of disk caches including write-through and write-back policies.

6. Evaluate different RAID levels (0, 1, 5, 6, 10) based on performance, fault tolerance, and storage efficiency for various application scenarios.

7. Analyze the impact of physical disk characteristics (RPM, transfer rate, cache size) on overall storage system performance.

8. Understand how solid-state drives differ from magnetic disks in terms of access time characteristics and why this matters for modern computing.
