# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define frames in the context of paging and explain their relationship to pages in virtual memory management.

2. Explain why proper frame allocation is critical for system performance and how it relates to page faults and thrashing.

3. Calculate the minimum number of frames required by a process based on the maximum memory references per instruction.

4. Apply equal allocation and proportional allocation strategies to determine frame distribution among processes.

5. Compare and contrast equal allocation, proportional allocation, and priority-based allocation with respect to efficiency and fairness.

6. Describe how the working set model dynamically allocates frames based on process behavior and its role in thrashing prevention.

7. Distinguish between local and global page replacement policies and explain their relationship to frame allocation.

8. Analyze scenarios and determine appropriate frame allocation strategies based on process characteristics and system constraints.
