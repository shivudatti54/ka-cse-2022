# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the critical section problem and its three fundamental requirements: mutual exclusion, progress, and bounded waiting.

2. Analyze Peterson's solution for two processes and demonstrate how it satisfies all requirements of the critical section problem.

3. Describe hardware synchronization primitives like TestAndSet and Swap instructions and explain how they provide atomic operations.

4. Implement process synchronization using semaphores, including both counting semaphores for resource management and binary semaphores for mutual exclusion.

5. Solve classical synchronization problems such as producer-consumer, readers-writers, and dining philosophers using appropriate synchronization mechanisms.

6. Distinguish between busy-wait synchronization and blocking synchronization, and identify scenarios where each approach is appropriate.

7. Evaluate the advantages and limitations of different synchronization approaches in terms of efficiency, complexity, and correctness.
