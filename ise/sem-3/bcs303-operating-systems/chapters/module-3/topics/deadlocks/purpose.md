# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define deadlock and explain its occurrence in operating systems with real-world examples
2. Identify and explain the four necessary conditions (Coffman conditions) for deadlock formation
3. Construct and analyze resource allocation graphs for deadlock detection in single and multiple resource instances
4. Apply the Banker's Algorithm to determine system safety and process resource requests
5. Compare and contrast deadlock prevention, avoidance, and detection strategies with their respective advantages and limitations
6. Design solutions for classical synchronization problems that avoid deadlock situations
7. Evaluate different deadlock recovery approaches and determine appropriate strategies for specific system requirements
8. Analyze system states to identify unsafe states that may lead to deadlock conditions
