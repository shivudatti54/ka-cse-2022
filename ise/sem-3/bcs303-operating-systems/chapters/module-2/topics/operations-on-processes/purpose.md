Learning "Operations on processes" is crucial in understanding how operating systems manage and control processes, allowing for efficient resource allocation and smooth system operation. This topic matters because it explains how to create, manage, and terminate processes, enabling effective system management and troubleshooting. In real-world applications, this knowledge is applied in process scheduling, resource allocation, and debugging, making it essential for system administrators and developers. This concept also connects to other topics, such as process synchronization, memory management, and inter-process communication, highlighting its importance in understanding the overall behavior of operating systems.
