# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define the concept of a process and distinguish between a program and a process in operating systems
2. Explain the five fundamental process states and describe the state transitions that occur during process execution
3. Identify and describe the components of a Process Control Block (PCB) and its role in process management
4. Compare and contrast the functions of long-term, short-term, and medium-term schedulers in process scheduling
5. Describe the main operations performed on processes including creation, termination, and synchronization
6. Evaluate different Inter-Process Communication (IPC) mechanisms and their appropriate use cases
7. Explain the concept of threads and compare thread-based with process-based concurrent programming
8. Analyze the advantages of multithreading in terms of resource utilization, performance, and system responsiveness
