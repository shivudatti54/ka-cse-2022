# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define process scheduling and explain the roles of long-term, short-term, and medium-term schedulers in operating system process management.

2. Identify and describe the five major scheduling criteria: CPU utilization, throughput, turnaround time, waiting time, and response time.

3. Compare and contrast major scheduling algorithms including FCFS, Shortest Job First, Priority Scheduling, and Round Robin with respect to their performance characteristics.

4. Calculate waiting time, turnaround time, and average waiting time for given process sets under different scheduling algorithms.

5. Explain the convoy effect in FCFS scheduling and starvation in priority scheduling, including potential solutions for each problem.

6. Describe thread scheduling considerations and explain how thread-level scheduling differs from process-level scheduling in modern operating systems.

7. Discuss the challenges of scheduling in multiple-processor systems, including processor affinity and load balancing concepts.
