# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. DEFINE A PROCESS and distinguish it from a program with clear reasoning and examples

2. IDENTIFY AND EXPLAIN the five standard process states (new, ready, running, waiting, terminated) and the transitions between them

3. DESCRIBE THE STRUCTURE AND COMPONENTS of a Process Control Block (PCB) and explain its role in process management

4. EXPLAIN HOW PROCESSES are created in both Unix-like systems (fork/exec) and Windows (CreateProcess)

5. UNDERSTAND PROCESS TERMINATION including normal termination, abnormal termination, and the role of parent process in collecting exit status

6. CLASSIFY PROCESSES into different types (interactive, batch, daemon, real-time) based on their characteristics and usage

7. ANALYZE PROCESS STATE TRANSITIONS in real-world programs and predict the sequence of states a process goes through during execution

8. COMPARE AND CONTRAST processes with threads, recognizing the relationship and differences between these fundamental operating system abstractions
