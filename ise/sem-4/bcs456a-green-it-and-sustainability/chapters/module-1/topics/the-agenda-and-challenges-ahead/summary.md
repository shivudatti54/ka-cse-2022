# **The Agenda and Challenges Ahead**

### Key Points

- **Agenda:**
  - Reduce carbon footprint and mitigate climate change
  - Ensure sustainable IT infrastructure and operations
  - Promote digital inclusion and accessibility
  - Foster innovation and entrepreneurship in Green IT
- **Challenges:**
  - High upfront costs of adopting Green IT technologies
  - Limited awareness and education among IT professionals and end-users
  - Inadequate infrastructure and data center management
  - Balancing business needs with sustainability goals
  - Cybersecurity and data privacy concerns in Green IT

### Definitions

- **Green IT:** The practice of using information technology in a way that minimizes its negative environmental impact.
- **Sustainability:** The ability of a system or process to maintain its functionality and efficiency over time.

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Carbon Footprint:** The amount of greenhouse gas emissions produced by an organization, product, or activity.
- **Energy Efficiency Ratio (EER):** A measure of the energy efficiency of a server or data center.

### Key Concepts

- **Green IT metrics:**
  - Energy consumption (kWh)
  - Carbon footprint (kg CO2e)
  - Data center power density (W/sqft)
  - e-Waste generation rates
- **Green IT standards and certifications:**
  - ISO 14001 (Environmental Management)
  - ISO 26000 (Corporate Social Responsibility)
  - Energy Star (EPA)
