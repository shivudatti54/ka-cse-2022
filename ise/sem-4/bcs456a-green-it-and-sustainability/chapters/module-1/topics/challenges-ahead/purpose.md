### Learning Purpose: Module 1 - Challenges Ahead

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the challenges is the critical first step in developing effective Green IT strategies. The exponential growth in digital technology consumption creates a significant environmental burden, from energy-hungry data centers to electronic waste. This topic is vital because it frames the urgency of the problem, highlighting the economic, regulatory, and ethical pressures that make sustainable IT a business imperative, not just an environmental concern.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify and analyze the key challenges at the intersection of IT and sustainability. This includes the massive energy consumption and carbon footprint of digital infrastructure, the resource depletion and toxic waste problems associated with hardware lifecycles, and the looming regulatory and compliance landscape. They will evaluate the technical, economic, and behavioral barriers to implementing greener IT solutions.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This foundational topic sets the stage for the entire module and course. The challenges identified here directly inform the solutions explored later, such as green software design, energy-efficient hardware, circular economy models, and sustainable data center management. It connects IT principles with core business concepts like risk management, corporate social responsibility (CSR), and cost optimization.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is directly applicable for roles in IT management, sustainability consulting, and corporate strategy. Professionals can conduct audits to assess their organization's IT carbon footprint, develop policies for responsible e-waste disposal, prepare for upcoming environmental regulations, and build a business case for investing in energy-efficient technologies.
