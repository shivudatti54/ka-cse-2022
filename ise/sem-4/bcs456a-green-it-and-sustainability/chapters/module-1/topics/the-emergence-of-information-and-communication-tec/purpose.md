# Learning Purpose: The Emergence of Information and Communication Technologies (ICTs)

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the historical emergence of ICTs is foundational. It provides the crucial context for why modern digital infrastructure has such a significant environmental footprint. You cannot effectively address the sustainability challenges of today without first understanding the technological evolution that created them.

**2. What will students learn?**
You will learn to trace the key developmental milestones of ICTs, from early computing to the internet and cloud era. This includes analyzing the driving forces behind this rapid growth and its concomitant rise in energy consumption, resource use, and electronic waste (e-waste), establishing the core problem that Green IT aims to solve.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic sets the stage for the entire course. It directly connects to subsequent modules on the **environmental impact of data centers**, the **principles of sustainable design**, and **circular economy strategies** for hardware. It provides the "why" behind the need for the solutions explored later.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge allows you to critically assess the life cycle of technology products, understand the carbon cost of digital services, and appreciate the urgency for corporate IT departments to audit and improve the sustainability of their legacy systems and infrastructure.
