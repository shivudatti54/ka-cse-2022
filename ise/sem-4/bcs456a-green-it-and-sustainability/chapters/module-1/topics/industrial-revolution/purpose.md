### Learning Purpose: The Industrial Revolution

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the Industrial Revolution is foundational because it marks the origin of modern industry, mass production, and the large-scale environmental impact of human activity. It establishes the critical historical context for why sustainable practices are urgently needed in today's technology-driven world, directly linking past industrial actions to current climate challenges.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn about the key technological and societal shifts of the Industrial Revolution. They will analyze its role as a catalyst for unprecedented economic growth and, concurrently, the beginning of significant environmental degradation through resource depletion and pollution, setting the stage for the need for Green IT.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic provides the historical precedent for Module 1's subsequent concepts. It directly connects to the rise of the **Digital Revolution** (the next major technological shift) and creates the necessary backdrop for understanding the principles of **sustainability** and the emerging field of **Green IT**, which seeks to mitigate the environmental footprint of modern industry and technology.

**4. Real-world applications**
This historical knowledge allows students to critically assess the life-cycle of modern technology, from manufacturing to disposal. It provides a framework for advocating corporate social responsibility, designing energy-efficient data centers, and developing policies that aim to decouple economic progress from environmental harm.
