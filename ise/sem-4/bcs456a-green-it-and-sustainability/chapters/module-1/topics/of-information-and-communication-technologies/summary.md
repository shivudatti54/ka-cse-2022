# Of Information and Communication Technologies (ICT)

=====================================================

## Definitions and Key Concepts

---

- **Information and Communication Technologies (ICTs)**: Refers to the use of technology to process, store, and communicate information.
- **ICTs**: Includes computer hardware, software, networking, and other related technologies.
- **Green ICT**: Focuses on reducing the environmental impact of ICTs.

## History of ICT

---

- **Industrial Revolution (18th-19th century)**: Marked the beginning of the ICT era with the introduction of telegraphy and telephony.
- **Computer Age (20th century)**: Saw the development of mainframe computers, minicomputers, and personal computers.
- **Internet and Mobile Revolution (late 20th century)**: Revolutionized communication and information exchange.

## Theories and Formulas

---

- ** Shannon's Theorem**: Defines the capacity of a communication channel: C = B \* L, where C is the channel capacity, B is the bandwidth, and L is the signal-to-noise ratio.
- **Bandwidth**: Measured in bits per second (bps) or hertz (Hz).

## Challenges Ahead

---

- **Digital Divide**: The gap between those with access to ICTs and those without.
- **Energy Consumption**: The environmental impact of ICTs, including e-waste and greenhouse gas emissions.
- **Cybersecurity**: Protecting ICT systems from unauthorized access and malicious attacks.

## Important Figures and Organizations

---

- **Alan Turing**: Developed the theoretical foundations of modern computer science.
- **Tim Berners-Lee**: Invented the World Wide Web.
- **United Nations**: Established the Global e-Framework to promote ICT for development.

## Quick Revision Tips

---

- Understand the history and evolution of ICTs.
- Familiarize yourself with key concepts and theories.
- Be aware of the challenges facing the ICT sector.
- Review definitions and formulas regularly.
