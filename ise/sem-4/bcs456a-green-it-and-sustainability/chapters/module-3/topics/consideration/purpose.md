# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1.  Differentiate between the Frye and Daubert standards for admissibility of scientific evidence.
2.  Explain the purpose and critical components of a Chain of Custody document.
3.  Identify the key Federal Rules of Evidence (FRE) that govern digital evidence (e.g., 402, 702, 901, 1002).
4.  Describe the ethical responsibilities and role of a digital forensic examiner as an expert witness.
5.  List common legal challenges to the admissibility of digital evidence and strategies to overcome them.
6.  Articulate how the Fourth Amendment's particularity requirement applies to digital search warrants.
