### Learning Purpose: Module 3 - ICT Technical Measures

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is critical because Information and Communication Technology (ICT) is a significant and growing consumer of global energy and a contributor to e-waste. Understanding the technical measures to mitigate this impact is essential for developing sustainable digital infrastructure and practices, ensuring that technological progress does not come at an unsustainable environmental cost.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core technical strategies and technologies used to improve the energy efficiency and reduce the environmental footprint of ICT systems. This includes principles of hardware efficiency (e.g., component selection, power management), software optimization for lower resource consumption, data center cooling techniques, and the concepts of virtualization and server consolidation.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module builds directly on the foundational concepts of Green IT introduced in earlier modules. It provides the technical "how-to" that operationalizes the broader sustainability policies and strategies discussed previously. It also sets the stage for later modules on metrics, reporting, and green software development, forming the practical engineering core of the subject.

**4. Real-world applications**
The knowledge gained is directly applicable to roles in IT management, systems administration, and cloud architecture. Students will be equipped to specify energy-efficient hardware, design optimized code, manage sustainable data centers, and implement virtualization solutions—skills highly valued by organizations aiming to reduce operational costs and meet corporate social responsibility (CSR) goals.
