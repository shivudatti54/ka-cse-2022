### Learning Purpose: Ecological Measures and Ethical Considerations

**1. Importance:**
This topic is critical as it moves beyond the technical specifications of Green IT to address its core purpose: minimizing the environmental footprint of the technology sector. It equips future IT professionals with the knowledge to evaluate the full ecological cost of digital solutions and to make ethically sound decisions that prioritize planetary health.

**2. Learning Outcomes:**
Students will learn to quantify the environmental impact of IT systems using key ecological measures like Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE), carbon accounting, and product lifecycles (from e-waste to sourcing). They will also explore the ethical dilemmas surrounding resource consumption, digital equity, and the responsibility of tech companies in fostering a sustainable future.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts:**
This module directly builds upon foundational knowledge of Green IT principles (Module 1) and sustainable design strategies (Module 2). It provides the crucial ethical and analytical framework needed to implement the technical solutions discussed throughout the course, connecting hardware efficiency, software design, and data center management to their ultimate real-world consequences.

**4. Real-World Applications:**
This knowledge is applied when selecting cloud providers based on their PUE, designing for energy efficiency and repairability, developing responsible e-waste policies, and advocating for corporate strategies that balance profit with ecological and social responsibility.
