# Systems Engineering for Designing Sustainable ICT-Based Architectures

=====================================

## Overview

---

- Systems engineering is a holistic approach to designing and managing complex systems.
- Sustainable ICT-based architectures aim to minimize environmental impact while meeting business requirements.

## Key Concepts

---

- **Systems Engineering Methodology**: A structured approach to designing, building, and maintaining complex systems.
- **System Dynamics**: The study of how complex systems change over time.
- **Life Cycle Assessment (LCA)**: A method for evaluating the environmental impacts of a product or system throughout its entire life cycle.
- **Circular Economy**: An economic model that aims to keep resources in use for as long as possible, extracting the maximum value from them.

## Design Principles

---

- **Reduced Energy Consumption**: Minimize energy consumption through efficient design and operation.
- **Minimized E-Waste**: Design for recyclability and reuse of electronic components.
- **Sustainable Materials**: Use sustainable materials in system design and construction.
- **Data-Driven Decision Making**: Use data analytics to inform system design and optimize performance.

## Important Formulas and Definitions

---

- **Carbon Footprint**: The amount of greenhouse gases emitted by a system or product.
- **Energy Payback Period (EPB)**: The time it takes for a system to generate enough energy to power its own operation.
- **Systemic Thinking**: A holistic approach to problem-solving that considers the entire system, not just its individual components.
- **Triple Bottom Line (TBL)**: A framework for measuring a system's performance using economic, social, and environmental metrics.

## Theorems and Laws

---

- **Zero-Waste Design Principle**: A design approach that aims to eliminate waste and minimize environmental impact.
- **Zero-Energy Building Principle**: A design approach that aims to design buildings that produce as much energy as they consume.
- **Stephen B. Irvine's Theorem**: States that the energy consumption of a system is proportional to the square of its output.
