# Learning Purpose: Dematerialization of the Product Chain

**1. Why is this topic important?**
The dematerialization of the product chain is a core tenet of Green IT, addressing the massive environmental footprint of physical goods. By shifting from atoms to bits, we can drastically reduce resource extraction, manufacturing waste, transportation emissions, and electronic waste (e-waste). This topic is crucial for understanding how to decouple economic growth from environmental degradation and achieve true sustainability.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define dematerialization and analyze strategies for its implementation. This includes studying concepts like product-to-service shifts (e.g., streaming media), virtualization, cloud computing, and the development of digital twins. They will evaluate the life-cycle environmental benefits and potential rebound effects of these digital solutions.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds on foundational Green IT principles from Module 1. It connects to life cycle assessment (LCA) by providing a method to reduce impact at the source. It also ties into circular economy models, sustainable design, and green software engineering, showing how digitalization is a key enabler for these broader concepts.

**4. Real-world applications**
Real-world applications are pervasive. Students will analyze companies like Netflix (dematerializing DVDs), Spotify (music), and Zoom (business travel). They will also examine how CAD software and digital prototypes dematerialize manufacturing design phases and how cloud platforms replace physical servers, reducing energy and material use.
