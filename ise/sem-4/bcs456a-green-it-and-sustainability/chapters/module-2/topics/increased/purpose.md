### Learning Purpose: Increased Focus on Green IT and Sustainability

**1. Why is this topic important?**
The ICT sector's energy consumption and environmental footprint are substantial and growing. This topic is critical because it addresses the direct link between technological advancement and its ecological cost. Understanding how to mitigate this impact is no longer optional but a core business, ethical, and regulatory imperative for any organization relying on IT infrastructure.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to quantify the environmental impact of IT operations, focusing on key metrics like Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE) and carbon emissions. They will analyze strategies to increase efficiency in data centers, hardware manufacturing, and end-user computing. This includes evaluating energy-efficient hardware, optimizing software algorithms, and implementing sustainable IT procurement and lifecycle management policies.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds on foundational Green IT principles from Module 1. It connects to cloud computing (e.g., sustainable cloud providers), circular economy models (e-waste recycling), corporate social responsibility (CSR) reporting, and ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) criteria. It also provides the measurable outcomes for the strategies introduced in subsequent topics like green software development.

**4. Real-world applications**
Students will be equipped to conduct basic energy audits of IT systems, make data-driven recommendations for reducing a company's digital carbon footprint, and contribute to sustainability reports. This knowledge is applicable to roles in IT management, operations, consulting, and policy development, helping organizations achieve both cost savings and sustainability goals.
