### **Learning Purpose: Functionality in Green IT**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding functionality is crucial because it shifts the sustainability conversation from simply "using less" to "using smarter." It challenges the notion that green IT means compromised performance, instead framing efficiency and reduced environmental impact as core features of a well-designed system. This mindset is fundamental for creating sustainable tech solutions that are both ecologically sound and economically competitive.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to analyze and evaluate IT systems (hardware, software, networks) through the lens of functional efficiency. This includes key metrics like performance-per-watt, power usage effectiveness (PUE), and the principles of energy-proportional computing. They will explore design strategies such as virtualization, resource pooling, and algorithmic efficiency that maximize output while minimizing resource consumption and electronic waste.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the practical bridge between the high-level **goals of Green IT** (covered in Module 1) and the specific **technologies and lifecycle management** that follow. It directly applies the principle of sustainability to core system design. Functionality is also deeply intertwined with **economics** (operational cost savings) and **corporate social responsibility**.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied in designing energy-efficient data centers, developing low-power software algorithms, optimizing cloud computing resource allocation, and selecting hardware based on total cost of ownership (TCO) rather than just upfront price. It is essential for roles in IT procurement, systems architecture, cloud engineering, and sustainable tech consulting.
