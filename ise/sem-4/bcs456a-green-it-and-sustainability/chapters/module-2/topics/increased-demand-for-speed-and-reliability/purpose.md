### Learning Purpose: Increased Demand for Speed and Reliability

**1. Why is this topic important?**
The exponential growth in data consumption, cloud services, and real-time applications places immense pressure on IT infrastructure. This demand directly conflicts with sustainability goals, as more powerful data centers and networks consume vast amounts of energy. Understanding this tension is crucial for developing IT strategies that balance performance with environmental responsibility.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will analyze the environmental cost of high-performance computing and 24/7 availability. They will learn to evaluate the energy efficiency of different infrastructure components (e.g., processors, storage, cooling systems) and explore strategies to decouple performance from power consumption, such as hardware optimization, virtualization, and advanced cooling techniques.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly on foundational Green IT principles (Module 1) by applying them to a core business driver: performance. It provides the critical link between energy-efficient hardware, software design (e.g., lean coding), and sustainable data center management, which are explored in subsequent modules.

**4. Real-world applications**
Students will be able to assess the carbon footprint of a cloud migration project, propose energy-efficient architectures for high-traffic web applications, and justify investments in more efficient, albeit sometimes more expensive, server hardware based on total cost of ownership and sustainability ROI.
