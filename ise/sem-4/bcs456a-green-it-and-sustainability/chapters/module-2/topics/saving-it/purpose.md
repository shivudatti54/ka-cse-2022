### Learning Purpose: Module 2 - Saving IT

**1. Why is this topic important?**
The IT sector is a significant and growing consumer of global energy and a contributor to electronic waste. "Saving IT" is crucial because it directly addresses the environmental footprint of technology by focusing on energy efficiency, resource conservation, and lifecycle management. Understanding how to reduce this impact is fundamental for any organization aiming to lower costs, comply with regulations, and achieve genuine sustainability goals.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn practical strategies to improve the energy efficiency of data centers, hardware, and software. This includes exploring concepts like power management settings, virtualization, server consolidation, and cloud computing efficiencies. They will also examine methods for extending hardware lifespan through proper maintenance and responsible end-of-life recycling and disposal practices.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is a direct application of the core Green IT principles introduced in Module 1. It connects hardware efficiency (Module 3) with the energy-saving potential of software and data management (Module 4). The principles learned here are also the foundation for larger strategic decisions, such as implementing sustainable software development practices and building a circular economy model for IT assets.

**4. Real-world applications**
Learners will be equipped to conduct basic energy audits of IT infrastructure, recommend specific power-saving configurations for devices, and develop policies for sustainable procurement and e-waste management. These skills are directly applicable in roles like IT management, systems administration, corporate sustainability, and green consulting.
