# Learning Purpose: Intelligent Energy Metering

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Intelligent Energy Metering is a critical foundation for Green IT because you cannot manage what you cannot measure. It provides the granular, real-time data necessary to understand and quantify the energy consumption of IT infrastructure and broader organizational facilities. This data is essential for identifying waste, establishing baselines, and validating the effectiveness of sustainability initiatives, moving beyond estimates to actionable insights.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core principles, components, and technologies behind intelligent metering systems, including sensors, IoT devices, and data analytics platforms. They will understand how to interpret energy usage data to identify trends, anomalies, and opportunities for efficiency gains. The module will also cover strategies for implementing a metering system and using its data to inform decision-making for cost and carbon reduction.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly connects to other Green IT concepts like energy-efficient hardware (providing data to justify upgrades), data center cooling optimization (pinpointing hotspots), and sustainable cloud computing (comparing provider efficiency). It provides the empirical evidence needed to support broader corporate sustainability reporting and Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) goals.

**4. Real-world applications**
Applications are widespread, from monitoring individual servers and entire data centers to tracking energy use across smart buildings and manufacturing floors. Companies use this data for internal carbon accounting, implementing automated load-shifting, achieving compliance with environmental standards, and creating transparent sustainability reports for stakeholders.
