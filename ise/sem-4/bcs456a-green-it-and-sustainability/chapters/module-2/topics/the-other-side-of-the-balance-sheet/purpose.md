### Learning Purpose: The Other Side of the Balance Sheet

**1. Why is this topic important?**
While much of Green IT focuses on reducing operational costs (the 'asset' side), this topic is crucial because it addresses the significant, and often hidden, environmental and social _liabilities_ created by the technology industry. Understanding the full lifecycle impact—from manufacturing to disposal—is essential for a holistic approach to sustainability.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify and assess the negative externalities of IT, including the carbon footprint of device manufacturing, supply chain ethics, e-waste management, and the resource consumption of data centers. They will analyze how these factors represent risks and potential costs that must be managed on an organization's "balance sheet."

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds on foundational Green IT principles (Module 1) by providing a more complete picture of a product's lifecycle. It connects to circular economy models, corporate social responsibility (CSR), and environmental, social, and governance (ESG) reporting frameworks, showing how liability management is integral to sustainable business strategy.

**4. Real-world applications**
Learners will be able to apply this knowledge to conduct more accurate product life cycle assessments (LCAs), develop responsible IT procurement and disposal policies, and contribute to transparent sustainability reporting that accounts for a company's entire environmental impact.
