### Learning Purpose: Videoconference as an Alternative to Business Travel

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is critical as business travel is a major contributor to an organization's carbon footprint and operational costs. Understanding and implementing effective videoconferencing strategies is a direct application of Green IT, allowing companies to maintain productivity while drastically reducing their environmental impact and achieving sustainability goals.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to quantify the environmental and economic savings of substituting travel with videoconferencing. They will evaluate different collaboration technologies, identify best practices for effective virtual meetings, and assess the limitations and challenges of this alternative, such as building rapport and "Zoom fatigue."

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly connects to core Green IT principles like measuring carbon footprint (Scope 3 emissions) and Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) of IT equipment. It also ties into broader Module 2 concepts on sustainable procurement (choosing green AV vendors) and the role of IT in enabling circular economy models through dematerialization.

**4. Real-world applications**
Students can apply this knowledge to conduct a cost-benefit analysis for their organization, develop a corporate travel policy that promotes virtual alternatives, and make informed decisions on investing in telepresence robotics or high-quality AV infrastructure to reduce the need for physical travel.
