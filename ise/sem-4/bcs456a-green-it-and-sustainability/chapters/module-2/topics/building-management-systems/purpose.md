### Learning Purpose: Building Management Systems

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Building Management Systems (BMS) are critical to Green IT because buildings are massive consumers of energy. A BMS centrally controls a building's operational equipment, such as HVAC, lighting, and power systems. Optimizing this control through IT is one of the most effective ways to drastically reduce a building's energy consumption and carbon footprint, directly contributing to organizational sustainability goals.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core components and architecture of a modern BMS. They will explore how sensors, actuators, and controllers are integrated with software to monitor and manage energy usage in real-time. The module will cover key strategies for using a BMS to improve efficiency, including data analytics for identifying waste, automating systems based on occupancy, and implementing predictive maintenance.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly connects to the core principles of Green IT, such as energy-efficient computing and data-driven decision-making. It applies the IoT (Internet of Things) concept by networking physical devices to collect and act on data. Understanding BMS is also foundational for grasping larger smart city infrastructures and corporate Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) reporting.

**4. Real-world applications**
Graduates will be able to design and recommend BMS strategies for smart buildings, data centers, and corporate campuses. They will understand how to leverage system data to comply with environmental regulations, achieve energy efficiency certifications like LEED, and significantly lower operational costs, making them valuable assets in facilities management, IT, and sustainability consulting roles.
