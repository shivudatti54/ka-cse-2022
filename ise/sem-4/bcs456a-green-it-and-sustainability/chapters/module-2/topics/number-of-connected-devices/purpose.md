### Learning Purpose: The Environmental Impact of Connected Devices

**1. Why is this topic important?**
The explosive growth of connected devices (IoT, smartphones, sensors) is a cornerstone of digital transformation, but it carries a significant, often hidden, environmental cost. Understanding this impact is crucial because the collective energy consumption, resource extraction for manufacturing, and electronic waste (e-waste) from billions of devices directly challenge the goals of Green IT and corporate sustainability. This topic moves the discussion from abstract principles to a tangible, quantifiable issue.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to quantify the lifecycle environmental impact of connected devices, from raw material extraction to end-of-life disposal. They will analyze the energy efficiency of devices and network infrastructure and evaluate strategies for sustainable device management, including responsible procurement, power management, longevity design, and circular economy principles for e-waste.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly connects to broader Green IT concepts like data center efficiency (which devices rely on) and sustainable software design (which impacts device performance). It also serves as a critical application of core sustainability principles, such as Lifecycle Assessment (LCA) and carbon accounting, providing a measurable metric for corporate ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) reporting.

**4. Real-world applications**
Learners will be able to audit an organization's device fleet to identify carbon hotspots, develop policies for greener procurement and device usage, and design strategies to extend product lifecycles and reduce e-waste. This is applicable to roles in IT management, sustainability consulting, supply chain logistics, and corporate strategy.
