# Learning Purpose: Sustainable Applications of Cloud Computing

**1. Why is this topic important?**
The exponential growth of digital infrastructure has made the IT sector a significant contributor to global energy consumption and carbon emissions. Understanding how cloud computing can be leveraged sustainably is crucial for future IT professionals to help organizations reduce their environmental impact while maintaining technological competitiveness.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core principles of Green IT as applied to cloud environments. They will analyze how cloud models (IaaS, PaaS, SaaS) promote resource efficiency through server consolidation, dynamic provisioning, and high utilization rates. They will also evaluate the environmental trade-offs of cloud computing, including the energy consumption of massive data centers and e-waste reduction.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds on fundamental knowledge of cloud computing models (from Module 2) and core Green IT principles like energy efficiency and circular economy (from Module 1). It provides a practical application for theoretical concepts, showing how leveraging shared, scalable resources is a key strategy for achieving corporate sustainability goals.

**4. Real-world applications**
Students will be able to assess and recommend sustainable cloud strategies, such as migrating on-premise servers to more efficient cloud providers, utilizing carbon-aware computing (shifting workloads to times/regions with greener energy), and selecting vendors committed to powering data centers with renewable energy sources.
