### Learning Purpose: Challenges in the Use of Cloud Computing As Green Technology

#### 1. Why is this topic important?

While cloud computing is often marketed as an energy-efficient "green" technology, its environmental impact is complex. This topic is critical for developing a realistic, nuanced understanding of Green IT, moving beyond simplistic claims to analyze the significant challenges that can undermine cloud computing's sustainability benefits.

#### 2. What will students learn?

Students will learn to identify and evaluate the key challenges in positioning cloud computing as a green technology. This includes analyzing the substantial energy consumption of massive data centers, the carbon intensity of their energy sources, the environmental cost of electronic waste from supporting hardware, and the phenomenon of rebound growth (the Jevons Paradox) where efficiency gains lead to increased overall consumption.

#### 3. How does it connect to other concepts?

This topic directly builds on prior knowledge of cloud computing fundamentals and core Green IT principles (Module 1). It connects to concepts like Life Cycle Assessment (LCA), carbon accounting, and corporate ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) strategies. It provides a critical counterpoint to the topic of "Green Data Centers," allowing for a more balanced and comprehensive view.

#### 4. Real-world applications

Students will be able to critically assess the green claims of cloud service providers (e.g., AWS, Azure, Google Cloud). This skill is essential for IT managers making procurement decisions, sustainability officers developing net-zero strategies, and policymakers crafting regulations to ensure the digital economy's growth is truly sustainable.
