### Learning Purpose: Runtime Energy Consumption Basics

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding runtime energy consumption is fundamental because it directly translates to the environmental impact and operational cost of software. Inefficient code can lead to massive, unnecessary energy use across data centers and devices, contradicting the core goals of Green IT. This knowledge empowers developers to write responsible, sustainable code.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define runtime energy consumption and identify the key hardware components (CPU, memory, storage, network) that contribute to it. They will explore fundamental programming concepts and patterns that lead to high energy use (e.g., inefficient loops, redundant calculations) and be introduced to basic principles for writing more energy-efficient algorithms.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the practical bridge between theoretical sustainability goals and technical implementation. It connects directly to hardware efficiency (from earlier modules), software design patterns, algorithm complexity (Big O notation), and lays the groundwork for measuring and optimizing carbon efficiency (in later topics).

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is directly applicable to designing and developing any software, from cloud-native applications and AI models to mobile apps and embedded systems. Optimizing for lower energy consumption reduces electricity costs, extends battery life, and minimizes the carbon footprint of digital products.
