### Learning Purpose: Direct Effects of Green IT

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the direct environmental effects of IT is crucial because the sector is a significant and growing consumer of energy and natural resources. It is responsible for a substantial portion of global carbon emissions, from manufacturing devices to powering vast data centers. This topic moves beyond theory to examine the tangible, measurable impact of technology on our planet, forming the essential foundation for any sustainability effort within the industry.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify and quantify the primary direct environmental impacts of information technology. This includes analyzing the energy consumption of hardware (like servers and end-user devices), the associated carbon emissions from electricity use, and the resource depletion and electronic waste (e-waste) generated throughout a device's lifecycle, from raw material extraction to disposal.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic provides the factual basis that underpins other modules. The direct effects are the "problem" that the strategies discussed throughout the course—such as green software engineering (Module 3), sustainable design (Module 4), and circular economy models (later modules)—aim to "solve." It directly links technical IT operations to their environmental consequences.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied when conducting carbon accounting for an IT department, performing life-cycle assessments (LCA) on products, making data-driven decisions about hardware refresh cycles, and designing energy-efficient data center cooling systems. It is the critical first step in developing a credible corporate sustainability report and compliance with environmental regulations.
