### Learning Purpose: Green IT and Sustainability

**1. Why is this topic important?**
The ICT sector is a significant and growing consumer of energy and a contributor to global emissions. This topic is crucial as it equips future IT professionals with the knowledge to mitigate the environmental impact of technology, ensuring it becomes a driver for sustainability rather than a liability.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to analyze the environmental footprint of IT infrastructure, from data centers to end-user devices. They will explore strategies for improving energy efficiency, adopting circular economy principles for e-waste, and leveraging technology like AI and cloud computing to solve environmental challenges.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module synthesizes concepts from across the course, including energy-efficient hardware design (Module 2), sustainable software development (Module 3), and green data management (Module 4). It provides the strategic framework for applying these technical skills to achieve broader corporate social responsibility and environmental goals.

**4. Real-world applications**
Graduates will be able to conduct carbon accounting for IT assets, design and implement Green IT policies for organizations, and advise on sustainable technology procurement and lifecycle management, making them valuable assets in any modern enterprise.
