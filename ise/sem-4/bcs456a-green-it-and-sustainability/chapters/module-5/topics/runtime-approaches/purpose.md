### Learning Purpose: Runtime Approaches

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Runtime approaches are critical because the operational phase of an IT system is typically its most energy-intensive. Optimizing resource usage, power management, and workload efficiency during runtime directly reduces energy consumption, carbon emissions, and operational costs, which are core objectives of Green IT.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn key strategies to minimize the environmental impact of running software and hardware. This includes techniques like dynamic voltage and frequency scaling (DVFS), efficient resource provisioning, containerization, and algorithms for effective workload distribution and consolidation to maximize utilization and minimize idle power waste.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly upon earlier module concepts like energy-efficient hardware design and sustainable software development. The strategies learned here are the practical application of those principles, putting them into action once a system is live. It also connects to broader sustainability goals, life-cycle assessment (LCA), and metrics for measuring operational efficiency.

**4. Real-world applications**
These techniques are applied daily in modern data centers and cloud computing platforms (e.g., AWS, Azure, Google Cloud) through automated scaling, server consolidation, and advanced power management. They are also crucial for developers creating efficient, scalable applications and for system administrators optimizing on-premise server performance and energy use.
