### 1. Why is this topic important?

This topic is critically important because the IT sector is a significant and growing consumer of energy and producer of electronic waste. Understanding Green IT is essential for developing sustainable practices that reduce the environmental footprint of technology, cut operational costs, and ensure compliance with increasing environmental regulations.

### 2. What will students learn?

Students will learn the core principles of a sustainable approach to IT. This includes strategies for energy-efficient data center design, responsible device lifecycle management (from green procurement to e-waste recycling), and the application of virtualization and cloud computing to consolidate resources and minimize energy consumption.

### 3. How does it connect to other concepts?

This topic directly builds on foundational IT concepts like networking, data management, and hardware. It connects the technical operation of IT systems to broader business and ethical concepts such as Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR), environmental policy, supply chain management, and the economic imperative of cost reduction through efficiency.

### 4. Real-world applications

The principles learned are applied in designing LEED-certified data centers, implementing corporate IT asset disposal programs, optimizing software for lower energy use, and making strategic decisions to adopt cloud services or utilize renewable energy sources for powering technology infrastructure.
