### Learning Purpose: Sustainability and the Product Life Cycle

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the product life cycle is fundamental to Green IT. It shifts the focus from a product's use-phase to its entire lifespan—from raw material extraction and manufacturing to disposal or recycling. This holistic view is crucial for identifying and mitigating a product's most significant environmental impacts, which often occur outside the office. It is the cornerstone of designing for sustainability, circularity, and reduced electronic waste.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to deconstruct the five main stages of the IT product life cycle: raw material acquisition, manufacturing, distribution, use, and end-of-life. They will analyze the environmental footprint (e.g., carbon emissions, energy use, e-waste) associated with each stage and explore strategies for improvement, such as Design for Environment (DfE), extending product longevity, and implementing responsible recycling and take-back programs.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon earlier concepts like carbon accounting and energy efficiency by providing a framework to apply them. It connects forward to corporate social responsibility (CSR) and green procurement policies, as the life cycle perspective is essential for making sustainable purchasing decisions and evaluating vendor claims (e.g., EPEAT certification).

**4. Real-world applications**
Students will be able to apply this knowledge to conduct life cycle assessments (LCAs), develop more sustainable IT procurement policies, and contribute to product design and corporate sustainability strategies that minimize environmental harm from cradle to grave.
