### Learning Purpose: Sustainability During Use

**1. Why is this topic important?**
The operational ("use") phase of an IT asset's lifecycle is its most energy-intensive, accounting for the majority of its carbon footprint. Understanding how to minimize energy consumption during this phase is critical for reducing the environmental impact of the tech sector and achieving corporate sustainability goals.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify key energy-consuming components within data centers, networks, and end-user devices. They will analyze and apply strategies for improving efficiency, including hardware optimization (e.g., dynamic voltage scaling), virtualization, cloud computing models, and the development of energy-efficient software algorithms.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon lifecycle assessment (LCA) from earlier modules, focusing on the most impactful phase. It is the practical application of Green IT principles, connecting hardware design (Module 3) to operational execution and tying energy efficiency directly to cost savings and regulatory compliance (Module 2).

**4. Real-world applications**
Learners will be able to evaluate and recommend actions for real-world scenarios, such as optimizing data center cooling (PUE), implementing power management policies across a company's device fleet, and selecting efficient cloud services based on workload requirements.
