# Learning Purpose: Module 5 - Overview and Scope of Green IT and Sustainability

### 1. Why is this topic important?

The ICT sector is a significant and growing consumer of global energy and a contributor to carbon emissions and electronic waste. Understanding the scope of Green IT is crucial because it provides the foundational knowledge needed to assess the environmental impact of technology and identify opportunities for creating sustainable, efficient, and responsible business practices.

### 2. What will students learn?

Students will learn to define Green IT and differentiate it from related concepts like Green _by_ IT. They will identify the key pillars of Green IT, including energy-efficient hardware, sustainable software design, responsible e-waste management, and green data centers. The module will establish the boundaries of the field, covering the entire technology lifecycle from design and manufacturing to usage and disposal.

### 3. How does it connect to other concepts?

This overview serves as the cornerstone for the entire course. It connects directly to subsequent modules on specific strategies like cloud computing (Module 6), e-waste recycling (Module 7), and measuring sustainability through metrics (Module 8). It also links business strategy (Module 1) with the technical execution of sustainable practices.

### 4. Real-world applications

This knowledge allows students to conduct initial audits of an organization's IT carbon footprint, contribute to corporate social responsibility (CSR) reports, and make informed decisions about purchasing Energy Star-rated equipment, implementing power management settings, or selecting vendors with strong take-back programs for obsolete hardware.
