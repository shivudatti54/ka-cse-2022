### **Learning Purpose: Evaluating Sustainability Effects**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding how to accurately evaluate the sustainability effects of IT systems is crucial. It moves beyond vague "green" claims to provide measurable, data-driven evidence of environmental impact. This is fundamental for making responsible decisions, ensuring regulatory compliance, meeting stakeholder expectations, and driving genuine progress toward sustainability goals, rather than just "greenwashing."

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to apply key frameworks and metrics—such as Life Cycle Assessment (LCA), Carbon Accounting, and Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE)—to quantitatively assess the environmental footprint of IT operations. This includes evaluating both the direct effects of data centers and devices and the broader, systemic enablement effects where IT solutions can reduce emissions in other sectors.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic synthesizes earlier concepts: it uses the **principles of Green IT** (Module 1), applies **energy efficiency metrics** (Module 2), and relies on **data center and hardware knowledge** (Modules 3 & 4). It provides the analytical tools needed to validate the strategies discussed throughout the course, connecting technical implementation to business and environmental outcomes.

**4. Real-world applications**
This skill is directly applicable to roles in IT management, sustainability consulting, and corporate strategy. Graduates will be equipped to conduct IT carbon audits, report on ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) performance, design genuinely sustainable IT infrastructures, and evaluate the ROI of green technology investments for an organization.
