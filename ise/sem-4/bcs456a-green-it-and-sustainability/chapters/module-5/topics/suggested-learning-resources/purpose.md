### **Learning Purpose: Suggested Learning Resources**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Identifying credible resources is critical because Green IT is a rapidly evolving field rife with greenwashing. Effective research skills empower students to cut through marketing hype, stay updated on genuine sustainable practices (like carbon-aware computing or circular economy models), and make informed decisions based on verified data and standards, ensuring their future work has a real, positive environmental impact.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to critically evaluate and curate a toolkit of high-quality resources, including academic journals, industry reports (e.g., from EPA, W3C), key sustainability frameworks (e.g., ISO 14000, GHG Protocol), and professional organizations. They will develop the skills to distinguish between credible sources and greenwashing, ensuring their knowledge is built on accurate, current, and authoritative information.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the foundational research layer for the entire module. It directly connects to understanding energy-efficient coding practices, assessing the lifecycle of hardware, calculating carbon footprints, and evaluating the business case for sustainable IT initiatives. Mastery of these resources provides the evidence and methodologies needed to support all other technical and strategic concepts covered.

**4. Real-world applications**
These skills are directly applicable for writing sustainability reports, making procurement decisions for energy-efficient hardware, designing low-impact software, advising on corporate ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) strategy, and auditing IT operations for compliance with environmental standards and regulations.
