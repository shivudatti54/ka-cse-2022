### Learning Purpose: Optimizing the Energy Consumption of an Application

**1. Why is this topic important?**
The digital world's energy demand is soaring, contributing significantly to global carbon emissions. Optimizing application energy consumption is therefore a critical pillar of Green IT, directly reducing the environmental impact of technology. It also lowers operational costs and improves system performance, making it essential for both ethical and business reasons.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify the key factors that contribute to an application's energy usage, such as inefficient code, hardware resource utilization, and database query design. They will be equipped with practical strategies and tools to measure, analyze, and optimize energy efficiency throughout the software development lifecycle.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly on previous concepts like efficient algorithms (from computer science fundamentals), cloud computing resource management, and sustainable design principles. It synthesizes technical knowledge with the broader sustainability goals introduced at the start of the module, applying them to a practical, code-level context.

**4. Real-world applications**
These skills are immediately applicable in roles like Software Engineering, DevOps, and Solutions Architecture. Students will be able to develop greener software, optimize existing applications for cloud platforms to save on energy costs, and make data-driven decisions that align with corporate sustainability mandates and ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance) goals.
