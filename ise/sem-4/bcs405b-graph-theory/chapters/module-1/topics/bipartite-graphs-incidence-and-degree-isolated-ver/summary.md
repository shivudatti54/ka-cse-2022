# Bipartite Graphs

- **Definition:** A bipartite graph is a graph whose vertices can be divided into two disjoint sets U and V such that every edge connects a vertex in U to one in V.

### Incidence and Degree

- **Incidence:** The incidence of a vertex in a graph is the number of edges that pass through it.
- **Degree:** The degree of a vertex is the number of edges incident on it.

### Isolated Vertex

- **Definition:** An isolated vertex (or isolated node) is a vertex of a graph that is connected to no other vertex.
- **Properties:**
  - An isolated vertex has degree 0.
  - A bipartite graph with an isolated vertex is not bipartite.
- **Example:** A graph with a single vertex connected to no other vertex is an isolated vertex.

Theorem: A graph has an isolated vertex if and only if it has no odd cycles.
