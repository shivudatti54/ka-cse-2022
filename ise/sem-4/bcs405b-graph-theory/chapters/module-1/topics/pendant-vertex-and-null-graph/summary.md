# **Pendant Vertex and Null Graph**

### Definitions and Key Points

- **Pendant Vertex**: A vertex in a graph that has degree 1. It is connected to only one other vertex.
- **Null Graph**: A graph that has no edges.
- **Key Properties**:
  - A null graph is also known as an empty graph.
  - It has no edges, but it has a set of vertices.
  - A null graph is a graph with zero edges.

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Degree of a Vertex**: The number of edges incident on a vertex.
  - Formula: d(v) = |E| - |N(v)|
  - Where d(v) is the degree of vertex v, |E| is the number of edges, and |N(v)| is the number of neighbors of v.
- **Handshaking Lemma**: The sum of the degrees of all vertices in a graph is equal to twice the number of edges.
  - Formula: Σd(v) = 2|E|

### Important Theorems

- **Handshaking Lemma**: A fundamental theorem in graph theory that relates the sum of degrees of vertices to the number of edges.
- **Vizing's Theorem**: For any graph, the maximum degree of any vertex is less than or equal to twice the minimum degree of any vertex.
