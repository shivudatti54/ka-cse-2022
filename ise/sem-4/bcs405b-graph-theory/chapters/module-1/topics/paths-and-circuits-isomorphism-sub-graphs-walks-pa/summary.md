# Paths and Circuits in Graph Theory

=====================================

## Definitions and Formulas

- **Path**: A sequence of vertices and edges between them, where each edge is traversed only once.
- **Circuit**: A path that starts and ends at the same vertex.
- **Walk**: A sequence of vertices and edges between them, where an edge may be traversed more than once.
- **Isomorphism**: Two graphs are isomorphic if there exists a bijection between their vertex sets that preserves adjacency.
- **Sub-graph**: A graph that is a subset of another graph.

## Important Theorems

- **Isomorphism Theorem**: If two graphs are isomorphic, then they have the same number of vertices, edges, and components.
- **Hamiltonian Circuit Theorem**: A connected graph with `n` vertices has a Hamiltonian circuit if and only if `n` is even.
- **Strongly Connected Component Theorem**: A connected graph has a strongly connected component if and only if it has a path from every vertex to every other vertex.

## Key Concepts

- **Connected Graph**: A graph where there is a path between every pair of vertices.
- **Disconnected Graph**: A graph where there is no path between some pair of vertices.
- **Component**: A connected sub-graph that is not part of a larger connected sub-graph.

## Important Formulas

- **Degree of a Vertex**: The number of edges incident on a vertex.
- **Degree of a Graph**: The sum of the degrees of all vertices in the graph.

## Revision Tips

- Understand the definitions of path, circuit, walk, and isomorphism.
- Know how to identify connected and disconnected graphs.
- Understand the concept of components and strongly connected components.
- Be able to prove isomorphism and Hamiltonian circuit theorems.
