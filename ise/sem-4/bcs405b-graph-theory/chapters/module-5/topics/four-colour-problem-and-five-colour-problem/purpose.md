The Four Colour Problem and Five Colour Problem are fundamental topics in Graph Theory that matter because they deal with the fundamental question of how to colour the vertices of a graph with the fewest number of colours possible, while ensuring that no two adjacent vertices share the same colour. This topic has real-world applications in various fields, including computer science, operations research, and urban planning, where graph colouring techniques can be used to optimize the allocation of resources, such as traffic light colours, road colours, or building colours. This topic also connects to other concepts in Graph Theory, such as graph matching and coverings, and has implications for problems like the Travelling Salesman Problem and the Assignment Problem.
