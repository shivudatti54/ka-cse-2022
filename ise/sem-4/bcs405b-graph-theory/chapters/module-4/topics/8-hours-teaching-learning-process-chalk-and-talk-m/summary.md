# **Teaching-Learning Process: Chalk and Talk Method/PowerPoint Presentation**

**Key Points:**

### Introduction

- Definition of a graph: A set of vertices connected by edges.
- Types of graphs: Connected, disconnected, simple, weighted, labeled.

### Key Concepts

- **Planar Graphs**:
  - Definition: A graph that can be drawn in a plane without any edge crossings.
  - Kuratowski's Theorem: A necessary and sufficient condition for a graph to be planar.
- **Planar Graphs and Euler's Formula**:
  - V - E + F = 2 (where V, E, and F are the numbers of vertices, edges, and faces)
- **Key Theorems**:
  - Kuratowski's Theorem (proof not required)
  - Four Color Theorem (states that any planar graph can be colored with 4 colors)

### Teaching Methods

- **Chalk and Talk Method**:
  - Lecture and discuss key concepts and formulas.
  - Use visual aids to illustrate examples and proofs.
- **PowerPoint Presentation**:
  - Use slides to present key concepts and formulas.
  - Include examples, diagrams, and illustrations to support the lecture.

### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Handshaking Lemma**: The sum of the degrees of all vertices in a graph is equal to twice the number of edges.
- **Degree of a Vertex**: The number of edges incident to a vertex.
- **Euler's Formula**: V - E + F = 2
- **Face of a Planar Graph**: A region enclosed by the edges of a planar graph.
