# **Geometric Dual**

### Definitions

- **Geometric dual**: The dual graph of a given planar graph is obtained by replacing each face of the original graph with a vertex, and each vertex is replaced with a face, such that the number of vertices and faces are equal.
- **Dual edges**: The edges of the dual graph are the diagonals of the original graph.

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Euler's formula** (for planar graphs): `V - E + F = 2`, where `V` is the number of vertices, `E` is the number of edges, and `F` is the number of faces.
- **Kuratowski's theorem**: A planar graph is embeddable in the plane if and only if it does not contain a subgraph homeomorphic to `K5` or `K3,3`.

### Key Points

- **Dual graph properties**:
  - The dual graph of a planar graph is also planar.
  - The dual graph of a graph is homeomorphic to the original graph.
  - The dual graph has the same number of vertices and faces as the original graph.
- **Dual edge properties**:
  - Dual edges are the diagonals of the original graph.
  - Dual edges are not edges of the original graph.
- **Relationship between original and dual graph**:
  - The number of edges in the original graph is equal to the number of diagonals in the dual graph.
  - The number of vertices in the original graph is equal to the number of faces in the dual graph.
