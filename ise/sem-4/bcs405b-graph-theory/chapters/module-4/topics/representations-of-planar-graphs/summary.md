# **Representations of Planar Graphs**

### Definitions and Notations

- A planar graph is a graph that can be drawn in a plane without any edge crossings.
- A planar embedding is an assignment of a plane to each edge of the graph such that no two edges intersect.
- A planar drawing is a planar embedding with additional labels (e.g., node labels, edge labels).

### Key Concepts

- **Kuratowski's Theorem**: A graph is planar if and only if it does not contain a subgraph homeomorphic to K5 or K3,3 (the complete graph on 5 vertices and the complete graph on 3 vertices with an added edge).
- **Planar embedding**: An assignment of a plane to each edge of the graph such that no two edges intersect.
- **Planar drawing**: A planar embedding with additional labels.

### Important Formulas

- **Euler's Formula**: For a planar graph, the number of vertices (V) minus the number of edges (E) plus the number of faces (F) is equal to 2: V - E + F = 2

### Important Theorems

- **Kuratowski's Theorem**: A graph is planar if and only if it does not contain a subgraph homeomorphic to K5 or K3,3.
- **Four Color Theorem**: Every planar graph can be colored with four colors such that no two adjacent vertices have the same color.

### Important Results

- **Cartesian Product**: The Cartesian product of two graphs G and H is a graph whose vertices are pairs of vertices from G and H, and two vertices are adjacent if and only if their corresponding vertices in G and H are adjacent.
- **Planarization**: A planarization of a graph is a planar embedding of the graph such that every face is bounded by at least three edges.
