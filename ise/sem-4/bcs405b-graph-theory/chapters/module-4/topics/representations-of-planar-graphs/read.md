# **Representations of Planar Graphs**

## **Introduction**

In graph theory, a planar graph is a graph that can be drawn in a plane without any edge crossings. Representations of planar graphs refer to the various ways in which a planar graph can be represented as a set of vertices, edges, and faces. In this study material, we will explore the different representations of planar graphs, including planar embedding, dual graph, and grid representation.

## **Planar Embedding**

A planar embedding of a graph is a way of drawing the graph in a plane such that no two edges intersect. This embedding can be thought of as a map of the graph onto a plane.

- Definition: A planar embedding of a graph G is a pair (V, F) where V is the vertex set of G and F is a set of faces of G such that each face is a polygon and each edge belongs to exactly two faces.
- Example: The complete graph K4 can be embedded in a plane with 4 vertices, 6 edges, and 4 faces.

## **Dual Graph**

The dual graph of a planar graph G is a graph whose vertices correspond to the faces of G and whose edges correspond to the edges of G.

- Definition: The dual graph of a planar graph G is a graph D whose vertices correspond to the faces of G and whose edges connect two vertices if the corresponding faces share an edge.
- Example: The dual graph of the complete graph K4 has 4 vertices and 6 edges.

## **Grid Representation**

A grid representation of a planar graph is a way of representing the graph as a set of points in a grid.

- Definition: A grid representation of a planar graph G is a set of points in a grid such that each point corresponds to a vertex of G and two points correspond to two adjacent vertices of G if the corresponding vertices are connected by an edge.
- Example: The complete graph K4 can be represented in a grid with 4 points along each edge.

## **Relationship Between Planar Embeddings**

There are several relationships between planar embeddings of a graph.

- **Lifting**: Given a planar embedding of a graph G, we can lift it to a higher-dimensional space by adding new vertices and edges to the graph.
- **Embedding**: Given two planar embeddings of a graph G, we can find a common embedding by taking the intersection of the two embeddings.
- **Duality**: Given a planar embedding of a graph G, we can find its dual graph by reversing the edges of the embedding.

## **Key Concepts**

- **Planar graph**: A graph that can be drawn in a plane without any edge crossings.
- **Planar embedding**: A way of drawing a graph in a plane such that no two edges intersect.
- **Dual graph**: The graph whose vertices correspond to the faces of a planar graph and whose edges correspond to the edges of the graph.
- **Grid representation**: A way of representing a planar graph as a set of points in a grid.
