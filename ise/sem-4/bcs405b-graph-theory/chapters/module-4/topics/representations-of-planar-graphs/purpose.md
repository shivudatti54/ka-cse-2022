The topic of Representations of planar graphs is crucial in graph theory as it provides a fundamental understanding of how to visualize and manipulate planar graphs, which are essential in various fields such as computer science, network science, and engineering.

This topic is vital because it enables researchers to effectively represent and analyze complex planar graphs, which have numerous real-world applications, including:

- Designing efficient network layouts for telecommunications and transportation systems
- Optimizing graph algorithms for solving complex problems in computer science and network science
- Modeling and analyzing complex systems in fields such as biology, economics, and social networks

Representations of planar graphs also connect to other concepts in graph theory, such as Kuratowski's theorem, which characterizes planar graphs, and graph embeddings, which provide a way to represent graphs as geometric objects.
