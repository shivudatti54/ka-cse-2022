### Revision Notes: Teaching-Learning Chalk and Talk Method / PowerPoint Presentation Process

#### Introduction

- Definition: A structured teaching method where the instructor lectures and writes on the board (chalk and talk)
- Advantages:
  - Interactive
  - Engaging
  - Cost-effective
- Disadvantages:
  - Limited for complex topics
  - May not suit all learning styles

#### PowerPoint Presentation Process

- **Preparation**
  - Create clear and concise slides
  - Use visual aids (images, diagrams, charts)
  - Include relevant formulas and theorems
- **Delivery**
  - Start with an introduction (title slide, objectives)
  - Use slides to support lectures (key points, examples)
  - Leave time for questions and discussions
- **Post-Session**
  - Distribute any additional materials (handouts, worksheets)
  - Follow up with students on their understanding

#### Key Formulas and Definitions

- **Distance between vertices**: d(u, v) = number of edges on the shortest path between u and v
- **Centroid**: a vertex with minimum distance to all other vertices
- **Pendant vertex**: a vertex with degree 1

#### Theorems

- **Traversing a tree**: a path from a root vertex to any other vertex
- **Rooted tree**: a tree with a designated root vertex

#### Tips for Effective Chalk and Talk Method / PowerPoint Presentation

- Use simple language and avoid jargon
- Use visual aids to support complex concepts
- Encourage student participation and questions
