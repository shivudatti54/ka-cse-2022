# Digraphs and Binary Relation

### Definition

- A **digraph** is a directed graph, where edges have direction and represent a one-way relationship between vertices.
- A **binary relation** is a subset of a Cartesian product of two sets, representing a relationship between pairs of elements.

### Key Concepts

- **Digraph Properties**:
  - Directed edges
  - Vertex labels
  - Edge labels (optional)
- **Binary Relation Properties**:
  - Subset of a Cartesian product
  - Reflexive, symmetric, antisymmetric, transitive (if applicable)
- **Types of Binary Relations**:
  - Equivalence relation
  - Partial order
  - Fuzzy relation

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Digraph:**
  - Number of edges: $m$
  - Number of vertices: $n$
  - In-degree: $\deg^+(v)$
  - Out-degree: $\deg^-(v)$
  - Edge capacity (if applicable): $c_e$
- **Binary Relation:**
  - Reflexive: $R \subseteq R \times A \times B$
  - Symmetric: $R \subseteq R^{-1}$
  - Antisymmetric: $R \cap R^{-1} = \emptyset$
  - Transitive: $R \circ R \subseteq R$

### Important Definitions

- **Digraph Homomorphism:** A mapping between digraphs that preserves edges and vertices.
- **Binary Relation Homomorphism:** A mapping between binary relations that preserves the relation.

### Important Results

- **Digraph Isomorphism:** Two digraphs are isomorphic if there exists a homomorphism between them.
- **Binary Relation Isomorphism:** Two binary relations are isomorphic if there exists a homomorphism between them.

### Quick Revision Tips

- Understand the difference between digraphs and binary relations.
- Know the properties of digraphs and binary relations.
- Practice identifying and proving reflexivity, symmetry, antisymmetry, and transitivity.
- Familiarize yourself with homomorphisms and isomorphisms between digraphs and binary relations.
