# **Cartesian Products and Relations**

### Definitions and Notations

- **Cartesian Product**: The set of all ordered pairs $(a, b)$ where $a$ is an element of set $A$ and $b$ is an element of set $B$. Notation: $A \times B$.
- **Relation**: A subset of the Cartesian product $A \times B$.
- **Partial Order**: A relation $\leq$ on a set $S$ that satisfies certain properties.

### Key Concepts

- **Union and Intersection of Relations**: The union of two relations $R_1$ and $R_2$ is the relation $R_1 \cup R_2$ containing all pairs $(a, b)$ such that $(a, b) \in R_1$ or $(a, b) \in R_2$. The intersection of two relations $R_1$ and $R_2$ is the relation $R_1 \cap R_2$ containing all pairs $(a, b)$ such that $(a, b) \in R_1$ and $(a, b) \in R_2$.
- **Composition of Relations**: The composition of two relations $R_1$ and $R_2$ on sets $A$ and $B$ is the relation $R_2 \circ R_1$ obtained by applying $R_1$ first and then $R_2$.
- **Equivalence Relations and Connected Components**: An equivalence relation on a set $A$ is a relation $\sim$ that is reflexive, symmetric, and transitive. The connected components of a relation $R$ on a set $A$ are the equivalence classes of $R$.

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Cantor's Diagonal Argument**: If $A$ and $B$ are infinite sets, then $A \times B$ is also infinite.
- **Begriffssche Insolitheit**: If $A$ and $B$ are infinite sets, then $A \times B$ is not countably infinite.
- **Hausdorff's Theorem**: If $A$ and $B$ are compact Hausdorff spaces, then $A \times B$ is compact and Hausdorff.

### Important Theorems

- **Isomorphism Theorem**: If $f: A \to B$ is a bijection between two groups, then $f$ induces an isomorphism between the groups $Aut(f): Aut(A) \to Aut(B)$.
- **Relation Theorem**: If $R$ is a relation on a set $A$, then $\exists$ an equivalence relation $\sim$ on $A$ such that $R \subseteq \sim$.
