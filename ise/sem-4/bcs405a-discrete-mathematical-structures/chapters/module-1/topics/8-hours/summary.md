### (8 hours) Discrete Mathematical Structures Revision Notes

#### Introduction

- Discrete mathematical structures is a branch of mathematics that deals with individual, distinct objects rather than continuous values.
- It involves the study of mathematical structures such as sets, relations, functions, graphs, and groups.

#### Key Concepts

- **Sets**:
  - Definition: A collection of distinct objects, known as elements or members.
  - Notation: A set is denoted by a capital letter (e.g., A, B, C).
  - Operations: Union (∪), Intersection (∩), Difference (-)
- ** Relations**:
  - Definition: A subset of a Cartesian product of two sets.
  - Notation: R = {(a, b) | a ∈ A, b ∈ B}
  - Types: Equivalence, Partial Order, Total Order
- **Functions**:
  - Definition: A relation between two sets that assigns to each element in the domain exactly one element in the range.
  - Notation: f: A → B
  - Properties: Injective, Surjective, Bijective
- **Graph Theory**:
  - Definition: A mathematical structure consisting of vertices connected by edges.
  - Notation: G = (V, E)
  - Concepts: Adjacency Matrix, Adjacency List, Traversal (DFS, BFS)
- **Group Theory**:
  - Definition: A set with a binary operation that satisfies the following properties: closure, associativity, identity, inverse.
  - Notation: G = (S, ∘)
  - Examples: Integers under addition, Symmetric Group

#### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Union and Intersection of Sets**:
  - A ∪ B = {a | a ∈ A or a ∈ B}
  - A ∩ B = {a | a ∈ A and a ∈ B}
- **Inclusion-Exclusion Principle**:
  - |A ∪ B| = |A| + |B| - |A ∩ B|
- **Group Theory:** Lagrange's Theorem
  - |G| = |S|
  - |H| = |S| for every subgroup H of G

#### Important Definitions

- ** Bijection**: A bijective function between two sets is both injective and surjective.
- **Equivalence Relation**: A binary relation that is reflexive, symmetric, and transitive.
- **Isomorphism**: A bijective function between two groups that preserves the operation.

This summary covers the key concepts, formulas, and definitions of discrete mathematical structures. It provides a concise revision guide for the topics covered in the 8-hour module.
