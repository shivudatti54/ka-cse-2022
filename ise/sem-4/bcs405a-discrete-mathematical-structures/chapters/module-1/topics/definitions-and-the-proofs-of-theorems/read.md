# **Definitions and the Proofs of Theorems**

## **Introduction**

In the realm of discrete mathematical structures, the study of definitions and the proofs of theorems is a fundamental aspect of mathematics. Definitions provide the foundation for mathematical statements, while proofs establish the validity of these statements. This section will delve into the world of definitions and the proofs of theorems, exploring key concepts and providing examples.

## **What are Definitions?**

A definition is a statement that assigns a meaning to a mathematical term or concept. It is a way of describing a mathematical object, property, or relationship in a clear and concise manner. Definitions are used to provide a common understanding of mathematical concepts and to establish a foundation for further study.

## **Types of Definitions**

There are several types of definitions, including:

- **Informal Definitions**: Informal definitions are statements that provide a general description of a mathematical concept. They are often used in everyday language and are not necessarily precise.
- **Formal Definitions**: Formal definitions are statements that provide a precise and unambiguous definition of a mathematical concept. They are often used in mathematical proofs and are more precise than informal definitions.
- **Recursive Definitions**: Recursive definitions are definitions that define a mathematical concept in terms of itself. They can be useful for defining complex mathematical objects.

## **Key Concepts**

- **Axioms**: Axioms are statements that are assumed to be true without proof. They serve as the foundation for mathematical theories and are often used to define mathematical concepts.
- **Postulates**: Postulates are statements that are assumed to be true and are used to define mathematical concepts. They are similar to axioms but are often used in more specific mathematical theories.
- **Theorems**: Theorems are statements that are proved to be true using logical reasoning and mathematical techniques. They are often used to establish the validity of mathematical statements.

## **Proving Theorems**

Proofs are the process of establishing the validity of mathematical statements. There are several types of proofs, including:

- **Direct Proof**: A direct proof is a proof that uses logical reasoning and mathematical techniques to establish the validity of a statement.
- **Indirect Proof**: An indirect proof is a proof that establishes the validity of a statement by showing that its negation leads to a logical contradiction.
- **Proof by Contradiction**: A proof by contradiction is a proof that establishes the validity of a statement by showing that its negation leads to a logical contradiction.

## **Examples**

- **Example 1**: Define the set of even integers.
  - Definition: The set of even integers is the set of all integers that are divisible by 2.
  - Key Points:
    - Definition is a statement that assigns a meaning to a mathematical term (even integers).
    - The definition provides a clear and concise description of the set of even integers.
- **Example 2**: Prove that the sum of two even integers is even.
  - Proof:
    1. Let `a` and `b` be two even integers.
    2. Then `a = 2k` and `b = 2m` for some integers `k` and `m`.
    3. Therefore, `a + b = 2k + 2m = 2(k + m)`.
    4. Since `k + m` is an integer, `2(k + m)` is even.
    5. Therefore, `a + b` is even.

## **Conclusion**

Definitions and the proofs of theorems are essential components of discrete mathematical structures. Definitions provide the foundation for mathematical statements, while proofs establish the validity of these statements. Understanding definitions and proofs is crucial for developing a solid foundation in mathematics and is a fundamental aspect of mathematical reasoning.
