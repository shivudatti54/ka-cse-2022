# **Logical Implication – Rules of Inference**

## **Introduction**

Logical implication is a fundamental concept in logic and mathematics, which deals with the relationship between two propositions. In this study material, we will explore the rules of inference related to logical implication, including the definition, types of implication, and various rules of inference.

## **Definition of Logical Implication**

Logical implication, denoted by →, is a binary operation that takes two propositions, p and q, as input and produces a proposition, r, such that:

- p → q ≡ ¬p ∨ q (where ≡ denotes logical equivalence)

In simpler terms, p → q is true if and only if either p is false or q is true (or both).

## **Types of Logical Implication**

There are two main types of logical implication:

- **Material Implication**: denoted by →, which is the most commonly used type of implication.
- **Tarski Implication**: denoted by ⇒, which is equivalent to material implication.

## **Rules of Inference**

The following are some of the most important rules of inference related to logical implication:

### 1. **Modus Ponens**

p → q
p

---

q

Modus ponens is a rule of inference that states that if p → q and p, then q is true.

### 2. **Modus Tollens**

p → q
¬q

---

¬p

Modus tollens is a rule of inference that states that if p → q and ¬q, then ¬p is true.

### 3. **Hypothetical Syllogism**

p → q
q → r

---

p → r

Hypothetical syllogism is a rule of inference that states that if p → q and q → r, then p → r is true.

### 4. **Converse Modus Ponens**

p → q
q

---

p

Converse modus ponens is a rule of inference that states that if p → q and q, then p is true.

### 5. **Disjunctive Syllogism**

p → q
¬p ∨ q

---

q

Disjunctive syllogism is a rule of inference that states that if p → q and ¬p ∨ q, then q is true.

## **Key Concepts**

- **Modus ponens**: a rule of inference that states that if p → q and p, then q is true.
- **Modus tollens**: a rule of inference that states that if p → q and ¬q, then ¬p is true.
- **Hypothetical syllogism**: a rule of inference that states that if p → q and q → r, then p → r is true.
- **Converse modus ponens**: a rule of inference that states that if p → q and q, then p is true.
- **Disjunctive syllogism**: a rule of inference that states that if p → q and ¬p ∨ q, then q is true.

Logical implication is a fundamental concept in logic and mathematics, which deals with the relationship between two propositions.
