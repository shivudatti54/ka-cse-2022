# **Logic Equivalence – The Laws of Logic**

## **Introduction**

Logic Equivalence is a fundamental concept in discrete mathematical structures, specifically in the field of logic. It deals with the relationships between statements and their transformations using logical operators. In this study material, we will delve into the laws of logic, which govern the behavior of logical expressions.

## **Definition**

Logic Equivalence is a binary relation between two statements, denoted as `A ≡ B`, which means that `A` and `B` have the same truth value under all possible interpretations. In other words, `A ≡ B` if and only if `A` and `B` are equivalent.

## **Laws of Logic**

### 1. **The Law of Identity**

- `A ≡ A` (Identity Law)
  - This law states that a statement is always logically equivalent to itself.
  - Example: `p ≡ p` (a statement is always true if and only if it is true)

### 2. **The Law of Commutativity**

- `A ≡ B` (Commutativity Law)
  - This law states that the order of the statements does not affect their logical equivalence.
  - Example: `p ≡ q` (the truth value of `p` and `q` are the same, regardless of order)

### 3. **The Law of Associativity**

- `(A ≡ B) ≡ (C ≡ D)` (Associativity Law)
  - This law states that the order in which we combine statements does not affect their logical equivalence.
  - Example: `((p ≡ q) ≡ r)` (the truth value of the compound statement is the same, regardless of order of evaluation)

### 4. **The Law of Distributivity**

- `A ≡ (B ∧ C)` (Distributivity Law)
  - This law states that a statement is logically equivalent to a conjunction of statements.
  - Example: `p ≡ (q ∧ r)` (the truth value of `p` is the same as the truth value of `q` and `r`)

### 5. **The Law of De Morgan**

- `A ≡ ¬(B ∧ ¬B)` (De Morgan's Law)
  - This law states that a statement is logically equivalent to the negation of a conjunction.
  - Example: `p ≡ ¬(q ∧ ¬q)` (the truth value of `p` is the same as the truth value of `¬q`)

### 6. **The Law of Double Negation**

- `A ≡ ¬¬A` (Double Negation Law)
  - This law states that a statement is logically equivalent to its negation.
  - Example: `p ≡ ¬¬p` (the truth value of `p` is the same as the truth value of `¬p`)

## **Key Concepts**

- **Logical Equivalence**: a binary relation between two statements, denoted as `A ≡ B`, which means that `A` and `B` have the same truth value under all possible interpretations.
- **Logical Operators**: `∧` (conjunction), `∨` (disjunction), `¬` (negation)
- **Compound Statements**: statements formed using logical operators

2. Show that `A ≡ B` implies `A ≡ C` using the Law of Transitivity.
3. Prove that `A ≡ B` implies `B ≡ A` using the Law of Symmetry.

## **Conclusion**

Logic Equivalence is a fundamental concept in discrete mathematical structures, and the laws of logic govern its behavior. By understanding these laws, we can analyze and evaluate logical expressions, making them a crucial tool in various fields, including computer science, mathematics, and philosophy.
