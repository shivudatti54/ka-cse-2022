# Logic Equivalence – The Laws of Logic

### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Complement**: $\overline{A} = true$ if $A$ is false, and vice versa.
- **Negation**: $\neg A = true$ if $A$ is false, and vice versa.
- **Conjunction**: $A \land B$ is true if both $A$ and $B$ are true.
- **Disjunction**: $A \lor B$ is true if either $A$ or $B$ is true.
- **Implication**: $A \rightarrow B$ is true unless $A$ is true and $B$ is false.

### Laws of Logic

- **The Law of Identity**: $A \equiv A$ (A is equivalent to itself)
- **The Law of Non-Contradiction**: $A \not\equiv \neg A$ (A cannot be equivalent to its negation)
- **The Law of Excluded Middle**: $A \equiv \neg A \lor A$ (A is equivalent to its negation or itself)
- **The Law of Contrapositive**: $A \equiv \neg B \rightarrow \neg A$ (If A implies B, then not B implies not A)
- **The Law of Distributive Equivalence**: $A \equiv (B \land C) \lor (B \lor C)$ (A is equivalent to the disjunction of B and C)
- **The Law of Associative Equivalence**: $A \equiv (B \land C) \land D$ (A is equivalent to the conjunction of B and C with D)

### Important Theorems

- **De Morgan's Law**: $\neg (A \land B) \equiv \neg A \lor \neg B$
- **The Law of Double Negation**: $\neg \neg A \equiv A$
