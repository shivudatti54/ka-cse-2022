1. **The Law of Non-Contradiction**: This law states that a statement cannot both be true and false at the same time. In other words, a statement is either true or false, but not both.
2. **The Law of Excluded Middle**: This law states that a statement is either true or false, with no middle ground. In other words, a statement is not both true and false.
3. **The Law of Identity**: This law states that a statement is equivalent to itself. In other words, a statement is true if and only if it is identical to itself.
4. **The Law of Commutativity**: This law states that the order of the operands does not affect the truth value of the expression. In other words, the order of the operands can be swapped without changing the truth value of the expression.
5. **The Law of Associativity**: This law states that the order in which the operands are grouped does not affect the truth value of the expression. In other words, the order in which the operands are grouped can be changed without changing the truth value of the expression.
