# Fundamental Principles of Counting

### Revision Notes

#### The Rules of Sum and Product

- The Rule of Product: If one event can occur in $n$ ways and a second independent event can occur in $m$ ways, then the events together can occur in $n \cdot m$ ways.
- The Rule of Sum: If one event can occur in $n$ ways and a second event can occur in $m$ ways, then the events together can occur in $n + m$ ways.

#### Permutations

- Definition: Arrangement of objects in a specific order.
- Formula: $P(n, r) = \frac{n!}{(n-r)!}$
- Properties:
  - Permutations are commutative and associative.
  - Permutations are not commutative if the objects are distinct.

#### Combinations

- Definition: Selection of objects without regard to order.
- Formula: $C(n, r) = \frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!}$
- Properties:
  - Combinations are commutative and associative.
  - Combinations are not commutative if the objects are distinct.

#### The Binomial Theorem

- Definition: Expansion of $(a + b)^n$.
- Formula: $(a + b)^n = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k} a^{n-k}b^k$
- Important Theorem: Binomial Theorem states that $(a + b)^n$ can be expanded into a sum of terms, where each term is a combination of $a$ and $b$ raised to a power.

#### Combinations with Repetition

- Definition: Selection of objects with repetition allowed.
- Formula: $C(n+r-1, r)$
- Properties:
  - Combinations with repetition are not commutative.
  - Combinations with repetition are associative.

#### Important Formulas and Theorems

- Pascal's Triangle: A triangular array of binomial coefficients.
- Theorem: If $P(n, r)$ and $C(n, r)$ are defined as above, then $P(n, r) = n \cdot C(n, r)$.
