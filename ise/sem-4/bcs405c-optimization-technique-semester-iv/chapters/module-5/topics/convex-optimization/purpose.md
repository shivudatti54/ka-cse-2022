# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1.  Define and identify convex sets and convex functions mathematically and geometrically.
2.  Classify a given optimization problem as convex or non-convex by analyzing its objective and constraint functions.
3.  Explain the critical relationship between local and global optima in convex optimization problems.
4.  Differentiate between the challenges and solution approaches associated with convex versus non-convex problems.
5.  Apply the second-derivative test (Hessian analysis) to verify the convexity of a twice-differentiable function.
6.  Recognize real-world examples of problems that typically fall into the convex or non-convex categories.
