### Learning Purpose: Sequential Search Methods (3-Point & Fibonacci)

**1. Importance:** This topic is crucial as it introduces deterministic, single-variable search techniques that form the foundation for more complex optimization algorithms. Understanding these methods is key to solving real-world engineering and business problems where finding a minimum or maximum efficiently is required, especially when derivative information is unavailable or the function is costly to evaluate.

**2. Student Learning:** Students will learn the underlying principles, iterative procedures, and termination criteria for both the 3-point (equal-interval) and Fibonacci search methods. They will be able to implement these algorithms to find the optimum of a unimodal function, compare their relative efficiency in terms of function evaluations, and understand how the Fibonacci method achieves the lowest possible theoretical error for a given number of steps.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts:** This builds directly upon the previous module's knowledge of unimodality and bracketing the minimum. These direct search methods are a precursor to more advanced multivariable optimization techniques like the simplex method and provide a benchmark for comparing the performance of stochastic and heuristic algorithms covered later in the course.

**4. Real-World Applications:** These algorithms are applied in engineering design (e.g., optimal parameter tuning), economics (e.g., profit maximization), and manufacturing (e.g., minimizing production cost or material usage) for problems where the objective function is defined by a complex simulation or physical experiment.
