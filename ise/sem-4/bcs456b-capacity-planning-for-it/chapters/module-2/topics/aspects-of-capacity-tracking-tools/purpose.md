### Learning Purpose: Aspects of Capacity Tracking Tools

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Capacity tracking tools are fundamental for proactive IT management. They enable organizations to monitor resource utilization, forecast future needs, and prevent costly performance bottlenecks or service outages. Understanding their aspects ensures efficient, reliable, and cost-effective IT operations.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core features and functionalities of modern capacity tracking tools, including real-time monitoring, data collection methods (e.g., logging, sampling), predictive analytics, and reporting dashboards. They will also evaluate key criteria for selecting an appropriate tool for a given business context.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds on the foundational capacity planning process covered in Module 1. It provides the practical means to implement the theoretical plans. It also connects directly to subsequent modules on performance management, budgeting (cost control), and strategic procurement of IT resources.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied to continuously monitor server CPU/memory usage, network bandwidth, and storage across on-premise data centers and cloud environments (e.g., AWS, Azure). It is used to generate actionable reports for IT managers, justify infrastructure investments, and ensure applications meet service level agreements (SLAs).
