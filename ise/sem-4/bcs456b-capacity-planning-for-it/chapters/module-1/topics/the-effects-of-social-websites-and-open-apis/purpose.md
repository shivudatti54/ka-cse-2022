### Learning Purpose: The Effects of Social Websites and Open APIs

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the impact of social websites and open APIs is critical because they are primary drivers of modern, unpredictable IT demand. Their viral nature can cause traffic to spike exponentially and without warning, making traditional capacity planning obsolete. Ignoring their influence risks severe performance degradation, outages, and a poor user experience.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify how social media integration and open APIs generate non-linear traffic patterns. They will analyze case studies to understand the "viral effect" and its implications for infrastructure. The module will equip them with strategies to model this unpredictable demand and build scalable, resilient systems capable of handling rapid growth.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the foundation for subsequent modules on forecasting (Module 2) and scalable architecture design (Module 3). It provides the "why" behind the need for agile and cloud-native capacity strategies, moving beyond static forecasts to dynamic, automated scaling principles.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is directly applicable to planning for product launches, marketing campaigns, and building platforms that rely on third-party integrations. It is essential for ensuring e-commerce sites, fintech apps, and SaaS products remain performant and available during critical growth periods.
