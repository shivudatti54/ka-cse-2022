### Learning Purpose: Quick and Dirty Math

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Effective capacity planning requires swift, data-informed decisions, not always precise calculations. "Quick and Dirty Math" provides the essential estimation techniques needed to rapidly assess resource requirements, identify potential bottlenecks, and determine project feasibility before committing to costly, detailed analysis.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn practical rules-of-thumb and approximation methods for estimating key IT capacity metrics. This includes techniques for "back-of-the-napkin" calculations for storage, network bandwidth, server load, and user concurrency to translate business needs into technical specifications quickly.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This foundational skill is the critical first step in the capacity planning lifecycle. It directly informs subsequent, more precise activities like performance benchmarking, demand forecasting, and financial modeling (TCO/ROI calculations). It bridges the gap between high-level business requests and technical implementation.

**4. Real-world applications**
These techniques are used daily by architects and system administrators to:

- Quickly size a new application's infrastructure needs during initial scoping.
- Troubleshoot performance issues by estimating expected vs. actual capacity.
- Communicate effectively with stakeholders using simplified, relatable figures to justify deeper investment or analysis.
- Perform rapid "what-if" scenarios for growth planning or consolidation projects.
