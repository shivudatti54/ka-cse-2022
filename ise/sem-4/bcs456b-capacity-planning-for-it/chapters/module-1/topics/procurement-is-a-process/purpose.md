# Learning Purpose: Procurement Is a Process

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Procurement is a critical, often overlooked, component of IT capacity planning. Viewing it merely as a purchasing activity leads to costly delays, budget overruns, and technical incompatibilities. Understanding procurement as a structured process ensures that acquired technology aligns precisely with strategic capacity requirements, maximizes value, and mitigates financial and operational risks.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn that procurement is a multi-stage lifecycle—from needs assessment and vendor evaluation to negotiation, acquisition, and asset management. They will identify the key stakeholders involved at each phase and analyze how effective procurement directly supports the timely and cost-effective scaling of IT infrastructure to meet business demands.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the practical execution of strategic capacity plans developed earlier. It directly connects to forecasting (defining technical requirements), financial planning (budgeting and TCO), vendor management (SLAs and partnerships), and the subsequent modules on deployment and lifecycle management, where procured assets are implemented and maintained.

**4. Real-world applications**
Learners will be able to structure an RFP (Request for Proposal), evaluate vendor quotes against technical and business criteria, and create a procurement timeline that ensures new hardware, software, or cloud services are acquired and ready for deployment before capacity shortages impact business operations.
