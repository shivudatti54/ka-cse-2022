# Learning Purpose: Performance and Capacity

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding performance and capacity is fundamental because it directly impacts an organization's ability to deliver reliable, efficient, and cost-effective IT services. Inadequate capacity planning leads to system failures, poor user experience, and unplanned expenditure, while over-provisioning results in significant financial waste on unused resources.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core terminology, principles, and objectives of capacity planning. This includes defining key performance indicators (KPIs), understanding workload characterization, and differentiating between performance testing and capacity planning. They will grasp how to establish a performance baseline and identify resource constraints within a system.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic provides the essential groundwork for subsequent modules. It directly connects to forecasting future demand (Module 2), choosing the right scaling strategies (Module 3), and implementing effective monitoring and management practices (Module 4). It is the cornerstone upon which the entire discipline of capacity planning is built.

**4. Real-world applications**
These concepts are applied daily by IT teams to right-size cloud infrastructure (e.g., AWS, Azure), plan for application launches, ensure e-commerce websites handle holiday traffic spikes, and maintain the performance of critical business databases, ultimately aligning IT resources with business goals.
