# **Setting Goals for Capacity: Different Kinds of Requirements and Measurements**

### Introduction

- Capacity planning is a process to determine the resources needed to meet future business needs.
- Setting goals for capacity involves understanding different types of requirements and measurements.

### Types of Requirements

- **Business Requirements**: Changes in business processes, new products, or increased demand.
- **Usage Requirements**: Changes in user behavior, new applications, or increased usage of existing applications.
- **Technical Requirements**: Changes in technology, hardware, or software.

### Measurements

- **Utilization**: The percentage of time a resource is being used.
- **Load**: The amount of work or traffic a resource can handle.
- **Capacity**: The maximum amount of work or traffic a resource can handle.
- **Demand**: The actual amount of work or traffic a resource is handling.

### Formulas and Definitions

- **Utilization Formula**: Utilization = (Actual Work / Maximum Capacity) x 100
- **Load Formula**: Load = (Actual Work / Available Capacity) x 100
- **The Law of Diminishing Returns**: As workload increases, productivity increases up to a point, then decreases.
- **The 80/20 Rule**: 80% of profits come from 20% of customer needs.

### Theorems

- **The First In, First Out (FIFO) Theorem**: The oldest resource is the first to be used.
- **The Shortest Job First (SJF) Theorem**: The shortest job is the first to be completed.

### Key Points

- Understand the types of requirements and measurements to set effective capacity goals.
- Utilize formulas and theorems to analyze and predict capacity needs.
- Consider the Law of Diminishing Returns and the 80/20 Rule when setting capacity goals.
