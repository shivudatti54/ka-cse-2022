# **Setting Goals for Capacity: Different Kinds of Requirements and Measurements**

## **Introduction**

Capacity planning is a critical component of IT planning that involves determining the resources and systems required to support an organization's goals and objectives. One of the key steps in capacity planning is setting goals for capacity, which requires identifying the different kinds of requirements and measurements. In this study material, we will explore the concept of setting goals for capacity, different kinds of requirements, and measurements.

## **What are Requirements?**

Requirements refer to the needs and expectations of an organization, which in turn define the capacity requirements of the IT systems. Requirements can be categorized into different types, including:

- **Functional Requirements**: These are the specific functions and processes that a system or application must perform.
- **Non-Functional Requirements**: These are the constraints on the system or application, such as security, scalability, and usability.
- **Performance Requirements**: These are the standards for the system or application's response time, throughput, and other performance characteristics.

## **Types of Requirements**

There are several types of requirements that must be considered when setting goals for capacity, including:

- **Service Level Agreement (SLA) Requirements**: These are the agreed-upon levels of service and performance that must be met by the IT systems.
- **Business Requirements**: These are the requirements of the business, which may include things like data storage, processing power, and network connectivity.
- **Technical Requirements**: These are the requirements of the technology itself, which may include things like hardware specifications, software requirements, and network architecture.

## **Types of Measurements**

There are several types of measurements that can be used to evaluate capacity, including:

- **Throughput**: This measures the volume of work or data that can be processed by the system.
- **Response Time**: This measures the time it takes for the system to respond to a request or action.
- **Concurrency**: This measures the number of users or processes that can be handled by the system simultaneously.
- **Availability**: This measures the percentage of time that the system is available and usable.

## **Example**

Suppose an organization has a customer relationship management (CRM) system that must support 1,000 users concurrently. The CRM system must respond to requests within 5 seconds, and the system must be available 99.99% of the time. In this case, the requirements for capacity would include:

- Functional requirements: The CRM system must support 1,000 users concurrently.
- Non-functional requirements: The CRM system must respond to requests within 5 seconds, and the system must be available 99.99% of the time.
- Performance requirements: The CRM system must meet the above requirements.

## **Conclusion**

Setting goals for capacity requires identifying the different kinds of requirements and measurements. By understanding the types of requirements and measurements, organizations can set realistic goals for capacity and ensure that their IT systems can support their objectives.

## **Key Takeaways**

- Requirements can be categorized into functional, non-functional, and performance requirements.
- There are several types of requirements that must be considered when setting goals for capacity, including SLA, business, and technical requirements.
- There are several types of measurements that can be used to evaluate capacity, including throughput, response time, concurrency, and availability.
- By understanding the types of requirements and measurements, organizations can set realistic goals for capacity and ensure that their IT systems can support their objectives.
