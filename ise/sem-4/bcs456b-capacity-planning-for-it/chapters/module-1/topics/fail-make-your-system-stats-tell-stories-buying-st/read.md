### Fail

**Definition:** Failure is an inevitable part of any system or process. It's the point at which a system or process is no longer able to meet its intended goals or requirements.

**Key Concepts:**

- **Failure Rate:** The rate at which failures occur within a system or process.
- **Mean Time Between Failures (MTBF):** The average time between failures in a system or process.
- **Mean Time To Recovery (MTTR):** The average time it takes to recover from a failure in a system or process.

**Example:** A web server experiences a 5% failure rate per month. To put this into perspective, if the server was up 99.99% of the time, it would fail approximately 1-2 times per year.

### Make Your System Stats Tell Stories

**Definition:** Analyzing system statistics can help identify trends and patterns that can inform business decisions.

**Key Concepts:**

- **Metrics:** Quantifiable data used to measure system performance.
- **KPIs (Key Performance Indicators):** Metrics used to measure business performance.
- **Dashboards:** Visual representations of system statistics used to track performance.

**Example:** A company tracks its customer satisfaction metrics using a dashboard. By analyzing the data, the company can identify areas of improvement and make data-driven decisions.

### Buying Stuff: Procurement Is a Process

**Definition:** Procurement is the process of acquiring goods, services, or works from external sources.

**Key Concepts:**

- **Procurement Process:** A series of steps involved in acquiring goods, services, or works.
- **Sourcing:** Identifying potential suppliers.
- **Negotiation:** Finalizing terms and conditions with suppliers.

**Example:** A company is looking to purchase new software for its employees. The procurement process involves sourcing potential suppliers, negotiating prices, and finalizing the purchase.

### Performance and Capacity: Two Different Animals

**Definition:** Performance and capacity are two distinct concepts in system management.

**Key Concepts:**

- **Performance:** The ability of a system to meet its intended goals and requirements.
- **Capacity:** The maximum amount of work a system can handle.

**Example:** A company's system is able to process 100 transactions per hour, but during peak hours, it only processes 80 transactions per hour. In this case, the system's performance is lower than its capacity.

### The Effects of Soc

**Definition:** Social factors can significantly impact system performance and capacity.

**Key Concepts:**

- **User Behavior:** Individual and group behavior can impact system performance and capacity.
- **Social Network Effects:** The impact of social connections on system performance and capacity.
- **Trust and Cooperation:** The importance of trust and cooperation in system performance and capacity.

**Example:** A company's system is experiencing high usage due to a social media trend. If users are able to share data and collaborate, the system's capacity may increase, but if users are unable to trust the system, performance may decrease.
