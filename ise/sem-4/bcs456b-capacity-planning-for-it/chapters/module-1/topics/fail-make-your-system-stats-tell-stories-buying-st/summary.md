# Revision Notes: Capacity Planning for IT

==============================================

### Overview

---

- Capacity planning is a crucial process to ensure IT systems can handle future demands.

### Key Concepts

---

- **Fail**: System failure can lead to downtime, loss of data, and revenue.
  - Definition: Unavailability of a system due to hardware, software, or human error.
  - Theorem: 1 - (Uptime / Total Time) = Downtime
- **Make Your System Stats Tell Stories**: Analyze system logs to identify trends and patterns.
  - Formula: System Uptime = (Total Time - Downtime) / Total Time
- **Buying Stuff: Procurement Is a Process**: Ensure timely and efficient procurement to meet system demands.
  - Definition: The process of acquiring goods or services to meet business needs.
  - Formula: Lead Time = Order Receipt Date - Order Placement Date
- **Performance and Capacity: Two Different Animals**: Understand the difference between performance and capacity.
  - Definition:
    - Performance: The ability of a system to meet current demands.
    - Capacity: The maximum ability of a system to handle future demands.
  - Formula: Capacity = Performance x Utilization
- **The Effects of Soc**: Consider social factors when planning IT capacity.
  - Definition: Social factors include user behavior, population growth, and economic trends.

### Important Formulas and Definitions

---

- Available Resource Utilization (ARU): = (Total Resource Utilization - Resource Not Available) / Total Resource Utilization
- System Utilization: = (Total Resource Utilization / Maximum Resource Utilization) x 100
- Lead Time: = Order Receipt Date - Order Placement Date
