### Learning Purpose: Module 1 - Setting Goals for Capacity

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Setting clear goals is the critical first step in capacity planning. Without well-defined objectives, IT investments can become misaligned with business needs, leading to either costly over-provisioning of resources or performance-degrading under-provisioning. This topic establishes the foundation for creating a proactive, cost-effective, and business-driven IT strategy.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn how to translate broad business objectives (e.g., "support a 20% user growth," "launch a new product line") into specific, measurable, and technical capacity requirements. This includes identifying key performance indicators (KPIs), understanding different types of demand (e.g., peak vs. average), and establishing baselines for current system performance.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
The goals defined here directly feed into subsequent modules. They become the requirements for forecasting future demand (Module 2) and are the benchmarks against which all infrastructure design and procurement decisions (Module 3) are measured. This process ensures that technical efforts remain tightly coupled with business outcomes.

**4. Real-world applications**
This skill is applied when preparing for a seasonal sales event to ensure e-commerce platforms can handle the traffic, when budgeting for new server hardware based on projected growth, or when defining service level agreements (SLAs) for a new application to guarantee a specific user experience.
