# Learning Purpose: Different Kinds of Requirements and Measurements

1.  **Why is this topic important?**
    Understanding the various types of requirements and their corresponding measurements is the foundational first step in effective capacity planning. Without accurately identifying performance, scalability, and availability needs, any subsequent planning is based on guesswork, leading to either costly over-provisioning or risky under-provisioning of IT resources.

2.  **What will students learn?**
    Students will learn to categorize and define key non-functional requirements, including performance (e.g., transactions per second), scalability (e.g., user growth), and availability (e.g., uptime percentage). They will also be introduced to the essential metrics and units of measurement (e.g., CPU utilization, IOPS, network throughput) used to quantify these requirements and establish baselines.

3.  **How does it connect to other concepts?**
    This topic provides the critical input data for all subsequent modules. The requirements and baselines defined here directly feed into forecasting future demand (Module 2), building predictive models (Module 3), and creating the final capacity plan (Module 4). It connects the business's needs to the technical specifications of the infrastructure.

4.  **Real-world applications**
    This knowledge is applied when gathering specifications for a new application rollout, setting performance Service Level Agreements (SLAs) with business units, and conducting initial assessments during IT audits or infrastructure modernization projects to ensure systems are aligned with business objectives.
