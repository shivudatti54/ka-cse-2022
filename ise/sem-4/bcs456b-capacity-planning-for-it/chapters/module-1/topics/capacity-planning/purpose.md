# Learning Purpose: Capacity Planning for IT

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Capacity Planning is a critical IT discipline that ensures technology resources are available, performant, and cost-effective. Without it, organizations risk system failures during peak demand, poor user experience, and wasted capital on underutilized infrastructure. Proactive capacity planning is fundamental to business continuity, agility, and financial efficiency.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core principles of forecasting IT resource needs based on business demand. This includes understanding key metrics (e.g., CPU, memory, storage, network bandwidth), differentiating between strategic and tactical planning, and utilizing basic forecasting techniques to predict future growth and performance requirements.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is intrinsically linked to other IT management domains. It directly connects to **performance management** (setting baselines and benchmarks), **financial planning** (managing CAPEX and OPEX), **cloud computing** (right-sizing instances), **disaster recovery** (ensuring sufficient failover capacity), and overall **IT service management** (SLAs).

**4. Real-world applications**
Students will be able to apply these concepts to optimize data center expenditures, plan for application launches or migrations, justify infrastructure investments to stakeholders, and prevent costly performance bottlenecks in production environments, ensuring IT reliably supports business goals.
