# Learning Purpose: Use it or lose it (your wallet)

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because inefficient use of procured IT capacity directly impacts an organization's financial health and operational agility. Wasted resources, such as unused cloud instances or over-provisioned servers, represent significant sunk costs and missed opportunities to fund other strategic initiatives.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn techniques to actively monitor, track, and reclaim underutilized IT resources. They will be able to identify "zombie" assets, understand the financial implications of idle capacity, and implement processes (e.g., automated scaling, resource scheduling, decommissioning policies) to ensure purchased capacity is fully leveraged, thus optimizing expenditure.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This concept is the practical application of forecasting and procurement covered in earlier modules. It connects directly to financial management (showback/chargeback), performance monitoring (using metrics to identify waste), and strategic planning, as reclaimed resources and budgets can be reallocated to new projects.

**4. Real-world applications**
This skill is applied daily by FinOps and cloud cost management teams. Students will be equipped to conduct regular resource audits, create alerting systems for low utilization, and establish governance policies that automatically scale down or terminate unused resources, ensuring the organization gets maximum value from every IT dollar spent.
