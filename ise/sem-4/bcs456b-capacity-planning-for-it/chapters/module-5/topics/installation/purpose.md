### Module 5: Installation - Learning Purpose

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Installation is the critical execution phase where capacity planning transitions from theory to reality. A poorly managed installation can lead to system failures, security vulnerabilities, and wasted resources, negating even the most meticulous planning. Understanding installation ensures new hardware and software are deployed correctly, meeting performance and availability targets from day one.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the procedural best practices for installing new IT infrastructure based on a capacity plan. This includes creating and following a detailed installation checklist, managing change control processes, validating that installed capacity matches specifications, and conducting initial load testing to verify performance against benchmarks.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly applies the forecasting and requirement analysis from earlier modules. It is the practical implementation of the theoretical capacity plan and acts as the foundation for subsequent modules on monitoring and scaling. The validation steps here provide the baseline metrics crucial for future planning cycles.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is essential for roles like Systems Administrators and Cloud Engineers deploying new servers, expanding data storage, or launching enterprise software. It ensures smooth roll-outs of IT projects, minimizes deployment risks and downtime, and guarantees that new systems operate as intended within their designed capacity limits from the moment they go live.
