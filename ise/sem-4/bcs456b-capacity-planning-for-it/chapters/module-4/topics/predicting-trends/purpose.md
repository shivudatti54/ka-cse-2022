# Learning Purpose: Predicting Trends in IT Capacity Planning

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Predicting trends is a cornerstone of proactive IT capacity planning. Without accurate forecasting, organizations risk either costly over-provisioning of resources or debilitating under-provisioning that leads to system failures and poor user experience. This topic is vital for ensuring IT infrastructure is scalable, cost-effective, and reliably aligned with future business demands.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify and analyze key data sources (e.g., application performance, business KPIs, market growth) to forecast future IT resource requirements. They will be introduced to quantitative techniques like time-series analysis and moving averages, as well as qualitative methods for anticipating disruptive trends, enabling them to create data-driven capacity forecasts.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module builds directly on prior knowledge of performance metrics and baseline establishment (Module 3). It uses that historical data as input for forecasting. Furthermore, its output—the capacity forecast—becomes the essential foundation for the subsequent modules on strategic planning, budgeting, and justifying infrastructure investments to stakeholders.

**4. Real-world applications**
This skill is directly applicable to predicting website traffic surges during sales, forecasting data storage needs for a new product launch, or planning compute capacity for migrating workloads to the cloud. It enables IT professionals to translate business strategy into a concrete, scalable technical plan.
