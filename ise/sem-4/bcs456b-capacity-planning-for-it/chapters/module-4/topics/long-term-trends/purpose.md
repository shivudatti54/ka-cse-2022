### **Learning Purpose: Long-Term Trends in IT Capacity Planning**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding long-term trends is critical because IT infrastructure decisions are costly, long-lasting investments. Failing to anticipate major technological shifts can lead to premature obsolescence, wasted capital, and an inability to support future business initiatives. This topic moves beyond reactive, short-term planning and equips professionals with a strategic, forward-looking perspective.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify, analyze, and model the impact of key macro-trends on infrastructure demand. This includes trends like AI/ML adoption, the exponential growth of IoT data, edge computing, and sustainability (Green IT). They will evaluate how these trends influence requirements for processing power, storage, network bandwidth, and data center design over a 3-5 year horizon.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module synthesizes prior concepts: it uses forecasting techniques from earlier modules and applies them to strategic, trend-based analysis. It connects financial planning (CAPEX vs. OPEX for new tech), architectural design (e.g., cloud vs. edge), and risk management by evaluating the uncertainty of future predictions.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is directly applied to creating robust technology roadmaps, justifying multi-year budget proposals for new infrastructure, and ensuring an organization's IT capacity remains a strategic enabler rather than a constraint. It is essential for roles in IT strategy, cloud architecture, and infrastructure management.
