### Learning Purpose: The Effects of Increasing Capacity

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the effects of increasing capacity is crucial for making informed, cost-effective IT investment decisions. It moves beyond the simple act of adding resources to analyzing the tangible outcomes, risks, and strategic value for the business, ensuring that capacity growth aligns with organizational goals.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to analyze the direct and indirect consequences of scaling IT infrastructure. This includes evaluating positive impacts (e.g., improved performance, handling traffic spikes) against potential negatives (e.g., soaring costs, underutilization, increased complexity). They will also explore the financial and performance trade-offs involved.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the practical application of earlier modules on forecasting demand and performance baselining. It directly informs subsequent topics on cost-benefit analysis and choosing scaling strategies (vertical vs. horizontal), forming a critical bridge between theoretical planning and execution.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied when justifying a server upgrade to management, planning for a major product launch, migrating to the cloud, or optimizing resource use in a data center to control operational expenses. It prevents costly over-provisioning and dangerous under-provisioning of resources.
