# Learning Purpose: Module 4 - Procurement

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Procurement is a critical bridge between capacity planning and tangible IT capability. A well-defined plan is useless without the ability to acquire the necessary resources efficiently and cost-effectively. This topic is vital because poor procurement practices can derail projects, inflate budgets, and lead to technological incompatibilities, ultimately jeopardizing the entire capacity strategy.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the end-to-end procurement lifecycle within an IT capacity context. This includes developing Requests for Proposal (RFPs), evaluating vendor bids based on technical and financial metrics, negotiating contracts with Service Level Agreements (SLAs), and managing vendor relationships. The goal is to ensure acquired resources (hardware, software, cloud services) precisely meet the specifications and timelines outlined in the capacity plan.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly applies the forecasting and requirement analysis skills from earlier modules. The capacity plan dictates the _what_ and _why_ for procurement, which then executes the _how_. It also connects to financial management (budgeting, TCO analysis) and risk management (assessing vendor stability, ensuring compliance).

**4. Real-world applications**
Students will be equipped to manage the acquisition process for essential IT infrastructure. This includes procuring new servers to handle projected user growth, sourcing cloud services to achieve scalability, or licensing software for a new department. These skills are fundamental for roles like IT Project Manager, Systems Architect, and Procurement Specialist.
