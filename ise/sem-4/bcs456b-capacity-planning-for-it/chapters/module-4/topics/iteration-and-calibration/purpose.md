### Learning Purpose: Iteration and Calibration in Capacity Planning

1.  **Why is this topic important?**
    Initial capacity plans are rarely perfect. This topic is crucial because it teaches that capacity planning is an iterative process, not a one-time event. Continuous calibration ensures IT resources remain aligned with fluctuating business demands, preventing costly over-provisioning or performance-impacting under-provisioning. It is fundamental to maintaining system reliability, controlling costs, and achieving operational agility.

2.  **What will students learn?**
    Students will learn the methodology for reviewing and refining capacity forecasts and plans. This includes analyzing performance data against predictions, identifying variances, and adjusting models and thresholds accordingly. They will understand how to establish a feedback loop for continuous improvement, turning planning from a static report into a dynamic, responsive process.

3.  **How does it connect to other concepts?**
    This topic directly builds upon prior modules on forecasting techniques (e.g., trend analysis) and performance monitoring. It takes the initial forecasts and the data collected from live systems and connects them, closing the loop. The outputs from iteration and calibration also feed directly into budgeting, procurement, and strategic planning cycles.

4.  **Real-world applications**
    Students will be able to apply these principles to regularly scheduled capacity reviews, using tools like monitoring dashboards (e.g., Grafana, Dynatrace) to validate predictions. This is essential for cloud cost optimization (e.g., right-sizing AWS EC2 instances), preparing for product launches, and ensuring SLAs are met after any significant change to the IT environment.
