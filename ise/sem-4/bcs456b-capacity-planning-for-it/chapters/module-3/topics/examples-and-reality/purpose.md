### Learning Purpose: Module 3 - Examples and Reality

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because it bridges the gap between theoretical capacity planning models and their real-world application. IT environments are dynamic and full of unforeseen variables; understanding how plans succeed or fail in practice prevents costly over-provisioning or disruptive under-provisioning of resources.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to analyze real-world case studies of successful and failed capacity planning initiatives. They will identify common pitfalls, constraints, and unexpected variables (e.g., sudden demand spikes, vendor limitations). The goal is to critique strategies and adapt theoretical models to messy, realistic business scenarios.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly applies the forecasting techniques and performance metrics from Modules 1 and 2. It demonstrates how those foundational concepts are used—and sometimes challenged—when creating a tangible plan, thereby setting the stage for Module 4, where students will build their own capacity plans.

**4. Real-world applications**
Learners will be equipped to evaluate capacity needs for concrete examples such as planning for a major e-commerce sales event, supporting a new SaaS application launch, or preparing IT infrastructure for organizational growth, ensuring plans are both robust and pragmatic.
