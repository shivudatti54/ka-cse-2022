### **Learning Purpose: Measurement in Capacity Planning**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Measurement is the empirical foundation of effective capacity planning. Without accurate data on current system performance (e.g., CPU utilization, memory consumption, I/O rates), planning is reduced to guesswork. This topic is critical because it teaches how to move from assumptions to evidence-based decision-making, ensuring IT resources are right-sized to meet business demands cost-effectively and avoid performance bottlenecks or wasteful over-provisioning.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify and define Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) and metrics relevant to IT infrastructure. They will understand techniques for collecting, baselining, and analyzing performance data to understand normal operation and trends. This includes evaluating tooling for monitoring (e.g., dashboards, APM tools) and interpreting data to distinguish between cyclical patterns, peaks, and genuine growth trends.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly links the theoretical models from earlier modules (forecasting demand, understanding scalability) to tangible data. Measurement provides the inputs needed for accurate forecasting and validates the models used. It is also a prerequisite for the subsequent modules on implementing scaling policies and managing costs, as you cannot automate what you do not measure.

**4. Real-world applications**
The skills learned are applied daily by cloud architects, system administrators, and site reliability engineers (SREs). Students will be equipped to create monitoring plans, establish performance baselines for new applications, and provide data-driven recommendations for infrastructure upgrades or cloud resource allocation, directly impacting operational efficiency and budget.
