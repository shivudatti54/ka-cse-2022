### **Learning Purpose: Capacity Planning for IT**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Effective capacity planning is the cornerstone of a stable, performant, and cost-efficient IT infrastructure. Without it, organizations risk crippling system failures during peak demand or waste significant capital on over-provisioned, underutilized resources. This topic is critical for ensuring business continuity, user satisfaction, and financial prudence in an increasingly digital world.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to forecast future IT resource requirements (compute, storage, network) based on business growth, trends, and performance data. They will master techniques for analyzing current utilization, modeling future scenarios ("what-if" analysis), and creating a strategic plan to scale resources proactively. The goal is to right-size infrastructure to meet demand with optimal performance at the lowest feasible cost.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds upon foundational concepts from IT operations, performance monitoring, and cloud economics. It is the practical application of data analysis and aligns with strategic IT governance and financial management (e.g., CapEx vs. OpEx). It also connects to broader business strategy, as IT capacity must directly support organizational goals and growth initiatives.

**4. Real-world applications**
This skill is applied daily by cloud architects, system administrators, and IT managers. Key applications include:

- Planning for a seasonal sales event on an e-commerce platform.
- Migrating and scaling an on-premise database to the cloud.
- Ensuring a new software launch can handle anticipated user load.
- Budgeting for future hardware refreshes or cloud subscription tiers.
