# **Experience Needs a Business Case**

## **Key Points**

- Experience design is a business-driven discipline, not a technical one
- Experience needs to meet business objectives and deliver tangible value
- Experience should be measurable, justifiable, and accountable
- Experience needs a business case to justify investment and resources

## **Definitions**

- **Business case**: A comprehensive document that outlines the business objectives, costs, benefits, and risks associated with a particular experience design project
- **Return on Investment (ROI)**: The excess return on investment generated by a project or experience design initiative compared to the cost of the project
- **Customer Value Proposition (CVP)**: A statement that describes the unique value that a product or service offers to a customer

## **Theorems and Formulas**

- **ROI Formula**: (Benefits - Costs) / Costs
- **Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) Formula**: (Average Order Value x Purchase Frequency x Customer Lifespan) / (Customer Acquisition Cost + Cost of Goods Sold)

## **Important Concepts**

- **Experience Map**: A visual representation of the user's journey and interactions with an experience
- **Business Model Canvas**: A strategic management tool that helps design and innovate business models
- **Value Proposition Canvas**: A tool used to design and innovate value propositions for a product or service

## **Key Takeaways**

- Experience design is a business-driven discipline that requires a clear business case to justify investment and resources
- Experience should be measurable, justifiable, and accountable to deliver tangible business value
- Business cases should include a clear CVP, ROI, and CLV to evaluate the success of an experience design project
