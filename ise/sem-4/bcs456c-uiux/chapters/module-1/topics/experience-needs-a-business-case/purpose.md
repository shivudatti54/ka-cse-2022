Learning "Experience needs a business case" is crucial in UI/UX as it underscores the significance of understanding the financial and strategic implications of user experience design. This topic matters because it highlights the need to justify and measure the value of user experience initiatives to stakeholders.

Real-world applications of this concept include:

- Conducting cost-benefit analyses to evaluate the ROI of UX design interventions
- Justifying design decisions to stakeholders, such as product managers or executives
- Developing business cases to secure funding for UX initiatives

This concept connects to other concepts in UI/UX, such as:

- User research and analysis, which informs design decisions and justifies investment in UX initiatives
- Design thinking and human-centered design, which prioritize user needs and experiences
- Business acumen and strategic planning, which require understanding the financial and operational implications of UX design.
