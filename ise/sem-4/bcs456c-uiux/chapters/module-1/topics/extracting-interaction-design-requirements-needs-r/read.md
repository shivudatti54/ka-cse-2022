# **Extracting Interaction Design Requirements: Needs & Requirements**

## **Introduction**

Interaction design is a crucial aspect of user experience (UX) design, as it focuses on the cognitive and motor processes that users engage with a product, system, or service. The needs and requirements of users are essential in crafting an effective interaction design that meets their expectations and provides a seamless experience. In this study material, we will explore the concepts of needs, requirements, and how to extract them to inform interaction design.

## **What are Needs?**

Needs refer to the fundamental reasons why users interact with a product, system, or service. They are the underlying motivations and goals that drive user behavior. Needs can be categorized into:

- **Functional needs**: The basic functions that users require from a product, such as navigation, communication, or information retrieval.
- **Emotional needs**: The emotional connections users form with a product, such as satisfaction, pleasure, or relaxation.
- **Social needs**: The social interactions and relationships users engage with, such as collaboration, community, or social status.

## **What are Requirements?**

Requirements are the specifications and constraints that define the functional and non-functional expectations of users. They are the explicit and implicit needs that users have, which interaction designers must prioritize when designing an interaction.

## **Types of Requirements**

There are two types of requirements:

- **Primary requirements**: The essential needs and expectations that users have, which must be met to ensure a basic level of functionality.
- **Secondary requirements**: The discretionary needs and expectations that users have, which are not essential but can enhance the overall experience.

## **Extracting Interaction Design Requirements**

To extract interaction design requirements, follow these steps:

1. **Conduct user research**: Gather data through interviews, surveys, usability testing, and contextual inquiry to understand users' needs and behaviors.
2. **Identify primary and secondary requirements**: Determine the essential and discretionary needs that users have.
3. **Analyze user behavior**: Observe how users interact with existing systems and products to identify patterns and pain points.
4. **Prioritize requirements**: Prioritize the requirements based on their importance and impact on the user experience.

## **Key Concepts**

- **User personas**: Representative user profiles that capture their needs, goals, and behaviors.
- **User journeys**: The sequence of interactions users engage with to achieve a specific goal.
- **Wireframes**: Low-fidelity visual representations of the interaction design.
- **Prototyping**: Interactive models of the interaction design to test and refine the experience.
- **Usability testing**: Testing the interaction design with real users to identify usability issues.

## **Best Practices**

- **Keep it simple**: Prioritize clarity and simplicity in the interaction design.
- **Be consistent**: Establish consistent patterns and layouts throughout the interaction.
- **Test and refine**: Continuously test and refine the interaction design based on user feedback.
- **Consider accessibility**: Ensure the interaction design is accessible to all users, including those with disabilities.

By understanding the needs and requirements of users, interaction designers can create effective and engaging interaction designs that meet the expectations of users and provide a seamless experience.
