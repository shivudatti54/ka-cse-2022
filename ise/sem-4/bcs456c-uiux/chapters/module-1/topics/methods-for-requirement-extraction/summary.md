# **Methods for Requirement Extraction**

**Definition:** Requirement extraction is the process of identifying and gathering data to create a detailed and accurate description of the system's functional and non-functional requirements.

**Importance:** Requirements extraction is a crucial step in the software development life cycle, as it provides a solid foundation for the design, development, and testing phases.

**Methods for Requirement Extraction:**

- **Interviews:**
  - In-depth interviews with stakeholders and users
  - Structured interviews to gather specific information
- **Surveys:**
  - Online and offline surveys to gather data from a larger audience
  - Closed-ended and open-ended questions
- **Observational Studies:**
  - Observing users interacting with the system
  - Note-taking and data collection
- **Document Analysis:**
  - Analyzing existing documents and reports
  - Identifying patterns and requirements
- **Focus Groups:**
  - Group discussions with stakeholders and users
  - Exploring different perspectives and opinions

**Formulas and Theorems:**

- **Kaplan and Norton's Balanced Scorecard:**
  - A framework for measuring organizational performance
  - Four perspectives: financial, customer, internal processes, and learning and growth
- **DeMarco's Nine Windows:**
  - A framework for understanding user needs and requirements
  - Nine windows: users, business, customers, technical, organizational, communication, project, market, and society

**Key Principles:**

- **Active listening:** Paying attention to users' needs and concerns
- **Empathy:** Understanding users' perspectives and feelings
- **Validation:** Verifying requirements through testing and feedback

**Important Terms:**

- **Functional requirements:** Requirements that define what the system should do
- **Non-functional requirements:** Requirements that define how the system should behave
- **Usability:** The degree to which a system is easy to use and understand
- **User experience:** The overall experience a user has when interacting with a system
