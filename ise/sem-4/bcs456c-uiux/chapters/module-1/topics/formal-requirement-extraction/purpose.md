# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1.  Define requirements elicitation and explain its critical importance in the software development lifecycle.
2.  Identify and categorize different types of stakeholders involved in a software project.
3.  Compare and contrast at least five different requirements elicitation techniques, including interviews, surveys, workshops, observation, and prototyping.
4.  Select appropriate elicitation techniques for a given project scenario and justify the selection.
5.  Describe common challenges faced during requirements elicitation and propose strategies to mitigate them.
6.  Explain how requirements elicitation activities integrate with subsequent steps of requirements analysis and validation.
