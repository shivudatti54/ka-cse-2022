# Learning Purpose: Extracting Interaction Design Requirements

### 1. Why is this topic important?

Extracting requirements is the critical first step in the design process. Without accurately identifying and understanding user needs, business goals, and technical constraints, a team risks building a product that is unusable, inefficient, or fails to meet its objectives. This topic establishes the foundation for all subsequent design and development work.

### 2. What will students learn?

Students will learn the core techniques for gathering and defining interaction design requirements. This includes conducting stakeholder interviews, facilitating user research (e.g., surveys, observation), creating user personas, and documenting user stories and scenarios. The outcome is the ability to synthesize findings into a clear, actionable requirements brief.

### 3. How does it connect to other concepts?

This process directly informs the information architecture (structuring content) and prototyping (building mockups) covered later in the module. The requirements serve as a benchmark for usability testing, ensuring the final product aligns with the initial user goals. It is the essential link between user research and actual design.

### 4. Real-world applications

This skill is applied in writing product briefs, feature specs, and user story maps. It is a fundamental task for UX researchers, product managers, and designers in agile and lean workflows, ensuring that every team member—from designers to developers—is aligned on what to build and why, ultimately saving time and resources.
