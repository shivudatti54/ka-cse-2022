# Module 1: From Usability to User Experience - Learning Purpose

### 1. Why is this topic important?

This topic is foundational because it shifts the perspective from a purely functional, task-completion view (usability) to a holistic understanding of the entire user's journey and feelings (user experience). In today's competitive digital landscape, products must not only work well but also be enjoyable, meaningful, and emotionally resonant to succeed and retain users.

### 2. What will students learn?

Students will learn the core definitions and differences between usability (efficiency, effectiveness, satisfaction) and user experience (encompassing emotions, beliefs, and perceptions). They will explore how UX builds upon a solid usability foundation by addressing user needs at a deeper level, aiming for delight and long-term engagement rather than just utility.

### 3. How does it connect to other concepts?

This topic is the cornerstone of the entire UI/UX curriculum. It provides the essential vocabulary and framework for all subsequent modules, such as user research, information architecture, interaction design, and prototyping. Understanding this hierarchy ensures design decisions are made with the full spectrum of the human experience in mind.

### 4. Real-world applications

This knowledge is directly applied when evaluating and designing any digital product. For example, a banking app must be usable (allowing easy money transfer) but a great UX makes the process feel secure, empowering, and even rewarding. This principle dictates product strategy, feature prioritization, and success metrics beyond mere bug counts.
