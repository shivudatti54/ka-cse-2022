### Learning Purpose: User Experience Needs a Business Case

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding how to build a business case for UX is critical because it moves UX from a perceived "nice-to-have" cost center to a strategic investment. It provides the justification for budget, resources, and executive buy-in, ensuring user-centered design is a priority from project inception.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to articulate the tangible value of UX in business terms. This includes identifying and quantifying key benefits such as increased conversion rates, reduced development costs (by fixing issues early), improved customer retention, and higher user satisfaction. They will learn the structure of a compelling proposal that aligns user goals with business objectives.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the foundational bridge between pure design theory and practical business strategy. It connects directly to subsequent modules on user research (to gather evidence for the case), UX metrics (to measure success), and design justification (to communicate ROI). It establishes UX as a core business function, not just a creative one.

**4. Real-world applications**
Professionals use this skill to secure funding for UX teams, justify design decisions to stakeholders, and prioritize features on a product roadmap. A strong business case is essential for any UX designer, researcher, or strategist aiming to prove their impact and drive organizational change.
