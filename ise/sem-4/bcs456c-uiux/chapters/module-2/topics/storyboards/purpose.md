# Learning Purpose: Storyboards

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Storyboards are a vital communication and ideation tool in UI/UX design. They transform abstract user needs and feature lists into a tangible, human-centered narrative. This visual storytelling technique is crucial for aligning cross-functional teams (developers, stakeholders, designers) on a shared vision for the user experience before investing time and resources in high-fidelity prototypes or code, preventing costly missteps.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to construct effective storyboards that depict a user's journey through a product. This includes defining a user persona, mapping key scenarios and tasks, sketching the sequence of interactions, and highlighting pain points and emotional states. They will develop the skill to use storyboards not just as a design artifact, but as a persuasive tool to advocate for user needs.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon **user research and personas** (Module 1), using that data to create narratives. It is a foundational step that comes after empathy mapping and before **wireframing and prototyping** (Module 3), providing the contextual "why" behind the screen layouts. It also connects to **usability testing**, as storyboards help formulate test scenarios.

**4. Real-world applications**
Storyboards are used to pitch product concepts to clients, secure funding, and guide the entire design process. They are applied when designing new features, improving existing user flows, and explaining complex system interactions. From a simple mobile app flow to a multi-step enterprise software process, storyboards make the user's story clear and compelling for everyone involved.
