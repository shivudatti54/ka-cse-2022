# Design Influencing User Behaviour

### Definitions and Theorems

- **Behavioral Design**: The use of design to influence user behavior, encouraging desired actions and outcomes.
- **User Behavior Theory**: The study of how users think, feel, and act when interacting with products, services, or systems.
- **Watt's Law**: The law of energy conservation, applicable to user behavior: "Energy = Work + Waste". (Waste = the amount of time and mental effort a user has to spend on a task)
- **Jakob Nielsen's Law**: "Every user clicks where they expect to click". (User behavior is influenced by expectations and familiarity)

### Key Concepts

- **User Experience (UX)**: The sum of all interactions between a user and a product, service, or system.
- **User Interface (UI)**: The visual and interactive elements that users interact with.
- **Heuristic Evaluation**: A usability testing method that assesses the design's adherence to cognitive heuristics.
- **Decision Fatigue**: The concept that users experience decreased motivation and increased fatigue due to repetitive decision-making.
- **Social Proof**: The phenomenon where users are influenced by the behavior of others, such as reviews and ratings.

### Design Principles and Techniques

- **Keep it Simple, Stupid (KISS)**: Design should be intuitive and easy to use.
- **Consistency**: Design should be consistent in terms of layout, typography, and behavior.
- **Feedback**: Provide immediate and clear feedback to users to reinforce desired behavior.
- **Gamification**: The use of game design elements to influence user behavior.
- **Anxiety of Missing Out (AOMO)**: Design that creates a sense of FOMO can influence user behavior.

### Important Formulas and Metrics

- **Conversion Rate Formula**: Conversion rate = (Number of successful outcomes / Total number of attempts) x 100
- **User Engagement Metrics**: Time spent, pages viewed, bounce rate, and click-through rate.

### Key Takeaways

- Design should be guided by user behavior theory and principles.
- Behavioral design can be used to influence user behavior and encourage desired actions.
- Design should be intuitive, consistent, and provide clear feedback to users.
