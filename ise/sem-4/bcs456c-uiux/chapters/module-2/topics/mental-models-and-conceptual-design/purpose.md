### **Learning Purpose: Mental Models and Conceptual Design**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Mental models are the internal representations users form about how a system works. Designing a UI that aligns with these pre-existing models drastically reduces cognitive load, making products feel intuitive and easy to learn. A flawed conceptual design that clashes with user expectations leads to confusion, frustration, and ultimately, product abandonment.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify and leverage users' mental models to inform design decisions. They will master techniques for creating key conceptual design artifacts, such as user flows, information architecture (IA) diagrams, and concept models. This process translates user research into a coherent blueprint for the entire user experience before visual design begins.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the crucial bridge between foundational research (Module 1: User Research) and concrete design (Module 3: Wireframing & Prototyping). It directly relies on personas and user stories to create realistic models and flows. A strong conceptual design ensures the subsequent prototypes and visual interfaces are built on a logical, user-centered foundation.

**4. Real-world applications**
This skill is applied when structuring the navigation for a website, mapping the checkout process for an e-commerce app, or organizing content in a complex SaaS tool. It is the foundational step for creating any digital product that feels effortless and logical to its users, from a simple mobile app to an enterprise software suite.
