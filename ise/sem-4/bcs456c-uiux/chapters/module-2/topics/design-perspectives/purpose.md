### Learning Purpose: Design Perspectives

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding different design perspectives is crucial because it moves students beyond a single, personal viewpoint. It fosters empathy, a core tenet of human-centered design, by revealing how diverse stakeholders (users, clients, developers, business owners) perceive a product. This awareness is fundamental to creating inclusive, effective, and commercially viable designs that satisfy both user needs and business goals.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify and analyze key perspectives, including the user's mental model, the business's commercial objectives, and the technical constraints of development. They will practice techniques like stakeholder mapping and perspective-shifting exercises to evaluate design choices from multiple angles, leading to more holistic and robust solutions.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the bridge between foundational user research (Module 1) and the practical application of design principles (later modules). It directly informs information architecture, interaction design, and usability testing by providing the critical lens through which all subsequent design decisions must be viewed and justified.

**4. Real-world applications**
This skill is applied daily in collaborative Agile/Scrum environments. Designers must effectively communicate and justify their work to product managers, argue for user needs against technical constraints with engineers, and ensure the final product aligns with the client's brand and business strategy, ultimately leading to higher adoption and success rates.
