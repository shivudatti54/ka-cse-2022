# **Sketching Summary**

**Definition:** Sketching is a design thinking technique used to generate and explore ideas through quick, informal drawings.

**Key Points:**

- **Benefits:**
  - Fosters creativity and imagination
  - Encourages rapid prototyping and iteration
  - Helps to identify and articulate design problems
  - Develops empathy and understanding of user needs
- **Types of Sketching:**
  - Freehand sketching
  - Digital sketching (e.g., paperless prototype)
  - Mind mapping
  - Wireframing
- **Design Principles:**
  - Keep it simple and intuitive
  - Focus on functionality and usability
  - Use simple shapes and lines
  - Experiment with different materials and tools
- **Design Thinking Framework:**
  - Empathize: Understand user needs and pain points
  - Ideate: Generate ideas and concepts
  - Prototype: Create a physical or digital representation
  - Test: Validate and refine the design
- **Important Formulas and Theorems:**
  - None specific to sketching, but understanding of design principles and human-centered design is essential.

**Key Theories and Concepts:**

- **User-Centered Design:** Prioritizes user needs and experience
- **Design Thinking:** A human-centered approach to problem-solving
- **Usability:** The ease with which a product can be used by its target audience

**Revision Tips:**

- Practice sketching regularly to improve your skills
- Use different materials and tools to explore different ideas
- Focus on keeping your sketches simple and intuitive
- Practice iterating and refining your ideas through sketching and prototyping.
