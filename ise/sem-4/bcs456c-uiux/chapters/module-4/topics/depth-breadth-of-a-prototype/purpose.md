# Learning Purpose: Depth & Breadth of a Prototype

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the depth (fidelity, detail, and functionality) and breadth (scope and range of features) of a prototype is crucial. It is the primary tool for communicating design intent, validating ideas, and identifying usability issues efficiently. Mastering this balance prevents wasted resources on over-engineered concepts or superficial tests that fail to uncover critical flaws.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to strategically choose the appropriate depth and breadth for a prototype based on its specific purpose—be it for a usability test, stakeholder feedback, or technical validation. They will differentiate between low-fidelity (e.g., paper sketches) and high-fidelity (e.g., interactive digital) prototypes and understand when to use each.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This concept directly connects to earlier modules on user research (informing what to prototype), information architecture (structuring the breadth), and interaction design (defining the depth). It is a foundational skill for effective usability testing and is essential for creating persuasive presentations to stakeholders and collaborating with developers.

**4. Real-world applications**
This skill is applied when quickly mocking up a user flow (breadth) to test navigation or creating a single, highly detailed screen (depth) to demonstrate a complex micro-interaction to a developer. It is used daily by UX designers, product managers, and researchers to iterate rapidly and de-risk product development.
