### **Learning Purpose: Paper Prototypes**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Paper prototyping is a foundational, low-fidelity technique crucial for fast, inexpensive, and collaborative design iteration. It shifts the focus from pixel-perfect visuals to core functionality and user flow, allowing teams to test and validate ideas before any code is written. This prevents costly changes later in the development process and ensures the product is built on a validated user-centered foundation.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to translate wireframes and user flows into tangible, testable paper models. They will practice crafting interactive elements (e.g., buttons, menus) and facilitating usability tests using these prototypes. The core skill is gathering actionable feedback on structure and navigation, not visual design, fostering a mindset of iterative improvement and user empathy.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly applies the theoretical concepts learned in earlier modules on user research, information architecture, and wireframing. It is the practical bridge between low-fidelity plans (wireframes) and high-fidelity, interactive prototypes. The feedback gathered here directly informs and refines the subsequent visual and interaction design stages.

**4. Real-world applications**
This technique is universally used in agile and lean startup environments for brainstorming, concept validation, and early usability testing. From designing a new app feature to planning a complex website redesign, paper prototyping is a vital tool for UX researchers, designers, and product managers to align stakeholders and build the right product.
