# Learning Purpose: Fidelity of Prototypes

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding fidelity is crucial because it dictates the cost, time, and effectiveness of the design feedback loop. Selecting the wrong fidelity can waste resources by over-building a concept for early feedback or failing to communicate a complex interaction adequately. It is a fundamental skill for efficient collaboration with stakeholders, developers, and users.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define and distinguish between low-fidelity (lo-fi) and high-fidelity (hi-fi) prototypes. They will master the appropriate use cases for each, such as using lo-fi sketches for rapid ideation and hi-fi interactive models for usability testing and developer handoff. The goal is to strategically select the right tool for each design phase.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic connects directly to prior modules on **Design Thinking** (ideation and testing) and **Wireframing**. It is a practical application of those principles and a prerequisite for subsequent modules on **Usability Testing** and **Design Handoff**, where the prototype's fidelity directly impacts the quality of feedback and implementation.

**4. Real-world applications**
This skill is applied daily by UX designers to:

- Quickly validate ideas with low-cost paper prototypes.
- Create pixel-perfect, interactive prototypes to secure stakeholder buy-in.
- Build detailed hi-fi prototypes that serve as a single source of truth for developers, ensuring a smooth transition from design to a functional product.
