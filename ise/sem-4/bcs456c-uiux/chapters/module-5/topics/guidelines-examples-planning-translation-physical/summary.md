# UX Design Guidelines: Revision Notes

=====================================

## Guidelines & Examples

- Design guidelines: standardized principles for designing user interfaces
- Examples:
  - Nielsen's heuristics
  - Jakob Nielsen's 10 usability principles
  - Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG)
- Formula: None

## Planning

- Planning: designing and organizing the user interface
- Theorem: "The user interface is the most important part of the product, as it is the first point of contact with the user."
- Definition: User-centered design (UCD)
- Formula: None

## Translation

- Translation: adapting design guidelines to different contexts
- Formula: None
- Definition: Adaptation of design principles to accommodate different user needs and environments

## Physical Action

- Physical action: designing interactions and behaviors
- Theorem: "The user's physical action should be intuitive and easy to understand."
- Definition: User experience (UX)
- Formula: None

## Outcomes

- Outcomes: evaluating the effectiveness of the design
- Formula: Nielsen's usability metrics:
  - Time on task
  - Errors
  - Satisfaction
- Theorem: "The design should be evaluated based on its effectiveness in achieving the desired outcome."

## Assessment

- Assessment: measuring the design's success
- Formula: None
- Definition: Evaluation of the design's usability and effectiveness

## Overall

- Overall: considering the design's impact and feedback
- Theorem: "The design should be iterative and continuous, with ongoing feedback and evaluation."
- Definition: Design thinking
- Formula: None

This summary should provide a concise overview of the key concepts and formulas related to UX design guidelines.
