### Learning Purpose: Module 3 - UX Measures

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding UX measures is crucial because it moves design decisions from subjective opinion to objective, data-driven validation. It provides the evidence needed to justify design choices, secure stakeholder buy-in, and ultimately create products that are not just usable but also effective and satisfying for real users.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify, define, and apply key quantitative and qualitative UX metrics. This includes performance metrics (e.g., task success rate, time-on-task), self-reported metrics (e.g., System Usability Scale - SUS), and behavioral metrics. They will gain hands-on experience with methods for collecting this data, such as usability testing and surveys, and learn how to analyze results to uncover actionable insights for iterative improvement.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the critical bridge between the theoretical concepts learned in earlier modules—like user research, wireframing, and prototyping—and the practical evaluation of those designs. It directly applies the user-centered design process by providing the tools to test prototypes and measure the outcomes of the UX strategies developed throughout the course.

**4. Real-world applications**
These skills are directly applicable to roles in product management, UX research, and design. Students will be equipped to conduct usability tests for a website, benchmark an app's experience against competitors, use analytics to identify pain points in a user flow, and present data-backed recommendations to improve a product's overall user experience.
