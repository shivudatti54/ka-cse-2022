### Learning Purpose: Basic Connectives and Truth Tables

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is fundamental because logic is the "grammar" of computer science and mathematics. It provides the rigorous framework for constructing, analyzing, and validating arguments, algorithms, and digital circuits. Mastering basic connectives and truth tables is the essential first step toward formal reasoning.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify and use the five primary logical connectives (NOT, AND, OR, IMPLIES, IFF). They will develop the skill to construct truth tables for any compound proposition, enabling them to determine its truth value under all possible scenarios and classify it as a tautology, contradiction, or contingency.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This knowledge is the direct prerequisite for more advanced topics in this course, such as logical equivalences, inference rules, and predicate logic. It is also the foundational language for subsequent studies in algorithm design (e.g., evaluating conditions), database querying (e.g., SQL WHERE clauses), and digital logic design (e.g., building logic gates).

**4. Real-world applications**
These concepts are applied daily in software development for writing conditional statements (`if/else`), crafting database search queries, and designing electrical circuits. Furthermore, they are crucial in fields like cybersecurity for formulating security policies and in legal reasoning for evaluating the validity of arguments.
