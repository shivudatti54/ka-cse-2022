### 1. Why is this topic important?

Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) in this context is crucial as it ensures students actively and consistently engage with the abstract and foundational concepts of Group Theory. Mastery of these concepts is essential for advanced studies in computer science, cryptography, and mathematics. CIE provides structured feedback, identifies learning gaps early, and promotes a deeper, more retained understanding than a single high-stakes exam.

### 2. What will students learn?

Students will learn to systematically apply the axioms of groups (closure, associativity, identity, inverse) to various algebraic structures. They will develop the skill to prove whether a given structure is a group, identify different types of groups (e.g., finite, cyclic, abelian), and analyze subgroup relations. The CIE process will assess and reinforce their ability to construct and comprehend these logical proofs and arguments.

### 3. How does it connect to other concepts?

Group Theory provides the algebraic foundation for numerous other domains. It directly connects to cryptography (e.g., cyclic groups in AES and RSA encryption), computer science (algebraic coding theory, symmetry in data structures), and even other areas of discrete mathematics like graph theory (automorphism groups) and combinatorics.

### 4. Real-world applications

The principles learned are applied in modern cryptography for securing digital communications, in graphics programming for modeling symmetries and rotations, and in error-correcting codes that ensure data integrity in storage and transmission systems like CDs and satellites.
