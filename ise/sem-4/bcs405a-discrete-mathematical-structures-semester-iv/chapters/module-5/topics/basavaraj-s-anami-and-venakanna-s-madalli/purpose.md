More than 50% of the world's population resides in urban areas, making cities the epicenters of modern life and the primary drivers of global change. Understanding urban systems is therefore crucial for addressing pressing challenges like climate change, resource management, and social equity. This module provides the foundational knowledge to analyze cities not as simple collections of buildings, but as complex, interconnected systems.

Students will learn to deconstruct the city into its core components—social, economic, environmental, and infrastructural—and understand the dynamic flows (e.g., of people, information, capital, water, energy) that connect them. They will move from a static view of urban form to a dynamic understanding of urban function.

This systemic perspective connects directly to other critical concepts in planning, such as sustainability (balancing these systems for long-term health), resilience (how systems withstand and adapt to shock), and justice (how the benefits and burdens of urban systems are distributed). It is the essential framework for diagnosing urban problems and designing effective, holistic interventions for creating more livable, sustainable, and equitable cities.
