# Learning Purpose: NoSQL Databases and Big Data Storage Systems

## 1. Why is this topic important?

This topic is critical because traditional relational databases are often inadequate for handling the massive volume, variety, and velocity of modern data (Big Data). Understanding NoSQL systems is essential for designing scalable, high-performance applications that power today's largest web services, social platforms, and data-driven enterprises.

## 2. What will students learn?

Students will learn the fundamental concepts, types (document, key-value, column-family, graph), and characteristics of NoSQL databases. They will understand the trade-offs between BASE and ACID properties and explore specific Big Data storage systems like Hadoop HDFS and Apache Cassandra, including their architecture and use cases.

## 3. How does it connect to other concepts?

This module builds upon prior knowledge of relational models (Module 2) and database architecture (Module 1). It contrasts SQL with NoSQL paradigms, providing a more complete picture of the data management landscape. It also connects to concepts of distributed systems, scalability, and data integrity.

## 4. Real-world applications

NoSQL databases are the backbone of major tech companies like Amazon (DynamoDB), Google (Bigtable), and Netflix (Cassandra). They are applied in social media feeds, real-time analytics, IoT sensor data management, personalized recommendations, and any scenario requiring flexible schemas and horizontal scalability.
