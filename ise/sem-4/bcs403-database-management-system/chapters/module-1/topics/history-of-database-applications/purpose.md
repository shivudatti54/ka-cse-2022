# Learning Purpose: History of Database Applications

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the history of database applications is crucial because it provides context for why modern database systems are designed the way they are. It highlights the evolution from simple file-based systems to complex, networked, and relational models, showcasing the continuous effort to solve problems of data redundancy, inconsistency, and inefficient access. This historical perspective prevents us from repeating past mistakes and helps appreciate the sophisticated tools we use today.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn about the key milestones in database technology, including the limitations of file-based systems, the development of hierarchical and network models, and the revolutionary advent of the relational model by E.F. Codd. They will understand the drivers behind each evolutionary step, such as the need for data independence, reduced redundancy, and more powerful query capabilities.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic lays the essential groundwork for the entire course. It directly connects to subsequent modules on data models (relational, hierarchical, network), database architecture (three-schema architecture), and SQL. Knowing the "why" behind the relational model's dominance makes learning its concepts, like normalization and relational algebra, more intuitive and meaningful.

**4. Real-world applications**
The evolution chronicled here explains the existence of legacy systems still in use today (e.g., hierarchical databases in mainframes) and the rationale for migrating to modern relational or NoSQL systems. It shows how technological progress in databases has been fundamental to the development of everything from ATMs and airline reservations to massive web applications like social media and e-commerce platforms.
