# **Mapping Conceptual Design into a Logical Design: Relational Database Design using ER-to-Relational mapping**

### Key Points

- **ER Diagram**: Entity-Relationship Diagram, a visual representation of data entities and their relationships.
- **Attribute**: A characteristic of an entity, represented by a column in the database table.
- **Relationship**: A connection between two or more entities, represented by a line in the ER diagram.
- **Entity Type**: A group of related attributes that describe an entity.

### ER-to-Relational Mapping

---

- **1:1 Relationship**: One-to-one relationship, represented by a single line in the ER diagram.
- **1:N Relationship**: One-to-many relationship, represented by a single line and a dashed line in the ER diagram.
- **M:N Relationship**: Many-to-many relationship, represented by two dashed lines in the ER diagram.
- **ER to Relational Schema Conversion**: Mapping ER entities to relational tables.
- **Entity-Attribute-Value (EAV)**: Representing attributes as separate rows.

### Important Formulas and Definitions

---

- **Normalization**: Ensuring data consistency and reducing data redundancy.
- **Denormalization**: Temporarily reducing data consistency for performance improvement.
- **Primary Key (PK)**: A unique identifier for each entity.
- **Foreign Key (FK)**: A field in a table that references the primary key of another table.

### Theorems

---

- **Entity-Relationship Theorem**: Ensures that the ER model is consistent and can be converted to a relational schema.
- **Data Dependency Theorem**: Ensures that the data dependencies are maintained in the relational schema.
