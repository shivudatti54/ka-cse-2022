# **Revision Notes: (8 hours) Optimization Technique**

## **Introduction**

- Also known as the "8-hour rule" or "8-hour workday"
- Suggests that the human brain can focus for approximately 8 hours before it needs a rest

## **Key Points**

### Formulas and Definitions

- **Attention span**: The amount of time a person can focus on a task before their attention wanders (approximately 8 hours)
- **Ultradian rhythms**: The natural cycles of the human brain that occur every 90-120 minutes, influencing productivity and focus
- **Work-rest ratio**: The ratio of work to rest time, which can be influenced by the 8-hour workday

### Theorems and Principles

- **Circadian rhythm**: The internal biological clock that regulates the sleep-wake cycle, influenced by the 8-hour workday
- **Productivity cycle**: The natural cycles of productivity that occur throughout the day, influenced by the 8-hour workday
- **Task segmentation**: Breaking down large tasks into smaller, manageable chunks to optimize productivity

### Applications

- **Time management**: Using the 8-hour workday to optimize productivity and work-life balance
- **Workplace design**: Designing workplaces to accommodate the ultradian rhythms and work-rest ratio
- **Personal productivity**: Using the 8-hour workday to set realistic goals and prioritize tasks

### Important Theorems and Studies

- **Ulric Neisser's study** (1967): Found that the human brain can focus for approximately 8 hours before it needs a rest
- **Hick's Law** (1952): Describes the relationship between reaction time and the amount of time spent on a task, relevant to the 8-hour workday
