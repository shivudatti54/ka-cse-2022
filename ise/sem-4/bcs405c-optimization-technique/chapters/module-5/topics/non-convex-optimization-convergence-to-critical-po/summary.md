# Non-Convex Optimization: Convergence to Critical Points

### Overview

Non-convex optimization is a field of study that deals with finding the global optimum of a non-convex objective function. This summary focuses on the convergence of non-convex optimization methods to critical points.

### Definitions and Theorems

- **Non-convex function**: A function whose level sets (contours of constant value) are not convex.
- **Critical point**: A point where the gradient of the objective function is zero or undefined.
- **Karamardian's Theorem**: For a non-convex function, if the gradient of the function is Lipschitz continuous, then the critical points are the only possible local minima.

### Convergence Theorems

- **Weierstrass Theorem**: Every continuous function on a compact set has a local minimum.
- **Karush-Kuhn-Tucker (KKT) Theorem**: A necessary and sufficient condition for a local minimum of a non-convex function is that the gradient of the function is zero at the point and the KKT conditions are satisfied.

### Convergence Methods

- **Gradient Descent (GD)**: An iterative method that converges to a local minimum of a convex function, but may not converge to a global minimum of a non-convex function.
- **Newton's Method**: An iterative method that converges to a local minimum of a convex function, but may not converge to a global minimum of a non-convex function.
- **Conjugate Gradient Method**: An iterative method that converges to a local minimum of a convex function, but may not converge to a global minimum of a non-convex function.

### Important Formulas

- **Gradient**: The vector of partial derivatives of the objective function with respect to each variable.
- **Hessian Matrix**: A matrix of second partial derivatives of the objective function with respect to each variable.

### Conclusion

Non-convex optimization is a challenging field of study that deals with finding the global optimum of a non-convex objective function. The convergence of non-convex optimization methods to critical points is a complex topic that involves various theorems and formulas.
