# **Revision Notes: Optimization Technique (8 hours)**

### Overview

---

- Optimization technique is used to find the maximum or minimum value of a function subject to constraints.
- This technique involves finding the critical points of the function and determining whether they correspond to maxima or minima.

### Key Concepts

---

- **Function**: A relation between a set of inputs (called the domain) and a set of possible outputs (called the range).
- **Derivative**: A measure of how a function changes as its input changes.
- **Critical Point**: A point where the derivative of a function is zero or undefined.
- **Second Derivative Test**: A method for determining whether a critical point corresponds to a maximum or minimum.

### Important Formulas and Theorems

---

- **First Derivative Test**:
  - If f'(x) > 0, then f(x) is increasing and the critical point is a local minimum.
  - If f'(x) < 0, then f(x) is decreasing and the critical point is a local maximum.
- **Second Derivative Test**:
  - If f''(x) > 0 at a critical point, then f(x) has a local minimum at that point.
  - If f''(x) < 0 at a critical point, then f(x) has a local maximum at that point.
- **Lagrange Multiplier Method**: A method for finding the maximum or minimum of a function subject to a constraint.

### Important Definitions

---

- **Optimization Problem**: A problem that involves finding the maximum or minimum value of a function subject to constraints.
- **Constraint**: A restriction on the values that the variables can take.

### Important Theorems

---

- **Extreme Value Theorem**: Any continuous function on a closed interval has a maximum and minimum value.
- **Weierstrass Theorem**: Any continuous function on a closed and bounded interval has a maximum and minimum value.
