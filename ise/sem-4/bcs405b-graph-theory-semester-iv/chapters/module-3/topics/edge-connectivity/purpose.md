### Learning Purpose: Edge Connectivity

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Edge connectivity is a fundamental concept in graph theory that quantifies a network's resilience to failure. It measures the minimum number of edges whose removal disconnects a graph, providing a precise metric for network robustness. Understanding this is crucial for designing and analyzing systems where continuous connection is vital.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define and compute the edge connectivity (`λ(G)`) of a graph. They will explore its relationship with the minimum vertex degree and master key theorems, such as Menger’s Theorem for edge-disjoint paths. The goal is to equip them with the skills to algorithmically determine the connectivity of any graph.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is deeply connected to vertex connectivity, network flows, and paths. It builds upon the foundational knowledge of graph types (e.g., connected graphs) and serves as a prerequisite for more advanced topics like max-flow min-cut theorems and the design of reliable networks.

**4. Real-world applications**
The principles of edge connectivity are directly applied to enhance the reliability of critical infrastructure, including computer networks, transportation systems, and power grids. It is essential for identifying vulnerabilities and ensuring these networks remain operational even if certain connections fail.
