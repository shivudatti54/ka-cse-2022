**Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE)**

**Introduction:**  
The Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) is a system of assessment conducted by educational institutions during an academic term. It emphasizes regular evaluation over the entire duration of the course rather than relying solely on end-term examinations.

**Key Components:**

1. **Regular Assessments:** CIE includes periodic tests, quizzes, assignments, projects, and laboratory work.
2. **Comprehensive Coverage:** It ensures that all portions of the syllabus are evaluated progressively.
3. **Feedback Mechanism:** Provides timely feedback to students, helping them identify areas for improvement.

**Advantages:**

- Reduces the burden and stress associated with single final exams.
- Encourages consistent study habits.
- Allows for a more holistic assessment of a student's capabilities.

**Conclusion:**  
CIE fosters a continuous learning process, making education more effective and aligned with the goal of in-depth understanding and skill development.

**Answer:**  
The Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) is an assessment method aimed at evaluating students' performance regularly throughout the semester. It typically includes components like unit tests, assignments, seminars, and projects. CIE helps in identifying students' strengths and weaknesses early, providing opportunities for improvement before the final examinations. This system promotes consistent academic effort and reduces dependence on a single end-term exam.

**Example:** In a typical engineering course, CIE might constitute 50% of the total marks, with the remaining 50% from the end-semester exam.

Thus, CIE ensures a balanced and continuous approach to student assessment.
