### Learning Purpose: Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) in Graph Theory

**1. Why is this topic important?**
The CIE is a critical pedagogical tool designed to shift the focus from a single high-stakes exam to a continuous, formative assessment process. It is important because it allows for regular evaluation of a student's conceptual understanding, problem-solving skills, and ability to apply Graph Theory principles. This ongoing feedback helps identify and address learning gaps early, ensuring steady progress and reducing end-of-semester pressure.

**2. What will students learn?**
Through the CIE, students will learn to demonstrate their proficiency in core Module 5 concepts (like planarity, graph coloring, or network flows, depending on your syllabus) through various methods. They will practice solving complex problems under time constraints, articulate theoretical concepts clearly in written assignments, and potentially collaborate in practical projects, reinforcing a deep and applied understanding.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
The problems and applications tested in the CIE will inherently connect Module 5 topics to previously learned concepts from earlier modules. For instance, a question might require using adjacency matrices (Module 2) to solve a network flow problem (Module 5) or applying traversal algorithms (Module 3) to a real-world scenario, demonstrating the cohesive and cumulative nature of the subject.

**4. Real-world applications**
The CIE will assess the ability to model real-world systems as graphs. This includes applications like optimizing network infrastructure (computer networks, utility planning), scheduling problems (exam timetabling using graph coloring), logistics (finding shortest paths for delivery routes), and analyzing social network connections, directly linking theoretical knowledge to practical, impactful problems.
