### Learning Purpose: Hamiltonian Paths and Circuits

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is a cornerstone of graph theory, introducing fundamental concepts that distinguish paths which traverse every vertex exactly once. Understanding Hamiltonian paths and circuits is crucial for analyzing network efficiency and connectivity, forming the theoretical basis for many algorithmic problems in computer science and operations research.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define, identify, and differentiate between Hamiltonian paths and cycles. They will explore necessary and sufficient conditions for their existence (e.g., Dirac's and Ore's theorems) and analyze why finding them is computationally difficult (NP-complete), introducing them to algorithmic complexity.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
It builds directly on prior knowledge of Eulerian paths and cycles, highlighting the key difference between traversing every _edge_ versus every _vertex_. This connects to graph connectivity, degree sequences, and provides a foundation for studying the Traveling Salesman Problem (TSP) and other optimization challenges.

**4. Real-world applications**
Applications include designing optimal routes for delivery services and logistics, network packet routing, sequencing tasks in manufacturing, designing circuit boards, and solving puzzles like the Knight's Tour on a chessboard.
