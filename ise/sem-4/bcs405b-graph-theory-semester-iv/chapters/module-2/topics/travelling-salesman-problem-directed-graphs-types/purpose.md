### Learning Purpose: Travelling Salesman Problem & Directed Graphs

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is fundamental as it introduces two critical, high-impact concepts in discrete mathematics and computer science. The Travelling Salesman Problem (TSP) is a classic optimization problem that models complex routing and scheduling tasks. Directed graphs (digraphs) are essential for representing relationships with inherent direction, such as those found in networks, dependencies, and flows.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to formally define and understand the computational complexity of the TSP, including approaches for finding approximate solutions. They will also learn to classify different types of digraphs (e.g., simple, symmetric, asymmetric, tournaments) and analyze their properties, such as connectivity and the existence of cycles.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module builds directly on prior knowledge of basic graph theory (from Module 1), including vertices, edges, and paths. It connects the TSP to algorithmic design, heuristic methods, and the study of NP-hard problems. Understanding digraphs provides a crucial foundation for subsequent topics like network flows, Markov chains, and database structures.

**4. Real-world applications**
The TSP is applied in logistics (package delivery routes), manufacturing (circuit board drilling), and DNA sequencing. Digraphs model real-world systems like the World Wide Web (link structure), social networks (follower relationships), task scheduling, and transportation networks (one-way streets).
