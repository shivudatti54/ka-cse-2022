### Learning Purpose: Euler Graphs

**1. Why is this topic important?**
The study of Euler graphs forms a cornerstone of Graph Theory. It addresses a fundamental and historically significant problem—finding a path that traverses every edge exactly once—which is the basis for many optimization and connectivity challenges in network science.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to characterize graphs that contain an Eulerian circuit or path using necessary and sufficient conditions (related to vertex degrees). They will develop the ability to algorithmically identify such paths and understand the fundamental differences between Eulerian and Hamiltonian paths.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is deeply connected to concepts of graph connectivity and vertex degrees. It provides a crucial foundation for later topics like Hamiltonian graphs (which focus on vertices) and serves as a key application for algorithms used in network analysis, circuit design, and operations research.

**4. Real-world applications**
The principles of Euler graphs are directly applied in designing efficient routes for garbage collection, postal delivery, and network routing protocols. They are also used in DNA fragment assembly in bioinformatics and in designing circuits on printed circuit boards (PCBs).
