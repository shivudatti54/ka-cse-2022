# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and distinguish between Eulerian and Hamiltonian paths and circuits
2. Apply the necessary and sufficient conditions for Eulerian paths/circuits in both directed and undirected graphs
3. Identify whether a given graph contains Eulerian or Hamiltonian paths/circuits
4. Understand the computational complexity differences between Eulerian and Hamiltonian problems
5. Apply relevant theorems (Dirac's, Ore's) to determine the existence of Hamiltonian cycles
6. Solve practical problems using Eulerian and Hamiltonian path concepts
