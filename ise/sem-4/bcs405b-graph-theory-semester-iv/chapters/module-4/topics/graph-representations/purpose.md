# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and construct the adjacency matrix and adjacency list representations for both directed and undirected graphs, including weighted variants.
2. Calculate the space complexity (in Big-O notation) for both representations given a graph's vertex and edge count.
3. Analyze the time complexity of fundamental operations (edge lookup, neighbor finding, edge insertion/removal) for each representation.
4. Compare and contrast the two representations, identifying the specific scenarios (e.g., dense vs. sparse graphs, common operations) where one is superior to the other.
5. Translate between a graphical depiction of a graph and its matrix/list representations, and vice versa.
6. Justify the choice of an appropriate graph representation for a given problem based on its requirements and constraints.
