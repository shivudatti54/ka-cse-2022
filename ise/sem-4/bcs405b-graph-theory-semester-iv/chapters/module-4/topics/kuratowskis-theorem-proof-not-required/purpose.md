### Learning Purpose: Kuratowski's Theorem

**1. Importance:**
Kuratowski's Theorem is a cornerstone of graph theory, providing a rigorous and elegant characterization of planar graphs. It is crucial because it moves beyond ad-hoc methods, offering a definitive, non-constructive test for planarity by identifying the fundamental obstructions: the complete graph K₅ and the complete bipartite graph K₃,₃.

**2. Student Learning:**
Students will learn the formal statement of Kuratowski’s theorem—that a finite graph is planar if and only if it does not contain a subgraph that is a subdivision of K₅ or K₃,₃. They will learn to identify these forbidden topological minors within more complex graphs, developing a critical eye for analyzing graph structure without needing a formal proof.

**3. Connections:**
This topic directly builds upon prior knowledge of graph isomorphism, subgraphs, subdivisions, and the definitions of planarity and non-planar graphs (like K₅ and K₃,₃ from earlier modules). It provides the theoretical foundation for algorithms that test for planarity, linking a key theoretical concept to practical computational applications.

**4. Real-World Applications:**
The theorem has significant applications in fields requiring efficient, conflict-free layout planning. This includes designing circuit boards (PCB design) to prevent wire crossings, optimizing transportation networks, and creating effective data flow diagrams in software engineering, ensuring clarity and minimizing complexity in system design.
