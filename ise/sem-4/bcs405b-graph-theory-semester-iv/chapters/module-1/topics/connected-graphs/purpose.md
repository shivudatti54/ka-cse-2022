### Learning Purpose: Connected Graphs

**1. Importance:**  
This topic is fundamental to graph theory as connectedness defines whether a network is traversable. Understanding it is crucial for analyzing the structure, efficiency, and robustness of any networked system.

**2. Learning Outcomes:**  
Students will learn to define and identify connected graphs, distinguish between connected and disconnected components, and understand key concepts like paths, cycles, bridges, and cut-vertices. They will also be introduced to algorithms for determining connectivity.

**3. Connections:**  
This concept directly builds upon prior knowledge of vertices, edges, and paths. It is a prerequisite for understanding more advanced topics like network flows, shortest path algorithms (Dijkstra's), Eulerian and Hamiltonian paths, and graph isomorphism.

**4. Real-World Applications:**  
Connected graphs model critical infrastructure like computer networks (ensuring all devices can communicate), social networks (mapping communities), and transportation systems (guaranteeing cities are reachable). Analyzing connectivity helps diagnose network vulnerabilities and design resilient systems.
