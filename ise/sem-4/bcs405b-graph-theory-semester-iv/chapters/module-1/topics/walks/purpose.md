# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1.  Precisely define and distinguish between a walk, a trail, and a path in a graph.
2.  Identify whether a given sequence of vertices and edges constitutes a walk, trail, path, circuit, or cycle.
3.  Explain the significance of paths in determining the connectivity of a graph.
4.  Correctly apply the definitions to small graphs, including multigraphs.
5.  Understand the hierarchical relationship: all paths are trails, and all trails are walks.
6.  Draw simple graphs and examples of walks, trails, and paths to illustrate the concepts.
