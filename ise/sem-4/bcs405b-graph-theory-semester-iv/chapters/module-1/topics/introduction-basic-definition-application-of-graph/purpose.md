### Learning Purpose: Introduction to Graph Theory

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Graph Theory is a fundamental pillar of discrete mathematics and computer science. It provides a powerful language and set of tools for modeling pairwise relationships between objects, making it essential for understanding complex networks and structures that appear across numerous scientific and engineering disciplines.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the basic definitions and terminology of graph theory, including vertices, edges, degrees, and simple graphs. They will understand the properties of finite graphs and be introduced to the vast array of practical applications that stem from these foundational concepts.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This introductory module lays the essential groundwork for all subsequent topics in graph theory, such as connectivity, paths, trees, and graph algorithms. It connects directly to other fields like data structures (trees, graphs), algorithms (searching, shortest path), linear algebra (adjacency matrices), and set theory.

**4. Real-world applications**
The applications are extensive and include modeling computer networks (routing), social networks (community detection), transportation systems (optimal routes), project scheduling (PERT charts), and the semantic web (knowledge graphs). Understanding these basics is the first step toward solving complex real-world problems.
