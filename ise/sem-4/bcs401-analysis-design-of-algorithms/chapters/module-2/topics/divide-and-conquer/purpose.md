# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1.  Describe the three fundamental steps (Divide, Conquer, Combine) of the Divide and Conquer paradigm.
2.  Analyze the time complexity of Divide and Conquer algorithms by formulating and solving their recurrence relations.
3.  Apply the Master Theorem to solve standard recurrence relations and determine algorithmic complexity.
4.  Explain the working of classic Divide and Conquer algorithms like Merge Sort, Quick Sort, and Binary Search, including their best-case, worst-case, and average-case time complexities.
5.  Differentiate between the Divide and Conquer strategy and other algorithm design paradigms like Dynamic Programming and Greedy Algorithms.
6.  Design a simple Divide and Conquer algorithm for a given problem by identifying the base case, division strategy, and combination method.
