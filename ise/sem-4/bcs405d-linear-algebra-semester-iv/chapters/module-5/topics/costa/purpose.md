### Learning Purpose: Costa's Method in Linear Algebra

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Costa's method is a significant optimization technique used to solve systems of linear equations, particularly those arising from least-squares problems. It is important because it provides a computationally efficient and stable alternative to direct methods like Gaussian elimination, especially for large-scale or ill-conditioned systems where numerical precision is critical.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the principles behind Costa's iterative method, including its algorithmic steps and convergence criteria. They will gain practical skills in implementing this technique to approximate solutions for `Ax = b` and understand how it minimizes the residual error, connecting it to the concept of orthogonal projection.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly on foundational knowledge of vector spaces, matrix decompositions (like QR factorization), and the theory behind least-squares solutions. It serves as a practical application of these concepts and contrasts with other iterative methods (e.g., Gauss-Seidel) and direct solution methods.

**4. Real-world applications**
Costa's method is applied in fields requiring solutions to large systems of equations, such as computational fluid dynamics, finite element analysis for structural engineering, and image reconstruction in computer tomography. Its efficiency makes it valuable in data fitting, signal processing, and any scenario involving extensive numerical computations.
