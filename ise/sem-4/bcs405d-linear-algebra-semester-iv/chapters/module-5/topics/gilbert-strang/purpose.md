### Learning Purpose: Gilbert Strang & Optimization Techniques

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Gilbert Strang is a seminal figure in linear algebra education. Studying his approach, particularly his emphasis on the fundamental connection between matrices and transformations, is crucial for developing a deep, intuitive understanding of optimization. His work provides the conceptual framework that underpins many advanced computational techniques.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to interpret and apply Strang’s key concepts, such as viewing matrix multiplication as a linear combination of columns and the column space. They will solidify their understanding of the four fundamental subspaces and, most importantly, learn how these ideas are used to solve optimization problems, including formulating and solving least-squares regression via the normal equations.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds upon earlier knowledge of vector spaces, orthogonality, projections, and eigenvalues. Strang’s "fundamental theorem of linear algebra" elegantly ties these concepts together, showing how they are essential for understanding the solvability of linear systems (`Ax=b`) and for finding optimal solutions, especially when exact solutions do not exist.

**4. Real-world applications**
The techniques derived from this framework are foundational across data science and engineering. Key applications include least-squares regression for machine learning and data fitting, computer graphics and animations using linear transformations, and optimizing solutions in fields like economics and network flow.
