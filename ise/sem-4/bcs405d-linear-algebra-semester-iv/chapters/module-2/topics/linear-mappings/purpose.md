### Learning Purpose: Linear Mappings

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Linear mappings are the fundamental language for describing transformations that preserve the structure of vector spaces. They are the bridge between abstract vector space theory and practical computation, forming the core of countless advanced mathematical and engineering concepts.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define a linear transformation and identify its key properties: kernel (null space) and image (range). They will master representing these transformations as matrices relative to a basis, enabling them to compute the action of a transformation on any vector. Techniques for finding a basis for both the kernel and image will also be covered.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is a direct application of previously learned concepts like vector spaces, subspaces, basis, and dimension (e.g., the Rank-Nullity Theorem). It provides the essential foundation for future topics, including eigenvalues/eigenvectors, diagonalization, and inner product spaces. The matrix representation directly links abstract linear algebra to computational methods.

**4. Real-world applications**
Linear mappings are indispensable in computer graphics for rotations and scaling, in data science for dimensionality reduction techniques like Principal Component Analysis (PCA), and in solving systems of differential equations. They are also crucial in quantum mechanics for representing state changes and in economics for modeling inputs and outputs in Leontief models.
