### Learning Purpose: Singular and Non-singular Linear Transformations

1. **Why is this topic important?**
   Understanding singular and non-singular transformations is fundamental because it distinguishes between transformations that are invertible (non-singular) and those that are not (singular). This concept is crucial for solving systems of linear equations, analyzing vector spaces, and forms the bedrock for more advanced topics like eigenvalues and diagonalization.

2. **What will students learn?**
   Students will learn to define and identify singular (non-invertible) and non-singular (invertible) linear transformations. They will master the connection between a transformation's invertibility, the rank and nullity of its associated matrix, and the determinant. This includes practical skills in determining invertibility and understanding the geometric implications of each type.

3. **How does it connect to other concepts?**
   This topic directly builds on prior knowledge of matrices, determinants, and vector spaces. It is intrinsically linked to fundamental concepts like the rank-nullity theorem and forms a prerequisite for understanding eigenvectors, diagonalization, and the theory behind solving `Ax = b`.

4. **Real-world applications**
   These concepts are vital in computer graphics for reversible transformations like rotations and scaling (non-singular), and in data science for identifying dependencies and reducing dimensionality (singular). They are also essential in engineering for analyzing the stability of systems and in cryptography for designing invertible encoding algorithms.
