# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1.  Explain the LIFO principle and why it is well-suited for specific computational problems.
2.  Differentiate between infix, prefix, and postfix notations of arithmetic expressions.
3.  Apply the stack-based algorithm to convert a given infix expression to its postfix form.
4.  Apply the stack-based algorithm to evaluate a given postfix expression.
5.  Describe the role of the stack in managing function calls and the call stack.
6.  Identify and explain at least three other real-world applications of stacks (e.g., backtracking, DFS, undo/redo).
