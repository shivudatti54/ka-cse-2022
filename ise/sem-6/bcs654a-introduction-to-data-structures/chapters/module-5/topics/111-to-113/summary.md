# Summary of 11.1 to 11.3: Sorting Algorithms

=====================================================

## Introduction

---

- **Sorting**: The process of arranging data in a specific order.
- **Types of Sorting**:
  - **Comparison-based sorting**: Compares elements to determine order.
  - **Non-comparison sorting**: Uses other techniques to determine order.

## Bubble Sort

---

- **Algorithm**: Repeatedly iterates through data, swapping adjacent elements if in the wrong order.
- **Time Complexity**:
  - Best-case: O(n)
  - Average-case: O(n^2)
  - Worst-case: O(n^2)
- **Space Complexity**: O(1)

## Selection Sort

---

- **Algorithm**: Selects the smallest (or largest) element, swaps it with the first element, and repeats.
- **Time Complexity**:
  - Best-case: O(n^2)
  - Average-case: O(n^2)
  - Worst-case: O(n^2)
- **Space Complexity**: O(1)

## Insertion Sort

---

- **Algorithm**: Iterates through data, inserting each element into its proper position.
- **Time Complexity**:
  - Best-case: O(n)
  - Average-case: O(n^2)
  - Worst-case: O(n^2)
- **Space Complexity**: O(1)

## Important Formulas and Definitions

---

- **Big O notation**: Measures the performance of an algorithm.
- **Time complexity**: A measure of an algorithm's performance.
- **Space complexity**: A measure of an algorithm's memory usage.

## Theorems

---

- **Stability**: A sorting algorithm is stable if it preserves the relative order of equal elements.
- **In-place sorting**: A sorting algorithm that operates without using additional storage.
