### Learning Purpose: Module 1 - Comments

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Comments are a fundamental practice for writing clean, maintainable, and collaborative code. They act as in-code documentation, explaining the 'why' behind complex logic, making codebases understandable for other developers and for your future self. Ignoring them leads to technical debt, bugs, and difficult team collaboration.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the syntax for writing single-line and multi-line comments in relevant languages (e.g., HTML, CSS, JavaScript). More importantly, they will learn the principles of writing effective, meaningful comments—what to comment (purpose, not mechanics), what to avoid (redundant or outdated comments), and how to use them to temporarily disable code during debugging.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the first step in learning professional coding standards. It connects directly to later modules on code readability, debugging techniques, version control (where commit messages complement comments), and collaborative team workflows. It establishes a foundation for writing code that is not just functional but also professional and maintainable.

**4. Real-world applications**
In real-world full-stack development, comments are essential in every project. They are used to document complex API endpoints, explain intricate business logic in server-side code, provide context for front-end components, and create guides for other developers joining the project, ensuring long-term project health and efficient team onboarding.
