# Learning Purpose: Issue Tracker

### 1. Why is this topic important?

Issue tracking is the central nervous system of professional software development. It is a critical tool for organizing, prioritizing, and collaborating on work across the entire development lifecycle. Mastering it is essential for any developer working on a team, as it provides structure, ensures accountability, and creates a transparent record of a project's progress and history.

### 2. What will students learn?

Students will learn to use a modern issue tracker (e.g., Jira, GitHub Issues) to create, manage, and resolve different types of tickets (bugs, features, tasks). They will understand core concepts like workflows, labels, priorities, assignees, and sprints. The goal is to equip them to effectively participate in and contribute to a structured development process.

### 3. How does it connect to other concepts?

This topic connects directly to **Version Control (Git)** (linking commits to specific issues), **Agile Methodology** (managing sprints and backlogs), **Project Planning**, and **Quality Assurance** (bug reporting). It is the glue that integrates the technical work of coding with the project management side of development, making it a cornerstone of full-stack workflows.

### 4. Real-world applications

Issue tracking is used universally in tech. Students will apply this knowledge to:

- Report and fix bugs in a systematic way.
- Break down large features into manageable development tasks.
- Collaborate with project managers, designers, and other developers.
- Contribute to open-source projects on platforms like GitHub.
- Track their own personal projects professionally.
