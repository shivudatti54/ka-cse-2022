### Phased Application of Security in Operational Environments

===========================================================

### Overview

---

The phased application of security in operational environments is a critical aspect of securing the Internet of Things (IoT). It involves gradually implementing security measures to protect against various threats, ensuring the continuity of operations.

### Key Points

---

- **Threat Strategy Matrix**: A framework for categorizing threats based on their likelihood and impact, helping to prioritize security measures.
  - Definition: Threat strategy matrix (TSM)
  - Formula: TSM = Likelihood x Impact
- **Defense-in-Depth (DiD)**: A security approach that involves implementing multiple layers of defense to prevent threats from reaching the operational environment.
- **Phased Security Implementation**: A structured approach to implementing security measures, starting with the most critical assets and working towards less critical ones.
- **Risk Assessment and Prioritization**: Identifying and prioritizing potential security risks to ensure that the most critical threats are addressed first.
- **Security by Design**: Incorporating security considerations into the design of operational systems and processes to prevent security breaches.
- **Operational Security (OpSec)**: Protecting the operational environment from insider threats and unauthorized access.

### Important Formulas and Definitions

---

- **Threat Model**: A mathematical representation of potential threats to a system or process.
- **Vulnerability Management**: The process of identifying, classifying, and mitigating vulnerabilities in operational systems and processes.

### Theorems and Concepts

---

- **The Law of the Instrument**: All security measures have a limited effectiveness and should be used in conjunction with others to provide comprehensive protection.
- **The 80/20 Rule**: 80% of security risks are related to 20% of the assets, emphasizing the importance of prioritizing security efforts.
