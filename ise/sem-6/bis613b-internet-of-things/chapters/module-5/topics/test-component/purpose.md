### Learning Purpose: Testing IoT Components

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Testing is a critical phase in IoT development to ensure reliability, security, and functionality. A single faulty component can compromise an entire system, leading to data breaches, system failures, or safety hazards. This topic is vital for building robust and trustworthy IoT solutions.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core principles and methodologies for testing individual IoT components, including sensors, actuators, and communication modules. They will gain practical skills in developing and executing test cases, interpreting results, and debugging common hardware and software issues to validate performance against specifications.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module builds directly upon knowledge of IoT architecture (Module 1), sensor/actuator operation (Module 2), connectivity protocols (Module 3), and data handling (Module 4). It provides the essential verification step before integrating components into a full system and deploying them, linking hardware knowledge to software validation practices.

**4. Real-world applications**
These skills are directly applicable to quality assurance in manufacturing smart devices, validating environmental sensors for precision agriculture, ensuring the reliability of medical wearables, and stress-testing components in industrial automation and smart city infrastructure.
