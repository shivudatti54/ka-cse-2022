# Formal Risk Analysis Structures: OTAVE and FAIR

=====================================================

### Overview

This section provides an overview of two formal risk analysis structures: OTAVE and FAIR.

### Definitions

- **OTAVE**: Operational Technology (OT) Analysis Framework for Enterprise Risk
- **FAIR**: Formula for Assessing Risk
- **Risk**: The likelihood and potential impact of an event or circumstance that could cause harm to an organization

### OTAVE:

- **Key Components:**
  - Functional Requirements
  - Technical Requirements
  - Physical Requirements
  - Operational Requirements
  - Environmental Requirements
- **Steps:**
  1.  Identify the operational technology (OT) and its functions
  2.  Identify the threats and vulnerabilities
  3.  Assess the likelihood and impact of each threat
  4.  Calculate the risk score
- **Formula:**
  - Risk Score = (Likelihood x Impact) / (Mitigation Measures x Consequence)
- **Theorem:** OTAVE assumes that the likelihood and impact of a threat are independent, and that the mitigation measures and consequences can be quantified.

### FAIR:

- **Key Components:**
  - Probability of Occurrence (POO)
  - Loss Expectancy (LE)
  - Sensitive Losses (SL)
- **Steps:**
  1.  Estimate the probability of occurrence (POO) of a threat
  2.  Estimate the loss expectancy (LE) of a threat
  3.  Calculate the sensitive losses (SL)
  4.  Calculate the risk score
- **Formula:**
  - Risk Score = (POO x LE) / SL
- **Theorem:** FAIR assumes that the probability of occurrence and loss expectancy can be estimated using expert judgment and historical data.

### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Loss Function:** The function used to calculate the loss or cost associated with a threat or vulnerability.
- **Risk Matrix:** A table used to visualize the likelihood and impact of a threat or vulnerability.
- **Risk Register:** A document that summarizes the identified risks and their associated risk scores.
