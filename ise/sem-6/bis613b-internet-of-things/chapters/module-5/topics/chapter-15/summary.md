### Chapter 15 Summary: Internet of Things (IoT) Security

#### Key Concepts

- **IoT Security**: Ensuring the security and integrity of IoT devices and networks.
- **Common Challenges**:
  - Lack of standardization in IoT devices and protocols.
  - Limited visibility and control over IoT devices.
  - Insufficient security measures in IoT devices.
  - Increased attack surface due to connected devices.

#### Key Definitions

- **IoT Device**: A device that can connect to the internet and exchange data.
- **Secure by Design**: Designing IoT devices with security in mind from the outset.
- **Zero Trust Architecture**: An architecture that assumes all devices and users are untrusted.

#### Important Formulas

- **Shannon's Entropy Formula**: H(X) = - ∑ (p(x) \* log2 (p(x)))

#### Important Theorems

- **Shannon's Source Coding Theorem**: The minimum rate required to perfectly encode a source is equal to its entropy.

#### Key Theorems in IoT Security

- **Kerckhoffs' Principle**: The security of a system should not depend on secrecy.
- **Bell's Theorem**: Any attempt to physically measure the waveform of a quantum system will disturb its state.

#### Important Concepts in Chapter 15

- **OT Security**: Operational Technology security, which includes the security of industrial control systems.
- **IT Security**: Information Technology security, which includes the security of devices and networks used in IT systems.
- **Hybrid Security**: Combining IT and OT security practices to protect both types of systems.
