# **Chapter-5: Internet of Things (IoT)**

## **Key Points:**

- **Definition:** Internet of Things (IoT) - network of physical devices, vehicles, home appliances, and other items embedded with sensors, software, and connectivity, allowing them to collect and exchange data.
- **Types of IoT:**
  - **Consumer IoT:** Wearable devices, smart home devices, and fitness trackers.
  - **Industrial IoT (IIoT):** Industrial automation, industrial sensors, and machine-to-machine communication.
  - **Autonomous IoT:** Self-sufficient devices, such as drones and self-driving cars.
- **IoT Architecture:**
  - **Device:** Physical device with sensors and actuators.
  - **Gateway:** Connects devices to the internet.
  - **Cloud:** Centralized data storage and processing.
  - **Edge Computing:** Local processing and analysis of data at the edge.
- **IoT Protocols:**
  - **CoAP (Constrained Application Protocol):** For low-power, low-bandwidth devices.
  - **MQTT (Message Queue Telemetry Transport):** For real-time data transmission.
  - **HTTP (Hypertext Transfer Protocol):** For web-based IoT applications.

## **Important Formulas and Definitions:**

- **Bandwidth:** Amount of data that can be transmitted in a given time.
- **Latency:** Time delay between data transmission and reception.
- **Precision:** Degree of accuracy in sensor readings.
- **IoT Security:** Measures to prevent unauthorized access and data breaches.

## **Important Theorems and Concepts:**

- **Noisy Channel Coding:** Techniques to improve data transmission reliability.
- **Data Compression:** Methods to reduce data size and improve transmission efficiency.
- **IoT Network Topology:** Physical and logical arrangement of IoT devices.
