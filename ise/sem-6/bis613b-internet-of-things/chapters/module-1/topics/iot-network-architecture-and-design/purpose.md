# Learning Objectives
After studying this topic, you should be able to:
1.  Define an expert system and list its key application areas.
2.  Identify, describe, and explain the function of the six core components of a typical expert system architecture.
3.  Differentiate between the knowledge base and the inference engine and explain the principle of 'separation of knowledge and control'.
4.  Compare and contrast forward chaining and backward chaining reasoning strategies, providing examples of problems suited for each.
5.  Explain the importance of the explanation facility and the knowledge acquisition process in the expert system lifecycle.
6.  Analyze the advantages and limitations of expert systems compared to human experts and conventional programs.
