# **Chapter-2 Revision Notes: Internet of Things (IoT)**

## **I. Introduction to IoT**

- The Internet of Things (IoT) is a network of physical devices, vehicles, home appliances, and other items that are embedded with sensors, software, and connectivity, allowing them to collect and exchange data.
- The term "IoT" was first coined in 1999 by Kevin Ashton.

## **II. Genesis of IoT**

- The IoT concept is based on the idea of connecting physical devices to the internet to create a network of interconnected objects.
- The first IoT device is believed to be the "Smart Card" developed in the 1950s.
- The IoT gained momentum with the development of wireless communication technologies such as Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and Zigbee.

## **III. IoT Digitization**

- IoT digitization refers to the process of converting physical objects into digital entities that can interact with each other and the internet.
- IoT digitization enables the creation of smart objects that can collect and exchange data, enabling automation, efficiency, and innovation.

## **IV. IoT Impact**

- IoT has the potential to revolutionize various industries such as healthcare, manufacturing, transportation, and energy management.
- IoT can enable real-time monitoring and control, improve efficiency, and reduce costs.
- IoT also raises concerns about data security, privacy, and cybersecurity.

## **V. Converge IT and OT**

- Converge IT and OT refers to the integration of IT and Operational Technology (OT) systems to enable seamless communication and data exchange.
- Converge IT and OT enables the creation of smart cities, industrial automation, and smart homes.

## **Key Formulas, Definitions, and Theorems**

- **IoT Formula:** IoT = (Number of Devices) x (Network Connectivity) x (Data Exchange)
- **IoT Definition:** IoT: A network of connected devices, vehicles, home appliances, and other items that can collect and exchange data.
- **IoT Theorem:** The IoT theorem states that the IoT has the potential to revolutionize various industries and enable real-time monitoring and control.

## **Important Terms**

- **Smart Object:** An object that can collect and exchange data, enabling automation, efficiency, and innovation.
- **IoT Device:** A device that is embedded with sensors, software, and connectivity, allowing it to interact with the internet.
- **Converge IT and OT:** The integration of IT and Operational Technology (OT) systems to enable seamless communication and data exchange.
