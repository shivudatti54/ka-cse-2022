### Learning Purpose: Converge IT and OT

**1. Why is this topic important?**
The convergence of Information Technology (IT) and Operational Technology (OT) is the foundational principle of the Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT). Traditionally siloed, integrating these domains is critical for enabling data-driven automation, predictive maintenance, and unprecedented efficiency in industrial and business environments. Understanding this merger is essential for building and securing modern IoT ecosystems.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to distinguish between the historically separate domains of IT (data-centric systems) and OT (physical process-centric systems). They will explore the technical, cultural, and security challenges of integrating them and be introduced to key technologies that facilitate this convergence, such as industrial gateways, protocols, and edge computing platforms.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the crucial link between core IoT architecture (sensors, networks, cloud) and its practical application in industry. It provides the context for subsequent modules on IoT data analytics (what data is generated) and cybersecurity (how to protect newly connected physical systems). It directly applies networking and data communication principles in a industrial setting.

**4. Real-world applications**
This convergence is applied in smart factories (Industry 4.0), where production line data optimizes operations; in smart grids, where IT systems balance energy demand from OT sensors; and in intelligent logistics, where warehouse management systems automatically control robotic fulfillment centers.
