### **Learning Purpose: Genesis of IoT**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the genesis of IoT is crucial because it provides the foundational context for everything that follows. It explains the technological, economic, and conceptual shifts that made IoT possible, moving it from science fiction to a tangible driver of the Fourth Industrial Revolution. Without this historical lens, IoT can seem like a static set of modern gadgets rather than a dynamic, evolving ecosystem with deep roots and a clear trajectory.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the key milestones in the development of IoT, from early concepts like Kevin Ashton's coining of the term to the convergence of enabling technologies (miniaturization of sensors, the proliferation of IPv6, and cheap, low-power computing). They will identify the core components that define an IoT system and recognize the pioneers and foundational ideas that shaped its emergence.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the cornerstone of the entire curriculum. It directly sets the stage for subsequent modules on IoT architecture, communication protocols, sensor technology, and data analytics. It connects concepts from networking, embedded systems, and wireless communication, showing how their convergence created a new paradigm for interacting with the physical world.

**4. Real-world applications**
The genesis story explains the origin of now-commonplace applications. This includes the evolution from basic RFID inventory tracking to modern smart city infrastructure, wearable health monitors, and industrial predictive maintenance systems. Understanding this evolution allows for better forecasting of future IoT trends and innovations.
