## Introduction

### Chapter 1: Genesis of IoT

#### Key Points

- The term "Internet of Things" (IoT) was first coined by Kevin Ashton in 1999.
- IoT is a network of physical devices, vehicles, home appliances, and other items embedded with sensors, software, and connectivity, allowing them to collect and exchange data.
- The concept of IoT was first applied in 1997 in a Wal-Mart store in Michigan, USA, to track the inventory of oranges.
- IoT has its roots in the 1960s and 1970s, when automation and robotics were introduced in manufacturing.
- The development of the internet and microelectronics in the 1980s and 1990s laid the foundation for IoT.

## Definitions and Concepts

- **M2M (Machine-to-Machine)**: Communication between devices without human intervention.
- **IoT Architecture**: consists of four layers: application, network, data management, and transport.

## Important Theorems and Formulas

- **Noise and Signal**: Any physical phenomenon that affects the transmission of data can be considered noise.
- **Fading Coefficient**: represents the degree of signal attenuation due to the presence of noise.

## Influential Theories

- **Internet of Services (IoS)**: a new paradigm that views the internet as a platform for providing services rather than just transferring data.
- **Industrial Internet**: a concept that applies IoT to industrial settings to improve efficiency and productivity.

## Key Players and Milestones

- **Kevin Ashton**: coined the term "Internet of Things" in 1999.
- ** Cisco Systems**: introduced the "Internet of Things" framework in 2012.
- **NSF (National Science Foundation)**: funded several IoT-related research projects in the 1990s and 2000s.
