### **Learning Purpose: Connecting Smart Things**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Connecting devices is the foundational act that transforms isolated objects into a coordinated "Internet of Things." Without reliable and secure connectivity, the data collection, automation, and intelligence that define IoT are impossible. Understanding the methods and trade-offs of different connection protocols is crucial for designing scalable, efficient, and robust IoT systems.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core principles of how smart devices communicate. This includes exploring the spectrum of connectivity protocols (e.g., Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, Zigbee, LoRaWAN, Cellular), analyzing their key characteristics (range, bandwidth, power consumption), and understanding the role of gateways in bridging different network types to the cloud.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the critical link between the physical components (sensors/actuators from Module 1) and the data processing layer (cloud platforms in subsequent modules). The choice of connectivity directly impacts power management, network architecture, data throughput, and security, making it a central decision that influences the entire system design.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge applies directly to designing connected systems for smart homes (using Zigbee for light bulbs), industrial monitoring (using LoRaWAN for remote sensors), wearable tech (using Bluetooth for health bands), and urban infrastructure (using cellular for smart meters).
