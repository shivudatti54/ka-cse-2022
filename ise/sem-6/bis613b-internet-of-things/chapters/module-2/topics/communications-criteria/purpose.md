### Learning Purpose: Communications Criteria in IoT

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding communications criteria is fundamental because the choice of how IoT devices connect and exchange data directly impacts the performance, scalability, security, and power efficiency of an entire IoT system. Selecting an inappropriate protocol or technology can lead to system failure, security vulnerabilities, or unsustainable costs.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to evaluate and compare the key criteria for IoT communications, including bandwidth, latency, range, power consumption, and network topology. They will analyze major protocols (e.g., Wi-Fi, Bluetooth Low Energy, LoRaWAN, Zigbee, cellular) against these criteria to determine the most suitable technology for a given application.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly on the foundational knowledge of IoT architecture from Module 1. It is a prerequisite for subsequent modules on data management, cloud integration, and security, as the chosen communication method dictates how data is handled and protected throughout its journey.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied when designing systems like smart home networks (balancing range and power), city-wide environmental sensors (using LPWAN for long-range, low power), or industrial automation (requiring high bandwidth and low latency).
