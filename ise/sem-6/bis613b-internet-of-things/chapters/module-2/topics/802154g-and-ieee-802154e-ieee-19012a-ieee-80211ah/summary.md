# IoT Communication Technologies

### 802.15.4g and IEEE 802.15.4e

- **802.15.4g**: A high-speed variant of IEEE 802.15.4, using OFDM (Orthogonal Frequency Division Multiplexing) modulation.
- **IEEE 802.15.4e**: A low-power variant of IEEE 802.15.4, using ZIGbee modulation.
- Key characteristics:
  - Low power consumption
  - Long battery life
  - Small size and low cost
  - High data rate (up to 10 Mbps)

### IEEE 1901.2a

- A standard for high-speed wireless power transmission over power lines.
- Key characteristics:
  - High power transmission (up to 900 W)
  - Long distance transmission (up to 100 meters)
  - Low latency

### IEEE 802.11ah

- A variant of the IEEE 802.11 standard for low-power, low-bandwidth wireless communication.
- Key characteristics:
  - Low power consumption
  - Low data rate (up to 1 Mbps)
  - Long battery life
  - Small size and low cost

### LoRaWAN

- A low-power wide-area network (LPWAN) technology.
- Key characteristics:
  - Low power consumption
  - Long range (up to 15 km)
  - Low cost
  - Low data rate (up to 27 kbps)

### NB-IoT

- A narrowband IoT (NB-IoT) technology.
- Key characteristics:
  - Low power consumption
  - Long range (up to 10 km)
  - Low cost
  - Low data rate (up to 1.2 kbps)

## Formulas and Definitions

- **SNR (Signal-to-Noise Ratio)**: A measure of the ratio of the desired signal to the background noise.
- **BER (Bit Error Rate)**: A measure of the number of errors in a given data transmission.

## Theorems

- **Shannon-Hartley Law**: A theorem that describes the relationship between the bandwidth and the data rate of a communication system.
- **Fano's Inequality**: A theorem that describes the minimum number of bits required to represent a given probability distribution.
