### Learning Purpose: Module 2 - IEEE 802.11ah

**1. Why is this topic important?**
IEEE 802.11ah (HaLow) is a pivotal Wi-Fi standard designed for the Internet of Things. It is important because it addresses the critical challenges of connecting a massive number of low-power, long-range devices, which is a fundamental requirement for scaling IoT ecosystems beyond short-range and power-hungry solutions.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core technical specifications of 802.11ah, including its operation in the sub-1 GHz license-exempt bands. They will understand how its extended range (up to 1 km) and superior wall penetration are achieved, and how features like restricted access window (RAW) and target wake time (TWT) drastically reduce device power consumption.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic connects directly to previous modules on IoT architecture and wireless communication fundamentals. It provides a crucial comparison to other LPWAN technologies like LoRaWAN and NB-IoT, and contrasts with conventional Wi-Fi standards (e.g., 802.11ac/ax), highlighting the trade-offs between data rate, range, and power.

**4. Real-world applications**
Students will explore its use in large-scale sensor networks for smart agriculture, environmental monitoring, industrial automation, and smart city infrastructure—applications where traditional Wi-Fi is impractical and cellular connectivity is too expensive or power-intensive.
