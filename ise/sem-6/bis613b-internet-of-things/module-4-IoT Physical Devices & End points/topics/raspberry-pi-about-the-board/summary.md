# About the Board (IoT Boards)

=====================================

### Overview

IoT boards are specialized single-board computers designed to support IoT applications, consisting of a microcontroller or microprocessor, memory, communication modules, and various interfaces for connecting sensors, actuators, and peripherals.

### Key Points

- **Core Components:** Every IoT board includes a microcontroller/microprocessor (the brain), memory (RAM, ROM, flash), interfaces (GPIO, UART, SPI, I2C), and communication modules (Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, Ethernet, cellular).
- **Arduino Boards:** Known for ease of use and versatility, widely used in prototyping and hobbyist IoT projects with a simple programming environment.
- **Raspberry Pi Boards:** Offer a full Linux operating system with more computing power, suitable for applications requiring advanced processing and serving as IoT gateways.
- **ESP32/ESP8266 Boards:** Provide built-in Wi-Fi and Bluetooth connectivity, making them ideal for wireless IoT applications at low cost.
- **Intel Edison/Galileo Boards:** Offer high computing power for applications requiring advanced processing and analytics at the edge.
- **Applications:** Smart home automation, industrial automation and monitoring, wearable devices, and environmental monitoring systems.

### Important Concepts

- Microcontroller vs Microprocessor: MCUs are single-chip, low-power, ideal for dedicated tasks; MPUs are more powerful, require external memory, and can run full OS
- Interface types: GPIO for digital I/O, UART for serial communication, SPI for high-speed data, I2C for multi-device connections
- Board selection depends on application requirements: computing power, connectivity options, available interfaces, power consumption, and cost
- Communication modules enable both wireless (Wi-Fi, Bluetooth) and wired (Ethernet) connectivity

### Notes

- Exam questions often ask to compare different IoT boards (Arduino vs Raspberry Pi vs ESP32) based on their features and suitable use cases.
- Remember that the choice of IoT board depends on specific application requirements such as processing needs, connectivity, and power constraints.
- Be able to identify the key components of an IoT board and explain the role of each component in the overall system.
