# Learning Purpose: IoT in Smart Cities

**1. Why this topic matters**
Smart cities represent one of the largest and most impactful applications of IoT, where interconnected sensor networks and data platforms optimize urban infrastructure, public services, and resource management. Understanding how IoT enables smart city solutions is essential as urbanization accelerates globally and cities seek technology-driven approaches to sustainability and quality of life.

**2. What you will learn**
You will learn the core architectural components of smart city IoT ecosystems, including sensor networks for traffic, energy, waste, and environmental monitoring. You will analyze communication infrastructure requirements, data analytics platforms for city-scale decision-making, and citizen-facing applications that deliver the benefits of smart city investments.

**3. How it connects to other topics**
Smart cities integrate concepts from across all modules: large-scale sensor deployments (Module 1), network management with SDN/NFV and operator requirements (Module 2), Python-based data processing (Module 3), and Raspberry Pi prototyping for city applications (Module 4). The massive data volumes generated connect directly to Module 5's big data analytics with Hadoop and Spark.

**4. Real-world relevance**
Cities worldwide deploy IoT for adaptive traffic management, smart street lighting, environmental air quality monitoring, intelligent waste collection routing, and smart water metering. Barcelona, Singapore, and Amsterdam are leading examples where IoT has measurably improved urban efficiency, sustainability, and citizen satisfaction.
