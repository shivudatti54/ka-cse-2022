# IoT Physical Devices and Endpoints

=====================================

### Overview

IoT physical devices (also called endpoints or nodes) are the building blocks of the IoT ecosystem, equipped with sensors, actuators, and communication interfaces that enable them to interact with the physical world and exchange data with other devices and systems over the internet.

### Key Points

- **Sensing and Actuation:** IoT devices use sensors to detect environmental changes (temperature, humidity, motion, pressure) and actuators to perform physical actions (controlling lights, motors, valves).
- **Communication:** Devices communicate using various protocols such as Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, Zigbee, and cellular networks to exchange data with other devices and cloud systems.
- **Processing and Storage:** IoT devices have limited onboard processing and storage capabilities, often supplemented by cloud-based services for heavy computation and data storage.
- **Power and Energy:** Devices are designed to be energy-efficient using low-power protocols and energy-harvesting techniques to minimize power consumption.
- **Device Types:** Include sensors, actuators, microcontrollers (Arduino, Raspberry Pi, ESP32), gateways (routers, modems), and wearables (smartwatches, fitness trackers).
- **Applications:** Smart home devices (thermostats, security cameras), industrial automation, wearable technology, and autonomous vehicles.

### Important Concepts

- Four key characteristics: sensing, communication, processing, and energy efficiency
- Device categories: sensors, actuators, microcontrollers, gateways, and wearables
- Design considerations: security, interoperability, scalability, and energy efficiency
- Gateway devices bridge IoT sensor networks to the internet

### Notes

- Exam questions often ask to list and explain the key characteristics of IoT physical devices with examples.
- Security, interoperability, scalability, and energy efficiency are the four critical design considerations that must be addressed in long-answer questions.
- Be prepared to classify IoT devices by type and provide real-world application examples for each category.
