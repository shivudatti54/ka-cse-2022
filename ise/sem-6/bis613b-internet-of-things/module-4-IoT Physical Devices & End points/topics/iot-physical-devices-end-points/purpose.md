# Learning Purpose: IoT Physical Devices and Endpoints

**1. Why this topic matters**
Physical devices and endpoints are the "things" in the Internet of Things, forming the interface between the digital and physical worlds. They collect environmental data through sensors and execute actions through actuators. Understanding their design, specifications, and operational characteristics is essential for building IoT systems that perform reliably in their intended environments.

**2. What you will learn**
You will learn to identify and differentiate between various IoT sensors (temperature, humidity, motion, light), actuators (motors, relays, displays), and development boards (Arduino, Raspberry Pi, ESP32). You will understand key device specifications such as operating voltage, communication interfaces, accuracy, and power consumption that drive hardware selection decisions.

**3. How it connects to other topics**
This topic extends the IoT device concepts and connects directly to the Raspberry Pi topics in Module 4, where a specific endpoint device is explored in detail. The data captured by these endpoints is processed using Module 3's Python programming and transmitted using Module 2's communication protocols. Module 5's analytics consume the data these devices generate.

**4. Real-world relevance**
Endpoint selection is a critical students decision in every IoT project. Smart factory deployments use vibration sensors for predictive maintenance, precision agriculture relies on soil moisture sensors, and smart buildings deploy occupancy sensors for energy optimization. Each application demands specific endpoint characteristics and capabilities.
