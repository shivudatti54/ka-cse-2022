# Learning Purpose: IoT Platforms Design Methodology

**1. Why this topic matters**
IoT platforms provide the middleware layer that connects devices to applications, handling device management, data ingestion, analytics, and application enablement. Understanding how to evaluate and design around IoT platforms is critical because platform selection impacts scalability, vendor lock-in, development speed, and the long-term viability of an IoT solution.

**2. What you will learn**
You will learn how IoT platforms fit into the overall design methodology, including the criteria for evaluating platforms based on device support, protocol compatibility, data processing capabilities, and integration options. You will understand how platform choices influence the functional view specification and how different IoT levels map to platform capabilities.

**3. How it connects to other topics**
This topic extends the IoT Design Methodology by focusing on the platform selection step. It connects to Module 1's IoT levels (which determine platform requirements), Module 2's management protocols (which platforms must support), and Module 4's Raspberry Pi projects where specific platforms are used for cloud connectivity and data visualization.

**4. Real-world relevance**
Engineers evaluate platforms like AWS IoT Core, Azure IoT Hub, Google Cloud IoT, and ThingsBoard when building production systems. The design methodology helps teams compare platforms objectively, avoiding costly mid-project platform migrations and ensuring the chosen platform supports current requirements while accommodating future growth.
