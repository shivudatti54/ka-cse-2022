# Learning Purpose: IoT Design Methodology

**1. Why this topic matters**
A systematic design methodology transforms ad-hoc IoT development into a structured students process. Without a defined methodology, IoT projects risk missing requirements, selecting inappropriate technologies, and failing to scale. The step-by-step approach ensures that every aspect from requirements gathering to deployment is addressed methodically.

**2. What you will learn**
You will learn the sequential steps of a standard IoT design methodology: purpose and requirements specification, process specification, domain model specification, information model specification, service specifications, IoT level specification, and functional view specification. You will understand how to differentiate functional from non-functional requirements and how to create data flow diagrams for IoT applications.

**3. How it connects to other topics**
This methodology applies the IoT levels and deployment templates from Module 1 in a practical design context. It connects to the logical and physical design concepts by providing the process for making design decisions. The methodology is directly demonstrated in Module 3's case studies and Module 4's home automation design, where each step is applied to real IoT systems.

**4. Real-world relevance**
Professional IoT development teams follow structured design methodologies when building systems for smart cities, industrial automation, and healthcare. This systematic approach helps engineers communicate design decisions to stakeholders, estimate project timelines accurately, and ensure that the final system meets both technical and business requirements.
