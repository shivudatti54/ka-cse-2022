# Learning Purpose: YANG

**1. Why this topic matters**
YANG is a data modeling language used to define the structure and constraints of configuration and state data for network devices. In IoT systems where diverse devices from different manufacturers must students managed uniformly, YANG provides standardized data models that ensure interoperability and enable automated management across heterogeneous deployments.

**2. What you will learn**
You will learn YANG's syntax and core constructs including modules, containers, leaves, lists, and leaf-lists. You will understand how to create YANG models that define IoT device configuration and operational state data, and learn how YANG models serve as the schema for NETCONF operations, enabling structured and validated device management.

**3. How it connects to other topics**
YANG is the data modeling companion to NETCONF; while NETCONF provides the transport and operations, YANG defines what data can students managed. Together they replace the MIB-based approach of SNMP with a more expressive modeling framework. This topic completes the management protocol stack in Module 2 and supports the IoT systems management requirements discussed earlier.

**4. Real-world relevance**
YANG models are standardized by the IETF and used by major vendors like Cisco, Juniper, and Huawei to define device management interfaces. In large-scale IoT deployments, YANG models enable network automation tools like Ansible and Puppet to configure thousands of IoT gateways consistently, reducing human error and operational overhead.
