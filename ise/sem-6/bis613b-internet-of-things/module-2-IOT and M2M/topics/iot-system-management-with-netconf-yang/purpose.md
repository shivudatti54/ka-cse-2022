# Learning Purpose: IoT System Management with NETCONF-YANG

**1. Why this topic matters**
Managing billions of heterogeneous IoT devices at scale requires standardized, secure, and automated protocols. NETCONF-YANG provides a modern framework that overcomes the limitations of SNMP by offering transactional configuration management, structured data modeling, and mandatory encryption, making it the preferred approach for enterprise IoT deployments.

**2. What you will learn**
You will learn how NETCONF and YANG work together as an integrated management framework for IoT systems. This includes the NETCONF protocol stack, core operations like get-config and edit-config, YANG data modeling constructs, and how to design management workflows for configuring, monitoring, and maintaining IoT device fleets.

**3. How it connects to other topics**
This topic synthesizes the individual NETCONF and YANG concepts into a unified IoT management workflow. It builds on the need for IoT systems management and contrasts with SNMP-based approaches covered earlier in Module 2. The SDN/NFV concepts also connect here, as NETCONF serves as a southbound interface in SDN controllers managing IoT infrastructure.

**4. Real-world relevance**
Smart city deployments use NETCONF-YANG to manage thousands of sensors, gateways, and edge devices from a single controller. Industrial IoT platforms like those in smart grids and manufacturing plants rely on this framework for zero-touch provisioning, automated firmware updates, and real-time configuration rollback when device changes fail.
