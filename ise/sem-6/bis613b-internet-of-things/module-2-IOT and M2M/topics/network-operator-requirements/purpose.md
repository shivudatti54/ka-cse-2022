# Learning Purpose: Network Operator Requirements

**1. Why this topic matters**
Network operators provide the connectivity backbone that allows IoT devices to communicate across wide areas. Understanding their technical and business requirements including coverage, bandwidth, latency, power efficiency, and device density is essential because selecting the wrong network technology can lead to system failures, excessive costs, or an inability to scale an IoT solution.

**2. What you will learn**
You will learn to evaluate the key requirements that network operators impose on IoT deployments, including coverage area, Quality of Service (QoS), security protocols, and Service Level Agreements (SLAs). You will understand how to match specific IoT application needs with suitable network technologies such as LPWAN, cellular (NB-IoT, LTE-M), and 5G based on their characteristics.

**3. How it connects to other topics**
This topic builds on the IoT enabling technologies from Module 1 and the IoT vs M2M comparison in Module 2 by focusing on the network infrastructure perspective. It directly relates to SDN/NFV as solutions for operators to meet diverse IoT requirements, and connects to the management protocols (SNMP, NETCONF-YANG) that operators use to manage their IoT infrastructure.

**4. Real-world relevance**
Network operator requirements drive real deployment decisions, such as choosing NB-IoT for smart metering in urban areas or LoRaWAN for agricultural sensors in rural regions. Telecom operators like Vodafone and AT&T offer dedicated IoT connectivity plans, and engineers must understand these requirements to design viable commercial IoT products.
