# Learning Purpose: Physical Design of IoT

**1. Why this topic matters**
The physical design of an IoT system encompasses the tangible hardware components that collect data from the environment and perform actions. Understanding sensors, actuators, microcontrollers, and their interconnections is fundamental because hardware choices directly determine system capabilities, power consumption, cost, and reliability.

**2. What you will learn**
You will learn to identify and describe the key physical components of IoT systems including sensors, actuators, processing units (microcontrollers and microprocessors), and communication modules. You will understand how to select appropriate hardware based on application requirements and learn about power management considerations critical for battery-operated IoT devices.

**3. How it connects to other topics**
Physical design works hand-in-hand with the Logical Design topic, together forming the complete IoT system architecture covered in Module 1. The hardware components discussed here are the devices managed by protocols in Module 2, programmed using Python in Module 3, and specifically realized through Raspberry Pi in Module 4.

**4. Real-world relevance**
Physical design decisions are made in every IoT project, from choosing a temperature sensor for a weather station to selecting a communication module for a remote agricultural monitoring system. Proper hardware selection ensures that devices operate reliably in their intended environment while meeting power and cost constraints.
