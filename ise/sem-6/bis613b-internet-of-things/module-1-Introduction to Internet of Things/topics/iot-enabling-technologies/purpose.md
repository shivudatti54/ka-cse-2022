# Learning Purpose: IoT Enabling Technologies

**1. Why this topic matters**
IoT does not exist in isolation; it relies on a convergence of multiple technologies including Wireless Sensor Networks, cloud computing, Big Data analytics, RFID, and various communication protocols. Understanding these enabling technologies is critical because the choice of technology directly impacts an IoT system's range, power consumption, data throughput, and scalability.

**2. What you will learn**
You will learn to identify and describe core enabling technologies such as WSN, RFID, cloud computing, Big Data, and embedded systems. You will compare communication protocols like BLE, Zigbee, LoRaWAN, and NB-IoT based on their range, power consumption, and data rate, and understand how each maps to different layers of the IoT architecture.

**3. How it connects to other topics**
This topic builds on the IoT introduction and directly supports the Physical and Logical Design topics by providing the technology options available for each design layer. The networking technologies covered here are explored in greater depth in Module 2 through SDN, NFV, and management protocols like SNMP and NETCONF-YANG.

**4. Real-world relevance**
Selecting the right enabling technology is a critical students decision. For example, LoRaWAN enables long-range agricultural monitoring where Wi-Fi cannot reach, while BLE is ideal for short-range wearable health devices. These technology choices determine the feasibility, cost, and performance of every real-world IoT deployment.
