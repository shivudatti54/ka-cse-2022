# Learning Purpose: Introduction to Internet of Things

**1. Why this topic matters**
The Internet of Things represents a paradigm shift where everyday physical objects gain the ability to sense, communicate, and act on data. Understanding the foundational definition, characteristics, and significance of IoT is essential before exploring its architecture, protocols, and applications. This topic sets the stage for the entire BCS701 course by establishing core terminology and concepts.

**2. What you will learn**
You will learn the formal definition of IoT, its core characteristics such as connectivity, sensing, and intelligence, and how IoT differs from traditional networked systems. You will also understand the various IoT communication models including Request-Response, Publish-Subscribe, Push-Pull, and Exclusive Pair, along with their use cases.

**3. How it connects to other topics**
This introduction provides the conceptual foundation for the Physical Design and Logical Design topics that follow in Module 1. The communication models introduced here are further explored in Module 2 through protocols like SNMP and NETCONF, while the enabling technologies overview links directly to the IoT Enabling Technologies topic.

**4. Real-world relevance**
IoT is transforming industries ranging from healthcare and manufacturing to agriculture and transportation. Understanding these fundamentals prepares you to analyze real-world IoT deployments such as smart homes, connected vehicles, and industrial automation systems that are increasingly part of modern students practice.
