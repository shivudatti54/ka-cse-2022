## **Introduction to Artificial Intelligence**

## **7.1: Probabilistic Reasoning**

Probabilistic reasoning is a form of reasoning that involves making decisions based on uncertainty. In the context of artificial intelligence, probabilistic reasoning is used to make predictions, classify instances, and make decisions under uncertainty.

### **Definition:**

Probabilistic reasoning is a method of reasoning that involves making decisions based on the likelihood of different outcomes. It is a way of representing and updating probabilities in the face of new information.

### **Types of Probabilistic Reasoning:**

- **Bayesian Reasoning:** Bayesian reasoning is a type of probabilistic reasoning that involves updating probabilities based on new information. It is based on Bayes' theorem, which describes how to update the probability of a hypothesis based on new data.
- **Frequency-Based Reasoning:** Frequency-based reasoning is a type of probabilistic reasoning that involves making decisions based on the frequency of different outcomes. It is based on the idea that the probability of an outcome is equal to the number of times it has occurred in the past.

## **7.2: Statistical Reasoning**

Statistical reasoning is a form of reasoning that involves making decisions based on data analysis. In the context of artificial intelligence, statistical reasoning is used to analyze data, identify patterns, and make predictions.

### **Definition:**

Statistical reasoning is a method of reasoning that involves making decisions based on data analysis. It is a way of representing and analyzing data to make predictions or decisions.

### **Types of Statistical Reasoning:**

- **Descriptive Statistics:** Descriptive statistics is a type of statistical reasoning that involves describing and summarizing data. It is used to describe the central tendency and variability of data.
- **Inferential Statistics:** Inferential statistics is a type of statistical reasoning that involves making conclusions based on a sample of data. It is used to make predictions or decisions based on the data.

## **Key Concepts:**

- **Probability:** Probability is a measure of the likelihood of an event occurring.
- **Uncertainty:** Uncertainty is the lack of knowledge or information about an event or outcome.
- **Bayes' Theorem:** Bayes' theorem is a mathematical formula that describes how to update the probability of a hypothesis based on new data.
- **Frequency Distribution:** Frequency distribution is a table or graph that shows the frequency of different outcomes.
- **Hypothesis Testing:** Hypothesis testing is a method of statistical reasoning that involves testing a hypothesis based on data analysis.

## **Examples:**

- **Example 1:** A doctor wants to diagnose a patient with a disease based on symptoms. The doctor uses Bayesian reasoning to update the probability of the disease based on the symptoms.
- **Example 2:** A company wants to analyze customer data to identify patterns. The company uses statistical reasoning to analyze the data and identify trends.
