### **Module 3: Statistical Reasoning - Learning Purpose**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Statistical reasoning is the mathematical backbone of modern AI. It provides the formal framework for handling uncertainty, making predictions, and learning from data. Without it, AI systems could not reason probabilistically, model real-world noise, or provide confidence measures for their decisions, rendering them brittle and unreliable.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn core concepts of probability (e.g., Bayes' Theorem) and how they form the basis for reasoning under uncertainty. They will explore foundational statistical learning models like Naïve Bayes classifiers and Gaussian Mixture Models. The module will cover how to apply these models to tasks like classification, clustering, and anomaly detection.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module is a crucial prerequisite for more advanced topics. It provides the statistical foundation for Machine Learning (Module 4), where concepts like Bayesian networks and expectation-maximization are used. It also connects to earlier modules on problem-solving by introducing a probabilistic, rather than purely deterministic, approach to finding solutions.

**4. Real-world applications**
The principles learned are directly applied in spam filters, medical diagnosis systems (calculating disease probability), recommendation engines, and financial fraud detection. Any AI system that must deal with incomplete or noisy information relies on statistical reasoning to make informed inferences.
