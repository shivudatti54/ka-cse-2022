### Revision Notes: Text Book 1 - Ch 17 and 20

#### Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

#### Module: Learning, Expert Systems

**Key Concepts:**

- **Learning Algorithms:**
  - Supervised Learning: algorithm learns from labeled examples (e.g. MNIST dataset)
  - Unsupervised Learning: algorithm discovers patterns in unlabeled data (e.g. clustering)
  - Reinforcement Learning: algorithm learns through trial and error (e.g. Q-learning)
- **Expert Systems:**
  - Rule-based systems that mimic human expertise
  - Uses knowledge representation and inference engines
- **Defining Expert Systems:**
  - A computer program that mimics the decision-making ability of a human expert
  - Typically uses a knowledge base and inference engine

**Important Formulas and Definitions:**

- **Error Function (Loss Function):** measures difference between predicted and actual output
  - e = (y - y')^2
  - where y = actual output, y' = predicted output
- **Activation Function:** introduces non-linearity in neural networks
  - common examples: sigmoid, ReLU (Rectified Linear Unit)
- **Bayes' Theorem:** updates probability estimates based on new evidence
  - P(A|B) = P(A and B) / P(B)

**Important Theorems:**

- **No Free Lunch Theorem (NFL Theorem):** no algorithm can optimize both accuracy and simplicity
- **Occam's Razor:** simpler explanations are generally more preferable to complex ones

**Key Terms:**

- **Knowledge Representation:** ways to store and organize expert knowledge
- **Inference Engine:** component of expert systems that draws conclusions from knowledge base
- **Neural Networks:** networks of interconnected nodes (neurons) that process information
