### **Module 5: Introduction to Artificial Intelligence and Expert Systems**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a transformative force reshaping industries and society. Understanding its core principles, particularly the historical and foundational role of Expert Systems, is crucial. It provides the conceptual bedrock for modern AI, illustrating how human expertise can be modeled and automated, which is a fundamental goal of the field.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core objectives and defining characteristics of AI. They will understand the architecture, components (knowledge base, inference engine), and operation of rule-based Expert Systems. This includes exploring their strengths in capturing explicit knowledge for specific domains and their limitations in handling uncertainty.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds upon earlier knowledge of data structures (for the knowledge base) and algorithms (for the inference engine). It provides the historical context and technical foundation for subsequent topics like machine learning (as a successor to hand-coded knowledge) and neural networks (which handle different types of problems).

**4. Real-world applications**
Expert Systems were pioneering applications in medicine (e.g., MYCIN for diagnosing infections), technical support troubleshooting, and financial loan approval. These systems exemplify the practical application of symbolic AI and are the direct ancestors of today's advanced decision-support systems and chatbots.
