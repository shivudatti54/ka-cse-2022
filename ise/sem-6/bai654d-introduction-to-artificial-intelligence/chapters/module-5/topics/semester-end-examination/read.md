### Core Concepts for Examination

Based on the  curriculum, the exam will likely focus on the advanced topics that form the bridge between theoretical AI and practical, real-world systems. Key areas include:

#### 1. Natural Language Processing (NLP)
NLP is the branch of AI that gives machines the ability to read, understand, and derive meaning from human languages.

*   **Core Idea:** It involves transforming unstructured language data into a structured form that a computer can process. This includes syntax (grammar structure) and semantics (meaning).
*   **Key Processes:**
    *   **Tokenization:** Breaking text into words, phrases, or other meaningful elements (tokens).
    *   **Stemming and Lemmatization:** Reducing words to their base or root form (e.g., "running" → "run").
    *   **Part-of-Speech (POS) Tagging:** Labeling each word in a sentence as a noun, verb, adjective, etc.
    *   **Named Entity Recognition (NER):** Identifying and classifying named entities like people, organizations, and locations.
*   **Example:** A chatbot like ChatGPT uses NLP to parse your question, understand its intent, and generate a coherent, relevant response.

#### 2. Expert Systems
These are AI systems that emulate the decision-making ability of a human expert.

*   **Core Idea:** They are built on a **knowledge base** (a repository of facts and rules) and an **inference engine** (a set of rules to reason over the knowledge base).
*   **Components:**
    *   Knowledge Base: Stores domain-specific facts and heuristic rules (IF-THEN rules).
    *   Inference Engine: Applies logical rules to the knowledge base to deduce new information or make decisions.
    *   User Interface: Allows communication between the user and the system.
*   **Example:** A medical diagnosis expert system. A user inputs symptoms (e.g., fever and cough). The inference engine matches these symptoms against rules in its knowledge base (e.g., `IF fever AND cough THEN possible flu`) to suggest a diagnosis.

#### 3. Robotics and AI
This area deals with integrating AI to create intelligent agents that can perceive their environment and take actions autonomously.

*   **Core Idea:** Robotics combines AI with mechanical engineering and electrical engineering to create machines (robots) that can perform tasks in the physical world.
*   **Key Concepts:**
    *   **Perception:** Using sensors (e.g., cameras, LIDAR) to understand the environment.
    *   **Cognition:** The AI "brain" that processes sensor data, plans a path, and makes decisions. This often involves search algorithms and machine learning.
    *   **Action:** Using actuators (e.g., motors, arms) to carry out the planned actions.
*   **Example:** A autonomous mobile robot in a warehouse uses perception to avoid obstacles, cognition to find the shortest path to a shelf, and action to move there and pick up an item.

#### 4. AI for Vision: Computer Vision
Computer Vision enables machines to interpret and understand visual information from the world, such as images and videos.

*   **Core Idea:** It involves acquiring, processing, analyzing, and understanding digital images to extract high-dimensional data and produce decisions.
*   **Key Techniques:**
    *   **Image Segmentation:** Partitioning an image into multiple segments to simplify its analysis.
    *   **Object Detection:** Identifying and locating objects within an image (e.g., detecting cars in a traffic camera feed).
    *   **Facial Recognition:** A specific application of object detection that identifies or verifies a person from a digital image.
*   **Example:** The facial recognition unlock feature on your smartphone is a classic application of computer vision.
