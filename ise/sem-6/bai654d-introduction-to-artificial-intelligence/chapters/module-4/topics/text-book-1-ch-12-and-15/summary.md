### Game Playing

---

- **Game Tree Search**: A tree data structure representing all possible game states and actions
- **Minimax Algorithm**: A recursive algorithm for evaluating game states and selecting the best move
  - **Minimax Formula**: `M = -∞` (max player), `M = ∞` (min player), `M = v(s)` (terminal state), `M = α ≤ M ≤ β` (alpha-beta pruning)
- **Alpha-Beta Pruning**: Optimizing the minimax algorithm by pruning branches with a value outside the alpha-beta window
- **Game-Tree Traversal**: Iterating through the game tree to evaluate game states and select moves
- **Negamax Algorithm**: A variant of minimax using negation to handle minimax values
- **Heuristics**: Using knowledge of the game to guide the search

### Natural Language Processing (NLP)

---

- **Tokenization**: Breaking down text into individual words or tokens
- **Syntax Analysis**: Parsing the grammatical structure of text
- **Semantic Analysis**: Interpreting the meaning of text
- **Stemming and Lemmatization**: Reducing words to their base form
- **Named Entity Recognition (NER)**: Identifying named entities in text (e.g. people, places, organizations)
- **Part-of-Speech (POS) Tagging**: Identifying the grammatical category of each word

### Important Formulas and Definitions

---

- **Definition of a Decision Tree**: A tree data structure representing the decision-making process
- **Definition of a Neural Network**: A computational model inspired by the structure and function of the human brain
- **Definition of Supervised Learning**: A type of machine learning where the algorithm is trained on labeled data

### Theorems

---

- **No Free Lunch Theorem**: A mathematical theorem stating that no algorithm can perform better than average on all possible problems
- **No Learning Algorithm Theorem**: A mathematical theorem stating that no algorithm can learn from data without any constraints or assumptions
