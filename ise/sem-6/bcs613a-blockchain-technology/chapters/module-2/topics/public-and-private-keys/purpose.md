# Learning Purpose: Public and Private Keys

### 1. Why is this topic important?
Public and private key cryptography is the fundamental security mechanism underpinning all blockchain networks. It is essential for creating digital identities, securing transactions, and ensuring that only the rightful owner can access and control their digital assets. Without a deep understanding of this asymmetric encryption, one cannot truly comprehend how trust and ownership are established in a decentralized system.

### 2. What will students learn?
Students will learn the cryptographic principles behind key pairs: how a private key is used to create digital signatures and a public key is used to verify them. They will understand the one-way mathematical relationship between them and why the private key must be kept secret. This includes practical skills like generating a key pair and understanding the process of signing and verifying a transaction.

### 3. How does it connect to other concepts?
This knowledge is the cornerstone for understanding core blockchain concepts like digital wallets, transaction execution, and wallet addresses (which are derived from public keys). It directly connects to topics like mining (verifying signatures) and smart contracts (authorizing contract functions). It is also the prerequisite for exploring more advanced topics like multi-signature wallets and digital identity solutions.

### 4. Real-world applications
This technology is applied whenever cryptocurrency is sent or received, such as using a Bitcoin or Ethereum wallet. It also secures access to decentralized applications (dApps) and is used in digital identity verification, secure communication channels, and tokenizing real-world assets on a blockchain.
