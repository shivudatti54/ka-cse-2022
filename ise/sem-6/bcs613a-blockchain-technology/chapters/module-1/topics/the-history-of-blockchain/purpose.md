# Learning Purpose: The History of Blockchain

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the history of blockchain is crucial as it provides the foundational context for its entire ecosystem. It moves beyond viewing blockchain as merely a technical specification and reveals its original purpose: to create a system of decentralized trust without intermediaries. This historical perspective is key to appreciating the philosophical and ideological principles that underpin current applications like Bitcoin and Ethereum.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn about the key milestones in the development of distributed ledger technology, starting from the cryptographic and conceptual precursors (e.g., Merkle Trees, Hashcash) to the 2008 Bitcoin whitepaper by Satoshi Nakamoto. They will trace the evolution from Bitcoin's genesis block to the development of Ethereum and smart contracts, understanding the motivations behind each major innovation.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This historical foundation is essential for grasping subsequent module topics. It explains the "why" behind core concepts like decentralization, consensus mechanisms (Proof-of-Work), and cryptographic hashing. It sets the stage for understanding the limitations of early blockchains that later innovations, such as smart contracts and alternative consensus models (e.g., Proof-of-Stake), were designed to solve.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge allows students to critically analyze the trajectory of the industry. They can better evaluate new projects by comparing their goals to the original ethos of blockchain and understand how real-world applications in finance (DeFi), supply chain, and digital identity are direct evolutions of ideas born from this history.
