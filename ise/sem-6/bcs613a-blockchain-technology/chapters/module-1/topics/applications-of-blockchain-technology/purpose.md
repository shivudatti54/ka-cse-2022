# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1.  Identify and describe at least five diverse applications of compiler technology beyond traditional program compilation.
2.  Explain the role of specific compiler phases (e.g., lexical analysis, parsing, semantic analysis) in enabling modern software development tools like IDEs and linters.
3.  Differentiate between Ahead-of-Time (AOT) and Just-In-Time (JIT) compilation, including their advantages and disadvantages.
4.  Analyze the symbiotic relationship between compiler design and computer architecture design.
5.  Provide concrete examples of program translation, such as binary translators and hardware synthesis tools.
6.  Evaluate a given software engineering problem and determine if compiler technology could be applied to solve it.
