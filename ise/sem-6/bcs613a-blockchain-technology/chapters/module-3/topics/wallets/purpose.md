### Learning Purpose: Module 3 - Wallets

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Cryptocurrency wallets are the primary gateway for users to interact with blockchain networks. Understanding them is crucial because they are responsible for securing digital assets and managing the cryptographic keys that represent ownership and identity on the blockchain. A lack of knowledge about wallets is a leading cause of asset loss, making this topic fundamental for both personal security and practical application.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core concepts of public and private keys, seed phrases, and their role in wallet functionality. They will differentiate between the main types of wallets—hot (software, web) and cold (hardware, paper)—and analyze their respective trade-offs between convenience and security. The module will also cover the process of sending and receiving transactions.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon previous modules on cryptographic hashing and public-key cryptography (Module 1) and transaction structures and blockchain data (Module 2). It provides the essential user-side context for how transactions are initiated and authenticated, creating a foundation for exploring Decentralized Applications (dApps) and DeFi protocols in subsequent modules, which require wallet interaction.

**4. Real-world applications**
Knowledge of wallets is essential for securely holding and transferring cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum. It is also a prerequisite for engaging with the real-world Web3 ecosystem, including purchasing NFTs, participating in decentralized finance (DeFi) lending/staking, and interacting with dApps across various blockchain platforms.
