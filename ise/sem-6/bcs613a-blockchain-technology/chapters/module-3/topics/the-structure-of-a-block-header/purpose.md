**Module 3: The Structure of a Block Header**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the block header is fundamental because it is the core component securing the entire blockchain. It contains the cryptographic data that links blocks together, making the chain immutable and tamper-evident. Grasping its structure is essential for anyone looking to understand how trust and security are achieved in a decentralized system without a central authority.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify and describe the six key fields of a standard block header: Version, Previous Block Hash, Merkle Root, Timestamp, Difficulty Target, and Nonce. They will understand the specific function of each field and how they work in concert to create a secure, chronological chain of blocks through the mining process.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon prior knowledge of cryptographic hashing (Previous Block Hash) and Merkle Trees (Merkle Root). It is the critical link that enables the Proof-of-Work consensus mechanism (via the Nonce and Difficulty Target) and provides the necessary data for light clients to verify transactions without downloading the entire blockchain.

**4. Real-world applications**
Analyzing block headers allows one to verify the validity of a blockchain, track transaction histories for auditing, and develop more efficient blockchain applications like simplified payment verification (SPV) wallets. It is also the first step for those interested in blockchain forensics or cryptocurrency mining.
