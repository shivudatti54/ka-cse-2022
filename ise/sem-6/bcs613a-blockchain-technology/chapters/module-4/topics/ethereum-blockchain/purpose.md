### Learning Purpose: Ethereum Blockchain

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Ethereum is the world's premier programmable blockchain and the foundation for most decentralized applications (dApps), decentralized finance (DeFi), and non-fungible tokens (NFTs). Understanding its architecture and functionality is crucial because it extends blockchain technology beyond simple transactions, enabling a new generation of trustless, automated digital agreements and services.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core components that distinguish Ethereum from earlier blockchains like Bitcoin. This includes the Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM), smart contracts, gas fees, and the account-based model. They will understand the transition from Proof-of-Work to Proof-of-Stake consensus and explore how dApps are built and interact with the blockchain.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module builds directly upon the foundational concepts of cryptography, distributed ledgers, and consensus mechanisms covered in earlier modules. It provides the critical technical basis for subsequent topics such as developing smart contracts, tokenization, and exploring specific DeFi and Web3 applications.

**4. Real-world applications**
Knowledge of Ethereum is directly applicable to using and developing dApps, creating and trading NFTs, participating in DeFi protocols (lending, borrowing, yield farming), and even building new decentralized autonomous organizations (DAOs). It is the bedrock of the current Web3 ecosystem.
