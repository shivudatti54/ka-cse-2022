### Learning Purpose: Module 4 - Forks

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Forks are a fundamental governance and upgrade mechanism in blockchain technology. They represent a critical juncture where a protocol's community must reach consensus, making them essential for understanding how decentralized networks evolve, handle disagreements, and implement changes. A poorly managed fork can lead to network instability and loss of value, while a successful one can enhance security and functionality.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to differentiate between hard forks and soft forks, analyzing the technical and social reasons each occurs. They will understand the process of initiating a fork, from proposing a change to the chain-splitting event, and examine the resulting outcomes, including the creation of new currencies (e.g., Bitcoin Cash) and backward-compatible upgrades (e.g., SegWit).

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon prior knowledge of consensus mechanisms (e.g., Proof-of-Work), blockchain immutability, and decentralized governance. It demonstrates the practical application of these concepts when a community must navigate protocol changes, highlighting the balance between immutability and the need for technological evolution.

**4. Real-world applications**
Understanding forks is crucial for participants in the crypto economy. It informs investment decisions during chain splits, helps developers plan and implement protocol upgrades, and allows network users to navigate changes safely. Major examples include the Ethereum fork to recover funds from The DAO hack and the ongoing upgrades to the Bitcoin protocol.
