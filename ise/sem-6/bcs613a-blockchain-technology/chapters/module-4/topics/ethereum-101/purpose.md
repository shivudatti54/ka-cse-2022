# Learning Objectives
After studying this topic, you should be able to:
1.  Define Ethereum and articulate its core purpose as a decentralized, programmable blockchain platform.
2.  Explain the function of Ether (ETH) and Gas, and perform calculations to determine transaction costs.
3.  Compare and contrast Ethereum's current Proof-of-Stake consensus mechanism with its previous Proof-of-Work mechanism, including key participants and processes.
4.  Describe the structure and components of an Ethereum transaction and block.
5.  Differentiate between Externally Owned Accounts (EOAs) and Contract Accounts (CAs), listing the capabilities of each.
6.  Explain the role and key characteristics of the Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM) in executing smart contract code.
