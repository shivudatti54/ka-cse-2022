### Learning Purpose: Currency (ETH and ETC)

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding Ethereum (ETH) and Ethereum Classic (ETC) is fundamental because they represent the two primary currencies of the world's most widely used blockchain platform for decentralized applications (dApps) and smart contracts. Their divergent histories, stemming from "The DAO" hack, provide a crucial case study on governance, philosophy, and the implications of immutability versus intervention in decentralized systems.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to differentiate between ETH and ETC, analyzing their shared technological origin and the philosophical fork that created them. They will examine the role of each as a native currency (or "gas") for powering transactions and smart contracts on their respective networks and assess their value propositions, security models, and market positions within the broader cryptocurrency ecosystem.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds on prior knowledge of blockchain fundamentals (Module 1), consensus mechanisms like Proof-of-Work/Proof-of-Stake (Module 2), and smart contracts (Module 3). It provides a concrete application for these concepts and sets the stage for exploring decentralized finance (DeFi) and dApps (later modules), which are predominantly built on the Ethereum network.

**4. Real-world applications**
ETH is the foundational asset for the entire Ethereum ecosystem, used to pay for transaction fees in applications ranging from NFT marketplaces to DeFi lending protocols. ETC continues to operate as a Proof-of-Work chain, appealing to miners and those who prioritize the original immutability principle. Understanding both is key for developers, investors, and anyone engaging with the Web3 space.
