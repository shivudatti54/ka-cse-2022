## **Introduction to Ethereum Blockchain**

### What is a Blockchain?

A blockchain is a decentralized, distributed ledger technology that enables secure, transparent, and tamper-proof transactions. It is a chain of blocks, each containing a set of transactions, that are linked together through cryptography.

### Key Characteristics of a Blockchain:

- **Decentralized**: Blockchain is a decentralized system, meaning that it is not controlled by a single entity.
- **Distributed**: Blockchain is a distributed system, meaning that it is replicated across multiple nodes.
- **Immutable**: Blockchain is an immutable system, meaning that once a transaction is written, it cannot be altered.
- **Transparent**: Blockchain is a transparent system, meaning that all transactions are visible to all participants.

### The Ethereum Stack

The Ethereum stack consists of the following components:

- **Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM)**: The EVM is the core component of the Ethereum network. It executes smart contracts and runs the blockchain.
- **Gas**: Gas is a measure of the computational effort required to execute a transaction or smart contract.
- **Block**: A block is a collection of transactions that are verified and added to the blockchain.
- **Block Reward**: The block reward is the reward given to the miner who creates a new block.

### Ethereum Clients and Releases

---

### Ethereum Client Types

- **Full Node**: A full node is a client that stores the entire blockchain and participates in the network by validating transactions and blocks.
- **Light Node**: A light node is a client that stores a copy of the blockchain, but does not participate in the network.
- ** wallets**: wallets are software programs that allow users to interact with the Ethereum network.

### Ethereum Releases

- **Frontier**: Frontier is the first version of the Ethereum network, released in 2015.
- **Homestead**: Homestead is the second major release of the Ethereum network, released in 2016.
- **Spurious Dragon**: Spurious Dragon is the third major release of the Ethereum network, released in 2017.
- ** Constantinople**: Constantinople is the fourth major release of the Ethereum network, released in 2019.
- **St. Petersburg**: St. Petersburg is the fifth major release of the Ethereum network, released in 2021.

### Ethereum Blockchain Example

---

- **Smart Contract**: A smart contract is a self-executing contract with the terms of the agreement written directly into lines of code.
- **Transaction**: A transaction is a request to move funds from one address to another.
- **Block Reward**: The block reward is the reward given to the miner who creates a new block.

### Key Concepts

---

- **Mining**: Mining is the process of validating transactions and creating new blocks.
- **Gas Limit**: The gas limit is the maximum amount of computational effort allowed to be expended on a transaction or smart contract.
- **Block Size**: The block size is the maximum number of transactions allowed in a block.

### Example Use Cases

---

- **Decentralized Finance (DeFi)**: DeFi is a financial system that operates on the Ethereum network, allowing for decentralized lending, borrowing, and trading.
- **Non-Fungible Tokens (NFTs)**: NFTs are unique digital assets that can be stored and traded on the Ethereum network.
- **Gaming**: Gaming is a popular use case for the Ethereum network, allowing for decentralized and immersive gaming experiences.
