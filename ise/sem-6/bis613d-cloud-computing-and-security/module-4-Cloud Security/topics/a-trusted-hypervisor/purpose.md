# A Trusted Hypervisor
### **Learning Purpose: A Trusted Hypervisor**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
A hypervisor is the foundational layer of cloud security, creating and managing the virtual machines that run all cloud workloads. A _trusted_ hypervisor is critical because it ensures the integrity, confidentiality, and isolation of these workloads, protecting them from both external attacks and internal threats from other tenants on the same physical hardware. Understanding this technology is essential for building secure and compliant cloud infrastructures.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core principles of a trusted hypervisor, including its role in secure isolation, attestation, and measured launch. They will explore the hardware and software mechanisms (e.g., Intel SGX, AMD SEV, Trusted Platform Modules) that enable this trust and analyze the potential attack surfaces and mitigation strategies specific to the virtualization layer.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon previous knowledge of virtualization (Module 2) and cloud security models (Module 3). It is a prerequisite for understanding advanced topics like confidential computing, secure multi-tenancy, and zero-trust architecture in the cloud. The trusted hypervisor acts as the critical link between physical hardware security and the security of Platform and Software-as-a-Service offerings.

**4. Real-world applications**
Knowledge of trusted hypervisors is applied when deploying sensitive workloads in public clouds (e.g., financial processing, healthcare data), ensuring regulatory compliance (e.g., GDPR, HIPAA), and building secure private clouds for government or enterprise use. It is the underlying technology that allows organizations to trust third-party cloud providers with their most critical data and applications.
