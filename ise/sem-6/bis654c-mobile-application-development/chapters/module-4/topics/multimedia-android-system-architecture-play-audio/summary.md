# **Multimedia: Android System Architecture – Play Audio and Video – Text to Speech**

## **Key Points**

### Audio and Video Playback

- **MediaPlayer**: A class used to play audio and video files.
- **MediaExtractor**: A class used to extract media content from a file.
- **MediaFormat**: A class used to represent the format of a media file.
- **AudioManager**: A class used to manage audio settings and play audio.

### Text to Speech

- **SpeechSynthesizer**: A class used to synthesize text to speech.
- **TextToSpeechEngine**: A class used to manage text to speech functionality.
- **SpeechRecognitionResult**: A class used to represent the result of speech recognition.

### Important Formulas

- None

### Definitions

- **MediaFormat**: A representation of the format of a media file, including its type, size, and other properties.
- **AudioFormat**: A representation of the format of an audio file, including its type, sample rate, and bit depth.

### Important Theorems

- **Theorem of Audio Synchronization**: A theorem that describes the synchronization of audio and video streams.
- **Theorem of Text to Speech**: A theorem that describes the synthesis of text to speech.

### Android Components

- **Intent**: A request to perform an action on a specific component.
- **Intent Filter**: A filter that specifies the components that can handle a particular intent.
- **Broadcast**: A message sent to multiple components.

### Activity Life Cycle

- **onCreate**: Called when the activity is created.
- **onStart**: Called when the activity becomes visible.
- **onStop**: Called when the activity is stopped.
- **onResume**: Called when the activity is resumed.
- **onPause**: Called when the activity is paused.
- **onDestroy**: Called when the activity is destroyed.

### Broadcast Life Cycle

- **onCreate**: Called when the broadcast is created.
- **onStart**: Called when the broadcast is started.
- **onStop**: Called when the broadcast is stopped.
- **onDestroy**: Called when the broadcast is destroyed.
