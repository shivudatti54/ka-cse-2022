### Learning Purpose: The Near Future of AI

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the near-term trajectory of AI is critical for proactively shaping its development. This topic moves beyond theoretical ethics to grapple with the immediate policy challenges and societal impacts that emerging AI capabilities will present, ensuring students are prepared to engage with real-world dilemmas.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to analyze and forecast key near-future AI trends, including the proliferation of agentic systems, advanced generative models, and autonomous technologies. They will evaluate the associated ethical risks (e.g., disinformation, job displacement, safety) and formulate pragmatic policy responses for governance, oversight, and equitable benefit.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic synthesizes concepts from previous modules on foundational ethics (Module 1), governance frameworks (Module 2), and algorithmic bias (Module 3). It applies these principles to a forward-looking context, requiring students to use their acquired knowledge to build practical, future-ready strategies.

**4. Real-world applications**
The skills developed are directly applicable to roles in AI policy-making, regulatory compliance, risk assessment, and strategic planning within government, think tanks, and private corporations. Students will be equipped to contribute to crafting legislation, industry standards, and ethical guidelines for imminent AI technologies.
