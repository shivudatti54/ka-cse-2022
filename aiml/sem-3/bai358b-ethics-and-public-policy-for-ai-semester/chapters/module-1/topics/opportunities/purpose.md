# Learning Purpose: Opportunities in AI Ethics and Public Policy

### 1. Why is this topic important?
Understanding the opportunities presented by AI is crucial for moving beyond a purely risk-based or defensive policy framework. It shifts the focus to how proactive, ethical governance can harness AI's potential to drive societal progress, economic growth, and solve complex global challenges, ensuring technology serves the public good.

### 2. What will students learn?
Students will learn to identify and critically evaluate positive AI applications across sectors like healthcare (e.g., personalized medicine), sustainability (e.g., climate modeling), and education (e.g., adaptive learning tools). They will analyze the policy enablers—such as innovation-friendly regulations and public-private partnerships—that are required to responsibly foster these opportunities.

### 3. How does it connect to other concepts?
This topic provides a necessary counterbalance to modules on AI risks and harms. It connects directly to foundational concepts of ethical principles (e.g., beneficence) by showing how they can be actively promoted, and to governance models by exploring how policy can be designed to incentivize positive innovation rather than merely mitigate negative outcomes.

### 4. Real-world applications
Real-world applications include deploying AI for disaster response coordination, developing inclusive financial systems through algorithmic credit scoring, and using AI-powered diagnostics to improve access to healthcare in remote areas. Students will examine case studies to understand the policy pathways that made these successes possible.
