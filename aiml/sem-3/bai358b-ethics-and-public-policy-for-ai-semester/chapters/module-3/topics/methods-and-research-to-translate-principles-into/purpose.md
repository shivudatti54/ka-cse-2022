**1. Why is this topic important?**
Translating high-level ethical principles (e.g., fairness, transparency) into concrete, operational practices is the most significant challenge in ethical AI governance. Without robust methods, principles remain abstract aspirations, leading to "ethics washing" and ineffective policies that fail to mitigate real-world harms.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn a toolkit of methodologies, including impact assessments (e.g., Algorithmic Impact Assessments), participatory design workshops, value-sensitive design, and auditing frameworks. They will critically evaluate different research approaches for measuring and enforcing compliance with ethical norms in AI systems.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the crucial bridge between the theoretical foundations covered in earlier modules (e.g., normative principles) and the subsequent modules on implementation (e.g., regulation, compliance). It provides the practical "how" for the "what" and "why" of AI ethics.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is directly applicable to roles in policy-making, AI auditing, and responsible innovation. Students will be equipped to design actionable guidelines, develop evidence-based standards for regulators, and create processes for internal governance within tech companies to ensure AI systems are developed and deployed responsibly.
