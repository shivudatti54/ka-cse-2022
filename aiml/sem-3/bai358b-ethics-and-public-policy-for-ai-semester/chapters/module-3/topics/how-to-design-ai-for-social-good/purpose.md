### **Learning Purpose: Designing AI for Social Good**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
As artificial intelligence becomes deeply embedded in societal infrastructure, its design choices have profound ethical consequences. This topic is crucial because it moves beyond abstract principles to the *practices* that determine whether AI systems exacerbate inequality, bias, and harm, or actively work to mitigate them and benefit humanity. It provides the ethical blueprint for creating technology that serves the public interest.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn a practical framework for designing, developing, and deploying AI systems with a primary goal of social benefit. This includes methodologies for identifying and mitigating biases, ensuring fairness and equity, incorporating participatory design with affected communities, and measuring success through societal impact rather than mere technical performance or profit.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the applied synthesis of previous modules. It directly connects core ethical principles (Module 1, e.g., justice, beneficence) and public policy mechanisms (Module 2, e.g., regulation, standard-setting) into the concrete technical and project management lifecycle of AI. It answers the "how" by integrating ethics and policy into the design process itself.

**4. Real-world applications**
Students will analyze and propose designs for applications in critical areas such as ethical algorithmic hiring tools, equitable healthcare diagnostics, transparent environmental monitoring systems, and AI-assisted tools for accessibility, connecting theoretical knowledge to tangible positive outcomes.
