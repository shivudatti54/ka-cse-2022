# Learning Purpose: An Initial Review of Publicly Available AI Ethics Tools

**1. Why is this topic important?**
As AI systems become more integrated into public services and infrastructure, translating ethical principles into actionable practice is a critical challenge. Publicly available tools provide a tangible starting point for practitioners to assess, audit, and mitigate risks, making the abstract concept of "AI Ethics" operational. Understanding this landscape is essential for ensuring responsible development and deployment.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify, categorize, and critically evaluate a range of existing AI ethics tools (e.g., assessment frameworks, fairness checkers, bias audits). They will assess the strengths, limitations, and appropriate use cases for different tools, moving from theoretical principles to practical application.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon prior modules covering ethical frameworks (e.g., utilitarianism, deontology) and core policy challenges (e.g., bias, transparency, accountability). It acts as the crucial link between that foundational theory and the subsequent modules on designing and implementing ethical AI policies and governance structures.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied by policy analysts, product managers, and developers to conduct preliminary risk assessments, select appropriate tools for auditing AI systems, and inform the creation of organizational checklists or procurement guidelines, ensuring compliance with emerging regulations.
