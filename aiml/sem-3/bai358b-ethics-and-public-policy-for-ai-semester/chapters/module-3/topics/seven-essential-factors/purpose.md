# Learning Purpose: Seven Essential Factors for AI Ethics & Public Policy

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding these seven factors is critical because they provide a foundational framework for developing, auditing, and regulating AI systems responsibly. They move ethical principles from abstract concepts into actionable criteria, ensuring AI is deployed in a way that is fair, safe, and aligned with public good, thereby mitigating risks like bias, privacy erosion, and social harm.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify, define, and evaluate the seven core factors (e.g., fairness, accountability, transparency, privacy, safety, societal impact, and contestability). They will analyze how these factors interrelate and can be operationalized into concrete public policies and organizational governance strategies for AI.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic synthesizes concepts from earlier modules on ethical frameworks (Module 1) and policy instruments (Module 2). It provides the crucial link that translates philosophical ethics and governance theory into a practical checklist for real-world application, forming the basis for subsequent modules on implementation and enforcement.

**4. Real-world applications**
This framework is directly applied by policymakers crafting AI regulations (e.g., the EU AI Act), by companies conducting algorithmic impact assessments, and by auditors evaluating systems for compliance. It provides the essential criteria for building public trust and ensuring technology serves humanity.
