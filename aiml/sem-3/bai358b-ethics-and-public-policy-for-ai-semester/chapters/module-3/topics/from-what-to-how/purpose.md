### Module 3 Learning Purpose: From What to How

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Moving from abstract principles ("the what") to concrete implementation ("the how") is the critical bridge between theory and practice. It is where ethical intentions are tested and where policy either succeeds or fails. Understanding this transition is essential to ensure that AI systems are developed and deployed responsibly, avoiding unintended harms and "ethics washing."

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the practical mechanisms for translating high-level AI ethics principles (e.g., fairness, transparency, accountability) into actionable policy and technical design. This includes studying governance models, auditing frameworks, standards (e.g., NIST AI RMF), compliance tools, and the challenges of operationalizing values in complex systems.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly on the foundational principles covered in earlier modules (e.g., Module 1: Core Principles, Module 2: Bias and Fairness). It provides the necessary toolkit to implement those concepts. It also sets the stage for subsequent modules on enforcement, legal liability, and global governance.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied in creating organizational AI ethics boards, developing internal audit checklists, designing algorithmic impact assessments for new products, and crafting procurement guidelines for governments and corporations to source ethical AI.
