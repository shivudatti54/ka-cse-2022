# Learning Purpose: Ethics and AI

**1. Why is this topic important?**
As AI systems are increasingly integrated into public services, healthcare, criminal justice, and hiring, their ethical implications become a matter of urgent public concern. This topic is vital because it addresses the potential for AI to cause significant societal harm through biased algorithms, privacy erosion, and a lack of accountability. Understanding these ethical challenges is the first step toward creating responsible and trustworthy AI that benefits all of society.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify and analyze core ethical issues in AI, including fairness, bias, transparency, accountability, and privacy. They will explore established and emerging ethical frameworks (e.g., utilitarianism, deontology) and apply them to evaluate real-world AI systems and policy proposals.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds on prior knowledge of technical AI concepts (Module 2) and public policy foundations (Module 1). It provides the crucial ethical lens through which to evaluate the policy recommendations and governance models that will be discussed in subsequent modules, ensuring technical and policy decisions are grounded in ethical principles.

**4. Real-world applications**
Students will be equipped to assess the ethical dimensions of applications like predictive policing tools, facial recognition deployment, automated resume screening, and healthcare diagnostics. This knowledge is essential for future roles in policy-making, regulatory compliance, AI auditing, and ethical technology design.
