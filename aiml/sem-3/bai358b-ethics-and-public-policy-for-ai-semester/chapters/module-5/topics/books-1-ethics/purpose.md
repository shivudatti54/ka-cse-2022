### Learning Purpose: Ethics and Public Policy for AI

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is critically important because AI systems are increasingly integrated into society, making consequential decisions that affect justice, employment, and individual rights. Establishing a robust ethical framework is foundational to ensuring these technologies are developed and deployed responsibly, mitigating risks like bias, privacy erosion, and a lack of accountability.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify and analyze core ethical challenges posed by AI, such as algorithmic bias, transparency (the "black box" problem), and autonomy. They will explore key ethical principles (e.g., fairness, accountability, transparency) and examine how these abstract concepts are translated into concrete public policies, governance models, and regulatory frameworks.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds on previous knowledge of AI fundamentals and technical capabilities. It connects the technical "how" of AI with the societal "should we," bridging the gap between computer science, philosophy, law, and political science. It sets the stage for subsequent modules on implementation, auditing, and future trends in AI governance.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied in drafting organizational AI ethics guidelines, evaluating the fairness of automated decision-making systems in banking or hiring, contributing to public policy debates, and designing AI systems that comply with emerging regulations like the EU AI Act.
