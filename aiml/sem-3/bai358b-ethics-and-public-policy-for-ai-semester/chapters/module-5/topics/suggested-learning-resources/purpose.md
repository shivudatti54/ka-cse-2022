### Learning Purpose: Module 5 - Suggested Learning Resources

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Curating effective resources is critical because AI ethics is a rapidly evolving field. The quality of your learning directly depends on the credibility and relevance of your sources. This module provides a vetted foundation, saving you from misinformation and ensuring you engage with authoritative, diverse, and current materials from philosophy, computer science, law, and sociology.

**2. What will students learn?**
You will learn to identify, evaluate, and utilize key types of resources, including academic journals (e.g., *Ethics and Information Technology*), pivotal books (e.g., "Weapons of Math Destruction"), foundational frameworks (e.g., EU AI Act, OECD Principles), and practical toolkits from leading research institutes. The goal is to equip you with the skills for self-directed, lifelong learning in this domain.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
These resources are the primary texts that underpin every other module. They provide the concrete evidence, theoretical arguments, and case studies for topics like algorithmic bias (Module 2), accountability (Module 3), and privacy (Module 4). They transform abstract principles into tangible analysis and debate.

**4. Real-world applications**
You will use these resources to conduct robust policy analysis, write informed briefs, assess the ethical implications of a real-world AI system, and stay current with regulatory changes. This skill is essential for roles in policy-making, AI governance, compliance, ethical auditing, and responsible technology development.
