### 1. Why is this topic important?
The work of Aaron Aboagye is critically important as it provides a tangible, real-world case study of the ethical challenges and policy responses in national AI governance. Examining a specific practitioner's approach moves the discourse from abstract principles to practical implementation, highlighting the complexities of translating ethical theory into effective public policy.

### 2. What will students learn?
Students will learn about the specific strategies, frameworks, and policy recommendations proposed or implemented by Aaron Aboagye in the context of AI governance. They will analyze the rationale behind these approaches, evaluate their effectiveness, and understand the trade-offs involved in balancing innovation with ethical safeguards like fairness, accountability, and transparency.

### 3. How does it connect to other concepts?
This topic directly connects to prior module concepts such as ethical frameworks (e.g., utilitarianism, deontology), algorithmic bias, and the role of regulatory bodies. It serves as a practical synthesis, demonstrating how these abstract concepts are operationalized by policymakers and integrated into a cohesive national strategy, linking technical, ethical, and legal domains.

### 4. Real-world applications
Analyzing this work provides a blueprint for understanding and critiquing real-world AI policy, such as national AI strategies, regulatory sandboxes, and certification schemes. This knowledge is essential for students aiming for careers in AI policy analysis, government advisory roles, compliance, or ethical auditing within tech companies.
