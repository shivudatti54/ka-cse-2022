What is the purpose of this module?

The purpose of this module is to provide students with a foundational understanding of the ethical considerations and societal implications of artificial intelligence. It aims to equip them with the critical thinking skills necessary to analyze, discuss, and form opinions on the responsible development and deployment of AI technologies. Students will explore the intersection of technology, ethics, and public policy, preparing them to engage as informed citizens and professionals in a world increasingly shaped by AI.

What will students learn?

Upon completing this module, students will be able to:
*   **Define** key ethical concepts relevant to AI, such as bias, fairness, transparency, accountability, and privacy.
*   **Identify** real-world instances where AI ethics and policy become critical issues (e.g., in criminal justice, hiring, or social media).
*   **Explain** the potential societal impacts of AI, including its effects on employment, economic inequality, and personal autonomy.
*   **Analyze** the challenges in creating and enforcing ethical guidelines and public policies for AI development.
*   **Evaluate** different stakeholder perspectives (developers, policymakers, the public) on a given AI ethical dilemma.
*   **Formulate** a reasoned argument on a contemporary issue in AI ethics and policy.

How does it connect to other concepts?

This module is the crucial capstone that connects the technical aspects of AI covered in previous modules (like algorithms, machine learning, and neural networks) to their human and societal context. It builds upon:
*   **Technical Understanding:** Students must understand how AI systems work to grasp how bias can be coded into algorithms or how automated decisions are made.
*   **Data Literacy:** Concepts from data analysis modules are directly relevant, as AI ethics often concerns the data used to train models (e.g., "garbage in, garbage out").
*   **Critical Thinking & Problem-Solving:** This module applies these skills to complex, open-ended problems that lack a single "correct" answer, mirroring real-world challenges.
*   **Civic Engagement:** It connects technology to civics by examining the role of government regulation, corporate responsibility, and public discourse in shaping our technological future.

Real-world applications

The concepts in this module have immediate and profound real-world applications, including:
*   **Justice System:** Assessing the fairness of risk-assessment algorithms used for bail and sentencing decisions.
*   **Healthcare:** Evaluating AI diagnostic tools for accuracy and bias across different patient demographics.
*   **Employment:** Examining AI-powered hiring software for discriminatory patterns against certain groups.
*   **Social Media & Misinformation:** Understanding how recommendation algorithms can amplify polarizing content and influence public opinion.
*   **Autonomous Vehicles:** Debating the ethical decisions programmed into self-driving cars and the policy needed for their safe integration.
*   **Surveillance:** Discussing the policy implications of facial recognition technology used by governments and corporations.

By engaging with these applications, students move from abstract theory to concrete understanding, seeing how the ethics and policies surrounding AI directly impact lives and societies.
