### Learning Purpose: Semester-End Examination

**1. Importance**
This examination is the critical summative assessment for the module, evaluating a student's holistic understanding of how ethical theories translate into actionable governance for AI. It moves beyond memorization to test the ability to critically analyze, synthesize, and apply complex concepts, which is essential for anyone aiming to shape responsible AI policy.

**2. Learning Outcomes**
Students will demonstrate their mastery by constructing well-reasoned arguments that:
*   **Analyze** complex AI policy dilemmas using foundational ethical frameworks (e.g., utilitarianism, deontology, virtue ethics).
*   **Evaluate** the effectiveness and trade-offs of different regulatory approaches (e.g., hard law vs. soft law, innovation vs. precaution).
*   **Propose** justified, pragmatic policy recommendations for real-world AI challenges.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts**
The exam synthesizes all prior module concepts, requiring students to connect ethical principles to technical constraints (e.g., bias in algorithms), economic incentives, legal structures, and societal impact. It tests the integrated application of knowledge rather than isolated ideas.

**4. Real-World Application**
Success in this exam reflects the ability to perform core tasks of a policy analyst, ethicist, or AI governance professional—skills directly applicable to roles in government regulatory bodies, ethics boards within corporations, and non-profit organizations advocating for responsible innovation.
