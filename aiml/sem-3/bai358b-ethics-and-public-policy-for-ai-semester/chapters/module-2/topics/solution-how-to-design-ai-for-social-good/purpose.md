### Learning Purpose: Designing AI for Social Good

**1. Importance**
This topic is critical because AI's societal impact is determined by its design. Without intentionality, even well-meaning AI can exacerbate inequality, violate privacy, or cause harm. Learning to design for social good ensures students can proactively create systems that are not only effective but also equitable, accountable, and beneficial to humanity.

**2. Learning Outcomes**
Students will learn a practical framework for ethical AI design. This includes methodologies for stakeholder engagement, value-sensitive design, and impact assessment. They will analyze case studies to identify both successful and harmful deployments, gaining skills to develop AI solutions that address real-world problems like poverty, healthcare access, and climate change.

**3. Connections**
This topic synthesizes core concepts from Module 1, such as ethical principles (justice, non-maleficence) and policy mechanisms (regulation, oversight). It acts as the practical application of that theoretical foundation, bridging the gap between abstract ethics and concrete technical and policy design. It also sets the stage for Module 3's focus on implementation, governance, and auditing.

**4. Real-World Applications**
The principles learned are directly applicable to developing AI systems in healthcare (diagnostic tools), agriculture (sustainable resource management), finance (ethical loan algorithms), and public service (bias-free resource allocation). Students will be equipped to contribute to projects that prioritize human welfare and social benefit.
