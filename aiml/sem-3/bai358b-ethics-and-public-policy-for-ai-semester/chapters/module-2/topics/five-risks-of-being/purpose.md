### Learning Purpose: Module 2 - The Five Risks of AI

**1. Importance**
This topic is critical because understanding the risks of AI is the first step toward creating ethical and safe systems. As AI becomes more integrated into society, its potential for harm—from perpetuating bias to causing physical danger—grows. Proactively identifying these risks is essential for developing effective public policies that protect citizens and build public trust in technology, rather than reacting to crises after they occur.

**2. Learning Outcomes**
Students will learn to identify, define, and analyze the five core risks associated with AI: bias and discrimination, lack of transparency ("black box" problem), privacy erosion, safety and security vulnerabilities, and potential for mass unemployment and social disruption. They will evaluate real-world case studies to understand the tangible consequences of these risks.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts**
This module builds directly on foundational concepts from Module 1 (e.g., ethical frameworks, definitions of AI). It provides the crucial "why" that informs the content of subsequent modules, which will cover risk mitigation strategies, governance models, and policy design. You cannot effectively regulate or build safe AI without first understanding what you are guarding against.

**4. Real-World Applications**
This knowledge is directly applicable to roles in government policy-making, AI ethics auditing, compliance, and product management. It equips students to conduct risk assessments for AI applications in healthcare, criminal justice, finance, and autonomous vehicles, ensuring they are deployed responsibly.
