### Module 2: Key Problems and Ethical Dilemmas in AI

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is critical because AI systems are being deployed at scale, making consequential decisions that impact human lives. Without a clear understanding of the key ethical problems, policymakers and developers risk creating and sanctioning systems that perpetuate harm, injustice, and social inequity.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify and analyze core ethical dilemmas in AI, including algorithmic bias and fairness, transparency (e.g., the "black box" problem), accountability gaps, privacy concerns with mass data collection, and the potential for autonomous systems to cause unintended harm.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module builds directly on the foundational ethical frameworks (e.g., utilitarianism, deontology) introduced in Module 1. It provides the essential problem set that subsequent modules will address, forming the basis for evaluating regulatory responses, design solutions (e.g., Ethical AI by Design), and governance models covered later in the course.

**4. Real-world applications**
Students will be able to critically assess real-world cases, such as biased hiring algorithms, discriminatory predictive policing tools, and fatal autonomous vehicle accidents. This knowledge is essential for roles in AI policy, compliance, auditing, and responsible product development.
