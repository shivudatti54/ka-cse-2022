### Learning Purpose: Seven Essential Factors for Ethical AI Policy

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because deploying AI without a structured ethical framework leads to significant risks, including algorithmic bias, privacy violations, and a lack of accountability. Understanding these seven factors provides a foundational checklist for policymakers, developers, and auditors to proactively identify and mitigate these risks, ensuring AI systems are developed and governed responsibly.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify, define, and apply the seven key factors (e.g., Fairness, Accountability, Transparency, Safety, Privacy, Human-Centered Values, and Societal Benefit) that are essential for analyzing and formulating ethical public policy for AI systems.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly on the introductory ethical frameworks from Module 1. It provides the concrete evaluative criteria needed to critically assess real-world AI case studies (later modules) and to draft effective governance strategies and regulatory proposals (future modules).

**4. Real-world applications**
These factors are applied by governments to create national AI strategies, by corporations to establish internal AI ethics boards and audit processes, and by NGOs to advocate for and evaluate policy. For example, they form the basis for regulatory tools like the EU AI Act's risk classification system.
