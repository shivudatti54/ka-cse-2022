### Learning Purpose: The Ethics of Algorithms

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Algorithms increasingly govern critical aspects of society, from criminal justice and loan approvals to social media content moderation. Understanding their ethical implications is paramount to preventing harm, combating bias, and ensuring these powerful tools are designed and deployed for the public good, not just private gain.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify and analyze key ethical challenges posed by algorithms, including bias and fairness, transparency ("black box" problem), accountability, and privacy. They will evaluate real-world cases and apply established ethical frameworks (e.g., deontology, utilitarianism) to assess algorithmic decision-making processes.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly on foundational ethics from Module 1, applying abstract principles to concrete technological problems. It provides the crucial ethical groundwork for subsequent modules on policy, governance, and regulation, creating a clear through-line from identifying a problem to crafting its solution.

**4. Real-world applications**
Students will examine applications in predictive policing, automated resume screening, and algorithmic content curation. This knowledge is essential for future policymakers, developers, and auditors tasked with creating laws, building responsible AI, or conducting algorithmic impact assessments.
