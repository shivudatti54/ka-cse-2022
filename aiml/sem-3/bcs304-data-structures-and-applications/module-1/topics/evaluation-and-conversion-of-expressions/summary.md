# Evaluation And Conversion Of Expressions
This chapter examined three fundamental expression notations—infix (operator between operands), prefix (operator before operands), and postfix (operator after operands)—with emphasis on why postfix notation is computationally preferred due to its inherent unambiguity and efficient stack-based evaluation in O(n) time. The infix-to-postfix conversion algorithm (Shunting-Yard) was formally presented with correctness proof based on stack invariants, and demonstrated through detailed examples tracing operator precedence handling. Additional coverage included postfix and prefix evaluation algorithms with worked numerical examples, the complete infix-to-prefix conversion method, and formal complexity analysis. Hard-level multiple-choice questions tested conceptual understanding, application (conversion), numerical evaluation, and algorithm analysis skills.
