### Learning Purpose: Sartaj Sahni and Susan Anderson-Freed

1. **Importance:** This topic is crucial as it focuses on the pioneering contributions of two foundational computer scientists. Studying their work provides historical context and a deeper appreciation for the theoretical underpinnings and practical design of the data structures we use today.

2. **Learning Outcomes:** Students will learn about the significant research and publications of Sartaj Sahni (renowned for his work in data structures, algorithms, and authoring a seminal textbook) and Susan Anderson-Freed (noted for her work on data structure implementation and pedagogy). They will understand how their efforts helped standardize the teaching and application of fundamental concepts.

3. **Connection to Other Concepts:** Their work directly connects to and underpins nearly every other module in this course, including the analysis of algorithms (e.g., Big-O notation), the implementation of core structures (like linked lists, trees, and graphs), and the principles of software engineering and efficient programming.

4. **Real-World Applications:** The textbooks and methodologies developed by Sahni and Anderson-Freed are used globally to train engineers. Their principles are applied in designing efficient databases, developing operating systems, optimizing network routing algorithms, and building performance-critical applications in fields like bioinformatics and finance.
