### **Learning Purpose: Continuous Internal Evaluation**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) is a critical pedagogical shift from high-stakes, end-of-term exams to a more holistic and formative assessment model. In a technical field like Data Analytics with R, it ensures consistent student engagement, provides regular feedback on coding and analytical skills, and reduces the pressure and anxiety associated with single, summative assessments. It is essential for fostering a deep, practical understanding of complex concepts rather than superficial, last-minute learning.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to design and implement a CIE framework for a data analytics course. This includes creating diverse assessment instruments (e.g., weekly R coding quizzes, mini-project submissions, peer code reviews, and data interpretation viva voce) that effectively measure progressive learning. They will understand how to use R and associated tools (like RMarkdown or Shiny apps) not just for analysis, but also as a medium for presenting and being evaluated on their work.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
CIE directly connects to the entire data analytics workflow. Weekly coding quizzes reinforce **data manipulation (dplyr)** and **visualization (ggplot2)**. Mini-projects assess the ability to integrate skills from **statistical modeling** and **programming fundamentals** into a complete analysis. Peer reviews build soft skills and expose students to diverse coding approaches, reinforcing **best practices in R programming**.

**4. Real-world applications**
This mirrors the agile, iterative process of a real-world data analyst or data scientist. Professionals continuously present findings, receive feedback on their code and models from peers and stakeholders, and refine their work—exactly what a CIE system simulates. It prepares students for a career where projects are developed and evaluated in stages, not in a single, final deliverable.
