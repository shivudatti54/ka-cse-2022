### Learning Purpose: Line Plots in R

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Line plots are a foundational and highly effective tool for visualizing trends over a continuous variable, most commonly time. In data analytics, identifying patterns, trends, and anomalies in temporal data is a core task. Mastering line plots is essential for any analyst, as they provide a clear and intuitive way to communicate how a metric changes, which is the first step in many analytical workflows.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to create, customize, and interpret line plots using `ggplot2`. This includes plotting single and multiple lines on the same graph, modifying aesthetics (e.g., colors, line types, points), and adding informative labels and titles. The goal is to transform raw time-series data into a compelling visual narrative that highlights key insights.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module builds directly on prior knowledge of data manipulation with `dplyr` and `tidyverse` principles to prepare time-series data for plotting. It serves as a crucial application of the grammar of graphics philosophy introduced earlier. Furthermore, the skills learned here are a prerequisite for more advanced time-series analysis, forecasting, and creating complex, multi-layered visualizations.

**4. Real-world applications**
Line plots are ubiquitous in fields like finance (stock prices over time), marketing (website traffic trends), operations (inventory levels), and public health (disease case counts). The ability to create a clear line plot enables analysts to answer critical business questions about performance, seasonality, and growth.
