### Learning Purpose: Box Plots

#### 1. Why is this topic important?
Understanding box plots is crucial as they provide a powerful and standardized method for visualizing the distribution of a dataset. They concisely summarize central tendency, dispersion, and potential outliers, enabling analysts to quickly grasp key statistical properties and compare distributions across different categories. This skill is fundamental for exploratory data analysis (EDA).

#### 2. What will students learn?
Students will learn to construct and interpret box plots using R's `ggplot2` package. They will master how to identify the five-number summary (minimum, Q1, median, Q3, maximum), detect skewness, and spot outliers. Furthermore, they will learn to create grouped box plots to compare distributions between different factors or groups within their data.

#### 3. How does it connect to other concepts?
This topic builds directly on prior knowledge of descriptive statistics (Module 2) and data visualization principles (Module 3). Creating box plots involves applying functions for data summarization (`summary()`, `quantile()`) and integrates seamlessly with the `ggplot2` grammar of graphics. The insights gained often lead to more advanced statistical testing and modeling in subsequent modules.

#### 4. Real-world applications
Box plots are widely used across industries to analyze and compare performance metrics, such as customer satisfaction scores across regions, website load times by browser, or sales figures by product category. They are indispensable for identifying anomalies, understanding variation, and communicating data-driven insights effectively to stakeholders.
