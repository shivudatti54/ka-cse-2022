### **Learning Purpose: Scatter Plots**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Scatter plots are a fundamental and powerful tool for visual data exploration. They are the primary method for visualizing the relationship between two continuous numeric variables, allowing analysts to quickly identify patterns, trends, correlations, and potential outliers. Mastering them is essential for any data-driven investigation in R.

**2. What will you learn?**
You will learn to create and customize effective scatter plots using `ggplot2`, the core R package for visualization. This includes mapping variables to aesthetics (x, y, color, size), adding trend lines (e.g., linear regression with `geom_smooth()`), setting titles and labels, and adjusting scales for clarity. You will also learn to interpret the visualized relationships.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly on prior knowledge of data wrangling (selecting and preparing the correct variables with `dplyr`) and foundational `ggplot2` syntax. It is a prerequisite for understanding more advanced visualizations like multi-panel plots (faceting) and serves as the visual basis for statistical concepts like correlation and regression analysis covered later in the course.

**4. Real-world applications**
Scatter plots are used ubiquitously to analyze real-world relationships, such as marketing spend vs. sales revenue, patient age vs. blood pressure, engine size vs. fuel efficiency, or social media engagement vs. website traffic. They are the first step in uncovering actionable insights from paired numerical data.
