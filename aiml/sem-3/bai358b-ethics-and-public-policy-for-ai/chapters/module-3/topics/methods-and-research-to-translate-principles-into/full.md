## **Chapter 9: How to Design AI for Social Good: Seven Essential Factors**

As AI technology continues to advance and become increasingly integrated into various aspects of our lives, the need for ethical considerations and responsible AI design has become more pressing than ever. In this chapter, we will delve into the methods and research necessary to translate principles into practices for designing AI that promotes social good.

## **Historical Context: The Emergence of AI Ethics**

The concept of AI ethics has been around for several decades, but it wasn't until the past few years that it has gained significant attention. The development of deep learning algorithms, in particular, has raised concerns about bias, accountability, and transparency in AI decision-making.

In 2016, the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) introduced the concept of "right to explanation" for AI-driven decisions, which requires that organizations provide transparent explanations for their AI-driven decisions.

In 2019, the United Nations gathered experts from around the world to discuss the ethics of AI, leading to the development of the UN's AI for Good Global Partnership.

## **Seven Essential Factors for Designing AI for Social Good**

Designing AI for social good requires a holistic approach that considers multiple factors. The following seven essential factors are crucial for creating AI systems that promote social good:

### 1. **Human-Centered Design**

Human-centered design is a methodology that prioritizes the needs and values of end-users. It involves creating AI systems that are transparent, explainable, and accountable.

- **Example:** The AI-powered healthcare chatbot "Woebot" uses human-centered design principles to provide mental health support to users.
