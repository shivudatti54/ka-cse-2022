# **Methods and Research to Translate Principles into Practices**

### Overview

- Translation of principles into practices is a crucial step in designing AI for social good
- This chapter focuses on methods and research to facilitate this process

### Key Concepts

- **Principles-based approach**: uses moral and ethical principles to guide AI design and development
- **Value alignment**: ensuring AI systems align with human values and ethics
- **Explainability**: developing AI systems that are transparent and interpretable

### Methods and Research

- **Design science research**: a systematic approach to designing and evaluating AI systems
- **Human-centered design**: involves stakeholders in the design process to ensure AI systems meet human needs
- **Value-sensitive design**: incorporates values and ethics into the design process
- **Ethics frameworks**: provide guidance on evaluating AI systems against moral and ethical principles
- **Case studies**: examine the application of principles-based approaches to real-world AI systems

### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Valence**: the degree to which an AI system's actions align with human values (e.g., fairness, transparency)
- **Dominance**: the degree to which an AI system's actions dominate human values (e.g., causing harm, deception)
- **Normative framework**: a set of principles and values that guide AI design and development

### Key Theorems

- **The principle of non-maleficence**: do no harm
- **The principle of beneficence**: do good
- **The principle of autonomy**: respect individual autonomy

### Quick Revision Tips

- Focus on developing a principles-based approach to AI design and development
- Prioritize value alignment and explainability in AI system design
- Utilize design science research, human-centered design, and value-sensitive design methods
- Apply ethics frameworks to evaluate AI systems against moral and ethical principles
