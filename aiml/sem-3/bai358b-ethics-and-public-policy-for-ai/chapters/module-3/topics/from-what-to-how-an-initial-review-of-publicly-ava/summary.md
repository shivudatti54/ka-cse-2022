# From What to How: An Initial Review of Publicly Available AI Ethics Tools

===========================================================

## Overview

---

This topic reviews publicly available AI ethics tools to transition from theoretical discussions to practical applications. The goal is to design AI systems that promote social good.

## Key Points

---

### Definitions and Theorems

- **Value Alignment**: The process of ensuring an AI system's goals and values align with human values.
- **Formal Verification**: The use of mathematical and computational methods to prove or disprove AI systems' behavior.
- **Robustness and Adversarial Robustness**: Measures to ensure AI systems' performance is not compromised by adversarial inputs.

### AI Ethics Tools

- **Ethics Guidelines and Standards**:
  - IEEE Global Initiative on Ethics of Autonomous and Intelligent Systems
  - European Union's AI Ethics Guidelines
- **Value Alignment Techniques**:
  - Reward Shaping
  - Goal Alignment
  - Value Learning
- **Formal Verification Tools**:
  - Satisfiability Modulo Theories (SMT)
  - Model Checking
- **Robustness and Adversarial Robustness Techniques**:
  - Data Augmentation
  - Regularization Techniques

### Formulas and Concepts

- **Optimization Formulations**:
  - Max-Min Optimization
  - Min-Max Optimization
- **Game Theoretical Frameworks**:
  - Nash Equilibrium
  - Evolutionary Game Theory

### Tools and Frameworks

- **AI Ethics Platforms**:
  - AI Now Institute's AI Ethics Platform
  - Future of Life Institute's AI Ethics Platform
- **Value Alignment Frameworks**:
  - Value Alignment Framework (VAF)
  - Goal-Alignment Framework (GAF)
