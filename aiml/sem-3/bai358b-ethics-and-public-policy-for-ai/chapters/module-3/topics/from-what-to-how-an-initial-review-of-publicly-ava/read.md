# **From What to How: An Initial Review of Publicly Available AI Ethics Tools**

## **Introduction**

As AI becomes increasingly integrated into various aspects of our lives, ensuring that AI systems are designed and developed with ethics in mind has become a pressing concern. From What to How refers to the process of transitioning from identifying the importance of ethics in AI development to implementing concrete measures to achieve this goal. This study material provides an initial review of publicly available AI ethics tools that can support this transition.

## **What is AI Ethics?**

AI ethics refers to the study of the moral principles and values that should guide the development and deployment of AI systems. It involves considering the potential impact of AI on individuals, society, and the environment, and ensuring that AI systems are designed and developed in a way that respects human rights, dignity, and well-being.

## **Key Concepts**

- **Bias**: The unequal treatment of individuals or groups, often resulting from AI systems that are trained on biased data.
- **Fairness**: The principle of ensuring that AI systems are fair and unbiased, and that they do not perpetuate or exacerbate existing social inequalities.
- **Transparency**: The principle of ensuring that AI decision-making processes are transparent and understandable, so that individuals can understand how AI systems arrive at their decisions.
- **Accountability**: The principle of ensuring that individuals and organizations are accountable for the actions of AI systems, and that they take responsibility for any harm caused by these systems.

## **Publicly Available AI Ethics Tools**

The following are some publicly available AI ethics tools that can support the transition from What to How:

### 1. AI Now Institute's AI Now Radar

- **Description**: The AI Now Radar is a tool that provides a comprehensive overview of the current state of AI development and deployment, and identifies areas where AI ethics is lacking.
- **Key Features**:
  - **AI Currents**: A list of the most significant AI-related news and events.
  - **AI Reports**: In-depth reports on the current state of AI development and deployment.
  - **AI Data**: A repository of data on AI-related technologies and applications.

### 2. Partnership on AI's (PAI) Framework for AI Development

- **Description**: The PAI Framework provides a set of guidelines for the development and deployment of AI systems, with a focus on ethics and transparency.
- **Key Features**:
  - **Ethics**: A set of principles and guidelines for ensuring that AI systems are ethical and respect human rights.
  - **Transparency**: A set of guidelines for ensuring that AI decision-making processes are transparent and understandable.
  - **Accountability**: A set of guidelines for ensuring that individuals and organizations are accountable for the actions of AI systems.

### 3. IEEE's Ethics in Action

- **Description**: Ethics in Action is a tool that provides a framework for ensuring that AI systems are developed and deployed in a way that respects human values and respects human rights.
- **Key Features**:
  - **Ethics Framework**: A set of principles and guidelines for ensuring that AI systems are ethical and respect human rights.
  - **Case Studies**: Examples of organizations that have successfully implemented ethics in AI development and deployment.

### 4. Google's AI Principles

- **Description**: Google's AI Principles provide a set of guidelines for ensuring that AI systems are developed and deployed in a way that respects human values and respects human rights.
- **Key Features**:
  - **Human-Centered**: AI systems should be designed and developed with human needs and values in mind.
  - **Diversity and Inclusion**: AI systems should be designed and developed to promote diversity and inclusion.
  - **Transparency and Accountability**: AI systems should be designed and developed to be transparent and accountable.

### 5. The European Union's AI Ethics Guidelines

- **Description**: The EU's AI Ethics Guidelines provide a set of principles and guidelines for ensuring that AI systems are developed and deployed in a way that respects human rights and respects human dignity.
- **Key Features**:
  - **Respect for Human Rights**: AI systems should be designed and developed to respect human rights and dignity.
  - **Transparency and Accountability**: AI systems should be designed and developed to be transparent and accountable.
  - **Fairness and Non-Discrimination**: AI systems should be designed and developed to promote fairness and non-discrimination.

## **Conclusion**

The transition from What to How requires the development and deployment of AI ethics tools that can support the implementation of ethics in AI development and deployment. The tools reviewed in this study material provide a starting point for this transition, and can be used to support the development of concrete measures to achieve the goal of designing AI systems for social good.
