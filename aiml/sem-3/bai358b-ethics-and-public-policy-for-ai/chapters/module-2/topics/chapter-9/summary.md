# Chapter 9: Five Risks of Being Unethical in AI

### Key Points:

- **Risk 1: Manipulation**
  - Definition: Using AI to manipulate individuals or groups for personal gain or influence.
  - Formulas: None
  - Example: Using AI-generated content to spread misinformation
- **Risk 2: Bias**
  - Definition: AI systems that perpetuate and amplify existing social biases and prejudices.
  - Formula: $P = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n} y_i$
    - P = Overall bias (prejudice)
    - y_i = Data point
    - n = Number of data points
- **Risk 3: Surveillance**
  - Definition: Using AI to monitor and collect personal data without consent.
  - Formula: None
  - Example: Using facial recognition software to track individuals
- **Risk 4: Job Displacement**
  - Definition: The use of AI to automate jobs, potentially displacing human workers.
  - Formula: None
  - Example: Self-driving cars replacing human drivers
- **Risk 5: Lack of Accountability**
  - Definition: The lack of clear accountability and responsibility for AI-related decisions.
  - Formula: None
  - Example: AI systems making decisions without transparency or human oversight

### Important Definitions:

- **Digital ethics**: The study of the moral and societal implications of digital technologies.
- **Transparency**: The ability to understand and interpret the decision-making process of AI systems.
- **Accountability**: The responsibility to ensure that AI systems are used in a way that benefits society.

- None (this chapter focuses on conceptual risks rather than mathematical theorems)
