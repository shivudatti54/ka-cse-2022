# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Identify and explain at least five major opportunities that AI presents for society, with specific reference to applications in the Indian context

2. Analyze and evaluate the risks and challenges associated with AI deployment, including bias, privacy concerns, job displacement, and accountability gaps

3. Articulate the core ethical principles for responsible AI development, including fairness, transparency, human oversight, privacy, safety, and accountability

4. Apply ethical frameworks to evaluate real-world AI applications and assess whether they uphold responsible AI principles

5. Compare different approaches to AI governance and policy, understanding the balance between innovation promotion and risk mitigation

6. Formulate policy recommendations for responsible AI deployment that consider India's unique socio-economic context and developmental priorities

7. Critically examine case studies of AI systems deployed in India and globally, identifying both beneficial applications and potential harms

8. Understand the multi-stakeholder nature of AI governance and describe the roles and responsibilities of government, industry, academia, and civil society
