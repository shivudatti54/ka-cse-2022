# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define statistical inference and explain its role in drawing conclusions about populations from sample data

2. Differentiate between population and sample, and identify parameters versus statistics in given scenarios

3. Explain the two main types of statistical inference: estimation and hypothesis testing

4. Distinguish between descriptive statistics and inferential statistics

5. Understand the importance of probability theory as the foundation for statistical inference

6. Recognize why random sampling is essential for valid inference

7. Apply the concepts of population, sample, parameter, and statistic to real-world computer science scenarios

8. Appreciate the practical applications of statistical inference in technology and data science contexts
