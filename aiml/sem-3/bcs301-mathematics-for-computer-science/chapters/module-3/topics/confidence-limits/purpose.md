### Learning Purpose: Confidence Limits

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Confidence limits are a cornerstone of statistical inference, providing a crucial measure of uncertainty in estimates. In computer science, where data-driven decisions are paramount, understanding the precision and reliability of results (e.g., from A/B tests, machine learning models, or network performance analysis) is essential. They prevent overconfidence in point estimates and are fundamental to rigorous, ethical data science.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to construct and interpret confidence intervals for population parameters, primarily the mean. This includes understanding the underlying concepts of sampling distributions, point estimates, margin of error, and the relationship between confidence level, sample size, and interval width. They will gain practical skills in calculating intervals using both z and t distributions.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon probability theory, sampling, and the central limit theorem from earlier modules. It is a prerequisite for more advanced inferential techniques like hypothesis testing (the next logical step) and regression analysis. The logic of confidence intervals also connects to concepts of uncertainty quantification in machine learning and Bayesian statistics.

**4. Real-world applications**
Confidence intervals are used everywhere data is analyzed. Key applications include determining the error in political polls, quantifying the performance range of a new algorithm, validating the results of a clinical trial for a new software-based therapy, and estimating the mean user response time on a website to ensure service level agreements (SLAs) are met.
