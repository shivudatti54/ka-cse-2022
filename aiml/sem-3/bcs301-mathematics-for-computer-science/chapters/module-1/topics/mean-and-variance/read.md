# Mean and Variance

### Introduction

In probability theory, the mean and variance are two important statistical measures that describe the distribution of a random variable. The mean, also known as the expected value, is a measure of the central tendency of a distribution, while the variance is a measure of the spread or dispersion of the distribution.

### Definition of Mean

The mean of a random variable X, denoted by E(X) or μ, is defined as the long-run average value of X. It is calculated as the sum of the product of each possible value of X and its probability.

- Formula: E(X) = ∑xP(x)
- Example: Suppose we roll a fair six-sided die. The possible values of X are 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6, with equal probabilities of 1/6. The mean is E(X) = (1+2+3+4+5+6) \* (1/6) = 3.5

### Definition of Variance

The variance of a random variable X, denoted by Var(X) or σ^2, is defined as the average of the squared differences between each possible value of X and the mean.

- Formula: Var(X) = E[(X-E(X))^2]
- Example: Continuing with the die roll example, the variance is Var(X) = E[(X-3.5)^2] = (1-3.5)^2 \* (1/6) + (2-3.5)^2 \* (1/6) + ... + (6-3.5)^2 \* (1/6) = 2.9167

### Types of Mean

There are two types of mean:

- **Population mean**: The mean of the entire population.
- **Sample mean**: The mean of a sample of data.

### Types of Variance

There are also two types of variance:

- **Population variance**: The variance of the entire population.
- **Sample variance**: The variance of a sample of data.

### Properties of Mean and Variance

- **Linearity**: E(aX + b) = aE(X) + b, where a is a constant and b is a constant.
- **Homogeneity**: E(aX) = aE(X), where a is a constant.
- **Independence**: E(X1 \* X2) = E(X1) \* E(X2), where X1 and X2 are independent random variables.
- **Zero mean**: Var(X) = E(X^2) - (E(X))^2, where Var(X) is the variance and E(X) is the mean.

### Real-World Applications

The mean and variance have many real-world applications:

- **Finance**: The mean and variance are used to calculate the expected return and volatility of investments.
- **Engineering**: The mean and variance are used to calculate the expected value and deviation of physical quantities such as temperature and pressure.
- **Medical research**: The mean and variance are used to calculate the expected value and variability of medical outcomes.

## Conclusion

In conclusion, the mean and variance are two important statistical measures that describe the distribution of a random variable. Understanding the definitions, formulas, and properties of mean and variance is essential for making informed decisions in many fields.
