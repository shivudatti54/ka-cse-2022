### Learning Purpose: Probability Mass & Density Functions, and Mathematical Expectation

**1. Importance:**  
This topic is fundamental as it provides the mathematical language to model and analyze uncertain events, which is a cornerstone of algorithms, machine learning, and data science. Understanding these functions is essential for quantifying randomness and making data-driven predictions.

**2. Learning Outcomes:**  
Students will learn to define, interpret, and differentiate between probability mass functions (PMFs) for discrete variables and probability density functions (PDFs) for continuous variables. They will calculate and apply mathematical expectation (expected value) to find the average outcome of a probabilistic event.

**3. Connections:**  
This knowledge directly builds upon core probability rules and set theory. It serves as the prerequisite for future modules on specific distributions (e.g., Binomial, Normal), statistical inference, Bayesian reasoning, and stochastic processes. It is also critical for algorithm analysis (e.g., average-case complexity).

**4. Real-World Applications:**  
These concepts are used to model real-world systems everywhere: from predicting user behavior and network traffic in computer systems, to training machine learning models, performing risk assessment in cybersecurity, and optimizing randomized algorithms.
