### Learning Purpose: Joint Probability Distribution for Two Discrete Random Variables

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding joint probability distributions is fundamental to modeling systems where multiple random variables interact. It provides the mathematical framework for analyzing correlated events, which is essential for developing more sophisticated probabilistic models in computer science.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define, calculate, and interpret the joint probability mass function (PMF) of two discrete random variables. This includes mastering key concepts like marginal probabilities and statistical independence. They will also learn to compute expectations and conditional probabilities from a joint distribution.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is a direct extension of single-variable probability, forming the crucial foundation for Module 2's subsequent study of Markov Chains. It is also a prerequisite for understanding machine learning concepts like Naive Bayes classifiers, Bayesian networks, and other multivariate models that rely on dependencies between variables.

**4. Real-world applications**
Joint distributions are used to model real-world scenarios with interconnected variables, such as the relationship between user click-through rates and webpage design (web analytics), the correlation between network latency and packet loss (networking), and the dependencies between words in a sentence for natural language processing (NLP).
