### Learning Purpose: Higher Transition Probabilities

1.  **Importance:** This topic is fundamental for analyzing the long-term behavior and evolution of stochastic systems. Moving beyond one-step transitions is crucial for making multi-step predictions, understanding stability, and identifying steady-state distributions, which are core to many computer science applications.

2.  **Learning Outcomes:** Students will learn to compute the probability of transitioning between states over `n` steps using the `n`-step transition matrix, `P^(n)`. They will derive this matrix, understand its properties, and connect it to the Chapman-Kolmogorov equations, which provide a recursive method for calculating these probabilities.

3.  **Connections:** This concept directly builds upon the foundation of discrete-time Markov chains and basic one-step transition matrices from previous lessons. It is a prerequisite for upcoming topics like classifying states, understanding periodicity, and, most importantly, analyzing limiting and stationary distributions.

4.  **Applications:** Higher transition probabilities are used to predict system behavior over time in numerous real-world scenarios, such as forecasting weather patterns days in advance, modeling the long-term spread of information or diseases in networks, predicting user navigation through a website, and evaluating the multi-step performance of algorithms.
