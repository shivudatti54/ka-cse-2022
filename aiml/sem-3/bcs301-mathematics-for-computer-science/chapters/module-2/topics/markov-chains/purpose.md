### **Learning Purpose: Markov Chains**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Markov Chains are a foundational probabilistic model for systems where the next state depends only on the current state. This "memoryless" property (Markov Property) is crucial for Computer Science, as it allows for the efficient modeling of complex, seemingly random processes in areas like artificial intelligence, performance analysis, and data science, making them computationally tractable.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to formally define a Markov Chain using state spaces and transition matrices. They will calculate multi-step probabilities, classify states (transient, recurrent), and analyze long-term behavior by finding stationary distributions. This provides the tools to model and predict the evolution of state-based systems over time.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon the joint probability distributions from earlier in the module, applying them to sequences of events. It is a fundamental prerequisite for understanding more advanced stochastic processes like Hidden Markov Models (used in speech recognition) and Markov Chain Monte Carlo methods (used for sampling and Bayesian inference). It also connects to graph theory, as a Markov Chain can be represented as a weighted directed graph.

**4. Real-world applications**
Markov Chains have vast applications, including:
*   **PageRank Algorithm:** Modeling web surfing behavior to rank search results.
*   **Predictive Text:** Suggesting the next word based on the current one.
*   **Queueing Theory:** Modeling line lengths in computer networks.
*   **Games & Simulations:** Generating random terrain or AI behaviors.
