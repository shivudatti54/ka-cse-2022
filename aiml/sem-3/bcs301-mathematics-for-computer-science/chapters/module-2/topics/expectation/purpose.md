# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and compute the expected value (mathematical expectation) of discrete and continuous random variables

2. Apply the linearity property of expectation to simplify complex calculations

3. Compute the expected value of functions of random variables without finding their distribution

4. Define and calculate variance and standard deviation using the E[X²] - (E[X])² formula

5. Calculate covariance and interpret its meaning in bivariate distributions

6. Apply the Law of Total Expectation (iterated expectation) in multi-stage probability problems

7. Compute expectations from joint probability distributions for two discrete random variables

8. Interpret the physical meaning of expectation in practical scenarios
