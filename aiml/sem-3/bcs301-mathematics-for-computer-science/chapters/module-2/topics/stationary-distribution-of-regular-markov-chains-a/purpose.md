### Learning Purpose: Stationary Distribution & Absorbing States

1.  **Importance:** This topic is fundamental for modeling and analyzing systems that evolve randomly over time. Understanding a Markov chain's long-term behavior (stationary distribution) and its terminal states (absorbing states) is crucial for predicting outcomes, optimizing processes, and making data-driven decisions in uncertain environments.

2.  **Learning Outcomes:** Students will learn to calculate the stationary distribution of a regular Markov chain, interpreting it as the long-run proportion of time the system spends in each state. They will also learn to identify absorbing states and analyze chains that contain them, determining the probability of absorption and the expected number of steps to reach termination.

3.  **Connections:** This builds directly on the foundation of transition matrices and state classification from earlier lessons. It provides the necessary tools to analyze more complex stochastic processes and serves as a prerequisite for advanced topics like PageRank algorithms (which use stationary distributions) and Monte Carlo methods.

4.  **Applications:** These concepts are applied everywhere from predicting consumer behavior in marketing (e.g., customer loyalty/churn) and estimating game lengths in probability games, to critical tech applications like predicting the long-term reliability of hardware systems and the foundational mechanics of Google's PageRank algorithm for web search.
