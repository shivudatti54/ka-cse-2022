# Learning Purpose: Introduction to Stochastic Processes

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Stochastic processes provide the fundamental mathematical framework for modeling systems that evolve randomly over time. They are essential in computer science for analyzing algorithms, designing networks, and building intelligent systems where uncertainty and random behavior are inherent.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define a stochastic process and identify its key components, such as state space and index set. They will connect their knowledge of joint probability distributions and Markov chains to understand more general random processes. The core learning outcome will be to characterize and classify processes based on their memory (Markov property) and the nature of their time and state spaces (discrete/continuous).

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is a direct extension of probability theory (Module 1) and the study of joint distributions and Markov chains (Module 2). It synthesizes these concepts into a dynamic model. It is a prerequisite for more advanced topics like queueing theory, machine learning (e.g., Hidden Markov Models, reinforcement learning), and performance analysis of computer systems.

**4. Real-world applications**
Stochastic processes model a vast array of real-world phenomena, including:
*   **Computer Networks:** Packet arrival times and queue lengths in routers.
*   **Algorithms:** Analyzing the average-case performance of randomized algorithms.
*   **AI & Machine Learning:** The underlying structure of speech recognition, predictive typing, and robot navigation.
*   **Finance:** Modeling fluctuating stock prices and market risks.
