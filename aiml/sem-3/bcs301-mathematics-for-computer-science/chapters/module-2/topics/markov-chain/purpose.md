# Learning Purpose: Markov Chains

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Markov Chains are fundamental for modeling systems that undergo transitions from one state to another in a probabilistic manner, where the future state depends only on the current state and not on the past. This "memoryless" property, known as the Markov property, is a powerful simplification used to analyze complex, stochastic processes across computer science, engineering, and data science.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to formally define a Markov Chain using state spaces and transition matrices. They will calculate multi-step probabilities, classify states (transient, recurrent), analyze long-term behavior to find steady-state distributions, and understand the implications of the Markov property.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon the joint probability distributions covered earlier in the module, applying them to sequences of dependent random variables. It provides the foundational theory for more advanced concepts like Hidden Markov Models (used in machine learning and NLP), Markov Decision Processes (reinforcement learning), and Google's PageRank algorithm.

**4. Real-world applications**
Markov Chains have diverse applications, including predicting weather patterns, modeling board games, generating auto-complete text, analyzing user navigation on websites (clickstreams), and building predictive models in finance and genetics.
