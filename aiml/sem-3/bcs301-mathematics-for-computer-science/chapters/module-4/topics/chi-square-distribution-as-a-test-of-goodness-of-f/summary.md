# Chi-square Distribution as a Test of Goodness of Fit

=====================================================

### Definition

---

- The chi-square distribution is a discrete distribution that models the sum of squared standard normal variables.
- It is used as a test of goodness of fit, comparing observed frequencies with expected frequencies.

### Key Formulas

---

- Chi-square statistic: χ² = Σ [(observed frequency - expected frequency)^2 / expected frequency]
- Degrees of freedom: k - 1, where k is the number of categories
- Chi-square distribution table or calculator for critical values

### Theorems

---

- **Goodness-of-fit test**: The null hypothesis is that the observed frequencies are a good representation of the expected frequencies.
- **Chi-square test**: The null hypothesis is that there is no significant difference between the observed and expected frequencies.

### Important Concepts

---

- **Expected frequency**: The frequency of the category if there were no difference between observed and expected frequencies.
- **Observed frequency**: The actual frequency of the category in the sample data.
- **P-value**: The probability of observing a value of the chi-square statistic at least as extreme as the one observed, assuming the null hypothesis is true.
- **Significance level**: A threshold for the P-value, typically 0.05, indicating whether the null hypothesis can be rejected.

### Critical Values

---

- Use a chi-square distribution table or calculator to find the critical values for a given significance level and degrees of freedom.

### Example

---

- Null hypothesis: The observed frequencies are a good representation of the expected frequencies.
- Alternative hypothesis: The observed frequencies are not a good representation of the expected frequencies.
- Example of a goodness-of-fit test: Testing whether a dataset follows a normal distribution.

### Important Notes

---

- The chi-square distribution is sensitive to the sample size and the number of categories.
- The degrees of freedom should be greater than or equal to 1.
- The P-value should be compared to the significance level to make a decision about the null hypothesis.
