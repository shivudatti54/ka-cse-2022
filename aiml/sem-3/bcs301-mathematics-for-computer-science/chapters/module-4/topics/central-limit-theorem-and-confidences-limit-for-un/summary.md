# **Central Limit Theorem and Confidence Limits for Unknown Mean**

## **Definitions and Theorems**

- **Central Limit Theorem (CLT)**: states that the distribution of the sample mean approaches a normal distribution as the sample size increases, regardless of the underlying distribution of the population.
- **Confidence Limit**: a range of values within which the true population mean is likely to lie, based on a given level of confidence.

## **Key Formulas and Results**

- **Mean of Sample Mean**: $\bar{x} = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n} x_i$
- **Standard Error of Sample Mean**: $\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}$
- **Z-Statistic**: $Z = \frac{\bar{x} - \mu}{\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}}$
- **Standard Normal Distribution**: $Z \sim N(0, 1)$
- **Confidence Interval**: $(\bar{x} - Z_{\alpha/2} \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}, \bar{x} + Z_{\alpha/2} \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}})$

## **Key Points**

- CLT is a powerful tool for making inferences about population means when the sample size is large.
- The width of the confidence interval decreases as the sample size increases.
- The choice of confidence level (e.g. 95%) affects the width of the interval.
- The CLT assumes that the sample is randomly selected from the population.

## **Important Concepts**

- **Large Sample Size**: a sample size that is sufficiently large to assume normality of the sample mean.
- **Asymptotic Normality**: a property of the sample mean that states it converges to a normal distribution as the sample size increases.
- **Margin of Error**: the maximum amount by which the true population mean may differ from the sample mean.
