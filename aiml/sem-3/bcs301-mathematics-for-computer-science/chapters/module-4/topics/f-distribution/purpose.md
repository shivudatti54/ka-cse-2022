# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define the F-distribution and explain its mathematical foundation as the ratio of two independent chi-squared random variables.

2. Identify and state the key properties of the F-distribution, including its domain, shape characteristics, and relationship to other distributions.

3. Compute the F-statistic for comparing two population variances and interpret the results in the context of hypothesis testing.

4. Apply the F-distribution in one-way ANOVA to test whether there are significant differences between three or more group means.

5. Utilize the F-distribution in regression analysis to test the overall significance of a regression model.

6. Read and interpret F-tables to find critical values for given significance levels and degrees of freedom combinations.

7. Distinguish between one-tailed and two-tailed F-tests and determine when each is appropriate in statistical inference.
