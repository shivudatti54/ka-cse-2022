# Learning Objectives

After studying Student's t-distribution, you should be able to:

1. EXPLAIN the origin and need for the t-distribution, particularly why it is used when the population standard deviation is unknown.

2. IDENTIFY the key properties of the t-distribution, including its symmetry, heavier tails compared to normal distribution, and convergence to normal distribution as degrees of freedom increase.

3. CALCULATE the test statistic for one-sample, two-sample, and paired t-tests in various scenarios.

4. DETERMINE the appropriate degrees of freedom for different t-test procedures based on sample sizes and experimental design.

5. CONSTRUCT confidence intervals for population means using t-distribution when population variance is unknown.

6. APPLY the t-test framework to test hypotheses about population means in real-world scenarios, including defining null and alternative hypotheses and making statistical decisions.

7. DISTINGUISH between independent samples and paired samples designs and select the appropriate test for each scenario.

8. INTERPRET the results of t-tests in terms of statistical significance and practical implications, including understanding Type I and Type II errors.
