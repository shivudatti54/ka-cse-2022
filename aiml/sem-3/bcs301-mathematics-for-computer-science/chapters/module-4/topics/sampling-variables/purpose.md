# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and distinguish between population and sample, and explain why sampling is necessary in statistical inference.

2. Differentiate between population parameters and sample statistics, and identify appropriate notation for each.

3. Explain the concept of a sampling distribution and describe its importance in statistical inference.

4. Calculate and interpret the standard error of the sample mean for both infinite and finite populations.

5. Apply the properties of expected values to demonstrate that the sample mean is an unbiased estimator of the population mean.

6. Compute sample mean, sample variance, and sample standard deviation from raw data.

7. Explain the Law of Large Numbers and its implications for sample size selection in practical applications.

8. Understand the sampling distribution of sample variance and its relationship to the chi-square distribution.
