### Learning Purpose: Semester-End Examination

**1. Importance:**  
This examination is crucial as it evaluates comprehensive understanding and application of Design of Experiments (DoE) and ANOVA, core statistical methods for validating hypotheses in computer science. Mastery ensures students can rigorously test algorithms, systems, and models, forming a foundation for evidence-based research and development.

**2. Learning Outcomes:**  
Students will demonstrate proficiency in designing experiments, formulating hypotheses, and executing ANOVA tests (one-way/two-way). They will learn to interpret p-values, F-statistics, and post-hoc results to draw meaningful conclusions about data variability and group differences, ensuring statistical rigor in their analyses.

**3. Interconnection:**  
This topic integrates prior knowledge of probability, descriptive statistics, and regression from earlier modules. It directly supports advanced topics like machine learning model evaluation, A/B testing for software, and performance analysis of algorithms, bridging gap between theoretical statistics and practical CS applications.

**4. Real-World Applications:**  
Applied in AI/ML for comparing model accuracy, software engineering for A/B testing UI changes, and network analysis for evaluating protocol performance. Industries use these methods to optimize systems, validate innovations, and ensure reliability in data-driven decisions.
