### **Learning Purpose: Continuous Internal Evaluation**

**1. Importance:** This topic is crucial as it moves beyond theoretical analysis to focus on the practical implementation and ongoing assessment of experimental designs. It ensures the validity and reliability of results in real-time, which is fundamental for drawing accurate, data-driven conclusions in computer science research and development.

**2. Student Learning:** Students will learn how to implement, monitor, and adjust a designed experiment during its execution. This includes techniques for collecting data consistently, checking underlying ANOVA assumptions (like normality and homogeneity of variance), and performing preliminary analyses to identify issues like outliers or insufficient data early in the process.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts:** This directly builds upon the prior knowledge of designing experiments and the ANOVA model from this module. It applies foundational statistical concepts like hypothesis testing and data distributions to a live setting. It also connects to broader themes of rigorous software testing, A/B testing for user experience, and the iterative nature of the scientific method.

**4. Real-World Applications:** This skill is essential for continuously validating machine learning models, optimizing algorithms, conducting live A/B tests on websites or applications, and managing quality control in automated systems. It ensures that empirical research in CS is both efficient and robust.
