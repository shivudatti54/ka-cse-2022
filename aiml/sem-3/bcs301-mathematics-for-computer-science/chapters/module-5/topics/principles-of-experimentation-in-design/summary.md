# Principles of Experimentation in Design

=====================================

## Definition

---

- Experimentation in design refers to the process of testing hypotheses and evaluating the effects of different variables on an outcome.
- It involves designing and conducting studies to collect data and draw conclusions about the relationships between variables.

## Key Principles

---

- **Controlled Variables**: Identify and control variables that could affect the outcome of the experiment.
- **Randomization**: Randomly assign participants or units to treatment or control groups to minimize bias.
- **Replication**: Repeat the experiment multiple times to increase the reliability of the results.
- **Blinding**: Conceal the treatment or control status of participants or units from those administering the experiment or collecting data.
- **Sampling**: Select a representative sample of participants or units from the population of interest.

## Formulas and Theorems

---

- **Sample Size Calculation**: n = (Z^2 \* σ^2) / E^2, where n is the sample size, Z is the Z-score, σ is the standard deviation, and E is the desired margin of error.
- **Confidence Interval**: CI = x̄ ± (Z \* σ / √n), where CI is the confidence interval, x̄ is the sample mean, Z is the Z-score, σ is the standard deviation, and n is the sample size.

## Important Terms

---

- **Variable**: A characteristic or attribute that is being manipulated or measured in an experiment.
- **Treatment**: The variable being manipulated in an experiment.
- **Control**: The variable being held constant in an experiment.
- **Outcome**: The result or effect being measured in an experiment.

## Key Theorems

---

- **Central Limit Theorem (CLT)**: The distribution of the sample mean approaches a normal distribution as the sample size increases.
- **Law of Large Numbers (LLN)**: The average of the results obtained from a large number of trials will converge to the expected value as the number of trials increases.
