# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Distinguish between analog and digital signals, explaining why digital systems offer advantages in terms of noise immunity and data precision.

2. Convert numbers between binary, decimal, octal, and hexadecimal number systems accurately and efficiently.

3. Perform binary arithmetic operations including addition, subtraction, multiplication, and understand the concept of carry and borrow.

4. Explain the representation of logical 0 and logical 1 in terms of voltage levels, and describe the significance of noise margins in digital circuits.

5. Identify the basic logic gates (AND, OR, NOT, NAND, NOR, XOR, XNOR) and their functions in terms of truth tables.

6. Understand the historical connection between Boolean algebra and digital circuit design, recognizing George Boole's contribution to modern computing.

7. Apply the two's complement representation for representing signed numbers in binary and perform subtraction using addition of two's complement.

8. Analyze simple digital circuits and predict their behavior based on input combinations and gate functions.
