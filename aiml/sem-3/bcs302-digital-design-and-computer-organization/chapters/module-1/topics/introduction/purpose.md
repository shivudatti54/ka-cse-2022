# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental differences between analog and digital signals and describe why digital systems have largely replaced analog systems in computing applications.

2. Describe the binary number system and explain why it is the natural choice for representing information in digital electronic circuits.

3. Perform conversions between binary and decimal number systems accurately.

4. Identify and describe the basic logic gates (AND, OR, NOT, NAND, NOR, XOR, XNOR) along with their truth tables and symbolic representations.

5. Explain the concept of voltage thresholds and noise margins in digital circuits.

6. Understand the hierarchical abstraction levels in digital design, from transistors to complete systems.

7. Explain what Hardware Description Languages (HDL) are and their role in modern digital design.
