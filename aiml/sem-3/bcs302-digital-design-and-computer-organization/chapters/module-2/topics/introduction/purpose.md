# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental differences between analog and digital representation of information, and describe why digital systems offer advantages in terms of noise immunity and design flexibility.

2. Perform conversions between binary, decimal, octal, and hexadecimal number systems, and represent signed numbers using two's complement representation.

3. Apply boolean algebra theorems and laws to simplify logical expressions, including De Morgan's theorems, distributive law, and absorption law.

4. Analyze and construct truth tables for basic logic gates (AND, OR, NOT, NAND, NOR, XOR, XNOR) and understand their logical operations.

5. Distinguish between combinational and sequential logic circuits based on the presence or absence of memory elements.

6. Derive boolean expressions from given circuit diagrams and design combinational circuits from boolean expressions or truth tables.

7. Optimize boolean expressions to minimize the number of gates required, reducing circuit complexity, cost, and propagation delay.
