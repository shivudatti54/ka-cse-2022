# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the architecture and functional organization of the Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU) within the basic processing unit.

2. Describe how arithmetic operations including addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division are performed by the ALU using binary arithmetic principles.

3. Implement logical operations (AND, OR, XOR, NOT) and explain their applications in bit manipulation and masking operations.

4. Perform shift and rotate operations and distinguish between logical, arithmetic, and rotate operations with their specific use cases.

5. Analyze how status flags are generated by the ALU and utilized by the control unit for conditional operations.

6. Evaluate the trade-offs in ALU design including operation set, hardware complexity, and propagation delay considerations.

7. Express ALU operations using register transfer notation and trace the data flow during arithmetic and logical operations.
