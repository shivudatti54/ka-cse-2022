# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the concept of Direct Memory Access (DMA) and its role in computer systems
2. Describe the architecture and components of a DMA controller, including its registers
3. Compare and contrast the three DMA transfer modes: burst, cycle stealing, and transparent
4. Differentiate between memory-to-device, device-to-memory, and memory-to-memory DMA transfers
5. Explain the bus arbitration process between CPU and DMA controller using BR and BG signals
6. Analyze the advantages and disadvantages of DMA compared to programmed I/O and interrupt-driven I/O
7. Understand the sequence of operations for initializing and completing a DMA transfer
8. Apply DMA concepts to solve numerical problems involving data transfer efficiency
