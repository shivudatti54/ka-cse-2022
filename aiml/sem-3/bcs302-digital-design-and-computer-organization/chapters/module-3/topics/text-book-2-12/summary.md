# **Text Book 2: 1.2 Digital Design and Computer Organization Revision Notes**

**Key Definitions:**

- **Digital Design:** The process of designing and constructing digital circuits and systems.
- **Computer Organization:** The study of how a computer's components are organized and interact with each other.

**Important Formulas and Theorems:**

- **Karnaugh Map (KMAP) Formula:**
  - Simplifying Boolean expressions using KMAP
  - Used to minimize Boolean functions
- **Truth Table:**
  - A table used to analyze the output of a digital circuit
  - Used to determine the output of a circuit for all possible input combinations
- **Combinational Logic:**
  - A digital circuit that produces an output based on a combination of inputs
  - Used in digital design for logic gates and combinational circuits

**Key Concepts:**

- **Boolean Algebra:**
  - A mathematical system used to analyze and design digital circuits
  - Deals with logical operations and expressions
- **Digital Circuit Analysis:**
  - The process of analyzing digital circuits to determine their behavior
  - Used to identify and troubleshoot digital circuit faults
- **Computer Architecture:**
  - The design and organization of a computer's internal components
  - Includes the central processing unit (CPU), memory, and input/output (I/O) systems
