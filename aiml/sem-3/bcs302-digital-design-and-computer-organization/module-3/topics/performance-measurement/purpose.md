# Performance Measurement
This module provides students with a rigorous, formal treatment of computer performance measurement. It establishes the mathematical foundations through derivations of the CPU performance equation and Amdahl's Law, enabling students to analyze trade-offs between instruction count, CPI, and clock frequency. The content covers essential performance metrics (execution time, throughput, MIPS, MFLOPS), benchmark methodology, and the theoretical limits of optimization. This foundation equips students to evaluate processor designs, conduct comparative performance analyses, and make informed optimization decisions—skills essential for computer architecture courses and professional practice.
