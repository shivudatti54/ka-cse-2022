### Learning Purpose: Continuous Internal Evaluation

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) is crucial as it shifts the focus from a single high-stakes exam to a more consistent and holistic assessment of skills. In data science, practical ability is as important as theoretical knowledge. CIE ensures students regularly engage with Python's data manipulation libraries (like Pandas & NumPy) and visualization tools (like Matplotlib & Seaborn), building and demonstrating proficiency incrementally, which is a more authentic reflection of professional work.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to demonstrate their Python for Data Science competencies through a series of practical assessments. This includes constructing dataframes, performing statistical analysis, cleaning datasets, and creating insightful visualizations. They will also develop skills in documenting their code and presenting their data-driven findings clearly and effectively.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
CIE integrates and applies concepts from all previous modules. It requires using fundamental Python syntax (Module 1), control structures (Module 2), functions (Module 3), and the data-specific libraries introduced (Module 4). This continuous practice solidifies understanding and reveals how these components work together to solve real data problems.

**4. Real-world applications**
This process mirrors the agile, iterative nature of data science projects in industry. Data scientists are consistently evaluated on their ability to deliver insights through code, create reproducible analyses, and effectively communicate results to stakeholders—all core skills practiced and assessed through a CIE framework.
