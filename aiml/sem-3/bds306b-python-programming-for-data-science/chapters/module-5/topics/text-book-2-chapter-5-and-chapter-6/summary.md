## **Chapter 5: Statistical Measures of Central Tendency and Variability**

- **Definition of Central Tendency:**
  - Average value of a dataset
  - Measures of central tendency: Mean, Median, Mode
- **Mean:**
  - Formula: $\bar{x} = \frac{\sum x_i}{n}$
  - Important: Sensitive to outliers
- **Median:**
  - Formula: $median = \frac{1}{2} (x_{n/2} + x_{(n+1)/2})$
  - Important: Less sensitive to outliers than Mean
- **Mode:**
  - Formula: $mode = argmax (f(x_i))$
  - Important: Most frequently occurring value in dataset
- **Measures of Variability:**
  - Range: $R = max(x_i) - min(x_i)$
  - Interquartile Range (IQR): $IQR = Q3 - Q1$
  - Standard Deviation (SD): $\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\sum (x_i - \bar{x})^2}{n}}$
  - Variance: $\sigma^2 = \frac{\sum (x_i - \bar{x})^2}{n}$

## **Chapter 6: Data Visualization**

- **Importance of Data Visualization:**
  - Communicates insights and patterns in data
  - Helps in data exploration and discovery
- **Types of Plots:**
  - Scatter Plot: $x$ vs. $y$
  - Bar Plot: categorical data
  - Histogram: distribution of continuous data
  - Box Plot: distribution of continuous data
- **Plotting Tips:**
  - Use appropriate scale
  - Avoid 3D plots
  - Use labels and titles
  - Color-coding and legends
