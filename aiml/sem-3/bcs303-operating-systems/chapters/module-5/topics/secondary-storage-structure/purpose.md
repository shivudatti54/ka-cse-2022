# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the physical structure of magnetic disks including tracks, sectors, cylinders, and their relationship to disk performance metrics.

2. Compare and contrast different disk scheduling algorithms including FCFS, SSTF, SCAN, C-SCAN, and LOOK with respect to throughput, average response time, and fairness.

3. Calculate total head movement and compare the efficiency of different disk scheduling algorithms for given request sequences.

4. Describe the various RAID levels (0, 1, 5, 6, 0+1) and analyze their performance, fault tolerance, and storage efficiency characteristics.

5. Explain the purpose of swap space in virtual memory systems and determine appropriate swap space sizes based on system requirements.

6. Differentiate between magnetic disk drives and solid-state drives, and explain how their underlying technology affects I/O performance and scheduling considerations.

7. Apply disk scheduling concepts to solve numerical problems commonly asked in University of Delhi semester examinations.
