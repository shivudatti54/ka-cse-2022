# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. DEFINE what a process is in the context of operating systems and EXPLAIN its key components including program code, stack, heap, and data sections.

2. DESCRIBE the Process Control Block (PCB) and IDENTIFY all its constituent fields along with their purposes in process management.

3. EXPLAIN the five fundamental process states (NEW, READY, RUNNING, WAITING, TERMINATED) and ILLUSTRATE how processes transition between these states during execution.

4. COMPARE and CONTRAST the three types of process scheduling (long-term, short-term, and medium-term) including their functions and impact on system performance.

5. ANALYZE various CPU scheduling algorithms including FCFS, SJF, Priority, and Round Robin, and CALCULATE performance metrics such as waiting time, turnaround time, and throughput.

6. EXPLAIN the process creation mechanism in Unix-like systems using fork and exec system calls, and DESCRIBE the process termination lifecycle including zombie and orphan states.

7. EVALUATE different Interprocess Communication (IPC) mechanisms including shared memory and message passing, and DETERMINE appropriate scenarios for their use.
