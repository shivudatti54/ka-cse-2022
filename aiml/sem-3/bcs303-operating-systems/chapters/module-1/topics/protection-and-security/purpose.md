# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental differences between protection and security in operating systems, including their respective scopes and objectives.

2. Describe access control mechanisms including access control lists and capability lists, comparing their advantages and disadvantages.

3. Analyze the Unix/Linux file permission system and interpret permission strings in both symbolic and octal representations.

4. Identify and categorize common security threats including unauthorized access, malware, denial of service attacks, and social engineering.

5. Explain authentication mechanisms including passwords, multi-factor authentication, and biometric verification, understanding their strengths and limitations.

6. Apply the principle of least privilege to design protection policies for system resources.

7. Understand formal security models including Bell-LaPadula and Biba models, and explain their roles in ensuring confidentiality and integrity.

8. Describe the complete authentication and authorization flow in secure systems, from user login to resource access decisions.
