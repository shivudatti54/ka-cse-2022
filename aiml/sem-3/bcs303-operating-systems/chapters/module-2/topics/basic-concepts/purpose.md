# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and explain the concept of a process in operating systems, distinguishing it clearly from a program.

2. Identify and describe the five process states and explain the transitions between them with appropriate examples.

3. Explain the structure and components of the Process Control Block (PCB), including its role in process management.

4. Describe the mechanism of context switching, including its purpose, procedure, and associated overhead costs.

5. Compare and contrast process creation mechanisms in Unix-like systems and Windows operating systems.

6. Analyze the relationship between parent and child processes and explain how processes interact through system calls.

7. Apply theoretical concepts to solve numerical problems related to context switching overhead and process scheduling.
