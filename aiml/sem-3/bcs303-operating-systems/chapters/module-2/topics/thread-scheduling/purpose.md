# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental differences between thread scheduling and process scheduling, including their respective scopes and overhead considerations.

2. Distinguish between Process Contention Scope (PCS) and System Contention Scope (SCS) threading models and analyze their implications for scheduling decisions.

3. Evaluate thread scheduling algorithms (FCFS, SJF, Round Robin, Priority) using standard scheduling criteria including CPU utilization, throughput, turnaround time, waiting time, and response time.

4. Calculate waiting times and turnaround times for various scheduling algorithms and compare their performance for given thread burst time scenarios.

5. Analyze the characteristics, advantages, and limitations of different thread scheduling algorithms in practical operating system contexts.

6. Describe real-time thread scheduling approaches including Rate Monotonic Scheduling and Earliest Deadline First, and explain their suitability for time-critical applications.

7. Explain the additional complexities introduced by multi-processor and multi-core architectures for thread scheduling, including processor affinity, load balancing, and cache coherency considerations.

8. Identify and explain solutions to common scheduling problems including starvation, convoy effect, and fairness issues in thread scheduling.
