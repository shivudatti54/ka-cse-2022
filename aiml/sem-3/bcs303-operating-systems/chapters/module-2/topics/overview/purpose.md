# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define what a process is in an operating system and explain the key differences between a program and a process

2. Describe the components of a Process Control Block (PCB) and explain its role in process management

3. Illustrate and explain the five possible states of a process and the transitions between them

4. Distinguish between long-term, short-term, and medium-term schedulers and explain their roles in the overall scheduling hierarchy

5. Analyze various process scheduling algorithms including FCFS, SJF, Priority Scheduling, and Round Robin, and calculate performance metrics like waiting time and turnaround time

6. Explain the process creation and termination operations, including the role of fork() and exec() system calls in UNIX systems

7. Compare and contrast different inter-process communication mechanisms, including shared memory and message passing models

8. Understand the fundamentals of multi-threaded programming, including the advantages of threads over processes and different threading models
