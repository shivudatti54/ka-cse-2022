# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define process scheduling and explain its fundamental role in operating system design
2. Distinguish between long-term, short-term, and medium-term schedulers and describe their specific functions
3. Analyze the five major scheduling criteria: CPU utilization, throughput, turnaround time, waiting time, and response time
4. Compare preemptive and non-preemptive scheduling approaches and evaluate their respective advantages and disadvantages
5. Apply scheduling algorithms (FCFS, SJF, Round-Robin) to compute waiting times, turnaround times, and average values
6. Explain the concept of scheduling queues and how processes move between different states in the operating system
7. Evaluate the suitability of different scheduling algorithms for various types of computing environments
8. Identify common pitfalls in scheduling problems, such as the convoy effect, and explain how different algorithms address them
