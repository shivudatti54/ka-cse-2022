# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and explain the five major scheduling criteria: CPU utilization, throughput, turnaround time, waiting time, and response time with appropriate mathematical formulations.

2. Compare and contrast First-Come First-Served (FCFS), Shortest Job First (SJF), Priority Scheduling, and Round Robin (RR) algorithms in terms of performance characteristics, advantages, and limitations.

3. Apply FCFS, SJF (both preemptive and non-preemptive), and Round Robin scheduling algorithms to solve numerical problems involving waiting time and turnaround time calculations.

4. Analyze the convoy effect phenomenon in FCFS and explain why it causes poor system utilization for short processes.

5. Design and explain Multilevel Queue and Multilevel Feedback Queue scheduling systems, including process migration criteria between queues.

6. Identify and propose solutions for the starvation problem in priority scheduling, specifically explaining the aging technique.

7. Evaluate the impact of time quantum selection on Round Robin scheduling performance and explain the trade-offs between response time and context switch overhead.

8. Explain the key challenges in multiple-processor scheduling including load balancing and cache affinity considerations.
