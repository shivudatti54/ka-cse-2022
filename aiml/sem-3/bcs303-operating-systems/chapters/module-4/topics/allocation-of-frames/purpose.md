# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define frames in the context of paging and explain their role in physical memory management.

2. Describe the equal allocation strategy and analyze its efficiency in scenarios with varying process sizes.

3. Apply the proportional allocation formula to calculate appropriate frame distribution among processes.

4. Compare and contrast global versus local frame allocation and explain their respective impacts on system behavior.

5. Explain how priority-based allocation works and discuss its relevance in real-time and interactive systems.

6. Understand the working set model and its application in making intelligent frame allocation decisions.

7. Analyze how frame allocation choices affect page fault rates and overall system performance.

8. Evaluate trade-offs between different allocation strategies and select appropriate strategies based on system requirements.
