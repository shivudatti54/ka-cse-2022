# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define deadlock and explain why it occurs in multi-programmed operating systems, providing real-world examples of deadlock situations.

2. Identify and explain the four necessary conditions for deadlock: mutual exclusion, hold and wait, no preemption, and circular wait.

3. Analyze resource allocation graphs to determine whether deadlock exists or may occur, distinguishing between cycles that guarantee deadlock and those that do not.

4. Apply the Banker's Algorithm to determine if a system state is safe and to decide whether resource allocation requests should be granted.

5. Compare and contrast deadlock prevention, avoidance, detection, and recovery strategies, including their advantages, disadvantages, and applicability.

6. Design approaches to prevent deadlock by attacking specific necessary conditions, particularly circular wait through resource ordering.

7. Explain the detection algorithm for deadlocks in systems with multiple resource instances and understand when detection should be invoked.

8. Evaluate recovery strategies including process termination and resource preemption, understanding the practical challenges in implementing each approach.
