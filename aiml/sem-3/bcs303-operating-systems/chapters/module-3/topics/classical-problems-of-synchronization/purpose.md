# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Identify and explain the four classical synchronization problems: producer-consumer, readers-writers, dining philosophers, and sleeping barber

2. Analyze the producer-consumer problem and implement solutions using semaphores with correct initial values and wait-signal sequences

3. Compare readers-preference and writers-preference solutions to the readers-writers problem, including their respective advantages and disadvantages

4. Explain deadlock conditions in the dining philosophers problem and evaluate different solution approaches

5. Trace semaphore-based solutions for all classical problems and verify correctness through execution simulation

6. Apply the four necessary conditions for deadlock (mutual exclusion, hold and wait, no preemption, circular wait) to analyze synchronization problems

7. Design synchronization solutions for new problems by combining concepts from these classical patterns

8. Evaluate synchronization solutions based on criteria including mutual exclusion, progress, bounded waiting, and freedom from starvation
