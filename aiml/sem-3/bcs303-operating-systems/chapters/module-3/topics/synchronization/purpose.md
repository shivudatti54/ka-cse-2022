# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental need for synchronization in multi-process and multi-threaded operating systems environments
2. Describe the critical section problem and its three essential requirements: mutual exclusion, progress, and bounded waiting
3. Analyze software synchronization solutions including Peterson's algorithm and understand their limitations
4. Explain hardware synchronization primitives such as test-and-set and compare-and-swap instructions
5. Implement process synchronization using semaphores (binary and counting) with proper wait() and signal() operations
6. Solve classical synchronization problems including producer-consumer, readers-writers, and dining philosophers
7. Compare different synchronization approaches and evaluate their trade-offs in terms of complexity, performance, and applicability
8. Apply synchronization concepts to design concurrent programs that maintain data consistency and avoid race conditions
