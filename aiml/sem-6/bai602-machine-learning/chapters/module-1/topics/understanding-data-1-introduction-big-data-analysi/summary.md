# **Understanding Data – 1: Revision Notes**

### Introduction

- Big Data is a term used to describe the large and complex data sets that are being generated at an unprecedented scale and speed.
- Machine learning is a subset of artificial intelligence that involves training algorithms to make predictions or decisions based on data.
- Need for Machine Learning: Efficient processing of large data sets, accurate predictions, decision-making, pattern recognition.

### Big Data Analysis Framework

- Data Ingestion: Collecting and cleaning data.
- Data Integration: Combining data from multiple sources.
- Data Storage: Storing large data sets.
- Data Processing: Analyzing and transforming data.
- Data Visualization: Presenting data in a meaningful and understandable format.

### Descriptive Statistics

- **Definition:** Descriptive statistics is the process of summarizing and describing the basic features of a data set.
- **Key Concepts:**
  - Mean (μ): Average value of a data set.
  - Median (m): Middle value of a data set.
  - Mode (m): Most frequently occurring value in a data set.
  - Standard Deviation (σ): Measure of dispersion of a data set.
  - Variance (σ^2): Average of squared differences from the mean.

### Univariate Data Analysis and Visualization

- **Definition:** Univariate analysis involves analyzing one variable at a time.
- **Key Concepts:**
  - Histograms: Visual representation of the distribution of a data set.
  - Box Plots: Visual representation of the distribution of a data set.
  - Scatter Plots: Visual representation of the relationship between two variables.
  - Correlation Coefficient (r): Measures the strength and direction of the linear relationship between two variables.

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- Central Limit Theorem (CLT): States that the distribution of the sample mean will be approximately normal for large sample sizes.
- Empirical Rule (68-95-99.7 Rule): States that about 68% of the data points fall within 1 standard deviation of the mean, about 95% fall within 2 standard deviations, and about 99.7% fall within 3 standard deviations.
