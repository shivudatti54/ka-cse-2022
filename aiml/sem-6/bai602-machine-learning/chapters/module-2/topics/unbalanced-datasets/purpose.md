### **Module 2: Unbalanced Datasets**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Real-world data is rarely perfectly balanced. Critical applications like fraud detection, medical diagnosis, and rare event prediction inherently feature severe class imbalances, where a class of interest (e.g., "fraudulent transaction") is vastly outnumbered. Standard algorithms biased toward the majority class perform poorly, leading to misleadingly high accuracy that masks catastrophic failure on the minority class. Understanding imbalance is therefore essential for building effective, ethical models.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify unbalanced datasets using evaluation metrics like precision, recall, F1-score, and ROC curves, moving beyond accuracy. They will implement strategic mitigation techniques, including data-level approaches (e.g., SMOTE, undersampling) and algorithm-level approaches (e.g., cost-sensitive learning, using class weights).

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly on foundational knowledge of classification algorithms (e.g., Logistic Regression, Decision Trees) and model evaluation. It forces a deeper understanding of bias-variance tradeoffs and connects to broader themes of model interpretability and ethics, as poor handling of imbalance can perpetuate systemic biases present in the data.

**4. Real-world applications**
Techniques from this module are directly applicable in numerous fields: identifying fraudulent credit card transactions, diagnosing rare diseases from medical images, predicting mechanical failure in industrial equipment, and filtering spam emails.
