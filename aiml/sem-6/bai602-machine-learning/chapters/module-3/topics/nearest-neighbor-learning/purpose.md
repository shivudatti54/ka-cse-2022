# Learning Objectives
After studying this topic, you should be able to:
1.  Explain the fundamental principle behind the K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) algorithm and why it is considered a 'lazy' learner.
2.  Describe the step-by-step process KNN uses to make a prediction for a new data point, for both classification and regression tasks.
3.  Compare and contrast different distance metrics (Euclidean, Manhattan) and understand the impact of choosing one over another.
4.  Analyze the bias-variance trade-off inherent in selecting the hyperparameter K and predict how the decision boundary changes with different K values.
5.  Differentiate between the standard KNN classifier and the Nearest Centroid classifier, listing the advantages and disadvantages of each.
6.  Identify the key challenges and limitations of KNN, such as computational expense and the curse of dimensionality, and suggest mitigation strategies like feature scaling.
