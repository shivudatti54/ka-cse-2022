# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1.  Define a Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) and list its key properties.
2.  Explain the step-by-step process of Prim's algorithm, including the role of its data structures.
3.  Explain the step-by-step process of Kruskal's algorithm, including the role of its data structures.
4.  Manually trace both algorithms on a given weighted graph to find its MST.
5.  Analyze and compare the time and space complexity of Prim's and Kruskal's algorithms.
6.  Choose the appropriate MST algorithm (Prim's or Kruskal's) based on the density of a given graph (sparse vs. dense).
