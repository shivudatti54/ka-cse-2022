### Learning Purpose: Social Robots and Active Appliances

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial as it examines the transition of AI from passive software tools to proactive, embodied agents that share our physical and social spaces. Understanding their design and impact is essential for creating technology that augments human capabilities ethically and effectively, rather than replacing or disrupting human interaction.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core principles of designing and evaluating interactive AI systems, including social robots (e.g., companion, assistive) and active appliances (e.g., smart home systems). They will explore key concepts such as embodiment, multi-modal interaction, user trust, and the ethical considerations of autonomy and privacy in these contexts.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly on foundational HCAI concepts from earlier modules, such as usability, trust calibration, and value-sensitive design. It applies these principles to physical, real-world applications, creating a tangible link between theoretical AI ethics and the practical challenges of building autonomous systems that operate in human environments.

**4. Real-world applications**
Applications are rapidly expanding into sectors including healthcare (eldercare robots), education (tutoring robots), domestic life (smart home assistants), and collaborative industry (cobots). Analyzing these applications provides critical insight into the future of human-AI collaboration.
