# Learning Purpose: DESIGN METAPHORS

### 1. Why is this topic important?
Design metaphors are a critical bridge between complex AI systems and human understanding. They provide a familiar conceptual framework, allowing users to form accurate mental models of how an AI operates. A well-chosen metaphor builds trust, manages expectations, and makes technology more accessible and intuitive, which is fundamental to creating truly human-centred AI.

### 2. What will students learn?
Students will learn to identify, analyze, and evaluate common AI design metaphors (e.g., assistant, oracle, agent). They will develop the skill to select and apply appropriate metaphors based on a system's capabilities and the user's cognitive needs. This includes understanding the strengths, limitations, and potential ethical implications of each metaphorical framing.

### 3. How does it connect to other concepts?
This topic connects directly to core HCAI principles like **trust**, **transparency**, and **explainability (XAI)**. It builds upon foundational user-centred **design methodologies** from earlier modules and informs subsequent topics on **interface design** and **evaluation techniques**. A metaphor dictates how functionality is presented and shapes the entire user experience.

### 4. Real-world applications
This skill is applied when designing virtually every AI-powered product. Examples include framing a chatbot as a "conversational partner," a recommendation system as a "knowledgeable guide," or an autonomous system as a "co-pilot." The choice of metaphor directly influences user interaction in domains like healthcare, finance, and personal computing.
