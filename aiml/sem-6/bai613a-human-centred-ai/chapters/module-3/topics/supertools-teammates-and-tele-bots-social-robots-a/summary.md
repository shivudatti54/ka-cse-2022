# **Human-Centred AI Revision Notes**

**Design Metaphors: Introduction, Science and Innovation Goals, Intelligent Agents**

### Chapter 11: Supertools

- Definition: Intentional tools designed to automate repetitive tasks, freeing humans from mundane work
- Characteristics:
  - Increase productivity
  - Enhance human capabilities
  - Can be customized to individual needs
- Examples: Virtual assistants, automated cleaning systems

### Chapter 12: Teammates

- Definition: Robotic systems designed to work alongside humans in collaborative tasks
- Characteristics:
  - Enhance human performance
  - Provide real-time feedback
  - Require human-robot interaction and coordination
- Examples: Industrial robots, autonomous vehicles

### Chapter 13: Tele-bots

- Definition: Remote-controlled robots designed for tasks that require precision and dexterity
- Characteristics:
  - Require human control and input
  - Can be used for complex tasks
  - Often used in fields like surgery and space exploration
- Examples: Remote-controlled drones, surgical robots

### Chapter 14: Social Robots

- Definition: Robots designed to interact with humans in a social setting
- Characteristics:
  - Can recognize and respond to human emotions
  - Can be used for companionship and entertainment
  - May require human-robot interaction and training
- Examples: Social robots for elderly care, humanoid robots

### Chapter 16: Active Appliances

- Definition: Intelligent appliances that can learn and adapt to human needs
- Characteristics:
  - Can recognize and respond to human behavior
  - Can be used for energy efficiency and cost-saving
  - May require human-robot interaction and training
- Examples: Smart home systems, intelligent refrigerators

**Important Formulas and Definitions**

- **Robotics formula:** R = (T + S + M) / (T + M)
  - Where R = robot performance, T = task difficulty, S = human skill, M = machine capability
- **Human-robot interaction (HRI) model:** HRI = (H + R) / (H + M)
  - Where H = human factor, R = robot factor, HRI = human-robot interaction

**Theorems**

- **Social learning theory:** Humans learn from observing others, including robots.
- **Human-centered design:** Designing robots and intelligent agents that prioritize human needs and well-being.
