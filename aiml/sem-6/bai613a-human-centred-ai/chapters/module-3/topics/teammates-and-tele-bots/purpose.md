### Learning Purpose: Teammates and Tele-bots

**1. Why is this topic important?**
As AI systems evolve from passive tools into active collaborators, understanding human-AI teaming is critical. This topic is vital for designing AI that augments human capabilities effectively, ensuring safety, trust, and shared understanding in high-stakes environments like healthcare and disaster response.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core principles of AI as a teammate, including shared mental models, transparency, and adjustable autonomy. They will explore the unique design challenges of tele-bots (remote-controlled physical agents), focusing on latency mitigation, operator trust, and effective feedback mechanisms.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This builds directly on foundational HCAI concepts like trust calibration and explainable AI (XAI). It applies ethical frameworks to a practical collaborative context and connects technical system design (from Module 2) with human factors and team dynamics.

**4. Real-world applications**
Applications include surgical robotics, where a surgeon remotely operates with AI assistance; space exploration, with humans and rovers collaborating on Mars; and search-and-rescue missions, where tele-bots navigate hazardous debris under human supervision.
