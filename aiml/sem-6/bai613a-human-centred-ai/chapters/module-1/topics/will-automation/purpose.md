### Learning Purpose: Will Automation?

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the potential for automation is foundational to Human-Centred AI (HCAI). It moves beyond technical feasibility to question what *should* be automated, ensuring technology augments human capabilities rather than simply replacing them. This critical examination is vital for designing systems that are ethical, beneficial, and aligned with human values and societal needs.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to critically analyse a task or process to evaluate its suitability for automation. They will explore key frameworks that distinguish between levels of automation (e.g., from full human control to full autonomy) and identify the associated technical, ethical, and economic trade-offs. This includes understanding the limitations of automation and the irreplaceable value of human judgment, creativity, and empathy.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly informs subsequent modules on **Ethics & Bias** (by asking *should* we automate a biased process?) and **Trust & Explainability** (by determining what level of understanding a user needs to trust an automated outcome). It provides the crucial "why" behind the technical "how" of AI system design, linking the goal of automation to the core HCAI principle of human control and well-being.

**4. Real-world applications**
This analysis is applied in domains like healthcare (automating diagnostics vs. patient care), autonomous vehicles (defining the driver's role), customer service (using chatbots vs. human agents), and industrial manufacturing (optimizing human-robot collaboration). It provides the strategic lens through which organisations can implement AI responsibly.
