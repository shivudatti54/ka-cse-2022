### **Learning Purpose: Will AI and Robots Lead to Widespread Unemployment?**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is critically important because the potential for AI and automation to disrupt the global labour market is one of the most pressing societal and economic issues of our time. Understanding the arguments and evidence is essential for future developers, policymakers, and business leaders to navigate the ethical implications and steer technological development in a way that benefits society.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to critically analyse the debate around automation and employment. They will explore key concepts like job displacement versus job creation, the distinction between task automation and full job replacement, and the "race between education and technology." Students will evaluate historical precedents and economic models to form evidence-based predictions.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly connects to core Human-Centred AI principles, such as ethics, fairness, and societal impact. It builds on economic theory and serves as a foundation for subsequent modules on designing AI for collaboration (augmentation vs. automation), policy and regulation, and the future of work, where solutions like reskilling and safety nets are discussed.

**4. Real-world applications**
The learning is applied to analysing real-world cases, from manufacturing robotics to generative AI in creative fields. Students will be equipped to contribute to strategic planning in organisations, inform policy on workforce transition, and develop AI systems that are designed to augment human capabilities rather than simply replace them.
