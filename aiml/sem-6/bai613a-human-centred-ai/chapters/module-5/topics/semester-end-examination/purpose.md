# Learning Purpose: Semester-End Examination

**1. Importance**
This examination is crucial as it assesses your integrated understanding of Human-Centred AI (HCAI) principles. It moves beyond testing rote memorization to evaluate your ability to critically apply ethical frameworks, design methodologies, and responsible practices—the core tenets of building trustworthy AI systems.

**2. Learning Outcomes**
Students will demonstrate their ability to synthesize and critique key concepts from the entire module. This includes arguing for ethical AI development, designing a human-centric AI application prototype, evaluating systems for bias, and proposing mitigation strategies, thereby proving mastery of the course material.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts**
The exam synthesizes all previous modules: ethical foundations (Module 1), UX design for AI (Module 2), bias and fairness (Module 3), and trust and transparency (Module 4). It requires you to intertwine these concepts, showing how technical design, ethical considerations, and human factors are inseparable in HCAI.

**4. Real-World Applications**
Successfully completing this exam mirrors real-world tasks such as an ethical design review, an AI impact assessment, or a project proposal defense. It prepares you for roles where justifying design choices and ensuring AI aligns with human values is paramount for product managers, ethicists, and developers.
