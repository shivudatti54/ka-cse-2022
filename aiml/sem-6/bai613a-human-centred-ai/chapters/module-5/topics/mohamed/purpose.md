### Module 5: Mohamed – A Case Study in Human-Centred AI

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because it moves theory into practice by examining a specific, real-world case. Studying "Mohamed" provides a concrete example of how AI systems can succeed or fail when interacting with a real person, highlighting the tangible human impact of design choices, data biases, and ethical oversights often discussed abstractly.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to deconstruct a real-life case study, identifying the specific human-centred AI (HCAI) principles at play. They will analyze the technical and ethical decisions made in the system's development, the resulting outcomes for the individual involved, and how a more rigorous HCAI approach could have led to a different, more equitable result.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This case study directly connects and applies concepts from across the curriculum: algorithmic bias (Module 2), fairness and accountability (Module 3), and participatory design (Module 4). It serves as a synthesis point, demonstrating how these separate principles are interdependent in practice and how neglecting one can compromise the entire system's human-centred goals.

**4. Real-world applications**
Understanding this case equips students to critically evaluate existing AI systems and proactively design better ones. The lessons learned are applicable to developing responsible AI in high-stakes domains like criminal justice, hiring, loan applications, and healthcare, where decisions profoundly affect human lives and require meticulous attention to human-centred values.
