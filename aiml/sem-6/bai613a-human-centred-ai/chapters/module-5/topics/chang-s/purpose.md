### Learning Purpose: Module 5 - Chang S

**1. Importance:**
This topic is crucial as it moves beyond designing AI *for* people to designing AI *with* people. Understanding participatory and co-design methodologies, often championed by scholars like Sara (S.) Chungwai Chang, is essential for creating equitable, accountable, and truly human-centred systems that avoid bias and serve diverse community needs.

**2. Learning Outcomes:**
Students will learn the principles and practices of participatory design (PD) in an AI context. This includes techniques for engaging stakeholders throughout the AI lifecycle, from problem definition to deployment and auditing. They will analyze case studies to understand how collaborative processes lead to more just and effective socio-technical outcomes.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts:**
This topic directly builds upon earlier modules on AI ethics (Module 2), bias and fairness (Module 3), and value-sensitive design (Module 4). It provides the practical framework for applying those ethical principles, ensuring that the theoretical concepts of fairness and accountability are actively shaped by the communities impacted by the technology.

**4. Real-World Applications:**
These methods are applied in developing community-focused tools, such as equitable algorithmic decision-making systems in healthcare or criminal justice, and co-designed AI for civic engagement and environmental monitoring. It is fundamental for any professional aiming to build responsible AI that earns public trust.
