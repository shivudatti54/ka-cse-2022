### Learning Purpose: Suggested Learning Resources

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Identifying and evaluating high-quality resources is a core academic and professional skill, especially in a rapidly evolving field like Human-Centred AI (HCAI). This topic is crucial because it empowers students to become independent, lifelong learners who can continue to explore advanced concepts, stay current with emerging research, and critically assess the validity of information long after the module concludes.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify, evaluate, and utilize a curated set of key resources relevant to HCAI. This includes seminal research papers, foundational books, influential industry blogs, and key academic journals. They will develop the skills to discern credible sources from popular commentary and understand how to effectively search for and synthesize information on specific HCAI subtopics.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic connects to and supports every other concept within this module and the broader subject. The provided resources offer pathways to deepen understanding of previously covered topics like ethics, fairness, transparency, and participatory design. They serve as the foundational material from which these other concepts are derived and debated.

**4. Real-world applications**
In professional practice, the ability to quickly find and apply relevant research is invaluable. Whether for writing a technical proposal, designing a fairer algorithm, justifying a design decision to stakeholders, or simply staying abreast of the competitive landscape, these research and evaluation skills are directly applicable to roles in AI development, policy-making, UX research, and ethics auditing.
