### Learning Purpose: Jae-Yoon Jung

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the work of pioneers like Jae-Yoon Jung is crucial as it provides concrete historical and technical context for the development of Human-Centred AI (HCAI). His research on **conversational recommender systems** exemplifies the shift from purely algorithmic AI to systems that prioritize human needs, preferences, and control, directly aligning with the core ethos of HCAI.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the foundational principles behind Jung’s work, specifically how to design AI systems that engage in natural, mixed-initiative dialogue with users. They will analyze the key components of such systems, including user intent recognition, preference elicitation, and explainable recommendations, moving beyond a simple “black box” model.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon earlier module concepts like usability, trust, and explainable AI (XAI). It demonstrates how theoretical HCAI frameworks are operationalized into practical systems. Jung’s work connects recommendation algorithms with human-computer interaction (HCI), showcasing the interdisciplinary nature of the field.

**4. Real-world applications**
The principles from this research are applied in many modern services. Students will recognize their influence in the conversational interfaces of virtual assistants (e.g., Alexa, Siri), streaming platforms that refine suggestions through dialogue (e.g., “taste tuning”), and e-commerce chatbots that help users discover products through conversation rather than simple search.
