### Learning Purpose: Module 5 - Human-Centred AI

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because it addresses the ethical and practical necessity of designing AI systems that augment human capabilities, align with human values, and operate transparently and fairly. As AI becomes increasingly integrated into society, a human-centred approach is vital to prevent harm, build trust, and ensure technology serves humanity's best interests, not the other way around.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core principles of Human-Centred AI (HCAI), focusing on frameworks for designing AI that is safe, trustworthy, and enhances user experience. They will explore methods for incorporating human feedback, ensuring fairness, mitigating bias, and maintaining meaningful human control over automated systems.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module synthesizes and applies concepts from earlier modules on ethics, bias in algorithms (Module 3), and explainable AI (XAI - Module 4). It provides the practical, design-focused culmination of those theoretical ideas, demonstrating how to build systems that responsibly implement ethical AI principles.

**4. Real-world applications**
The principles of HCAI are applied in the development of collaborative robots (cobots), AI-assisted medical diagnostics, content recommendation systems that prioritize user well-being, and equitable loan-approval algorithms. It is the foundational philosophy behind creating AI that is a beneficial partner in healthcare, finance, education, and daily life.
