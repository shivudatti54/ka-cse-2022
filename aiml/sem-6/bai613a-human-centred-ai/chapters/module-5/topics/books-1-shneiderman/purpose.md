### Learning Purpose: Module 5, Topic: Books 1. Shneiderman

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding Ben Shneiderman's work is crucial because it provides a critical, human-centric framework for designing and evaluating AI systems. It moves the discourse beyond pure technical performance, focusing instead on how AI can enhance human capabilities, ensure reliability, and maintain ethical responsibility. This perspective is vital for developing trustworthy and socially beneficial technology.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core principles of Shneiderman’s influential framework, often summarized by his eight golden rules for design and his emphasis on human control (HCI) over automation (HAI). They will analyze his concepts of reliability, safety, and trustworthiness, and understand how to apply a human-centred thinking process to AI design challenges.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds on earlier modules concerning AI ethics and system design. It offers a practical, user-experience-focused complement to more abstract ethical theories (e.g., utilitarianism, deontology) and technical concepts like model explainability (XAI). It also serves as a foundation for discussing governance and policy in subsequent modules.

**4. Real-world applications**
These principles are applied in designing collaborative AI tools across healthcare (e.g., diagnostic aids that support doctor decision-making), autonomous vehicles (ensuring driver awareness and control), and customer service chatbots. They are fundamental for creating systems where humans and AI work together effectively, safely, and ethically.
