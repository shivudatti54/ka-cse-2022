# Practice, Safety Culture through Business Management Strategies

## Revision Notes

### Introduction

- Business management strategies play a crucial role in establishing a safe culture.
- Safety culture is a shared set of values, attitudes, and practices that promote a safe working environment.

### Key Points

- **Safety Culture Models**:
  - Organizational culture model
  - Safety culture model by Hurtz and Hedge
  - National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health (NIOSH) model
- **Business Management Strategies**:
  - Leadership commitment and communication
  - Employee engagement and participation
  - Safety training and awareness programs
  - Performance measurement and feedback
- **Trustworthy Certification**:
  - Independent oversight and auditing
  - Certification by third-party organizations (e.g. ISO 45001)
  - Regular reviews and assessments

### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Safety Performance Index (SPI)**: a measure of an organization's safety performance
- **Loss Frequency (LF)**: the number of losses per unit of time
- **Loss Severity (LS)**: the severity of each loss

### Theorems and Concepts

- **The Law of Unintended Consequences**: unexpected events can have significant safety impacts
- **The Human Factor in Safety**: human behavior and decision-making play a critical role in safety
- **The 5 Whys**: a method for identifying root causes of safety issues
