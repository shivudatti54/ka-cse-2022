### Learning Purpose: Reliable Systems Based on Sound Software Engineering Practice

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Building AI on robust software engineering (SE) foundations is critical for safety, security, and trust. Without it, even the most brilliant AI models can fail catastrophically due to bugs, scaling issues, or security vulnerabilities. This practice ensures AI systems are not just theoretically sound but also dependable, maintainable, and integrable into real-world applications, moving them from prototypes to production-ready solutions.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to apply core SE principles—such as version control, continuous integration/deployment (CI/CD), rigorous testing, and modular design—specifically to AI development. They will understand how to create reproducible ML pipelines, manage model and data versioning, and write tests for data and models, not just code.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly operationalizes the principles of Human-Centred AI (HCAI). Reliability is a prerequisite for fairness, accountability, and transparency (e.g., a buggy system cannot be audited). It connects algorithmic ethics (Module 2) to practical deployment (Module 5) and ensures that the user trust built through design (Module 3) is not broken by technical failures.

**4. Real-world applications**
This is essential for deploying high-stakes AI in healthcare (diagnostic systems), finance (fraud detection), autonomous vehicles, and any commercial product. It is the standard practice in industry to ensure systems scale, can be updated safely, and remain secure against attacks, thereby upholding their human-centred promise.
