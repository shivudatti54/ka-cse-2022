### Module 2: Defining Reliable AI

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Establishing reliability is fundamental to building trustworthy AI systems. Without a clear definition and methodology for ensuring reliability, AI can produce unpredictable, unsafe, or biased outcomes, eroding user confidence and posing significant risks in critical applications like healthcare or autonomous systems.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define "reliability" in an AI context, moving beyond simple accuracy to encompass consistency, robustness against adversarial attacks, and performance in edge cases. They will be introduced to key evaluation metrics and testing frameworks designed to stress-test AI models and ensure they perform as expected in real-world scenarios.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the practical extension of ethical AI principles (Module 1), providing the technical rigor needed to achieve fairness, safety, and accountability. It directly informs subsequent modules on robust system design and transparent model interpretation, forming a core pillar of the development lifecycle.

**4. Real-world applications**
Defining reliability is critical for deploying AI in high-stakes domains. This includes creating robust medical diagnostic tools, reliable fraud detection systems in finance, and ensuring the consistent, safe performance of autonomous vehicles under diverse and challenging conditions.
