# Learning Purpose: Two-Dimensional HCAI Framework

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because it provides a structured, practical model for moving beyond the abstract ideal of "human-centred" AI. It offers designers and developers a concrete tool to analyze, critique, and envision AI systems based on the balance of automation and human control, ensuring technology augments rather than replaces human capability.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to deconstruct and apply the two-dimensional HCAI framework, which maps AI systems based on levels of automation (from low to high) and human autonomy (from low to high). They will identify where a system falls on this map and use it to propose design improvements that better balance efficiency with meaningful human control and oversight.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This framework directly operationalizes the core human-centred AI principles from Module 1. It connects to future modules on ethics (by highlighting trade-offs in control) and design (by providing a blueprint for creating balanced systems). It serves as the foundational analytical tool for the course.

**4. Real-world applications**
This model is used to analyze and redesign real-world systems, such as comparing the high-automation/low-autonomy design of social media recommendation engines with the more balanced design of collaborative AI tools like GitHub Copilot, which augment a programmer's autonomy.
