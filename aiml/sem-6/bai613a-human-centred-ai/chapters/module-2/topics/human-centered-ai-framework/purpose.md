### Learning Purpose: HUMAN-CENTERED AI FRAMEWORK

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because it provides the foundational methodology for designing AI systems that are ethical, trustworthy, and beneficial for people. It moves beyond pure technical performance to ensure AI aligns with human values, needs, and context, mitigating risks like bias, opacity, and poor user experience.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core components and stages of a structured HCAI framework. This includes principles for human-AI collaboration, methods for stakeholder engagement, iterative design processes for building trust, and techniques for evaluating AI impact on human well-being and societal outcomes.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This framework operationalizes the ethical principles from Module 1 (e.g., fairness, transparency) into a practical design process. It connects directly to subsequent modules on implementation (e.g., prototyping, evaluation) by providing the structure needed to build and assess human-centred AI systems effectively.

**4. Real-world applications**
This framework is applied in developing responsible recommendation systems, clinical decision-support tools that augment (not replace) doctors, accessible AI features in consumer products, and public sector algorithms designed for equitable outcomes, ensuring technology serves humanity.
