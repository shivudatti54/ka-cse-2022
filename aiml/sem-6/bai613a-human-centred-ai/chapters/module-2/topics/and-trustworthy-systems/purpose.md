# Learning Purpose: Module 2 - Trustworthy Systems

### 1. Why is this topic important?
As AI systems become deeply integrated into critical sectors like healthcare, finance, and justice, their decisions have profound societal impact. Trust is the cornerstone of user adoption and ethical deployment. Without it, AI risks causing harm, perpetuating bias, and eroding public confidence. This topic is vital for building systems that are not only effective but also responsible, fair, and aligned with human values.

### 2. What will students learn?
Students will learn the core pillars of trustworthy AI: fairness (mitigating algorithmic bias), accountability (audit trails and responsibility), transparency (explainable AI/XAI), and robustness (security and safety). They will analyze case studies of AI failures and successes, and apply frameworks and technical methods to assess and improve the trustworthiness of a system.

### 3. How does it connect to other concepts?
This topic is the practical application of the ethical principles introduced in Module 1. It directly connects to user experience (UX) design (building trust through interaction) and precedes modules on governance and regulation, as trustworthy systems are a prerequisite for legal compliance and effective policy.

### 4. Real-world applications
Skills from this module are applied in developing unbiased hiring algorithms, creating explainable loan-approval systems, building secure autonomous vehicles, and implementing transparent diagnostic tools in healthcare—any domain where reliability and fairness are non-negotiable.
