### Learning Purpose: Design Guidelines and Examples

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding and applying design guidelines is crucial for moving beyond theoretical concepts of Human-Centred AI (HCAI) to building practical, ethical, and effective systems. It provides a concrete framework to ensure AI is designed *with* and *for* humans, mitigating risks like bias, opacity, and loss of control.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify, analyze, and apply core HCAI design principles (e.g., respect for human autonomy, fairness, transparency). They will evaluate real-world AI interfaces and systems against these guidelines and practice drafting design solutions for common AI interaction challenges.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic operationalizes the ethical foundations from Module 1, turning abstract values into actionable design rules. It also provides the essential toolkit for the subsequent modules on prototyping and evaluation (e.g., heuristic evaluation), ensuring that those processes are grounded in established best practices.

**4. Real-world applications**
These guidelines are directly applied by UX designers, product managers, and AI developers to create trustworthy applications—from explainable loan-approval algorithms and controllable recommendation engines to transparent healthcare diagnostics and accountable content moderation systems.
