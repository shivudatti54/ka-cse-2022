### Learning Purpose: Autoregressive Integrated Moving Average (ARIMA) Processes

**1. Importance**
ARIMA models are a cornerstone of modern time series analysis. They are vital because they provide a structured, powerful, and widely applicable framework for forecasting non-stationary data—the most common type found in real-world scenarios like economics, weather, and business metrics. Understanding ARIMA is fundamental to building accurate, interpretable predictive models.

**2. Learning Outcomes**
Students will learn to identify non-stationary time series and apply differencing to achieve stationarity. They will understand how to combine Autoregressive (AR) and Moving Average (MA) components into a unified ARIMA(p,d,q) model. Students will master the model identification process using Autocorrelation (ACF) and Partial Autocorrelation (PACF) plots and learn to estimate parameters and use the model for forecasting.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts**
This module directly builds on prior knowledge of stationarity (from Module 1), AR processes, and MA processes. It integrates the concepts of differencing (the "I") with these models. ARIMA serves as a crucial prerequisite for more advanced models like Seasonal ARIMA (SARIMA) and ARIMAX, which incorporate external regressors.

**4. Real-World Applications**
ARIMA models are extensively used for forecasting stock prices, predicting product demand and sales revenue, estimating energy load, forecasting economic indicators like GDP or unemployment rates, and in meteorology for weather prediction.
