### Learning Purpose: Calculating Forecasts and Probability Limits

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because it moves beyond simply modeling time series data to making actionable, quantified predictions about the future. Calculating a point forecast provides a single best estimate, but understanding probability limits (forecast intervals) is what truly assesses risk and uncertainty. This is the foundation for reliable decision-making in volatile environments.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the mechanics of generating point forecasts from established models (e.g., ARIMA, ETS). Crucially, they will learn to calculate and interpret prediction intervals, which define a range of likely future values with a specified level of statistical confidence. This involves understanding the propagation of error and model variance.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds on the foundational concepts covered in Module 1, such as model identification, estimation, and diagnostics. A well-specified and validated model is a prerequisite for accurate forecasting. It also provides the critical link to decision-making modules, where forecasts are evaluated and acted upon.

**4. Real-world applications**
This skill is applied everywhere forecasting is essential: predicting product demand for inventory management, estimating future stock prices or economic indicators in finance, forecasting energy load for grid operators, and predicting patient outcomes or disease spread in public health. The intervals quantify the "known unknowns," allowing for robust planning.
