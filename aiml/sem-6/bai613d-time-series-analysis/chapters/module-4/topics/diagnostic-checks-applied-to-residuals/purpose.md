### Learning Purpose: Diagnostic Checks Applied to Residuals

**1. Why is this topic important?**
A model is only valid if its residuals (the differences between observed and predicted values) behave like white noise. Diagnostic checking is the critical final step that verifies a model's adequacy and ensures its statistical assumptions are met. Skipping this leads to unreliable forecasts, poor decisions, and a fundamental misunderstanding of the underlying process.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to apply a suite of diagnostic tests to model residuals. This includes analyzing the Autocorrelation Function (ACF) and Partial ACF plots to detect leftover patterns, conducting statistical tests (e.g., Ljung-Box test) for residual autocorrelation, and assessing normality and homoscedasticity. The goal is to determine if the residuals are uncorrelated and random.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the essential follow-up to model identification and parameter estimation (Modules 2 & 3). It acts as a feedback mechanism: if diagnostics fail, you must return to the identification stage and propose a new, better-suited model. It directly ensures the validity of the forecasting and inference concepts covered in subsequent modules.

**4. Real-world applications**
This is a non-negotiable practice in fields like finance (validating risk models), supply chain (ensuring accurate demand forecasts), economics (testing macroeconomic models), and quality control (monitoring manufacturing processes for anomalies). It is the key to building robust, trustworthy models used for real-world decision-making.
