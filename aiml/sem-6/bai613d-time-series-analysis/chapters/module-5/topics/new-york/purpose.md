### Learning Purpose: Module 5 - New York Case Study

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial as it moves from theoretical models to a practical, complex real-world application. Analyzing time series data from a major metropolitan hub like New York provides a rich context to understand the challenges of working with noisy, high-frequency data influenced by multiple seasonal and exogenous factors.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to apply core time series techniques (e.g., decomposition, ARIMA, SARIMA) to a multifaceted dataset, potentially encompassing areas like transportation volume, energy consumption, or financial markets. They will practice model selection, diagnostics, and forecasting within a realistic scenario, honing their analytical and interpretative skills.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module synthesizes concepts from previous modules, including stationarity, autocorrelation, trend/seasonality decomposition, and forecasting. It acts as a capstone experience, requiring students to integrate their knowledge to build a robust analytical pipeline from data preprocessing to final interpretation.

**4. Real-world applications**
The skills gained are directly applicable to forecasting urban demand (e.g., for public utilities or traffic management), analyzing economic indicators, and modeling financial data from the NYSE. This provides a foundational skill set for roles in data science, economics, urban planning, and finance.
