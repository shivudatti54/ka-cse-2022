### Learning Purpose: Semester-End Examination (Time Series Analysis)

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This examination is crucial as it serves as a comprehensive assessment of your ability to apply time series analysis techniques independently. It validates your understanding of the entire course, ensuring you can synthesize concepts to solve complex, unseen problems—a key skill for data-driven decision-making in professional settings.

**2. What will students learn?**
You will demonstrate your mastery of the core concepts covered in the course. This includes identifying patterns (trend, seasonality), constructing ARIMA (Autoregressive Integrated Moving Average) models, performing diagnostics, and generating forecasts. The exam assesses both your technical proficiency in model building and your critical judgment in interpreting results.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
The exam integrates all prior modules. You will connect data preprocessing (stationarity, differencing) from Module 2 with model identification (ACF/PACF plots) from Module 3, parameter estimation from Module 4, and forecasting techniques. This synthesis shows how each step is a vital part of a cohesive analytical pipeline.

**4. Real-world applications**
This summative evaluation mirrors real-world scenarios where analysts must provide accurate forecasts without guidance. These skills are directly applicable in finance (predicting stock prices), economics (forecasting GDP), supply chain management (demand planning), and meteorology (weather forecasting), making your expertise highly valuable.
