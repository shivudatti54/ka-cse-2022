### Learning Purpose: Continuous Internal Evaluation in Time Series Analysis

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) is crucial because time series analysis is a cumulative subject where concepts build upon one another. CIE provides regular feedback, ensuring students master foundational skills like data stationarity, model identification (ARIMA), and forecasting *before* moving on. It transforms a high-stakes final exam into a manageable learning process, identifying gaps early to prevent falling behind.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to systematically apply theoretical concepts to practical problems through regular assignments, quizzes, and case studies. They will develop the skill to critically evaluate their own work, diagnose model fit (e.g., using ACF/PACF plots and residual analysis), and refine their analytical approach based on iterative feedback.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
CIE directly integrates with all prior modules: descriptive analysis, smoothing techniques, and ARIMA modeling. It forces the practical synthesis of these concepts, ensuring that theoretical knowledge of autocorrelation, seasonality, and differencing is correctly applied to build and validate predictive models.

**4. Real-world applications**
This process mirrors the iterative, feedback-driven nature of real-world analytics in fields like finance (risk modeling), supply chain (demand forecasting), and public health (disease outbreak prediction). Professionals constantly build, test, evaluate, and refine their models; CIE instills this essential cyclical practice.
