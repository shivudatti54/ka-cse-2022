### **Learning Purpose: Likelihood and Sum-of-Squares Functions**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is fundamental because it provides the core mathematical and statistical framework for building and validating time series models. Understanding these functions is crucial for moving beyond simply fitting a model to rigorously evaluating its quality, precision, and reliability. They form the basis for parameter estimation, hypothesis testing, and model selection.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define and interpret the likelihood function for time series models, understanding it as a measure of model probability given the observed data. They will connect the principle of Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE) to the simpler Least Squares (Sum-of-Squares) method, learning how to derive estimators, compute them in statistical software, and interpret their output for diagnostic purposes.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds upon the ARIMA model structures introduced earlier. It provides the mathematical machinery needed to fit these models' parameters optimally. It is a prerequisite for subsequent topics like model diagnostics (e.g., checking residuals), forecasting (by providing confidence intervals), and advanced concepts like model selection criteria (AIC, BIC), which are derived from the likelihood function.

**4. Real-world applications**
These techniques are applied everywhere time series models are used: in finance to estimate the volatility of stock returns (e.g., GARCH models), in economics to forecast GDP or inflation, in epidemiology to model disease spread, and in engineering for signal processing and quality control. They ensure predictions are not just point estimates but come with quantifiable uncertainty.
