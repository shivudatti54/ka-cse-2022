# Mobile Application Development

## Introduction to Android OS: Android Description – Open Handset Alliance – Android

### Chapter 1: Introduction to Android OS

#### What is Android OS?

Android is an open-source mobile operating system (OS) developed by the Open Handset Alliance (OHA). It was created by Android Inc. in 2003 and was later acquired by Google in 2005.

#### Key Features of Android OS

- Open-source and customizable
- Based on the Linux kernel
- Supports a wide range of devices, including smartphones and tablets
- Provides a flexible and modular architecture
- Offers a variety of user interfaces and experiences

#### History of Android OS

- 2003: Android Inc. is founded by Andy Rubin, Rich Miner, Nick Sears, and Chris White.
- 2005: Google acquires Android Inc. and integrates it into the Android ecosystem.
- 2008: The first Android device, the T-Mobile G1, is released.
- 2010: Android 2.2 (Froyo) is released, featuring a more robust browser and improved performance.
- 2011: Android 4.0 (Ice Cream Sandwich) is released, introducing a new design language and improved performance.

#### Android OS Architecture

- ** kernel mode**: The kernel is the core of the operating system, responsible for managing hardware resources and providing services to applications.
- **user mode**: User mode applications run on top of the kernel, interacting with the kernel for services and resources.
- ** Dalvik virtual machine**: The Dalvik VM is a virtual machine that runs Android apps, providing a sandboxed environment for each app.

### Chapter 2: Open Handset Alliance (OHA)

#### What is OHA?

The Open Handset Alliance (OHA) is a collaborative effort between Google and several leading technology companies to develop and promote the Android operating system.

#### Key Partners of OHA

- Google
- Samsung
- HTC
- Motorola
- Sony Ericsson
- LG
- Motorola
- One Plus

#### Benefits of OHA

- **Open-source**: Android is open-source, allowing developers to contribute to the code and create custom versions for various devices.
- **Customization**: The open-source nature of Android enables device manufacturers to customize the OS to suit their devices' specific needs.
- **Competition**: The OHA's collaborative approach promotes competition among device manufacturers, driving innovation and lower prices.

#### OHA's Role in Android Development

- **Standardization**: The OHA works to establish standards for Android development, ensuring compatibility across devices.
- **Testing and certification**: The OHA provides testing and certification programs for Android devices, ensuring they meet certain standards for performance and security.
- **Support and maintenance**: The OHA provides support and maintenance for Android devices, including security patches and software updates.

#### OHA's Impact on the Android Ecosystem

- **Increased adoption**: The OHA's efforts have contributed to the widespread adoption of Android, with over 2 billion devices sold worldwide.
- **Improved user experience**: The OHA's focus on user experience has led to the development of more intuitive and user-friendly interfaces.
- **Economic benefits**: The OHA's collaborative approach has created new economic opportunities for device manufacturers and developers.
