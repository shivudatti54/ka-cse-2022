The purpose of learning Chapters 6 and 7 on mobile application development is to understand the concept of Intent, Intent Filters, Activity Life Cycle, and Broadcast Life Cycle, which are fundamental components of Android app development.

This topic matters because it provides a crucial understanding of how Android apps interact with each other and with the device's operating system.

Real-world applications include developing apps that respond to user input, such as launching other apps, sharing data, or sending notifications.

This topic connects to other concepts, such as the overall application flow and the AndroidManifest.xml file, as Intent and Intent Filters play a critical role in determining which activities a user can launch from an app.
