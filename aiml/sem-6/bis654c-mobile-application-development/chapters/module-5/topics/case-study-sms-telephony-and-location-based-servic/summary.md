# Case Study: SMS Telephony and Location Based Services

=============================================

### Overview

---

- SMS (Short Message Service) is a text messaging service used for sending short messages between mobile devices.
- Location Based Services (LBS) utilize the device's location information to provide location-based services.
- This case study examines the integration of SMS and LBS in Android applications.

### Key Points

---

- **SMS Telephony:**
  - SMS messages are sent using the Short Message Service (SMS) protocol.
  - SMS messages are limited to 160 characters.
  - SMS is often used for instant messaging and short messaging services.
- **Location Based Services (LBS):**
  - LBS utilize the device's location information to provide location-based services.
  - GPS (Global Positioning System) is a common method used to determine location.
  - LBS is often used in navigation systems and mapping applications.
- **Integration of SMS and LBS:**
  - SMS can be used to send and receive location-related data.
  - LBS can be used to determine the location of the device based on the mobile network's location information.

### Important Formulas, Definitions, and Theorems

---

- **SMS Formula:** `SMS = (Length of message) / 160`
- **Location Based Services Formula:** `LBS = (Device location) + (Location accuracy)`
- ** GSM (Global System for Mobile Communications) Theorem:** GSM is a standard for mobile communication that allows for the use of SMS and LBS.
