# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should students able to:

1. Identify and describe the four classical synchronization problems: Producer-Consumer, Readers-Writers, Dining Philosophers, and Sleeping Barber
2. Explain the specific synchronization challenges posed by each classical problem
3. Implement solutions to classical synchronization problems using semaphores
4. Analyze potential issues (deadlock, starvation) in synchronization solutions and propose improvements
5. Compare and contrast the synchronization requirements of different classical problems
6. Apply the concepts of mutual exclusion, progress, and bounded waiting to real synchronization scenarios
