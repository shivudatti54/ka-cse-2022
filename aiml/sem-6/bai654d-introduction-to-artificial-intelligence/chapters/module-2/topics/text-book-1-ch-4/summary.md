### Text Book 1: Ch 4 - Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

#### Revision Notes

**Key Concepts:**

- Introduction to Artificial Intelligence (AI) and its applications
- Knowledge Representation (KR) and the role of KR in AI
- Formal representation of knowledge using predicate logic

**Definition of Terms:**

- **Knowledge Representation (KR):** The process of encoding knowledge in a form that can be processed by a computer.
- **Predicate Logic:** A formal system for representing and reasoning about knowledge using logical statements and predicates.

**Predicate Logic Formulas and Rules:**

- **Predicate Logic Formula:** `F(x)`: `F` is a predicate, and `x` is a variable.
- **Universal Quantifier:** ∀ (for all)
- **Existential Quantifier:** ∃ (there exists)
- **Modus Ponens:** If `P` implies `Q`, and `P` is true, then `Q` is true.

**Theorems and Results:**

- **Resolution Rule:** A rule for deducing new formulas from a set of axioms and a set of conclusions.
- **Unification:** The process of finding a substitution that makes two formulas equivalent.

**Important Formulas and Theorems:**

- **Monotonicity:** If `P` implies `Q` and `Q` is a subset of `R`, then `P` implies `R`.
- **Monotonicity Theorem:** If a set of formulas `A` is closed under implication, then `A` is closed under resolution.

**Key Takeaways:**

- Understanding the basics of predicate logic and its role in knowledge representation is crucial for building AI systems.
- Knowledge representation is a fundamental aspect of AI, and predicate logic provides a formal framework for representing and reasoning about knowledge.
