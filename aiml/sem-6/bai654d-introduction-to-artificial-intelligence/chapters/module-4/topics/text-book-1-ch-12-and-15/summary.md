### Introduction

---

- **Artificial Intelligence (AI)**: The development of computer systems able to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence.
- **Game Playing**: A subfield of AI that focuses on developing algorithms and strategies for playing games.

### Game Playing (Ch 12)

---

- **Minimax Algorithm**: A recursive algorithm used for decision making in games like chess and tic-tac-toe.
  - Formula: `max(M, s) = max(a) { max(min(M', s') | a) }`, where `M` is the maximizer, `s` is the state, and `a` is the action.
- **Alpha-Beta Pruning**: An optimization technique used to reduce computation in the minimax algorithm.
- **Game Trees**: A tree-like data structure representing all possible moves and their outcomes in a game.

### Natural Language Processing (Ch 15)

---

- **Natural Language Processing (NLP)**: The branch of AI that deals with the interaction between computers and human language.
- **Tokenization**: The process of breaking down text into individual words or tokens.
- **Part-of-Speech Tagging**: The process of identifying the grammatical category of each word in a sentence.
- **Named Entity Recognition (NER)**: The process of identifying named entities such as people, places, and organizations in a sentence.

### Key Definitions

---

- **State**: The current situation or status of a game or system.
- **Action**: The possible action or move in a game or system.
- **Decision**: The choice made based on the current state and possible actions.
- **Optimization**: The process of finding the best solution among a set of possible solutions.
