### Revision Notes for Text Book 1: Ch 7 - Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

#### Key Concepts

- **Artificial Intelligence (AI)**: branch of computer science focused on creating intelligent machines
- **Machine Learning (ML)**: subset of AI that enables machines to learn from data without being explicitly programmed
- **Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs)**: primary model for machine learning, inspired by biological neural networks

#### Statistical Reasoning

- **Bayes' Theorem**: updates probability estimates based on new evidence (P(A|B) = P(B|A) \* P(A) / P(B))
- **Naive Bayes**: probabilistic model that assumes independence between features, simplifying complex distributions
- **Conditional Probability**: P(A|B) = P(A and B) / P(B)

#### Symbolic Reasoning under Uncertainty

- **Probability Theory**: mathematical framework for quantifying uncertainty
- **Markov Decision Processes (MDPs)**: mathematical model for decision-making under uncertainty
- **Decision Theory**: framework for evaluating decisions under uncertainty

#### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Entropy**: measure of uncertainty (H = -∑p(x) \* log2(p(x)))
- **Information Gain**: measure of reduction in uncertainty (IG = H(X) - H(X|Y))
- **Rough Set Theory**: mathematical approach to dealing with uncertainty and imprecision

#### Theorems

- **Bayes' Rule**: P(A|B) = P(B|A) \* P(A) / P(B)
- **Markov's Inequality**: P(X ≥ a) ≤ E(X) / a

These key points should provide a concise revision guide for Text Book 1: Ch 7 - Introduction to Artificial Intelligence.
