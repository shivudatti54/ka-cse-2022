## **Learning and Expert Systems**

### Key Points

- **Learning**: The ability of a system to improve its performance on a task by learning from experience.
- **Expert Systems**: Computer systems that mimic the decision-making ability of a human expert in a particular domain.
- **Knowledge Representation**: Methods for representing knowledge in a way that can be used by a computer.
- **Inference Engine**: A component of an expert system that uses rules to draw conclusions based on the knowledge represented.

### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Bayes' Theorem**: A mathematical formula used in decision-making under uncertainty, which calculates the probability of an event based on prior knowledge and new evidence.
  - P(A|B) = P(A and B) / P(B)
- **Decision Tree**: A graphical representation of a decision-making process, used in expert systems to reason about complex problems.
- **Rule-Based System**: A type of expert system that uses a set of rules to reason about a problem and arrive at a solution.

### Theorems and Concepts

- **Theorem 1**: The law of total probability, which states that the probability of an event is equal to the sum of the probabilities of each possible cause of the event.
- **Probabilistic Reasoning**: A method of reasoning that uses probability theory to make decisions under uncertainty.

### Important Rules and Concepts

- **Rule 1**: If the input is A and B, then the output is C.
- **Inference**: The process of drawing conclusions based on the knowledge represented in a rule-based system.

### Important Expert Systems Concepts

- **Forward Chaining**: A method of reasoning that involves using rules to derive new conclusions based on existing knowledge.
- **Backward Chaining**: A method of reasoning that involves using rules to derive new conclusions based on existing knowledge.
