### Learning Purpose: Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE)

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Continuous Internal Evaluation is a critical pedagogical tool that moves beyond a single high-stakes final exam. It is important because it provides ongoing, formative feedback on a student's grasp of complex AI concepts, allowing for timely intervention and personalized learning paths. It encourages consistent engagement with the material, which is essential for mastering the cumulative and rapidly evolving field of AI.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to demonstrate their understanding of AI fundamentals through a variety of assessments (e.g., quizzes, projects, presentations). They will develop the ability to apply theoretical knowledge to practical problems, critically analyze case studies, and effectively communicate their findings. The process aims to build a comprehensive and applied understanding of the module's content.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
CIE directly connects to and reinforces all prior modules (e.g., Machine Learning, Neural Networks, Ethics). By continuously applying and being assessed on these interconnected concepts, students solidify the relationships between them. For instance, a project may require using a machine learning algorithm (Module 3) while considering its ethical implications (Module 4), creating a holistic learning experience.

**4. Real-world applications**
The skills developed through CIE mirror real-world AI development cycles. In industry, AI projects are iterative, requiring constant testing, evaluation, and refinement—much like the CIE process. This approach prepares students for professional environments where continuous integration, evaluation, and collaboration are standard practice for building robust and effective AI systems.
