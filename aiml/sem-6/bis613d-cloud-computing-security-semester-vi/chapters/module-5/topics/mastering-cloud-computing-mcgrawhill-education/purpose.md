### Learning Purpose: Mastering Cloud Computing (McGraw-Hill Education)

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding cloud security is paramount as organizations increasingly migrate critical data and infrastructure to cloud environments. This topic addresses the core principles and practices needed to protect sensitive information, ensure compliance, and maintain business continuity in the cloud, making it a fundamental skill for modern IT professionals.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify key security threats and vulnerabilities specific to cloud platforms. They will explore essential security concepts, including Identity and Access Management (IAM), data encryption, network security controls, and security shared responsibility models as presented in the McGraw-Hill text.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds upon knowledge of networking, data storage, and virtualization from earlier semesters. It applies core cybersecurity principles to the unique, scalable, and on-demand context of cloud computing, integrating concepts of risk management and compliance.

**4. Real-world applications**
The skills learned are directly applicable to securing services on major platforms like AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud. Students will be equipped to contribute to real-world tasks such as designing secure cloud architectures, implementing access policies, and ensuring data protection for enterprise applications.
