### Learning Purpose: The History of Blockchain

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the history of blockchain is crucial because it provides context for its revolutionary nature. It moves beyond the hype of cryptocurrencies, showing the evolution of a solution designed to solve the fundamental problem of establishing digital trust without a central authority. This foundation is essential for critically evaluating its potential and limitations.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn about the key milestones and figures in blockchain's development. This includes the cryptographic and conceptual precursors to Bitcoin, the publication of Satoshi Nakamoto's 2008 whitepaper, the mining of the Genesis Block, and the subsequent forks and developments (like Ethereum) that expanded blockchain's use beyond digital currency.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This historical context directly sets the stage for all subsequent modules. It introduces core concepts like decentralization, cryptographic hashing, and consensus mechanisms (Proof-of-Work) that will be explored in-depth later. It explains the "why" behind blockchain's design, making technical concepts easier to grasp.

**4. Real-world applications**
The history itself is a case study in problem-solving. The principles established—immutability, transparency, and decentralization—are now being applied to create real-world applications like supply chain tracking, secure voting systems, decentralized finance (DeFi), and smart contracts, moving far beyond its original purpose.
