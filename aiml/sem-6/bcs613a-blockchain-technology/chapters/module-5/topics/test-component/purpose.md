### Learning Purpose: Module 5 - Blockchain Components

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the core components of a blockchain is fundamental to grasping how the technology functions. It moves beyond the abstract concept of a "distributed ledger" and reveals the precise cryptographic and data structures that enable its key properties of decentralization, immutability, and transparency. You cannot critically evaluate different blockchain platforms or their applications without this foundational knowledge.

**2. What will students learn?**
In this module, you will learn to deconstruct a blockchain into its essential components. This includes understanding the role of **cryptographic hashes** (e.g., SHA-256) in creating data fingerprints, the structure of **blocks** and **chains**, and the function of **transactions**. You will also explore the critical role of **consensus mechanisms** (like Proof-of-Work and Proof-of-Stake) in maintaining network agreement and security without a central authority.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This knowledge connects directly to previous modules on cryptography and distributed systems. It provides the necessary technical foundation for upcoming topics such as smart contracts (which are stored on the blockchain), decentralized applications (dApps), and the specific architectures of major platforms like Bitcoin and Ethereum. This module is the crucial link between theory and practical implementation.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge allows you to analyze real-world use cases. You'll understand how a Bitcoin transaction is processed, how a supply chain log achieves immutability, or how a land registry record becomes tamper-proof. It is the essential literacy needed to assess the viability and security of any proposed blockchain solution across industries like finance, logistics, and governance.
