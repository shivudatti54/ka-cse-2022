### **Learning Purpose: Module 5 - Blockchain Technology**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Blockchain is a foundational, disruptive technology reshaping industries by enabling trust, transparency, and security in digital transactions without central intermediaries. Understanding its mechanics is crucial for navigating the future of finance, supply chains, governance, and data management.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core architecture of blockchain, including distributed ledgers, cryptographic hashing, and consensus mechanisms (e.g., Proof-of-Work, Proof-of-Stake). They will analyze how blocks are created and chained, ensuring immutability. The module will also differentiate between various types of blockchains (public, private, consortium) and introduce smart contracts and their self-executing logic.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic integrates concepts from cryptography (hashing, digital signatures), computer networking (peer-to-peer systems), and database management (distributed ledgers). It directly builds upon earlier modules on data security and financial technology, providing a practical application for theoretical principles of decentralization and trustless verification.

**4. Real-world applications**
Knowledge of blockchain is directly applicable in cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin, transforming supply chain logistics for traceability, enabling secure digital identities, facilitating decentralized finance (DeFi) applications, and revolutionizing voting systems and intellectual property management through smart contracts.
