### Learning Purpose: Module 4 - Currency (ETH and ETC)

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding Ethereum's native currencies, ETH and ETC, is foundational to engaging with the world's most-used blockchain for decentralized applications (dApps). It is crucial for comprehending how value is transferred, how network security is incentivized through mining/staking, and the real-world consequences of blockchain governance and consensus.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the distinct purposes of Ether (ETH) as the fuel for the Ethereum network and the historical context behind Ethereum Classic (ETC). They will analyze key concepts like "gas" fees, transaction costs, and the economic models that secure each network. This includes comparing their consensus mechanisms (Proof-of-Work vs. Proof-of-Stake) and the philosophical split (the DAO hack) that created ETC.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon prior knowledge of blockchain fundamentals, consensus algorithms, and smart contracts (Modules 1-3). It provides the critical economic layer that enables those smart contracts to execute. Furthermore, it sets the stage for later modules on DeFi and dApps, which are built primarily on the Ethereum network and require ETH to operate.

**4. Real-world applications**
Knowledge of ETH is essential for using dApps, trading NFTs, participating in DeFi protocols (e.g., lending, borrowing), and paying for network transactions. Understanding the ETC fork provides a critical case study in blockchain governance, immutability, and how communities respond to security crises, lessons applicable to any new protocol.
