### Learning Purpose: Module 4 - Ethereum Blockchain

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Ethereum is a foundational pillar of the Web3 ecosystem. Unlike the Bitcoin blockchain, which is primarily a distributed ledger for cryptocurrency, Ethereum introduced a globally accessible state machine capable of executing smart contracts and hosting decentralized applications (dApps). Understanding Ethereum is therefore crucial for anyone looking to build, invest in, or engage with the next generation of decentralized internet services, financial systems (DeFi), and digital assets (NFTs).

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core components that distinguish the Ethereum blockchain, including its account-based model, the Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM), gas fees, and consensus mechanisms (initially Proof-of-Work, now Proof-of-Stake). The module will provide a practical understanding of how to write a basic smart contract, deploy it to a test network, and interact with it, forming the essential skills for dApp development.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module builds directly upon the general principles of blockchain technology covered in earlier modules (decentralization, immutability, consensus). It provides the specific architectural knowledge required to understand more advanced concepts like Layer-2 scaling solutions (e.g., Optimistic Rollups), decentralized autonomous organizations (DAOs), and the technical underpinnings of the DeFi and NFT markets explored later in the course.

**4. Real-world applications**
Ethereum’s programmability is the backbone of major real-world innovations. It powers trillion-dollar DeFi protocols for lending and borrowing (e.g., Aave), acts as the primary settlement layer for most NFT marketplaces, and enables new organizational structures through DAOs. Its transition to Proof-of-Stake also addresses critical issues of scalability and energy consumption, paving the way for broader adoption.
