# Learning Purpose: Module 4 - Forks

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding forks is crucial because they represent fundamental events in a blockchain's lifecycle. They are the primary mechanism for protocol upgrades, resolving community disputes, and responding to security crises like hacks. Grasping forks is essential for anyone involved in development, investment, or governance within the blockchain ecosystem, as they can drastically alter a network's rules, value, and future direction.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to differentiate between the two main types of forks: soft forks (backward-compatible upgrades) and hard forks (permanent splits creating a new chain). They will examine the technical mechanisms that trigger a fork and the social, political, and economic motivations behind them, using historical case studies like Bitcoin/Bitcoin Cash and Ethereum/Ethereum Classic.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon core concepts like consensus mechanisms (e.g., Proof-of-Work), decentralization, and immutability. It demonstrates how a theoretically immutable ledger can still evolve and adapt. Furthermore, it connects to cryptocurrency valuation, network governance, and the philosophical debate over what constitutes the "true" chain.

**4. Real-world applications**
Knowledge of forks allows for better risk assessment for investors and traders. Developers must understand forks to write robust smart contracts and contribute to protocol development. This understanding is also key for participants in decentralized autonomous organizations (DAOs) who vote on future upgrade proposals that could result in forks.
