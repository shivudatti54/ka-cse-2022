# Blockchain Technology

### Chapter 4: 111-148

#### Key Points

- **Blockchain**: A distributed digital ledger technology that records transactions across a network of computers.
- **Blockchain Structure**:
  - Blocks: A collection of transactions
  - Chain: A sequence of blocks
- **Consensus Mechanisms**:
  - Proof of Work (PoW): Energy-intensive algorithm
  - Proof of Stake (PoS): Less energy-intensive algorithm
- **Hash Functions**:
  - One-way function
  - Used for data integrity and encryption
- **Cryptography**:
  - Public Key Infrastructure (PKI)
  - Digital Signatures
- **Blockchain Types**:
  - Public Blockchains (e.g., Bitcoin)
  - Private Blockchains (e.g., enterprise networks)
  - Consortium Blockchains (e.g., hybrid models)

#### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Hash Function**: `h(x) = one-way function`
- **Digital Signature**: `σ(x) = encrypted message`
- **Public Key**: `pk = (p, q, e)`
- **Private Key**: `sk = (p, q, d)`

#### Theorems and Concepts

- **Birthday Paradox**: The probability of two people sharing a birthday is greater than 50%
- **Halting Problem**: A fundamental result in computability theory
- **Game Theory**: Analyzing strategic decision-making in blockchain networks

#### Quick Revision

- Blockchain is a distributed digital ledger technology
- Consensus mechanisms ensure network agreement
- Hash functions ensure data integrity
- Cryptography secures blockchain communications
- Different types of blockchain exist for various use cases
