# Learning Objectives
After studying this topic, you should be able to:
1.  Describe the structure and key characteristics of the Bitcoin peer-to-peer network.
2.  Differentiate between the roles and functions of full nodes, mining nodes, and SPV (lightweight) nodes.
3.  Explain how transactions and blocks are propagated and validated across the network.
4.  Define the cryptographic components (private key, public key, address) and their relationship within a Bitcoin wallet.
5.  Compare and contrast different types of wallets (custodial vs. non-custodial, hot vs. cold, HD vs. non-HD) based on their security and usability trade-offs.
6.  Trace the lifecycle of a transaction from its creation in a wallet to its confirmation on the blockchain.
