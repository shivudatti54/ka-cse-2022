### Learning Purpose: Module 3 - Bitcoin Definition

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding Bitcoin's definition is foundational because it is the first and most prominent application of blockchain technology. It serves as the archetypal case study for how a decentralized, trustless, peer-to-peer digital currency system operates, setting the standard for the entire cryptocurrency ecosystem.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to precisely define Bitcoin not just as a digital currency, but as a protocol and a decentralized network. This includes understanding its core components: the blockchain (a public ledger), mining (Proof-of-Work consensus), cryptographic principles, and the economic model governing its issuance and transactions.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon the cryptographic hashing and distributed ledger concepts introduced in earlier modules. It provides the essential context needed to explore subsequent topics, such as comparing other consensus mechanisms (e.g., Proof-of-Stake in Ethereum) and analyzing the trade-offs of different blockchain designs like scalability and privacy.

**4. Real-world applications**
Beyond its use as a speculative asset or medium of exchange, understanding Bitcoin’s protocol is crucial for grasping its real-world applications. These include its role as "digital gold" (a store of value), its use in cross-border remittances, and its foundational inspiration for thousands of other cryptocurrencies and decentralized financial (DeFi) applications.
