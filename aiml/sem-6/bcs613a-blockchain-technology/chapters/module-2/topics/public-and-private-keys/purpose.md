# Learning Purpose: Public and Private Keys

## 1. Why is this topic important?
Public and private key cryptography is the foundational security mechanism of blockchain technology. It is what enables users to securely own and control their digital assets without relying on a central authority. Understanding this asymmetric encryption is crucial for grasping how trust and ownership are established in a decentralized network.

## 2. What will students learn?
Students will learn the core cryptographic principles behind key pairs: that a public key acts as a publicly shareable address for receiving assets, while its corresponding private key is a secret password that must be secured to authorize transactions (i.e., create digital signatures). They will understand that losing a private key means losing access forever.

## 3. How does it connect to other concepts?
This concept directly links to creating a cryptocurrency wallet (Module 1), is essential for understanding how transactions are signed and broadcast to the network (Module 3), and forms the basis for smart contract authentication and more advanced cryptographic techniques used in various blockchain protocols.

## 4. Real-world applications
Beyond sending and receiving cryptocurrency (e.g., Bitcoin, Ethereum), this technology is used for securing digital identities, logging into applications without passwords, signing legal documents digitally, and proving ownership of tokenized real-world assets like real estate or art.
