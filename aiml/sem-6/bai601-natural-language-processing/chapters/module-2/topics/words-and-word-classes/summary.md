# **Words and Word Classes**

### Definitions

- **Word**: a unit of language consisting of one or more phonemes (distinct sounds) or orthographic symbols (written symbols)
- **Word class** (or **part of speech**): a category into which a word falls based on its grammatical function and meaning

### Word Classes

- **Nouns**:
  - Definition: words that refer to people, places, things, or ideas
  - Examples: cat, city, happiness
- **Verbs**:
  - Definition: words that express actions, events, or states
  - Examples: run, eat, be
- **Adjectives**:
  - Definition: words that modify or describe nouns or pronouns
  - Examples: happy, big, blue
- **Adverbs**:
  - Definition: words that modify or describe verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs
  - Examples: quickly, very, loudly
- **Pronouns**:
  - Definition: words that replace nouns in a sentence
  - Examples: he, she, it
- **Prepositions**:
  - Definition: words that show relationships between words or phrases
  - Examples: in, on, under
- **Conjunctions**:
  - Definition: words that connect words, phrases, or clauses
  - Examples: and, but, or
- **Interjections**:
  - Definition: words that express emotion or feeling
  - Examples: oh, wow, ouch

### Theories

- **Latin Part of Speech Theory**:
  - Classifies words into seven parts of speech: noun, verb, adjective, adverb, pronoun, preposition, and conjunction
- **Bloomfield's Part of Speech Theory**:
  - Classifies words into nine parts of speech: noun, verb, adjective, adverb, pronoun, preposition, conjunction, interjection, and article

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Chomsky's Universal Grammar**:
  - A theoretical framework that proposes that all humans are born with an innate ability to recognize and generate an infinite number of sentences
- **Zipf's Law**:
  - A statistical law that describes the distribution of word frequencies in language
