# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1.  Define Part-of-Speech (POS) tagging and explain its significance in the NLP pipeline.
2.  Identify and describe the major part-of-speech categories (e.g., noun, verb, adjective) and explain the concept of a tag set.
3.  Explain the core challenge of lexical ambiguity with concrete examples.
4.  Compare and contrast the rule-based, stochastic (HMM), and transformation-based (Brill) approaches to POS tagging, including their advantages and disadvantages.
5.  Describe the function of the transition probability, emission probability, and the Viterbi algorithm within an HMM tagger.
6.  Discuss the specific challenges and considerations involved in POS tagging for Indian languages.
