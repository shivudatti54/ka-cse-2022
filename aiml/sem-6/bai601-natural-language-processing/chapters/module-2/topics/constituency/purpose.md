### Learning Purpose: Constituency

**1. Why is this important?**
Understanding constituency is fundamental to NLP because it reveals the hierarchical, grammatical structure of sentences. This structure is essential for moving beyond simple "bag-of-words" models to truly interpreting meaning, resolving ambiguity, and understanding the relationships between words.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn how sentences are broken down into nested constituents (e.g., noun phrases, verb phrases) using context-free grammars and parsing techniques. They will gain hands-on experience with constituency parsing algorithms and learn to interpret parse trees to deconstruct sentence grammar.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds on foundational concepts like syntax and context-free grammars (Module 1). It is a prerequisite for more advanced topics like dependency parsing (a different structural view), semantic role labeling (who did what to whom), and question answering, which all rely on accurate syntactic analysis.

**4. Real-world applications**
Constituency parsing is crucial for grammar checking tools, complex information extraction systems, sentiment analysis that understands the target of an opinion, and the development of more accurate machine translation and text-to-speech systems.
