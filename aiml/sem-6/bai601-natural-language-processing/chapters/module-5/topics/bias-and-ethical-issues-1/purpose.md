### **Learning Purpose: Bias and Ethical Issues in NLP**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
NLP systems are increasingly integrated into high-stakes domains like hiring, justice, and healthcare. These systems can perpetuate and amplify societal biases present in their training data, leading to discriminatory and harmful outcomes. Understanding these ethical pitfalls is crucial for developing fair, accountable, and trustworthy AI.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify common types of bias (e.g., gender, racial, ideological) within datasets and model outputs. They will explore technical methods for bias mitigation (pre-processing, in-processing, post-processing) and critically assess the ethical frameworks for responsible AI development and deployment.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon foundational concepts like **data preprocessing** (Module 2) and **model training & evaluation** (Module 3/4). It provides the critical ethical lens through which to view the entire NLP pipeline, connecting technical implementation to its real-world societal impact and consequences.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is essential for creating equitable applications, including:
*   **Recruitment software** that filters resumes fairly.
*   **Content moderation tools** that avoid discriminatory flagging.
*   **Clinical diagnostic aids** that work equally well across patient demographics.
*   **Translators and voice assistants** that are inclusive and respectful.
