# Learning Objectives
After studying this topic, you should be able to:
1.  Define Machine Translation and identify its core challenges rooted in language divergences.
2.  Categorize and provide examples of different types of language divergences (lexical, syntactic, etc.).
3.  Explain the Encoder-Decoder architecture, including the role of the encoder, context vector, and decoder.
4.  Analyze the challenges of low-resource translation and describe techniques like back-translation to mitigate them.
5.  Compare and contrast automatic evaluation metrics (BLEU, METEOR, TER) and understand the principles of human evaluation.
6.  Identify potential sources of bias in MT systems and discuss the ethical implications of deployed translation technology.
