**Why this topic matters:**
Learning to improve performance in low-resource scenarios is crucial for machine translation as it enables the development of language systems that can effectively translate languages with limited training data. This is essential for bridging the language gap in regions with scarce linguistic resources, promoting global communication, and enhancing economic development.

**Real-world applications:**
The techniques learned in this topic have real-world applications in industries such as international business, tourism, and education, where translation is critical for effective communication and collaboration across languages and geographical boundaries. For instance, Google Translate can be improved to support more languages, facilitating global access to information and services.

**Connection to other concepts:**
This topic is closely related to other concepts in machine translation, such as language modeling, word embeddings, and transfer learning. By incorporating techniques to improve performance in low-resource scenarios, researchers can develop more robust and versatile language models that can adapt to new languages and domains, ultimately enhancing the overall quality of machine translation systems.
