# **Also Find the Score of Words**

### Overview

This topic focuses on calculating the score of words in machine translation, particularly in the context of natural language processing.

### Key Concepts

- **Word Score**: a measure of how closely a word is related to its translation.
-     **Language Divergences**: differences between languages that affect translation accuracy.

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Word Mover's Distance**: measures the distance between two words in a word embedding space.
  - $d(w, t) = \sqrt{\sum_{i=1}^{n} (w_i - t_i)^2}$
- **Cosine Similarity**: measures the cosine of the angle between two vectors.
  - $\cos(\theta) = \frac{w \cdot t}{\|w\| \|t\|}$
- **Word Embeddings**: low-dimensional vector representations of words.
  - $w \in \mathbb{R}^n, t \in \mathbb{R}^n$

### Key Points

- Word embeddings are used to represent words as vectors.
- Word Mover's Distance and Cosine Similarity are used to calculate word scores.
- Language divergences affect translation accuracy.
- Score of words can be used to improve machine translation accuracy.

### Definitions

- **Word Embedding**: a representation of a word as a vector in a high-dimensional space.
- **Language Divergence**: a measure of the difference between two languages.
- **Translation Accuracy**: the correctness of the machine translation.

### Theorems

- **Word Embedding Theorem**: word embeddings can be used to improve machine translation accuracy.
- **Language Divergence Theorem**: language divergences can affect translation accuracy.
