# **Textbook 1: Ch - Natural Language Processing**

## **Key Points**

### Origins of NLP

- Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a subfield of Artificial Intelligence (AI) that deals with the interaction between computers and humans in natural language.
- NLP is closely related to machine learning and deep learning.

### Language and Knowledge

- Language can be represented as a set of symbols, words, and rules.
- Knowledge representation is a crucial aspect of NLP, as it deals with how to represent knowledge in a machine-readable format.

### Notation and Terminology

- **L** : the language
- **V** : the vocabulary
- **E** : the environment
- **C** : the context

### Definitions

- **Natural Language**: a human language that is used for communication.
- **Formal Language**: a language that is defined by a set of rules and algorithms.
- **Knowledge Representation**: a way to represent knowledge in a machine-readable format.

### Theorems

- **No Free Lunch Theorem**: there is no general-purpose algorithm that can solve all problems efficiently.
- **Valiant's Algorithm**: a theoretical framework for understanding the limits of computation.

### Important Formulas

- **Bayes' Theorem**: P(A|B) = P(A and B) / P(B)
- **Markov Chain**: a mathematical system that undergoes transitions from one state to another.

### Other Key Concepts

- **Tokenization**: the process of breaking down text into individual words or tokens.
- **Part-of-Speech Tagging**: the process of identifying the part of speech (e.g. noun, verb, adjective) of each word in a sentence.
