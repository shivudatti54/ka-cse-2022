# Learning Purpose: Bigram Models in NLP

**1. Importance:** Bigrams are a fundamental concept in statistical Natural Language Processing (NLP). They provide a simple yet powerful way to model the probability and structure of language by capturing the likelihood of a word given its immediate predecessor. This is crucial for understanding basic language patterns, co-occurrence, and context at a local level, forming the bedrock for more complex NLP models.

**2. Learning Outcomes:** Students will learn to define a bigram and understand its role in calculating sentence probability. They will be able to build a simple bigram language model from a corpus, including calculating probabilities and handling smoothing techniques to account for unseen word pairs.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts:** This topic is a direct building block for more advanced n-gram models (trigrams, etc.) and is a core component of probabilistic language modeling. It connects directly to key NLP applications like text generation, autocorrect, and machine translation. Understanding bigrams is essential before moving to modern neural approaches (like RNNs and Transformers) that address the limitations of fixed-context models.

**4. Real-World Applications:** Bigram models are used in practical applications such as basic predictive text and autocomplete systems, spelling and grammar correction tools, and as a foundational element in early information retrieval and sentiment analysis systems.
