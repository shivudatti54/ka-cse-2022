### Introduction to FrameNet
Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a subfield of artificial intelligence that deals with the interaction between computers and humans in natural language. One of the key challenges in NLP is understanding the meaning of words and phrases in context. FrameNet is a lexical database that provides a framework for understanding the meaning of words and phrases by identifying the semantic roles played by entities in a sentence. In this module, we will explore the concept of FrameNet and its applications in NLP.

### Core Concepts of FrameNet
FrameNet is based on the idea of "frame semantics," which was introduced by Charles Fillmore in the 1970s. According to this theory, words and phrases evoke frames, which are cognitive structures that represent stereotyped situations. A frame is a set of concepts and relationships that are associated with a particular word or phrase. For example, the word "buy" evokes a frame that includes the concepts of "buyer," "seller," "goods," and "price."

The core concepts of FrameNet include:

* **Frames**: A frame is a set of concepts and relationships that are associated with a particular word or phrase.
* **Frame elements**: Frame elements are the specific concepts and relationships that are part of a frame. For example, the frame element "buyer" is part of the "buy" frame.
* **Lexical units**: A lexical unit is a word or phrase that evokes a frame. For example, the word "buy" is a lexical unit that evokes the "buy" frame.
* **Semantic roles**: Semantic roles are the roles played by entities in a sentence. For example, in the sentence "John bought a book," "John" plays the role of "buyer" and "book" plays the role of "goods."
