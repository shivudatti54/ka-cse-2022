## Natural Language Processing

### Module 1

#### Revision Notes

#### Definitions and Formulas

- **Tokenization**: breaking down text into individual words or tokens
  - Formula: `Token = Character Sequence`
- **Part-of-Speech (POS) Tagging**: identifying the grammatical category of each word
  - Formula: `POS Tag = Word Form`
- **Named Entity Recognition (NER)**: identifying named entities in text (e.g., names, locations)
  - Formula: `NER = Entity Type`
- **Dependency Parsing**: analyzing sentence structure by identifying relationships between words
  - Formula: `Dependency = Word-Word Relationship`

#### Key Concepts

- **Stemming and Lemmatization**: reducing words to their base form
- **Stop Words**: common words (e.g., "the", "and") that do not carry much meaning
- **Syntactic Analysis**: breaking down sentence structure into constituent parts

#### Important Theorems

- **Chomsky's Hypothesis**: all human languages can be generated by a set of rules
- **Markov's Algorithm**: using n-grams to predict the next word in a sentence

#### Other Important Points

- **Context-Free Grammar**: a formal grammar system for defining language structures
- **Parse Trees**: visual representations of sentence structure
