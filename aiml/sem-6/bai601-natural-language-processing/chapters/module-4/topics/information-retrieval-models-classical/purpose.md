### Learning Purpose: Information Retrieval Models - Classical

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Classical models form the foundational bedrock of modern search technology. Understanding them is crucial for grasping how search engines interpret and rank documents in response to a user query, a core function in the digital information age.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the principles, mechanics, and mathematical foundations of the three primary classical models: Boolean, Vector Space, and Probabilistic models. They will understand how each model represents documents and queries, calculates relevance, and ranks results.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module builds directly on text preprocessing (tokenization, stemming) and text representation (bag-of-words, TF-IDF) from earlier modules. It provides the essential groundwork upon which more advanced concepts like machine learning-based ranking and neural information retrieval are built.

**4. Real-world applications**
These models are the engine behind library catalog systems, early web search engines, and enterprise search within organizations (e.g., legal document discovery). While often enhanced with modern techniques, the logic of these classical approaches remains highly influential.
