### Learning Purpose: Why Use Deep Learning?

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the "why" behind deep learning is foundational. It moves beyond the "how" to establish the critical motivation for investing time in these complex models. This topic highlights the unique problem-solving capabilities of deep learning compared to traditional machine learning, justifying its pivotal role in modern AI.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the key advantages of deep learning, including its ability to automatically extract hierarchical features from raw, high-dimensional data (e.g., images, text, sound). They will understand how deep neural networks scale with data and compute power, often achieving superior performance on tasks where hand-crafted feature engineering is impractical.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic provides the essential rationale for the entire module. It directly contrasts with simpler linear models and shallow networks covered in prerequisite ML courses. It sets the stage for subsequent topics like different neural network architectures (CNNs, RNNs) and reinforcement learning, which often leverage deep learning for function approximation.

**4. Real-world applications**
The applications are vast and transformative. Students will see its use in enabling facial recognition in smartphones, powering natural language processing in virtual assistants like Siri and Alexa, recommending content on Netflix and YouTube, and allowing self-driving cars to perceive their environment.
