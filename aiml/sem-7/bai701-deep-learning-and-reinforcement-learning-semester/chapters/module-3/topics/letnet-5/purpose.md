### Learning Purpose: LeNet-5

**1. Why is this topic important?**
LeNet-5 is a foundational convolutional neural network (CNN) architecture developed by Yann LeCun et al. It is historically significant as one of the first successful applications of CNNs, proving their efficacy for practical image recognition tasks, most notably handwritten digit classification. Studying it provides crucial insight into the core architectural principles (convolution, pooling, fully connected layers) that underpin all modern deep learning computer vision models.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the detailed architecture of LeNet-5, including the function and arrangement of its convolutional, subsampling (pooling), and dense layers. They will understand how these layers hierarchically extract features from raw pixel data—from simple edges to complex patterns—for classification. The topic also covers the historical context and the network's limitations by today's standards.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
LeNet-5 is the direct precursor to more advanced CNNs like AlexNet, VGG, and ResNet. Understanding its structure is essential for grasping how these later architectures evolved by using techniques like ReLU activation and deeper layers. It directly builds upon prior knowledge of neural networks and serves as a practical application of convolution operations learned in previous modules.

**4. Real-world applications**
The principles behind LeNet-5 pioneered the use of CNNs in numerous real-world applications. These include optical character recognition (OCR) in systems that process bank checks and postal codes, as well as basic object recognition and early facial detection systems. Its legacy is seen in virtually every modern computer vision system, from medical image analysis to autonomous vehicle perception.
