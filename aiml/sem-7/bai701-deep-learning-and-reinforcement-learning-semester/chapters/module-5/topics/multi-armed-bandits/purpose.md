# Learning Purpose: Multi-Armed Bandits

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Multi-Armed Bandits (MAB) provide a fundamental framework for understanding the core trade-off in reinforcement learning: exploration versus exploitation. This is a critical concept for designing intelligent systems that must learn optimal decisions through trial and error in uncertain environments, forming the basis for more advanced RL algorithms.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the mathematical formulation of the MAB problem and evaluate strategies (policies) like ε-greedy, UCB (Upper Confidence Bound), and Thompson Sampling to solve it. They will understand how to balance exploring new options with exploiting known rewards to maximize cumulative gain.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
MABs serve as a simplified introduction to key RL concepts like value estimation, regret, and policies. This foundational knowledge directly scales to full Reinforcement Learning problems (Markov Decision Processes) and is a building block for more complex algorithms like Q-learning.

**4. Real-world applications**
The principles of multi-armed bandits are widely applied in real-world systems such as clinical trials, A/B testing for website design, online advertising, and recommendation systems, where efficiently allocating resources to the best option is crucial.
