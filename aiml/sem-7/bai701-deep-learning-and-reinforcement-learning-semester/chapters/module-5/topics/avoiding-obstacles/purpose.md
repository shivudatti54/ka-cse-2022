### Learning Purpose: Avoiding Obstacles

**1. Importance:**  
This topic is crucial as obstacle avoidance is a fundamental safety and efficiency requirement for autonomous systems. Mastering it ensures that AI agents, like robots or self-driving cars, can navigate complex, unpredictable environments without collisions, which is essential for real-world deployment.

**2. Learning Outcomes:**  
Students will learn to implement and compare key obstacle avoidance methods. This includes geometric techniques (e.g., Potential Fields) for predictable environments and deep reinforcement learning (DRL) algorithms (e.g., DQN, DDPG) that enable agents to learn sophisticated avoidance policies directly from sensor data through trial and error.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts:**  
This topic directly builds upon core concepts from the module. It applies deep neural networks (for processing LiDAR or camera input) and reinforcement learning principles (like reward shaping for penalizing collisions). It also connects to broader themes of path planning, agent perception, and safe exploration.

**4. Real-World Applications:**  
The techniques learned are directly used in the navigation systems of self-driving cars, autonomous drones, warehouse robots, and robotic assistants. This skill is vital for creating intelligent agents that can operate reliably alongside humans in dynamic settings.
