### **Learning Purpose: Man-in-the-middle (MitM) Attack**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
MitM attacks are a fundamental and pervasive threat where an attacker secretly intercepts and potentially alters communication between two parties. Understanding them is crucial because they directly compromise the core principles of data security: confidentiality, integrity, and authenticity. These attacks can lead to severe consequences, including stolen credentials, financial fraud, and data breaches.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define a MitM attack and identify common techniques like ARP spoofing, DNS spoofing, and rogue Wi-Fi access points. They will analyze how these attacks exploit network protocols to eavesdrop on or manipulate data transmissions. The module will also cover key mitigation strategies, including the use of encryption (e.g., HTTPS, VPNs) and certificate-based authentication.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is a practical application of cryptographic principles learned in Module 1, demonstrating why encryption and secure key exchange are essential. It directly relates to network security concepts (like secure protocol design) and sets the stage for later modules on intrusion detection and public key infrastructure (PKI), which provide the primary defenses against such attacks.

**4. Real-world applications**
Knowledge of MitM attacks is directly applicable to securing daily activities like online banking, email communication, and corporate network access. IT professionals use this understanding to configure secure networks, implement monitoring systems, and educate users on the dangers of unsecured public Wi-Fi, making it a vital skill for roles in cybersecurity and network administration.
