### Learning Purpose: Description of the Algorithm

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the fundamental algorithms that underpin data security is crucial. It moves beyond abstract concepts to provide a concrete foundation for how confidentiality, integrity, and authentication are technically achieved. You cannot effectively implement, evaluate, or troubleshoot security protocols without knowing how their core components work.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the operational mechanics of essential cryptographic algorithms. This includes analyzing the steps of symmetric encryption (e.g., AES), asymmetric encryption (e.g., RSA), and hashing functions (e.g., SHA-256). The focus will be on their design principles, inputs/outputs, and their respective strengths and limitations.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This knowledge is the essential building block for all subsequent topics. It directly connects to Module 1's concepts of threats and risks by providing the countermeasures. It is the prerequisite for understanding secure communication protocols (SSL/TLS), digital signatures, public key infrastructure (PKI), and password storage mechanisms covered later.

**4. Real-world applications**
This applies directly to securing web traffic (HTTPS), encrypting sensitive data stored in databases, verifying software updates have not been tampered with, and protecting the integrity of digital contracts through hashing. It is the invisible technology protecting everyday online transactions and communications.
