# Text Book 2: Chapter 10 - Data Security and Privacy

=============================================

## Course Overview

---

- Module: 10 hours
- Topic: Data Security and Privacy

## Key Points

---

- **Definition of Data Security and Privacy**
  - Data Security: Protecting data from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction.
  - Data Privacy: Protecting personal information from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, or misuse.
- **Types of Threats**
  - Insider Threats: Authorized personnel who intentionally or unintentionally compromise data.
  - External Threats: Unauthorized individuals or entities that compromise data.
- **Security Measures**
  - Access Control: Controlling who can access data.
  - Authentication: Verifying user identity.
  - Authorization: Controlling user actions.
  - Encryption: Protecting data from unauthorized access.
- **Data Privacy Laws and Regulations**
  - General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
  - Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA)
  - Data Protection Act 2018
- **Importance of Data Security and Privacy**
  - Protecting sensitive information
  - Maintaining trust and reputation
  - Complying with laws and regulations

## Important Formulas and Theorems

---

- **Shannon's Source-Channel-Coding Theorem**: Establishes the trade-off between data rate and error probability.
- **Data Encryption Algorithm**: Used to protect data from unauthorized access.

## Key Terms

---

- **Data Breach**: Unauthorized access to sensitive information.
- **Data Loss**: Unauthorized destruction or modification of data.
- **Data Corruption**: Unauthorized modification of data.
