# Revision Notes: Data Security and Privacy (17.2 Chapter 20.1)

=============================================

## Overview

---

- Data Security: Protecting data from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, disruption, modification, or destruction.
- Data Privacy: Protecting personal information from unauthorized access, use, or disclosure.

## Key Concepts

---

- **Data Privacy Laws**:
  - General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
  - Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA)
  - California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)
- **Data Breach**: Unauthorized access to sensitive data.
- **Data Loss**: Irreversible loss of data.

## Definitions

---

- **Confidentiality**: Protection of sensitive information from unauthorized disclosure.
- **Integrity**: Protection of data from unauthorized modification or destruction.
- **Availability**: Protection of data from unauthorized access or loss.

## Theorems and Formulas

---

- **Data Privacy Theorem**: Measures the level of risk associated with a data breach.
  - Risk = Probability x Impact
- **Data Security Theorem**: Measures the level of risk associated with a data loss.
  - Risk = Probability x Impact

## Important Terms

---

- **Data Protection Officer (DPO)**: Responsible for ensuring data security and privacy compliance.
- **Data Encryption**: Converting data into a secure format to protect it from unauthorized access.
- **Secure Sockets Layer (SSL)**: A cryptographic protocol used to secure online transactions.

## Best Practices

---

- Implement robust data encryption and access controls.
- Regularly back up and duplicate critical data.
- Conduct regular security audits and risk assessments.
- Implement incident response plans in case of data breaches or losses.
