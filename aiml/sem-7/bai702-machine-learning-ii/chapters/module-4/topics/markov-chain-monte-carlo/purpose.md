### Learning Purpose: Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC)

**1. Why is this topic important?**
MCMC is a fundamental computational technique for approximating complex, high-dimensional probability distributions. It is crucial because many real-world problems in machine learning and statistics involve intractable integrals or posterior distributions that cannot be solved analytically. MCMC provides a powerful and versatile tool to sample from these distributions, enabling probabilistic inference.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core principles of MCMC, focusing on key algorithms like the Metropolis-Hastings algorithm and Gibbs Sampling. They will understand how these methods use Markov chains to generate samples from a target distribution and learn to assess the convergence and performance of these samplers.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly on foundational knowledge of Bayesian statistics (e.g., calculating posterior distributions) and probability (Markov chains, random variables). It is a practical implementation tool for models introduced earlier, such as Bayesian Linear Regression, Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA), and Gaussian Processes.

**4. Real-world applications**
MCMC is widely applied for Bayesian inference in fields like epidemiology (disease modeling), finance (risk assessment), astrophysics (cosmological parameter estimation), and machine learning (training complex probabilistic models and deep generative networks).
