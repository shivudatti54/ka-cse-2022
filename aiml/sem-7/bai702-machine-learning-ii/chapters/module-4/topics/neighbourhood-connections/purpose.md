### Learning Purpose: Neighbourhood Connections

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding neighbourhood-based methods is fundamental as they form a core class of intuitive, non-parametric machine learning algorithms. Unlike complex models that build internal parameters, these methods make predictions based directly on the proximity of data points, making them highly effective for both classification and regression tasks where the assumption of underlying data patterns is unclear.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core mechanics, advantages, and limitations of key neighbourhood-based algorithms, specifically k-Nearest Neighbours (k-NN) and its variants. This includes selecting appropriate distance metrics, determining the optimal value for *k*, and understanding the impact of the curse of dimensionality. They will also implement these algorithms to solve practical problems.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic connects directly to **Module 3 (Similarity and Distance Metrics)** by applying those metrics to find nearest neighbours. It provides a strong contrast to **parametric models** (like linear regression from ML I) and serves as a foundational concept for more advanced techniques, such as **graph-based algorithms, clustering (e.g., DBSCAN)**, and **manifold learning**, which also rely on local data relationships.

**4. Real-world applications**
These algorithms are widely used in recommendation systems (e.g., "users who liked this also liked..."), anomaly detection in network security, medical diagnosis (identifying similar patient cases), and semantic search for retrieving similar documents or images.
