### Learning Purpose: Examples of Using SOM & MCMC

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial as it moves beyond theoretical models to their practical application. Understanding how Self-Organizing Maps (SOM) and Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) are implemented is essential for solving complex, high-dimensional real-world problems, from visualizing big data to performing sophisticated Bayesian inference.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to implement and interpret the results of both algorithms. For SOMs, this includes creating visualizations for cluster analysis and dimensionality reduction. For MCMC, the focus is on sampling from complex probability distributions to approximate posterior integrals for Bayesian modeling and parameter estimation.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module builds directly on foundational knowledge. SOMs connect to unsupervised learning, clustering (from ML I), and dimensionality reduction (like PCA). MCMC is the practical extension of Bayesian statistics, probability theory, and sampling methods introduced earlier, enabling the analysis of models where analytical solutions are impossible.

**4. Real-world applications**
*   **SOM:** Customer segmentation in marketing, anomaly detection in network security, and genomic data visualization in bioinformatics.
*   **MCMC:** Informing political and financial risk analysis, powering Bayesian neural networks, and estimating parameters for complex epidemiological models.
