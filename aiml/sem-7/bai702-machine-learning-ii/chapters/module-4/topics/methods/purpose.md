# Learning Objectives
After studying this topic, you should be able to:
1.  Derive the Newton search direction from the second-order Taylor series expansion.
2.  Execute one iteration of Newton's method on a given simple function.
3.  Explain the convergence properties (quadratic) and practical limitations of Newton's Method.
4.  Articulate the core idea behind Quasi-Newton methods and the role of the Secant Condition.
5.  Differentiate between the BFGS and DFP update formulas and state which is more commonly used.
6.  Compare and contrast Gradient Descent, Newton's Method, and BFGS in terms of computational cost, convergence rate, and storage requirements.
