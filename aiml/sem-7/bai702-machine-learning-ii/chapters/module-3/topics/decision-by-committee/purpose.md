### Learning Purpose: Decision by Committee

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because it introduces ensemble methods, a powerful class of algorithms that significantly improve the accuracy and robustness of machine learning models. By leveraging the "wisdom of the crowd," these techniques mitigate the limitations of individual, potentially overfitted, weak learners, leading to more reliable and generalizable predictions.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the fundamental principle behind ensemble learning: combining multiple models to produce a single, superior committee decision. They will understand specific algorithms like Bagging (Bootstrap Aggregating) and its flagship implementation, the Random Forest. The module will cover how these methods reduce variance and prevent overfitting compared to using a single model.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This concept builds directly on the foundational models learned in earlier modules, such as Decision Trees (which act as the "committee members"). It contrasts with Boosting (another ensemble technique) and provides a practical solution to the overfitting problem often encountered with complex models. It is a key stepping stone to understanding more advanced ensemble methods.

**4. Real-world applications**
Ensemble methods are widely applied in industries requiring high-stakes predictions. Key applications include:
*   **Finance:** For credit scoring and algorithmic trading to assess risk.
*   **Healthcare:** In medical diagnosis (e.g., identifying diseases from scans) and predicting patient outcomes.
*   **E-commerce:** Powering recommendation systems to suggest products to users.
*   **Computer Vision:** For object detection and image classification tasks.
