# Learning Objectives
After studying this topic, you should be able to:
1. Define and explain the concept of parse trees and their role in representing the syntactic structure of a sentence.
2. Identify and explain the different components of a parse tree, including the start symbol, terminal symbols, non-terminal symbols, and production rules.
3. Draw and interpret parse trees for different grammars and sentences.
4. Understand and explain the concept of ambiguity in grammars and languages, and identify examples of ambiguous grammars.
5. Compare and contrast parse trees with other data structures, such as abstract syntax trees (ASTs) and stacks.
6. Apply the concepts of parse trees to real-world problems and scenarios, such as parsing and interpreting programming languages.
