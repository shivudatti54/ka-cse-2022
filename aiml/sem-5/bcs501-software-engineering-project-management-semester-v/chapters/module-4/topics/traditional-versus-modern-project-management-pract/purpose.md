### Learning Purpose: Traditional vs. Modern Project Management

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the evolution from traditional (e.g., Waterfall) to modern (e.g., Agile, Scrum) practices is crucial. It provides the foundational knowledge to select the right project management methodology based on a project's specific context, constraints, and goals. This decision directly impacts a project's likelihood of success, efficiency, and team dynamics.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to distinguish the core principles, processes, and artifacts of traditional and modern methodologies. They will analyze the strengths and weaknesses of each approach, focusing on areas like requirements flexibility, client collaboration, and risk management. The goal is to equip them to make informed, justified choices between methodologies.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic connects directly to requirements engineering (static vs. evolving), software development life cycles (predictive vs. iterative), and quality assurance (phase-gated vs. continuous). It acts as a bridge between theoretical project management frameworks and their practical application in software development.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied when choosing a methodology for a new project, transitioning a team from Waterfall to Agile, or managing hybrid projects. It is essential for roles like Project Manager, Scrum Master, and Product Owner, enabling them to lead teams effectively in dynamic tech environments.
