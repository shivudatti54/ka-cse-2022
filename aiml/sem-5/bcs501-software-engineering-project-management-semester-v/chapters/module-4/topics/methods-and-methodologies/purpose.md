### Learning Purpose: Methods and Methodologies

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Selecting the right development methodology is a foundational project management decision that dictates a project's workflow, communication, and ability to adapt to change. Understanding the spectrum of methods, from predictive (Waterfall) to adaptive (Agile/Scrum), is crucial for delivering software successfully, on time, and within budget.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to distinguish between various methodologies, their core principles, and phases. They will understand the specific rituals, roles, and artifacts of Agile frameworks like Scrum and Kanban. The goal is to equip them with the knowledge to evaluate project constraints and choose the most appropriate method.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the practical bridge between core software engineering concepts (requirements, design, testing) and project management (scheduling, risk management, team leadership). The chosen methodology directly impacts how all other lifecycle activities are planned and executed.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied daily by software teams globally. Students will be prepared to immediately contribute to a Scrum team as a Developer, Product Owner, or Scrum Master, or to participate in a structured Waterfall project, understanding their role within the larger process.
