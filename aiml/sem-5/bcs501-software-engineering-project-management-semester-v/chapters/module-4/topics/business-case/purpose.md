### **Learning Purpose: Business Case**

*   **1. Why is this topic important?**
    A Business Case is the fundamental justification for any software project. It is the critical document that answers the question: "Why are we investing resources in this project?" It ensures that projects are aligned with strategic goals, provide tangible value, and are worth the investment before any significant time or money is spent.

*   **2. What will students learn?**
    Students will learn to systematically create and evaluate a Business Case. This includes identifying business needs, analyzing potential solutions, conducting cost-benefit analysis (CBA), assessing risks, and defining success criteria. They will learn to structure this analysis into a formal, persuasive document for stakeholder approval.

*   **3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
    This topic is the crucial bridge between **initial project ideation** (from Module 1) and formal **project planning & initiation** (Module 5). It directly utilizes skills from **requirement analysis** (Module 2) to define scope and draws on **estimation techniques** (Module 3) to forecast costs. It also sets the foundation for later **project monitoring**, where the actual benefits are tracked against the Business Case predictions.

*   **4. Real-world applications**
    The ability to develop a robust Business Case is essential for any role proposing a new system, product, or feature. It is used by business analysts, project managers, product owners, and entrepreneurs to secure funding and executive buy-in, ensuring that only the most valuable and viable projects are pursued.
