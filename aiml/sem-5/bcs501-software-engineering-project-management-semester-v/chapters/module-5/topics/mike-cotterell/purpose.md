### **Learning Purpose: Mike Cotterell & Risk Storming**

*   **1. Why is this topic important?**
    This topic is crucial because software project failure is most often linked to poor requirements gathering and unmanaged risks, not technical shortcomings. Mike Cotterell's work, particularly the "Risk Storming" technique, provides a practical, collaborative framework for proactively identifying and mitigating project threats early. Understanding this approach is vital for shifting from reactive problem-solving to proactive project assurance, which is a core responsibility of a software engineer.

*   **2. What will students learn?**
    Students will learn the principles and concrete steps of facilitating a Risk Storming workshop. This includes techniques for engaging diverse stakeholders (developers, clients, managers) to visually map out and prioritize risks related to requirements, technology, and people. They will learn to convert identified risks into actionable mitigation tasks, turning a theoretical exercise into a practical project management tool.

*   **3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
    This topic directly integrates with and operationalizes prior concepts. It is the practical application of **risk management** theory. It relies on **agile methodologies** (e.g., collaboration, iterative planning) and enhances **requirements engineering** by exposing ambiguities and assumptions. It also connects to **software quality assurance** and **project planning** by safeguarding the project's scope, timeline, and budget.

*   **4. Real-world applications**
    Students will be equipped to run a Risk Storming session in any agile project kick-off (e.g., Sprint Zero). This skill is directly applicable in roles like **Software Project Manager, Scrum Master, Product Owner, or Business Analyst** to de-risk product launches, major feature developments, and client engagements, significantly increasing the likelihood of project success.
