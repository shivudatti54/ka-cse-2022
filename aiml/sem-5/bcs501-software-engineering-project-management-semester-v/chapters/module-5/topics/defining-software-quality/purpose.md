### **Learning Purpose: Defining Software Quality**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Defining software quality is a foundational pillar of Software Engineering. Without a clear, shared definition, teams cannot build the right product, measure progress, or deliver value. It moves quality from a subjective concept ("bug-free") to an objective, measurable set of criteria that aligns development with business goals and user needs, ultimately saving time and cost while ensuring customer satisfaction.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define software quality using established models like McCall's, Boehm's, and ISO 25010 (SQuaRE). They will distinguish between functional correctness and non-functional requirements (e.g., reliability, usability, maintainability). The module also covers how to create a "Quality Attributes" list tailored to a specific project's context.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is intrinsically linked to requirements engineering (defining what to build), testing (verifying against the definition), and project management (prioritizing quality efforts within scope, time, and budget). It provides the metrics needed for objective assessment in software metrics and acts as the ultimate goal for the entire development lifecycle.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied when creating a "Definition of Done," establishing test plans, selecting a technology stack based on maintainability, negotiating project scope with stakeholders, and conducting formal quality audits or reviews to ensure a product meets its intended market and compliance standards.
