### Learning Purpose: Importance of Software Quality

**1. Why is this important?**
Understanding software quality is foundational, as it directly impacts user satisfaction, safety, security, and the total cost of ownership. Neglecting quality leads to system failures, financial losses, and damage to a company's reputation. This topic establishes why quality is a core business concern, not just a technical one.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define software quality through its key attributes (reliability, usability, efficiency, etc.) and understand the tangible consequences of poor quality. They will be introduced to the cost of quality (CoQ) model, including prevention, appraisal, and failure costs, to make a business case for investing in quality assurance processes early in the lifecycle.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This is the "why" behind many other concepts. It connects directly to requirements engineering (clear, testable requirements), testing techniques (the primary appraisal activity), project management (scheduling and budgeting for QA), and maintenance (reducing future failure costs). It justifies the methodologies and standards covered throughout the curriculum.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied when arguing for adequate QA resources in project planning, selecting appropriate quality standards (e.g., ISO 25010) for a domain (e.g., medical, aerospace), and making informed trade-off decisions between quality, time, and cost constraints in any development project.
