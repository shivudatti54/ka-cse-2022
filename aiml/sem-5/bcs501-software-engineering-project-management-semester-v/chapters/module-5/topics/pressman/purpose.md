### Learning Purpose: Pressman's Software Engineering Framework

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding Pressman's framework is crucial as it provides a comprehensive, industry-standard model for the software development lifecycle (SDLC). It introduces systematic, disciplined, and quantifiable approaches to software development, which are essential for delivering high-quality, maintainable, and reliable software on time and within budget.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core principles, phases, and best practices outlined by Roger Pressman, including requirement engineering, design concepts, construction, testing strategies, and deployment. They will understand how to apply process models (like waterfall, iterative, and agile) and manage project elements such as risk, quality, and schedule within this established framework.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic synthesizes prior knowledge of programming and systems analysis by placing them within a structured engineering context. It directly connects to project management modules by providing the methodological backbone for planning, executing, and controlling software projects, ensuring technical work aligns with business objectives.

**4. Real-world applications**
This framework is applied globally to manage complex software projects across industries like finance, healthcare, and aerospace. Students will be equipped to participate in or lead development teams, implement robust testing and quality assurance plans, and choose the appropriate process model for any given project scenario.
