**1. Why is this topic important?**
Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) is a critical topic as it moves assessment from a high-stakes, end-of-semester event to an integrated, ongoing process. This approach is vital in software engineering, which is inherently iterative. CIE provides continuous feedback, allowing educators to identify learning gaps early and students to track their progress, fostering a growth mindset. It mirrors the Agile and DevOps principles of constant feedback and incremental improvement that students will encounter professionally.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the purpose, mechanisms, and benefits of CIE systems. This includes understanding various assessment tools like quizzes, assignments, mini-projects, and presentations. They will learn how to effectively participate in and benefit from this process by managing their workload consistently throughout the semester, rather than cramming for final exams.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
CIE directly connects to core module concepts like **Agile methodology** (sprints and iterative reviews) and **project management** (tracking progress via milestones and deliverables). It provides the practical framework for applying theoretical knowledge from other software engineering topics, ensuring that learning is continuously validated and reinforced.

**4. Real-world applications**
In the professional world, CIE mirrors the continuous integration/continuous deployment (CI/CD) pipeline, where code is constantly tested and integrated. It also reflects the performance review cycles and key performance indicators (KPIs) used in industry to gauge project health and individual contributor performance, making students better prepared for their future careers.
