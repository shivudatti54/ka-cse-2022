### **Learning Purpose: Module 5 (Textbook: Bob Hughes, Tata McGraw Hill)**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This module, based on a foundational textbook, is crucial as it transitions students from theoretical programming to the disciplined practice of building software within real-world constraints. It introduces the core principles of managing scope, time, cost, quality, and risk, which are essential for delivering successful software projects and functioning effectively in a professional team environment.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the key phases of the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) and various process models like Waterfall, Iterative, and Agile. They will be introduced to essential project management activities, including feasibility study, project planning, scheduling, cost estimation, and risk management. The goal is to equip them with the terminology and fundamental concepts to analyze project needs and apply structured methodologies.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This knowledge integrates directly with prior technical skills. It provides the management framework for the requirements engineering, system design, coding, and testing techniques learned in earlier semesters. It also sets the stage for advanced topics like Agile practices, DevOps, and software quality assurance by establishing the baseline from which these modern approaches evolved.

**4. Real-world applications**
These concepts are applied whenever software is built, from a small mobile app to large enterprise systems. Understanding them allows graduates to contribute to project planning, communicate effectively with project managers, understand deadlines and budgets, and appreciate the business context behind technical tasks, making them more valuable and versatile team members.
