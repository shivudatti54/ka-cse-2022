### 1. Why is this topic important?
Scenario-based modeling is a cornerstone of user-centric software development. It is vital because it transforms abstract requirements into concrete, understandable narratives, ensuring that all stakeholders share a common vision of system behavior. This prevents costly misunderstandings and rework later in the project lifecycle.

### 2. What will students learn?
Students will learn to create and utilize key scenario-based models like **use cases**, **user stories**, and **activity diagrams**. They will gain the practical skill of translating user needs and functional requirements into clear, testable scenarios that effectively guide the design and development process.

### 3. How does it connect to other concepts?
This topic is the crucial link between initial **requirements engineering** (Module 1) and subsequent **design and architecture** (later modules). The scenarios created here become the foundation for designing system components, defining test cases, and managing project scope and user expectations.

### 4. Real-world applications
These techniques are universally applied in Agile and traditional methodologies. From specifying how a user checks out on an e-commerce site to modeling a complex banking transaction, scenario-based models are used to validate requirements with clients, plan development sprints, and ensure the final product aligns with business goals.
