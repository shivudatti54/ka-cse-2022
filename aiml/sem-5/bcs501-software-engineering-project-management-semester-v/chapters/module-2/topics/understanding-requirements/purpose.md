# Learning Purpose: Understanding Requirements

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding requirements is the foundational step in any software project. Inaccurate or incomplete requirements are a primary cause of project failure, leading to cost overruns, missed deadlines, and products that do not meet user needs. This topic is crucial for establishing a clear, shared vision between stakeholders and developers, ensuring the final software is built correctly from the outset.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the techniques and processes for eliciting, analyzing, specifying, validating, and managing software requirements. This includes distinguishing between functional and non-functional requirements, creating use cases and user stories, and developing requirement specification documents (SRS). They will gain practical skills in conducting stakeholder interviews and negotiating requirements.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This knowledge directly informs subsequent modules. A solid set of requirements is essential for effective system design (Module 3), accurate effort estimation and project planning (Project Management modules), and forms the basis for creating test cases (Software Testing). It is the critical link between client needs and all downstream engineering activities.

**4. Real-world applications**
These skills are applied daily by business analysts, product managers, and project leads across all industries. From gathering needs for a new mobile app to defining complex system constraints for financial or healthcare software, the ability to correctly understand and document requirements is a highly valued and universal competency in the tech workforce.
