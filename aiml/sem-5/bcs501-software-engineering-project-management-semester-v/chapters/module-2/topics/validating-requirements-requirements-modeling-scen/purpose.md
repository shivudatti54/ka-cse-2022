### Learning Purpose: Validating Requirements & Requirements Modeling Scenarios

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is critical because building software based on incorrect or misunderstood requirements is the primary cause of project failure, leading to massive budget overruns, delays, and unusable products. Validating requirements ensures that what is being built actually meets user needs and business objectives before costly development begins. Modeling scenarios (like use cases and user stories) translate abstract needs into clear, actionable specifications for the entire team.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn techniques to validate requirements through reviews, prototypes, and test cases. They will master the skill of creating and analyzing requirements models, specifically use case diagrams and user stories, to visualize system functionality from the user's perspective. This bridges the gap between stakeholder expectations and technical implementation.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the crucial link between initial requirements elicitation (gathering needs) and subsequent system design & development. It directly feeds into creating architectural diagrams, test plans, and project schedules. It also connects deeply to project management, as validated requirements form the stable foundation for scope, cost, and time management.

**4. Real-world applications**
These skills are applied daily by Business Analysts, Product Managers, and Software Architects. They are used to create product backlogs in Agile (Scrum), define features for Minimum Viable Products (MVPs), and secure stakeholder sign-off, ensuring everyone has a shared and accurate vision of the final product.
