**Learning Purpose: Negotiating Requirements**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Negotiating requirements is a critical skill because it directly impacts project success. Unrealistic, conflicting, or poorly defined requirements are a primary cause of project failure, budget overruns, and low customer satisfaction. This process ensures that stakeholder needs are balanced against technical feasibility, resource constraints, and business objectives, creating a realistic and agreed-upon foundation for development.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn techniques for facilitating discussions between stakeholders, developers, and management. This includes identifying conflicts, managing expectations, prioritizing features using models like MoSCoW, and achieving consensus. They will understand how to create win-win outcomes and document negotiated agreements in a clear, unambiguous manner.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the practical bridge between initial requirement elicitation (gathering needs) and subsequent system design and project planning. It directly applies concepts from stakeholder management and communication skills. The prioritized backlog produced is a key input for Agile sprints and traditional project scheduling, influencing timelines, costs, and risk management plans.

**4. Real-world applications**
These skills are applied daily by product owners, business analysts, and project managers. They are used in sprint planning meetings to decide what gets built next, in change control boards to evaluate new feature requests, and in client reviews to align vision with a feasible product roadmap, ensuring deliverables provide maximum value.
