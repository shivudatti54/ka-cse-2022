### Learning Purpose: Establishing the Groundwork

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Establishing the groundwork is the critical first phase that determines a project's overall trajectory. A weak foundation leads to scope creep, budget overruns, and project failure. This topic is essential because it teaches the disciplined process of defining what needs to be built, why it's valuable, and if it's feasible, thereby aligning stakeholder vision and setting the stage for success.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core activities for initiating a software project. This includes defining business needs through problem statements, conducting feasibility studies (technical, economic, legal), establishing clear project scope, and identifying key stakeholders. They will be introduced to creating foundational documents like a project charter or vision statement to formally authorize the project.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module is the prerequisite for all subsequent project management and engineering activities. The scope defined here directly informs requirements engineering (Module 3). The feasibility analysis connects to cost and effort estimation (Module 4), and the stakeholder list is vital for communication planning (Module 5). It is the "why" before the "how."

**4. Real-world applications**
These principles are applied in every software project kick-off. Practically, this is the stage where a project manager creates a business case to secure funding, a product manager defines the product's core objectives, and a team assesses technological risks before committing to a solution, ensuring resources are invested wisely.
