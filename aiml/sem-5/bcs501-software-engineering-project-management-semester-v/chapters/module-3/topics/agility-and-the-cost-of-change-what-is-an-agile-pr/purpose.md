### Learning Purpose: Agility and the Cost of Change

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding agility and the cost of change is critical because it challenges the traditional "big design up front" mindset of software development. It explains why responding to change is more valuable than rigidly following a plan, a principle central to modern software project management. This topic is foundational for adopting practices that reduce risk and increase a project's chance of success in volatile environments.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define an agile process and differentiate it from traditional, plan-driven methodologies like Waterfall. They will analyze how the cost of change curve differs between these approaches, understanding why agile methods embrace changing requirements even late in development. The core values and principles of the Agile Manifesto will be introduced as the philosophical bedrock of all agile frameworks.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic provides the *why* behind the *how* of specific agile practices covered later (e.g., Scrum, XP). It connects directly to project planning, risk management, and requirements engineering, showing how an iterative approach mitigates the risk of building the wrong product. It also sets the stage for understanding how team structure, customer collaboration, and continuous delivery are enabled by an agile mindset.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is directly applied when choosing the right project management methodology for a new project. It is used to justify the business value of agility to stakeholders and to structure teams for rapid feedback and adaptation. Understanding this concept is essential for any role in a modern software development team, from developers and testers to project managers and product owners.
