# Ecosystem: Structure of Ecosystem, Types

### Structure of Ecosystem

- **Producers:** Autotrophic organisms (plants, algae) that produce organic matter through photosynthesis
- **Consumers:** Herbivores (primary consumers), carnivores (secondary consumers), decomposers (tertiary consumers)
- **Energy Flow:** Energy flows from producers to consumers through a food chain or food web
- **Ecological Niche:** The role and position of an organism within its environment
- **Biosphere:** The global sum of all ecosystems

### Types of Ecosystems

#### Forest Ecosystems

- **Characteristics:** Dense vegetation, complex food web, diverse wildlife
- **Key Producers:** Trees, shrubs, and other woody plants
- **Key Consumers:** Herbivores (deer, insects), carnivores (lions, bears)

#### Desert Ecosystems

- **Characteristics:** Hot and dry climate, limited vegetation, specialized adaptations
- **Key Producers:** Succulent plants, cacti
- **Key Consumers:** Insects, reptiles, small mammals

#### Wetlands Ecosystems

- **Characteristics:** Aquatic and terrestrial ecosystems with high water table
- **Key Producers:** Aquatic plants (algae, water lilies), grasses
- **Key Consumers:** Fish, birds, insects

#### Riverine Ecosystems

- **Characteristics:** Ecosystems along rivers and streams
- **Key Producers:** Aquatic plants (algae, aquatic grasses), trees along riverbanks
- **Key Consumers:** Fish, birds, otters

#### Oceanic Ecosystems

- **Characteristics:** Ecosystems in the ocean, including coral reefs, estuaries
- **Key Producers:** Phytoplankton, coral reefs
- **Key Consumers:** Fish, whales, dolphins

### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Energy Pyramid:** A graphical representation of energy flow in an ecosystem
- **Carrying Capacity:** The maximum number of individuals of a species an ecosystem can support indefinitely
- **Biodiversity:** The variety of different species of plants, animals, and microorganisms that live in an ecosystem

### Key Theorems

- **Law of Energy Conservation:** Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only converted from one form to another
- **Law of Conservation of Mass:** Matter cannot be created or destroyed, only converted from one form to another
