# **Self-Study Component (SSC): Components of the Environment**

## **Key Points**

- **Biological Environment**
  - Living organisms and their interactions
  - Ecosystems, food chains, food webs, and nutrient cycles
  - Biodiversity, species diversity, and ecosystem services
- **Physical Environment**
  - Natural resources, energy, and water
  - Climate, weather, and natural hazards
  - Geology, landforms, and natural features
- **Human Environment**
  - Social, economic, and cultural factors
  - Population growth, urbanization, and resource consumption
  - Impact of human activities on the environment

## **Definitions and Theorems**

- **Ecological Footprint**: the amount of land and resources required to sustain human activities
- **Carrying Capacity**: the maximum number of individuals of a species that an environment can support
- **Thermoregulation**: the ability of an organism to regulate its temperature
- **Ecological Succession**: the process of change in the species composition of an ecosystem over time

## **Important Formulas**

- **Carrying Capacity Formula**: C = k \* P \* R
- **Ecological Footprint Formula**: EF = A \* I \* P
- **Thermoregulation Formula**: Q = mc\*ΔT

## **Revision Tips**

- Review the different components of the environment and their interactions
- Understand the key concepts of ecological succession, carrying capacity, and thermoregulation
- Practice calculating ecological footprint and carrying capacity using the provided formulas
