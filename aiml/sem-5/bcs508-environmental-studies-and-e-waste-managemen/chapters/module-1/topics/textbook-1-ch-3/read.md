## **Ecosystems and Sustainability**

## **3.1: Definition of Ecosystem**

An ecosystem is a complex network of living organisms (plants, animals, and microorganisms) interacting with each other and their physical environment. It includes all the living and non-living components that interact with each other, such as air, water, soil, and sunlight.

- Key components of an ecosystem:
  - Biotic factors: living organisms (plants, animals, microorganisms)
  - Abiotic factors: non-living components (air, water, soil, sunlight)

## **3.2: Types of Ecosystems**

There are several types of ecosystems, including:

- **Terrestrial ecosystems**: land-based ecosystems, such as forests, grasslands, and deserts
- **Freshwater ecosystems**: ecosystems found in rivers, lakes, and wetlands
- **Marine ecosystems**: ecosystems found in oceans and seas
- **Atmospheric ecosystems**: the layer of gases surrounding the Earth, including the ozone layer

## **3.3: Interdependence in Ecosystems**

Ecosystems are interconnected and interdependent, meaning that the health and well-being of one component can affect the others. This interdependence is essential for maintaining the balance and stability of the ecosystem.

- Examples of interdependence:
  - Plants provide oxygen for animals, while animals pollinate plants
  - Decomposers break down dead organic matter, releasing nutrients for new growth

## **3.4: Human Impact on Ecosystems**

Human activities have significantly impacted ecosystems, leading to changes in their structure, function, and biodiversity.

- Examples of human impact:
  - Deforestation and habitat destruction
  - Pollution and climate change
  - Overfishing and overhunting

## **3.5: Sustainability**

Sustainability refers to the ability of ecosystems to maintain their structure and function over time, while supporting the needs of humans and other organisms.

- Key principles of sustainability:
  - **Conservation**: protecting and preserving natural resources
  - **Sustainable use**: using resources in a way that maintains their health and productivity
  - **Restoration**: repairing damaged or degraded ecosystems

## **Key Concepts**

- Ecosystem: a complex network of living and non-living components interacting with each other
- Interdependence: the interconnectedness of components in an ecosystem
- Sustainability: the ability of ecosystems to maintain their structure and function over time
