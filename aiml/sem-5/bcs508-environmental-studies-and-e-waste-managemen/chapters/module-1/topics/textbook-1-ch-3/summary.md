# **Textbook 1: CH- 3 Summary**

### Key Points

- **Ecosystem Services**: Ecosystem services are goods and services provided by nature, including:
  - Air and water filtration
  - Climate regulation
  - Soil formation and maintenance
  - Pest control
  - Medicine and pharmaceuticals

- **Biodiversity**: Biodiversity refers to the variety of different species of plants, animals, and microorganisms that live in an ecosystem.

- **Sustainability**: Sustainability is the practice of meeting the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.

### Important Formulas

- **Carrying Capacity**: C = P x R, where C = carrying capacity, P = population size, and R = resource availability
- **Marginal Rate of Substitution**: MRS = (∂U/∂x) / (∂U/∂y), where U is utility, x is a good, and y is another good

### Important Definitions

- **Ecological Footprint**: The ecological footprint of an individual or population is the amount of land required to produce the resources consumed by that individual or population.
- **Carbon Footprint**: The carbon footprint of an individual or population is the amount of greenhouse gas emissions produced by that individual or population.

### Important Theorems

- **The Law of Diminishing Marginal Utility**: As the quantity of a good consumed increases, the marginal utility of the good decreases.
- **The Substitution Effect**: When the price of one good increases, consumers will substitute it with another good.
