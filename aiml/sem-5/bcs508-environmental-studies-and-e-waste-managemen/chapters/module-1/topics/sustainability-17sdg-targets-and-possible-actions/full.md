# Sustainability: 17 SDG Targets and Possible Actions

=====================================================

## **Introduction**

The United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) aim to create a better world for all by 2030. The 17 SDGs encompass a wide range of targets, from poverty reduction to climate change, and environmental conservation. This document will delve into the 17 SDG targets, their possible actions, and provide examples, case studies, and applications.

## **Historical Context**

The SDGs were adopted by the United Nations General Assembly in 2015, building upon the Millennium Development Goals (MDGs) established in 2000. The SDGs recognized the need for a more comprehensive and integrated approach to sustainable development, incorporating economic, social, and environmental aspects.

## **The 17 SDG Targets**

### 1. No Poverty (SDG 1)

- Target: End poverty in all its forms, everywhere.
- Possible Actions:
  - Implement poverty reduction strategies, such as cash transfers and job creation programs.
  - Increase access to education and skills training.
  - Promote social protection programs, such as unemployment insurance.

### 2. Zero Hunger (SDG 2)

- Target: End hunger, achieve food security, and improved nutrition.
- Possible Actions:
  - Implement sustainable agriculture practices, such as organic farming and agroforestry.
  - Increase access to nutritious food, particularly for vulnerable populations.
  - Support small-scale farmers and local food systems.

### 3. Good Health and Well-being (SDG 3)

- Target: Ensure healthy lives and prevent disease.
- Possible Actions:
  - Implement universal health coverage, including access to preventive care and healthcare services.
  - Promote healthy lifestyles, such as regular exercise and balanced diets.
  - Improve access to clean water, sanitation, and hygiene (WASH) facilities.

### 4. Quality Education (SDG 4)

- Target: Ensure inclusive and equitable quality education.
- Possible Actions:
  - Increase access to quality education, particularly for marginalized groups.
  - Implement teacher training programs to improve teaching quality.
  - Promote lifelong learning and skills development.

### 5. Gender Equality (SDG 5)

- Target: Achieve gender equality and empower all women and girls.
- Possible Actions:
  - Implement policies and programs to promote gender equality in education, employment, and politics.
  - Increase access to education and economic opportunities for women and girls.
  - Support women's leadership and entrepreneurship.

### 6. Clean Water and Sanitation (SDG 6)

- Target: Ensure availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all.
- Possible Actions:
  - Implement water conservation and efficiency measures.
  - Increase access to clean water, sanitation, and hygiene (WASH) facilities.
  - Promote wastewater management and reuse.

### 7. Affordable and Clean Energy (SDG 7)

- Target: Ensure access to affordable, reliable, sustainable energy for all.
- Possible Actions:
  - Invest in renewable energy sources, such as solar and wind power.
  - Promote energy efficiency and conservation measures.
  - Expand access to electricity, particularly in rural and underserved areas.

### 8. Decent Work and Economic Growth (SDG 8)

- Target: Promote sustained, inclusive, and sustainable economic growth.
- Possible Actions:
  - Implement policies and programs to promote fair labor standards and decent work.
  - Increase access to education and skills training.
  - Support small-scale enterprises and entrepreneurship.

### 9. Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure (SDG 9)

- Target: Build resilient infrastructure, promote inclusive and sustainable industrialization, and foster innovation.
- Possible Actions:
  - Invest in infrastructure development, particularly in transportation and energy.
  - Promote sustainable industrial practices and circular economy.
  - Support innovation and entrepreneurship.

### 10. Reduce Inequalities (SDG 10)

- Target: Reduce inequality within and among countries.
- Possible Actions:
  - Implement policies and programs to promote social protection and social inclusion.
  - Increase access to education and economic opportunities for marginalized groups.
  - Support women's empowerment and gender equality.

### 11. Cities and Human Settlements (SDG 11)

- Target: Make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable.
- Possible Actions:
  - Implement urban planning and development strategies that prioritize sustainability and social equity.
  - Increase access to affordable housing and education.
  - Promote waste management and pollution reduction.

### 12. Responsible Consumption and Production (SDG 12)

- Target: Ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns.
- Possible Actions:
  - Implement policies and programs to reduce waste and pollution.
  - Promote sustainable agriculture practices and reduce food waste.
  - Support circular economy and product design for recyclability.

### 13. Climate Action (SDG 13)

- Target: Take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts.
- Possible Actions:
  - Invest in renewable energy sources and reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
  - Promote energy efficiency and conservation measures.
  - Support climate-resilient infrastructure development.

### 14. Life Below Water (SDG 14)

- Target: Conserve and sustainably use the world's oceans, seas, and marine resources.
- Possible Actions:
  - Implement marine protected areas and reduce overfishing.
  - Promote sustainable aquaculture and fishing practices.
  - Support ocean cleanup and waste management.

### 15. Life on Land (SDG 15)

- Target: Protect, restore, and promote sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems.
- Possible Actions:
  - Implement reforestation and afforestation efforts.
  - Promote sustainable agriculture practices and reduce deforestation.
  - Support conservation and restoration of natural habitats.

### 16. Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions (SDG 16)

- Target: Promote peaceful and inclusive societies.
- Possible Actions:
  - Implement policies and programs to promote social cohesion and tolerance.
  - Increase access to education and economic opportunities for marginalized groups.
  - Support human rights and the rule of law.

### 17. Partnerships for the Goals (SDG 17)

- Target: Strengthen the means of implementation and revitalize the global partnership for sustainable development.
- Possible Actions:
  - Implement partnerships and collaborations between governments, businesses, and civil society.
  - Increase access to funding and resources for sustainable development.
  - Support knowledge-sharing and capacity-building.

## **E-Waste Management**

Electronic waste (e-waste) management is critical for achieving the SDG targets, particularly SDG 12 and SDG 13. E-waste management can be achieved through:

- Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR): companies are responsible for the waste generated by their products.
- Circular Economy: e-waste is designed to be recycled and reused.
- Recycling and Recovery: e-waste is recycled and reused to recover valuable materials.

## **Case Studies and Applications**

- **City of Copenhagen**: Copenhagen has implemented a waste-to-energy program, where waste is converted into heat and electricity.
- **Singapore**: Singapore has implemented a e-waste management program, where e-waste is collected and recycled.
- **Kenya**: Kenya has implemented a recycling program for plastic bottles, where waste is collected and recycled to produce new plastic products.
