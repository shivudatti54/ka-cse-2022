# **Sustainability: 17SDG Targets and Possible Actions**

## **Introduction**

The United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) aim to create a better future for all. The 17 SDGs include targets related to sustainability, which is essential for environmental studies and e-waste management.

## **17 SDG Targets Related to Sustainability**

- **SDG 4: Quality Education**
  - Target 4.7: Ensure that all learners acquire the knowledge and skills needed to promote sustainable development
  - Possible action: Integrate sustainable development into school curricula
- **SDG 6: Clean Water and Sanitation**
  - Target 6.2: Ensure the universal and equitable access to safe and affordable drinking water for all
  - Possible action: Implement water conservation measures in communities
- **SDG 7: Affordable and Clean Energy**
  - Target 7.3: Support the transition to 100% renewable energy
  - Possible action: Promote renewable energy sources and energy efficiency measures
- **SDG 8: Decent Work and Economic Growth**
  - Target 8.5: Achieve fair and equitable distribution of economic benefits
  - Possible action: Implement policies to reduce income inequality
- **SDG 9: Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure**
  - Target 9.4: Upgrade infrastructure to support sustainable transport systems
  - Possible action: Invest in sustainable transportation infrastructure
- **SDG 10: Reduced Inequalities**
  - Target 10.2: End all forms of discrimination and inequality
  - Possible action: Implement policies to reduce discrimination and promote equality
- **SDG 12: Responsible Consumption and Production**
  - Target 12.1: Implement sustainable production and consumption patterns
  - Possible action: Encourage sustainable consumption and production practices among consumers
- **SDG 13: Climate Action**
  - Target 13.3: Take urgent action to reduce GHG emissions
  - Possible action: Implement policies to reduce GHG emissions and transition to renewable energy
- **SDG 14: Life Below Water**
  - Target 14.2: Protect and conserve marine ecosystems
  - Possible action: Implement policies to protect marine ecosystems and reduce pollution
- **SDG 15: Life on Land**
  - Target 15.5: Protect, restore, and promote sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems
  - Possible action: Implement policies to protect and restore terrestrial ecosystems
- **SDG 16: Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions**
  - Target 16.4: Ensure access to justice for all
  - Possible action: Implement policies to ensure access to justice for all
- **SDG 17: Partnerships for the Goals**
  - Target 17.17: Revitalize the global partnership for sustainable development
  - Possible action: Foster partnerships among governments, businesses, and civil society to achieve sustainable development

## **Theorem: Circular Economy**

A circular economy is a regenerative system that aims to keep resources in use for as long as possible, extract the maximum value from them, and recover and regenerate materials.

## **Formula: Life Cycle Assessment (LCA)**

LCA is a method used to assess the environmental impacts of a product or service throughout its entire life cycle.

Key Points:
• Sustainability is essential for environmental studies and e-waste management.
• The 17 SDG targets related to sustainability include targets for education, clean water and sanitation, energy, industry, innovation, infrastructure, reduced inequalities, responsible consumption and production, climate action, life below water, life on land, peace, justice, and strong institutions, and partnerships for the goals.
• Possible actions for achieving sustainability include integrating sustainable development into school curricula, implementing water conservation measures, promoting renewable energy sources, implementing policies to reduce income inequality, and encouraging sustainable consumption and production practices.
