# Environmental Studies and E-Waste Management

### E-resource: 1

#### Key Points

- **Definition of E-resource:** Electronic resource, also known as e-resource or e-material, refers to digital information, such as e-books, articles, and multimedia content, used for learning and research purposes.
- **Importance of E-resources:**
  - Convenience and accessibility
  - Cost-effectiveness and reduced physical storage needs
  - Ability to update and revise content easily
- **Characteristics of E-resources:**
  - Intangible and digital in nature
  - Can be accessed through various devices and platforms
  - May include multimedia content, such as videos and animations
- **Benefits of E-resources:**
  - Reduced paper waste and environmental impact
  - Improved accessibility for students with disabilities
  - Enhanced learning experience through interactive content

#### Formulas and Theorems

- None specific to E-resources, but applicable to environmental studies and sustainability:
  - The 3Rs (Reduce, Reuse, Recycle) principle
  - The circular economy concept
  - The environmental impact assessment (EIA) process

#### Important Terms

- **Digital literacy:** The ability to effectively use digital technologies and resources.
- **E-waste management:** The process of safely disposing of electronic waste and managing its environmental impact.
- **Sustainability:** The practice of meeting current needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.
