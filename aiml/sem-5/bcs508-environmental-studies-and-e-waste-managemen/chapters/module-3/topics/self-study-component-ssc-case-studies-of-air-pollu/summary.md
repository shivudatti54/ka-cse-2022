# **Self-Study Component (SSC) Revision Notes: Air Pollution Episodes**

### Key Definitions

- **Air Pollution**: The introduction of pollutants into the atmosphere in quantities and over periods sufficient to cause adverse effects on environmental, human health, or welfare.
- **Pollutant**: Any substance or agent that is introduced into the atmosphere in concentrations or amounts sufficient to cause adverse effects.

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Stoichiometric equation**: A mathematical representation of the chemical reaction between a pollutant and a receptor (e.g., ozone + nitrogen oxides → ground-level ozone)

### Key Points: Case Studies of Air Pollution Episodes

- **Beijing Air Pollution Episode (2013)**:
  - Caused by a combination of factors, including agricultural burning, industrial activities, and vehicular emissions
  - Resulted in extreme air quality, with PM2.5 levels exceeding 300 μg/m³
  - Led to widespread health impacts, including respiratory and cardiovascular problems
- **Indian Air Pollution Episode (2016)**:
  - Caused by the burning of fossil fuels, agricultural waste, and industrial activities
  - Resulted in severe air quality, with PM2.5 levels exceeding 400 μg/m³
  - Led to significant health impacts, including increased mortality rates
- **California Wildfire-Induced Air Pollution (2018)**:
  - Caused by the combustion of vegetation and other organic materials during wildfires
  - Resulted in significant air quality impacts, with PM2.5 levels exceeding 500 μg/m³
  - Led to widespread health impacts, including respiratory problems

### Key Theorems

- **Brennan's Theorem**: States that the severity of air pollution impacts is directly related to the concentration of pollutants and the duration of exposure.
- **Stevens' Theorem**: States that the effects of air pollution on human health are dependent on individual susceptibility, pollutant type, and exposure duration.

### Important Concepts

- **Source apportionment**: The process of attributing air pollution to specific sources, such as industrial activities or vehicular emissions
- **Emissions trading**: A market-based approach to reducing greenhouse gas emissions
- **Particulate matter (PM)**: A common air pollutant, composed of small particles and droplets in the atmosphere
