# Environmental Pollution: Water Pollution, Noise Pollution, Air Pollution

**Water Pollution**

- **Sources:**
  - Industrial effluents
  - Agricultural runoff
  - Domestic sewage
  - Plastic waste
- **Impacts:**
  - Groundwater contamination
  - Eutrophication
  - Loss of biodiversity
  - Waterborne diseases
- **Preventive Measures:**
  - Implement wastewater treatment facilities
  - Use eco-friendly cleaning products
  - Promote water conservation
  - Implement plastic waste management
- **Public Health Aspects:**
  - Cancer
  - Neurological disorders
  - Cardiovascular diseases

**Noise Pollution**

- **Sources:**
  - Industrial machinery
  - Transportation
  - Construction
  - Recreational activities
- **Impacts:**
  - Hearing loss
  - Sleep disturbance
  - Stress and anxiety
  - Cardiovascular diseases
- **Preventive Measures:**
  - Implement noise regulations
  - Use noise barriers
  - Promote soundproofing
  - Encourage quiet activities
- **Public Health Aspects:**
  - Hearing impairment
  - Cardiovascular disease

**Air Pollution**

- **Sources:**
  - Fossil fuel combustion
  - Industrial processes
  - Vehicle emissions
  - Agricultural burning
- **Impacts:**
  - Respiratory problems
  - Cardiovascular diseases
  - Cancer
  - Neurological disorders
- **Preventive Measures:**
  - Implement clean energy sources
  - Promote energy efficiency
  - Regulate emissions
  - Encourage sustainable agriculture
- **Public Health Aspects:**
  - Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)
  - Lung cancer
  - Asthma

**Formulas and Definitions**

- **Concentration**: The amount of a substance per unit volume of air or water.
- **Pollution Index**: A measure of the level of pollution in a given area.
- **Eutrophication**: The excessive growth of aquatic plants due to high levels of nutrients.
- **Particulate Matter (PM)**: Tiny particles suspended in the air.

**Theorems**

- **The Six-Step Pollution Cycle**: A model describing the source, transport, transformation, deposition, storage, and fate of pollutants.
- **The Air Pollution Control Act**: A regulatory framework for controlling air pollution in the United States.
