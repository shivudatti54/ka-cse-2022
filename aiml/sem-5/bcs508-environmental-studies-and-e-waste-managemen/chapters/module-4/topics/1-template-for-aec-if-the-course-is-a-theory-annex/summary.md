# **Environmental Studies and E-Waste Management Revision Notes**

**Module: Waste Management - Case Studies on Waste Management Options (Textbook 1: CH-6)**

## **Key Points:**

- **Definition of E-Waste:** Electronic waste (e-waste) refers to waste generated from the disposal of electronic devices, including computers, smartphones, televisions, and other electronic equipment.
- **Types of E-Waste:**
  - Hazardous waste: contains toxic substances like lead, mercury, and cadmium.
  - Non-hazardous waste: does not contain toxic substances.
- **Waste Management Options:**
  - **Recycling:** recovering valuable materials from e-waste.
  - **Recovery:** extracting valuable materials from e-waste, but not necessarily recovering them in their original form.
  - **Energy Recovery:** generating energy from e-waste through incineration or gasification.
  - **Landfilling:** disposing of e-waste in landfills.
- **Importance of E-Waste Management:**
  - Reduces greenhouse gas emissions.
  - Conserves natural resources.
  - Prevents pollution.
- **Challenges in E-Waste Management:**
  - Lack of infrastructure.
  - Limited awareness.
  - High costs.

## **Key Formulas and Definitions:**

- **Waste Generation Rate:** The rate at which waste is generated per unit of GDP or population.
- **Waste Management Hierarchy:** A pyramid representing the preferred order of waste management practices, from prevention to disposal.
- **Life Cycle Assessment (LCA):** A method of evaluating the environmental impacts of a product throughout its entire life cycle.

## **Important Theorems and Concepts:**

- **The 4Rs:** Reduce, Reuse, Recycle, Recover.
- **The Waste Pyramid:** A visual representation of the waste management hierarchy.
