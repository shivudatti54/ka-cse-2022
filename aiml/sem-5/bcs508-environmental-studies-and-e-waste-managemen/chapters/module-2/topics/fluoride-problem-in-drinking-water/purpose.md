**Why this topic matters:**
The fluoride problem in drinking water is crucial because excessive fluoride consumption can lead to health issues such as dental fluorosis, skeletal fluorosis, and even thyroid problems. It's essential to understand the risks associated with fluoride in drinking water to develop effective strategies for mitigating its negative effects. This knowledge can help communities make informed decisions about water treatment and quality.

**Real-world applications:**
The fluoride problem in drinking water has real-world implications for public health, environmental sustainability, and economic development. For instance, learning about fluoride can help communities design water treatment systems that incorporate fluoride removal or optimization. Additionally, understanding the health impacts of fluoride can inform policy decisions related to water quality and resource management.
