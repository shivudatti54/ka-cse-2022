# Natural Resources: Water Resources – Availability & Quality Aspects

===========================================================

## Introduction

---

Water is the most essential natural resource for human existence. It is necessary for drinking, agriculture, industry, and energy production. However, the availability and quality of water have become a major concern due to increasing demand, climate change, and pollution. This chapter will delve into the aspects of water resources, including availability and quality, and discuss historical context, modern developments, and applications.

## Historical Context

---

Water has been a vital resource since ancient times. In ancient civilizations, water was considered a sacred resource, and its management was often controlled by the government or religious authorities. The availability of water for agriculture and drinking was often determined by the location of a town or city.

In the 19th century, the Industrial Revolution led to a significant increase in water demand for industrial processes, such as textile production and mining. This led to the development of water treatment technologies and the construction of large-scale water supply systems.

## Modern Developments

---

In recent years, the world has faced numerous challenges related to water scarcity and quality. Climate change has led to more frequent and severe droughts and floods, which have strained water resources. The increasing population and economic growth have led to increased water demand, particularly in developed countries.

The World Health Organization (WHO) estimates that 844 million people lack access to clean drinking water, and 2.1 billion people live without improved sanitation facilities. This has led to the development of new technologies and strategies to improve water access and quality.

### Water Scarcity

Water scarcity is a growing concern in many parts of the world. It is estimated that 40% of the world's population lives in water-scarce regions. Water scarcity can be caused by a variety of factors, including drought, over-extraction of groundwater, and climate change.

### Water Quality

Water quality is a major concern, particularly in developing countries. Contaminants such as bacteria, viruses, and chemicals can pose serious health risks to humans. The WHO estimates that 80% of waterborne diseases are caused by contaminated water.

## Availability

---

Water availability refers to the quantity of water available for human consumption, agriculture, industry, and energy production. The availability of water is influenced by various factors, including precipitation, evaporation, runoff, and groundwater recharge.

### Types of Water Sources

There are four main types of water sources:

1. **Surface Water**: Rivers, lakes, and reservoirs.
2. **Groundwater**: Underground aquifers.
3. **Fog Collection**: Fog can be collected and used for irrigation.
4. **Atmospheric Water**: Water vapor can be collected and condensed for use.

### Water Balance

The water balance is the equilibrium between the inputs and outputs of water in a given region. The components of the water balance include:

1. **Precipitation**: Water that falls to the ground.
2. **Evaporation**: Water that evaporates from the ground.
3. **Runoff**: Water that flows over the ground.
4. **Infiltration**: Water that seeps into the ground.
5. **Percolation**: Water that moves through the soil and becomes groundwater.

## Quality

---

Water quality refers to the chemical, physical, and biological properties of water. The quality of water is influenced by various factors, including climate, geology, land use, and human activities.

### Factors Affecting Water Quality

1. **Climate**: Temperature, precipitation, and evaporation rates can affect water quality.
2. **Geology**: The type of rocks and soil can affect the chemical composition of water.
3. **Land Use**: Agricultural and urban activities can lead to pollution and changes in water chemistry.
4. **Human Activities**: Industrial, domestic, and agricultural activities can release pollutants into water bodies.

### Water Quality Parameters

1. **pH**: The measure of acidity or alkalinity.
2. **Temperature**: The measure of the water's temperature.
3. **Turbidity**: The measure of water clarity.
4. **Bacteria**: The presence of microorganisms that can cause disease.
5. **Chemicals**: The presence of pollutants such as pesticides, fertilizers, and heavy metals.

## Applications

---

Water resources have numerous applications, including:

1. **Drinking Water**: Water is the primary source of drinking water for humans.
2. **Agriculture**: Water is used for irrigation and crop growth.
3. **Industry**: Water is used for manufacturing, cooling, and other industrial processes.
4. **Energy Production**: Water is used in nuclear power plants and other energy production facilities.

---

### Case Study 1: Cape Town Water Crisis

In 2018, Cape Town, South Africa, faced a severe water crisis due to drought. The city implemented strict water restrictions, including a ban on using water for non-essential purposes.

### Case Study 2: Water Scarcity in California
