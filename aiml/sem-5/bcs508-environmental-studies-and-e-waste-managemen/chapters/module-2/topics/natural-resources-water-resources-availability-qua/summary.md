# **Natural Resources: Water Resources - Availability & Quality Aspects**

## **Definition and Importance of Water Resources**

- Water is a vital natural resource essential for human consumption, agriculture, industry, and ecosystem balance.
- Availability and quality of water resources are crucial for sustainable development and environmental conservation.

## **Availability of Water Resources**

- Total freshwater available on Earth: ≈ 1.386 billion km³ (1.386 x 10^12 m³)
- Freshwater availability: ≈ 2.9% of total freshwater (≈ 40,000 km³)
- Distribution of fresh water:
  - 68.7% on land
  - 2.5% in groundwater
  - 29.8% in the atmosphere (as water vapor)

## **Quality Aspects of Water Resources**

- **Physical properties:**
  - Temperature
  - pH
  - Turbidity
- **Chemical properties:**
  - Hardness
  - Alkalinity
  - Nutrient levels
- **Biological properties:**
  - Bacterial contamination
  - Parasite presence
  - Aquatic life diversity

## **Key Formulas and Theorems**

- **Bottcher's Rule:** A = I / (1 - r) (approximation for groundwater recharge)
- **Hydrologic Cycle:** Water is continuously cycled between the atmosphere, land, and oceans.

## **Important Concepts**

- **Water scarcity:** Excessive demand for water resources exceeding supply
- **Water pollution:** Contamination of water by chemicals, sediments, or organisms
- **Water conservation:** Practices and technologies to reduce water usage and protect water resources.
