# **Water Borne Diseases & Water Induced Diseases**

## **Introduction**

Water is essential for human life, but contaminated water can cause various diseases and health problems. Waterborne diseases and water-induced diseases are types of illnesses that spread through contaminated water. In this study, we will explore the causes, symptoms, and prevention strategies for these diseases.

## **What are Water Borne Diseases?**

Waterborne diseases are illnesses caused by the consumption of contaminated water. These diseases can spread rapidly if proper precautions are not taken. Examples of waterborne diseases include:

### Key Concepts:

- **Pathogens**: Microorganisms that cause disease, such as bacteria, viruses, and parasites.
- **Contamination**: The presence of pathogens or other substances in water that can cause disease.
- **Waterborne transmission**: The spread of disease through contaminated water.

## **Examples of Water Borne Diseases:**

- **Cholera**: A bacterial disease caused by Vibrio cholerae, which can lead to severe diarrhea and dehydration.
- **Typhoid Fever**: A bacterial disease caused by Salmonella Typhi, which can cause fever, headache, and abdominal pain.
- **Dysentery**: A bacterial disease caused by Shigella, which can cause diarrhea, abdominal pain, and blood in stool.

## **Causes of Water Borne Diseases:**

- **Poor Water Treatment**: Inadequate treatment of water can lead to the presence of pathogens.
- **Improper Storage and Distribution**: Contaminated water can spread through storage and distribution systems.
- **Surrounding Environment**: Contaminated soil, air, and surrounding environment can also spread disease.

## **Water Induced Diseases**

Water-induced diseases are illnesses caused by exposure to contaminated water, but not necessarily through direct consumption. Examples of water-induced diseases include:

### Key Concepts:

- **Chemical Contamination**: Exposure to chemicals, such as heavy metals, pesticides, and industrial waste, which can cause disease.
- **Physical Contamination**: Exposure to physical contaminants, such as sediment, silt, and algae, which can cause disease.

## **Examples of Water Induced Diseases:**

- **Lead Poisoning**: Exposure to lead, which can cause brain damage, developmental delays, and organ damage.
- **Skull Flu**: Exposure to algal toxins, which can cause respiratory problems, skin irritation, and other health issues.
- **Antimony Poisoning**: Exposure to antimony, which can cause skin problems, liver damage, and other health issues.

## **Prevention Strategies**

### Key Concepts:

- **Proper Water Treatment**: Regular treatment of water to remove pathogens and other contaminants.
- **Safe Storage and Distribution**: Proper storage and distribution of water to prevent contamination.
- **Surrounding Environment Management**: Proper management of surrounding environment to prevent contamination.

**Prevention Strategies:**

- **Use of Water Treatment Plants**: Regular use of water treatment plants to remove pathogens and other contaminants.
- **Proper Storage and Distribution**: Proper storage and distribution of water to prevent contamination.
- **Surrounding Environment Management**: Proper management of surrounding environment to prevent contamination.

## **Conclusion**

Waterborne diseases and water-induced diseases are serious health problems that can be prevented with proper precautions. Understanding the causes, symptoms, and prevention strategies for these diseases is essential for maintaining good health and preventing the spread of disease.
