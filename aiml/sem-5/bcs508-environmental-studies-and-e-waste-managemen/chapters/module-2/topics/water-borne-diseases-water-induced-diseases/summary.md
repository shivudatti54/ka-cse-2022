# Water Borne Diseases & Water Induced Diseases

### Definition

- Water borne diseases: diseases caused by the consumption of contaminated water
- Water induced diseases: diseases caused by the interaction with contaminated water, such as skin infections

### Causes

- Bacteria (e.g. E. coli, Salmonella)
- Viruses (e.g. Rotavirus, Norovirus)
- Parasites (e.g. Giardia, Cryptosporidium)
- Chemicals (e.g. pesticides, heavy metals)
- Radioactive substances

### Effects

- Gastrointestinal diseases
- Respiratory diseases
- Skin diseases
- Neurological diseases

### Examples

- Cholera
- Dysentery
- Typhoid fever
- Leptospirosis

### Key Formulas

- **Coliform bacteria count**: number of coliform bacteria per 100 mL of water
- **E. coli count**: number of E. coli bacteria per 100 mL of water

### Theorems

- **The principle of "contamination is the key"**: water can become contaminated at any point from source to consumption
- **The concept of "dose-response"**: the risk of disease increases with the dose and duration of exposure to contaminated water

### Important Terms

- **Water quality index**: a measure of the overall quality of water based on its physical, chemical, and biological characteristics
- **Waterborne pathogens**: microorganisms that can cause disease through contaminated water
