Learning about water-borne diseases and water-induced diseases is crucial as it highlights the devastating impact of contaminated water on human health and the environment. This topic matters because it underscores the urgent need for safe and sustainable water management practices, emphasizing the importance of protecting public health and preventing water-borne epidemics. Real-world applications include developing effective water treatment systems, implementing public health policies, and promoting water conservation practices.

This knowledge also connects to other concepts in Environmental Studies and E-Waste Management, as it underscores the interconnectedness of environmental and health issues. Understanding the causes and consequences of water-borne diseases informs strategies for mitigating the effects of climate change, pollution, and waste management on natural resources and human well-being.
