# **E-waste (Management and Handling) Rules**

## **Introduction**

E-waste, or electronic waste, refers to discarded electronic devices and equipment that pose environmental and health risks if not managed properly. The E-waste (Management and Handling) Rules, 2011, is an Indian government regulation aimed at reducing the environmental and health impacts of e-waste.

## **Definition of E-waste**

- E-waste is defined as any electronic product that is discarded or intended to be discarded, including but not limited to:
  - Electronic equipment such as computers, telephones, televisions, air conditioners, refrigerators, and washing machines
  - Electronic components, such as capacitors, resistors, and printed circuit boards
  - Battery, including lead acid, alkaline, nickel cadmium, nickel metal hydride, lithium ion, and lithium polymer batteries
- E-waste includes any waste generated during the production, use, and disposal of electronic products.

## **Producers' Responsibility**

- Producers of electronic products are required to:
  - Design and manufacture products with recyclability and reusability in mind
  - Ensure that products are sold with a minimum of 70% recyclable content
  - Provide a 5-year warranty and free of charge repair and replacement of defective parts
  - Establish a fund for the collection and recycling of e-waste
  - Ensure that products are not used or sold in a way that contributes to e-waste generation

## **Collectors and Transporters' Responsibility**

- Collectors and transporters of e-waste are required to:
  - Ensure that collected e-waste is disposed of in an environmentally sound manner
  - Label and transport e-waste in a manner that prevents any leakage or spillage
  - Ensure that the collected e-waste is handed over to a registered e-waste treatment and disposal facility
  - Ensure that the e-waste is not mixed with household waste

## **Treatment and Disposal Facilities**

- Treatment and disposal facilities must ensure that e-waste is handled, treated, and disposed of in an environmentally sound manner
- Facilities must be registered and equipped to handle and process e-waste in a safe and environmentally sound manner

## **Penalties for Non-Compliance**

- Failure to comply with the E-waste (Management and Handling) Rules, 2011, can result in penalties, including:
  - Imprisonment of up to 3 years and/or a fine of up to 10 lakhs
  - Imprisonment of up to 7 years and/or a fine of up to 50 lakhs for large-scale non-compliance

## **Key Concepts**

- Design for recyclability and reusability
- Producer responsibility
- Collector responsibility
- Treatment and disposal facilities
- Penalties for non-compliance
