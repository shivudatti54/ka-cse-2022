### Self-Study Component (SSC) Revision Notes: E-Waste Management

#### Module: E - Waste Management

#### Topic: E-Waste (Management) Amendment Rules, 2023, 2024

**Textbook 1: CH- 6, Textbook 2: CH-2, e-resource:3**

### Key Points:

- **Definition of E-Waste:** Electronic waste (e-waste) refers to discarded electronic devices containing hazardous materials such as lead, mercury, and cadmium.
- **E-Waste Generation:** According to the e-Waste (Management) Amendment Rules, 2023, e-waste is generated mainly through:
  - Consumer disposal (53.4%).
  - Manufacturer and refurbisher disposal (24.8%).
  - Institutional disposal (12.8%).
- **Prohibited Items:** Under the e-Waste (Management) Amendment Rules, 2024, the following items are prohibited from being exported:
  - Hazardous components (e.g., lead, mercury, cadmium).
  - Certain types of electronic components (e.g., CRTs, fluorescent tubes).
- **Permitted Items:** The following items can be exported:
  - Non-hazardous components (e.g., plastics, metals).
  - Recyclable materials (e.g., copper, aluminum).
- **E-Waste Treatment and Recycling:** The e-Waste (Management) Amendment Rules, 2023, and 2024, mandate that e-waste be treated and recycled in an environmentally responsible manner.

### Important Formulas/Definitions/Theorems:

- **Waste Minimization:** The principle of reducing waste generation through design and production processes.
- **Waste Reduction:** The reduction of waste through recycling, reusing, and composting.
- **Circular Economy:** An economic model that aims to keep resources in use for as long as possible, extract the maximum value from them, and recover and regenerate materials.
- **Zero-Waste-to-Landfill (ZWTL):** A goal to eliminate waste sent to landfills and increase waste recycling and composting.
