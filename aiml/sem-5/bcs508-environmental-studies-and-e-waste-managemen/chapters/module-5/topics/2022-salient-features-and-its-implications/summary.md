# Environmental Studies and E-Waste Management

### 2022 - Salient Features and its Implications

#### Key Points

- **Global E-Waste Generation**:
  - 50 million metric tons of e-waste generated in 2022 (Source: International Telecommunication Union (ITU))
  - Projected to reach 74 million metric tons by 2030 (Source: ITU)
- **E-Waste Management**:
  - 15% of global e-waste is not collected or recycled (Source: World Economic Forum)
  - Developing countries face significant challenges in e-waste management due to lack of infrastructure and resources
- **Recycling Technologies**:
  - Advanced recycling technologies, such as mechanical separation and hydrometallurgy, improve e-waste recycling rates
  - Closed-loop recycling of e-waste materials is becoming increasingly important
- **Challenges and Implications**:
  - E-waste's toxic substances pose environmental and health risks if not managed properly
  - The global e-waste crisis requires a coordinated effort from governments, industries, and individuals

#### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **E-Waste Generation Rate**: The amount of e-waste generated per unit of GDP (Source: UN Environment Programme)
- **Life Cycle Assessment (LCA)**: A method to evaluate the environmental impacts of e-waste throughout its entire life cycle

#### Theorems and Key Concepts

- **The Waste Hierarchy**: A principle guiding waste management practices, prioritizing reduction, reuse, and recycling over disposal
- **Circular Economy**: A concept promoting the sharing, leasing, and product-as-a-service models to reduce waste and environmental impacts
