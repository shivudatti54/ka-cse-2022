# **Composition and Generation Revision Notes**

### Definition and Key Concepts

- **Composition**: The process of breaking down waste materials into their constituent components, typically through physical or chemical means.
- **Generation**: The production or creation of waste, including waste from economic, social, and technological activities.

### Types of Waste Composition

- **Physical composition**: Breakdown of waste into its physical components, such as recyclables, non-recyclables, and hazardous waste.
- **Chemical composition**: Breakdown of waste into its chemical components, including hazardous substances and pollutants.

### Waste Composition Theorems

- **Waste Composition Equation**: m1 _ C1 + m2 _ C2 + ... + mn _ Cn = m _ W, where m = mass of each component, C = composition of each component, and W = total waste composition.
- ** waste composition theorem**: The composition of waste is determined by the properties of its constituent components.

### Important Formulas

- **Recycling rate formula**: Recycling rate = (amount of waste recycled / total amount of waste generated) \* 100.
- **Hazardous waste generation rate formula**: Hazardous waste generation rate = (amount of hazardous waste generated / population) \* 100.

### Important Definitions

- **Waste hierarchy**: A framework for managing waste, prioritizing prevention, reuse, recycling, and final disposal.
- **Closed-loop production**: A production system where materials are constantly cycled back into production, minimizing waste generation.

### Important Environmental Impacts

- **Environmental impacts of waste composition**: Waste composition can have significant environmental impacts, including pollution, climate change, and resource depletion.
- **Eco-friendly waste management practices**: Practices such as reducing, reusing, recycling, and composting can minimize environmental impacts.

### Quick Revision Tips

- Review the waste composition equation and recycling rate formula to ensure you understand key concepts.
- Memorize important definitions, such as the waste hierarchy and closed-loop production.
- Practice calculating waste generation rates and recycling rates to improve your problem-solving skills.
