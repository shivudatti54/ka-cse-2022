**Why it matters:**
Learning about composition and generation is crucial as it helps understand the origins, nature, and impact of waste on the environment. Understanding the composition and generation of waste enables individuals to make informed decisions about waste management, reducing the environmental harm caused by improper disposal.

**Real-world applications:**
The composition and generation of waste have significant real-world applications in waste management practices, such as recycling, composting, and landfill management. By understanding how waste is generated and composed, waste management systems can be optimized to minimize waste disposal costs, reduce greenhouse gas emissions, and promote sustainable practices.

**Connection to other concepts:**
The composition and generation of waste is closely linked to other concepts in Environmental Studies, such as resource depletion, pollution, and climate change. Understanding the composition and generation of waste also informs strategies for waste reduction, reuse, and recycling, which are essential for achieving sustainable development goals and mitigating the environmental impacts of human activities.
