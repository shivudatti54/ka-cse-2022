**Why this topic matters**: Understanding the global context in e-waste is crucial as it highlights the transboundary nature of electronic waste, where its generation, transportation, and disposal have significant environmental and health impacts on countries beyond the initial producer or consumer. This understanding is essential for developing effective e-waste management policies and strategies that consider the global implications. E-waste can have devastating effects on local communities, ecosystems, and human health.

**Real-world applications**: Learning about the global context in e-waste has practical applications in developing countries with limited e-waste management infrastructure. It helps policymakers, businesses, and individuals understand the need for coordinated international efforts to address e-waste, promote responsible consumption, and establish effective recycling and disposal systems. This knowledge can inform the development of national and international e-waste management policies.

**Connection to other concepts**: Understanding the global context in e-waste is closely related to other environmental studies concepts, such as sustainable consumption and production, circular economy, and climate change. It also intersects with concepts like international environmental law, human rights, and social justice, highlighting the need for a human rights-based approach to e-waste management. By learning about the global context in e-waste, students can develop a more nuanced understanding of the complex relationships between environmental, social, and economic factors.
