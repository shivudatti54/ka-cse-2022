# **Requirement Modeling Strategies: Flow Oriented Modeling**

**Key Concepts:**

- **Flow Oriented Modeling (FOM)**: A system modeling approach that focuses on the flow of information and resources through a system.
- **Flow Diagram**: A visual representation of the flow of information and resources through a system.
- **Activity Model**: A flow-oriented representation of a system's activities and their dependencies.
- **State Machine Model**: A flow-oriented representation of a system's states and their transitions.

**Important Formulas:**

- **Flow Model Equation**: F = Q / C, where F is the flow rate, Q is the quantity of resources, and C is the capacity of the system.
- **Throughput Equation**: T = F x t, where T is the throughput, F is the flow rate, and t is the time period.

**Definitions:**

- **Flow Rate**: The rate at which resources flow through a system.
- **Capacity**: The maximum amount of resources that a system can process per unit time.
- **Throughput**: The amount of work that a system can complete per unit time.

**Theorems:**

- **The Law of Conservation of Flow**: The flow rate of resources into a system is equal to the flow rate of resources out of the system.
- **The Law of Capacity**: The capacity of a system is equal to the maximum flow rate that can be achieved.

**Key Points:**

- FOM is a systematic approach to modeling complex systems.
- Flow diagrams and activity models are used to represent the flow of information and resources.
- State machine models are used to represent the states and transitions of a system.
- FOM can be used to analyze and improve the performance of systems.
