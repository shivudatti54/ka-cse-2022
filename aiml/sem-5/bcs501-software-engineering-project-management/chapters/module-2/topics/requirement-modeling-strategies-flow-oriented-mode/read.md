# **Requirement Modeling Strategies : Flow Oriented Modeling**

## **Introduction**

Requirement modeling is a critical phase in the software development life cycle that involves capturing, analyzing, and documenting the needs of the stakeholders. It provides a foundation for the development of a software system that meets the requirements of the end-users. In this module, we will explore the different requirement modeling strategies, with a focus on flow-oriented modeling.

## **Definition of Requirement Modeling**

Requirement modeling is the process of creating a detailed description of the functional and non-functional requirements of a software system. It involves identifying, analyzing, and documenting the needs of the stakeholders, and providing a basis for the development of the software system.

## **Types of Requirement Modeling**

### 1. Structured Analysis

Structured analysis is a requirement modeling approach that involves breaking down the system into its components and analyzing the interactions between them. It is based on a structured approach to requirements gathering, which involves identifying the inputs, outputs, and processes of the system.

### 2. Object-Oriented Analysis and Design (OOAD)

OOAD is a requirement modeling approach that involves modeling the system as a collection of objects that interact with each other. It is based on the principles of object-oriented design, which involves identifying the objects, their attributes, and their interactions.

### 3. Flow-Oriented Modeling

Flow-oriented modeling is a requirement modeling approach that involves modeling the system as a flow of activities, processes, and interactions. It is based on the idea that the system can be represented as a flow diagram, which illustrates the sequence of activities and interactions that occur during the system's operation.

## **Flow-Oriented Modeling Techniques**

### 1. Five-Step Flow Model

The five-step flow model is a technique used in flow-oriented modeling that involves:

- Identifying the system's goals and objectives
- Analyzing the system's inputs and outputs
- Modeling the system's processes and activities
- Identifying the system's interfaces and dependencies
- Developing a flow diagram that illustrates the system's flow

### 2. Business Process Model and Notation (BPMN)

BPMN is a standard for modeling business processes and activities. It provides a visual representation of the system's flow, which can be used to identify and document the system's requirements.

### 3. Unified Modeling Language (UML)

UML is a standard for modeling software systems. It provides a set of visual representations, including flowcharts, state machines, and activity diagrams, that can be used to model the system's flow.

## **Key Concepts**

- **Activity**: A sequence of tasks or operations that are performed by the system.
- **Process**: A collection of activities that are performed by the system to achieve a specific goal.
- **Interface**: A point of interaction between the system and its users or other systems.
- **Dependency**: A relationship between two or more activities or processes that are dependent on each other.

## **Conclusion**

Flow-oriented modeling is a requirement modeling approach that involves modeling the system as a flow of activities, processes, and interactions. It is a useful technique for capturing the requirements of complex systems, and can be used in conjunction with other requirement modeling techniques to provide a comprehensive understanding of the system's needs.
