# **Software Engineering & Project Management Study Material**

## **Chapter 6: Requirements Engineering (6.1 to 6.5)**

### 6.1 Introduction to Requirements Engineering

**Definition:** Requirements engineering is the process of defining, analyzing, documenting, and managing stakeholder needs and expectations to develop a software system that meets their requirements.

**Importance:** Requirements engineering is a critical stage in the software development life cycle as it ensures that the software meets the needs of end-users and stakeholders.

**Example:** A software company is developing a mobile app for tracking fitness goals. The requirements engineer would identify and document the needs of the users, such as tracking workouts, setting goals, and monitoring progress.

### 6.2 Types of Requirements

#### Functional Requirements

- Define what the software should do
- Specify the functionality of the software
- Examples: login functionality, payment processing, data analysis

#### Non-Functional Requirements

- Define the characteristics of the software
- Specify the constraints of the software
- Examples: performance, security, usability

#### Quality Requirements

- Define the quality attributes of the software
- Specify the standards for the software
- Examples: reliability, maintainability, scalability

### 6.3 Requirements Engineering Process

#### Requirements Gathering

- Identify stakeholder needs and expectations
- Collect and document requirements
- Examples: user interviews, surveys, focus groups

#### Requirements Analysis

- Analyze and validate requirements
- Identify potential issues and dependencies
- Examples: use cases, data flow diagrams

#### Requirements Specification

- Document and formalize requirements
- Create a requirements specification document
- Examples: use cases, system specifications

#### Requirements Management

- Manage and control changes to requirements
- Ensure requirements are met throughout the development process
- Examples: requirements tracking, change management

### 6.4 Requirements Engineering Tools and Techniques

#### Use Cases

- A graphical representation of user interactions
- Helps identify functional requirements and user needs

#### Data Flow Diagrams

- A graphical representation of data flow and processing
- Helps identify non-functional requirements and dependencies

#### Use Case Diagrams

- A graphical representation of user interactions and system components
- Helps identify functional requirements and system architecture

### 6.5 Best Practices for Requirements Engineering

#### Collaborative Approach

- Involve stakeholders and end-users in requirements gathering
- Encourages active listening and feedback

#### Iterative Approach

- Develop requirements in an iterative and incremental manner
- Ensures requirements are met and can be refined

#### Documented Requirements

- Document requirements clearly and concisely
- Ensures requirements are understandable and accessible

By following these best practices and using a variety of tools and techniques, software engineers can ensure that requirements are gathered, analyzed, and managed effectively throughout the development process.
