# **Empirical Estimation Models**

**Definition:** Empirical estimation models use historical data to predict the effort required for a software development project.

**Key Concepts:**

- **Empirical Estimation Models:** Models that estimate effort based on historical data and empirical data.
- **Types of Empirical Estimation Models:**
  - **Step Chart Method:** Plotting effort (x-axis) against time (y-axis) to estimate the slope of the effort curve.
  - **Cockburn Method:** Estimating effort based on a set of fixed rules.
  - **Trio Method:** Estimating effort using a combination of expert judgment and historical data.
  - **Three-Point Estimate:** Estimating effort as the average of three estimates: optimistic, most likely, and pessimistic.
- **Important Formulas:**
  - **Step Chart Method:** Effort (E) = k \* sqrt(T)
  - **Cockburn Method:** Effort (E) = (s \* d) / (w + 1)
- **Theorems:**
  - **Law of Diminishing Returns:** As the number of people working on a task increases, the rate of progress decreases.
  - **Law of Increasing Productivity:** As the number of people working on a task increases, the rate of progress increases.

**Key Terms:**

- **Slope:** The rate of change of effort over time.
- **Time:** The time required to complete a task.
- **Effort:** The amount of work required to complete a task.

**Revision Tips:**

- Understand the differences between empirical estimation models.
- Learn to apply the formulas and theorems.
- Practice estimating effort using historical data.
