# **Chapter 38 Summary and Revision Notes - Exploratory Data Analysis**

## **Overview**

- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) is a process of summarizing and visualizing datasets to understand their structure and distribution.

## **Key Concepts**

- **Data Preprocessing**: Cleaning, transforming, and preparing data for analysis.
- **Data Visualization**: Using plots and charts to understand data distribution and relationships.
- **Summary Statistics**: Calculating mean, median, mode, standard deviation, and variance to describe data distributions.

## **Formulas and Definitions**

- **Mean**: μ = (Σx) / n, where μ is the mean, x is each data point, and n is the number of data points.
- **Median**: The middle value of a dataset when it is sorted in ascending order.
- **Mode**: The value that appears most frequently in a dataset.
- **Standard Deviation**: σ = √((Σ(x - μ)^2) / (n - 1)), where σ is the standard deviation, x is each data point, μ is the mean, and n is the number of data points.

## **Important Theorems**

- **Central Limit Theorem (CLT)**: The distribution of sample means approaches a normal distribution as the sample size increases.
- **Empirical Rule**: About 68% of the data points fall within 1 standard deviation of the mean, about 95% fall within 2 standard deviations, and about 99.7% fall within 3 standard deviations.

## **Key Techniques**

- **Histograms**: Visual representation of the distribution of a dataset.
- **Box Plots**: Visual representation of the distribution of a dataset, showing the median, quartiles, and outliers.
- **Scatter Plots**: Visual representation of the relationship between two variables.

## **Revision Tips**

- Review data preprocessing techniques, such as handling missing values and outliers.
- Practice creating summaries and visualizations for different datasets.
- Review statistical formulas and definitions, including mean, median, mode, and standard deviation.
- Familiarize yourself with important theorems, such as CLT and Empirical Rule.
- Practice applying key techniques, such as histograms, box plots, and scatter plots.
