# Learning Purpose: Continuous Internal Evaluation

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) is crucial as it shifts the focus from high-stakes, end-of-term exams to an ongoing assessment of a student's understanding and practical skills throughout the module. This approach reduces exam pressure, provides regular feedback for improvement, and ensures a more accurate and holistic measurement of a student's ability to apply EDA concepts.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn how their proficiency in performing Exploratory Data Analysis—including data cleaning, visualization, and statistical summarization—is formatively assessed. They will understand the CIE criteria, which likely evaluate their coding practices, analytical reasoning, ability to derive insights, and effective communication of results.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
CIE directly assesses the cumulative application of all prior EDA modules. Success depends on integrating knowledge of data importing (Module 1), cleaning (Module 2), visualization (Module 3), and statistical analysis (Module 4). It tests the student’s ability to synthesize these skills into a complete, coherent analytical workflow.

**4. Real-world applications**
This mirrors the agile, iterative process of a data analyst in industry, where projects are continuously evaluated through milestones, code reviews, and presentations of findings to stakeholders. It prepares students for a professional environment where consistent performance and the ability to receive and incorporate feedback are valued over a single final deliverable.
