### Learning Purpose: Module 3 - Working with Time Series

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Time series data, which tracks values over sequential time intervals, is ubiquitous in fields like finance, economics, and operations. Understanding its unique structure is critical because standard statistical techniques often fail, leading to inaccurate models and forecasts if temporal dependencies like trends and seasonality are ignored.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core techniques for visualizing, decomposing, and interpreting time series data. This includes plotting data to identify patterns, calculating rolling statistics to smooth noise, and decomposing a series into its trend, seasonal, and residual components to understand its underlying structure.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module builds directly on prior EDA modules focused on summary statistics and visualization. It provides the essential foundation for more advanced concepts in the curriculum, particularly time series forecasting (e.g., ARIMA models) and machine learning, where understanding temporal patterns is a prerequisite for feature engineering and model building.

**4. Real-world applications**
The skills learned are directly applicable to analyzing stock prices for investment, forecasting product demand for supply chain management, monitoring website traffic, and evaluating the impact of policy changes over time.
