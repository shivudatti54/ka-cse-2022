### Module 5: Computer Networks – A Systems Approach

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because modern networks are complex, interconnected systems, not just a collection of devices and cables. Understanding the "systems approach" is essential for designing, managing, and troubleshooting scalable and efficient networks that form the backbone of global digital communication, from the internet to cloud infrastructures.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to analyze networks as integrated systems, understanding how layers (physical to application) and components (protocols, routers, switches) interact to provide end-to-end services. They will grasp key design principles, including resource allocation, congestion control, routing algorithms, and network management, moving beyond isolated concepts to a holistic view.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module synthesizes knowledge from previous modules on networking fundamentals, data link layer protocols, IP addressing, and transport layer services (like TCP). It provides the foundational systems thinking required for advanced topics in network security, distributed systems, and cloud computing covered later in the curriculum.

**4. Real-world applications**
This systems-level understanding is directly applied in roles like network architect, cloud engineer, and DevOps specialist. It is used to design robust corporate networks, optimize content delivery networks (CDNs), implement software-defined networking (SDN), and ensure reliable service delivery for applications like video streaming and IoT.
