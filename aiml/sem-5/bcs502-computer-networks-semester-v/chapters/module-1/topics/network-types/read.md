# Network Types: An Introduction

**Subject:** Computer Networks (Semester V)
**Module:** Module 1

A computer network is a collection of interconnected devices that can communicate and share resources. Networks are not one-size-fits-all; they are categorized based on their geographical coverage, design, and purpose. Understanding these types is fundamental to designing, implementing, and managing efficient network systems.

---

## Core Concepts: Classification of Networks

Networks are primarily classified based on their scale and reach. The main categories are:

### 1. Personal Area Network (PAN)

A PAN is the most basic and smallest type of network, designed for personal use within a range of about 10 meters.

*   **Scope:** Very small area, typically a single room.
*   **Technology:** Wireless technologies like **Bluetooth** (e.g., connecting a wireless mouse to a laptop) and **Infrared** (IrDA) are common. Wired PANs can involve USB connections.
*   **Example:** Synchronizing data between a smartphone and a computer, connecting wireless headphones to a phone.

### 2. Local Area Network (LAN)

A LAN connects devices within a limited geographical area such as a home, school, office building, or a campus (like ).

*   **Scope:** Typically a single building or a group of closely located buildings.
*   **Technology:** Primarily uses wired technologies like **Ethernet** (with twisted-pair cables) or wireless technologies like **Wi-Fi (IEEE 802.11)**.
*   **Characteristics:**
    *   High data transfer speeds (bps).
    *   Privately owned and managed.
    *   Low error rates and cost.
*   **Example:** The network in your college computer lab, the Wi-Fi network in your hostel, a home network connecting PCs, printers, and smart TVs.

### 3. Metropolitan Area Network (MAN)

A MAN spans a larger geographical area than a LAN, such as a city or a large campus.

*   **Scope:** Covers an entire city or a large campus (e.g., connecting multiple  campuses across a city).
*   **Technology:** Often uses high-capacity fiber-optic cables and may be operated by a single entity (like a municipality) or a large service provider. Technologies like **ATM (Asynchronous Transfer Mode)** and **Frame Relay** have been used, with modern MANs often relying on high-speed Ethernet.
*   **Example:** Cable TV network infrastructure across a city, a network connecting all branches of a bank within a metropolitan area.

### 4. Wide Area Network (WAN)

A WAN covers a broad geographical area, such as a country, continent, or even the entire globe.

*   **Scope:** Large geographic area (e.g., state, country, world).
*   **Technology:** Utilizes long-distance transmission systems like **leased lines**, **satellites**, and **microwave links**. The internet is the largest WAN in existence. Routers are essential devices for interconnecting different networks within a WAN.
*   **Characteristics:**
    *   Lower data transfer rates compared to LANs due to longer distances.
    *   Owned and managed by multiple public and private entities (telecom companies).
*   **Example:** The global internet. A multinational corporation connecting its office networks in Bangalore, London, and New York via a private WAN.

### 5. Other Notable Types

*   **Campus Area Network (CAN):** A network spanning multiple LANs within a university or corporate campus. It is larger than a LAN but smaller than a MAN (e.g., the network connecting all departments and hostels of ).
*   **Storage Area Network (SAN):** A high-speed special-purpose network that interconnects and presents shared pools of storage devices to multiple servers.

## Key Points & Summary

| Network Type | Acronym | Typical Range               | Example Technologies          | Key Example                          |
| :----------- | :------ | :-------------------------- | :---------------------------- | :----------------------------------- |
| **Personal**        | PAN     | 10 meters                   | Bluetooth, Infrared, USB      | Wireless headset connected to a phone |
| **Local**           | LAN     | Up to 1-2 km                | Ethernet, Wi-Fi (IEEE 802.11) | College lab network, Home Wi-Fi      |
| **Metropolitan**    | MAN     | Several kms (entire city)   | ATM, Metro Ethernet           | City-wide cable network              |
| **Wide**            | WAN     | Worldwide                   | Leased Lines, Satellites      | The Internet                         |

*   Networks are classified primarily by their **geographical coverage**.
*   **LANs** are characterized by high speed, private ownership, and confinement to a small area.
*   **WANs** connect multiple LANs over large distances, typically using public carrier networks.
*   The **Internet** is the ultimate example of a global WAN.
*   The choice of network type depends on requirements like cost, speed, and the area that needs to be covered.

Understanding these fundamental types provides the groundwork for exploring more complex networking concepts like topologies, protocols, and the OSI/TCP-IP models.
