# Learning Purpose: Network Models

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding network models is fundamental because they provide the structured framework and common language that governs all communication on the internet and modern networks. They are the essential blueprints that allow diverse hardware and software from various vendors to interoperate seamlessly. Without these standardized models, the global internet as we know it would not function.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the layered architecture of the OSI (Open Systems Interconnection) and TCP/IP models. They will identify the function and purpose of each layer—from the Physical layer dealing with hardware and signals to the Application layer handling user interfaces. They will also understand the concepts of encapsulation and de-encapsulation as data moves through these layers.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is the foundational bedrock for the entire course. It directly connects to and provides context for all subsequent modules, including specific protocols (like TCP, UDP, IP, HTTP), network devices (routers, switches), network design, and troubleshooting methodologies. It explains *how* and *why* these other components work together.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied whenever a web page loads, an email is sent, or a video is streamed. It is used by network engineers to design, build, and troubleshoot corporate networks and internet infrastructure. It is also critical for cybersecurity professionals to understand vulnerabilities and defend attacks at specific layers.
