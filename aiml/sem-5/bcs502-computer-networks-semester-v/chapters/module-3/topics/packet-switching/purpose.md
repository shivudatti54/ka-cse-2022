# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1.  Define packet switching and explain its importance in modern communication networks.
2.  Describe the structure of a packet, including the header and payload.
3.  Explain the difference between connection-oriented and connectionless packet switching.
4.  Identify the primary advantages and disadvantages of each type of packet switching.
5.  Describe the purpose of packet switching in a network.
6.  Explain how packet switching is used in the Internet.
