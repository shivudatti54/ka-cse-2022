# **Structured Text Retrieval**

### Overview

Structured text retrieval refers to the process of extracting relevant information from structured data sources, such as XML documents. This topic covers the power of structuring text, early text retrieval models, XML retrieval, and evaluation metrics.

### Key Points

#### Structuring Power

- Structured data provides more accurate and relevant information than unstructured data.
- Structuring text allows for easier querying and retrieval of specific information.

#### Early Text Retrieval Models

- **Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency (TF-IDF)**: A widely used model that calculates the importance of words in a document.
- **Inverted Index**: A data structure that maps words to their locations in a document collection.
- **Retrieval Model**: A mathematical model that calculates the relevance of a query to a document.

#### XML Retrieval

- **XML Schema**: A set of rules that defines the structure and organization of an XML document.
- **XML Query**: A language used to retrieve specific data from an XML document.
- **XML Retrieval Evaluation**: Measures the effectiveness of an XML query in retrieving relevant data.

#### XML Retrieval Evaluation

- **Precision**: The ratio of relevant documents to all retrieved documents.
- **Recall**: The ratio of relevant documents to all documents in the collection.
- **F1-score**: The harmonic mean of precision and recall.

### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Term Frequency (TF)**: The frequency of a word in a document.
- **Inverse Document Frequency (IDF)**: The rarity of a word in the document collection.
- **Relevance Score**: A measure of the importance of a document to a query.
- **Query Expansion**: The process of expanding a query to include related terms.

### Theorems

- **Theorem of Relevance**: The concept that relevance is a subjective measure that depends on the context and purpose of the search query.
- **Theorem of Information Retrieval**: The concept that information retrieval is a process of searching for relevant information in a large corpus of text.
