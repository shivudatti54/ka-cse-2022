## **3.1: Introduction to Information Retrieval Models**

### Definition

Information retrieval (IR) models are mathematical frameworks used to retrieve relevant documents or information from a large database or repository.

### Key Concepts

- **Relevance**: The degree to which a document contains the desired information.
- **Query**: The user's request for information.
- **Documents**: The collection of text or data from which information is retrieved.
- **Model**: A mathematical framework used to represent the relationship between queries and documents.

### Example

Suppose a user searches for "climate change" on a search engine. The IR model would analyze the query and retrieve the most relevant documents from the database.

## **3.2: Classic Information Retrieval Models**

### Definition

Classic IR models are traditional models that focus on the frequency of words in documents and their relevance to the query.

### Key Concepts

- **Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency (TF-IDF)**: A scoring function that calculates the importance of each word in a document.
- **Vector Space Model**: A mathematical framework that represents documents as vectors and queries as vectors.
- **Cosine Similarity**: A measure of similarity between two vectors.

### Example

Using TF-IDF, a document with the words "climate change" and "sustainability" would be scored higher than a document with the words "weather" and "sunshine".

## **3.3: Alternative Set- theoretic Models**

### Definition

Set-theoretic models are IR models that represent documents as sets of features and queries as sets of features.

### Key Concepts

- **Set Operations**: Union, intersection, and difference operations used to combine and compare sets.
- **Jaccard Similarity**: A measure of similarity between two sets.
- **Venn Diagrams**: Visual representations of set operations.

### Example

Using Jaccard Similarity, two documents with overlapping sets of features (e.g., "climate change" and "sustainability") would be similar.

## **3.4: Probabilistic Models**

### Definition

Probabilistic IR models are models that assign probabilities to the relevance of documents based on their content.

### Key Concepts

- **Bayes' Theorem**: A mathematical framework for updating probabilities based on new evidence.
- **Lambda Model**: A probabilistic model that represents the relevance of documents as a weighted sum of their features.
- **Language Modeling**: A technique used to estimate the probability of a document given its content.

### Example

Using Bayes' Theorem, a document with a high probability of containing "climate change" would be assigned a high relevance score.

## **3.5: Graph-based Models**

### Definition

Graph-based IR models represent documents and queries as graphs and use graph operations to compute relevance scores.

### Key Concepts

- **Graph Neural Networks (GNNs)**: A type of neural network used to represent graph structures.
- **Graph Convolutional Networks (GCNs)**: A type of neural network used to compute graph convolutional layers.
- **PageRank**: A ranking algorithm that assigns scores to nodes in a graph.

### Example

Using GNNs, a document with a high degree of connectivity to other documents related to "climate change" would be assigned a high relevance score.

## **3.6: Deep Learning-based Models**

### Definition

Deep learning-based IR models use neural networks to represent documents and queries and compute relevance scores.

### Key Concepts

- **Natural Language Processing (NLP)**: A subfield of computer science that deals with the interaction between computers and human language.
- **Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs)**: A type of neural network used to represent sequential data.
- **Attention Mechanisms**: A technique used to focus on specific parts of the input data.

### Example

Using RNNs, a document with a high probability of containing "climate change" would be assigned a high relevance score.

This study material covers the key concepts and techniques in IR modeling, including classic models, set-theoretic models, probabilistic models, graph-based models, and deep learning-based models.
