### 1.1 Introduction to Information Retrieval

- Definition: The process of searching, selecting, retrieving, organizing, and disseminating information from a vast collection of data.
- Importance: IR is a crucial skill in today's digital world, where information is abundant but relevant information is scarce.

### 1.2 The IR Problem

- Definition: The problem of finding the most relevant documents from a large collection of documents.
- Challenges:

* Overload of irrelevant documents
* Limited time for search
* Diverse document collections

### 1.3 IR System

- Definition: A software system designed to retrieve relevant documents from a database of documents.
- Components:

* Preprocessing
* Indexing
* Retrieval
* Postprocessing

### 1.4 The Web

- Definition: A global network of interconnected documents and servers.
- Characteristics:

* Vast collection of documents
* Dynamic and constantly changing
* Accessible through web browsers

### Important Formulas and Definitions:

- **Frequency of a term (TF)**: The number of times a term appears in a document.
- **Inverse Document Frequency (IDF)**: A measure of the rarity of a term in the entire document collection.
- **Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency (TF-IDF)**: A weighted sum of TF and IDF.
- **Vector Space Model (VSM)**: A mathematical representation of documents as vectors in a high-dimensional space.

### Important Theorems:

- **Boltzmann's Law**: A statistical law that describes the probability of a document being relevant.
- **Jaccard Similarity**: A measure of similarity between two documents.

### Key Concepts:

- Text pre-processing
- Indexing and searching
- Retrieval algorithms
- Post-processing and evaluation metrics
