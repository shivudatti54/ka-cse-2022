# **7.1 - Introduction to Data Communications**

**Definition:** Data communications refer to the exchange of data between two or more devices over a communication network.

**Explanation:** Data communications involve the transmission, reception, and processing of data in a network. It is a critical component of computer networks, as it enables devices to share information and communicate with each other.

**Key Concepts:**

- **Data**: A set of values or characters that are used to represent information.
- **Communication Network**: A network of devices connected together to exchange data.
- **Transmission**: The process of sending data from one device to another.
- **Reception**: The process of receiving data at a device.

**Example:**

Suppose you want to send an email to a friend. Your email client (device) sends the email data to a nearby router, which forwards it to the internet. The internet transmits the data to a mail server, which receives and stores the email. Your friend's email client then retrieves the email from the mail server and displays it on their screen.

# **7.2 - Types of Data Communication Networks**

There are several types of data communication networks, including:

### 1. Wired Networks

- **Definition**: Wired networks use physical cables to connect devices together.
- **Characteristics**: Wired networks are more secure and reliable than wireless networks.
- **Examples**: Ethernet cables, coaxial cables.

### 2. Wireless Networks

- **Definition**: Wireless networks use radio waves or infrared light to connect devices together.
- **Characteristics**: Wireless networks are more convenient and flexible than wired networks.
- **Examples**: Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, cellular networks.

### 3. Local Area Networks (LANs)

- **Definition**: LANs are networks that connect devices within a limited geographical area, such as a home or office building.
- **Characteristics**: LANs are typically used for sharing files, printers, and other resources.
- **Examples**: Home networks, office networks.

### 4. Metropolitan Area Networks (MANs)

- **Definition**: MANs are networks that connect devices within a metropolitan area, such as a city or town.
- **Characteristics**: MANs are typically used for sharing resources and providing internet access.
- **Examples**: City-wide networks, campus networks.

### 5. Wide Area Networks (WANs)

- **Definition**: WANs are networks that connect devices over a large geographical area, such as a country or continent.
- **Characteristics**: WANs are typically used for sharing resources and providing remote access to networks.
- **Examples**: Internet, cellular networks.

# **7.3 - Network Models**

There are several network models, including:

### 1. The OSI Model

- **Definition**: The OSI model is a 7-layer model that describes the functions of a network.
- **Layers**:
  - Physical Layer (Layer 1)
  - Data Link Layer (Layer 2)
  - Network Layer (Layer 3)
  - Transport Layer (Layer 4)
  - Session Layer (Layer 5)
  - Presentation Layer (Layer 6)
  - Application Layer (Layer 7)
- **Example**: The OSI model is widely used in network design and troubleshooting.

### 2. The TCP/IP Model

- **Definition**: The TCP/IP model is a 4-layer model that describes the functions of a network.
- **Layers**:
  - Network Access Layer (Layer 1)
  - Internet Layer (Layer 2)
  - Transport Layer (Layer 3)
  - Application Layer (Layer 4)
- **Example**: The TCP/IP model is widely used in internet protocols and network communication.

Key Concepts:

- **Layered model**: A model that describes the functions of a network in layers.
- **Protocol**: A set of rules and procedures used to communicate between devices on a network.
- **Network architecture**: The design and organization of a network.
