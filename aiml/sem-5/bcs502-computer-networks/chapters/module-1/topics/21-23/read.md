# **2.1 - Introduction to Data Communications**

**Definition:** Data communications refers to the process of transmitting data between two or more devices over a communication medium, such as a physical wire or wireless link.

**Types of Data Communications:**

- **Point-to-Point (PtP) Communication:** Direct communication between two devices.
- **Point-to-MultiPoint (PtMP) Communication:** Communication between one device and multiple devices.
- **MultiPoint (MP) Communication:** Communication between multiple devices.

**Key Concepts:**

- **Data:** Digital information, such as text, images, and audio.
- **Transmission Medium:** The physical or wireless link used to transmit data.
- **Data Link Layer:** The layer of the OSI model responsible for error-free transfer of data frames between two devices.

# **2.2 - Network Types**

**Definition:** A network is a collection of interconnected devices that communicate with each other.

**Types of Networks:**

- **Local Area Network (LAN):** A network that connects devices within a limited geographical area, such as a home or office building.
- **Wide Area Network (WAN):** A network that connects devices over a larger geographical area, such as a city or country.
- **Metropolitan Area Network (MAN):** A network that connects devices within a metropolitan area, such as a city.
- **Wireless Network (WLAN):** A network that connects devices wirelessly.

**Key Concepts:**

- **Interconnected Devices:** Devices connected to a network, such as computers, printers, and routers.
- **Communication Medium:** The physical or wireless link used to transmit data between devices.
- **Network Topology:** The physical or logical arrangement of devices within a network.

# **2.3 - Network Models**

**Definition:** A network model is a conceptual representation of a network's structure and components.

**Types of Network Models:**

- **Physical Model:** A model that represents the physical components of a network, such as cables and routers.
- **Logical Model:** A model that represents the logical components of a network, such as devices and protocols.
- **OSI Model:** A model that divides a network into seven layers, each responsible for a specific function.
- **TCP/IP Model:** A model that replaces the OSI model, with a focus on Internet Protocol (IP) and Transmission Control Protocol (TCP).

**Key Concepts:**

- **Layers:** The OSI model consists of seven layers, each responsible for a specific function, such as data link and transmission.
- **Service:** A function provided by a network, such as routing and switching.
- **Interface:** A point of connection between a device and a network.

By understanding the concepts and models discussed in this topic, you will gain a solid foundation in computer networks and be able to analyze and design networks for various applications.
