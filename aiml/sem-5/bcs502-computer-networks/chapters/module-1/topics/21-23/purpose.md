Learning "2.1 - 2.3" on computer networks is crucial as it covers the fundamental concepts of data communication protocols, network models, and the OSI and TCP/IP models, which are essential for designing, implementing, and managing modern computer networks.

This topic matters because it provides a solid foundation for understanding how data is transmitted and received over a network, enabling professionals to troubleshoot and optimize network performance.

Real-world applications include designing local area networks (LANs), wide area networks (WANs), and the Internet, as well as understanding network protocols such as HTTP, FTP, and DNS.

This topic connects to other concepts in computer networking by laying the groundwork for understanding network topologies, network security, and network protocols, making it essential for professionals in the field of computer networking.
