# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and explain the concept of a Finite Automaton.
2. Describe the differences between a DFA and an NFA.
3. Explain the purpose of the transition function in a Finite Automaton.
4. Provide examples of languages that can and cannot be recognized by a DFA.
5. Compare and contrast the recognition power of DFAs and NFAs.
6. Apply the concepts of Finite Automata to solve problems in the Theory of Computation.
