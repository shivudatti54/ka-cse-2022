## **5.1 Introduction to Image Degradation**

- Image degradation refers to the loss of quality in an image due to various factors such as noise, distortion, and compression.
- Types of image degradation:
  - Random noise
  - Systematic noise
  - Blur
  - Artifacts

## **5.2 Models of Image Degradation**

- **Additive Model**: Image degradation is a linear combination of the original image and a degradation term.
- **Multiplicative Model**: Image degradation is a product of the original image and a degradation term.
- **Bayesian Model**: Image degradation is a probabilistic model that describes the degradation process.

## **5.3 Image Restoration**

- **Filtering**: Used to remove noise from an image.
- **Deblurring**: Used to remove blur from an image.
- **Super-Resolution**: Used to improve the resolution of an image.

## **5.4 Image Reconstruction**

- **Inverse Filter**: Used to remove a known degradation term from an image.
- **Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE)**: Used to estimate the original image from a degraded image.
- **Regularization Techniques**: Used to improve the quality of the restored image.

## **Important Formulas and Definitions**

- **Mean Squared Error (MSE)**: Measures the difference between the original and restored images.
- **Mean Absolute Error (MAE)**: Measures the difference between the original and restored images.
- **Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR)**: Measures the quality of an image.
- **Wiener Filter**: A linear filter used to remove noise from an image.

## **Theorems**

- **Cramer-Rao Lower Bound (CRLB)**: A lower bound on the variance of an estimator.
- **Bayes' Theorem**: A theorem used to update the probability of a hypothesis based on new evidence.
