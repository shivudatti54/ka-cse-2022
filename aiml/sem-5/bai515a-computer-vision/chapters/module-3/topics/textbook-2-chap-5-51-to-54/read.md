## **Image Restoration and Reconstruction**

### 5.1 Image Degradation Models

Image degradation models describe the process of how images are distorted or deteriorated over time. These models are essential in understanding the effects of noise, blur, and other forms of image degradation.

**Types of Image Degradation:**

- **Additive Noise:** A type of noise that is added to the original image, resulting in a decrease in image quality.
- **Multiplicative Noise:** A type of noise that is multiplied with the original image, resulting in a decrease in image quality.
- **Blurring:** A type of degradation that occurs when an image is distorted due to the loss of sharpness.

**Examples of Image Degradation:**

- **Phantom Menace:** A 1999 film that features a scene where a character's face is distorted due to additive noise.
- **Blurry Photos:** Many photographs taken with a smartphone or camera lens can exhibit blurring due to the lack of sharpness.

### 5.2 Image Restoration Models

Image restoration models aim to reverse the effects of image degradation and restore the original image.

**Types of Image Restoration Models:**

- **Filter-Based Methods:** These models use filters to remove noise and blur from the image.
- **Transform-Based Methods:** These models use transforms, such as the Fourier Transform, to remove noise and blur from the image.
- **Machine Learning-Based Methods:** These models use machine learning algorithms to remove noise and blur from the image.

**Examples of Image Restoration Models:**

- **Wiener Filter:** A filter-based method that uses the Wiener filter equation to remove noise from an image.
- **Bilateral Filter:** A filter-based method that uses bilateral filtering to remove noise and blur from an image.

### 5.3 Image Reconstruction Models

Image reconstruction models aim to create a new image that is identical to the original image, despite the presence of noise and blur.

**Types of Image Reconstruction Models:**

- **Linear Models:** These models assume a linear relationship between the original image and the restored image.
- **Non-Linear Models:** These models assume a non-linear relationship between the original image and the restored image.
- **Model-Based Models:** These models use a physical model of the imaging process to reconstruct the original image.

**Examples of Image Reconstruction Models:**

- **Deconvolution:** A model-based method that uses deconvolution to reconstruct the original image.
- **Deep Learning-Based Models:** These models use deep learning algorithms to reconstruct the original image.

### 5.4 Image Quality Metrics

Image quality metrics are used to measure the quality of an image after restoration or reconstruction.

**Types of Image Quality Metrics:**

- **Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio (PSNR):** A metric that measures the ratio of the peak amplitude of a signal to the noise level.
- **Structural Similarity Index Measure (SSIM):** A metric that measures the similarity between two images based on luminance, contrast, and structural features.

**Examples of Image Quality Metrics:**

- **PSNR:** A metric used to evaluate the quality of an image after restoration or reconstruction.
- **SSIM:** A metric used to evaluate the similarity between two images after restoration or reconstruction.
