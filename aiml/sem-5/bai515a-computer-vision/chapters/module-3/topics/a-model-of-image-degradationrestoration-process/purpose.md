# Learning Objectives
After studying this topic, you should be able to:
1.  Define and write the mathematical model for image degradation.
2.  Explain the role and characteristics of the Point Spread Function (PSF) and additive noise in the degradation process.
3.  Differentiate between common noise models (Gaussian, Salt-and-Pepper) based on their cause and visual characteristics.
4.  Describe the steps involved in a general image restoration process.
5.  Compare and contrast the Inverse and Wiener restoration filters, highlighting the limitations of the former and the advantages of the latter.
6.  Solve basic numerical problems applying the degradation model in the frequency domain.
