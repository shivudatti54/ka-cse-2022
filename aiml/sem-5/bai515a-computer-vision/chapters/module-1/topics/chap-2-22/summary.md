# **Revision Notes for Chapter 2.2: Image Formation**

## **What is Image Formation?**

- Image formation is the process of converting light into an electrical signal that can be processed by a computer.
- It involves capturing and measuring the intensity of light in an image.

## **Types of Image Formation**

- **Photometric Image**: represents the amount of light in an image.
- **Intensity Image**: represents the amount of light in an image, normalized to a specific range.

## **Key Concepts**

- **Radiance**: the amount of light emitted or reflected by an object per unit solid angle per unit area (L).
- **Irradiance**: the amount of light falling on a surface per unit area (E).
- **Viewing Angle**: the angle between the line of sight and the normal to the surface.

## **Important Formulas**

- **Radiance Formula**: R = E / (π \* cos(θ))
- **Irradiance Formula**: E = R \* (cos(θ) + sin(θ))

## **Important Definitions**

- **Photometric Camera**: converts light into an electrical signal using a photodiode or phototransistor.
- **Intensity Camera**: uses a photodiode or phototransistor to measure the intensity of light in an image.

## **Theorems**

- **Snell's Law**: describes how light bends as it passes from one medium to another (n1 sin(θ1) = n2 sin(θ2)).
- **Abbe's Law**: relates the wavelength of light to the refractive index of a medium (λ = λ0 \* (n - 1) / n).

## **Revision Tips**

- Understand the difference between photometric and intensity images.
- Be familiar with the formulas and definitions related to radiance and irradiance.
- Practice calculating the viewing angle and using Snell's and Abbe's laws.
