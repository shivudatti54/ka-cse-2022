**Computer Vision Revision Notes - Chapter 1 (1.1)**

### Introduction

- Computer Vision (CV): Field of study that enables computers to interpret and understand visual data from images and videos.
- Brief History:
  - 1960s: First computer vision systems developed.
  - 1970s-1980s: Interest in CV grew with advancements in computer hardware and software.
  - 1990s: CV gained significant attention with the introduction of machine learning and deep learning techniques.

### Image Formation

- Photometric Image:
  - 2D representation of light intensity across an image sensor.
  - Captures information about the amount of light reflected by objects in the scene.

### Key Concepts and Formulas

- Image Formation Models:
  - Pinhole Camera Model: Describes image formation using a pinhole camera.
  - Lens Camera Model: Describes image formation using a lens.
- Image Formation Equations:
  - Lens Equation: 1/f = 1/do + 1/di
  - Magnification Equation: M = -di/do

### Important Definitions

- Image Sensor: Converts light into electrical signals.
- Pixel: The smallest unit of an image sensor.
- Resolution: Number of pixels in an image.

### Important Theorems

- Ray Optics: Describes how light rays interact with optical systems.
- Geometric Optics: Describes how light rays interact with optical systems, emphasizing the importance of rays and their paths.
