**Why this topic matters:**
Learning boundary preprocessing, specifically boundary following and chain codes, is crucial in morphological image processing as it enables the analysis and representation of image boundaries, which are essential for various image segmentation and feature extraction tasks. Accurate boundary detection is vital for applications such as object recognition, image classification, and medical image analysis.

**Real-world applications:**
Boundary preprocessing has numerous practical applications in fields like computer vision, medical imaging, and robotics. For instance, it can be used to detect edges in images for object recognition, segment organs in medical images, and track the movement of objects in surveillance videos.

**Connection to other concepts:**
Boundary preprocessing is closely related to other morphological image processing techniques, such as erosion and dilation, which are used to open and close image boundaries. Understanding chain codes, a representation of boundary points, is also essential for analyzing the structure of morphological operations and their effects on image boundaries.
