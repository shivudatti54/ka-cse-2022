**Instructions:**

**Section A: Multiple Choice Questions (20 marks)**
1. Which of the following is a vector quantity?
   a) Mass
   b) Speed
   c) Force
   d) Energy

2. What is the SI unit of power?
   a) Joule
   b) Watt
   c) Newton
   d) Pascal

3. A car accelerates from rest to 20 m/s in 5 seconds. What is its acceleration?
   a) 4 m/s²
   b) 5 m/s²
   c) 10 m/s²
   d) 20 m/s²

4. Which law states that for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction?
   a) Newton's First Law
   b) Newton's Second Law
   c) Newton's Third Law
   d) Law of Conservation of Energy

5. What is the work done by a force of 10 N moving an object 5 m in the direction of the force?
   a) 2 J
   b) 50 J
   c) 0.5 J
   d) 500 J

**Section B: Short Answer Questions (30 marks)**
6. Define momentum and state its SI unit. (2 marks)

7. Explain the difference between speed and velocity. (2 marks)

8. A ball is thrown vertically upwards with an initial velocity of 20 m/s. Calculate the maximum height it reaches. (Use g = 10 m/s²) (4 marks)

9. Describe the principle of conservation of energy. Give an example. (4 marks)

10. A force of 50 N is applied to an object of mass 10 kg. Calculate the acceleration produced. (3 marks)

11. What is the difference between elastic and inelastic collisions? Provide an example of each. (4 marks)

12. Calculate the kinetic energy of a car of mass 1000 kg moving at a speed of 20 m/s. (3 marks)

13. Explain why a person feels a jerk when a bus suddenly starts moving. (2 marks)

14. A bullet of mass 0.02 kg is fired from a gun with a velocity of 200 m/s. Calculate its momentum. (3 marks)

15. What is power? How is it related to work and time? (3 marks)

**Section C: Long Answer Questions (50 marks)**
16. Derive the equations of motion for uniformly accelerated motion using graphical method. (10 marks)

17. State and prove the work-energy theorem. (10 marks)

18. Explain the concept of center of mass. How does it relate to the stability of an object? (10 marks)

19. Discuss the laws of conservation of momentum and energy. How are they applied in solving problems related to collisions? (10 marks)

20. A block of mass 5 kg is pulled along a horizontal surface by a force of 20 N acting at an angle of 30° to the horizontal. If the coefficient of kinetic friction is 0.2, calculate the acceleration of the block. (10 marks)
