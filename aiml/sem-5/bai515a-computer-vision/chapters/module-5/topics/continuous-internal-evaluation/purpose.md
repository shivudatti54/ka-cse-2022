### Learning Purpose: Continuous Internal Evaluation

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) is crucial as it moves beyond a single high-stakes exam to provide a more holistic and accurate assessment of a student's understanding and skills in Computer Vision. It allows for consistent feedback, helping to identify and address knowledge gaps early, fostering a deeper and more continuous engagement with the complex, project-oriented material typical of this field.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn how their proficiency in key Computer Vision concepts (e.g., image filtering, feature detection, object recognition) is measured through a variety of methods. This includes the execution and evaluation of practical coding assignments, mini-projects, quizzes, and presentations. They will develop the ability to iteratively improve their work based on feedback.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
CIE directly connects theoretical knowledge from previous modules (like image processing and machine learning fundamentals) to practical implementation. It assesses not just the final output but the process—reinforcing the importance of each step in the computer vision pipeline, from data preprocessing and algorithm selection to analysis and interpretation of results.

**4. Real-world applications**
This process mirrors the agile, iterative development cycles used in industry for building robust computer vision systems. It prepares students for professional environments where work is consistently reviewed and refined, such as in developing autonomous vehicle perception systems, medical image analysis software, or quality control automation on production lines.
