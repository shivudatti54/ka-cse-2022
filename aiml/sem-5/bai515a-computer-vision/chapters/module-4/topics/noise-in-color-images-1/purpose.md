### Learning Purpose

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding noise in color images is fundamental because real-world image data from cameras and sensors is never perfect. Effectively identifying and mitigating different types of noise (e.g., Gaussian, salt-and-pepper) is a critical preprocessing step that significantly impacts the performance of subsequent computer vision tasks like object detection and segmentation.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify various types of noise that specifically affect color images, understanding how noise manifests differently in individual RGB channels versus color spaces like HSV. They will explore and implement filtering techniques, such as median and Gaussian blurring, and evaluate their effectiveness in preserving color information while reducing noise.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic builds directly on prior knowledge of image representation (pixels, color models) and grayscale image filtering from earlier modules. It provides the essential foundation for more advanced concepts in Module 4, including image restoration techniques and more complex computer vision algorithms that require clean input data.

**4. Real-world applications**
The principles learned are directly applicable in numerous fields: enhancing medical imaging for better diagnosis, improving the quality of photographs in smartphone cameras, and pre-processing video frames for surveillance and autonomous vehicle systems to ensure reliable analysis.
