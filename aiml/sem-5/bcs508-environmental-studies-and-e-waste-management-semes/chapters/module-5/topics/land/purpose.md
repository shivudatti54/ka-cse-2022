### **Learning Purpose: Landfills and E-Waste Management**

**1. Why is this important?**
The disposal of electronic waste (e-waste) in landfills poses a severe threat to environmental and human health due to the leaching of toxic heavy metals and chemicals into soil and groundwater. Understanding this critical issue is essential for developing sustainable waste management solutions.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn about the specific hazards e-waste presents in a landfill context, including the processes of leachate formation and soil contamination. They will analyze the long-term environmental consequences and evaluate the limitations of landfills as a solution for e-waste.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly connects to prior modules on **waste classification** (identifying e-waste), **environmental cycles** (how toxins disrupt ecosystems), and **policy frameworks** (regulations governing landfill disposal). It sets the stage for subsequent modules on **recycling, recovery, and circular economy models** as superior alternatives.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is vital for careers in urban planning, environmental policy, waste management engineering, and public health. It empowers students to advocate for and contribute to improved municipal waste management strategies, reducing the environmental burden of landfills.
