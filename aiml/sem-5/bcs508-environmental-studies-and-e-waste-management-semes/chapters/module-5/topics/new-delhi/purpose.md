### Learning Purpose: New Delhi & E-Waste Management

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because New Delhi is a global epicenter for the informal processing and recycling of electronic waste (e-waste). Studying it provides a powerful, real-world case study of the severe environmental and health impacts of improper e-waste handling, highlighting the urgent need for sustainable management solutions.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will analyze the socio-economic drivers behind Delhi's e-waste hubs, like the infamous informal recycling market in Seelampur. They will evaluate the associated environmental contamination (soil, water, air) and public health risks for workers and communities, contrasting this with the objectives of India's E-Waste Management Rules.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This case study directly connects to previous modules on life-cycle assessment, the global e-waste trade, and policy frameworks. It synthesizes concepts of environmental justice, urban pollution, and the complex challenge of integrating informal waste sectors into formal, regulated systems.

**4. Real-world applications**
Understanding this case equips students to critically assess e-waste policy effectiveness, design awareness campaigns targeting consumer disposal habits, and propose innovative strategies for formalizing recycling sectors that protect both the environment and vulnerable labor forces.
