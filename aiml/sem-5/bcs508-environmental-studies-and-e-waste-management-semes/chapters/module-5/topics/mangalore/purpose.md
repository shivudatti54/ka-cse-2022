# Learning Purpose: Mangalore as a Case Study in E-Waste Management

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Mangalore is a critical case study due to its status as a major port city and a growing IT/industrial hub in India. This makes it a significant entry point and generator of electronic waste (e-waste). Studying Mangalore provides a localized, real-world context to understand the operational challenges, logistical frameworks, and socio-economic impacts of e-waste, moving beyond abstract theory.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will analyze the specific e-waste flow within Mangalore, including its sources (households, businesses, the port), collection mechanisms, and the role of informal and formal recycling sectors. They will evaluate the environmental and health hazards posed by current practices and assess the policies and infrastructure aimed at managing this waste stream sustainably.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly connects to prior modules on global e-waste generation, the lifecycle of electronics, toxic materials (like lead and mercury), and national policies like the E-Waste Management Rules. It synthesizes these concepts into a single, tangible geographical context, bridging the gap between global issues and local action.

**4. Real-world applications**
The knowledge gained empowers students to conduct community audits, propose improved municipal collection systems, advocate for responsible recycling initiatives, and engage in awareness campaigns. It provides a model for critically analyzing e-waste management in any urban center, making students capable of developing context-specific solutions.
