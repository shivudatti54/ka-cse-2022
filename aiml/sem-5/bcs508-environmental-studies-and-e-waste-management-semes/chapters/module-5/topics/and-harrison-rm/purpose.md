### **Learning Purpose: Harrison R.M. and E-Waste Management**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the research of Harrison R.M. (a leading environmental chemist) is crucial because it provides the scientific foundation for e-waste policy. His work on the chemical speciation, release mechanisms, and environmental pathways of hazardous heavy metals and persistent organic pollutants (POPs) from discarded electronics directly informs why e-waste is a critical global health and environmental issue, moving beyond mere awareness to a mechanistic understanding.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify the key toxic contaminants (e.g., lead, mercury, brominated flame retardants) found in e-waste as highlighted by Harrison's research. They will analyze the specific environmental and human health risks associated with the informal recycling practices common in developing nations, linking scientific data to real-world consequences.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly integrates concepts from environmental chemistry (pollutant behavior), toxicology (health impacts), and previous modules on waste management principles. It bridges the gap between pure science (Harrison's research) and applied social sciences, such as environmental policy, economics (circular economy), and international regulations like the Basel Convention.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied in formulating and critically evaluating evidence-based e-waste management strategies, designing safer recycling technologies, advocating for stronger extended producer responsibility (EPR) laws, and making informed consumer choices regarding electronics disposal and purchasing.
