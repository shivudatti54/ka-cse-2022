### Learning Purpose: E-Waste Management

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is critically important because electronic waste (e-waste) is the world's fastest-growing waste stream, posing severe environmental and public health risks due to toxic materials like lead and mercury. Proper management is essential for conserving finite resources, reducing pollution, and promoting a circular economy.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define e-waste, analyze its environmental and health impacts, and evaluate various management strategies. This includes understanding the entire lifecycle of electronics, from production to disposal, and exploring solutions like recycling, refurbishment, and extended producer responsibility (EPR).

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly connects to prior learning on pollution, resource conservation, sustainable development, and waste management hierarchies. It integrates principles from environmental science, public policy, economics, and corporate social responsibility, providing a holistic view of a complex environmental challenge.

**4. Real-world applications**
The knowledge gained is directly applicable to careers in environmental consulting, corporate sustainability, and waste management policy. Students will be equipped to make informed decisions as consumers, advocate for better recycling programs, and contribute to developing sustainable electronic product lifecycles in the professional world.
