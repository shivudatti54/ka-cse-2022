### **Learning Purpose: Oxford University Press as a Case Study in Sustainable Publishing**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
The publishing industry, including academic giants like Oxford University Press (OUP), is a significant consumer of resources (paper, ink, plastics, electronics) and a generator of waste. Analyzing OUP's policies provides a tangible, real-world framework to assess corporate environmental responsibility, sustainable supply chain management, and the specific challenge of e-waste from digital publishing platforms.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will evaluate OUP’s publicly stated environmental policies, sustainability reports, and operational practices. They will learn to critically analyze the lifecycle of published materials (from print books to e-books), identify potential e-waste streams (e-readers, tablets, servers), and assess the effectiveness of the company's waste reduction and recycling initiatives.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This case study directly connects to core module concepts like Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR), circular economy models, Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) of products, and corporate greenwashing versus genuine sustainability. It bridges the gap between theoretical waste management principles and their application in a major global industry.

**4. Real-world applications**
This analysis equips students with the skills to audit other corporations, make informed choices as consumers (e.g., print vs. digital), and propose data-driven recommendations for improving sustainability within the publishing and broader media sectors, ultimately reducing their environmental footprint.
