# Learning Purpose: Wetlands in Environmental Studies

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Wetlands are among the most productive and vital ecosystems on Earth. They are crucial for biodiversity, water purification, flood control, and carbon sequestration. Understanding their role is fundamental to addressing modern environmental challenges like water scarcity, pollution, and climate change. Their rapid degradation makes their study urgent for future conservation efforts.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to define wetlands and identify their different types (e.g., marshes, swamps). They will analyze the key ecological functions wetlands perform and the ecosystem services they provide to human society. The module will also cover the major threats to wetlands and the principles and strategies for their effective conservation and management.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly connects to prior knowledge of ecosystems, biodiversity, and the hydrologic cycle. It provides a critical foundation for subsequent modules on pollution (e.g., how wetlands filter e-waste leachates), water resource management, and climate change mitigation. It also introduces key environmental concepts like ecosystem services and sustainable management.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge is applied in careers in environmental conservation, urban planning (using wetlands for stormwater management), and policy-making. Students will be equipped to assess the health of local wetland areas, understand the environmental impact of development projects, and contribute to community-based conservation and restoration initiatives.
