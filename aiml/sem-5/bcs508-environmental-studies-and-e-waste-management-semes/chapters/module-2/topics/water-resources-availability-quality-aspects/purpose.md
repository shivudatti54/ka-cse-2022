### Learning Purpose: Water Resources – Availability & Quality Aspects

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Water is a fundamental and finite resource essential for all life and human activity. Understanding its availability and quality is critical because it is directly threatened by pollution, over-extraction, and climate change. This topic is the foundation for responsible water management and conservation, making it a core pillar of environmental studies.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will analyze the global and local distribution of freshwater resources, assess the physical, chemical, and biological indicators of water quality, and identify major pollutants and their sources. They will learn to interpret water quality data and understand the direct impact of contamination on ecosystem and human health.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is intrinsically linked to prior learning on ecosystems and provides the crucial context for subsequent modules. It directly connects to e-waste management, as improper disposal leads to heavy metal leaching (like mercury and lead) that contaminates water supplies. It also ties into climate change (altering availability) and policy/regulation.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge applies to careers in environmental consulting, public health, and water resource management. Students will be equipped to engage in community initiatives for water conservation, understand public water quality reports, and advocate for policies that protect watersheds from industrial and electronic waste pollution.
