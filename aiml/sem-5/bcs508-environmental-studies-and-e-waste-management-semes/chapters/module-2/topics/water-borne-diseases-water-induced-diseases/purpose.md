### **Learning Purpose: Waterborne & Water-Induced Diseases**

**1. Importance:** This topic is critical as these diseases are a leading cause of global morbidity and mortality, particularly in vulnerable communities. Understanding them is fundamental to public health, environmental justice, and achieving UN Sustainable Development Goals related to clean water and sanitation (SDG 6) and good health (SDG 3).

**2. Student Learning Outcomes:** Students will learn to differentiate between waterborne diseases (caused by pathogenic microorganisms in contaminated water, e.g., cholera, typhoid) and water-induced diseases (where water provides a habitat for vectors, e.g., malaria, dengue). They will analyze the environmental factors that propagate these diseases and the engineering and policy interventions used for prevention and control.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts:** This topic directly integrates knowledge from public health microbiology, hydrology, climate science (e.g., how climate change expands vector habitats), and urban planning. It is a core component of the broader "E-Waste Management" module, as improper electronic disposal can leach heavy metals and toxins into water supplies, compromising health and ecosystems.

**4. Real-World Application:** This knowledge is applied by public health officials in disease surveillance, by engineers designing water treatment and sanitation systems, by policymakers crafting regulations, and by communities advocating for and managing clean water resources, directly linking environmental health to human well-being.
