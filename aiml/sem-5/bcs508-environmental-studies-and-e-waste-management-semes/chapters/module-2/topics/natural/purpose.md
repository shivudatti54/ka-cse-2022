### Learning Purpose: Natural Resources & E-Waste

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because it examines the fundamental conflict between the consumption of finite natural resources for electronics and the unsustainable generation of electronic waste (e-waste). Understanding this link is key to developing responsible production, consumption, and disposal habits to mitigate environmental degradation and resource depletion.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to identify the specific natural resources (e.g., rare earth metals, water, fossil fuels) required to manufacture electronic devices. They will analyze the entire product lifecycle, from resource extraction to disposal, and evaluate how improper e-waste management leads to pollution and the loss of valuable materials that could be recovered through recycling.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly connects to earlier modules on ecosystems and pollution by showing how toxics from e-waste leach into soil and water. It also builds a foundation for subsequent modules on circular economy principles, sustainable product design, and policy frameworks for waste management, by highlighting the need to close the resource loop.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge applies directly to careers in environmental consulting, waste management policy, and corporate sustainability. It empowers students to make informed choices as consumers, advocate for stronger extended producer responsibility (EPR) laws, and participate in or promote community e-waste recycling and refurbishment programs.
