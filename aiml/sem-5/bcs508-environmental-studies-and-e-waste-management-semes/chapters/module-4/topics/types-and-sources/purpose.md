### Learning Purpose: Types and Sources of E-Waste

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the types and sources of electronic waste (e-waste) is fundamental to addressing one of the world's fastest-growing waste streams. Its improper disposal poses severe environmental and health risks due to toxic components like lead and mercury. This knowledge is the first critical step toward developing effective management and recycling strategies.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to categorize e-waste into distinct types (e.g., ICT equipment, consumer electronics, large appliances) and identify their primary sources (households, industries, businesses, hospitals). They will analyze the life cycle of electronic products—from manufacture to obsolescence—to understand how and why they become waste.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly connects to prior learning on waste management principles, environmental pollution, and the concept of a linear vs. circular economy. It provides the essential foundation for subsequent modules on e-waste recycling techniques, policy frameworks, and the economic opportunities within the waste management sector.

**4. Real-world applications**
This knowledge enables students to contribute to practical solutions such as designing consumer awareness campaigns, improving municipal waste collection systems, advocating for producer responsibility, and making informed personal choices about electronics consumption and disposal.
