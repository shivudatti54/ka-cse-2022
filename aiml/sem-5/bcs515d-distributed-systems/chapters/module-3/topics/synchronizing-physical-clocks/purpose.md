Learning to synchronize physical clocks is crucial in distributed systems as it enables accurate communication and coordination among nodes, ensuring that data is processed and transmitted in a timely manner. This topic matters because it directly impacts the reliability, performance, and scalability of distributed systems, particularly in applications that require precise timing, such as financial transactions, scientific simulations, and telecommunications. Real-world applications include cloud computing, distributed databases, and real-time systems. This concept also connects to other concepts, such as process synchronization, event handling, and global state management.
