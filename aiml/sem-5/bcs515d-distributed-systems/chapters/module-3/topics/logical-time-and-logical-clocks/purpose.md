**Why it Matters:**
Learning about logical time and logical clocks is crucial in distributed systems, as it enables the synchronization and coordination of processes and events across multiple nodes and machines. It ensures that all nodes agree on the current state of the system, preventing inconsistencies and errors.

**Real-world Applications:**
Logical time and logical clocks have significant real-world applications in distributed systems, such as distributed databases, cloud computing, and big data processing. They are used in synchronization protocols like Paxos and Raft, which enable reliable communication and consensus among multiple nodes.

**Connection to Other Concepts:**
Logical time and logical clocks are closely related to other important concepts in distributed systems, such as global states, events, and process states. They are also intertwined with concepts like consistency models, replication protocols, and fault tolerance, all of which are essential for building reliable and efficient distributed systems.
