# Logical Time and Logical Clocks

=====================================

## Definitions and Concepts

- **Logical Time**: A theoretical concept that assumes a linear, one-way flow of time, independent of physical clocks.
- **Logical Clock**: A theoretical clock that represents the sequence of events in a system, without considering the physical clock's accuracy.
- **Synchronization**: The process of aligning logical clocks across a distributed system.

## Formulas and Theorems

- **Causal Order**: A sequence of events that can be determined based on their causal relationships.
- **Temporal Logic**: A formal system for expressing and reasoning about temporal relationships between events.
- **Lamport's Bakery Algorithm**: A synchronization algorithm that ensures logical clocks are synchronized.

## Key Points

- **Causes and Effects**: A cause precedes its effect in time.
- **Global State**: The state of a system at a particular instant in time.
- **Tolerance**: The allowed deviation of a process clock from the system clock.
- **Local Clock**: A clock maintained by a process to keep track of its own time.
- **Global Clock**: A clock that represents the system's overall time.

## Important Theorems

- **Harrison's Rule**: A synchronization algorithm that ensures logical clocks are synchronized.
- **Lamport's Bakery Theorem**: A theorem that proves the correctness of the Lamport's Bakery Algorithm.

## Key Concepts

- **Temporal Coupling**: The relationship between the events of two processes.
- **Temporal Separation**: The relationship between the events of two processes that are not directly coupled.
- **Temporal Relativity**: The concept that the time experienced by a process is relative to its own clock.
