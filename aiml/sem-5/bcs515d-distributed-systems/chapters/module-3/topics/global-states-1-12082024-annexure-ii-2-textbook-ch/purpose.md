Learning about global states in distributed systems is crucial as it enables the management of shared resources and synchronization of processes across multiple nodes, ensuring efficient and reliable operation of complex systems. This topic matters because it helps prevent inconsistencies and deadlocks in distributed systems, where multiple processes interact and share resources.

Real-world applications of global states include operating systems, databases, and cloud computing platforms, where multiple nodes need to cooperate and synchronize to provide a unified and coherent experience.

This concept connects to other ideas in distributed systems, such as synchronization, communication, and concurrency, as it provides a framework for managing the interactions between processes and resources in a distributed environment.
