### **Learning Purpose: Distributed Systems (Semester-End Examination)**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Distributed systems form the backbone of modern computing, powering everything from cloud platforms and social networks to financial trading systems. Understanding their principles is crucial because centralized solutions are often insufficient for today's scalability, fault tolerance, and performance demands. Mastery of this topic is fundamental for any software engineer or architect building large-scale, reliable applications.

**2. What will students learn?**
This module equips students to analyze and evaluate the core challenges and solutions in distributed systems. You will learn to articulate the trade-offs in design choices concerning consistency models (e.g., CAP theorem), replication, fault tolerance, and consensus protocols. The examination will assess your ability to critically apply these concepts to solve complex, real-world distributed computing problems.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This knowledge integrates and builds upon previous concepts in computer networks (communication), operating systems (concurrency, resource management), databases (transactions, consistency), and algorithms. It synthesizes these areas to address the unique problems that arise when systems are scaled across multiple, independent nodes.

**4. Real-world applications**
The principles learned are directly applied in the technologies we use daily. This includes cloud computing services (AWS, Azure), distributed databases (Cassandra, MongoDB), content delivery networks (CDNs), blockchain networks, and large-scale web applications like Google Search and Netflix, which rely on distributed systems for resilience and global reach.
