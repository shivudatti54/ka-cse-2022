### Learning Purpose: Characterization of Distributed Systems

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the fundamental characterization of distributed systems is crucial because it defines the core challenges and design principles behind modern computing infrastructure. This knowledge forms the foundation for building and managing scalable, reliable, and efficient systems that power the internet, cloud services, and major applications.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the key defining characteristics that distinguish distributed systems from centralized ones, including concurrency, lack of a global clock, independent failure of components, and geographic distribution. They will be introduced to the core design goals that drive their architecture: transparency, openness, scalability, and fault tolerance.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic provides the essential vocabulary and conceptual framework for all subsequent modules. It directly connects to concepts like communication protocols (Module 2), synchronization (Module 3), replication (Module 4), and consistency models (Module 5), as these are all mechanisms designed to achieve the goals identified during characterization.

**4. Real-world applications**
The principles learned here are directly applied in real-world technologies such as cloud computing platforms (AWS, Azure), large-scale web services (Google Search, Netflix), content delivery networks (CDNs), and blockchain networks, all of which are inherently distributed.
