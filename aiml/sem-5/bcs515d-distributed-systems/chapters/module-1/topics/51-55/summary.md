# 5.1-5.5: Characterization of Distributed Systems

=============================================

### 5.1: Definition and Characteristics of Distributed Systems

---

- A distributed system is a collection of independent computers that appear to be a single cohesive system.
- Characteristics:
  - Decentralization: No single point of failure.
  - Autonomy: Each node operates independently.
  - Distributed control: No centralized control.
  - Shared resources: Access to shared resources.

### 5.2: Benefits of Distributed Systems

---

- **Fault tolerance**: If one node fails, others can continue to operate.
- **Scalability**: Add or remove nodes as needed.
- **Improved responsiveness**: Resources can be allocated dynamically.

### 5.3: Types of Distributed Systems

---

- **Homogeneous**: All nodes are identical.
- **Heterogeneous**: Nodes have different capabilities.

### 5.4: Distributed System Topologies

---

- **Bus topology**: All nodes are connected to a central bus.
- **Star topology**: All nodes are connected to a central node.
- **Mesh topology**: Each node is connected to every other node.

### 5.5: Communication in Distributed Systems

---

- **Message passing**: Nodes send and receive messages.
- **Shared memory**: Nodes share a common memory space.
- **Distributed file systems**: Nodes store and share files.

### Important Formulas and Theorems

---

- **Paxos algorithm**: Ensures consistency in distributed systems.
- **Raft consensus algorithm**: Ensures consistency and fault tolerance.
- **Optimistic concurrency control**: Ensures consistency in distributed systems.

### Definitions

---

- **Client-server architecture**: A decentralized system where clients request services from servers.
- **Peer-to-peer architecture**: A decentralized system where nodes are equal and interact with each other directly.
