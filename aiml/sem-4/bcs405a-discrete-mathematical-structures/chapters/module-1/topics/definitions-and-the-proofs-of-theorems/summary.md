# **Definitions and Proofs of Theorems**

## **I. Definitions**

- **Definition**: A statement that defines a term or a concept.
- **Axiom**: A statement that is assumed to be true without proof.
- **Postulate**: A statement that is assumed to be true and used as a basis for further reasoning.
- **Theorem**: A statement that has been rigorously proved to be true.

## **II. The Process of Proofs**

- **Direct Proof**: A proof that shows the truth of a statement by direct reasoning.
- **Indirect Proof (Proof by Contradiction)**: A proof that shows the truth of a statement by assuming its negation and showing that it leads to a contradiction.
- **Proof by Mathematical Induction**: A proof that shows the truth of a statement for all positive integers.

## **III. Important Theorems**

- **Axiom of Induction**: If a statement is true for 1, and if it is true for all positive integers k, then it is true for k+1.
- **Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic**: Every positive integer can be represented uniquely as a product of prime numbers.

## **IV. Important Formulas**

- **Modus Ponens**: If P is true and Q is true and P implies Q, then Q is true.
- **Modus Tollens**: If P is true and Q is false and P implies Q, then P is false.

## **V. Key Concepts**

- **Existence**: The ability to show that something exists.
- **Uniqueness**: The ability to show that something exists in only one way.
- **Equivalence**: The ability to show that two statements are the same.

## **VI. Quick Tips**

- Always read the statement carefully before trying to prove it.
- Use diagrams and charts to help visualize the proof.
- Check your work by plugging in examples and checking your answer.
