# Quantifiers

## Introduction

Quantifiers are logical operators used to specify the scope of variables in a statement. They are used to express "for all" and "there exists".

## Definitions

- **Universal Quantifier**: ∀ (for all)
- **Existential Quantifier**: ∃ (there exists)

## Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Universal Quantifier**: ∀x ∈ S, P(x) (for all x in S, P(x) is true)
- **Existential Quantifier**: ∃x ∈ S, P(x) (there exists an x in S, P(x) is true)

## Quantifier Rules

- **Universal Quantification**:
  - Commutativity: ∀x ∈ S, P(x) = P(y) ∀y ∈ S
  - Associativity: ∀x ∈ S, P(x) = ∀y ∈ S, P(y) = ∀z ∈ S, P(z)
  - Distributivity: ∀x ∈ S, P(x) ∧ Q(x) = ∀x ∈ S, P(x) ∧ ∀y ∈ S, Q(y)
- **Existential Quantification**:
  - Commutativity: ∃x ∈ S, P(x) = P(y) ∃y ∈ S
  - Associativity: ∃x ∈ S, P(x) = ∃y ∈ S, P(y) = ∃z ∈ S, P(z)
  - Distributivity: ∃x ∈ S, P(x) ∧ Q(x) = ∃x ∈ S, P(x) ∧ ∃y ∈ S, Q(y)

## Important Theorems

- **Tarski's Generalization Theorem**: ∀x ∈ S, P(x) ∧ ∀x ∈ S, P(x) → P(x)
- **Monadic Theorem**: ∀x ∈ S, P(x) → ∃x ∈ S, P(x)

## Key Points

- Quantifiers are fundamental in mathematical logic and are used to express "for all" and "there exists" statements.
- Universal quantification is commutative, associative, and distributive.
- Existential quantification is commutative, associative, and distributive.
- Tarski's Generalization Theorem and Monadic Theorem are important results in quantifier logic.
