# **Basic Connectives and Truth Tables**

### Introduction

---

In discrete mathematical structures, logic plays a crucial role in understanding the relationships between statements. Basic connectives are used to form complex statements from simpler ones, and truth tables provide a systematic way to evaluate the truth of these statements. In this section, we will explore the basic connectives and their corresponding truth tables.

### Basic Connectives

---

- **Conjunction (AND)**
  - Definition: Two statements are connected by AND if both statements must be true for the compound statement to be true.
  - Symbol: ∧
  - Truth Table:

    | A   | B   | A ∧ B |
    | --- | --- | ----- |
    | T   | T   | T     |
    | T   | F   | F     |
    | F   | T   | F     |
    | F   | F   | F     |

- **Disjunction (OR)**
  - Definition: Two statements are connected by OR if at least one statement must be true for the compound statement to be true.
  - Symbol: ∨
  - Truth Table:

    | A   | B   | A ∨ B |
    | --- | --- | ----- |
    | T   | T   | T     |
    | T   | F   | T     |
    | F   | T   | T     |
    | F   | F   | F     |

- **Negation (NOT)**
  - Definition: A statement is connected by NOT if it is false.
  - Symbol: ¬
  - Truth Table:

    | A   | ¬A  |
    | --- | --- |
    | T   | F   |
    | F   | T   |

- **Exclusive Disjunction (XOR)**
  - Definition: Two statements are connected by XOR if exactly one statement must be true for the compound statement to be true.
  - Symbol: ⊕
  - Truth Table:

    | A   | B   | A ⊕ B |
    | --- | --- | ----- |
    | T   | T   | F     |
    | T   | F   | T     |
    | F   | T   | T     |
    | F   | F   | F     |

### Examples

---

- Suppose we have two statements: "It is sunny" and "It is warm." We can connect them using AND, which would give us the statement "It is sunny and warm."
- We can also connect them using OR, which would give us the statement "It is sunny or warm."
- If we negate the statement "It is sunny," we get the statement "It is not sunny."
- If we use XOR to connect the statements "It is sunny" and "It is warm," we get the statement "Either it is sunny or it is warm, but not both."

### Conclusion

---

In this section, we have explored the basic connectives AND, OR, NOT, and XOR, and their corresponding truth tables. We have also provided examples of how to use these connectives to form complex statements. Understanding basic connectives and truth tables is essential for building more complex logical expressions and evaluating their truthfulness.
