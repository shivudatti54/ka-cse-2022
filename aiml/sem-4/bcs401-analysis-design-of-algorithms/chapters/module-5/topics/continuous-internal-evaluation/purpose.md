### Learning Purpose: Continuous Internal Evaluation

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) is crucial as it shifts the focus from a single high-stakes exam to an ongoing assessment of a student's grasp of complex algorithmic concepts. This approach provides regular feedback, identifies learning gaps early, and reduces exam pressure, fostering a deeper and more consistent understanding throughout the module.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to engage consistently with the curriculum through various CIE components like quizzes, assignments, and presentations. They will develop the ability to apply algorithmic design techniques (e.g., divide-and-conquer, dynamic programming) to solve problems regularly, thereby reinforcing their analytical and problem-solving skills in a sustained manner.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
CIE directly connects to and reinforces all prior modules (e.g., asymptotic analysis, sorting, greedy methods) and upcoming ones (e.g., NP-completeness). Regular assessments ensure that foundational knowledge is solid, which is essential for understanding more advanced topics. It integrates theoretical knowledge with practical coding and analysis skills.

**4. Real-world applications**
The iterative process of CIE mirrors the agile development cycles and code review processes used in the software industry. Regularly designing, analyzing, and refining algorithms prepares students for real-world scenarios where solutions are developed, tested, and improved upon continuously, not just in a single final effort.
