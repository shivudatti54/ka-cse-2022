# Analysis & Design of Algorithms - Module 5: Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE)

## Introduction

The **Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE)** is a crucial component of the  curriculum designed to assess your understanding and progress *throughout* the semester, rather than relying solely on a single final examination. For a challenging subject like the Analysis & Design of Algorithms, CIE provides a structured way to gauge your grasp of fundamental concepts, problem-solving skills, and ability to apply algorithmic techniques. A strong performance in CIE not only secures a significant portion of your final grade but also ensures you are thoroughly prepared for the end-semester exam.

## Core Concepts of CIE

The CIE for ADA is typically divided into two components, each carrying 50% of the total CIE marks:

1.  **CIE-1 (First Internal Assessment Test):**
    *   **Weightage:** 50% of CIE marks.
    *   **Syllabus Coverage:** Usually covers content from the first half of the syllabus (e.g., Modules 1, 2, and 3). This often includes:
        *   Introduction to Algorithms and Analysis (Time & Space Complexity)
        *   Divide and Conquer paradigms (e.g., Merge Sort, Quick Sort)
        *   Greedy Methods (e.g., Knapsack, Job Sequencing, Dijkstra's Algorithm)

2.  **CIE-2 (Second Internal Assessment Test):**
    *   **Weightage:** 50% of CIE marks.
    *   **Syllabus Coverage:** Focuses on the latter half of the syllabus (e.g., Modules 4 and 5). Since this content is for Module 5, your CIE-2 will likely cover:
        *   **Dynamic Programming:** Core principle of overlapping subproblems and optimal substructure.
        *   Key problems like the **0/1 Knapsack Problem**, **Multistage Graphs**, **All-Pairs Shortest Paths (Floyd-Warshall)**, and **Traveling Salesperson Problem (TSP)**.
        *   **Backtracking:** Concept and applications, e.g., **n-Queens Problem**.
        *   **Branch and Bound:** General method and its application to problems like the **0/1 Knapsack**.

### How It's Scored

Your final CIE marks are calculated as the average of CIE-1 and CIE-2. For example:
*   CIE-1 Score: 42/50
*   CIE-2 Score: 45/50
*   **Final CIE Marks:** (42 + 45) / 2 = **43.5/50**

These 50 marks are then scaled down to contribute to your final semester grade. Scoring well in both tests is paramount.

## Examples & Application

The questions in CIE tests are designed to test both theoretical understanding and practical application. For Module 5 topics, expect questions like:

**Example 1: Dynamic Programming (0/1 Knapsack)**
*   **Question:** "Solve the 0/1 Knapsack problem for n=4, profits p = {10, 12, 15, 8}, weights w = {2, 3, 5, 4}, and knapsack capacity W = 7 using the dynamic programming approach. Show the complete DP table and trace the solution to find the selected items."
*   **This tests:** Your ability to apply the DP tabulation method step-by-step.

**Example 2: Backtracking (n-Queens)**
*   **Question:** "Apply the Backtracking algorithm to solve the 4-Queens problem. Show the state-space tree and indicate the promising and non-promising nodes until you find a solution."
*   **This tests:** Your understanding of the backtracking process and the concept of pruning non-promising branches.

**Example 3: Conceptual**
*   **Question:** "Differentiate between Greedy Method and Dynamic Programming. Explain with respect to the Knapsack problem variants."
*   **This tests:** Your deep conceptual understanding of how these two algorithm design techniques differ.

## Key Points & Summary

*   **Purpose:** CIE is a continuous assessment tool to evaluate your ongoing learning in the ADA course.
*   **Structure:** Consists of two tests (CIE-1 and CIE-2), each worth 50% of the total CIE marks.
*   **Focus of Module 5 (CIE-2):** Primarily on **Dynamic Programming**, **Backtracking**, and **Branch and Bound** algorithms.
*   **Preparation Strategy:**
    1.  **Understand Concepts:** Don't just memorize steps. Understand *why* Dynamic Programming uses a table or *how* Backtracking prunes the search space.
    2.  **Practice Problems:** Manually solve standard problems (Knapsack, TSP, n-Queens) on paper. Drawing the DP table or state-space tree is crucial.
    3.  **Review Past Papers:** Familiarize yourself with the question pattern and difficulty level of internal tests.
    4.  **Clarify Doubts:** Use CIE as a feedback mechanism to identify weak areas and clarify them before the final exam.

A strong performance in CIE, especially for the algorithm-intensive Module 5, lays a solid foundation for your success in the semester-end examination.
