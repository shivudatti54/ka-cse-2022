### 1. Why is this topic important?
Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) is a vital component of the academic framework. It is important because it shifts the focus from a single high-stakes final exam to a more comprehensive assessment of a student's understanding throughout the semester. This allows for the consistent monitoring of progress in a challenging subject like Group Theory, identifies learning gaps early, and encourages continuous engagement with the material.

### 2. What will students learn?
Students will learn to demonstrate their understanding of core Group Theory concepts—such as groups, subgroups, cyclic groups, and cosets—through a variety of assessments. These may include periodic tests, quizzes, assignments, and problem-solving sessions. The process helps them develop the ability to apply definitions and theorems to solve problems consistently and reinforces learning through regular practice.

### 3. How does it connect to other concepts?
The concepts assessed in this module, like algebraic structures and symmetry operations, are foundational to many advanced topics in discrete mathematics and computer science. Successfully navigating this evaluation ensures students have the necessary proficiency to grasp subsequent subjects, including cryptography, coding theory, and algorithm design, which all rely heavily on abstract algebraic principles.

### 4. Real-world applications
The problem-solving and analytical skills honed through mastering Group Theory are directly applicable in real-world domains. These include designing secure cryptographic systems for data encryption, analyzing molecular symmetry in chemistry (crystallography), and developing error-correcting codes used in digital communication and data storage. CIE ensures students can reliably apply this theoretical knowledge.
