### 1. Why is this topic important?
Group Theory is a fundamental cornerstone of modern algebra and discrete mathematics. It provides a formal framework for understanding symmetry, structure, and abstract operations, which are central to higher mathematics and numerous technological fields. Mastering this topic is crucial for developing the abstract reasoning skills necessary for advanced computer science and mathematics.

### 2. What will students learn?
Students will learn the core axioms of a group (closure, associativity, identity, and inverses). They will explore key concepts like subgroups, cyclic groups, and permutation groups. The goal is to understand how to analyze and classify algebraic structures based on their properties and symmetries.

### 3. How does it connect to other concepts?
This knowledge directly builds upon sets, relations, and functions from earlier modules. It provides the essential algebraic foundation for more advanced topics like rings and fields, cryptography, and coding theory. It also connects to linear algebra (through matrix groups) and computer science (via algebraic automata theory).

### 4. Real-world applications
Group theory has powerful applications in cryptography (e.g., the RSA algorithm relies on properties of multiplicative groups), computer graphics (for modeling rotations and symmetries), quantum mechanics (describing particle spin), and solving the Rubik's Cube. It is the language of symmetry used across science and engineering.
