### Learning Purpose: Function Composition and Inverse Functions

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is fundamental because it provides the tools to build complex functions from simpler ones and to reverse computational processes. Understanding composition and inverses is critical for analyzing the structure of systems, designing algorithms, and working with data transformations, forming a cornerstone of higher mathematics and computer science.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to combine functions through composition (`f(g(x))`) and determine when a function is invertible. They will develop the skills to find the inverse of a bijective function and verify the relationship `f⁻¹(f(x)) = x`. This includes understanding the conditions for invertibility, such as a function being one-to-one and onto.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This knowledge directly builds upon the previous study of relations, functions, and their properties (injectivity, surjectivity). It is a prerequisite for core concepts in linear algebra (matrix inverses), calculus (inverse trigonometric functions, chain rule), cryptography (encryption/decryption functions), and database theory (query operations).

**4. Real-world applications**
Composition models sequential processes, such as chaining software functions or manufacturing assembly lines. Inverse functions are essential for cryptography (decrypting a message), computer graphics (applying and reversing transformations), and data retrieval (reconstructing original input from output).
