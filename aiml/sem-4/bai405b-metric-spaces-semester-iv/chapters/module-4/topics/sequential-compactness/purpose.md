### Learning Purpose: Sequential Compactness in Metric Spaces

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Sequential compactness is a fundamental pillar of analysis. It provides a powerful and intuitive framework for proving the existence of important mathematical objects, like limits and extrema, especially in infinite-dimensional contexts. Understanding this concept is crucial for advancing in higher mathematics, particularly in real analysis, functional analysis, and topology.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the formal definition of a sequentially compact set and its equivalence to compactness in metric spaces. They will develop the skill to prove that a set is compact by examining its sequences and to use the Bolzano-Weierstrass property to extract convergent subsequences. This includes applying these techniques to solve problems involving continuity and convergence.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly connects to and unifies previous concepts. It relies on an understanding of convergence, Cauchy sequences, and completeness. Most importantly, it is logically equivalent to the open-cover definition of compactness *in metric spaces*, bridging a crucial gap between sequential and topological reasoning. It also provides essential tools for proving key results like the Extreme Value Theorem.

**4. Real-world applications**
The principles of sequential compactness are vital in applied mathematics for establishing the existence of solutions. They are used in numerical analysis to ensure convergence of approximation schemes, in optimization theory to guarantee that a continuous function achieves its minimum on a set, and in the calculus of variations to prove the existence of minimizers.
