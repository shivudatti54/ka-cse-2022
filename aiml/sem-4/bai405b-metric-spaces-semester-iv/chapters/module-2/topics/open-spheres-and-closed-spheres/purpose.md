### Learning Purpose: Open & Closed Spheres in Metric Spaces

**1. Importance:** This topic is foundational as open and closed spheres (or balls) are the fundamental building blocks for defining nearly all topological concepts in metric spaces. They provide the intuitive geometric interpretation of concepts like proximity, distance, and boundaries, which are central to analysis.

**2. Student Learning Outcomes:** Students will formally define and visualize open spheres `B(x₀, r)` and closed spheres `B[x₀, r]`. They will learn to distinguish between them based on the inclusion of their boundary points. This includes developing the skill to determine whether a point is interior, exterior, or on the boundary of a sphere.

**3. Connections:** This knowledge is a direct prerequisite for understanding more complex ideas:
*   **Open/Closed Sets:** These are defined using unions and intersections of open spheres.
*   **Topology:** The collection of all open sets (the topology) is generated from these basic open spheres.
*   **Continuity & Limits:** The epsilon-delta definition of continuity relies entirely on the concept of open spheres.

**4. Applications:** Beyond pure mathematics, these concepts underpin algorithms in machine learning (e.g., k-NN classification within a radius), computer graphics (distance fields and collision detection), and physics (defining neighborhoods in phase space).
