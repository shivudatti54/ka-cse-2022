### Learning Purpose: Boundary of a Set

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Understanding the boundary of a set is a fundamental concept in metric spaces and topology. It is crucial for precisely defining and distinguishing between open and closed sets, which form the bedrock of continuity, limits, and convergence—key ideas in analysis and many applied fields.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the formal definition of the boundary (or frontier) of a set `A` in a metric space as the set of points whose neighborhoods intersect both `A` and its complement. They will develop the skill to identify and prove whether a point is a boundary point and explore the properties of the boundary, such as it being a closed set.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is deeply interconnected with previously learned concepts. The boundary is defined using the closure and interior of a set (`∂A = Ā \ A°`), directly linking these ideas. It is essential for characterizing open sets (which have no boundary points) and closed sets (which contain all their boundary points), paving the way for studying connectedness and compactness.

**4. Real-world applications**
The concept of a boundary has direct applications in computer graphics and image processing for edge detection and region identification. In geography and GIS, it is used to define territorial borders. In optimization and control theory, understanding the boundary of a feasible region is critical for finding optimal solutions.
