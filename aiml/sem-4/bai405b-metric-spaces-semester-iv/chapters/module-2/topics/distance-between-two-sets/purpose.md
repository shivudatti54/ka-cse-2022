### Learning Purpose: Distance Between Two Sets

**1. Importance:**  
This topic is fundamental in analysis and topology as it generalizes the concept of distance from points to sets. It is a crucial tool for defining and working with key notions like compactness, connectedness, and continuity in a broader context.

**2. Student Learning Outcomes:**  
Students will learn the formal definition of the distance between two subsets of a metric space, \( d(A, B) = \inf\{d(a, b) : a \in A, b \in B\} \). They will analyze conditions under which this distance is zero, particularly when sets are disjoint, and explore its properties, such as its relationship with closures and the triangle inequality for sets.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts:**  
This concept directly builds upon the definition of a metric and distance between points. It is intrinsically linked to the ideas of closed sets, limit points, and the closure of a set. Understanding this distance is a prerequisite for studying more advanced topics like separation axioms, Hausdorff distance, and complete metric spaces.

**4. Real-World Applications:**  
The principle is used in computational geometry for collision detection in robotics and computer graphics. It is also applied in optimization problems to find the minimal distance between two feasible sets and in data analysis for measuring the separation between distinct clusters of data points.
