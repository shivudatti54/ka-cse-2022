### Introduction

The concept of 8 hours has been a widely debated topic in various fields, including economics, psychology, and productivity. The idea that humans can focus and be productive for a specific period, typically 8 hours, has its roots in historical context. In this module, we will delve into the historical context, modern developments, and applications of the 8-hour workday.

### Historical Context

The 8-hour workday has its origins in the Industrial Revolution. In the late 18th century, factory owners and managers began to recognize the importance of regular working hours to increase productivity and reduce worker fatigue. The 8-hour workday was initially seen as a compromise between the demands of the factory owners and the workers.

In 1844, the first 8-hour workday was implemented in the United States, with the passage of the 10-hour day law in Massachusetts. This law limited the workday to 10 hours, with a 30-minute break in between. However, this law was later repealed in 1850.

It wasn't until the early 20th century that the 8-hour workday became a standard in many countries. In 1914, the American Federation of Labor (AFL) advocated for an 8-hour workday, which eventually became a national standard in the United States.

### Modern Developments

The 8-hour workday has undergone significant changes over the years. With the rise of the digital age, remote work, and the gig economy, the traditional 8-hour workday has become less relevant. Many companies now adopt flexible work arrangements, allowing employees to work longer hours or shorter days.

In 2019, Microsoft announced that it would adopt a 4-day workweek, where employees would work 35 hours a week. Similarly, Amazon has implemented a program called "30 hours and off," where employees can work up to 30 hours a week and receive full benefits.

### Applications

The 8-hour workday has numerous applications in various fields:

#### **Economics**

- The 8-hour workday is widely considered the optimal work period for maximizing productivity.
- Research has shown that productivity peaks around 8 hours of work, with subsequent hours leading to decreased productivity.
- The 8-hour workday has been shown to reduce absenteeism, turnover, and health issues.

#### **Psychology**

- The 8-hour workday is closely tied to the concept of the "ultradian rhythm," which refers to the natural cycles of productivity and rest.
- Research has shown that the human brain can focus for up to 8 hours before it needs a break.
- The 8-hour workday can help reduce stress and improve overall mental health.

#### **Productivity**

- The 8-hour workday is widely adopted by many companies, including Apple, Google, and Facebook.
- Research has shown that the 8-hour workday can lead to increased productivity, creativity, and innovation.
- The 8-hour workday can also help reduce burnout and improve work-life balance.
