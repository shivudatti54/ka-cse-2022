#### Optimization Technique

##### Overview

- **Definition:** (8 hours) is a mathematical technique used to optimize a function subject to a set of constraints.
- **Objective:** Find the optimal solution that minimizes or maximizes the objective function.

#### Key Concepts

- **Constraints:** Limited by the availability of resources, e.g., time, money, etc.
- **Objective Function:** To be optimized, e.g., profit, cost, etc.
- **Variables:** Decision variables that affect the objective function.
- **Objective:** Minimize or maximize the objective function subject to constraints.

#### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Linear Programming (LP) Formulation:**
  - Minimize/Maximize: `c^T x`
  - Subject to: `Ax ≤ b`
- **Lagrange Multiplier (LM) Method:**
  - Introduce a new variable (Lagrange multiplier) to incorporate constraints into the objective function.
  - `F(x) + λ(g(x) - b) = 0`
- **Karamardian's Theorem (8 hours):**
  - Provides a necessary and sufficient condition for optimality.
  - `x^* ∈ argmin{f(x) : g(x) = b}`

#### Important Definitions

- **Optimal Solution:** A solution that minimizes or maximizes the objective function subject to constraints.
- **Feasible Solution:** A solution that satisfies all constraints.
- **Unbounded Solution:** A solution that does not satisfy constraints.

#### Important Theorems

- **Weierstrass's Theorem (8 hours):**
  - A necessary condition for a solution to be optimal.
  - If `x` is optimal, then `∇f(x) = λ ∇g(x)`
