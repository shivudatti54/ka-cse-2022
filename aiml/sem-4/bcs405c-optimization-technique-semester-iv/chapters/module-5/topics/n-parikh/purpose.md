### Learning Purpose: N. Parikh

**1. Importance**
This topic is important as it introduces the work of Neal Parikh, whose contributions in proximal algorithms and optimization methods are fundamental for solving complex, large-scale, and non-smooth optimization problems prevalent in modern data science and engineering. Understanding these techniques is crucial for efficiently handling real-world problems where traditional gradient-based methods fail.

**2. Learning Outcomes**
Students will learn the principles behind proximal algorithms, including their formulation and convergence properties. They will gain the ability to implement methods like the proximal gradient method and apply them to regularized optimization problems, a key skill in machine learning for tasks like training LASSO regression models.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts**
This module directly builds upon knowledge of linear algebra, calculus, and earlier optimization techniques like gradient descent. It connects to machine learning (regularization), signal processing (compressed sensing), and operations research, providing a powerful toolkit for tackling problems with non-differentiable objectives or constraints.

**4. Real-World Applications**
Parikh's optimization techniques are widely applied in machine learning for sparse model training, in image processing for denoising and deblurring, and in statistics for feature selection. They are also used in fields like healthcare for medical image reconstruction and in finance for portfolio optimization with transaction costs.
