# Learning Purpose: Sequential Search (3-Point & Fibonacci Search)

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is fundamental because it introduces powerful and efficient numerical methods for finding the minimum of a unimodal function when an initial interval of uncertainty is known. Unlike calculus-based methods, these direct search techniques are robust and do not require derivative information, making them applicable to a wider range of practical problems, including those with complex or non-differentiable objective functions.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the underlying principles, algorithms, and convergence characteristics of two classic sequential search methods: the 3-point (equal interval) search and the Fibonacci search. They will understand how to iteratively reduce the interval of uncertainty to pinpoint the optimum and will be able to compare the efficiency and number of function evaluations required by each strategy.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module builds directly on the foundation of unimodality and bracketing the minimum. It provides a concrete introduction to iterative optimization algorithms, setting the stage for more advanced techniques like Golden Section Search and gradient-based methods covered later in the course. It reinforces core concepts of algorithm efficiency and convergence.

**4. Real-world applications**
These methods are applied in engineering design (e.g., optimizing material thickness or component length), econometrics for parameter calibration, and manufacturing process optimization (e.g., finding the optimal temperature or pressure that minimizes cost or maximizes yield) where experiments or simulations are expensive and each function evaluation must be minimized.
