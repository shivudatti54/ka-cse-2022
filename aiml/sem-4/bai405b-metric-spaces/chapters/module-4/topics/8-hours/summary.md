# Compactness

### Definitions

- **Compact space**: A metric space in which every open cover has a finite subcover.
- **Complete metric space**: A metric space in which every Cauchy sequence converges to a point in the space.

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Bolzano-Weierstrass Theorem**: Every bounded sequence in a metric space has a convergent subsequence.
- **Heine-Borel Theorem**: A compact metric space is complete.
- **Tychonoff's Theorem**: The product of compact metric spaces is compact.

### Key Points

- A metric space is compact if and only if it is complete and totally bounded.
- A sequence is Cauchy if and only if it is bounded and has a convergent subsequence.
- Every metric space is compact if it is complete and totally bounded.
- The Bolzano-Weierstrass Theorem implies the Heine-Borel Theorem.
- Tychonoff's Theorem states that the product of compact metric spaces is compact.

### Important Results

- **Compactness implies completeness**: A compact metric space is complete.
- **Completeness implies compactness**: A complete metric space is compact if and only if it is totally bounded.
- **Tychonoff's Theorem**: The product of compact metric spaces is compact.
