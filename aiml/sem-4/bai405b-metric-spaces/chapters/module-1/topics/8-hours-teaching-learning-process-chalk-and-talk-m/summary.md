### Teaching-Learning Process: Chalk and Talk Method / PowerPoint Presentation

#### METRIC SPACES Module, Theory of Sets

**Teaching-Learning Process:**

- **Chalk and Talk Method:**
  - Pros: Engaging, interactive, and hands-on experience
  - Cons: Limited space, time constraints, and instructor's ability to convey complex concepts
- **PowerPoint Presentation:**
  - Pros: Visual aids, easily accessible, and convenient for large classes
  - Cons: Limited engagement, lack of interactivity, and potential for distractions

**Key Points:**

- **Chalk and Talk Method:**
  - Definition: Instructor-led presentation using chalk and blackboard
  - Formula/Definition: Not applicable
  - Theorem: None
- **PowerPoint Presentation:**
  - Definition: Instructor-led presentation using PowerPoint software
  - Key Features:
    - Visual aids (diagrams, images, charts)
    - Interactive elements (hyperlinks, quizzes)
    - Time-saving and convenient for large classes
  - Important Formulas/Definitions:
    - None specific to this method
  - Important Theorems:
    - None specific to this method

**Revision Notes:**

- Emphasize the importance of engaging students and conveying complex concepts effectively
- Highlight the benefits and limitations of both methods
- Focus on developing effective teaching skills, including preparation, delivery, and assessment
