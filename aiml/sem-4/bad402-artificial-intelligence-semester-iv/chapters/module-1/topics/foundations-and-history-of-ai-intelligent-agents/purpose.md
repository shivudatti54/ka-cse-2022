### Learning Purpose: Foundations and History of AI & Intelligent Agents

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic provides the essential groundwork for the entire field of AI. Understanding its history, foundational concepts, and the role of intelligent agents is crucial for appreciating how modern AI systems are conceived, designed, and evaluated. It establishes a common vocabulary and a historical context, separating AI myths from its practical realities and scientific principles.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the key philosophical ideas and historical milestones that shaped AI. They will define intelligence from an AI perspective and understand the characteristics of rational agents. The module will cover different types of agents and their environments, introducing the fundamental concept of the Perception-Action cycle and how agents are evaluated using performance measures.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This foundation is directly connected to all subsequent AI concepts. The agent paradigm is the central framework for studying problem-solving, search algorithms, knowledge representation, reasoning, machine learning, and robotics. Understanding an agent's environment (e.g., fully/partially observable) dictates which AI techniques (e.g., neural networks, rule-based systems) are most appropriate to employ.

**4. Real-world applications**
The intelligent agent model is universal. It applies to simple vacuum-cleaning robots, complex recommendation systems (e.g., Netflix, Amazon), autonomous vehicles perceiving their environment, and virtual assistants like Siri or Alexa. Every AI system can be analyzed as an agent interacting with its world to achieve goals.
