### **Learning Purpose: Representing and Using Domain Knowledge**

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Domain knowledge is the specific, expert information a system needs to reason about a particular problem area (e.g., medical diagnosis, financial forecasting). Without a structured way to represent this knowledge, an AI system cannot understand or operate effectively within its intended domain. This topic is therefore fundamental to building intelligent, expert systems that go beyond simple data processing to make informed decisions.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core methods for formally encoding human expertise into a machine-readable format. This includes studying key knowledge representation (KR) techniques such as ontologies, semantic networks, frames, and logic-based systems (e.g., propositional and first-order logic). They will also learn how AI systems utilize this knowledge through inference mechanisms like forward and backward chaining to draw new conclusions and solve complex problems.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds upon earlier concepts of intelligent agents and search algorithms. It provides the "knowledge" that allows search-based problem-solving to be efficient and targeted. Furthermore, it is a prerequisite for more advanced topics like expert systems, natural language processing (which requires vast amounts of structured world knowledge), and knowledge graphs, which are essential for modern information retrieval and recommendation engines.

**4. Real-world applications**
The principles of knowledge representation are applied in numerous fields. These include:
*   **Expert Systems:** Such as medical diagnosis tools (e.g., MYCIN) and technical support troubleshooters.
*   **Semantic Web:** Organizing internet data into a meaningful, interconnected structure using ontologies (e.g., Schema.org).
*   **Natural Language Understanding:** Enabling chatbots and virtual assistants to comprehend user queries within a context.
*   **Robotics:** Allowing robots to understand their environment and the objects within it to perform tasks.
