### Learning Purpose: Depth First Search (DFS)

**1. Importance:**  
DFS is a foundational algorithm for traversing and searching graph and tree data structures. Its systematic, deep-prioritizing approach is crucial for solving problems where exploring a single path to completion is necessary before backtracking, forming the basis for more complex AI techniques.

**2. Student Learning Outcomes:**  
Students will learn to:
*   Define the DFS algorithm and its LIFO (stack-based) mechanics.
*   Implement DFS recursively and iteratively.
*   Analyze its time and space complexity (O(V+E)).
*   Identify its completeness and optimality conditions.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts:**  
DFS is a core uninformed search strategy, providing a contrast to Breadth-First Search (BFS). It is the foundational step for understanding more advanced concepts like backtracking, branch and bound, and heuristic-guided searches like Depth-Limited and Iterative Deepening DFS.

**4. Real-World Applications:**  
DFS is applied in scenarios requiring exhaustive path exploration, such as:
*   Solving mazes and puzzles (e.g., Sudoku solvers).
*   Cycle detection in networks.
*   Topological sorting for task scheduling.
*   Pathfinding in game AI and web crawler navigation.
