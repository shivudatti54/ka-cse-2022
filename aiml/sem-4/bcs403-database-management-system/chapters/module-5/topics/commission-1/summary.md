## COMMISSION) 1 Revision Notes

### DATABASE MANAGEMENT SYSTEM

#### Key Concepts

- **Commission**: A payment made by an agent or intermediary to a third party for their services in selling a product or service.
- **Dealer Commission**: A commission paid by a merchant to a dealer for selling their product.
- **Broker Commission**: A commission paid by a buyer to a broker for facilitating a transaction.
- **Agent Commission**: A commission paid by a principal to an agent for their services.

#### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Commission Formula**: Commission = (Sales - Cost) x Rate
- **Commission Rate**: A percentage of the sale price that is paid as commission.
- **Merchant's Commission**: A commission paid by a merchant to a dealer for selling their product.
- **Dealer's Commission**: A commission paid by a buyer to a dealer for selling their product.

#### Theorems and Concepts

- **The Law of Supply and Demand**: The price and quantity of a product are determined by the intersection of supply and demand curves.
- **The Law of Diminishing Marginal Utility**: As the quantity of a product increases, the marginal utility decreases.
- **Opportunity Cost**: The cost of choosing one option over another.

#### Important Terms

- **Dealer**: An intermediary between a buyer and a merchant.
- **Broker**: An intermediary between a buyer and a seller.
- **Agent**: An intermediary between a principal and a third party.

### Key Takeaways

- Commission is a payment made for services rendered.
- Commission rates vary depending on the type of transaction.
- Merchant's commission, dealer's commission, and broker's commission are all types of commissions.
