**Purpose and Importance of Adding Primary Key and Not Null Constraints to the Employee Table:**

**1. Why this topic matters:**
Adding primary key and not null constraints to the employee table is crucial for maintaining data integrity and ensuring that each record in the table is unique and complete. This topic matters as it lays the foundation for organizing and managing large datasets effectively.

**2. Real-world applications:**
In a real-world scenario, a company's human resources department uses an employee database to record employee information, including names, salaries, and job titles. Adding primary key and not null constraints ensures that each employee record is unique and complete, allowing for efficient data retrieval and analysis.

**3. Connection to other concepts:**
This topic is closely related to normalization and indexing, as primary key and not null constraints are essential for achieving normalization and creating efficient indexes. It also connects to database design and data modeling, as the choice of constraints can significantly impact the overall structure and performance of the database.
