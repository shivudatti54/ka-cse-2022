**Topic Summary: Textbook 1: Ch 7.1 to 7.3 - DATABASE MANAGEMENT SYSTEM**

**1. Why this topic matters:**
Understanding database management system (DBMS) concepts is crucial in today's data-driven world, where efficient and secure data management is essential for organizations to make informed decisions. Learning about database design, normalization, and indexing helps individuals design and implement effective databases that support business operations. This knowledge is vital in various industries, including finance, healthcare, and e-commerce.

**2. Real-world applications:**
In real-world scenarios, database management system concepts are applied in various ways, such as:

- Designing database structures for e-commerce websites to manage customer information and transactions.
- Implementing data normalization techniques to reduce data redundancy and improve data integrity.
- Optimizing database performance using indexing techniques to enhance query speed.

**3. How it connects to other concepts:**
The concepts covered in chapters 7.1 to 7.3 in Textbook 1 - Database Management System, connect to other concepts in the module by:

- Building on the foundation of data modeling and database design.
- Integrating with topics on data warehousing, data mining, and data governance.
- Providing a solid understanding of database concepts that can be applied to other areas of the course, such as data analysis, data visualization, and data ethics.
