### Learning Purpose: Database Design Theory

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Database Design Theory provides the formal foundation for creating efficient, reliable, and scalable databases. Without a solid theoretical grounding, databases are prone to data redundancy, update anomalies, and integrity problems, leading to inaccurate data and poor application performance. This theory is crucial for transforming informal requirements into a robust, well-structured database schema.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the core principles of normalization, including functional dependencies and normal forms (1NF, 2NF, 3NF, and BCNF), to eliminate data redundancy. They will also be introduced to decomposition algorithms that preserve data and dependencies, ensuring the logical consistency of the database.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module directly builds upon the Entity-Relationship (ER) modeling covered previously, providing a formal method to convert an ER diagram into an efficient relational schema. It is a prerequisite for understanding subsequent topics like query processing, optimization, and transaction management, as the physical implementation's efficiency hinges on a sound logical design.

**4. Real-world applications**
These principles are applied whenever a new database is developed, from banking systems ensuring transaction accuracy to e-commerce platforms maintaining product and customer information without duplication. Proper design is critical for data warehousing, business intelligence, and any application where data integrity and performance are paramount.
