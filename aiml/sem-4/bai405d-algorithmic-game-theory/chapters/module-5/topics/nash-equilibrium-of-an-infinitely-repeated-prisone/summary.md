# Nash Equilibrium of an Infinitely Repeated Prisoner's Dilemma

### Definitions

- **Prisoner's Dilemma**: A game theory scenario where two prisoners must decide whether to cooperate or defect, with each having a dominant strategy to maximize their individual payoff.
- **Nash Equilibrium**: A stable state in a game where no player can improve their payoff by unilaterally changing their strategy, assuming all other players keep their strategies unchanged.
- **Infinitely Repeated Prisoner's Dilemma**: A variant of the Prisoner's Dilemma where the game is repeated infinitely, allowing for strategic interactions.

### Key Formulas and Theorems

- **Payoff Matrix**:
  | | Cooperate (C) | Defect (D) |
  | --- | --- | --- |
  | Cooperate (C) | (3, 3) | (0, 5) |
  | Defect (D) | (5, 0) | (1, 1) |
- **Nash Equilibrium**: A state where neither player can improve their payoff by changing their strategy.
- ** saddle point theorem**: In a payoff matrix, a Nash Equilibrium occurs at the saddle point where the maximum value in one row equals the minimum value in the column.

### Key Points

- **Nash Equilibrium in Infinitely Repeated Prisoner's Dilemma**: The Nash Equilibrium is a mixed strategy equilibrium, where both players play a mixed strategy that is a probability distribution over the possible actions.
- **Pareto Optimality**: The Nash Equilibrium is Pareto optimal, meaning that no player can improve their payoff without making another player worse off.
- **Evolutionary Stability**: The Nash Equilibrium is evolutionarily stable, meaning that it is resistant to deviations from the equilibrium strategy.
- **Folk Theorem**: The folk theorem states that, in infinitely repeated games, every saddle point is a Nash Equilibrium.

### Important Results

- **Nash's Equilibrium Selection**: In infinitely repeated games, Nash's equilibrium selection theorem states that the Nash Equilibrium will be chosen by the players.
- **Fitzroy's Result**: Fitzroy's result states that, in infinitely repeated games with complete information, the Nash Equilibrium is the only stable equilibrium.
