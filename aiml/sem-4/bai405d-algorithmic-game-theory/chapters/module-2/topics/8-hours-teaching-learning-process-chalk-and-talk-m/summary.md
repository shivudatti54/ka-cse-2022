# **Teaching-Learning Process: Chalk and Talk Method / PowerPoint Presentation**

## **Definition**

- The chalk and talk method is a traditional teaching method where the instructor lectures to students using a chalkboard or whiteboard.
- PowerPoint presentation is a more modern method using a computer-based presentation software.

## **Key Points**

- **Chalk and Talk Method**
  - Advantages:
    - Encourages active listening and engagement
    - Allows for immediate feedback and clarification
    - Can be used for small or large groups
  - Disadvantages:
    - Limited visual aids
    - Instructor's speaking skills are crucial
    - Can be time-consuming
- **PowerPoint Presentation**
  - Advantages:
    - Easy to create and update content
    - Can include visual aids such as images and videos
    - Can be used for remote or online classes
  - Disadvantages:
    - Requires technical skills
    - Can be distracting if overused
    - May not encourage active engagement

## **Important Formulas and Theorems**

- **Nash Equilibrium**: A state in a game where no player can improve their outcome by unilaterally changing their strategy, assuming all other players keep their strategies unchanged.
- **Mixed Strategy**: A strategy where a player randomizes their action, making it difficult for other players to predict their next move.

## **Teaching Strategies**

- **Direct Instruction**: The instructor provides a detailed explanation of the material.
- **Guided Practice**: The instructor provides guidance and support as students practice the material.
- **Independent Practice**: Students work on the material on their own.

## **Revision Tips**

- Review the key points for both methods.
- Practice creating PowerPoint presentations.
- Focus on developing your speaking and presentation skills.
