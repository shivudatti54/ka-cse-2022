# Equilibrium

- **Definition:** A state where no player can improve their payoff by unilaterally changing their strategy, assuming all other players keep their strategies unchanged.
- **Key Concepts:**
  - **Nash Equilibrium (NE):** A state where no player can improve their payoff by changing their strategy, assuming all other players keep their strategies unchanged.
  - **Mixed Strategy:** A probability distribution over a set of pure strategies, used to model randomization.
  - **Pareto Optimality:** A state where no player can improve their payoff without making another player worse off.
- **Important Formulas:**
  - **Nash Equilibrium Condition:** For two players, if the best response to player 1's strategy is strategy 1, and the best response to player 2's strategy is strategy 2, then (1, 2) is a Nash Equilibrium.
  - **Mixed Strategy Nash Equilibrium:** A state where both players randomize over their strategies, and no player can improve their payoff by changing their mixed strategy.
- **Important Theorems:**
  - **Nash Equilibrium Theorem:** A state where no player can improve their payoff by changing their strategy, assuming all other players keep their strategies unchanged, is a Nash Equilibrium.
  - **Pigou-Dickson Theorem:** A state where both players randomize over their strategies, and no player can improve their payoff by changing their mixed strategy, is a Mixed Strategy Nash Equilibrium.
- **Important Definitions:**
  - **Pure Strategy:** A specific action taken by a player.
  - **Mixed Strategy:** A probability distribution over a set of pure strategies.
  - **Payoff Matrix:** A table showing the payoff for each player for each possible combination of strategies.
- **Key Equilibrium Concepts:**
  - **Dominant Strategy:** A strategy that is the best response for all players, regardless of the other players' strategies.
  - **Strong Nash Equilibrium:** A state where no player can improve their payoff by changing their strategy, and no player can improve their payoff by deviating from their strategy, assuming all other players keep their strategies unchanged.
