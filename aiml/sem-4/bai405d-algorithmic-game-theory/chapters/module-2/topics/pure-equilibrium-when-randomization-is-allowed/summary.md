# Pure Equilibrium when Randomization is Allowed

=====================================================

### Introduction

In game theory, a pure equilibrium is a stable state where no player can improve their payoff by unilaterally changing their strategy. When randomization is allowed, players can choose a probability distribution over their strategies, making the equilibrium analysis more complex.

### Key Points

- **Definition:** A pure equilibrium exists when the expected value of a player's payoff is maximized, given their strategy profile.
- **Mixed Strategy Nash Equilibrium (MSNE):** A mixed strategy Nash equilibrium occurs when no player can improve their payoff by unilaterally changing their mixed strategy.
- **Key Formula:** Expected utility = ∑(probability × payoff)
- **Key Theorem:** The Mixed Strategy Nash Equilibrium Theorem: A mixed strategy Nash equilibrium exists and is unique if the game is finite and complete.

### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Expected Utility:** The expected value of a player's payoff, given their strategy profile.
- **Mixed Strategy:** A probability distribution over a player's pure strategies.
- **Pure Strategy:** A single strategy chosen by a player.

### Important Concepts

- **Randomization:** The ability of players to choose a probability distribution over their strategies.
- **Mixed Equilibrium:** A stable state where no player can improve their payoff by unilaterally changing their mixed strategy.

### Relevance to ALGORITHMIC GAME THEORY

- **Algorithmic Design:** Understanding pure equilibrium with randomization is crucial for designing algorithms that can play strategic games effectively.
- **Game-Theoretic Analysis:** Analyzing mixed strategy Nash equilibria helps in understanding the strategic behavior of players in games.
