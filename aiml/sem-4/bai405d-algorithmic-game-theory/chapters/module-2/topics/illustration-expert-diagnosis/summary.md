# Illustration: Expert Diagnosis

=====================================================

### Definition

---

Expert diagnosis is a strategic game where one player, the diagnoser, tries to determine the state of the object being examined (the patient), while the other player, the expert, tries to mislead the diagnoser.

### Key Points

---

- **Mixed Strategies:** Players use probabilistic strategies to randomize their actions.
- **Nash Equilibrium:** The optimal strategy for the expert to mislead the diagnoser.
- **Minimax Theorem:** The minimum maximum value of an opponent's strategy.

### Important Formulas and Definitions

---

- **Bayes' Theorem:** P(A|B) = P(A and B) / P(B)
- **Bayes' Rule:** P(H|X) = P(X|H) \* P(H) / P(X)
- **Strategic Game Theory:** A game where players make strategic decisions based on their payoffs and opponent's actions.
- **Nash Equilibrium:** A state where no player can improve their payoff by unilaterally changing their strategy.

### Theorems

---

- **Minimax Theorem:** The maximum value of a game is equal to the minimum value of the game.

### Example

---

Suppose the expert wants to mislead the diagnoser about the state of the object being examined. The expert can use a mixed strategy to randomize their actions, while the diagnoser uses a mixed strategy to randomize their diagnosis.

### Key Takeaways

---

- Expert diagnosis is a strategic game where players use mixed strategies to randomize their actions.
- The Nash equilibrium is the optimal strategy for the expert to mislead the diagnoser.
- The minimax theorem can be used to determine the optimal strategy for the expert.
