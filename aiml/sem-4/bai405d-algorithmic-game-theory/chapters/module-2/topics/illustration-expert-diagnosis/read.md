# **Illustration: Expert Diagnosis**

## **Introduction**

Expert Diagnosis is a type of game that involves a diagnostician and a patient, where the diagnostician aims to identify the patient's disease or condition, while the patient tries to mislead the diagnostician. This game is a classic example of a strategic game, where players may randomize their actions, and the concept of a Mixed Strategy Nash equilibrium comes into play.

## **Game Structure**

In Expert Diagnosis, there are two players:

- **Diagnostician (D)**: The player who tries to identify the patient's disease or condition.
- **Patient (P)**: The player who tries to mislead the diagnostician.

The game can be represented as a tree, where each node represents a question or action taken by the diagnostician, and the edges represent the possible responses from the patient.

## **Definitions**

- **Mixed Strategy Nash Equilibrium**: A mixed strategy Nash equilibrium is a situation where both players have a mixed strategy, and no player can improve their expected payoff by unilaterally changing their strategy.
- **Expected Payoff**: The expected payoff is the average payoff that a player expects to receive from a particular action or strategy.

## **Key Concepts**

- **Payoff Matrix**: A payoff matrix is a table that shows the payoffs for each possible combination of actions taken by the two players.
- **Strategy**: A strategy is a set of actions that a player takes in response to the actions taken by the other player.
- **Randomization**: Randomization is the process of assigning a probability to each action, rather than taking a fixed action.

## **Gameplay**

Here is an example of how the Expert Diagnosis game can be played:

1.  The diagnostician asks a question, such as "Do you have a fever?"
2.  The patient responds with a yes or no.
3.  Based on the patient's response, the diagnostician asks a follow-up question, such as "Do you have a headache?"
4.  This process continues until the diagnostician makes a diagnosis or until the patient decides to terminate the game.

## **Mixed Strategy Nash Equilibrium**

In the Expert Diagnosis game, the diagnostician may use a mixed strategy, where they assign a probability to each question or action. Similarly, the patient may also use a mixed strategy, where they assign a probability to each response.

A Mixed Strategy Nash equilibrium occurs when both players have a mixed strategy, and no player can improve their expected payoff by unilaterally changing their strategy.

## **Example**

Suppose we have a diagnostician who uses a mixed strategy, where they ask question 1 with a probability of 0.6, question 2 with a probability of 0.4, and so on. The patient uses a mixed strategy, where they respond "yes" with a probability of 0.7, "no" with a probability of 0.3, and so on.

Using a payoff matrix, we can calculate the expected payoff for each possible combination of actions taken by the two players. After analyzing the payoff matrix, we can conclude that the game is in a Mixed Strategy Nash equilibrium, where both players have a mixed strategy, and no player can improve their expected payoff by unilaterally changing their strategy.
