# **Illustrations: Cournot’s Duopoly Game with Imperfect Information**

### Definitions and Notations

- **Cournot's Duopoly Game**: A classic game in economics where two firms produce a homogeneous product and compete with each other.
- **Imperfect Information**: Each firm has incomplete knowledge about the other firm's technology, cost, or demand.
- **Game Theory**: The study of strategic decision making in situations where the outcome depends on the actions of multiple individuals or firms.

### Key Points

- **Cournot's Model**: Each firm produces a quantity of output, and the total market demand is a function of the sum of the two firms' outputs.
- **Firm's Objective**: Maximize profit, subject to a linear production cost function.
- **Imperfect Information**:
  - **Private Information**: Each firm has private information about its own cost or demand.
  - **Common Knowledge**: Firms have no information about the other firm's private information.

### Formulas and Theorems

- **Cournot's Price Equation**:
  - \(p = \frac{2}{q_1 + q_2}\)
  - where \(p\) is the price, and \(q_1\) and \(q_2\) are the quantities produced by firms 1 and 2, respectively.
- **First-Order Conditions**:
  - \(\frac{\partial \pi_1}{\partial q_1} = \frac{\partial \pi_2}{\partial q_2} = 0\)
  - where \(\pi_1\) and \(\pi_2\) are the profits of firms 1 and 2, respectively.
- **Nash Equilibrium**:
  - A state where no firm can improve its payoff by unilaterally changing its strategy, assuming the other firm's strategy remains unchanged.
- **Rational Expectations**:
  - Each firm uses its private information to make predictions about the other firm's behavior.
- **Bayesian Nash Equilibrium**:
  - A state where no firm can improve its payoff by unilaterally changing its strategy, assuming the other firm's strategy remains unchanged, and taking into account the other firm's expectations about the other firm's behavior.

### Important Results

- **Cournot's Lemma**: The quantity produced by each firm is inversely proportional to the price.
- **Second-Order Conditions**: The profit-maximizing quantities are unique and interior.

### Conclusion

Cournot's duopoly game with imperfect information is a classic example of a Bayesian game, where each firm has private information about its own cost or demand. The game has multiple equilibria, including the Nash equilibrium and the Bayesian Nash equilibrium. Understanding these concepts is essential for analyzing strategic decision making in competitive markets.
