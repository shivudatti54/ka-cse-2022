# Providing a Public Good

=====================================

## Definition

---

- A public good is a product or service that is non-excludable (cannot be excluded from others) and non-rivalrous (can be consumed by multiple people simultaneously without reducing its value).
- Examples: national defense, public parks, public education.

## Tragedy of the Commons

---

- A situation where multiple individuals or groups have access to a shared resource, leading to overconsumption and degradation of the resource.
- Formulated by Garrett Hardin in 1968.

## Game Theory

---

- To analyze the provision of public goods, game theory can be applied.
- Types of games:
  - **Prisoner's Dilemma**: a classic game that illustrates the conflict between individual and collective rationality.
  - **Public Goods Game**: a game that models the provision of public goods.

## Theoretical Models

---

- **Public Goods Production**: a model that examines the optimal production of a public good in the presence of free-riding.
- **Voluntary Provision**: a model that studies the provision of public goods through voluntary contributions.

## Key Formulas and Theorems

---

- **Free-Rider Problem**: A(1-p) = 0, where A is the amount contributed by an individual and p is the probability of others contributing.
- **Tragedy of the Commons**: A > K, where A is the total demand for the resource and K is the carrying capacity of the resource.

## Revision Notes

---

- The provision of public goods is a classic problem in game theory and economics.
- The Tragedy of the Commons and the free-rider problem are key concepts in understanding the challenges of providing public goods.
- Game theory can be applied to analyze the provision of public goods and to design mechanisms to encourage cooperation.
- The optimal provision of public goods depends on various factors, including the level of public awareness, the availability of alternative providers, and the degree of free-riding.

## Important Theorems

---

- **Coakley's Theorem**: states that the optimal level of public good provision is the one that maximizes the social surplus.
- **Brams and Taylor's Theorem**: states that the optimal level of public good provision is the one that minimizes the free-rider problem.
