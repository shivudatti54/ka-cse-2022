# **Illustrations: The Ultimatum Game**

## **Introduction**

The Ultimatum Game is a simple, yet powerful concept in game theory that has far-reaching implications in economics, sociology, and psychology. In this study material, we will delve into the world of the Ultimatum Game, exploring its definition, mechanics, and applications.

## **Definition**

The Ultimatum Game is a two-player game where one player (Proposer) is given a sum of money and must divide it between themselves and another player (Reserrer). The Proposer proposes a division of the money, and the Reserrer has two options: accept or reject the proposal. If the Reserrer accepts, the game ends, and both players receive their allocated shares. If the Reserrer rejects, the game ends, and neither player receives anything.

## **Mechanics**

Here is a step-by-step breakdown of the game mechanics:

1. **Initial Setup**: The game begins with a sum of money, typically denoted as `x`.
2. **Proposer's Turn**: The Proposer is given the sum `x` and must divide it between themselves and the Reserrer.
3. **Proposal**: The Proposer proposes a division of the money, specifying two numbers: `a` and `b`, where `a` is the amount the Proposer will receive and `b` is the amount the Reserrer will receive.
4. **Reserrer's Turn**: The Reserrer receives the proposal and has two options:
   - **Accept**: Accept the proposal and receive the allocated amount `b`.
   - **Reject**: Reject the proposal and receive nothing.
5. **Game End**: If the Reserrer accepts, the game ends, and both players receive their allocated shares. If the Reserrer rejects, the game ends, and neither player receives anything.

## **Key Concepts**

- **Proposer**: The player who proposes the division of the money.
- **Reserrer**: The player who receives the proposal and has the final say.
- **Acceptance**: When the Reserrer accepts the proposal.
- **Rejection**: When the Reserrer rejects the proposal.
- **Payoff**: The amount each player receives at the end of the game.

## **Applications**

The Ultimatum Game has numerous applications in various fields, including:

- **Economics**: The game can be used to study fairness and cooperation in economic decision-making.
- **Sociology**: The game can be used to study fairness and cooperation in social interactions.
- **Psychology**: The game can be used to study cognitive biases and decision-making under uncertainty.

## **Experiments and Results**

Numerous experiments have been conducted to study the Ultimatum Game, and the results are fascinating.

- **Fairness**: Experiments have shown that players tend to reject proposals that are perceived as unfair, and accept proposals that are perceived as fair.
- **Cooperation**: Experiments have shown that players are more likely to cooperate when the proposal is fair, and less likely to cooperate when the proposal is unfair.
- **Cognitive Biases**: Experiments have shown that players are susceptible to cognitive biases, such as the endowment effect and the sunk cost fallacy.

## **Conclusion**

The Ultimatum Game is a powerful tool for studying fairness, cooperation, and decision-making under uncertainty. Its applications extend across various fields, and its results have significant implications for our understanding of human behavior.
