# **Illustrations: The Ultimatum Game**

## **Definition and Premise**

- The ultimatum game is a simple economic experiment designed by Werner Güth and his colleagues in 1982.
- Two players, usually named Alice and Bob, are given a sum of money to divide between them.
- Alice proposes a division of the money, and Bob can either accept or reject the proposal.
- If Bob rejects the proposal, both players receive nothing.

## **Key Points**

- **Pareto Optimality**: A solution is Pareto optimal if no player can improve their payoff without making another player worse off.
- **Nash Equilibrium**: A Nash equilibrium is a stable state where no player can improve their payoff by unilaterally changing their strategy, assuming the other player's strategy remains the same.
- **Proposed Division**: Alice's proposed division of the money is often represented as (x, y), where x is the amount given to Alice and y is the amount given to Bob.
- **Acceptance Probability**: The probability that Bob accepts Alice's proposal.

## **Important Formulas and Definitions**

- ** utils(Alice, Bob, x, y) = x + 1\*y**: Alice's utility function, where x is the amount given to Alice and y is the amount given to Bob.
- ** utils(Bob, Alice, x, y) = -y + 1\*x**: Bob's utility function, where x is the amount given to Alice and y is the amount given to Bob.
- **V(x, y) = max\_{x,y} [utils(Alice, Bob, x, y)]**: The maximum utility that can be achieved by Alice and Bob, given the proposed division (x, y).

## **Theorems**

- **The Ultimatum Game Theorem**: If Alice and Bob are rational, then Bob will reject any proposal that gives him less than 33% of the total amount.
- **The Ultimatum Game Paradox**: In most cases, Alice will choose a proposal that gives her more than 50% of the total amount, even if it means Bob gets nothing.
