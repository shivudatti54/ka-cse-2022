# Stackelberg’s Model of Duopoly

=====================================================

## Introduction

---

Stackelberg's model of duopoly is a game theoretical model that describes the strategic interaction between two firms in a competitive market. The model was first introduced by Johan B. S. Stackelberg in 1934 and is a fundamental concept in industrial organization and game theory.

## Key Components

---

### Definition

Stackelberg's model of duopoly is a two-player game where two firms, Firm A and Firm B, compete to maximize their profits. The model assumes that Firm A is the leader and Firm B is the follower.

### Assumptions

- Both firms produce a homogeneous product.
- Both firms have constant marginal costs.
- The market is competitive, and customers have perfect information about the firms' product quality.
- The firms have perfect information about each other's strategies.

### Game Tree

---

The game tree for Stackelberg's model of duopoly consists of the following stages:

1. **Stage 1:** Firm A decides on its production level, denoted by Q1.
2. **Stage 2:** Firm B observes Firm A's production level and decides on its production level, denoted by Q2.

## Strategic Form

---

The strategic form of Stackelberg's model of duopoly can be represented as follows:

|                         | Firm B's Action | Firm B's Profit       |
| ----------------------- | --------------- | --------------------- |
| **Firm A's Action:** Q1 | Q2              | π(A, B) = (Q1 - Q2)^2 |
|                         | Q1              | π(A, B) = (Q1 - Q2)^2 |

### Firm A's Strategy

---

Firm A's strategy is to maximize its profit, π(A, B), subject to the constraint that Q1 ≤ Q_max.

- Q_max is the maximum production level that Firm A can produce.
- The profit function π(A, B) is quadratic in Q1 and Q2.

### Firm B's Strategy

---

Firm B's strategy is to maximize its profit, π(B, A), subject to the constraint that Q2 ≤ Q_max.

- Q_max is the maximum production level that Firm B can produce.
- The profit function π(B, A) is quadratic in Q1 and Q2.

## Equilibrium

---

The equilibrium of Stackelberg's model of duopoly is a pair of strategies (Q1, Q2) that maximize the profits of both firms.

- The equilibrium is characterized by the following conditions:
  - Firm A's profit is maximized.
  - Firm B's profit is maximized.
  - The production levels of both firms are positive.

## Examples

---

### Example 1

Suppose Firm A decides to produce Q1 = 100 units, and Firm B decides to produce Q2 = 80 units. The profits of both firms are:

- Firm A: π(A, B) = (100 - 80)^2 = 400
- Firm B: π(B, A) = (80 - 100)^2 = 400

This is an equilibrium of the game.

### Example 2

Suppose Firm A decides to produce Q1 = 120 units, and Firm B decides to produce Q2 = 90 units. The profits of both firms are:

- Firm A: π(A, B) = (120 - 90)^2 = 900
- Firm B: π(B, A) = (90 - 120)^2 = 900

This is also an equilibrium of the game.

## Conclusion

---

Stackelberg's model of duopoly is a fundamental concept in industrial organization and game theory. The model describes the strategic interaction between two firms in a competitive market and provides insights into the equilibrium of the game. The model has been widely used to study the behavior of firms in various industries, including retailing, manufacturing, and services.

### Key Takeaways

- Stackelberg's model of duopoly is a game theoretical model that describes the strategic interaction between two firms in a competitive market.
- The model assumes that Firm A is the leader and Firm B is the follower.
- The model is characterized by a quadratic profit function for both firms.
- The equilibrium of the game is characterized by the maximization of profits by both firms.
