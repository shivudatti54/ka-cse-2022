# Stackelberg's Model of Duopoly

### Definition

Stackelberg's model is a game theory model that describes a situation where one firm (the "leader") sets its price and the other firm (the "follower") sets its price in response to the leader's strategy.

### Key Points

- **Leader (L)**: Sets its price and production level first.
- **Follower (F)**: Sets its price and production level in response to the leader's strategy.
- **Assumptions**:
  - Perfect competition in both markets.
  - No product differentiation.
  - No externalities.
  - No economies of scale.
- **Equilibrium**: (L, F) is an equilibrium if:
  - L maximizes profits given F's reaction function.
  - F maximizes profits given L's reaction function.

### Important Formulas and Definitions

- **Reaction Function**: F's decision rule, which depends on L's price.
- **Profit Maximization**: L and F maximize their respective profits subject to the reaction function.
- **Stackelberg Equilibrium**: (L, F) is a Stackelberg equilibrium if it is an equilibrium under both leaders and followers.

### Theorem: Stackelberg's Monotonicity Theorem

If the follower's reaction function is monotone increasing in the leader's price, then the leader's profit is maximized at the Nash equilibrium.

### Important Theorems

- **Nash Equilibrium**: (L, F) is a Nash equilibrium if no player can improve its payoff by unilaterally changing its strategy, assuming all other players keep their strategies unchanged.
- **First-Price Auction**: In a first-price auction, the winner pays the highest price. This is similar to a Stackelberg game where the leader sets the price and the follower sets the quantity.

### Key Concepts

- **Price Leadership**: The leader sets the price, and the follower sets the quantity in response.
- **Quantity Leadership**: The follower sets the price, and the leader sets the quantity in response.
- **Price and Quantity Interdependence**: The leader and follower's prices and quantities are interdependent.
