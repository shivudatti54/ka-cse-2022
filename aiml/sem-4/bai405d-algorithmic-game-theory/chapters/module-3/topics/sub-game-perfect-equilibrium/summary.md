# Sub-Game Perfect Equilibrium

### Definitions

- **Sub-game perfect equilibrium (SGPE)**: A concept in game theory where a game is considered to have a sub-game perfect equilibrium if it satisfies two conditions:
  - The game has a sub-game perfect equilibrium in each of its sub-games.
  - The strategy chosen in each sub-game is optimal with respect to the strategies chosen in the other sub-games.
- **Sub-game**: A subset of the original game where the players make decisions in isolation.

### Key Concepts

- **Sub-game perfect sequential equilibrium**: A concept that extends sub-game perfect equilibrium to sequential games.
- **Backward induction**: A method used to solve games with sub-game perfect equilibrium.
- **Mixed strategies**: A strategy that involves playing a probability distribution over all possible actions.
