# Sub-Game Perfect Equilibrium

### Introduction

Sub-game perfect equilibrium is a concept in game theory that extends the idea of Nash equilibrium to sub-games within a larger game. In a Nash equilibrium, no player can improve their payoff by unilaterally changing their strategy, assuming all other players keep their strategies unchanged. However, in a large game with multiple sub-games, it may not be possible for a player to make a unilateral change that affects the payoff without also affecting the outcome of other sub-games.

### Definition

Sub-game perfect equilibrium is a strategy profile that is a Nash equilibrium in every sub-game of the larger game. In other words, if we consider a sub-game as a separate game, the strategy profile is a Nash equilibrium in that sub-game.

### Conditions

For a sub-game perfect equilibrium to exist, the following conditions must be met:

- **Nash equilibrium in every sub-game**: The strategy profile must be a Nash equilibrium in every sub-game.
- **No deviations are possible**: No player can improve their payoff by deviating from their strategy without affecting the outcome of other sub-games.

### Properties

Sub-game perfect equilibrium has several properties:

- **Existence**: Sub-game perfect equilibrium exists in every sub-game of the larger game.
- **Uniqueness**: There is only one sub-game perfect equilibrium in every sub-game.
- **Pareto optimality**: The sub-game perfect equilibrium is Pareto optimal, meaning that no other strategy profile can improve the payoff of at least one player without worsening the payoff of another player.

### Examples

Consider a game with two players, A and B, and two sub-games: Sub-game 1 and Sub-game 2. In Sub-game 1, player A decides to play either a high or a low strategy, while in Sub-game 2, player B decides to play either a cooperate or a defect strategy.

Suppose the payoff matrix for the larger game is:

|        | B Cooperate | B Defect |
| ------ | ----------- | -------- |
| A High | (10, 10)    | (5, 5)   |
| A Low  | (5, 5)      | (0, 0)   |

The sub-game perfect equilibrium of Sub-game 1 is A Low, since A cannot improve their payoff by playing High without affecting the outcome of Sub-game 2. The sub-game perfect equilibrium of Sub-game 2 is B Cooperate, since B cannot improve their payoff by defecting without affecting the outcome of Sub-game 1.

The sub-game perfect equilibrium of the larger game is A Low and B Cooperate, since it is a Nash equilibrium in both Sub-game 1 and Sub-game 2.

### Conclusion

Sub-game perfect equilibrium is an important concept in game theory that extends the idea of Nash equilibrium to sub-games within a larger game. It provides a framework for analyzing and understanding the behavior of players in complex games with multiple sub-games.
