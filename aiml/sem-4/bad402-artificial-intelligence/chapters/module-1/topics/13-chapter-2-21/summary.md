# **Summary: Chapter 2-2.1 - Artificial Intelligence**

### Definitions and Fundamentals

- **Artificial Intelligence (AI)**: The development of computer systems able to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as learning, problem-solving, and decision-making.
- **Intelligent Agents**: Autonomous entities that perceive their environment, take actions, and make decisions to achieve specific goals.

### Key Concepts

- **Robotics**: The integration of AI and robotics to create intelligent machines that can interact with and adapt to their environment.
- **Machine Learning (ML)**: A subset of AI that enables machines to learn from data and improve their performance over time.
- **Neural Networks**: A type of ML model inspired by the human brain, composed of interconnected nodes (neurons) that process and transmit information.

### Important Formulas and Theorems

- **Bayes' Theorem**: P(A|B) = P(A and B) / P(B), used in ML and decision-making.
- **Markov Decision Process (MDP)**: A mathematical framework for modeling decision-making in complex environments, defined by:
  - States (S)
  - Actions (A)
  - Rewards (R)
  - Transition probabilities (P)

### Important Terms

- **Supervised Learning**: A type of ML where the algorithm is trained on labeled data to learn patterns and relationships.
- **Unsupervised Learning**: A type of ML where the algorithm is trained on unlabeled data to discover hidden patterns and relationships.
- **Reinforcement Learning (RL)**: A type of ML where the algorithm learns through trial and error by interacting with an environment and receiving rewards or penalties.

### Important Authors and Theorists

- **Alan Turing**: Proposed the Turing Test, a measure of a machine's ability to exhibit intelligent behavior equivalent to, or indistinguishable from, that of a human.
- **Marvin Minsky**: Developed the theory of artificial neural networks and wrote "Perceptrons," a seminal work on multi-layer perceptrons.
- **Frank Rosenblatt**: Developed the perceptron, a type of neural network that can learn linearly separable patterns.
