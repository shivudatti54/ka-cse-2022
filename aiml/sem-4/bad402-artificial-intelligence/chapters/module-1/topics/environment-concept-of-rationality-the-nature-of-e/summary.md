# **Environment, Concept of Rationality, The Nature of Environment, The Structure of Agents**

## **Environment**

- Definition: The set of opportunities and obstacles that an agent faces.
- Types of Environments:
  - **Epistemic Environment**: The agent's knowledge about the environment.
  - **Action Environment**: The agent's ability to take actions in the environment.
  - **Dynamics Environment**: The way the environment changes over time.
- Notions:
  - **State of the Environment**: The current situation in the environment.
  - **Transition Model**: The probability distribution over the next state given the current state.

## **Concept of Rationality**

- Definition: The ability of an agent to make decisions that maximize its utility or minimize its cost.
- Types of Rationality:
  - **Strong Rationality**: The agent has a complete and consistent decision theory.
  - **Weak Rationality**: The agent has a decision theory that is consistent but incomplete.
- Notions:
  - **Utility Function**: A function that assigns numerical weights to different states of the environment.
  - **Decision Theory**: The set of rules that govern the agent's decision-making process.

## **The Nature of Environment**

- Definition: The underlying structure of the environment, including its dynamics and the agent's ability to interact with it.
- Notions:
  - **Structural Complexity**: The level of complexity of the environment's structure.
  - **Environmental Complexity**: The level of complexity of the environment's dynamics.

## **The Structure of Agents**

- Definition: The underlying structure of the agent, including its perception, reasoning, and action capabilities.
- Notions:
  - **Agent's Knowledge**: The agent's knowledge about the environment.
  - **Agent's Abilities**: The agent's capabilities, such as perception, reasoning, and action.
  - **Agent's Goals**: The agent's objectives and desiderata.

## Key Formulas:

- **Markov Decision Process (MDP)**: A mathematical framework for modeling decision-making in environments.
- **Bellman Equations**: A set of equations that govern the optimal decision-making process in MDPs.
- **Value Function**: A function that represents the expected utility of a state.

## Important Definitions:

- **Epistemic Incompleteness**: The agent's lack of knowledge about the environment.
- **Action Incompleteness**: The agent's inability to take certain actions in the environment.
- **Environmental Uncertainty**: The uncertainty about the environment's dynamics.

## Important Theorems:

- **No-Opportunity Principle**: The optimal strategy for an agent in an MDP is to always choose the action that maximizes the expected utility of the next state.
- **Consistency Principle**: The optimal strategy for an agent in an MDP is to be consistent with its previous decisions.
