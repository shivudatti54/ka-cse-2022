## **1.1: What is Artificial Intelligence?**

Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the development of computer systems that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as learning, problem-solving, decision-making, and perception.

## **History of AI**

The concept of AI dates back to ancient Greece, where myths of automatons and self-moving machines were told. However, the modern concept of AI began to take shape in the mid-20th century.

- **1950s:** The Dartmouth Summer Research Project on Artificial Intelligence, led by John McCarthy, Marvin Minsky, Nathaniel Rochester, and Claude Shannon, is considered the birthplace of AI as a field of research.
- **1956:** The first AI program, Logical Theorist, was developed by Allen Newell and Herbert Simon.
- **1960s:** AI research focused on rule-based systems and expert systems.
- **1970s:** AI research shifted to machine learning and neural networks.
- **1980s:** The AI Winter, a period of reduced funding and interest in AI research, occurred due to the failure of AI to deliver on its promises.
- **1990s:** The resurgence of AI research led to advancements in machine learning and the development of AI applications in areas like image recognition and natural language processing.

## **Key Concepts**

- **Intelligent Agents:** Autonomous systems that can perceive their environment and act upon it to achieve a goal.
- **Machine Learning:** A subset of AI that involves training algorithms to learn from data and improve their performance over time.
- **Neural Networks:** A type of machine learning model inspired by the structure and function of the human brain.

## **Types of AI**

- **Narrow or Weak AI:** Designed to perform a specific task, such as facial recognition or language translation.
- **General or Strong AI:** A hypothetical AI system that possesses human-like intelligence and can perform any intellectual task.

## **Applications of AI**

- **Virtual Assistants:** AI-powered assistants like Siri, Alexa, and Google Assistant can perform tasks such as scheduling appointments, sending messages, and making recommendations.
- **Image Recognition:** AI-powered image recognition systems can identify objects, people, and patterns in images, with applications in areas like self-driving cars and medical diagnosis.
- **Natural Language Processing:** AI-powered NLP systems can understand and generate human language, with applications in areas like customer service and language translation.

## **Case Study: Chatbots**

Chatbots are AI-powered systems that can engage in natural language conversations with humans. They use NLP and machine learning algorithms to understand user input and respond accordingly.

- **Example:** A chatbot that provides customer support for a company can use NLP to understand user queries and respond with relevant information or escalate the issue to a human customer support agent.
