### Summary: Text book 1, Chapter 1- 1.1

#### Artificial Intelligence

- **Definition:** Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the development of computer systems that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as learning, problem-solving, and perception.
- **History:** AI has a rich history that spans several decades, with key milestones including:
  - 1950s: Dartmouth Summer Research Project on Artificial Intelligence
  - 1960s: Development of the first AI programs
  - 1970s: Expert systems and rule-based systems
  - 1980s: Rise of AI Winter
  - 1990s: Resurgence of AI research

#### Intelligent Agents

- **Definition:** An intelligent agent is a computer program that perceives its environment, solves problems, and takes actions based on its goals and preferences.
- **Key Characteristics:**
  - Autonomy: Ability to act independently
  - ReActive: Ability to respond to changes in the environment
  - ProActive: Ability to plan and make decisions
  - Learning: Ability to improve its performance over time

- **Types of Intelligent Agents:**
  - Simple Reflex Agents: Respond to inputs with pre-defined actions
  - Model-Based Reflex Agents: Use a model of the environment to make decisions
  - Utility-Based Agents: Make decisions based on a utility function

- **Formulas and Definitions:**

- **Artificial Intelligence (AI) Definition:** AI = (Perception + Problem-Solving + Acting) ∩ (Human Intelligence)
- **Markov Decision Process (MDP):** A mathematical framework for modeling decision-making processes

- **Important Theorems:**

- **No Free Lunch Theorem:** Any algorithm that solves a problem will have a suboptimal solution
- **Occam's Razor:** The simplest explanation is usually the best one
