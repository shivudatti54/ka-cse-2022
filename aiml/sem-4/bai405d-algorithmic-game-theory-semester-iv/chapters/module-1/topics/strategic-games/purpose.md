### Learning Purpose: Strategic Games

1.  **Importance:** This topic is foundational to algorithmic game theory as it provides the formal framework for analyzing interactive decision-making among rational agents. Understanding strategic games is crucial because it models countless real-world competitive and cooperative scenarios where the outcome for one participant depends on the actions of others.

2.  **Learning Outcomes:** Students will learn to formally define a strategic game using normal form, identify and differentiate between solution concepts like dominant strategies and the Nash equilibrium, and analyze the strategic interplay in classic models (e.g., Prisoner's Dilemma, Coordination Games). They will develop the ability to predict likely outcomes when multiple self-interested parties interact.

3.  **Connections:** This module directly builds upon concepts from game theory and algorithms. It provides the essential groundwork for subsequent topics, such as analyzing more complex extensive-form games, mechanism design (creating games with desired outcomes), and calculating the Price of Anarchy (quantifying the efficiency loss due to selfish behavior).

4.  **Applications:** The principles of strategic games are applied in diverse fields, including designing auction and bidding systems online, formulating corporate competitive strategy, managing network traffic routing, and modeling economic and political phenomena.
