### Learning Purpose: Examples of Nash Equilibrium

**1. Importance:**
This topic is crucial as Nash Equilibrium (NE) is the foundational solution concept in non-cooperative game theory. Understanding specific examples transforms the abstract definition into a tangible tool, demonstrating how rational agents behave in strategic situations. It provides the essential lens through which most multi-agent interactions are analyzed.

**2. Student Learning:**
Students will learn to identify and compute Nash Equilibria in canonical games such as the Prisoner's Dilemma, Battle of the Sexes, and Coordination Games. They will analyze the outcomes, evaluating their efficiency (e.g., Pareto optimality) and stability. This develops the practical skill of modeling simple strategic scenarios and predicting their likely outcomes.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts:**
This module directly builds upon the formal definition of NE from previous lectures. It sets the stage for more advanced concepts like mixed-strategy equilibria, the existence proofs of Nash, and the limitations of NE (e.g., in games with multiple equilibria). It is also a prerequisite for studying mechanism design and auctions.

**4. Real-World Applications:**
Examples of NE model real-world phenomena in economics (olopoly markets), politics (voting strategies), and sociology (social norms). Understanding these applications is key to analyzing strategic decision-making in business, policy design, and even everyday interactions like negotiating or navigating traffic.
