# Learning Purpose: Introduction to Strategic Games

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is fundamental as strategic games model the core interactions studied in economics, computer science, and politics. Understanding them is the first step in analyzing how rational, self-interested agents make decisions that affect each other, which is critical for designing efficient algorithms and digital marketplaces.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to formally represent strategic situations as **normal-form games**, identify key solution concepts like the **Nash Equilibrium**, and differentiate between pure and mixed strategies. This provides the analytical framework to predict outcomes in multi-agent systems.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This module lays the essential groundwork for the entire course. It is directly connected to subsequent topics such as mechanism design, auctions, and algorithmic pricing, where the principles of strategic interaction are applied to more complex, structured environments.

**4. Real-world applications**
The concepts are applied in myriad areas, including:
*   **Tech Industry:** Designing online ad auctions.
*   **Economics:** Modeling competition between firms.
*   **Networking:** Routing traffic in congested networks.
*   **Politics:** Analyzing voting systems and political campaigns.
