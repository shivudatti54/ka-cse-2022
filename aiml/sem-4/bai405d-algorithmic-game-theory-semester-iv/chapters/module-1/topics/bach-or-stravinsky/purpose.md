### Learning Purpose: Bach or Stravinsky (BoS)

**1. Importance:**
This topic is foundational as it introduces one of the simplest and most elegant models of a coordination game. It serves as a crucial entry point for understanding how multiple pure-strategy Nash Equilibria can exist in a game and the subsequent challenge of equilibrium selection, a central problem in multi-agent interactions.

**2. Student Learning:**
Students will learn to model a scenario with conflicting preferences where players have a common interest in coordinating but disagree on the outcome. They will identify the game's two pure-strategy Nash Equilibria, analyze the payoffs, and be introduced to solution concepts like payoff-dominance and risk-dominance used to predict which equilibrium might be selected.

**3. Connection to Other Concepts:**
The BoS game acts as a direct contrast to the Prisoner's Dilemma, highlighting games without a dominant strategy. It is a building block for more complex concepts like correlated equilibria, learning in games, and the analysis of conventions and social norms that help agents coordinate in the real world.

**4. Real-World Applications:**
The principles of BoS apply to any scenario requiring coordination with competing standards, such as choosing between competing technologies (Betamax vs. VHS, Blu-ray vs. HD DVD), agreeing on a communication protocol, or even coordinating social plans among friends with different preferences.
