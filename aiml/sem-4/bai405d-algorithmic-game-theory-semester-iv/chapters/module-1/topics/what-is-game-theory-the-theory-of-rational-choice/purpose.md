### Learning Purpose: What is Game Theory? The Theory of Rational Choice

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic provides the foundational vocabulary and core principles for the entire field. Understanding rational choice theory—the assumption that individuals make decisions to maximize their personal utility—is crucial, as it is the bedrock upon which all game-theoretic models are built. It establishes a framework for analyzing strategic interactions, which is essential for the rest of the course and for understanding behavior in competitive environments.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the formal definition of a game, including the key components of players, strategies, and payoffs. They will understand the concept of rationality in a strategic context and be introduced to solution concepts like **Strictly Dominant Strategies** and the **Nash Equilibrium**. This module equips them with the tools to model simple strategic situations and predict likely outcomes.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This introductory module is the prerequisite for all subsequent topics. The theory of rational choice directly leads into the analysis of more complex games (extensive-form, Bayesian, etc.) and advanced solution concepts studied throughout algorithmic game theory. It connects to computer science through the algorithmic design of incentive-compatible systems (e.g., auctions and markets).

**4. Real-world applications**
These principles are applied to model competition between firms, auction design, routing in networks, and online advertising markets. It provides a lens to analyze any scenario where the outcome for one participant depends on the choices of others, from economics and political science to the design of multi-agent AI systems.
