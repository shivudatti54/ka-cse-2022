### Learning Purpose: Module 5 - Algorithmic Game Theory (Based on Hillier & Lieberman)

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Algorithmic Game Theory (AGT) is crucial because it provides the mathematical framework to analyze and design systems where self-interested human users interact with automated, algorithmic processes. It is the backbone of modern digital marketplaces, online platforms, and networked systems, ensuring they are efficient, stable, and resistant to strategic manipulation.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to model strategic interactions between rational agents in computational settings. This includes analyzing Nash equilibria in games, understanding mechanism design to incentivize desired behaviors (e.g., truth-telling in auctions), and computing solutions for complex, multi-agent systems. The module focuses on the intersection of game theory and computer science.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic synthesizes core concepts from earlier modules on game theory (strategic form games, equilibria) and algorithms (complexity, optimization). It directly applies optimization techniques to solve equilibrium problems and leverages economic principles to design systems with desirable properties, bridging discrete mathematics, economics, and computer science.

**4. Real-world applications**
AGT is applied ubiquitously in:
*   **E-commerce:** Designing auction and pricing algorithms for platforms like eBay and Google Ads.
*   **Network Routing:** Managing traffic flow in congested networks where users choose selfish paths.
*   **Blockchain & Cryptocurrencies:** Designing incentive-compatible consensus mechanisms like Proof-of-Stake.
*   **Matching Markets:** Creating efficient and fair algorithms for school choice, medical residency matches, and kidney exchanges.
