### Learning Purpose: Continuous Internal Evaluation

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Continuous Internal Evaluation (CIE) is a crucial pedagogical tool that shifts the focus from high-stakes, end-of-term exams to an ongoing assessment of a student's grasp of complex material. In a challenging subject like Algorithmic Game Theory, which blends intricate mathematical concepts with strategic thinking, CIE ensures students consistently engage with the material, identify knowledge gaps early, and receive regular feedback, leading to a deeper and more robust understanding.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to apply the core principles of game theory—such as Nash Equilibrium, mechanism design, and auctions—to solve progressively complex problems through assignments, quizzes, and case studies. They will develop the ability to formulate real-world strategic interactions into mathematical models and analyze them, honing both their analytical and critical thinking skills.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
CIE directly assesses comprehension of foundational concepts from earlier modules (e.g., non-cooperative games, algorithmic mechanism design). This continuous practice reinforces these connections and prepares students for advanced topics like price of anarchy and learning in games, ensuring a cohesive understanding of the entire curriculum.

**4. Real-world applications**
The problem-solving skills developed through CIE are directly applicable to real-world systems governed by strategic interactions. This includes designing efficient online ad auctions, analyzing market competition, formulating policies for public goods, and optimizing routing in networks, making the theoretical concepts tangible and relevant.
