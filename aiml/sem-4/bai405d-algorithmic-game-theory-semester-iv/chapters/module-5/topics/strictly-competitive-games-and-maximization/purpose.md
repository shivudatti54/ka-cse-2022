### Learning Purpose: Strictly Competitive Games and Maximin

**1. Importance:** This topic is fundamental as it models scenarios of pure conflict, where one agent's gain is another's loss. Understanding these zero-sum interactions is crucial for analyzing strategic decision-making in adversarial environments.

**2. Student Learning:** Students will learn to formally define strictly competitive games, identify their key properties, and calculate security strategies using the **maximin** principle. They will understand how to find the **value of the game** and the concept of a **saddle point** equilibrium.

**3. Connections:** This concept is a cornerstone of classical game theory, providing a baseline for analyzing more complex, non-zero-sum games covered later. It builds directly on the foundational knowledge of strategic-form games and Nash Equilibrium from earlier modules, illustrating a special case where equilibria are interchangeable and lead to a predictable outcome.

**4. Applications:** The maximin strategy is applied in security (e.g., allocating patrols), auction bidding, poker, and other strategic games. It provides a robust, conservative approach for making decisions against rational opponents in high-stakes, competitive situations like cybersecurity and national defense.
