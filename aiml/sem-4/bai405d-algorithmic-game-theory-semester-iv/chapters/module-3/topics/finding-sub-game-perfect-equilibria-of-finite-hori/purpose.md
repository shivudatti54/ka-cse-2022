### Learning Purpose: Finding Sub-game Perfect Equilibria of Finite Horizon Games

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial because it addresses a major shortcoming of standard Nash equilibria in sequential games: the possibility of non-credible threats. Sub-game perfect equilibrium (SPE) refines the solution concept by requiring that players' strategies constitute a Nash equilibrium in *every* sub-game, ensuring the outcome is dynamically consistent and credible. This is fundamental for analyzing strategic interactions that unfold over time.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to solve finite sequential games using the method of backward induction. They will develop the skill to systematically analyze a game from its final decision point backwards to the beginning, identifying optimal moves at each stage to eliminate incredible threats and pinpoint the SPE.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This concept builds directly on the foundational knowledge of Nash equilibrium and extensive-form game representation from earlier modules. It is a precursor to analyzing more complex infinite horizon games, where backward induction is replaced by other methods, and connects to mechanism design, where credible implementation is key.

**4. Real-world applications**
The principles of SPE and backward induction are applied in economics to model oligopoly competition, in political science to analyze legislative bargaining, and in computer science to design protocols for multi-agent systems. It provides the tools to predict credible outcomes in any multi-stage interaction, from corporate bidding wars to strategic policy decisions.
