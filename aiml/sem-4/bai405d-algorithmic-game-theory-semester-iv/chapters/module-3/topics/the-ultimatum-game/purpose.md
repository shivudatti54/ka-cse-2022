### Learning Purpose: The Ultimatum Game

**1. Why is this topic important?**
The Ultimatum Game is a foundational experiment in behavioral economics and game theory. It challenges the classical assumption of purely rational, self-interested actors by demonstrating that human behavior is often driven by fairness, reciprocity, and emotion. Understanding this discrepancy is crucial for designing algorithms and systems that interact with real people.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the mechanics of the Ultimatum Game, analyze its standard and behavioral outcomes, and calculate its Nash Equilibrium. They will explore how actual human decisions deviate from theoretical predictions and examine concepts like fairness, altruism, and spite, which are critical for modeling real-world strategic interactions.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic directly builds upon concepts of strategic form games, Nash Equilibrium, and backward induction from earlier modules. It serves as a perfect bridge to more complex topics like bargaining theory, repeated games, and behavioral game theory, providing a concrete example of how psychology influences strategic decision-making.

**4. Real-world applications**
Insights from the Ultimatum Game are applied in designing auction mechanisms, corporate bargaining, political negotiation, and digital marketplaces. It is also essential for creating credible AI and algorithmic systems that must account for human notions of fairness to be accepted and effective.
