### Learning Purpose: Backward Induction

**1. Why is this topic important?**
Backward induction is a fundamental technique for solving finite sequential games with perfect information. It is crucial because it provides a powerful, logical method for predicting the outcome of multi-stage strategic interactions, forming the basis for the concept of a Subgame Perfect Nash Equilibrium (SPNE).

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn the formal procedure of backward induction: starting from the final decision nodes of a game tree and reasoning backwards to the beginning to determine the optimal strategy for each player at every possible point. They will master how to apply this algorithm to solve games and identify credible, non-credible threats, and promises.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
This topic is a direct application of dynamic game theory and is the primary method for finding SPNE, a refinement of the basic Nash Equilibrium. It builds upon prior knowledge of game trees (extensive form) and strategic form games, while also serving as a foundational concept for analyzing more complex topics like bargaining and repeated games.

**4. Real-world applications**
The principles of backward induction are applied in economics to model oligopoly competition (e.g., Stackelberg duopoly), in political science to analyze sequential voting or bargaining, and in computer science for designing algorithms in AI and multi-agent systems where agents make sequential decisions.
