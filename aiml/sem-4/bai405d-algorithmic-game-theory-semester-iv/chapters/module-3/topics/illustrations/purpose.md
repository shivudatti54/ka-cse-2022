### Learning Purpose: Illustrations in Algorithmic Game Theory

**1. Why is this topic important?**
This topic is crucial as it moves from abstract theory to tangible examples, solidifying understanding of complex equilibrium concepts and incentive structures. By analyzing specific models, students learn to recognize the practical design trade-offs and computational challenges inherent in real-world systems governed by strategic interactions.

**2. What will students learn?**
Students will learn to apply core AGT concepts—like Nash Equilibrium, Price of Anarchy, and mechanism design—to canonical models. This includes analyzing the strategic behavior in auctions (e.g., Vickrey), network routing (e.g., Pigou's example), and facility location games. They will calculate efficiency metrics and evaluate the performance of algorithms in strategic environments.

**3. How does it connect to other concepts?**
Illustrations directly build upon the foundational equilibrium concepts and solution algorithms covered in earlier modules. They provide the critical link between the abstract game-theoretic models (Module 1) and the algorithmic mechanisms for complex systems (later in Module 3 and beyond), demonstrating how theory is implemented and tested.

**4. Real-world applications**
These illustrations model key real-world applications, including online ad auctions, routing and congestion in transportation networks, and platform design for social good (e.g., matching markets). Understanding these models is essential for designing efficient and incentive-compatible digital marketplaces and infrastructure.
