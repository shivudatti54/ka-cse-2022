### Purpose  
The Master Theorem is a fundamental tool for analyzing the time‑complexity of divide‑and‑conquer algorithms, which are at the heart of many real‑world CS applications such as sorting, searching, and parallel computing. Mastering this theorem enables you to predict how an algorithm scales with input size, guiding the selection and optimization of efficient solutions in large‑scale software systems.

### Learning Objectives
- **Identify** recurrence relations of the form \(T(n)=aT(n/b)+f(n)\) in given algorithms.  
- **Apply** the Master Theorem to compute asymptotic bounds for such recurrences.  
- **Derive** recurrence equations from pseudo‑code or high‑level descriptions of divide‑and‑conquer algorithms.  
- **Compare** the theoretical bounds with empirical measurements to validate the analysis.  
- **Evaluate** the limitations of the Master Theorem and recognize when alternative analysis methods are required.  
- **Design** efficient divide‑and‑conquer solutions by anticipating the impact of the theorem’s outcome on algorithm performance.
