## Purpose
Asymptotic notations provide the mathematical language to describe the efficiency and scalability of algorithms, independent of hardware specifics. Mastering these notations enables data‑structure designers and developers to predict performance, make informed algorithmic choices, and build systems that meet real‑world time and space constraints.

## Learning Objectives
- **Define** the concepts of time and space complexity using Big‑O, Big‑Ω, and Big‑Θ notations.  
- **Compare** the growth rates of common functions (e.g., constant, logarithmic, linear, polynomial, exponential).  
- **Apply** asymptotic analysis to determine worst‑case, average‑case, and best‑case behavior of algorithms.  
- **Compute** the complexity of simple algorithms by identifying dominant operations and summing their costs.  
- **Evaluate** the suitability of algorithmic solutions based on given constraints and input sizes.  
- **Differentiate** between upper, lower, and tight bounds and select the appropriate notation for a scenario.  
- **Use** asymptotic notations to reason about the scalability of data‑structure operations (e.g., arrays, linked lists, trees).  
- **Predict** the impact of algorithmic choices on system performance in practical computing contexts.
