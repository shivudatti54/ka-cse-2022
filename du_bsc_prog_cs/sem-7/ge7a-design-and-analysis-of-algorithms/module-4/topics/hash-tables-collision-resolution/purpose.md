# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental concept of hash tables and the role of hash functions in index computation.

2. Define collision in hash tables and explain why collisions are inevitable in practice.

3. Compare and contrast open addressing and chaining as collision resolution techniques.

4. Analyze linear probing, quadratic probing, and double hashing with respect to their probe sequences and clustering problems.

5. Calculate and interpret the load factor, and explain its impact on hash table performance.

6. Determine the average-case and worst-case time complexities for insertion, deletion, and search operations under different collision resolution techniques.

7. Evaluate the trade-offs between different collision resolution methods and select appropriate techniques for specific application scenarios.

8. Explain the purpose and process of rehashing in hash tables.
