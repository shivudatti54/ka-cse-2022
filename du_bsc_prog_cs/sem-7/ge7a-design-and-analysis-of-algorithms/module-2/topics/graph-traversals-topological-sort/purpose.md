## Purpose  
Graph structures model many real‑world systems such as task scheduling, project management, and network routing. Mastering graph traversals and topological sorting equips students with the algorithmic techniques needed to efficiently process dependencies and design scalable solutions for directed acyclic graphs (DAGs).

## Learning Objectives
- **Define** and differentiate between depth‑first search (DFS) and breadth‑first search (BFS) in the context of graph traversal.  
- **Implement** DFS and BFS to visit all vertices and edges of a graph correctly.  
- **Explain** the concept of a directed acyclic graph (DAG) and its significance for topological ordering.  
- **Apply** Kahn’s algorithm and a DFS‑based approach to compute a topological order of a DAG.  
- **Analyze** the time and space complexities of graph traversal and topological sort algorithms.  
- **Compare** different valid topological orderings and evaluate their correctness.  
- **Design** a solution that uses topological sorting to schedule tasks with dependencies in a real‑world scenario.  
- **Demonstrate** cycle detection in a directed graph and argue why topological sorting is impossible when cycles are present.
