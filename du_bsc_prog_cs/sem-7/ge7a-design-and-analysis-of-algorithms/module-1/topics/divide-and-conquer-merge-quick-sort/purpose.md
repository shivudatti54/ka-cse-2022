## Purpose / Learning Objectives

Sorting is a foundational operation in virtually every software system, from database management and search engines to real‑time data processing and operating system utilities. Mastery of **Divide‑and‑Conquer** sorting algorithms—*Merge Sort* and *Quick Sort*—equips students with the analytical tools to design, implement, and optimize efficient solutions for large‑scale data handling, while deepening their understanding of algorithmic complexity, recursion, and practical trade‑offs.

### Learning Objectives
- **Explain** the divide‑and‑conquer paradigm and how it applies to Merge Sort and Quick Sort.  
- **Implement** both Merge Sort and Quick Sort in a programming language, ensuring correct handling of base cases and recursion.  
- **Analyze** the best‑case, worst‑case, and average‑case time‑and‑space complexities of each algorithm.  
- **Compare** the performance of Merge Sort and Quick Sort on diverse input sizes and distributions (random, nearly sorted, reverse sorted).  
- **Evaluate** the stability, memory usage, and scalability of the two methods in practical contexts.  
- **Apply** the partitioning technique of Quick Sort to solve related problems such as selection and k‑th element queries.  
- **Design** a hybrid sorting algorithm that combines the strengths of Merge Sort and Quick Sort to improve overall efficiency.
