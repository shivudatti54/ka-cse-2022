# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and differentiate between entities, entity sets, attributes, and relationships in the context of the E-R model
2. Identify and describe the various types of attributes including simple, composite, single-valued, multi-valued, derived, and key attributes
3. Distinguish between strong and weak entities and explain the concept of total and partial participation
4. Determine the cardinality ratios (1:1, 1:N, M:N) of relationships based on business requirements
5. Draw complete E-R diagrams using Chen's notation with proper representation of entities, attributes, relationships, and constraints
6. Convert E-R diagrams to equivalent relational database schemas, handling weak entities and M:N relationships appropriately
7. Analyze real-world scenarios to extract entities, attributes, and relationships for database design
8. Evaluate given E-R diagrams for correctness and suggest improvements for better database modeling
