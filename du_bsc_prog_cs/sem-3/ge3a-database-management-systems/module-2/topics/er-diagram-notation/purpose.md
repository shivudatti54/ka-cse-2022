## Purpose and Learning Objectives  

ER (Entity‑Relationship) diagram notation is the lingua franca for visualising the structure of data in relational databases. Mastering this standard notation enables computer scientists to translate real‑world requirements into precise, implementable designs, ensuring data integrity, scalability, and clear communication among developers, stakeholders, and DBMS tools.

### Learning Objectives  

- **Define** the fundamental components of ER diagrams: entities, attributes, and relationships.  
- **Identify** the symbols and conventions used in major notations (Chen, Crow’s foot, UML).  
- **Apply** cardinality (1:1, 1:N, M:N) and participation constraints to model business rules accurately.  
- **Construct** ER diagrams from a set of textual requirements using the selected notation.  
- **Distinguish** between strong and weak entities and represent them correctly in the diagram.  
- **Analyze** existing ER diagrams to evaluate design quality, redundancy, and normalization potential.  
- **Design** a complete ER schema for a given case study, choosing the most appropriate notation for the context.
