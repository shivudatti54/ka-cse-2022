# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Describe the three levels of the ANSI/SPARC architecture (external, conceptual, and internal) and explain the purpose of each level in database management.

2. Define data independence and distinguish between logical data independence and physical data independence with appropriate examples.

3. Explain how the three-schema architecture achieves data independence and why this capability is essential for database system maintenance and evolution.

4. Analyze how queries and data modifications are transformed through the three levels of abstraction in a DBMS.

5. Evaluate the role of external schemas (views) in providing user-specific perspectives and security in database systems.

6. Apply the concept of data independence to real-world database modification scenarios and determine which type of independence is relevant.

7. Identify the benefits of the three-schema architecture in terms of data abstraction, security, and system maintenance.

8. Prepare for examination questions by understanding common terminology, important years (1975 for ANSI/SPARC), and typical question patterns related to this topic.
