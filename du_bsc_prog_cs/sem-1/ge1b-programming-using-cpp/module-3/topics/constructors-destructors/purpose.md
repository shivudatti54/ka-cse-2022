### Purpose  
Constructors and destructors are fundamental to controlling object initialization and cleanup in C++, enabling proper resource management through the RAII (Resource Acquisition Is Initialization) paradigm. Mastering these special member functions is essential for building robust, efficient, and maintainable software in real‑world CS domains such as systems programming, embedded systems, and large‑scale applications.  

### Learning Objectives  
- **Explain** the role of constructors and destructors in the object lifecycle.  
- **Implement** default, parameterized, copy, and move constructors for C++ classes.  
- **Differentiate** between constructors, destructors, and other special member functions.  
- **Apply** RAII principles to manage resources like memory, files, and network sockets.  
- **Design** classes that ensure proper initialization and cleanup to prevent leaks and undefined behavior.  
- **Utilize** initializer lists for efficient and correct member initialization.  
- **Analyze** the order of constructor and destructor calls in single and inheritance hierarchies.  
- **Demonstrate** exception‑safe practices in constructors and destructors.
