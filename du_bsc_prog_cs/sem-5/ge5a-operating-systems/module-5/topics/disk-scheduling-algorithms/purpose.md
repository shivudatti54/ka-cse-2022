# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the physical structure of magnetic disks including tracks, sectors, cylinders, seek time, rotational delay, and transfer time
2. Describe and differentiate between all major disk scheduling algorithms: FCFS, SSTF, SCAN, C-SCAN, LOOK, and C-LOOK
3. Calculate total head movement and average seek time for each algorithm given a sequence of disk requests
4. Analyze the trade-offs between throughput, average seek time, response time, and fairness among different algorithms
5. Identify the problem of starvation in SSTF and explain why SCAN-family algorithms provide more uniform service times
6. Compare SCAN with C-SCAN, explaining when each is preferred based on system requirements
7. Apply the "elevator algorithm" concept to solve real-world scheduling problems
8. Evaluate which algorithm is most suitable for different operating system environments and workloads
