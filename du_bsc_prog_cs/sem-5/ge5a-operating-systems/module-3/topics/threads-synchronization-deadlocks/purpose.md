**Purpose**  
Understanding threads synchronization and deadlocks is essential for developing reliable, high‑performance software in modern computing environments. Mastery of these concepts enables students to prevent concurrency bugs, efficiently manage shared resources, and design systems that avoid the catastrophic failures that deadlocks can cause.

**Learning Objectives**
- **Explain** the principles of thread synchronization and the conditions that lead to deadlock.  
- **Identify** the four necessary conditions for deadlock (mutual exclusion, hold‑and‑wait, no preemption, circular wait).  
- **Apply** synchronization primitives (e.g., mutexes, semaphores, condition variables) to protect shared resources.  
- **Analyze** real‑world scenarios to detect potential deadlocks and evaluate their impact on system behaviour.  
- **Design** deadlock‑free algorithms using strategies such as resource ordering, preemption, and timeout mechanisms.  
- **Implement** multi‑threaded programs in a programming language (e.g., C/Python) that demonstrate safe concurrency and avoidance of deadlock.
