**Purpose & Learning Objectives**

Understanding the fundamentals of signals transmission media is essential for designing, implementing, and maintaining reliable computer networks. This topic equips students with the knowledge to select appropriate media based on performance, cost, and environmental constraints, thereby ensuring efficient data communication in real‑world CS environments.

- **Identify** the primary categories of transmission media (guided vs. unguided).  
- **Explain** the characteristics of each media type—bandwidth, attenuation, noise, and interference.  
- **Compare** twisted‑pair, coaxial, and fiber‑optic cables in terms of speed, distance, and cost.  
- **Analyze** the principles of electromagnetic wave propagation in wireless media.  
- **Design** a basic LAN topology selecting suitable media for given requirements.  
- **Evaluate** the trade‑offs between security, scalability, and performance for different media.  
- **Select** appropriate transmission media for specific scenarios (e.g., campus backbone, enterprise Wi‑Fi).  
- **Describe** emerging trends such as PoE, 5G, and future fiber‑optic advancements.
