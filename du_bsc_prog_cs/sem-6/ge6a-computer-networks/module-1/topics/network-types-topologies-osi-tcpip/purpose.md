## Purpose & Learning Objectives  

**Purpose:** This topic provides the foundational understanding of how computers interconnect, enabling the design, implementation, and troubleshooting of modern communication systems. Mastery of network topologies, the OSI model, and the TCP/IP protocol suite is essential for developing scalable, secure, and reliable applications in any sector of the IT industry.  

**Learning Objectives**  
- Identify various network types (LAN, MAN, WAN) and their typical use‑cases.  
- Compare physical and logical network topologies (bus, star, ring, mesh, etc.).  
- Explain the seven layers of the OSI model and the function of each layer.  
- Describe the four‑layer TCP/IP model and map it to the OSI layers.  
- Analyze the process of encapsulation and de‑encapsulation of data as it moves through the network layers.  
- Design a simple LAN by selecting an appropriate topology and IP addressing scheme.  
- Evaluate the performance, scalability, and fault‑tolerance characteristics of different topologies.  
- Differentiate between connection‑oriented (TCP) and connectionless (UDP) protocols in the TCP/IP suite.
