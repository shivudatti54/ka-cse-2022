# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and distinguish between the three transmission modes: simplex, half-duplex, and full-duplex, with respect to data flow direction and communication efficiency.

2. Explain the operation of network devices at their respective OSI model layers, including hubs, repeaters, bridges, switches, routers, gateways, and access points.

3. Compare and contrast hub versus switch functionality, analyzing their impact on network performance, collision domains, and bandwidth utilization.

4. Identify appropriate transmission modes for various real-world communication scenarios and justify the selection based on communication requirements.

5. Design a basic network infrastructure by selecting appropriate devices based on organizational requirements such as number of users, connectivity needs, and budget constraints.

6. Analyze the differences between collision domains and broadcast domains and explain how different network devices affect these domains.

7. Understand the role of MAC addresses in switch operation and the role of IP addresses in router operation for making forwarding decisions.
