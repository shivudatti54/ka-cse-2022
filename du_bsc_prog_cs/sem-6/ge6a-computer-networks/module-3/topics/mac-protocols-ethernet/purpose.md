# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental multiple access problem in computer networks and describe why regulation of shared media access is necessary.

2. Analyze the working of ALOHA protocols (Pure and Slotted), calculate their efficiency, and compare their performance characteristics.

3. Describe CSMA protocols and explain how carrier sensing reduces collision probability compared to ALOHA.

4. Explain the CSMA/CD mechanism used in Ethernet, including collision detection, jam signals, and the Binary Exponential Backoff algorithm.

5. Interpret MAC addresses, distinguish between unicast, multicast, and broadcast addresses, and explain address structure.

6. Construct and analyze Ethernet frames according to IEEE 802.3 specification, identifying all header fields and their purposes.

7. Compare Ethernet standards (10BASE-T, 100BASE-TX, 1000BASE-T) and understand the naming convention.

8. Differentiate between hub and switch operation, and explain how switched Ethernet transforms network architecture from shared-medium to dedicated pathways.
