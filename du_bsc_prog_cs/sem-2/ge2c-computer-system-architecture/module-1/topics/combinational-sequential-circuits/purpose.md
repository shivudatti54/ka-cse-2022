# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Distinguish between combinational and sequential circuits with clear reasoning based on their operational characteristics.

2. Identify and explain the functions of various combinational circuits including adders, multiplexers, encoders, and decoders.

3. Analyze and design basic sequential circuits using flip-flops, understanding their behavior through truth tables and timing diagrams.

4. Compare different types of flip-flops (SR, JK, D, T) and explain their specific applications in digital systems.

5. Design and analyze synchronous and asynchronous counters, understanding the advantages and disadvantages of each type.

6. Understand the concept of state in sequential circuits and represent it using state diagrams and state tables.

7. Apply Boolean algebra and Karnaugh maps for minimizing combinational circuit logic expressions.

8. Explain the practical applications of combinational and sequential circuits in modern computing systems.
