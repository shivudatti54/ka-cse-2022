# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Construct and interpret 2-variable, 3-variable, and 4-variable Karnaugh Maps from truth tables and Boolean expressions.

2. Apply K-map grouping rules to minimize Boolean functions into optimal sum-of-products or product-of-sums forms.

3. Describe the operation, characteristic equations, and truth tables of all four fundamental flip-flop types: SR, D, JK, and T.

4. Differentiate between level-triggered latches and edge-triggered flip-flops in terms of timing behavior and practical applications.

5. Construct excitation tables for flip-flops and use them to determine required inputs for specific state transitions.

6. Analyze sequential circuits by determining next states and outputs given present states and inputs.

7. Apply K-map minimization techniques to solve real-world digital design problems involving both combinational and sequential logic.
