# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Convert numbers between decimal, binary, octal, and hexadecimal number systems accurately, understanding positional value calculations.

2. Explain and apply different signed number representations including sign-magnitude, 1's complement, and 2's complement, recognizing their advantages and limitations.

3. Perform binary arithmetic operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division) using appropriate techniques and detect overflow conditions.

4. Interpret and construct IEEE 754 floating-point representations for single and double precision formats, including special values.

5. Analyze binary coded decimal (BCD) representation and compare it with pure binary encoding for numerical applications.

6. Evaluate the trade-offs between different number representations in terms of range, precision, and computational efficiency.

7. Solve numerical problems involving two's complement arithmetic and floating-point conversions commonly asked in DU examinations.

8. Understand the role of data representation in computer architecture and its implications for ALU design and performance.
