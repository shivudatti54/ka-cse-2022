## Purpose  
Understanding number‑system complements is crucial for how computers represent signed integers, perform subtraction, and detect errors in data storage and transmission. This knowledge forms the basis of efficient digital‑circuit design, low‑level programming, and reliable system operation in real‑world computing.

## Learning Objectives
- **Define** the concepts of 1’s complement, 2’s complement, and radix complement.  
- **Convert** between binary, octal, hexadecimal, and decimal using complement representations.  
- **Perform** binary subtraction using 1’s and 2’s complement methods.  
- **Identify** overflow conditions in signed arithmetic and explain how complements enable detection.  
- **Analyze** the role of complements in error‑detection schemes such as checksums.  
- **Design** simple adder/subtractor circuits that utilize complement arithmetic.  
- **Apply** complement arithmetic to solve practical problems in low‑level programming.  
- **Evaluate** the advantages and limitations of different complement systems.
