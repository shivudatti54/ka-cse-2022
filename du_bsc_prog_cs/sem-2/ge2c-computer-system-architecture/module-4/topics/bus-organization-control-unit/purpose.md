## Purpose  
The Bus Organization and Control Unit forms the backbone of communication among the CPU, memory, and I/O devices in any computer system. Mastering this topic equips students to analyze, debug, and optimize hardware interactions, as well as to design modern, high‑performance computing platforms that meet real‑world constraints.

## Learning Objectives
- **Explain** the role and components of a system bus (address, data, and control lines).  
- **Identify** the characteristics of different bus organizations (shared, hierarchical, and point‑to‑point).  
- **Compare** major bus standards (e.g., PCI, USB, ISA, AGP) in terms of bandwidth, timing, and scalability.  
- **Analyze** bus arbitration and timing mechanisms that resolve access conflicts among multiple masters.  
- **Design** a simple bus control unit using state‑machine logic and timing diagrams.  
- **Evaluate** the impact of bus architecture on overall system performance metrics such as latency and throughput.  
- **Implement** basic bus communication protocols in a hardware description language (e.g., VHDL or Verilog).
