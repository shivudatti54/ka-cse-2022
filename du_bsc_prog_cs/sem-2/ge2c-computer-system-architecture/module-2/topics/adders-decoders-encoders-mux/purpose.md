## Purpose  
Adders, decoders, encoders, and multiplexers are fundamental combinatorial circuits that directly implement the arithmetic, addressing, and data‑routing operations of modern processors and memory systems. Mastering these building blocks equips students to design efficient digital subsystems for CPUs, embedded devices, and communication hardware—skills that are essential for any real‑world computer architecture or hardware‑design career.

## Learning Objectives  
- **Explain** the operation of half‑adder, full‑adder, and their truth tables.  
- **Design** a 4‑bit ripple‑carry adder using full adders and evaluate its propagation delay.  
- **Analyze** 2‑to‑4 and 3‑to‑8 decoder circuits for address decoding applications.  
- **Implement** a priority encoder and compare its behavior with a standard binary encoder.  
- **Compare** multiplexer and demultiplexer functions in data‑routing and signal‑selection contexts.  
- **Utilize** multiplexers to realize given Boolean functions and simplify logic circuits.  
- **Synthesize** decoder‑based memory‑addressing schemes and assess their hardware cost.  
- **Evaluate** trade‑offs between different adder architectures (ripple‑carry, carry‑look‑ahead) in terms of speed, area, and power.
