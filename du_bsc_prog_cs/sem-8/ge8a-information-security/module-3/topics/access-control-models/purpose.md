## Purpose and Learning Objectives

**Purpose**  
Access control models are the foundation of secure information systems, dictating how resources are protected and who can interact with them. Understanding these models enables future computer scientists to design, implement, and maintain systems that safeguard data while meeting regulatory and business requirements.

**Learning Objectives**  
- **Explain** the core principles and components of discretionary, mandatory, role‑based, and attribute‑based access control.  
- **Compare** the security guarantees and trade‑offs of each model in terms of confidentiality, integrity, and availability.  
- **Analyze** real‑world scenarios to select the most appropriate access control strategy for a given application.  
- **Design** an access control policy that enforces least‑privilege and separation of duties.  
- **Implement** role‑based access control (RBAC) mechanisms using a programming language or security framework.  
- **Evaluate** the effectiveness of access control mechanisms through vulnerability assessment and audit trails.  
- **Differentiate** between authentication, authorization, and accountability, and explain how they interrelate.  
- **Apply** legal and ethical considerations (e.g., privacy, compliance) when formulating access control policies.
