## Purpose  
In today’s hyper‑connected environment, reliable authentication mechanisms are the frontline defense for safeguarding data and ensuring that only authorized users can access systems and services. Mastering these mechanisms enables students to design, assess, and manage secure authentication solutions that meet the demands of modern information‑security challenges.

## Learning Objectives
- **Explain** the role and importance of authentication in preserving confidentiality, integrity, and availability.  
- **Differentiate** among authentication factors (knowledge, possession, biometric) and their combinations.  
- **Analyze** widely used authentication protocols such as PAP, CHAP, OAuth, SAML, and OpenID Connect.  
- **Compare** single‑factor and multi‑factor authentication approaches in terms of security, usability, and implementation cost.  
- **Design** a secure authentication workflow for a typical web or cloud‑based application.  
- **Evaluate** the strengths and vulnerabilities of password‑based, biometric, and token‑based authentication methods.  
- **Implement** basic authentication mechanisms (e.g., password hashing, token generation) using scripting or programming tools.  
- **Assess** emerging trends, including passwordless authentication and zero‑trust architecture, for future‑ready security practices.
