# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental architecture of IoT systems and identify the role of wireless communication protocols in IoT deployments.

2. Compare and contrast major IoT wireless protocols (WiFi, Zigbee, BLE, LoRaWAN, Z-Wave) including their frequency bands, data rates, power consumption, and security features.

3. Analyze the unique security challenges inherent to IoT environments, including resource constraints, device heterogeneity, and physical accessibility.

4. Identify and describe major attack vectors in IoT wireless security, including eavesdropping, replay attacks, man-in-the-middle attacks, denial of service attacks, and firmware exploitation.

5. Evaluate real-world IoT security incidents (such as the Mirai botnet) and extract lessons learned regarding IoT security best practices.

6. Design and recommend security frameworks for securing IoT deployments, including network segmentation, encryption strategies, and secure device management.

7. Apply knowledge of IoT security principles to analyze case scenarios and solve problem-based questions in examination contexts.

8. Understand emerging trends in IoT security, including lightweight cryptography, secure boot mechanisms, and intrusion detection systems tailored for IoT environments.
