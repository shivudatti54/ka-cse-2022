# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental principles of public-key (asymmetric) cryptography and distinguish it from symmetric cryptography.

2. Describe the RSA algorithm, including the mathematical foundation, key generation process, encryption, and decryption procedures.

3. Analyze the security assumptions underlying public-key cryptosystems, particularly the integer factorization problem.

4. Explain the concept and properties of digital signatures, including authentication, integrity, and non-repudiation.

5. Understand the role of cryptographic hash functions in digital signature schemes and their required security properties.

6. Describe the components and functioning of Public Key Infrastructure (PKI), including digital certificates and Certificate Authorities.

7. Evaluate the practical applications of public-key encryption and digital signatures in real-world systems like HTTPS and digital contracts.

8. Identify common security considerations and potential vulnerabilities in public-key cryptographic systems.
