## Purpose  

Databases store the core digital assets of virtually every organization, making their security essential to protect sensitive information from unauthorized access, data breaches, and compliance violations. This topic equips students with the ability to identify threats, apply robust safeguards, and maintain the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data in real‑world computer‑science environments.

## Learning Objectives  

- **Identify** common threats and vulnerabilities that affect database systems.  
- **Explain** the core principles of confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA) in the context of database security.  
- **Apply** appropriate authentication, authorization, and access‑control mechanisms to restrict database access.  
- **Design** encryption strategies for data at rest and in transit to safeguard sensitive information.  
- **Analyze** audit logs and monitoring tools to detect suspicious activities and potential breaches.  
- **Evaluate** regulatory compliance requirements (e.g., GDPR, IT Act) and their implications for database security.  
- **Implement** backup, recovery, and disaster‑recovery procedures to ensure data availability and integrity.
