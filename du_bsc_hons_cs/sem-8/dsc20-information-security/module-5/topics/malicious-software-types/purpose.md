### Purpose  
Understanding the various types of malicious software is essential for modern computer scientists, as cyber‑attacks become more sophisticated and pervasive across industries, governments, and personal devices. This knowledge underpins the design of resilient systems, effective security policies, and incident‑response strategies that protect data and preserve trust in digital services.

### Learning Objectives  
- **Identify** the primary categories of malware (viruses, worms, trojans, ransomware, spyware, rootkits, etc.).  
- **Explain** the propagation mechanisms and payload behaviors characteristic of each malware type.  
- **Classify** real‑world malware examples according to their taxonomy and attack vectors.  
- **Differentiate** between file‑infector, boot‑sector, macro, and script‑based malware.  
- **Analyze** the impact of malware on confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information systems.  
- **Evaluate** existing detection and mitigation techniques (antivirus, intrusion‑detection systems, behavior‑based analysis).  
- **Design** a basic anti‑malware strategy incorporating prevention, detection, and response measures.  
- **Assess** emerging trends such as polymorphic malware, ransomware‑as‑a‑service, and AI‑driven attacks.
