# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and distinguish between computer viruses, worms, Trojans, and bots, explaining their fundamental differences in propagation mechanisms and operational characteristics.

2. Explain the anatomy of a computer virus, including its infection mechanism, trigger conditions, and payload components.

3. Analyze how computer worms propagate autonomously through network vulnerabilities and understand their potential for rapid, large-scale infections.

4. Identify the deceptive techniques employed by Trojan horses and explain how they differ from self-replicating malware in both distribution and execution.

5. Describe the concept of botnets, their formation, command-and-control architectures, and their role in coordinated cyberattacks.

6. Evaluate real-world examples of malware incidents (such as Morris Worm, WannaCry, Mirai) and extract lessons learned regarding prevention and response.

7. Apply knowledge of malware characteristics to propose appropriate detection methods and preventive security controls for different scenarios.

8. Synthesize understanding of all malware types to analyze compound threats that combine multiple malware characteristics in sophisticated attacks.
