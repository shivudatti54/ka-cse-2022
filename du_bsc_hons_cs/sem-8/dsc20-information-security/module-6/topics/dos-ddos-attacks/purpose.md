# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define Denial of Service (DoS) and Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks and explain their fundamental differences

2. Classify DoS/DDoS attacks into their major categories (volumetric, protocol, and application layer) with examples of each type

3. Explain the mechanics of common attack vectors including SYN floods, UDP floods, HTTP floods, and amplification attacks

4. Analyze how botnets are formed and their role in executing large-scale DDoS attacks

5. Identify the key indicators of a DoS/DDoS attack through network traffic analysis

6. Evaluate various mitigation strategies including traffic scrubbing, rate limiting, and CDN-based protection

7. Apply theoretical knowledge to design basic defense strategies against different types of DoS/DDoS attacks

8. Relate real-world DDoS incidents to the theoretical concepts studied and draw lessons for organizational security
