# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental problem that Public Key Infrastructure solves in secure communications and describe why asymmetric cryptography alone is insufficient without a trust mechanism.

2. Define digital certificates and describe the structure and key fields of X.509 certificates, including the role of the Certificate Authority in binding public keys to identities.

3. Differentiate between the various types of digital certificates (DV, OV, EV, SSL/TLS, code signing, S/MIME) based on their validation requirements and use cases.

4. Compare and contrast Certificate Revocation List (CRL) and Online Certificate Status Protocol (OCSP) as mechanisms for checking certificate validity, including their advantages and disadvantages.

5. Describe the complete certificate lifecycle from generation through revocation, including the roles of Registration Authority and Certificate Authority at each stage.

6. Analyze the hierarchical trust model used in web PKI and explain how certificate chain verification works from the leaf certificate to the root CA.

7. Apply knowledge of PKI concepts to real-world scenarios such as secure web browsing (HTTPS), email encryption, and code signing, explaining how trust is established in each case.

8. Evaluate the security properties provided by PKI and identify potential vulnerabilities and attack vectors related to certificate-based authentication.
