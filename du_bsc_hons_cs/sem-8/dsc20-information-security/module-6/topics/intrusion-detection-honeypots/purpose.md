## Purpose and Learning Objectives  

In the contemporary threat landscape, intrusion detection honeypots provide a controlled, low‑risk environment to capture real‑world attacker behavior and generate actionable threat intelligence, thereby strengthening the design and evaluation of security controls. Understanding honeypot technology equips computer‑science graduates with practical skills to detect, analyze, and mitigate malicious activities that traditional preventive measures may miss.  

- **Explain** the fundamental concepts of honeypots and their role within intrusion detection frameworks.  
- **Identify** the various categories of honeypots (low‑interaction, high‑interaction, research, production) and appropriate use cases.  
- **Design** a low‑interaction honeypot architecture tailored for an academic network environment.  
- **Implement** a honeypot using open‑source tools (e.g., Honeyd, Cowrie) and configure logging and alerting mechanisms.  
- **Analyze** the captured attack data to extract attacker tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs).  
- **Evaluate** the effectiveness of honeypots in improving threat detection rates and security awareness.  
- **Compare** the strengths and limitations of honeypots versus conventional IDS/IPS solutions.
