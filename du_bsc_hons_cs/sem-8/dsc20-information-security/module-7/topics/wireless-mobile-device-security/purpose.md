### Purpose  
Wireless mobile devices have become the primary gateway for personal, business, and government interactions, making their security a cornerstone of modern information‑security practice. Mastering the unique threats and protective mechanisms of wireless communications and mobile platforms equips Computer Science graduates to safeguard data, preserve privacy, and meet regulatory compliance in diverse real‑world environments.  

### Learning Objectives  
- **Identify** the primary threats and vulnerabilities specific to wireless networks and mobile devices.  
- **Explain** the architecture of contemporary mobile operating systems and the security controls they provide.  
- **Assess** the risk profile of widely used wireless protocols (e.g., Wi‑Fi, Bluetooth, NFC) and their common implementation weaknesses.  
- **Design** secure configuration guidelines and policies for enterprise mobile‑device management (MDM).  
- **Implement** robust authentication and encryption solutions such as WPA3, EAP‑TLS, and certificate‑based access control.  
- **Evaluate** the effectiveness of mobile security tools and techniques through penetration testing and security audits.  
- **Mitigate** attacks like man‑in‑the‑middle, rogue access points, and malicious app exploits by applying defense‑in‑depth strategies.
