## Purpose

Public Key Encryption forms the backbone of modern secure communication, enabling secure data transmission, digital signatures, and authentication mechanisms that power e-commerce, online banking, and confidential messaging across the internet. Understanding asymmetric cryptography is essential for computer science professionals to design and implement secure systems that protect sensitive information in an increasingly interconnected digital world.

## Learning Objectives

- **Explain** the fundamental principles and mathematical foundations of public key cryptography
- **Compare** symmetric and asymmetric encryption techniques and their respective use cases
- **Describe** the RSA algorithm and its application in key generation, encryption, and decryption
- **Analyze** the role of digital signatures in ensuring data integrity and authentication
- **Apply** public key infrastructure (PKI) concepts for certificate management and trust establishment
- **Evaluate** the security strengths and vulnerabilities of popular public key cryptosystems
- **Implement** encryption and decryption processes using standard cryptographic libraries
- **Design** secure communication solutions employing hybrid encryption approaches
