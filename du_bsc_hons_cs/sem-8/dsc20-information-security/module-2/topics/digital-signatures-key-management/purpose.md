# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental concepts of digital signatures and how they differ from encryption in providing authentication and non-repudiation.

2. Describe the RSA digital signature algorithm, including key generation, signing, and verification processes with mathematical foundations.

3. Analyze the Digital Signature Standard (DSS) and the Elliptic Curve Digital Signature Algorithm (ECDSA), comparing their security assumptions and efficiency.

4. Evaluate the importance of key management in cryptographic systems and explain the complete key lifecycle from generation to destruction.

5. Design appropriate key management strategies for different scenarios, considering factors like key storage, distribution, and rotation.

6. Explain the components and functioning of Public Key Infrastructure (PKI), including certificate authorities, registration authorities, and certificate revocation mechanisms.

7. Apply digital signature verification and certificate chain validation processes in practical security scenarios.

8. Identify common vulnerabilities in key management and propose mitigation strategies to ensure cryptographic security.
