# Security Concepts, Threats & Attacks

## Introduction

Information Security protects digital assets through three foundational principles: **Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability (CIA Triad)**. For BSc (Hons) Computer Science students at Delhi University, understanding these concepts is essential as per the NEP 2024 UGCF syllabus.

---

## Core Security Concepts

- **CIA Triad**
  - *Confidentiality*: Ensuring information is accessible only to authorized users
  - *Integrity*: Maintaining accuracy and consistency of data
  - *Availability*: Ensuring systems and data are accessible when needed

- **Authentication vs Authorization**
  - Authentication verifies user identity (passwords, biometrics, tokens)
  - Authorization determines access rights after verification

- **Security Layers**: Physical, Network, Application, and Data security

- **Common Security Models**: Bell-LaPadula (confidentiality), Biba (integrity)

---

## Types of Threats

- **Malware**: Viruses, worms, trojans, ransomware, spyware
- **Phishing**: Deceptive emails/websites stealing credentials
- **Social Engineering**: Manipulating individuals to reveal confidential information
- **Insider Threats**: Malicious or negligent employees
- **Zero-Day Exploits**: Attacks targeting unknown vulnerabilities
- **Denial of Service (DoS/DDoS)**: Overwhelming systems to deny service
- **Man-in-the-Middle (MITM)**: Intercepting communications between parties

---

## Types of Attacks

- **Passive Attacks**: Eavesdropping, traffic analysis (harder to detect)
- **Active Attacks**: Modification, masquerade, denial of service (detectable)
- **Injection Attacks**: SQL Injection, XSS (Cross-Site Scripting)
- **Password Attacks**: Brute force, dictionary attacks, rainbow tables
- **Network Attacks**: ARP spoofing, DNS poisoning, session hijacking

---

## Key Terms for Exam

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Vulnerability | Weakness in system that can be exploited |
| Threat | Potential danger that can exploit vulnerability |
| Risk | Likelihood of threat causing damage |
| Exploit | Method to take advantage of vulnerability |

---

## Conclusion

Information Security requires understanding the CIA triad, recognizing various threats (malware, phishing, social engineering), and knowing attack vectors (passive, active, injection). Students should focus on the relationship between vulnerabilities, threats, and appropriate countermeasures. This topic forms the foundation for advanced security topics in the Delhi University syllabus.
