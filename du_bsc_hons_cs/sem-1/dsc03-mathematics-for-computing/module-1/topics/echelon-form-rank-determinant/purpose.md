**Purpose**

Understanding echelon forms, matrix rank, and determinants is fundamental for solving systems of linear equations that arise in areas such as computer graphics, machine learning, network analysis, and cryptography. Mastery of these concepts equips computer science students with the algebraic tools needed to analyze data structures, optimize algorithms, and model real‑world problems efficiently.

**Learning Objectives**

- **Define** row echelon and reduced row echelon forms and distinguish between them.  
- **Compute** the rank of a matrix by applying elementary row operations to obtain its echelon form.  
- **Determine** the determinant of a square matrix and use its properties to assess invertibility.  
- **Apply** rank and determinant criteria to ascertain the consistency and number of solutions of linear systems.  
- **Analyze** linear independence of vectors using the rank of the corresponding matrix.  
- **Utilize** determinant values to interpret geometric transformations (e.g., scaling, orientation) in computer graphics.  
- **Solve** practical computational problems (e.g., network connectivity, dimensionality reduction) by leveraging rank‑based reasoning.
