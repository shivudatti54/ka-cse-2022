# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define the characteristic polynomial of a square matrix and explain its significance.
2. Compute the characteristic polynomial of a given matrix.
3. State the Cayley-Hamilton theorem and explain its implications.
4. Verify the Cayley-Hamilton theorem for a given matrix.
5. Apply the Cayley-Hamilton theorem to solve problems involving matrices and their properties.
6. Understand the importance of the characteristic polynomial and Cayley-Hamilton theorem in computing and other fields.
7. Analyze the properties of matrices using the characteristic polynomial and Cayley-Hamilton theorem.
8. Solve problems involving the characteristic polynomial and Cayley-Hamilton theorem.
