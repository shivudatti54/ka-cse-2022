### Purpose  
Positive‑definite matrices are at the heart of many computer‑science tools: they guarantee convexity in optimization, underpin kernel methods in machine learning, and enable stable numerical computations. Markov chains, on the other hand, provide a rigorous framework for modelling stochastic processes that drive algorithms for web ranking, recommendation systems, and simulation. Mastering these concepts equips students to design efficient, reliable, and scalable solutions for modern data‑driven applications.

### Learning Objectives
- **Define** positive‑definite and positive‑semidefinite matrices and articulate their essential properties.  
- **Apply** the notion of positive definiteness to quadratic forms and determine the convexity of optimization problems.  
- **Analyze** discrete‑time Markov chains, compute stationary distributions, and assess their convergence behaviour.  
- **Utilize** Cholesky decomposition to solve linear systems efficiently when the coefficient matrix is positive definite.  
- **Implement** basic Markov‑chain‑based algorithms such as PageRank or simple random‑walk models for real‑world problems.  
- **Evaluate** the role of positive‑definite kernels in machine‑learning techniques like support vector machines and Gaussian processes.  
- **Demonstrate** the use of covariance matrices (as positive‑definite structures) in data‑analysis tasks such as dimensionality reduction and probabilistic modelling.
