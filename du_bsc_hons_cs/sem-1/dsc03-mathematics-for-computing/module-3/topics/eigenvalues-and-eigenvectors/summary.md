# Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

*   **Eigenvalues**: Scalar values that represent the amount of change in a linear transformation.
*   **Eigenvectors**: Non-zero vectors that remain unchanged under a linear transformation.
*   **Characteristic Equation**: An equation obtained by detaching the diagonal elements of a matrix and setting them equal to zero.
*   **Eigenvalue Decomposition**: A way to represent a matrix as a sum of its eigenvalues and eigenvectors.
*   **Orthogonality**: Eigenvectors corresponding to distinct eigenvalues are orthogonal to each other.
*   **Multiplicity**: The number of times an eigenvalue appears in the characteristic equation.
*   **Eigenspace**: The set of all eigenvectors corresponding to a particular eigenvalue.

## Important Formulas and Theorems

*   **Characteristic Equation**: |A - λI| = 0
*   **Eigenvalue Decomposition**: A = λ1v1v1^T + λ2v2v2^T + … + λnvnn^T
*   **Orthogonality**: v1^T v2 = 0 (for distinct eigenvalues)
*   **Multiplicity**: The number of times an eigenvalue appears in the characteristic equation.

## Key Points

*   Eigenvalues and eigenvectors help us understand the underlying structure of matrices and their behavior when transformed.
*   Eigenvalue decomposition is a powerful tool for image compression, stability analysis, and machine learning.
*   Eigenvectors can be visualized as directions that remain unchanged under linear transformations.
*   The characteristic equation is used to compute eigenvalues.
*   The power method and QR algorithm are iterative techniques for computing eigenvalues and eigenvectors.
*   Eigenvalues can be used to analyze the stability of systems.
*   Eigenvalue decomposition can be used for image compression.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

*   Confusing eigenvalues with eigenvectors.
*   Not understanding the properties of eigenvalues and eigenvectors.
*   Not being able to visualize eigenvectors as directions that remain unchanged under linear transformations.
*   Not understanding the significance of eigenvalue decomposition in various applications.
