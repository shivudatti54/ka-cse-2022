# Vector Algebra: Dot and Cross Product - Summary

## Key Definitions and Concepts

* Dot product: a scalar product that combines two vectors to produce a scalar value.
* Cross product: a vector product that combines two vectors to produce a new vector.
* Magnitude: the length or size of a vector.
* Angle between two vectors: the angle between the directions of the two vectors.

## Important Formulas and Theorems

* Dot product formula: `a · b = |a| |b| cos(θ)`
* Cross product formula: `a × b = |a| |b| sin(θ) n`
* Commutative property of dot product: `a · b = b · a`
* Distributive property of dot product: `a · (b + c) = a · b + a · c`
* Scalar multiplication property of dot product: `(ka) · b = k(a · b)`

## Key Points

* The dot product is a scalar product that combines two vectors to produce a scalar value.
* The cross product is a vector product that combines two vectors to produce a new vector.
* The dot product is commutative, distributive, and scalar multiplication.
* The cross product is anti-commutative, distributive, and scalar multiplication.
* The dot product can be used to find the angle between two vectors.
* The cross product can be used to find the area of a parallelogram.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

* Forgetting to check the order of the vectors when calculating the cross product.
* Not visualizing the vectors and their operations to better understand the concepts.
* Not understanding the properties of the dot product and cross product.
* Not practicing enough to become comfortable with the material.
