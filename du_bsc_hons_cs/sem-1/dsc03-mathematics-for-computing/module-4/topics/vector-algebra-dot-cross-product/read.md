# Vector Algebra: Dot and Cross Product

## Introduction

Vector algebra is a branch of mathematics that deals with the study of vectors and their properties. It is a fundamental subject that is used in many areas of science and engineering, including computer science, physics, and engineering. In this topic, we will discuss two important concepts in vector algebra: the dot product and the cross product.

The dot product and cross product are two ways of multiplying vectors. The dot product is a scalar product that combines two vectors to produce a scalar value, while the cross product is a vector product that combines two vectors to produce a new vector. These products have many applications in computer science, including graphics, game development, and physics simulations.

In this topic, we will learn about the properties and applications of the dot product and cross product. We will also learn how to calculate these products and use them to solve problems in vector algebra.

## Key Concepts

### Dot Product

The dot product of two vectors `a` and `b` is denoted by `a · b` and is defined as:

`a · b = |a| |b| cos(θ)`

where `|a|` and `|b|` are the magnitudes of the vectors `a` and `b`, and `θ` is the angle between them.

The dot product has the following properties:

* Commutative: `a · b = b · a`
* Distributive: `a · (b + c) = a · b + a · c`
* Scalar multiplication: `(ka) · b = k(a · b)`

### Cross Product

The cross product of two vectors `a` and `b` is denoted by `a × b` and is defined as:

`a × b = |a| |b| sin(θ) n`

where `|a|` and `|b|` are the magnitudes of the vectors `a` and `b`, `θ` is the angle between them, and `n` is a unit vector perpendicular to both `a` and `b`.

The cross product has the following properties:

* Anti-commutative: `a × b = -b × a`
* Distributive: `a × (b + c) = a × b + a × c`
* Scalar multiplication: `(ka) × b = k(a × b)`

## Examples

### Example 1: Calculating the Dot Product

Find the dot product of the vectors `a = (2, 3, 4)` and `b = (5, 6, 7)`.

Solution:

`a · b = (2)(5) + (3)(6) + (4)(7) = 10 + 18 + 28 = 56`

### Example 2: Calculating the Cross Product

Find the cross product of the vectors `a = (2, 3, 4)` and `b = (5, 6, 7)`.

Solution:

`a × b = (3(7) - 4(6), 4(5) - 2(7), 2(6) - 3(5)) = (21 - 24, 20 - 14, 12 - 15) = (-3, 6, -3)`

### Example 3: Finding the Angle between Two Vectors

Find the angle between the vectors `a = (2, 3, 4)` and `b = (5, 6, 7)`.

Solution:

`cos(θ) = (a · b) / (|a| |b|) = 56 / (sqrt(2^2 + 3^2 + 4^2) sqrt(5^2 + 6^2 + 7^2)) = 56 / (sqrt(29) sqrt(110))`

`θ = arccos(56 / (sqrt(29) sqrt(110))) ≈ 35.4°`
