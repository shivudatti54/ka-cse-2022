## Purpose
The Laplacian operator is a cornerstone of modern computer science, underpinning techniques in image processing, computer graphics, and data‑driven learning. Its matrix form (the graph Laplacian) enables key algorithms for clustering, dimensionality reduction, and network analysis, making it indispensable for real‑world applications such as computer vision, AI, and distributed systems.

## Learning Objectives
- Explain the mathematical definition and geometric meaning of the Laplacian operator in both continuous and graph settings.
- Compute the Laplacian matrix for given graphs and interpret its eigenvalues and eigenvectors.
- Apply the Laplacian filter in image processing tasks such as edge detection and Laplacian‑of‑Gaussian blob detection.
- Analyze the role of the spectral properties of the Laplacian in clustering and dimensionality reduction (e.g., spectral clustering, Laplacian Eigenmaps).
- Utilize the Laplacian to solve simple partial differential equations governing diffusion or heat flow in physical simulations.
- Design algorithms that leverage the Laplacian for mesh smoothing, segmentation, or feature extraction in computer graphics and machine learning.
