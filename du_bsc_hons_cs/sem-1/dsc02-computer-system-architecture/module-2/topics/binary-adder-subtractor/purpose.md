**Purpose**  
Binary adders and subtractors are the fundamental arithmetic units that underpin all modern processors and digital signal‑processing systems. Mastering their design and analysis equips students with the essential hardware‑level insight needed for efficient CPU/ALU implementation, low‑power circuit design, and performance optimization in real‑world computing devices.

**Learning Objectives**  
- **Explain** the operation of half‑adder, full‑adder, and binary subtractor circuits.  
- **Design** ripple‑carry and more efficient adder topologies (e.g., carry‑lookahead, carry‑select).  
- **Implement** binary addition and subtraction using basic logic gates and evaluate truth‑table compliance.  
- **Analyze** propagation delay, fan‑out, and area‑power trade‑offs of various adder circuits.  
- **Compare** the performance characteristics of synchronous versus asynchronous adder designs.  
- **Apply** adder‑subtractor modules to construct a simple arithmetic logic unit (ALU) component.  
- **Evaluate** the impact of different adder architectures on overall processor speed and energy efficiency.
