## Purpose

Decoders, encoders, and multiplexers are core combinational‑logic components that perform address selection, data routing, and binary‑code conversion in virtually every digital system—from microprocessors and memory chips to communication transceivers. Understanding their operation and design enables the creation of efficient, scalable hardware implementations and lays the groundwork for advanced topics such as bus arbitration, FPGA design, and system‑on‑chip architectures.

## Learning Objectives

- **Explain** the functional behavior of decoders, encoders, and multiplexers and their role in digital systems.  
- **Design** combinational circuits using decoders and multiplexers to meet given logical specifications.  
- **Implement** encoder and decoder modules in a hardware description language (e.g., VHDL/Verilog).  
- **Analyze** truth tables, timing diagrams, and propagation delays of these components.  
- **Compare** different multiplexer topologies (e.g., tree, hierarchical) in terms of speed, area, and power.  
- **Evaluate** the impact of decoders and multiplexers on CPU datapath, memory addressing, and I/O selection.  
- **Construct** hierarchical digital designs that integrate multiple decoders, encoders, and multiplexers.  
- **Apply** fault‑detection techniques to identify errors in encoding/decoding logic.
