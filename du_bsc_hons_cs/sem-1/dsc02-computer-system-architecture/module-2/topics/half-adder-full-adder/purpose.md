## Purpose
The half adder and full adder are the fundamental building blocks of binary arithmetic in digital systems. Mastering these circuits equips students to design and optimize arithmetic logic units (ALUs) and higher‑level arithmetic circuits that underpin modern processors, microcontrollers, and field‑programmable gate arrays (FPGAs).

## Learning Objectives
- Explain the truth tables, sum and carry functions of a half adder and a full adder.  
- Design the Boolean expressions and gate‑level schematics for both adders.  
- Implement half adder and full adder circuits using basic logic gates.  
- Analyze critical parameters such as propagation delay, fan‑in, and hardware cost.  
- Compare the performance of ripple‑carry adders with carry‑look‑ahead techniques.  
- Synthesize multi‑bit binary adders by cascading full adders.  
- Evaluate the impact of adder design choices on processor speed, area, and power consumption.  
- Construct and verify a 4‑bit adder through simulation or hardware testing.
