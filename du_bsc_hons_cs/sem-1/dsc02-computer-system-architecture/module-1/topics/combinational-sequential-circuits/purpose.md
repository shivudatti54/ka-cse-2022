**Purpose**  
Combinational and sequential circuits are the building blocks of all modern digital systems, ranging from simple embedded devices to high‑performance processors. Mastering their design and analysis enables computer scientists to develop efficient, reliable hardware solutions and to optimize system performance at the register‑transfer level.

**Learning Objectives**  
- Explain the fundamental differences between combinational and sequential logic.  
- Design and construct combinational circuits (e.g., adders, multiplexers) using Boolean algebra and gate‑level implementation.  
- Analyze sequential circuits, including flip‑flops, registers, and counters, to determine state behavior.  
- Implement finite state machines (FSMs) to model control logic in processors and embedded systems.  
- Evaluate timing characteristics such as propagation delay, setup/hold times, and clock skew in sequential designs.  
- Compare different implementation technologies (e.g., CMOS, FPGA) for combinational‑sequential circuits in terms of speed, power, and cost.
