## Purpose
Understanding the instruction cycle and pipelining is essential for designing high‑performance processors and writing efficient software, as these concepts form the backbone of modern CPU architectures. Mastery of pipelining enables students to analyze, optimize, and exploit parallelism at the hardware level, directly impacting real‑world computing performance and code efficiency.

## Learning Objectives
- **Explain** the stages of the instruction cycle and their roles in CPU operation.  
- **Analyze** the pipelining concept and its effect on instruction throughput and latency.  
- **Identify** pipeline hazards (structural, data, and control) and propose appropriate mitigation techniques.  
- **Design** a basic multi‑stage pipeline for a given instruction‑set architecture.  
- **Compute** speedup, efficiency, and throughput of pipelined versus non‑pipelined processors.  
- **Evaluate** the impact of pipeline depth and clock frequency on overall system performance.  
- **Compare** different pipeline scheduling techniques and their trade‑offs in contemporary processors.
