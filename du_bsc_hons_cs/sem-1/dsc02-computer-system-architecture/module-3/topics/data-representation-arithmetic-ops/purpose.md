## Purpose and Learning Objectives

**Purpose**  
Data representation and arithmetic operations are fundamental to how computers process numeric information, influencing processor design, low‑level programming, and performance‑critical applications. Mastery of binary, two’s‑complement, and floating‑point formats enables efficient algorithm implementation and accurate numeric computing in areas such as embedded systems, graphics, and scientific computation.

**Learning Objectives**
- Explain the representation of signed and unsigned integers in binary and two’s‑complement forms.  
- Perform binary addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division, including overflow detection.  
- Convert numbers among binary, octal, hexadecimal, and decimal bases.  
- Describe the IEEE‑754 floating‑point standard and interpret normalized and denormalized values.  
- Apply arithmetic algorithms for fixed‑point and floating‑point operations in hardware or software.  
- Analyze the impact of rounding, truncation, and overflow on numeric accuracy and error propagation.  
- Design a simple arithmetic logic unit (ALU) or pipeline for basic integer operations.  
- Evaluate the performance and energy trade‑offs of alternative data‑representation schemes.
