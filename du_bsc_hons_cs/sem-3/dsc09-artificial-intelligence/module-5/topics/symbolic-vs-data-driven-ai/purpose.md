**Purpose**  
Understanding the distinction between symbolic (rule‑based) and data‑driven (machine‑learning) AI is essential for modern computer scientists, as it shapes how we design, interpret, and responsibly deploy intelligent systems across industry, research, and society.

**Learning Objectives**  
- Explain the core principles and historical evolution of symbolic AI and data‑driven AI.  
- Compare the representational mechanisms, reasoning capabilities, and scalability of each approach.  
- Analyze the strengths and limitations of hybrid architectures that combine symbolic reasoning with statistical learning.  
- Evaluate real‑world applications (e.g., expert systems, deep learning, neuro‑symbolic models) in terms of performance, interpretability, and ethical considerations.  
- Design a simple neuro‑symbolic solution that leverages both knowledge graphs and neural networks for a given problem.  
- Implement basic symbolic inference (e.g., logic programming) and a data‑driven model (e.g., neural classifier) in code.  
- Critically assess the impact of data bias, transparency, and regulatory requirements on AI system choices.  
- Apply the comparative analysis to select an appropriate AI paradigm for a specific industry scenario.
