# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the "black box" problem in deep learning and its implications for AI deployment in high-stakes domains

2. Analyze the trade-off between model interpretability and predictive accuracy, and determine appropriate approaches based on application context

3. Compare and contrast major Explainable AI (XAI) techniques including LIME, SHAP, attention mechanisms, and counterfactual explanations

4. Identify and mitigate various sources of bias in AI systems—historical, representation, measurement, aggregation, and evaluation bias

5. Apply foundational AI ethics principles (fairness, transparency, accountability, privacy) to real-world AI development scenarios

6. Evaluate AI systems using different fairness metrics and understand the tensions between competing fairness criteria

7. Interpret regulatory frameworks including GDPR, EU AI Act, and India's emerging AI governance landscape

8. Design and critique responsible AI systems that balance performance with ethical considerations and explainability requirements
