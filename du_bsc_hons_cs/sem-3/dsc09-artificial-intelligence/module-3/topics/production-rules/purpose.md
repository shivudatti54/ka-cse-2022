# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define production rules and explain their IF-THEN structure in the context of AI knowledge representation

2. Identify and describe the three essential components of a production system: Production Memory, Working Memory, and Inference Engine

3. Compare and contrast forward chaining (data-driven) and backward chaining (goal-driven) reasoning strategies

4. Apply forward chaining and backward chaining to solve problems by tracing rule execution through working memory

5. Explain various conflict resolution strategies including recency, specificity, priority, and first principles

6. Analyze the advantages of production rules (modularity, naturalness, transparency) and their limitations (scalability, represent expressiveness)

7. Design simple production systems for real-world domains like diagnosis, classification, or decision-making scenarios

8. Relate production rules to practical expert system applications and tools like CLIPS used in industry
