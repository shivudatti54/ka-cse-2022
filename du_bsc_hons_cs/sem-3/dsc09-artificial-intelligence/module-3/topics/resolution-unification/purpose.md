## Resolution Unification

**Purpose**  
Resolution and unification are the backbone of automated reasoning and logic‑programming languages, enabling AI systems to infer new knowledge from symbolic representations. Understanding these mechanisms is essential for building intelligent applications such as theorem provers, diagnostic engines, and constraint solvers. This module equips BSc (Hons) Computer Science students with the theoretical foundations and practical skills required to design and implement reasoning engines within the NEP 2024 UGCF framework.

**Learning Objectives**  
- **Define** unification and resolution in the context of first‑order logic.  
- **Explain** the unification algorithm and its role in computing most‑general unifiers.  
- **Apply** unification to pairs of literals to derive compatible term substitutions.  
- **Apply** the resolution inference rule to generate new clauses from a given knowledge base.  
- **Analyze** the soundness and completeness properties of resolution‑based inference.  
- **Implement** a basic unification algorithm in a programming language (e.g., Python).  
- **Evaluate** the computational complexity of resolution and identify optimization strategies (e.g., set‑of‑support, linear resolution).  
- **Compare** different resolution strategies and select appropriate ones for specific AI problem domains.
