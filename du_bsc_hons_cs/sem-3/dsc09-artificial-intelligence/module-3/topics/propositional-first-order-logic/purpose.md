### Purpose
Propositional and first‑order logic form the mathematical backbone of knowledge representation and automated reasoning in computer science. They power AI techniques such as logic programming, planning, and knowledge graphs, as well as formal methods used in software verification and specification. Mastering these logics enables students to build explainable, reliable intelligent systems and to reason rigorously about complex computational problems.

### Learning Objectives
- **Define** the syntax and semantics of propositional and first‑order logic.  
- **Apply** truth‑table methods and logical equivalences to evaluate and simplify propositions.  
- **Construct** formal proofs using inference rules such as modus ponens, resolution, and natural deduction.  
- **Differentiate** between propositional and first‑order logic, focusing on quantifiers, predicates, and variable binding.  
- **Formalize** real‑world statements and problem specifications in first‑order logic.  
- **Implement** basic automated reasoning algorithms (e.g., SAT solving, resolution).  
- **Analyze** the decidability and computational complexity of logical inference tasks.  
- **Evaluate** the role of logic in AI applications such as knowledge representation, planning, and rule‑based systems.
