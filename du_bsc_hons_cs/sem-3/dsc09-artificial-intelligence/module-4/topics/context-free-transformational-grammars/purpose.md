## Purpose  

Context‑Free Transformational Grammars provide a formal mechanism for modeling the hierarchical structure of both natural and programming languages, enabling AI systems to perform accurate syntactic analysis, semantic interpretation, and language generation. Understanding these grammars equips computer scientists with the theoretical foundation and practical algorithms needed to build robust natural language processing, machine translation, and automated code‑synthesis applications.

### Learning Objectives  

- **Explain** the core concepts of context‑free grammars and identify their limitations in capturing natural‑language phenomena.  
- **Describe** how transformational rules extend context‑free grammars to represent syntactic transformations such as movement and constituency changes.  
- **Analyze** the computational complexity of parsing algorithms for context‑free and transformational grammars.  
- **Design** context‑free and transformational grammars that encode specific syntactic patterns for given language fragments.  
- **Implement** parsing algorithms (e.g., CYK, Earley, or chart parsing) to process grammars with transformational productions.  
- **Compare** the expressive power and efficiency of different grammatical models in AI‑driven language tasks.  
- **Apply** transformational grammar techniques to practical problems such as syntactic parsing, machine translation, or code generation.  
- **Evaluate** the performance of grammar‑based NLP systems using appropriate metrics and interpret the results.
