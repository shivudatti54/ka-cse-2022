## Purpose
Parsing techniques form the backbone of many real‑world Computer Science systems, from compilers and code analysis tools to natural‑language interfaces and knowledge‑representation systems in AI. Mastery of these methods equips students to design efficient language processors, build robust semantic parsers, and integrate syntactic understanding into AI applications such as chatbots, formal verification, and automated reasoning.

## Learning Objectives
- **Explain** the role of lexical analysis and tokenization in the overall parsing pipeline.  
- **Implement** a top‑down recursive‑descent parser for a given context‑free grammar.  
- **Design** a context‑free grammar and associated parse tables for a small programming language.  
- **Apply** parser‑generation tools (e.g., ANTLR, Yacc) to construct a syntax analyzer with semantic actions.  
- **Analyze** the complexity and trade‑offs of LL, LR, and LALR parsing algorithms.  
- **Compare** deterministic and non‑deterministic parsing techniques in terms of error recovery and ambiguity handling.  
- **Utilize** semantic actions to construct Abstract Syntax Trees (ASTs) and support downstream AI tasks.  
- **Evaluate** modern parsing approaches (e.g., neural and statistical parsers) for natural‑language understanding within AI systems.
