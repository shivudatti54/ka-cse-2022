### Purpose  
Game‑playing algorithms are the cornerstone of many AI systems, from classic board games to modern video games and decision‑support tools. The Minimax algorithm paired with alpha‑beta pruning supplies an efficient mechanism to explore game trees, allowing intelligent agents to make optimal or near‑optimal moves within stringent time limits. Mastering these techniques equips students with essential skills for building agents that reason under uncertainty and limited computational resources.

### Learning Objectives  
- **Explain** the theoretical basis of the Minimax algorithm and its role in two‑player zero‑sum games.  
- **Implement** Minimax with alpha‑beta pruning in code, ensuring correct handling of the alpha and beta parameters.  
- **Analyze** the time‑ and space‑complexity improvements achieved by alpha‑beta pruning compared to plain Minimax.  
- **Compare** various evaluation functions and discuss their impact on decision quality and search depth.  
- **Apply** the algorithm to a concrete two‑player game (e.g., Tic‑Tac‑Toe, Connect Four) and assess its performance.  
- **Evaluate** the trade‑offs between search depth, branching factor, and real‑time response in practical scenarios.  
- **Discuss** limitations of basic alpha‑beta pruning and contemporary extensions (e.g., iterative deepening, transposition tables).  
- **Design** enhancements or hybrid strategies (e.g., Monte‑Carlo Tree Search) that build on the Minimax framework.
