### Purpose
In the context of the BSc (Hons) Computer Science curriculum (NEP 2024 UGCF), mastering heuristic search techniques is essential for solving complex decision‑making problems in areas such as autonomous navigation, game development, and logistics. The A* algorithm, a cornerstone of Best‑First search, combines an admissible heuristic with cost minimisation to deliver optimal solutions efficiently, making it a vital tool for both academic study and practical applications.

### Learning Objectives
- Explain the theoretical foundations of heuristic search and the role of admissible heuristics.  
- Analyze the completeness, optimality, and time/space complexity of the A* algorithm.  
- Implement Best‑First and A* search algorithms in code for a representative domain (e.g., grid‑based pathfinding).  
- Compare the performance and solution quality of greedy Best‑First, A*, and uniform‑cost search on benchmark problems.  
- Evaluate the impact of heuristic admissibility and consistency on A*’s efficiency and optimality.  
- Design appropriate heuristic functions for real‑world problems such as route planning, puzzle solving, and robot navigation.
