# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental differences between informed and uninformed search algorithms in AI.

2. Describe the Breadth-First Search algorithm, including its data structure, completeness property, and optimality guarantees.

3. Implement and trace through Depth-First Search operations, understanding when to use recursion versus explicit stack.

4. Analyze the time and space complexity of BFS and DFS in terms of branching factor and depth.

5. Apply Hill Climbing as a local search technique for optimization problems with appropriate heuristics.

6. Compare and contrast the advantages and disadvantages of BFS, DFS, and Hill Climbing in various problem contexts.

7. Identify the limitations of Hill Climbing, including local maxima, plateaus, and ridges, and propose solutions.

8. Select appropriate search strategies based on problem characteristics such as solution depth, state space size, and optimality requirements.
