# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the origin, structure, and purpose of the Turing Test as proposed by Alan Turing in 1950.

2. Describe the standard Turing Test and the extended Total Turing Test, including their respective requirements and limitations.

3. Analyze the Chinese Room Argument by John Searle and evaluate its implications for machine intelligence and consciousness.

4. Classify AI systems according to capability-based categories: Artificial Narrow Intelligence (ANI), Artificial General Intelligence (AGI), and Artificial Super Intelligence (ASI).

5. Classify AI systems according to functionality-based categories: reactive machines, limited memory AI, theory of mind AI, and self-aware AI.

6. Differentiate between weak AI (narrow AI) and strong AI (general AI) and provide examples of each.

7. Critically evaluate the significance and limitations of the Turing Test as a measure of machine intelligence.

8. Apply these classification frameworks to analyze real-world AI systems and determine their appropriate categories.
