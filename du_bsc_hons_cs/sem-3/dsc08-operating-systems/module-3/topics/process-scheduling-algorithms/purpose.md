# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the concepts of CPU scheduler, dispatcher, and scheduling criteria (CPU utilization, throughput, turnaround time, waiting time, response time).

2. Differentiate between preemptive and non-preemptive scheduling algorithms and their implications on system performance.

3. Analyze and implement the First Come First Served (FCFS) scheduling algorithm and calculate waiting time, turnaround time, and throughput.

4. Apply the Shortest Job First (SJF) algorithm in both preemptive and non-preemptive modes, and explain why SJF provides optimal average waiting time.

5. Implement Priority Scheduling (with and without preemption) and discuss the problem of starvation along with the aging solution.

6. Demonstrate the Round Robin scheduling algorithm with a given time quantum and evaluate its performance characteristics.

7. Compare and contrast Multilevel Queue and Multilevel Feedback Queue scheduling algorithms and explain their advantages and disadvantages.

8. Select appropriate scheduling algorithms for different types of systems (batch, interactive, real-time) based on their performance requirements.
