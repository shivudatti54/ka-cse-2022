# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the internal structure of magnetic disks including platters, tracks, sectors, cylinders, and the relationship between these components.

2. Calculate disk access time by combining seek time, rotational latency, transfer time, and controller overhead.

3. Analyze and compare different disk scheduling algorithms (FCFS, SSTF, SCAN, C-SCAN, LOOK) with respect to throughput, average response time, and fairness.

4. Describe the architecture of Solid State Drives including NAND flash types (SLC, MLC, TLC, QLC), pages, blocks, and the controller's role.

5. Evaluate the performance differences between HDDs and SSDs, understanding why SSDs offer superior random access performance.

6. Explain SSD-specific concepts including wear leveling, garbage collection, TRIM, and write amplification.

7. Understand RAID configurations (RAID 0, 1, 5, 6, 10) and their trade-offs between performance, capacity, and fault tolerance.

8. Apply disk scheduling algorithms to solve numerical problems and determine head movement sequences.
