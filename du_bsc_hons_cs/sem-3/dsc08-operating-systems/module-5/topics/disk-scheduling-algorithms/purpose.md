**Purpose**  
Disk scheduling algorithms are fundamental to the performance of modern operating systems, directly influencing throughput, response time, and overall system efficiency. Understanding these algorithms equips computer scientists to design and optimize storage subsystems in everything from personal computers to large‑scale data centers and cloud services.

**Learning Objectives**  
- **Describe** the role of disk scheduling in managing I/O requests and its impact on system performance.  
- **Identify** the key metrics (seek time, rotational latency, throughput, and starvation) used to evaluate disk scheduling algorithms.  
- **Compare** common algorithms such as FCFS, SSTF, SCAN, C‑SCAN, LOOK, and C‑LOOK in terms of efficiency and fairness.  
- **Analyze** how different access patterns (sequential vs. random) affect the performance of each algorithm.  
- **Implement** a simulation of at least two disk scheduling algorithms to observe their behavior under various workloads.  
- **Evaluate** the trade‑offs between seek optimization and response time in real‑world storage environments.  
- **Differentiate** traditional magnetic‑disk scheduling from scheduling considerations for solid‑state drives (SSDs).  
- **Design** a strategy to select an appropriate disk scheduling algorithm based on specific application requirements and hardware characteristics.
