## Purpose & Learning Objectives

Understanding the definition, purpose, and structure of an operating system is essential because it underpins all software‑hardware interaction, enabling efficient resource management, security, and user experience in every computing device. Mastery of these concepts equips students with the foundation to design, develop, and optimize reliable systems that meet the demands of modern real‑world applications.

- **Define** the term “operating system” and articulate its core functions in a computing environment.  
- **Explain** the historical evolution of operating systems and how past design decisions influence contemporary systems.  
- **Identify** the principal components of an OS (kernel, memory manager, process scheduler, file system, I/O subsystem).  
- **Compare** monolithic, microkernel, and hybrid OS architectures with respect to performance, reliability, and security.  
- **Analyze** the OS role in resource allocation, including CPU scheduling, memory management, and device handling.  
- **Evaluate** trade‑offs among multiprogramming, multitasking, and multiuser models in terms of throughput and responsiveness.  
- **Design** a simple OS structure (e.g., layered or modular) demonstrating principles of abstraction and modularity.  
- **Assess** the impact of OS design choices on system reliability, security, and energy efficiency in real‑world deployments.
