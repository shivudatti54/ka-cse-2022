### Purpose  
Operating systems are the foundation of every computing device, acting as the central authority that allocates and manages hardware resources such as the CPU, memory, I/O devices, and storage. Mastering how an OS functions as a resource manager is crucial for building efficient, scalable, and reliable software and for diagnosing performance bottlenecks in real‑world systems—from mobile phones to cloud‑based data centers.

### Learning Objectives  
- **Explain** the role of an operating system as a resource manager and its impact on system behavior.  
- **Analyze** various CPU‑scheduling algorithms (e.g., FCFS, SJF, Round‑Robin) and evaluate their performance trade‑offs.  
- **Compare** memory‑management techniques such as paging, segmentation, and virtual memory, and assess their efficiency.  
- **Identify** deadlock conditions and apply prevention, avoidance, and detection strategies in multitasking environments.  
- **Design** a simple scheduling policy for a given workload, considering criteria like throughput, turnaround time, and fairness.  
- **Implement** process synchronization primitives (e.g., semaphores, mutexes) to ensure safe resource sharing among concurrent tasks.  
- **Evaluate** the effect of resource allocation policies on overall system performance and energy consumption.
