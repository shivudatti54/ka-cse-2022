## Purpose and Learning Objectives

Page replacement algorithms are fundamental to virtual memory management in operating systems, enabling efficient use of limited physical memory while providing the illusion of a large address space. Understanding these algorithms is crucial for developing high‑performance systems, optimizing resource utilization, and diagnosing memory‑related bottlenecks in real‑world applications.

- Explain the concept of paging and the need for page replacement.  
- Analyze the working of classic algorithms such as FIFO, LRU, and Optimal.  
- Compare the algorithms in terms of page fault rates and computational overhead.  
- Implement a page replacement simulator to evaluate algorithm performance under various workloads.  
- Design and justify a page replacement strategy for a given system configuration.  
- Evaluate the impact of page replacement on system throughput and response time.  
- Critique the assumptions and limitations of theoretical algorithms in practical operating systems.
