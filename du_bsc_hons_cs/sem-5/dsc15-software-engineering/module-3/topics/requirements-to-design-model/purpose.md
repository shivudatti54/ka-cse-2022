**Purpose**  
Understanding how to move from raw stakeholder requirements to a coherent design model is essential for building reliable, maintainable software systems. Mastery of the Requirements‑to‑Design transition equips computer scientists with systematic methods to translate ambiguous needs into precise specifications, reduce costly rework, and ensure that the final product fulfills both functional and quality expectations.

**Learning Objectives**  
- **Identify** stakeholder needs using elicitation techniques such as interviews, surveys, and prototyping.  
- **Differentiate** functional requirements from non‑functional quality attributes (performance, security, usability, etc.).  
- **Create** use‑case, activity, and domain models to capture and communicate system behavior.  
- **Establish** traceability links between requirements and design artifacts to ensure completeness and consistency.  
- **Assess** design alternatives against defined quality criteria and stakeholder constraints.  
- **Translate** validated requirements into high‑level architectural and detailed design models (e.g., UML class and sequence diagrams).
