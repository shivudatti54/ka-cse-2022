## Purpose and Learning Objectives

### Purpose
In modern software development, delivering reliable, secure, and high‑performance products is essential to meet user expectations, reduce maintenance costs, and maintain competitive advantage. Quality Control and Assurance (QC/QA) provide systematic processes, techniques, and metrics to detect defects early, verify functional and non‑functional requirements, and continuously improve development practices, thereby ensuring compliance with industry standards and stakeholder confidence.

### Learning Objectives
- **Explain** the concepts of quality control and quality assurance and their role in the software development lifecycle.  
- **Apply** various testing techniques (unit, integration, system, acceptance) to identify defects at different stages of development.  
- **Design** test strategies and test plans aligned with project requirements, risk assessment, and coverage objectives.  
- **Implement** automated testing frameworks and continuous integration pipelines to enable rapid feedback and regression testing.  
- **Evaluate** software quality metrics (e.g., defect density, code coverage, mean time to failure) to inform improvement decisions.  
- **Analyze** quality models and standards such as ISO/IEC 25010 and CMMI for compliance and benchmarking.  
- **Differentiate** between verification and validation activities and select appropriate methods for each.  
- **Construct** comprehensive test suites and documentation that meet regulatory, organizational, and client quality goals.
