# Risk Identification Projection Mitigation
## Software Engineering — BSc (Hons) CS (NEP 2024 UGCF, Delhi University)

### Introduction
Risk management is a critical phase in software engineering that ensures project success by proactively identifying, analyzing, and addressing potential threats. The **Risk Identification → Projection → Mitigation** framework helps teams minimize cost overruns, schedule delays, and project failures.

---

### Key Concepts

**Risk Identification**
- Process of discovering and documenting potential risks that may affect project objectives
- **Techniques**: Brainstorming, Delphi technique, SWOT analysis, checklist analysis, root cause analysis
- **Categories**:
  - *Project Risks*: Budget, schedule, resource constraints
  - *Technical Risks*: Technology obsolescence, performance issues, security vulnerabilities
  - *Organizational Risks*: Staff turnover, stakeholder conflicts
  - *External Risks*: Regulatory changes, market shifts, natural disasters

**Risk Projection (Risk Analysis)**
- Estimates the probability and impact of identified risks
- **Quantitative Methods**: Expected Monetary Value (EMV), Monte Carlo simulation, PERT analysis
- **Qualitative Methods**: Risk matrix (Probability × Impact), risk urgency assessment
- **Risk Prioritization**: High, Medium, Low based on severity scores

**Risk Mitigation**
- Develops strategies to reduce or eliminate risks
- **Avoidance**: Change project plan to eliminate risk
- **Transference**: Shift impact to third parties (insurance, contracts)
- **Minimization**: Reduce probability/impact through controls
- **Acceptance**: Acknowledge and prepare contingency plans for unavoidable risks

---

### Risk Management Process (UGCF Aligned)

| Phase | Activities |
|-------|------------|
| Identification | List risks, categorize, document |
| Projection | Analyze probability, estimate impact |
| Mitigation | Plan responses, monitor continuously |
| Control | Track, review, update risk register |

---

### Conclusion
Effective risk management is essential for successful software project delivery. The **Identify → Project → Mitigate** framework enables systematic risk handling, ensuring timely delivery within budget while maintaining quality standards—a key competency for software engineers under Delhi University's NEP 2024 curriculum.
