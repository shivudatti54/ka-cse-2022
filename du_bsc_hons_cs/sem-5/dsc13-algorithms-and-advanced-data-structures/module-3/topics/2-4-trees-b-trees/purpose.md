# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and explain the structural properties of 2-4 trees including node types (2-node, 3-node, and 4-node)

2. Describe the characteristics and properties of B-trees, including the relationship between order (m) and minimum degree (t)

3. Compare and contrast 2-4 trees with B-trees, identifying their similarities and differences

4. Trace through search, insertion, and deletion operations on both 2-4 trees and B-trees with examples

5. Analyze the time complexity of fundamental operations on 2-4 trees and B-trees

6. Explain why B-trees are preferred for database indexing and file systems, relating to disk I/O optimization

7. Apply the splitting and merging rules during tree modification operations

8. Calculate the maximum and minimum height of a B-tree given the number of keys and order
