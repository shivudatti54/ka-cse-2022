# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and distinguish between the complexity classes P, NP, NP-Complete, and NP-Hard with precise formal definitions.

2. Explain the significance of the P versus NP problem and its implications for computer science.

3. Identify whether a given problem belongs to P, NP, NP-Complete, or NP-Hard based on its properties.

4. Construct polynomial-time reductions between NP-complete problems to prove NP-completeness.

5. Apply Cook-Levin theorem and use SAT/3-SAT as the foundation for proving other problems NP-complete.

6. Differentiate between decision problems and optimization problems, and classify them appropriately in complexity hierarchies.

7. Provide polynomial-time verifiable certificates for problems in NP and explain why certain problems are not in NP.

8. Analyze practical implications of computational complexity in real-world algorithm design and problem-solving scenarios.
