# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the difference between hierarchical and non-hierarchical clustering.
2. Describe the types of hierarchical clustering (agglomerative and divisive).
3. Define and calculate different distance metrics (Euclidean, Manhattan, Minkowski, and cosine similarity).
4. Apply hierarchical and non-hierarchical clustering algorithms to real-world problems.
5. Choose the right distance metric for a given problem.
6. Interpret the results of clustering algorithms.
7. Evaluate the performance of clustering algorithms.
8. Identify the strengths and weaknesses of different clustering algorithms.
