# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define the components of a Decision Tree, including root nodes, internal nodes, and leaf nodes.
2. Explain the different types of Decision Trees, including classification and regression trees.
3. Describe the process of constructing a Decision Tree using a splitting criterion.
4. Identify the importance of pruning in Decision Trees.
5. Apply Decision Trees to real-world problems.
6. Evaluate the performance of a Decision Tree using metrics such as accuracy and mean squared error.
7. Recognize the limitations of Decision Trees, including overfitting and underfitting.
8. Use Decision Trees as a building block for more complex algorithms, such as Random Forests and Gradient Boosting.
