**Purpose**

Naive Bayes classifiers are a cornerstone of real‑world Machine Learning, providing fast, scalable, and interpretable solutions for tasks such as spam detection, sentiment analysis, and medical diagnosis. Understanding this algorithm equips students with a solid grasp of probabilistic reasoning and the ability to build efficient classification pipelines in industry‑grade applications.

**Learning Objectives**

- Explain the Bayes theorem and its role in probabilistic classification.  
- Apply the Naive Bayes algorithm to categorical and text‑based datasets using appropriate variants (e.g., Multinomial, Bernoulli, Gaussian).  
- Implement Naive Bayes models in Python with libraries such as scikit‑learn.  
- Evaluate classifier performance using metrics like accuracy, precision, recall, and F1‑score.  
- Compare Naive Bayes against other classifiers (e.g., Decision Trees, Logistic Regression) in terms of speed, accuracy, and suitability.  
- Analyze the impact of feature independence assumptions on model performance and identify scenarios where they may be violated.  
- Utilize smoothing techniques (e.g., Laplace smoothing) to handle zero‑frequency problems.  
- Design and execute a complete end‑to‑end Naive Bayes pipeline from data preprocessing to model deployment.
