# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Distinguish between virtual circuit switching and datagram switching based on their operational characteristics, connection management, and forwarding mechanisms.

2. Explain the three phases of virtual circuit operation: connection establishment, data transfer, and connection teardown.

3. Compare the advantages and disadvantages of virtual circuits versus datagrams in terms of reliability, scalability, quality of service, and implementation complexity.

4. Identify and describe the different addressing schemes used at various OSI layers, including MAC addresses at the data link layer and IP addresses at the network layer.

5. Perform IP address subnetting calculations, including determining network addresses, broadcast addresses, and usable host ranges given an IP address and subnet mask.

6. Understand the hierarchical nature of IP addressing and explain how CIDR enables efficient route aggregation and scalable routing in the Internet.

7. Analyze how routing decisions differ between virtual circuit networks (path predetermined) and datagram networks (per-packet routing).

8. Evaluate the practical applications and real-world examples of each switching paradigm in contemporary and legacy networking technologies.
