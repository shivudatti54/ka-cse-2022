## Purpose  
Understanding IPv4 and IPv6 is essential for designing, implementing, and managing modern networks, as these protocols form the backbone of Internet communication and dictate how data is addressed, routed, and delivered across diverse infrastructures. Mastery of these protocols enables students to address the challenges of IP address scarcity, security, and the ongoing global transition from IPv4 to IPv6.

## Learning Objectives
- **Explain** the structure, addressing scheme, and packet format of IPv4 and IPv6.  
- **Compare** the key differences between IPv4 and IPv6, including address space, header complexity, and security features.  
- **Analyze** the operation of key network‑layer mechanisms such as subnetting, NAT, and address autoconfiguration.  
- **Design** appropriate IP addressing and subnetting plans for given network requirements using both IPv4 and IPv6.  
- **Evaluate** the impact of IPv4 exhaustion and the rationale behind IPv6 adoption in real‑world scenarios.  
- **Configure** routers and hosts to operate in IPv4‑only, IPv6‑only, and dual‑stack environments.  
- **Implement** basic routing protocols and IPsec for secure communication in IPv6 networks.  
- **Differentiate** between stateless address autoconfiguration (SLAAC) and DHCPv6, and select the appropriate method for specific network contexts.
