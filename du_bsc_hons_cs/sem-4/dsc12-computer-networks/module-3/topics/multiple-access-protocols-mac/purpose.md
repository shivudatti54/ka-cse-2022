## Purpose  
Understanding Multiple Access (MAC) protocols is crucial for designing reliable, high‑performance communication networks that share a common transmission medium, such as Ethernet or Wi‑Fi. These protocols directly determine channel utilization, latency, and fairness, which are key metrics in real‑world CS applications ranging from local area networks to wireless sensor systems. Mastering this topic equips students with the analytical and design skills needed to evaluate, compare, and optimize channel access mechanisms in modern networked systems.

## Learning Objectives
- **Explain** the fundamental channel allocation problem in shared‑medium networks.  
- **Compare** the operation and performance of random‑access protocols (ALOHA, Slotted ALOHA, CSMA, CSMA/CD, CSMA/CA).  
- **Analyze** contention‑free protocols such as Token Ring, Token Bus, and FDDI.  
- **Design** a simple MAC protocol for a given network scenario, specifying rules for channel acquisition and release.  
- **Evaluate** the trade‑offs among throughput, delay, and fairness for different MAC schemes.  
- **Differentiate** between contention‑based and contention‑free MAC mechanisms and their suitability for various environments.  
- **Identify** the role of MAC addresses in frame addressing and delivery within a LAN.  
- **Apply** analytical models (e.g., throughput‑vs‑load curves) to predict the efficiency of ALOHA and CSMA variants.
