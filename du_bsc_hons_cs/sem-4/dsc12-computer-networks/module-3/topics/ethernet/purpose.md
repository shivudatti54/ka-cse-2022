# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the historical evolution of Ethernet technology from 10 Mbps to 400 Gbps standards and describe the IEEE 802.3 standard architecture.

2. Analyze the complete Ethernet frame structure, including all fields from Preamble through FCS, and understand their specific roles in network communication.

3. Demonstrate understanding of the CSMA/CD protocol, including carrier sensing, collision detection, and the binary exponential backoff algorithm.

4. Interpret MAC addresses, including their 48-bit structure, OUI assignment, and distinguish between unicast, multicast, and broadcast addresses.

5. Compare different Ethernet topologies (bus vs. star) and evaluate the advantages of switched Ethernet over hub-based architectures.

6. Differentiate between collision domains and broadcast domains, and explain how network devices (hubs, switches, routers) affect these domains.

7. Apply knowledge of Ethernet standards naming conventions to decode specifications like 10BASE-T, 1000BASE-LX, and 10GBASE-T.

8. Solve numerical problems related to minimum frame size calculation and collision detection timing in Ethernet networks.
