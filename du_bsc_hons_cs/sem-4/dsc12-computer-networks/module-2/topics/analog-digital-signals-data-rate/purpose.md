# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Distinguish between analog and digital signals with examples from real-world communication systems.

2. Explain the key properties of signals: amplitude, frequency, phase, and wavelength, and their significance in data transmission.

3. Differentiate between bit rate and baud rate, and establish the mathematical relationship between them.

4. Apply Nyquist Theorem to calculate maximum data rate for a noiseless channel with given bandwidth and signal levels.

5. Apply Shannon's Capacity Theorem to calculate theoretical maximum capacity of a noisy channel.

6. Convert Signal-to-Noise Ratio from decibels to actual ratio and vice versa.

7. Analyze how bandwidth and noise affect data transmission rates in computer networks.

8. Evaluate the practical limitations of increasing data rates in communication systems.
