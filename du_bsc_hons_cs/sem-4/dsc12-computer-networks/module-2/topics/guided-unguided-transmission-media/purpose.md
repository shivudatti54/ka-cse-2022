### Purpose
Understanding guided (wired) and unguided (wireless) transmission media is essential for designing, deploying, and maintaining efficient, reliable, and scalable computer networks. This knowledge enables future professionals to select appropriate media based on bandwidth, distance, cost, and environmental factors, and to troubleshoot connectivity issues in real‑world systems.

### Learning Objectives
- **Identify** the main types of guided (e.g., twisted pair, coaxial, fiber‑optic) and unguided (e.g., radio, microwave, infrared) transmission media.  
- **Compare** the characteristics—such as bandwidth, attenuation, interference susceptibility, and cost—of each media type.  
- **Explain** the principles of signal propagation, modulation, and encoding for both guided and unguided media.  
- **Analyze** the performance trade‑offs and suitability of media for various network scenarios (LAN, WAN, wireless PAN).  
- **Design** a basic network layout selecting appropriate transmission media to meet given performance and budget constraints.  
- **Evaluate** the impact of physical layer impairments (e.g., noise, signal loss) on network reliability and propose mitigation strategies.  
- **Describe** emerging trends in transmission technology, such as 5G, Wi‑Fi 6, and advanced fiber‑optic standards.
