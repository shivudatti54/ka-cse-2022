## Purpose
Multiplexing techniques—Frequency Division Multiplexing (FDM), Time Division Multiplexing (TDM), and Wavelength Division Multiplexing (WDM)—are fundamental for maximizing the capacity and efficiency of modern communication channels. Mastering these methods equips BSc (Hons) Computer Science students with the practical skills to design, analyze, and troubleshoot high‑speed data transmission systems, in line with the NEP 2024 UGCF emphasis on applied network engineering.

## Learning Objectives
- **Explain** the core concept of multiplexing and its importance in optimizing bandwidth utilization.
- **Compare** the operational principles, advantages, and limitations of FDM, TDM, and WDM.
- **Analyze** how each multiplexing technique affects signal integrity, latency, and spectral efficiency.
- **Design** a simple multiplexed communication link using an appropriate scheme for a given transmission medium.
- **Evaluate** the suitability of FDM, TDM, or WDM for various network scenarios (copper, fiber‑optic, wireless).
- **Apply** mathematical models to calculate channel capacity and bandwidth allocation in multiplexed systems.
- **Synthesize** knowledge of multiplexing to diagnose and resolve common network performance issues.
