**Purpose**  
Understanding transmission modes and the devices that implement them is essential for building efficient, scalable, and reliable computer networks. This topic enables students to analyse data‑flow requirements, select appropriate hardware, and troubleshoot communication problems in real‑world CS environments.

**Learning Objectives**  
- Explain the characteristics of simplex, half‑duplex, and full‑duplex transmission modes.  
- Differentiate between the OSI layers at which repeaters, hubs, bridges, switches, routers, and gateways operate.  
- Analyse the impact of transmission mode choice on bandwidth, latency, and collision domains.  
- Design a small LAN by selecting suitable devices based on speed, topology, and mode requirements.  
- Configure a switch to create VLANs that isolate traffic in different duplex configurations.  
- Evaluate network performance using diagnostic tools and adjust device settings accordingly.  
- Identify common device failures and apply troubleshooting strategies for transmission‑mode issues.
