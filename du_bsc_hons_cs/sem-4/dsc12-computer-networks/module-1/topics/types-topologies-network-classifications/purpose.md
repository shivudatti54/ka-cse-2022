# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Classify computer networks based on geographical scope (PAN, LAN, MAN, WAN, CAN) and explain the characteristics, advantages, and typical use cases of each type.

2. Identify and describe all major network topologies (bus, star, ring, mesh, tree, hybrid) including their working principles, advantages, disadvantages, and practical applications.

3. Compare and contrast different network types and topologies in terms of performance, cost, reliability, and scalability.

4. Explain network classifications based on transmission medium (wired vs wireless), architecture (client-server vs P2P), and ownership (private vs public).

5. Apply the concepts of network types and topologies to design appropriate solutions for given scenarios, such as setting up networks for educational institutions.

6. Analyze real-world examples of networks in the Indian context, including university campus networks, metropolitan area networks, and enterprise networks.

7. Understand the significance of VPN as a virtual network technology and its role in extending private networks over public infrastructure.
