## Purpose
ER Diagram notation is the cornerstone of conceptual database design, providing a visual language to capture entities, attributes, and relationships in a way that is independent of implementation details. Mastering this notation enables computer scientists to translate real‑world requirements into precise data models, facilitating clear communication among developers, stakeholders, and DBAs, and laying the foundation for efficient query construction and system optimization. Proficiency in ER modeling is a sought‑after skill in software development, data engineering, and information‑system management.

## Learning Objectives
- **Identify** the fundamental components of an ER diagram (entities, attributes, relationships).  
- **Explain** the various ER notation styles (Chen, Crow’s foot, UML class diagrams) and their symbols.  
- **Construct** an ER diagram for a given business scenario, including proper entity‑relationship representation.  
- **Apply** cardinality and participation constraints to specify relationship limits and optionalities.  
- **Distinguish** between strong and weak entities and model identifier dependence.  
- **Model** inheritance, specialization, and generalization hierarchies in ER diagrams.  
- **Analyze** an ER schema to detect and eliminate redundancies and design flaws.  
- **Translate** an ER diagram into an equivalent relational schema, defining primary and foreign keys.
