# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and differentiate between entities, entity sets, attributes, and relationships in the context of database systems

2. Identify and classify various types of attributes including simple, composite, single-valued, multi-valued, and derived attributes with appropriate examples

3. Construct Entity-Relationship (ER) diagrams using standard notation conventions for entities, attributes, and relationships

4. Analyze relationship cardinality (1:1, 1:N, M:N) and participation constraints (total vs partial) from real-world scenarios

5. Explain the concept of weak entities and their role in database design, including identification of owner entities and partial keys

6. Determine the degree of relationships (unary, binary, ternary) and apply appropriate modeling techniques for complex associations

7. Transform verbal requirements into complete ER diagrams representing real-world database scenarios
