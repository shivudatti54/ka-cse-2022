## Purpose & Learning Objectives

Understanding how to define and enforce **constraints on relationships** is essential for building reliable, maintainable database systems. These constraints guarantee data integrity, prevent anomalies, and support correct business rules in real‑world applications ranging from banking to healthcare.

- **Identify** the different categories of relationship constraints (entity integrity, referential integrity, domain, and user‑defined constraints).  
- **Define** primary keys, foreign keys, and their roles in enforcing referential integrity.  
- **Explain** cardinality (1:1, 1:N, M:N) and participation (total vs. partial) constraints in ER modeling.  
- **Apply** SQL DDL commands (e.g., `PRIMARY KEY`, `FOREIGN KEY`, `CHECK`, `UNIQUE`) to implement constraints in a relational schema.  
- **Design** relational schemas that avoid insertion, deletion, and update anomalies through proper constraint specification.  
- **Analyze** the impact of cascade actions (`ON DELETE`, `ON UPDATE`) on data consistency.  
- **Evaluate** the effectiveness of constraint enforcement mechanisms in multi‑user DBMS environments.  
- **Implement** constraint testing scenarios to verify data integrity under various transaction conditions.
