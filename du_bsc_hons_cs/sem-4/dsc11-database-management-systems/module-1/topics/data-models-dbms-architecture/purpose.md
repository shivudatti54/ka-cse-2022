**Purpose**  
Understanding data models and the architecture of Database Management Systems is essential for building reliable, scalable, and maintainable software systems. This knowledge enables students to design robust databases, ensure data independence, and effectively leverage DBMS features for real‑world applications.

**Learning Objectives**  
- Explain the three‑schema architecture (internal, conceptual, external) and its role in achieving data abstraction and independence.  
- Compare hierarchical, network, relational, and object‑oriented data models in terms of structure, flexibility, and query capabilities.  
- Analyze the concepts of logical and physical data independence and their impact on system evolution.  
- Design a conceptual schema using Entity‑Relationship (ER) modeling for a given problem domain.  
- Implement a relational schema in SQL, defining tables, keys, constraints, and relationships.  
- Evaluate the effects of different storage structures, indexing strategies, and query optimization techniques on performance.  
- Differentiate among internal, conceptual, and external level representations of data.  
- Apply transaction management principles to enforce ACID properties in a DBMS environment.
