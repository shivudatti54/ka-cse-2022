### Purpose and Learning Objectives  

**Purpose**  
Databases are the backbone of modern information systems, enabling efficient storage, retrieval, and management of vast amounts of data. Understanding the purpose and core characteristics of database systems equips computer science professionals to design reliable, scalable, and secure data solutions that meet real‑world business and research needs.

**Learning Objectives**  
- **Define** the fundamental purpose of a database management system (DBMS) in information processing.  
- **Explain** the key characteristics of databases, including data independence, integrity, security, and concurrency control.  
- **Differentiate** between relational, NoSQL, and object‑oriented database models based on their structural and operational features.  
- **Identify** the components of a DBMS architecture (e.g., storage engine, query processor, transaction manager).  
- **Apply** the principles of data modeling to create normalized relational schemas for typical business scenarios.  
- **Design** a simple database schema, specifying tables, keys, constraints, and relationships.  
- **Evaluate** the trade‑offs of different DBMS characteristics (e.g., performance vs. scalability) in given application contexts.  
- **Analyze** case studies to demonstrate how purpose‑driven database characteristics solve real‑world data challenges.
