## Purpose

Data manipulation is at the heart of virtually every software system. Mastery of the DML statements—SELECT, INSERT, UPDATE, and DELETE—enables students to interact with relational databases, build data‑driven applications, and ensure information accuracy and performance in real‑world computing environments.

## Learning Objectives

- Explain the syntax and semantics of the four fundamental DML statements.  
- Apply INSERT, UPDATE, and DELETE commands to add, modify, and remove data in tables.  
- Demonstrate the use of SELECT with various clauses (WHERE, ORDER BY, GROUP BY, HAVING) to retrieve precise data.  
- Design queries that join multiple tables to synthesize information from related entities.  
- Optimize DML operations through appropriate indexing, transaction control, and batch processing.  
- Analyze the effects of DML statements on data integrity, consistency, and concurrency.  
- Compare ACID properties of transactions and their influence on DML behavior.
