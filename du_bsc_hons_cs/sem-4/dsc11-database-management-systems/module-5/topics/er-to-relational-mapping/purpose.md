## Purpose and Learning Objectives  

The ability to accurately map an Entity‑Relationship (ER) model to a relational schema is a core skill for any database professional. It ensures that the high‑level business requirements captured in an ER diagram are faithfully translated into a set of tables that can be efficiently queried, updated, and maintained in a relational DBMS.  

- **Explain** the components of an ER model and their relational equivalents.  
- **Apply** rules to transform entity types, attributes (simple, composite, multivalued), and relationships into relational tables.  
- **Identify** primary keys, foreign keys, and referential integrity constraints in the converted schema.  
- **Design** normalized relational schemas that preserve the semantics of the original ER diagram.  
- **Compare** one‑to‑one, one‑to‑many, and many‑to‑many relationship mappings and choose appropriate strategies.  
- **Implement** transformations for weak entities and derived attributes.  
- **Analyze** the impact of the mapping on query performance and data integrity.  
- **Evaluate** the resulting relational design against the original business requirements.
