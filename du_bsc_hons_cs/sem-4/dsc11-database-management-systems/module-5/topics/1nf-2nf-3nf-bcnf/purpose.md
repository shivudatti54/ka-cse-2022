# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and explain the concept of functional dependencies in relational database design
2. Identify candidate keys, prime attributes, and non-prime attributes in a given relation
3. Explain the requirements of First Normal Form (1NF) and convert unnormalized relations to 1NF
4. Determine whether a relation is in Second Normal Form (2NF) and eliminate partial dependencies
5. Analyze and remove transitive dependencies to achieve Third Normal Form (3NF)
6. Apply Boyce-Codd Normal Form (BCNF) decomposition to eliminate problematic functional dependencies
7. Compare and contrast the different normal forms (1NF, 2NF, 3NF, BCNF) with respect to data redundancy and anomaly prevention
8. Perform step-by-step normalization decomposition while preserving lossless joins and dependencies
