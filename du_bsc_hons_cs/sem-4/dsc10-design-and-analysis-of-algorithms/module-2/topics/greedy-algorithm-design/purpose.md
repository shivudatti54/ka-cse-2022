### Purpose and Learning Objectives

**Purpose:**
Greedy Algorithm Design is a cornerstone of efficient problem-solving in computer science, providing optimal or near-optimal solutions for complex optimization problems in fields such as network routing, data compression, and resource management. Mastering this technique equips students with the ability to make locally optimal choices to achieve global solutions, a critical skill for developing scalable software systems.

**Learning Objectives:**
- **Identify** the fundamental properties (optimal substructure and greedy-choice property) that characterize greedy algorithms.
- **Apply** greedy strategies to solve classic optimization problems such as Minimum Spanning Tree (Kruskal’s/Prim’s) and Shortest Path (Dijkstra’s).
- **Analyze** the correctness of a greedy algorithm using proof by induction or contradiction.
- **Compare** the greedy approach with other algorithmic paradigms like Divide and Conquer and Dynamic Programming.
- **Design** efficient greedy solutions for real-world scenarios, including task scheduling and data compression (e.g., Huffman coding).
- **Evaluate** the time and space complexity of greedy algorithms to ensure scalability.
