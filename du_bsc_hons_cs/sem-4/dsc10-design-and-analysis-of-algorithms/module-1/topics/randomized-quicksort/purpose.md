# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. **Explain** the fundamental limitations of standard quicksort and why worst-case scenarios occur in practice.

2. **Describe** the randomized quicksort algorithm and its pivot selection strategy using random sampling.

3. **Implement** randomized quicksort in Python or C++ with proper random pivot selection before partitioning.

4. **Analyze** the expected running time of randomized quicksort by setting up and solving recurrence relations.

5. **Prove** that the expected time complexity of randomized quicksort is O(n log n) using probability theory.

6. **Distinguish** between worst-case, average-case, and expected-case time complexities and explain their practical significance.

7. **Apply** tail bounds or Chernoff bounds to show that the probability of worst-case behavior becomes negligible for large inputs.

8. **Compare** randomized quicksort with other sorting algorithms (merge sort, heap sort) based on time complexity, space complexity, and practical performance.
