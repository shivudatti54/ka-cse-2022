# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental cloud pricing models (Pay-as-you-go, Reserved Instances, Spot Instances) and identify appropriate use cases for each.

2. Calculate and compare Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) for cloud versus on-premises infrastructure deployments.

3. Apply cost optimization strategies including right-sizing, auto-scaling, resource scheduling, and storage tiering to reduce cloud spending.

4. Analyze the economics of elasticity and scaling, including the relationship between marginal costs, marginal benefits, and optimal scaling thresholds.

5. Distinguish between Capital Expenditure (CapEx) and Operational Expenditure (OpEx) accounting treatments and explain their implications for financial planning.

6. Design cost allocation strategies using tagging and resource-level accounting to attribute cloud costs to business units or projects.

7. Evaluate trade-offs between different scaling approaches and recommend appropriate solutions based on given workload characteristics and budget constraints.

8. Critically assess real-world cloud billing scenarios and provide optimization recommendations that balance performance requirements against cost efficiency.
