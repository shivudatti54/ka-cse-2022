# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define Service Level Agreements (SLAs) in the context of cloud computing and explain their fundamental purpose in provider-customer relationships.

2. Identify and explain the key components of a cloud SLA, including service description, availability metrics, performance parameters, and service credit mechanisms.

3. Calculate downtime allowances and availability percentages using standard SLA formulas for various SLA tiers (99%, 99.9%, 99.99%, etc.).

4. Compare and contrast SLA offerings from major cloud providers (AWS, Azure, GCP), understanding their specific commitments and credit structures.

5. Analyze the shared responsibility model and distinguish between provider and customer responsibilities as defined in cloud SLAs.

6. Apply SLA concepts to design highly available cloud architectures that meet specific business continuity requirements.

7. Evaluate real-world scenarios to determine SLA violations, service credit eligibility, and appropriate remedies.

8. Critically assess SLA exclusions, limitations, and the importance of reading SLA documentation carefully before cloud service adoption.
