## Purpose

Cloud computing has become the backbone of modern IT infrastructure, with over 90% of enterprises now utilizing cloud services for their operations. Understanding the historical evolution of cloud computing and the major service providers is essential for computer science graduates to remain competitive in today's technology-driven job market and make informed decisions about cloud adoption strategies.

## Learning Objectives

- **Identify** the key milestones in the evolution of cloud computing from mainframe computing to modern cloud services
- **Describe** the three main cloud service models (IaaS, PaaS, SaaS) and their distinguishing characteristics
- **Compare** the leading cloud service providers (AWS, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud) in terms of services, market share, and pricing models
- **Analyze** the impact of cloud computing on business models, IT operations, and digital transformation
- **Evaluate** the benefits and challenges of cloud adoption for organizations across various industries
- **Explain** the concept of cloud deployment models (public, private, hybrid, and multi-cloud)
- **Recognize** the security, privacy, and compliance considerations in cloud computing environments
