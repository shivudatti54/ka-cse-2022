# Cloud Computing: Benefits and Limitations

## Introduction
Cloud Computing has revolutionized the way computing resources are delivered and consumed in modern IT infrastructure. As per the Delhi University NEP 2024 UGCF syllabus for BSc (Hons) Computer Science, understanding the advantages and challenges of cloud computing is essential for students. This summary provides a quick revision of the key benefits and limitations.

---

## Benefits of Cloud Computing

### 1. Cost Efficiency
- **Reduced Capital Expenditure:** Eliminates need for upfront hardware purchases
- **Pay-as-you-go Model:** Pay only for used resources
- **Lower Maintenance Costs:** No local server maintenance required

### 2. Scalability and Flexibility
- **On-demand Resources:** Instantly provision resources as needed
- **Elasticity:** Automatically scale up or down based on workload
- **Global Accessibility:** Access services from anywhere with internet

### 3. Reliability and Performance
- **High Availability:** Multiple data centers ensure service continuity
- **Disaster Recovery:** Built-in backup and redundancy mechanisms
- **Performance:** Content Delivery Networks (CDN) improve speed

### 4. Management and Maintenance
- **Automatic Updates:** Service providers handle software updates
- **Centralized Storage:** Data accessible from multiple devices
- **Reduced IT Burden:** Less burden on in-house IT teams

### 5. Collaboration and Accessibility
- **Remote Access:** Teams can collaborate from different locations
- **Multi-tenancy:** Share resources efficiently among users
- **Environmentally Friendly:** Optimized resource utilization reduces energy consumption

---

## Limitations of Cloud Computing

### 1. Security and Privacy Concerns
- **Data Breaches:** Risk of unauthorized access to sensitive data
- **Vendor Lock-in:** Difficulty switching between cloud providers
- **Shared Infrastructure:** Multi-tenant environments pose security risks

### 2. Dependency on Internet Connectivity
- **Always-on Requirement:** Requires stable internet connection
- **Latency Issues:** May cause delays for real-time applications
- **Offline Limitations:** Cannot access services without internet

### 3. Performance and Control Issues
- **Limited Control:** Users have limited control over infrastructure
- **Performance Variability:** Can be affected by network congestion
- **Hardware Dependencies:** Reliance on provider's hardware capabilities

### 4. Cost Management Challenges
- **Unexpected Charges:** Usage can exceed estimates
- **Long-term Costs:** Can become expensive over time
- **Data Egress Fees:** Charges for transferring data out of cloud

### 5. Compliance and Legal Issues
- **Data Residency:** Data stored in different jurisdictions
- **Regulatory Compliance:** Meeting specific industry standards
- **Service Level Agreements (SLAs):** Reliance on provider commitments

---

## Conclusion
Cloud Computing offers significant advantages including cost savings, scalability, and accessibility, making it a preferred choice for modern businesses. However, students must also understand its limitations—security concerns, internet dependency, and control issues—to make informed decisions. For exam preparation, focus on balancing both benefits and limitations while understanding real-world applications.
