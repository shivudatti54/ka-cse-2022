## Purpose  
Cloud computing has revolutionized the way modern organizations provision, scale, and manage IT resources, making it a cornerstone of contemporary software design and service delivery. Understanding the benefits and limitations of cloud services is crucial for computer scientists to make informed architectural decisions, optimize costs, ensure security, and align technology solutions with business objectives.

## Learning Objectives
- **Identify** the core benefits of cloud computing such as scalability, elasticity, cost‑efficiency, and rapid provisioning.  
- **Analyze** the key limitations and challenges, including security concerns, vendor lock‑in, compliance issues, and potential downtime.  
- **Evaluate** trade‑offs between on‑premises and cloud‑based solutions in terms of performance, cost, and operational overhead.  
- **Compare** different cloud service models (IaaS, PaaS, SaaS) and deployment models (public, private, hybrid) with respect to their advantages and drawbacks.  
- **Design** cloud‑native architectures that leverage benefits while mitigating identified limitations.  
- **Assess** the impact of cloud limitations on business continuity, data governance, and regulatory compliance.  
- **Explain** the financial implications of cloud pricing models (e.g., pay‑as‑you‑go, reserved instances) and their effect on total cost of ownership.
