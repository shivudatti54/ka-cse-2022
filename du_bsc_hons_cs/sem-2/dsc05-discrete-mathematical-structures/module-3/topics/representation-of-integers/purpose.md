# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the concept of positional number systems and describe the relationship between base-r representations and their decimal equivalents.

2. Convert integers between binary, octal, decimal, and hexadecimal number systems using appropriate algorithms.

3. Compare and contrast different signed number representations (sign-magnitude, one's complement, and two's complement) including their advantages and limitations.

4. Perform binary arithmetic operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division) using two's complement representation.

5. Determine the range of values representable in n-bit signed and unsigned integer representations.

6. Analyze overflow conditions in binary arithmetic and explain how they are detected in two's complement systems.

7. Apply integer representation concepts to solve practical problems in computer programming and digital logic design.

8. Evaluate why two's complement has become the universal standard for signed integer representation in modern computer systems.
