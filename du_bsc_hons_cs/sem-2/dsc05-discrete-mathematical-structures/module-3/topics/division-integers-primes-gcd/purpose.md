# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Apply the Division Algorithm to express any integer as a quotient and remainder when divided by a positive integer.

2. Identify prime numbers and explain their fundamental properties, including Euclid's proof of the infinitude of primes.

3. State and prove the Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic (Unique Prime Factorization theorem).

4. Compute the Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) of two integers using the Euclidean algorithm.

5. Apply the Extended Euclidean Algorithm to find integers x and y satisfying Bézout's identity: ax + by = gcd(a, b).

6. Determine whether two integers are coprime (relatively prime) based on their GCD.

7. Express composite numbers as products of their prime factors through systematic factorization.

8. Analyze the efficiency of the Euclidean algorithm and explain its significance in computational applications.
