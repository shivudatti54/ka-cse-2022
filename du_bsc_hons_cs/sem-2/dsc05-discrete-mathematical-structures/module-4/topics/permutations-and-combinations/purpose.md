# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Apply the fundamental principle of counting to solve counting problems involving multiple tasks

2. Distinguish between permutations and combinations and identify which concept applies to a given problem

3. Calculate permutations of n objects taken r at a time using the formula P(n,r) = n!/(n-r)!

4. Calculate combinations of n objects taken r at a time using the formula C(n,r) = n!/(r!(n-r)!)

5. Solve problems involving permutations with repeated elements and circular permutations

6. Apply Pascal's identity and symmetry properties of combinations to simplify calculations

7. Use the binomial theorem to expand expressions of the form (a + b)^n and identify specific terms

8. Analyze real-world scenarios and formulate appropriate counting models to solve applied problems
