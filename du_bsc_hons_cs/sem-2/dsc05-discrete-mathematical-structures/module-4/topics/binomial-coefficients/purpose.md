# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define binomial coefficients and compute them using the fundamental formula $\binom{n}{k} = \frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!}$.

2. Apply the Binomial Theorem to expand expressions of the form $(a+b)^n$ and identify specific coefficients in such expansions.

3. Construct and utilize Pascal's Triangle to compute binomial coefficients for small values of n and k.

4. Prove and apply key identities including Pascal's Identity, symmetry property, and Vandermonde's Identity.

5. Provide combinatorial interpretations and proofs for binomial coefficient identities.

6. Evaluate special sums involving binomial coefficients such as $\sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k}$ and related variations.

7. Apply binomial coefficients to solve real-world selection and counting problems in computer science contexts.
