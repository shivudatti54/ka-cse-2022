# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. **Apply the formula for permutations of multi-sets** to count arrangements when some objects are identical, using the division principle with factorials.

2. **Solve combinations with repetition problems** using the stars and bars method and the formula C(n + r - 1, r) for selecting r items from n distinct types with replacement.

3. **Calculate circular permutations** for arrangements around a table, distinguishing between cases where reflection is considered the same or different.

4. **Analyze distribution problems** involving distinct and identical objects into distinct and identical boxes, applying the appropriate counting formulas.

5. **Apply the principle of inclusion-exclusion** to solve complex counting problems with restrictions, including derangement problems.

6. **Distinguish between permutation and combination scenarios** based on whether order matters in the problem statement.

7. **Solve real-world applications** including anagram counting, committee formation with repeated roles, seating arrangements, and resource distribution problems.
