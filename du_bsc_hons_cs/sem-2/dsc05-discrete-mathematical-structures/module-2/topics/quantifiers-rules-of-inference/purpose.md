# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and distinguish between universal and existential quantifiers, understanding their precise logical meanings and notations.

2. Express statements in both symbolic logical form and natural English, converting between these representations accurately.

3. Apply De Morgan's laws for quantifiers to negate quantified statements, demonstrating the relationship between ∀ and ∃ under negation.

4. Identify and correctly apply each of the fundamental rules of inference including Modus Ponens, Modus Tollens, Hypothetical Syllogism, and Disjunctive Syllogism.

5. Construct formal proofs for arguments involving both propositional and quantified statements using the rules of inference.

6. Determine whether a given argument is valid by analyzing its logical structure and identifying any fallacies present.

7. Apply universal and existential instantiation and generalization to move between general statements and specific instances in formal proofs.

8. Evaluate real-world arguments, expressed in natural language, by formalizing them and checking their validity using logical methods.
