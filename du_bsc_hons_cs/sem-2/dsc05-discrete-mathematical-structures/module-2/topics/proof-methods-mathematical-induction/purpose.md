# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. State and explain the Principle of Mathematical Induction (weak induction) and identify its two essential components: base case and inductive step.

2. Construct complete induction proofs for statements about natural numbers, demonstrating logical rigor in establishing the base case and inductive step.

3. Distinguish between weak (simple) mathematical induction and strong (complete) mathematical induction, recognizing scenarios where each is appropriately applied.

4. Apply strong mathematical induction to prove statements where the truth of P(k+1) depends on multiple preceding cases, particularly in number theory and divisibility problems.

5. Analyze and prove properties of recursive algorithms using mathematical induction, establishing correctness of base cases and recursive calls.

6. Explain the logical equivalence between weak induction, strong induction, and the Well-Ordering Principle in discrete mathematics.

7. Identify and avoid common mistakes in induction proofs, such as incorrect base cases, improper use of the inductive hypothesis, or logical fallacies in the inductive step.

8. Apply mathematical induction to prove properties of data structures, including tree heights, recurrence relations, and algorithmic complexity bounds.
