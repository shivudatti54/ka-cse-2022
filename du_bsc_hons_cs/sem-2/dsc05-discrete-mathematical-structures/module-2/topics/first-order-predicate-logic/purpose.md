# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and distinguish between predicates, variables, constants, and quantifiers in first-order predicate logic

2. Construct well-formed formulas using atomic formulas, logical connectives, and quantifiers following proper syntactic rules

3. Translate English statements into precise predicate logic formulas and vice versa, identifying appropriate predicates, variables, and quantifiers

4. Apply De Morgan's laws for quantifiers to negate statements containing universal and existential quantifiers

5. Determine the truth values of quantified statements under given interpretations with specified domains

6. Analyze and distinguish between free and bound variables, and identify whether a formula is a sentence (closed formula)

7. Explain the significance of quantifier order and demonstrate why ∀x∃y P(x,y) and ∃y∀x P(x,y) are not equivalent in general

8. Apply predicate logic concepts to solve problems related to database queries, formal specifications, and mathematical reasoning
