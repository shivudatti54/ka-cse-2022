# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define graph isomorphism and explain the conditions required for two graphs to be isomorphic
2. Identify and verify graph invariants that are preserved under isomorphism
3. Apply the degree sequence test to determine whether two graphs can be isomorphic
4. Define planar graphs and explain what makes a graph planar
5. Apply Euler's formula (n - m + f = 2) to solve problems involving vertices, edges, and faces in planar graphs
6. Use the inequality m ≤ 3n - 6 to test for non-planarity in simple graphs
7. Apply Kuratowski's Theorem to determine whether a given graph is planar or non-planar
8. Explain the concept of graph homeomorphism and its relationship to planarity
9. Construct and interpret dual graphs of plane embeddings
10. Apply these concepts to solve real-world problems in network design and circuit layout
