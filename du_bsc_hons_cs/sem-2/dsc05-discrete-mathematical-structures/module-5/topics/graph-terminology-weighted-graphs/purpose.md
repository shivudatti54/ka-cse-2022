# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and distinguish between fundamental graph concepts including vertices, edges, simple graphs, directed and undirected graphs, loops, and parallel edges.

2. Identify and explain special graph structures such as complete graphs, bipartite graphs, planar graphs, trees, and subgraphs with their distinguishing properties.

3. Calculate the degree of vertices in undirected graphs and in-degree/out-degree in directed graphs, applying the Handshaking Lemma to solve problems.

4. Analyze paths, cycles, and connectivity in graphs, distinguishing between connected, disconnected, strongly connected, and weakly connected directed graphs.

5. Understand weighted graphs as a generalization of basic graphs, where edges carry numerical values representing costs, distances, or capacities.

6. Apply Dijkstra's algorithm to find shortest paths in weighted graphs with non-negative edge weights, demonstrating step-by-step execution.

7. Solve optimization problems using Minimum Spanning Tree (MST) algorithms, specifically Kruskal's and Prim's algorithms, and understand their practical applications.
