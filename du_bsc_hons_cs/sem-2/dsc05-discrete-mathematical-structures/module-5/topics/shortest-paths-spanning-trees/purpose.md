## Purpose
Shortest‑path and spanning‑tree algorithms are fundamental to many real‑world computer‑science applications, from routing packets in the Internet and guiding GPS navigation to designing cost‑effective transportation and communication networks. Understanding these techniques enables students to develop efficient solutions for resource allocation, logistics, and large‑scale data‑processing problems, while also laying the groundwork for advanced algorithmic study.

### Learning Objectives
- **Define** the key concepts of weighted graphs, shortest paths, and minimum spanning trees.  
- **Identify** the optimal‑substructure property that underpins shortest‑path problems.  
- **Apply** Dijkstra’s algorithm to compute single‑source shortest paths in graphs with non‑negative edge weights.  
- **Apply** the Bellman‑Ford algorithm to handle graphs with negative edge weights and detect negative‑weight cycles.  
- **Construct** minimum spanning trees using Prim’s and Kruskal’s algorithms.  
- **Compare** the time‑complexity and suitability of various shortest‑path and MST algorithms in practical contexts.  
- **Analyze** real‑world scenarios (e.g., network routing, transportation planning) to select appropriate algorithms.
