# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and identify partial orders (posets) and verify the three defining properties: reflexivity, antisymmetry, and transitivity

2. Distinguish between comparable and incomparable elements in a poset and represent finite posets using Hasse diagrams

3. Identify maximal, minimal, greatest, and least elements in a given poset and understand the distinction between these concepts

4. Determine upper bounds, lower bounds, least upper bounds (suprema), and greatest lower bounds (infima) for subsets of a poset

5. Define a lattice as a poset where every pair of elements has both supremum (join) and infimum (meet), and verify whether a given poset forms a lattice

6. Explain bounded, complete, distributive, and complemented lattices with examples, and identify the two minimal non-distributive lattices (M₃ and N₅)

7. Apply partial ordering and lattice concepts to solve practical problems in computer science contexts such as dependency analysis and information ordering
