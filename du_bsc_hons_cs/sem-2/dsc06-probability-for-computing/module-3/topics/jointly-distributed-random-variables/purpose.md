# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. **Define and construct** joint probability distributions (PMF, PDF, CDF) for two or more random variables, and verify their validity.

2. **Derive marginal distributions** from a given joint distribution by appropriate summation or integration, and interpret their meaning.

3. **Compute conditional distributions** and conditional expectations, relating them to Bayes' theorem and applications like Bayesian classifiers.

4. **Determine independence** of random variables using the factorization criterion, and distinguish between independence and zero correlation with precise counterexamples.

5. **Calculate covariance and correlation coefficient** for jointly distributed random variables and interpret their significance in terms of linear association.

6. **Set up and evaluate** double integrals and double sums over non-trivial regions to compute probabilities, expectations, and variances of functions of two random variables.

7. **Apply the variance addition formula** $\text{Var}(X+Y) = \text{Var}(X) + \text{Var}(Y) + 2\text{Cov}(X,Y)$ correctly and recognize when the cross term vanishes.

8. **Connect joint distributions to computing applications** such as performance analysis of parallel systems, probabilistic inference in machine learning, and modeling correlated random phenomena in algorithms.
