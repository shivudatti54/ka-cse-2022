# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define covariance and explain its meaning in the context of two random variables
2. Calculate covariance for both discrete and continuous random variables using appropriate formulas
3. State and verify the key properties of covariance including symmetry, relationship to variance, and behavior under linear transformations
4. Define Pearson's correlation coefficient and explain why it is preferred over covariance for comparing relationships
5. Calculate the correlation coefficient from raw data and from given covariance values
6. Interpret correlation values in terms of strength and direction of linear relationships
7. Identify the limitations of covariance and correlation as measures of relationship
8. Apply correlation analysis to practical computing scenarios such as feature selection in machine learning
