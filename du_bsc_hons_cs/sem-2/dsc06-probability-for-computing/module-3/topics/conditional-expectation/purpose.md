# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and interpret conditional expectation E[X | Y = y] for both discrete and continuous random variables

2. Apply the Law of Total Expectation (Tower Rule) to compute expectations by conditioning on appropriate random variables

3. Recognize and use the properties of conditional expectation including linearity, taking out what is known, and behavior under independence

4. Understand that conditional expectation E[X | Y] is itself a random variable that is a function of Y

5. Apply the law of total variance to decompose variance into conditional and between-group components

6. Compute conditional expectations from joint probability distributions systematically

7. Apply conditional expectation concepts to solve computing-related problems in algorithm analysis, data structures, and queuing systems
