# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Apply Markov's Inequality to compute upper bounds on probabilities for non-negative random variables given only the expected value.

2. Apply Chebyshev's Inequality to compute upper bounds on probabilities given the mean and variance of a distribution.

3. Distinguish between the applicability and tightness of Markov's and Chebyshev's inequalities in different scenarios.

4. Explain the Weak Law of Large Numbers and its proof using Chebyshev's Inequality.

5. State and interpret the Central Limit Theorem, understanding its significance in approximating distributions of sample means.

6. Apply the Central Limit Theorem to compute probabilities about sample means for large sample sizes (n ≥ 30).

7. Use standardization to convert problems involving normal approximations to standard normal form for table lookup.

8. Relate these probabilistic inequalities to computing applications such as algorithm analysis, randomized algorithms, and performance estimation.
