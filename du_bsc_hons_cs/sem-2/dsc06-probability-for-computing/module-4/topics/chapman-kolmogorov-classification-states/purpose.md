## Purpose
The Chapman‑Kolmogorov equations are fundamental for modelling and analysing stochastic processes that underlie many real‑world computing systems—such as algorithm performance, network traffic, and data‑mining pipelines. Understanding how to classify states in Markov chains enables you to predict system behaviour, optimise resource allocation, and design robust algorithms (e.g., PageRank, hidden Markov models) used across modern computer science.

## Learning Objectives
- **Explain** the Chapman‑Kolmogorov equations and their role in describing transition probabilities for stochastic processes.  
- **Classify** states of a Markov chain (transient, recurrent, periodic, aperiodic, communicating) using appropriate criteria.  
- **Derive** the n‑step transition matrix for finite‑state Markov chains by applying the Chapman‑Kolmogorov relations.  
- **Apply** Markov chain models to analyse practical computing problems such as queuing systems, routing protocols, and algorithm runtime.  
- **Compute** stationary distributions and limiting probabilities for irreducible, aperiodic Markov chains.  
- **Evaluate** the convergence properties of a Markov chain based on its state classification.  
- **Design** simple stochastic models (e.g., random walks, PageRank) that leverage Chapman‑Kolmogorov principles.  
- **Analyse** real‑world CS applications (web ranking, speech recognition, reliability modelling) by mapping them to state‑classification problems.
