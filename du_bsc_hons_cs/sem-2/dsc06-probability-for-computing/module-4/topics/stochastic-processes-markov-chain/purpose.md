# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and distinguish between stochastic processes and Markov chains in the context of discrete time

2. Explain the Markov property (memoryless property) and articulate its significance in modeling real-world systems

3. Construct the transition probability matrix for a given Markov chain and verify its properties (non-negative entries, row sums equal to 1)

4. Compute n-step transition probabilities using matrix multiplication and the Chapman-Kolmogorov equations

5. Classify states in a Markov chain as transient or recurrent, periodic or aperiodic, and identify absorbing states

6. Determine whether a Markov chain is irreducible and understand its implications

7. Compute the stationary distribution by solving the linear equations πP = π subject to Σπᵢ = 1

8. Apply Markov chain concepts to model and solve computing-related problems such as queueing systems, web navigation, and weather modeling

9. Interpret the long-run behavior of Markov chains and explain the conditions under which convergence to stationary distribution occurs
