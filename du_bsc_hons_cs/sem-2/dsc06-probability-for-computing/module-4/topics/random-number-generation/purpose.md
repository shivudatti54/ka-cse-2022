# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Distinguish between True Random Number Generators (TRNG) and Pseudo-Random Number Generators (PRNG), explaining their respective advantages, limitations, and appropriate use cases.

2. Implement and analyze the Linear Congruential Generator (LCG) algorithm, understanding how parameters a, c, and m affect the quality and period of generated sequences.

3. Evaluate the five key properties of random number generators: uniformity, independence, unpredictability, period length, and reproducibility.

4. Apply the Chi-Square test to verify the uniformity of randomly generated sequences and interpret the results correctly.

5. Explain the concept of seeding in PRNGs and its importance in reproducible scientific computing and debugging.

6. Describe at least three practical applications of random number generation in computing, including Monte Carlo simulation, cryptographic key generation, and statistical sampling.

7. Identify common quality issues in random number generators, such as short periods, serial correlation, and lattice structure, and explain their practical implications.
