**Purpose**  
In modern computer‑science applications—from machine learning and data mining to cryptography and network communications—quantifying uncertainty and information is essential. Mastering entropy, mutual information, and Kullback–Leibler (KL) divergence equips students with the theoretical foundations to design efficient algorithms, measure information gain, and assess probabilistic models in real‑world systems.

**Learning Objectives**  
- **Define** entropy, mutual information, and KL divergence mathematically and interpret their information‑theoretic meanings.  
- **Compute** entropy for discrete probability distributions and explain its role in measuring uncertainty.  
- **Analyze** mutual information to quantify dependencies between random variables and apply it to feature selection and data‑mining tasks.  
- **Derive** KL divergence as a measure of information loss when approximating one distribution with another.  
- **Apply** KL divergence in model selection, Bayesian inference, and variational inference techniques.  
- **Compare** entropy‑based metrics across different algorithmic contexts (e.g., decision trees, clustering, information gain).  
- **Evaluate** trade‑offs between compression efficiency and statistical modeling using information‑theoretic principles.  
- **Implement** basic algorithms to estimate entropy, mutual information, and KL divergence from data.
