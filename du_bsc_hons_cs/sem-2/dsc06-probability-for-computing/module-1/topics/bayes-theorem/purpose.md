# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and explain conditional probability and its relationship to joint probability through the chain rule.

2. State Bayes' Theorem mathematically and explain the meaning of each component: prior probability, likelihood, marginal likelihood, and posterior probability.

3. Apply Bayes' Theorem to solve problems involving inverse probability, particularly in medical diagnosis and classification scenarios.

4. Use the law of total probability to calculate marginal likelihood when computing Bayes' Theorem.

5. Explain the significance of prior probability (base rate) and its impact on posterior probability, especially in scenarios with rare events.

6. Describe how Bayes' Theorem is applied in Naive Bayes classification for tasks like spam filtering and text classification.

7. Analyze real-world computing problems and translate them into probability notation to apply Bayes' Theorem correctly.
