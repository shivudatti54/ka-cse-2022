# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define and identify the sample space for a given random experiment in computing contexts
2. Represent events as subsets of sample spaces and distinguish between simple and compound events
3. Perform operations on events (union, intersection, complement, difference) and interpret their meanings
4. Identify mutually exclusive, exhaustive, and complementary events in various scenarios
5. Apply the three Kolmogorov axioms of probability to verify valid probability assignments
6. Derive and apply fundamental probability rules including the complement rule and inclusion-exclusion principle
7. Construct probability spaces for finite sample spaces with equally likely outcomes
8. Solve real-world computing problems by modeling scenarios as probability spaces and computing event probabilities
9. Apply the law of total probability for computing probabilities of compound events
10. Critically analyze probability calculations and identify common errors in reasoning about uncertain events
