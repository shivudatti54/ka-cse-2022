# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define a pushdown automaton (PDA) formally as a 7-tuple and explain the purpose of each component.

2. Distinguish between deterministic and non-deterministic pushdown automata and explain their differences in language recognition capability.

3. Explain the two methods of acceptance (by final state and by empty stack) and prove their equivalence.

4. Design pushdown automata for simple context-free languages such as {0ⁿ1ⁿ | n ≥ 1} and palindromes.

5. Trace the execution of a PDA on a given input string by writing successive instantaneous descriptions.

6. Understand the relationship between pushdown automata and context-free grammars in language recognition.

7. Apply PDA concepts to solve exam problems involving language acceptance and automaton construction.
