# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Construct parse trees for given strings and grammars.
2. Recognize ambiguous grammars and explain why they are ambiguous.
3. Resolve ambiguity in a grammar using one of the methods discussed in the topic.
4. Apply parsing algorithms, such as the Earley parser and the CYK parser, to resolve ambiguity.
5. Analyze a grammar to determine whether it is ambiguous or unambiguous.
6. Construct unambiguous grammars for given languages.
7. Use parser generator tools, such as yacc or ANTLR, to generate parsers from grammars.
8. Understand the importance of parse trees and ambiguity in the context of formal grammars.
