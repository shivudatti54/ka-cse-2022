## Purpose and Learning Objectives

Understanding the relationship between Context‑Free Grammars (CFGs) and Pushdown Automata (PDAs) is essential for designing efficient parsers and compilers, modelling hierarchical structures in programming languages, and advancing formal verification and natural‑language processing techniques. Mastery of this connection equips computer scientists to build robust software tools that can analyze, transform, and generate syntactically correct code.

- **Explain** the theoretical equivalence between CFGs and PDAs in terms of language recognition.  
- **Construct** a PDA from a given CFG using the standard conversion algorithm.  
- **Convert** a PDA into an equivalent CFG that generates the same language.  
- **Analyze** the role of the stack in PDAs for modelling nested structures and context‑free properties.  
- **Differentiate** between deterministic and nondeterministic PDAs and their relationship to grammar classes.  
- **Design** parse (derivation) trees for strings generated by a CFG and accepted by a PDA.  
- **Apply** the CFG–PDA relationship to real‑world parsing tasks such as compiler front‑ends and syntax‑directed translation.  
- **Prove** pumping‑lemma results for context‑free languages using PDA‑based reasoning.
