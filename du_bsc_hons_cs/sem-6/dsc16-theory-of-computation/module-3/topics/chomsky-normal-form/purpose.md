# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define Chomsky Normal Form (CNF) and state the conditions that productions must satisfy in CNF.

2. Explain the necessity and advantages of converting context-free grammars to CNF in theory of computation.

3. Apply the step-by-step algorithm to eliminate ε-productions from a context-free grammar.

4. Eliminate unit productions from a context-free grammar using the closure method.

5. Remove useless symbols (unreachable and non-generating) from a grammar.

6. Convert productions with more than two symbols on the RHS to binary form by introducing new non-terminals.

7. Convert a given context-free grammar completely to Chomsky Normal Form through systematic application of all steps.

8. Understand the relationship between CNF and parsing algorithms, particularly the CYK algorithm.
