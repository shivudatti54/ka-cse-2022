# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define a context-free grammar (CFG) formally as a 4-tuple G = (V, Σ, R, S) and explain the purpose of each component.

2. Demonstrate derivations by applying production rules to generate strings from the start symbol, distinguishing between leftmost and rightmost derivations.

3. Construct and interpret parse trees (derivation trees) for strings in a CFG-generated language.

4. Identify ambiguity in grammars and determine whether a given grammar is ambiguous by finding multiple parse trees for the same string.

5. Convert a context-free grammar to Chomsky Normal Form (CNF) by systematically applying all required transformations.

6. Convert a context-free grammar to Greibach Normal Form (GNF) and explain its significance in PDA construction.

7. Apply the pumping lemma for context-free languages to prove that certain languages are not context-free.

8. Connect CFGs with pushdown automata and explain how they form equivalent computational models for context-free languages.
