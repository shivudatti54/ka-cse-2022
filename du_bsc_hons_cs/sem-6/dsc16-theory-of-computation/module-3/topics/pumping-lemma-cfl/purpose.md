## Purpose & Learning Objectives

The Pumping Lemma for Context-Free Languages is a cornerstone of formal language theory, providing a systematic method to demonstrate that certain languages cannot be generated by any context‑free grammar. In real‑world Computer Science, this lemma underpins the design of compilers, parsers, and static‑analysis tools that verify program syntax and behaviour. Mastering this lemma equips students with analytical skills essential for advanced topics such as language recognition, software verification, and formal methods.

- **Explain** the formal statement of the pumping lemma for context‑free languages.  
- **Apply** the lemma to prove that specific languages (e.g., \(a^n b^n c^n\)) are not context‑free.  
- **Construct** counterexamples that demonstrate the necessity of each pumping condition.  
- **Analyze** the limitations of the pumping lemma and distinguish it from the regular‑language pumping lemma.  
- **Design** pumping sequences for given context‑free grammars to illustrate the lemma’s constructive aspect.  
- **Evaluate** the role of the pumping lemma in the development of parsing and syntax‑analysis tools.  
- **Compare** the pumping lemma for CFLs with the pumping lemma for regular languages, highlighting their theoretical and practical implications.
