**Purpose & Learning Objectives**

Understanding alphabets, strings, and languages is fundamental to the theory of computation and underpins many practical areas such as compiler design, text processing, and cryptography. Mastery of these concepts enables students to formally describe computational problems and reason about the limits of what can be computed.

- **Define** alphabets, strings, and languages, and illustrate their standard notation.  
- **Perform** operations on strings (concatenation, reversal, substring, prefix, suffix) and on languages (union, intersection, complement, Kleene star).  
- **Distinguish** between finite and infinite languages with concrete examples from text‑processing tasks.  
- **Construct** regular expressions over a given alphabet to capture specific language patterns.  
- **Analyze** the relationship between regular languages, regular expressions, and finite automata.  
- **Apply** concepts of alphabets and strings in designing lexical analyzers for compilers.  
- **Demonstrate** closure properties of language families under various set operations.
