# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define recursively enumerable (RE) languages and explain the conditions for a language to be Turing-recognizable
2. Define recursive (decidable) languages and explain how they differ from RE languages in terms of halting behavior
3. Prove whether a given language belongs to the RE or recursive class using Turing machine constructions
4. Understand and apply closure properties of RE and recursive languages under various operations
5. Explain the relationship between recursive and recursively enumerable languages (Recursive ⊂ RE)
6. Identify and explain at least three decidable problems and three undecidable problems in formal language theory
7. Apply reduction techniques and Rice's Theorem to prove the undecidability of problems
8. Comprehend the significance of the Church-Turing thesis in defining computability
