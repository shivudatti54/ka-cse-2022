# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define a Turing Machine formally as a 7-tuple (Q, Σ, Γ, δ, q₀, q_accept, q_reject) and explain each component's role in computation.

2. Describe the components of a Turing Machine (finite control, tape, read-write head) and explain how they interact during computation.

3. Differentiate between Turing-recognizable (recursively enumerable) and decidable (recursive) languages with examples.

4. Explain the Church-Turing Thesis and its significance in establishing the Turing Machine as the formal model of computation.

5. Analyze the Halting Problem and explain why it is undecidable, understanding the proof technique of diagonalization.

6. Design simple Turing Machines for language acceptance problems like string copying, palindrome recognition, and language {0ⁿ1ⁿ}.

7. Compare deterministic and non-deterministic Turing Machines and understand the P vs NP relationship.

8. Explain the equivalence of various Turing Machine variants (multi-tape, multi-head) to the standard Turing Machine.
