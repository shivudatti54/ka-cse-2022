# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define decidability, undecidability, and recognizability in the context of computational theory
2. Explain Turing's diagonalization proof demonstrating the undecidability of the Halting Problem
3. Apply Rice's Theorem to determine the decidability status of language properties
4. Use reduction techniques to prove that new problems are undecidable
5. Distinguish between decidable, recognizable, and co-recognizable language classes
6. Analyze whether given decision problems are decidable or undecidable
7. Understand the practical implications of undecidability for software engineering and computing
