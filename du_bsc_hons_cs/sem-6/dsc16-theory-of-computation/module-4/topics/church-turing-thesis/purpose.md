## Purpose  
The Church–Turing thesis articulates the fundamental notion of computability and delineates the boundaries of what any algorithmic process can achieve. Grasping this thesis equips computer scientists with a theoretical lens to evaluate problem feasibility, design efficient algorithms, and recognize inherent limits that impact real‑world areas such as artificial intelligence, cryptography, and software engineering.  

## Learning Objectives  
- **Explain** the Church–Turing thesis and its historical significance in computability theory.  
- **Compare** Turing machines with other formal models (e.g., lambda calculus, recursive functions) to illustrate their equivalence.  
- **Analyze** the implications of the thesis on decidability, particularly the halting problem.  
- **Demonstrate** how the thesis defines the theoretical limits of algorithmic computation in practice.  
- **Evaluate** the relevance of the thesis to modern computational fields such as AI, cryptography, and algorithm design.  
- **Apply** the thesis to reason about the inherent capabilities and constraints of contemporary computer systems.
