## Purpose  
Understanding the properties of regular languages equips computer scientists with essential tools for designing efficient text‑processing, lexical analysis, and pattern‑matching systems that underpin compilers, network protocols, and software verification. Mastery of closure properties, pumping lemmas, and minimal automata also provides a foundation for more advanced topics in formal languages and complexity theory.

## Learning Objectives
- **Explain** the significance of regular languages in real‑world applications such as lexical analyzers and network packet filtering.  
- **Demonstrate** closure of regular languages under union, intersection, complement, concatenation, and Kleene star.  
- **Apply** the Pumping Lemma to prove that a given language is not regular.  
- **Construct** minimal deterministic finite automata (DFA) using state elimination or Myhill‑Nerode equivalence classes.  
- **Compare** regular expressions and finite automata in terms of expressive power and algorithmic implementation.  
- **Analyze** decidability results for regular language problems (e.g., emptiness, membership, equivalence).  
- **Design** finite‑state transducers to model simple input‑output behaviors in hardware and software systems.  
- **Evaluate** the efficiency of algorithms for regular language operations (e.g., membership testing, transformation).
