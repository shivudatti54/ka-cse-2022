## Purpose  
Deterministic Finite Automata (DFA) provide a fundamental model for representing and analyzing systems with a finite number of states, such as lexical analyzers, text‑search algorithms, and network protocol controllers. Mastering DFAs equips computer scientists with rigorous tools to reason about pattern recognition, hardware design, and formal verification in practical computing contexts.

## Learning Objectives  
- **Explain** the formal definition and components of a DFA.  
- **Construct** DFAs that accept or reject given regular languages.  
- **Analyze** the behavior of a DFA for any input string.  
- **Design** minimized DFAs using state‑equivalence and partitioning techniques.  
- **Compare** DFAs with nondeterministic finite automata (NFAs) in terms of expressiveness and complexity.  
- **Apply** the pumping lemma for regular languages to prove that certain languages are not regular.  
- **Implement** DFA‑based pattern‑matching algorithms in software.  
- **Evaluate** the practical relevance of DFAs in real‑world applications such as lexical analysis and circuit design.
