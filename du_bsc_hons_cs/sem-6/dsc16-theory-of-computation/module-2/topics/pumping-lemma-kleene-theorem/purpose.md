### Purpose
The Pumping Lemma provides a fundamental test for proving that a language is **not** regular, while Kleene’s Theorem establishes the equivalence between regular expressions and finite automata—core concepts that underpin lexical analysis, pattern‑matching, and verification tools used in real‑world software development. Mastering these results equips students with formal reasoning techniques essential for designing reliable compilers, security protocols, and text‑processing systems.

### Learning Objectives
- **Identify** regular and non‑regular languages by applying the Pumping Lemma.  
- **Explain** the statement and proof of the Pumping Lemma for regular languages.  
- **Apply** Kleene’s Theorem to convert regular expressions into equivalent finite automata and vice‑versa.  
- **Construct** deterministic and nondeterministic finite automata that generate languages described by given regular expressions.  
- **Prove** the equivalence of regular expressions and finite automata using closure properties and the constructions in Kleene’s Theorem.  
- **Analyze** the limitations of the Pumping Lemma and avoid common mis‑applications.  
- **Design** simple lexical analyzers that translate regular expressions into finite‑state machines for tokenization.  
- **Distinguish** between the pumping lemma for regular languages and the pumping lemma for context‑free languages.
