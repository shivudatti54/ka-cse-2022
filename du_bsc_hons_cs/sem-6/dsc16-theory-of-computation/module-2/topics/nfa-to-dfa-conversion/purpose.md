# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1.  Explain the concept of epsilon closure and its role in the NFA to DFA conversion algorithm.
2.  Apply the NFA to DFA conversion algorithm to a given NFA.
3.  Compute the next states for each input symbol in the DFA.
4.  Mark states in the DFA as accepting based on the accepting states of the NFA.
5.  Simplify the resulting DFA by removing any unnecessary states or transitions.
6.  Convert NFAs to DFAs with multiple accepting states.
7.  Explain the importance of the NFA to DFA conversion algorithm in compiler design and other applications.
8.  Identify and correct errors in an NFA to DFA conversion.
