# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Define Nondeterministic Finite Automata (NFA) and its components.
2. Design an NFA to recognize a given language.
3. Explain the concept of equivalence between NFA and DFA.
4. Convert an NFA to a DFA.
5. Prove that a language is regular by showing that it can be recognized by an NFA.
6. Understand the limitations of NFA and how they can be overcome using more powerful models of computation.
7. Analyze the time and space complexity of NFA-based algorithms.
8. Apply NFA in real-world problems such as text search, pattern matching, and compiler design.
