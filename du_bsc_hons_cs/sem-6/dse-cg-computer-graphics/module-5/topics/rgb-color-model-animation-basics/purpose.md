# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the RGB color model and its role in digital display systems, distinguishing between additive and subtractive color models.

2. Represent colors using RGB values in both integer (0-255) and normalized (0-1) formats, and calculate color combinations.

3. Describe how secondary colors (cyan, magenta, yellow) are formed through additive mixing of primary RGB colors.

4. Understand the concept of color depth and its impact on the range of representable colors in digital systems.

5. Explain the fundamental principles of frame-based computer animation, including frame rates and their effect on perceived motion quality.

6. Apply linear interpolation techniques to calculate intermediate positions between keyframes in animation.

7. Compare linear interpolation with easing functions and explain how easing creates more natural motion.

8. Identify and explain key animation principles including timing, spacing, keyframes, and in-between frames.

9. Understand the purpose and function of the alpha channel in digital color representation and transparency handling.
