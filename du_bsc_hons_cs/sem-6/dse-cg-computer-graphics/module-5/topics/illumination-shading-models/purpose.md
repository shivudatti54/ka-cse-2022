# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the three components of the Phong illumination model: ambient, diffuse, and specular reflection, including their physical meanings and mathematical formulations.

2. Apply Lambert's cosine law to compute diffuse reflection intensity given surface normal and light direction vectors.

3. Differentiate between flat, Gouraud, and Phong shading models based on their computational approach and visual quality.

4. Implement bilinear interpolation for computing color values across polygon surfaces in Gouraud shading.

5. Analyze the effect of the shininess coefficient (specular exponent) on the appearance of specular highlights.

6. Compare the Phong and Blinn-Phong illumination models, explaining the role of the halfway vector.

7. Evaluate trade-offs between shading methods in terms of computational cost and visual quality for different applications.
