## Purpose
Polygon‑filling algorithms are fundamental to any raster‑based graphics system, enabling the conversion of abstract geometric descriptions into visible pixels on screens. Mastering these techniques equips computer scientists with the skills to build efficient rendering pipelines, interactive GUIs, CAD tools, and real‑time games—core components of modern software development.

## Learning Objectives
- **Explain** the role of polygon filling within the graphics rendering pipeline and its impact on visual fidelity.  
- **Implement** scan‑line, flood‑fill, and boundary‑fill algorithms to convert polygons into pixel‑based images.  
- **Analyze** the time‑ and space‑complexity of each filling method and discuss scalability.  
- **Compare** the advantages and limitations of fill techniques for real‑time versus batch rendering scenarios.  
- **Optimize** fill routines for modern GPU architectures and hardware‑accelerated graphics contexts.  
- **Apply** polygon‑filling methods to interactive 2‑D applications such as CAD, GIS, and GUI frameworks.
