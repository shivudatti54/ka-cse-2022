# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental concepts of raster graphics and how geometric primitives are represented using pixels on a display screen.

2. Describe the Digital Differential Analyzer (DDA) algorithm for line drawing, including its step-by-step procedure and the mathematical basis for calculating intermediate points.

3. Implement the Bresenham's line algorithm and explain why it is more efficient than DDA in terms of computational complexity and integer arithmetic usage.

4. Apply the Midpoint Circle Algorithm to generate points on a circle's circumference, demonstrating understanding of circle symmetry and the decision parameter approach.

5. Analyze the scan-line polygon fill algorithm, including the handling of edge intersections and special cases at vertices.

6. Compare and contrast the various raster scan line algorithms in terms of performance, accuracy, and appropriate use cases.

7. Evaluate the quality of line rendering by understanding concepts like aliasing, step size, and the approximation of continuous mathematical curves using discrete pixels.

8. Apply these algorithms to solve practical graphics problems and understand their significance in modern graphics rendering pipelines.
