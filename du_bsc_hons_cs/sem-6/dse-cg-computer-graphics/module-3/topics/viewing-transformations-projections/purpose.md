## Purpose & Learning Objectives

This topic underpins the rendering pipeline that transforms three‑dimensional scenes into the two‑dimensional images we see on screens. Mastery of viewing transformations and projections is essential for creating realistic visualizations, interactive games, simulations, and applications in fields such as CAD, virtual reality, and medical imaging.

- Explain the role of viewing transformations and projection in the graphics pipeline.  
- Distinguish between orthographic and perspective projection models and their mathematical formulations.  
- Compute view‑matrix and projection‑matrix transformations for a given camera setup.  
- Apply projection matrices to map 3D world coordinates to normalized device coordinates.  
- Derive the clipping equations used in conjunction with projection to eliminate invisible geometry.  
- Analyze the effect of camera parameters (position, orientation, focal length, field of view) on the resulting image.  
- Compare different projection techniques and select appropriate methods for specific real‑world applications.  
- Implement a basic viewing and projection pipeline using a graphics API such as OpenGL.
