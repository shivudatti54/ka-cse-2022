### Purpose & Learning Objectives

Raster scan and random scan display technologies form the backbone of all modern visual output systems, from CRT monitors to high‑resolution LCD and OLED panels. Mastering these concepts equips computer scientists with the knowledge needed to select, configure, and optimize display hardware for diverse real‑world applications such as gaming, scientific visualization, and embedded user interfaces.

- **Explain** the fundamental principles of raster and random scan display mechanisms.  
- **Differentiate** between raster scan (pixel‑by‑pixel) and random scan (vector) architectures.  
- **Describe** the components of a raster display system, including frame buffer, video controller, and refresh circuitry.  
- **Identify** the advantages and limitations of each scan method in terms of resolution, refresh rate, and image quality.  
- **Analyze** how scan parameters (horizontal/vertical sync, refresh rate) influence perceived performance and power consumption.  
- **Design** simple graphical primitives using a raster frame buffer and appropriate drawing algorithms.  
- **Evaluate** the impact of modern display technologies (LCD, LED, OLED) on the evolution of raster scanning.  
- **Compare** historical vector displays with contemporary raster devices in terms of rendering pipelines and application domains.
