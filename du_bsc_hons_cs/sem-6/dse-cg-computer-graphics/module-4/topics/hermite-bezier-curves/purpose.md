## Purpose  

Hermite and Bezier curves are fundamental to modern computer‑graphics applications such as vector‑graphic editors, CAD systems, animation packages, and game engines, where smooth, controllable shapes are required. Mastering their mathematical underpinnings enables you to implement efficient curve rendering, interpolation, and shape manipulation—skills that transfer directly to spline‑based surface modeling, robotics path planning, and font design.

## Learning Objectives  

- **Define** the parametric equations of Hermite and Bezier curves and identify their basis functions.  
- **Explain** the geometric meaning of control points, tangent vectors, and the role they play in shaping the curve.  
- **Apply** Hermite and Bezier interpolation techniques to construct smooth curves that satisfy given end‑point constraints.  
- **Compare** the continuity properties (C⁰, C¹, G¹) and local control characteristics of Hermite versus Bezier representations.  
- **Implement** algorithms for curve evaluation (e.g., de Casteljau, Horner) and subdivision (splitting a curve at a parameter value).  
- **Utilize** these curves in practical graphics tasks such as drawing paths, animating trajectories, and generating procedural geometry.  
- **Analyze** the effect of polynomial degree and control‑point placement on curve shape, curvature, and computational cost.  
- **Design** interactive tools for real‑time curve manipulation and visualization within a graphics application.
