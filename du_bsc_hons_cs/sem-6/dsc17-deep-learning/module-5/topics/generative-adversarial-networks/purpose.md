# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the fundamental concept of Generative Adversarial Networks and the adversarial framework involving generator and discriminator networks.

2. Derive and interpret the minimax objective function that governs GAN training, understanding both the generator's and discriminator's optimization goals.

3. Analyze the training dynamics of GANs, including the equilibrium condition where the generator successfully models the true data distribution.

4. Identify and explain common training challenges in GANs such as mode collapse, non-convergence, and vanishing gradients, along with standard mitigation strategies.

5. Compare different variants of GANs including DCGAN, Conditional GAN (cGAN), and Wasserstein GAN (WGAN), understanding their architectural differences and improvements.

6. Design a basic GAN architecture for a given data generation problem, specifying appropriate network components, activation functions, and training procedures.

7. Evaluate the quality of generated samples and discuss real-world applications of GANs in computer vision and other domains.

8. Apply your understanding of GANs to solve examination problems involving mathematical formulations, architecture design, and conceptual analysis.
