## Purpose
Autoencoders are a core unsupervised deep‑learning model that learn compact latent representations of data, enabling real‑world applications such as dimensionality reduction, anomaly detection, image denoising, and generative modeling. Mastering autoencoders equips Computer Science graduates with essential skills to build data‑driven pipelines for feature extraction, compression, and creative AI solutions demanded in industry and research.

## Learning Objectives
- **Explain** the architecture and training dynamics of basic and variational autoencoders.  
- **Implement** autoencoder models using modern deep‑learning frameworks (e.g., TensorFlow/PyTorch).  
- **Apply** autoencoders for dimensionality reduction and feature learning in varied datasets.  
- **Analyze** latent space representations to interpret learned encodings and support downstream tasks.  
- **Design** autoencoder‑based solutions for tasks such as denoising, anomaly detection, and recommendation systems.  
- **Evaluate** reconstruction performance using appropriate metrics and interpret errors.  
- **Compare** autoencoders with alternative representation‑learning techniques (e.g., PCA, GANs).  
- **Leverage** pre‑trained autoencoders for transfer learning in domain‑specific applications.
