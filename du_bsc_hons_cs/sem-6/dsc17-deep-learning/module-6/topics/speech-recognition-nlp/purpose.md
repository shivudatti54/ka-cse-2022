## Purpose  
Speech recognition is a cornerstone of modern human‑computer interaction, powering voice assistants, transcription services, accessibility tools, and immersive AI experiences. Deep learning has dramatically improved the accuracy and robustness of speech‑to‑text systems, making it essential for Computer Science graduates to understand and apply these techniques in real‑world applications.

## Learning Objectives
- **Explain** the fundamentals of speech signal processing and its connection to NLP.  
- **Analyze** the architecture of state‑of‑the‑art deep learning models for speech recognition (e.g., CNN, RNN, Transformer, CTC).  
- **Implement** an end‑to‑end speech‑to‑text pipeline using frameworks such as TensorFlow or PyTorch.  
- **Evaluate** model performance using metrics like Word Error Rate (WER), latency, and robustness to noise.  
- **Design** data preprocessing pipelines that handle varied accents, speaking rates, and acoustic environments.  
- **Compare** traditional GMM‑HMM approaches with modern deep learning methods in terms of performance and scalability.  
- **Apply** optimization techniques such as transfer learning, model pruning, and quantization for deploying speech models on resource‑constrained devices.
