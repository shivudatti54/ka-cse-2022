### Purpose
Computer Vision is reshaping industries by enabling machines to interpret visual data with human‑like accuracy, powering innovations ranging from autonomous vehicles to smart healthcare diagnostics. Mastery of deep‑learning‑based vision techniques equips Computer Science graduates with the skills to build real‑world systems that can sense, understand, and act upon visual information.

### Learning Objectives
- **Explain** the fundamental concepts and architectures (e.g., CNNs, Vision Transformers) that underpin modern computer vision models.  
- **Implement** end‑to‑end pipelines for image classification, object detection, and semantic segmentation using popular deep‑learning frameworks.  
- **Analyze** the performance of vision systems, interpreting metrics such as mAP, IoU, and precision‑recall to guide model improvements.  
- **Design** data augmentation and preprocessing strategies that enhance model robustness and generalization.  
- **Evaluate** ethical considerations and bias issues inherent in visual AI applications, proposing mitigation approaches.  
- **Apply** transfer learning and pre‑trained models to solve domain‑specific vision problems with limited labeled data.  
- **Compare** emerging trends (e.g., self‑supervised learning, multimodal models) and assess their impact on future computer vision research.  
- **Develop** a complete vision project— from problem definition to deployment—demonstrating end‑to‑end proficiency.
