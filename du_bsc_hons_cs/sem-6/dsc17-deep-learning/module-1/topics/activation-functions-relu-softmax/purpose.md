## Purpose
Activation functions are fundamental components that introduce non‑linearity into neural networks, enabling them to learn complex patterns and approximate arbitrary mappings. ReLU and Softmax are the most widely used activation functions in modern deep learning models—ReLU for hidden layers to improve efficiency and mitigate vanishing gradients, and Softmax for multi‑class output layers to produce probability distributions. Mastering these functions is essential for building effective machine‑learning solutions in fields such as computer vision, natural language processing, and speech recognition.

## Learning Objectives
- **Explain** the role of activation functions in enabling neural networks to model non‑linear relationships.  
- **Compare** the properties, computational cost, and typical use cases of ReLU versus Softmax.  
- **Implement** ReLU and Softmax functions using a deep learning framework such as TensorFlow or PyTorch.  
- **Analyze** the effect of ReLU on gradient flow and its influence on training stability in deep architectures.  
- **Apply** Softmax to multi‑class classification problems and interpret its output as a probability distribution.  
- **Design** a neural network layer selecting appropriate activation functions for a given task.  
- **Evaluate** the impact of different activation functions on model convergence, accuracy, and generalization.  
- **Differentiate** between ReLU variants (e.g., Leaky ReLU, PReLU) and justify their selection in specific scenarios.
