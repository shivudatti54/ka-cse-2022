## Purpose and Learning Objectives

Multilayer feedforward networks are the foundation of many real‑world AI systems—ranging from image recognition and natural language processing to predictive analytics—enabling computers to learn complex, non‑linear mappings from data. Mastering this topic equips students with the practical skills to design, train, and debug deep learning models, paving the way for advanced research and industry‑ready AI solutions.

- Explain the architectural components and computational graph of a multilayer feedforward network.  
- Describe forward propagation and the role of activation functions in enabling non‑linear transformations.  
- Derive and apply the backpropagation algorithm to compute gradients efficiently.  
- Implement a multilayer feedforward network using a deep learning framework on a real dataset.  
- Analyze the influence of hyperparameters (e.g., learning rate, number of layers, hidden units) on model performance.  
- Evaluate training dynamics, including convergence, overfitting, and regularization strategies.  
- Compare feedforward networks with other neural architectures for classification and regression tasks.
