## Purpose
Artificial Neurons – Perceptron is the cornerstone of neural network theory and provides the basic computational unit for pattern‑recognition and classification tasks that underpin many real‑world computer‑science applications such as spam filtering, image recognition, and autonomous decision‑making. By mastering the perceptron model, students gain insight into fundamental concepts—weights, bias, activation, and linear separability—that are essential for understanding and designing modern deep‑learning systems.

## Learning Objectives
- **Explain** the mathematical formulation and algorithmic steps of a single‑layer perceptron.  
- **Differentiate** between linearly separable and non‑linearly separable data sets.  
- **Implement** a perceptron learning algorithm in Python to train a binary classifier.  
- **Analyze** the convergence conditions and the effect of learning rate on perceptron training.  
- **Design** a perceptron‑based solution for a practical binary classification problem.  
- **Evaluate** the limitations of the perceptron and the necessity of multi‑layer architectures.  
- **Compare** the perceptron learning rule with gradient‑based optimization used in deeper networks.
