# Learning Objectives

After studying this topic, you should be able to:

1. Explain the concept of greedy layerwise training and describe why it was developed to address challenges in training deep neural networks.

2. Compare and contrast greedy layerwise training with traditional end-to-end backpropagation, highlighting the advantages and disadvantages of each approach.

3. Describe the architecture and working principles of autoencoders, and explain their role in unsupervised pretraining of deep networks.

4. Implement the greedy layerwise pretraining algorithm for stacked autoencoders, including the freezing mechanism and representation extraction steps.

5. Understand the mathematical foundations of why greedy pretraining provides better initializations, including the concept of hierarchical feature learning.

6. Explain how greedy layerwise training addresses the vanishing gradient problem and enables training of much deeper networks.

7. Describe the complete workflow from unsupervised pretraining to supervised fine-tuning in practical deep learning applications.

8. Evaluate when greedy layerwise pretraining is appropriate versus when modern end-to-end training methods are sufficient.
