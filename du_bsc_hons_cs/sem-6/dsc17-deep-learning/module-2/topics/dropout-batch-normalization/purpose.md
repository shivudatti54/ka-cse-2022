### Purpose  
In modern AI‑driven software, deep neural networks are the backbone of applications such as computer vision, natural language processing, and speech recognition. Understanding how to combat overfitting (via dropout) and stabilize training (via batch normalization) is essential for building scalable, efficient, and robust models that generalize well in production environments. This module equips students with the theoretical foundations and practical skills needed to integrate these techniques into real‑world deep learning solutions.

### Learning Objectives
- **Explain** the concept of overfitting and the necessity of regularization in deep learning.  
- **Describe** the dropout mechanism, its probabilistic sampling strategy, and its effect on network training.  
- **Illustrate** the batch‑normalization process, including how it normalizes activations and adjusts parameters during training.  
- **Apply** dropout and batch normalization to a convolutional or recurrent neural network using a modern deep learning framework (e.g., TensorFlow or PyTorch).  
- **Analyze** the impact of these techniques on convergence speed, gradient flow, and model performance.  
- **Evaluate** the trade‑offs between model complexity, training time, and generalization ability.  
- **Design** a complete deep‑learning pipeline that incorporates both dropout and batch normalization for a specified problem domain.
