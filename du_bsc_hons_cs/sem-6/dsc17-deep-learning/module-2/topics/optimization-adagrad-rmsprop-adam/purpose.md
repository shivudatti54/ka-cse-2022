## Purpose  

Adaptive gradient methods such as **Adagrad**, **RMSprop**, and **Adam** are the backbone of efficient deep learning model training in industry‑scale applications. Mastering these optimizers enables computer scientists to design faster, more stable training pipelines for real‑world AI systems, from computer vision to natural language processing.

## Learning Objectives  

- **Explain** the underlying mechanics of adaptive learning‑rate methods and their role in mitigating vanishing gradients.  
- **Implement** Adagrad, RMSprop, and Adam optimizers from scratch in a deep learning framework.  
- **Compare** the convergence behavior, computational overhead, and sensitivity to hyperparameters of each algorithm on benchmark datasets.  
- **Analyze** the impact of momentum and bias‑correction terms on training dynamics for various network architectures.  
- **Evaluate** the suitability of each optimizer for specific problem domains (e.g., sparse data, non‑stationary objectives).  
- **Apply** the selected optimizer to a real‑world deep learning project, optimizing model performance under resource constraints.
